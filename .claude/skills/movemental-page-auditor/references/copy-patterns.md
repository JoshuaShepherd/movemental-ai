# Copy & language patterns — flag list and canonical vocabulary

## 1. SaaS-isms and hype to flag on sight

Cut or rewrite on sight. If any of these appear on a Movemental page, something has gone wrong:

| Pattern | Why it fails |
| ------- | ------------ |
| unlock, revolutionize, supercharge, 10x, game-changing, cutting-edge, next-generation | Startup vocabulary, inflates the claim, reads as posturing |
| AI-powered, AI-driven, AI-first | Reduces AI to a buzzword; Movemental's position is AI in context |
| seamless, frictionless, effortless | Implies zero trade-offs; erodes trust |
| empowering, impactful, transformative (as adjectives) | Vague; signals marketing, not thought |
| at scale, enterprise-grade, world-class | Unverifiable status claim |
| join the movement, be the change | Emotional manipulation without argument |
| in today's fast-moving world / in this rapidly changing landscape | Generic framing, filler |
| trusted by industry leaders, loved by teams | Social-proof cliché without names |
| the future of X is here | Cliché; no argument |
| turbocharge, skyrocket, explode | Hyperbole |
| your AI copilot / your AI partner | Anthropomorphization; overstates autonomy |
| solutions (standalone, generic) | "We offer solutions" = nothing; be specific |
| journey (standalone) | Vague; prefer "path" (canonical) or concrete verbs |

**Borderline — use only with reason:**

- **Transform, transformation** — acceptable in formation contexts ("formation transforms judgment"), not acceptable as a marketing adjective ("transform your organization").
- **Platform** — acceptable when technical; avoid when it hides vagueness.
- **Framework** — acceptable (the AI Stewardship Sequence is a framework); avoid when inflating a list.
- **Innovative, innovation** — almost always cuttable.

## 3. Five Movemental voice markers (check positively)

Every page should read as though it came from the same hand as the book and `docs/articles/the-movemental-thesis.md`. These are the five markers to check for:

### Marker 1 — Clarity

Every sentence transmits information. No sentence is "atmospheric."

**Passing example:** "Safety is the organization's explicit answer to the questions it will be ashamed not to have asked before AI sits down inside its real work."

**Failing example:** "In today's evolving landscape, organizations must navigate change with intention and purpose." (Transmits nothing.)

### Marker 2 — Precision

Named concepts used with their canonical meaning. No word is a placeholder.

**Passing:** "Informational intelligence — scattered, it cannot compound or form people."

**Failing:** "Our content layer is optimized for AI readiness." (Vague; drift from canon.)

### Marker 3 — Seriousness

No winking, no emoji-substitutes, no fake-casual. The tone is that of someone who has thought about this for a decade.

**Passing:** "This is not primarily a tool problem. It is a problem of judgment, responsibility, and organizational coherence."

**Failing:** "Let's face it — AI is everywhere, and it's kind of a lot!" (Casual, retreats from the claim.)

### Marker 4 — Warmth without sentimentality

Human voice; no saccharine emotional appeals. The author cares about the reader's situation without inflating it.

**Passing:** "You don't have to figure this out alone. We'll help you begin — wisely."

**Failing:** "We believe in a better future for every organization on its sacred journey." (Saccharine; evasive.)

### Marker 5 — Authority without grandiosity

Confident statements, grounded in the work, without self-declared credentials.

**Passing:** "The product was shaped inside real movement, church, nonprofit, and institutional questions — not imported from generic software logic."

**Failing:** "We are the world's leading platform for mission-driven AI transformation." (Self-declared; no proof.)

## 4. Repeated phrasing — monotony patterns

Flag any of these within a single page:

- Three or more sentences starting with the same subject ("We do not..." / "We begin..." / "We believe...").
- Three or more sections where the eyebrow + headline + short explanation shape is repeated without variation (see `typography-checks.md` for the visual version).
- The same italicized phrase (via SerifEm) appearing more than once in the same viewport.
- The same idea expressed three different ways in three paragraphs without adding information.

---

## 5. Elegant-but-empty — the quiet failure

The most common Movemental-specific failure is not SaaS-ism; it is **elegant prose that sounds load-bearing and isn't.** Flag these:

- Sentences that could be cut with no change in meaning.
- Sentences that paraphrase the previous sentence with slightly different words.
- Sentences that gesture at a concept without naming it.
- Abstract nouns stacked ("clarity, confidence, and integrity") without a concrete example.

**Test:** if you can replace the sentence with "[gesture toward importance]" and the page reads the same, the sentence is decoration.

## 6. Audience-specific voice calibration

- **Nonprofit pages** — name donors, boards, missions. Avoid ecclesial metaphors.
- **Church pages** — pastoral, discipleship, formation language is canonical here.
- **Institution pages** — faculty, curriculum, accreditors, institutional memory.
- **Homepage** — broadly applicable for the first four sections; audience-specific lines acceptable below the fold.
- **Evidence, walkthrough, system** — technical, constrained, precise.

**Risk:** collapsing all three audiences into one voice. Audience pages should sound like they were written for that audience specifically, without losing the canonical vocabulary.

---

## 7. Rewriting guidance

When the audit recommends a rewrite, the rewrite should:

1. **Preserve the canonical vocabulary** (do not replace "formation" with "training," etc.).
2. **Make the claim more concrete, not more decorative.**
3. **Shorten, not lengthen** — almost always.
4. **Move from adjective to verb** ("transformative experience" → "forms judgment").
5. **Move from abstract noun to concrete artifact** ("content strategy" → "donor letters, pastoral notes, faculty memory").

Use the `alan-voice` skill for a final voice pass if deep rewriting is needed.
