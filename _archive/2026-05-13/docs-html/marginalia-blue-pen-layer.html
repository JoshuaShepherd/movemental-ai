Human layer: blue pen marginalia

Five annotations that read like someone leaned in with a ballpoint: connectors, loops, brackets, and underlines. Pair with the highlighter demo for clean body copy plus human overlay.

Example 1 — Margin note + elbow connector

tie this to last quarter spend — otherwise it sounds theoretical

We should fund the pilot from operating budget, not a special project line. The narrative has to connect finance and outcomes in one thread. Make the budget story explicit early.

Example 2 — Looped circle (not a perfect ellipse)

Every rollout needs a single owner with authority to say no to scope creep. accountable sponsor is the phrase people nod at — then ignore unless it is named.

Example 3 — Vertical bracket + squeezed sidenote
yes — but only if logged

Leaders often ask for AI transparency. In practice, that means durable audit trails users can follow without an engineering degree: sources, edits, and abstentions in plain language.

Example 4 — Squiggle underline (emphasis without highlight)

If the summary cannot cite where it learned a claim, treat it as a draft hypothesis — not a finished answer. The squiggle reads faster than another paragraph of policy.

Example 5 — Small path jitter on click (replayable)

Documentation is not overhead when it is the difference between a team that remembers its decisions and a team that re-litigates them monthly.

worth the extra hour while memory is fresh
