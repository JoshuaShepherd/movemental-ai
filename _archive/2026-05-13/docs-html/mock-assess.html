AI Stewardship Sequence · Integrity Diagnostic

Where is your organization actually starting?

A short diagnostic for senior leaders who suspect their AI conversation is further along than their AI posture is. Forty-five minutes to complete with two or three colleagues in the room. A six-page read-back inside five business days, read with you on a thirty-minute call.

Six dimensions

Six honest questions, in order.

The diagnostic walks the same Sequence the engagement walks: Safety, Sandbox, Skills, Solutions — broken into six readable dimensions, each with its own short section.

  • 01

    Sequence integrity

    Whether your organization has walked Safety, Sandbox, Skills, Solutions in order — or skipped a stage and is now working backward to recover it.

  • 02

    Posture clarity

    Whether the senior team can state, in one paragraph, the organization's relationship to AI in language a board would ratify.

  • 03

    Refusal capacity

    Whether the organization has named, in writing, three things AI is not allowed to touch — and whether anyone outside the senior team knows what they are.

  • 04

    Sandbox truth

    Whether your staff have explored real AI work against real organizational data, with the senior team watching, before any deployment.

  • 05

    Formation, not training

    Whether the staff judgment around AI is built or borrowed — formed in your context, or imported from someone else's.

  • 06

    Solution restraint

    Whether tools have been deployed only after the human work, or whether tools are pulling the human work behind them at speed.

Preview

Forty-five minutes, six sections, no scoring.

The diagnostic has 22 questions across six sections. None of them are timed. None of them are scored. The point is to make the conversation more honest, not to rank your organization.

Section 1 of 6 · Sequence integrity Question 1 of 4

Has your senior team named, in writing, what AI is allowed to touch in your organization?

  • Yes — drafted, ratified by board or elders, and shared with staff.
  • Drafted by the senior team, not yet ratified.
  • Discussed in meetings, not drafted.
  • Not yet — staff are using AI ahead of any agreement.

This is a styled preview, not the live form. The full diagnostic adds a short follow-up after each question ("Show your work — one or two sentences"), which is what the read-back is built from.

The read-back

A six-page narrative, not a number.

You complete the diagnostic. We read it. Within five business days, you receive a written read-back and a calendar invite for a thirty-minute call to walk it.

  • The read-back document Six pages, one per dimension. What we read in your answers, in plain language. Where the Sequence is intact and where it is not. No score, no benchmark, no rank.
  • A thirty-minute call One of the Movemental founders reads the document with you. The point of the call is to make sure we got the read right. We will revise the document if we did not.
  • A short list of next moves Three things to do next. Sometimes the answer is "do less, slower." Sometimes the answer is "this is further along than you thought." Both are real findings.
  • Permission to share it If the diagnostic is honest, share it with your board. If it is not yet, tell us what we missed and we will rewrite the section.

What this is not

The line, on the page.

Editorial note

This is not a maturity model. It is not a benchmark. It is not a score. We will not tell you that your organization is at "level 3 of 5." We will not produce a percentile. There is no leaderboard, public or private. If you are looking for a tool that ranks your organization against a peer set, this is not that tool, on purpose. Maturity-model framing is the most common way the AI conversation is being polluted right now. Movemental is built to refuse it.

The diagnostic is held confidentially by the Movemental founders. It is not shared, sold, aggregated, or anonymized into any benchmark. It is yours.

Where this sits

Before the first proposal, not after.

We use the Integrity Diagnostic to read whether the timing is right before any proposal is written. If you are not yet ready for the conversation it asks you to have, the diagnostic itself is the engagement — there is no further conversation needed yet, and we will tell you so. If you are ready, the diagnostic is the first artifact we write together, and the rest of the engagement begins from what it surfaces.

Common questions

What leaders ask before they take it.

Does this cost anything?

No. The diagnostic is free, including the thirty-minute read-back call. We invest the time because the diagnostic is also how we read whether an engagement is a fit. If it is not, we want to know that before either of us has spent more than an hour.

Can our board take it?

Yes, and we recommend it. The most useful version of the diagnostic is taken twice: once by the senior staff and once by the board or elder team. The gap between the two answer sets is usually the most diagnostic part.

Can we take it more than once?

Yes. Most organizations re-take it after the Sandbox stage — the answers shift, sometimes dramatically. Some questions only become answerable after the human work has been done.

Is the data confidential?

Yes. The diagnostic is held by the Movemental founders and is not shared, sold, aggregated, or anonymized into any benchmark. The read-back is yours; the raw answers stay with us only as long as needed to complete the read-back.

Why isn't there a public leaderboard?

On purpose — see the editorial note above. A leaderboard would change what the diagnostic is for. The point is honesty inside the room, not ranking across rooms.

What if we are not ready to take it?

Read the field guide first. The diagnostic assumes the senior team has read it, or read enough of it to know what the four stages are and why the order matters. Taking the diagnostic without that grounding usually produces answers that need to be rewritten on the read-back call, which is a slower path to the same place.
