Definition

Trusted voices are an ecosystem layer, not an audience. Read the definition.

Why credibility now lives in networks.

AI has removed the practical assurance of human provenance. The old credibility signals, publication, institutional affiliation, format, no longer guarantee authorship. The question changes from where was this published? to who stands behind this?

Movemental's answer is a small scene of named voices whose public work, named relationships, and stated posture can carry weight in writing. We do not recruit. We do not pay for endorsements. We do not publish people whose voice has not already shaped the field.

This page is the public surface of that scene. Every voice here has signed on, in writing, to the Movemental approach to AI adoption. Their work links out to the platforms they already lead.

Who is a movement leader?

The definition Movemental works with. This page is not for nominating someone, including yourself. It exists so the term means something specific when we use it.

A movement leader does

  • Carry a body of public work that other practitioners in the field already cite.
  • Lead a network, school, or community that exists in writing and in the world.
  • Have public peers who would say their work has changed how the field thinks.
  • Show, in their own work, that formation precedes deployment of new tools.

A movement leader does not

  • Need to be famous. Several movement leaders are known inside their tradition and quiet outside it.
  • Need to have written a book. Some have. Some have written essays, founded fellowships, or led networks for two decades.
  • Pay to be listed here, accept payment to be listed here, or get listed because they asked.
  • Speak on behalf of the others. Each voice on this page speaks for themselves.
