Pathways

A degree is an arc, not a catalog.

Every program in Kairos is modeled as five linked phases, each with coursework, cohort, mentorship, and institutional signals in one view.

M.Div · Missional leadership · three-year arc
Illustrative pathway · phases not semesters
  1. 01 Phase I Discernment & orientation Calling interview, corpus induction, cohort placement, mentor match.
  2. 02 Phase II Theological foundations Scripture, tradition, method — graded and formation-reviewed together.
  3. 03 Phase III Embedded practice Practicum inside a real community, supervisor feedback logged in-system.
  4. 04 Phase IV Integrative thesis Work sits inside the knowledge graph; advisors respond inline; citations stay linked.
  5. 05 Phase V Sending & alumni Commissioning, partner placement, alumni thread stays live for decades.
Inside the platform

Three views that turn a catalog into an institution.

03 · Knowledge graph

The institution's living theological memory.

Faculty lectures and student work land in the same linked node graph. Citations stay live, positions evolve over time, and two years later a student can trace the development of an argument back to the lecture that planted it.

  • Lecture · paper · framework · position · debate — all modeled as nodes.
  • Faculty scholarship and student learning draw from one corpus.
  • Queryable by humans and bounded AI, always with citations.
04 · Relational graph

People, not registrar rows.

Every student, faculty member, mentor, alumnus, and partner is a node that keeps existing after graduation. The institution remembers who it has taught, who it has sent, and who is now forming the next cohort.

  • One graph spans admissions → student → alumnus → mentor → adjunct.
  • Mentor and cohort placement informed by real institutional context.
  • Alumni stay reachable, teachable, and meaningful to current students.
07 · Institutional intelligence

See formation depth, not just completion.

Leadership sees the institution as it actually is. Pathway completion, retention and drift, theological development, mentor coverage, alumni fidelity — formation next to accreditation, not parallel to it.

  • Formation depth per cohort, surfaced weekly.
  • Early-warning drift detection before it becomes a record.
  • Accreditation-mapped outcomes drawn from the same source.
06 · Bounded AI

An institution-critical AI layer that serves the tradition — and shows its work.

Kairos ships AI grounded in your corpus, scoped to your institution, and constitutionally unable to answer without citing sources. It is a research partner, a writing support, and a pedagogical co-designer — not a substitute for faculty judgment.

  • Research assistant grounded in your graph, with live citations.
  • Writing assistant that supports thinking, never replaces it.
  • Faculty design support for courses, rubrics, and assessments.
  • Bounded grading assistance — rubric alignment only, never final judgment.
  • Cross-tradition navigation across authors and positions.
