How we work

Leadership, strategy, and engineering as one thread.

The public story of Movemental — fragmentation, integration, and formation — is not a marketing overlay. It reflects how we actually think about product decisions: humans accountable for judgment, systems that respect continuity, and design that treats credibility as cumulative rather than performative.

Three people carry that posture into the work — a missional strategist, a movement thinker, and a platform architect. What shows up in the product is what shows up in the room.

Who you’re working with

Three public faces, one posture.

Co-founders Brad Brisco and Alan Hirsch, alongside founder and CTO Joshua Shepherd. Missional ecclesiology, movement thinking, and the engineering discipline that holds the platform together — held by the same three people from first call to shipped system.

Brad Brisco

CEO & Co-founder

Missional church strategist, author, and multiplication leader — helping churches and planters bridge institutional structures and grassroots mission.

Brad has shaped how North American churches understand missional transition, covocational church planting, and neighborhood-based mission — through published books, curriculum, organizational leadership with NAMB’s Send Network, and extensive training roles including Forge America and Forge Kansas City.

Representative works include The Missional Quest, Next Door as It Is in Heaven (with Lance Ford), and Covocational Church Planting. He co-founded the Sentralized conference and keeps an active voice through the Missional Church Network.

Alan Hirsch

Chief Missiologist & Co-founder

Movement thinker, author, and founding influence behind Movemental’s formation philosophy. Decades of work in missional theology, church planting, and leadership development.

Alan is known for helping the church recover its apostolic nature — and rediscover movement as its native expression. His current work translates four decades of frameworks into formational resources that equip leaders wherever they are.

Author of The Forgotten Ways, 5Q, Reframation, The Permanent Revolution, and more. Co-founder of the 5Q Collective and Movement Leaders Collective; partner with 100 Movements and Forge across six continents.

Joshua Shepherd

CTO & Founder

Full-stack architect with deep experience in platform infrastructure, AI systems, and movement-leader technology. Builds the system layer that holds everything together.

Joshua is a writer, formation guide, and the creator of Trail Guide — a hybrid digital and relational ecosystem designed to equip spiritual leaders and imaginative practitioners for life in a rapidly evolving machine age.

Not a futurist by training, but a shepherd by instinct. His recent work centers on the question: how do we remain human in the age of machines — and how do we do it together?

At Movemental, Joshua leads product and engineering — translating that posture into infrastructure for thought leaders and formation-minded organizations.

Our posture

What shows up in the product is what shows up in the room.

  1. 01

    Humans accountable for judgment.

    AI does not decide what matters. People do. Every consequential call in a Movemental system is owned by a named human, not a model.

  2. 02

    Systems that respect continuity.

    A life’s work does not begin this quarter. We build infrastructure that can carry thirty years of writing forward — not a campaign stack that forgets itself by Q3.

  3. 03

    Credibility as cumulative.

    Trust is built slowly and broken quickly. Our products are designed so that every small signal points the same direction — the same voice, the same care, the same discipline, over years.
