# AI Principles Page (`/ai-principles`)

## Purpose
Publish Movemental’s seven AI commitments, transparency system, and voice-preservation guarantees to build trust with leaders, audiences, and accountability partners.

## Audience
- Movement leaders vetting AI philosophy.
- Readers wanting to understand transparency badges.
- Media/investors reviewing ethical stance.

## Outcomes
1. Visitors understand the seven core principles (voice-centered AI, theological integrity, transparency, human agency, ethics, network verification, continuous improvement).
2. Users can see how transparency badges work across content.
3. Page links to `/transparency` and governance/Trust Center resources.

## Component Map
1. **Hero Banner**
   - Statement: “AI that amplifies, never replaces, the movement leader.”
   - Subtext referencing voice preservation + theological integrity.
2. **Principles Accordion**
   - Each principle expanded with commitments, practices, measurements.
   - Include real examples and metrics (95% theological accuracy, 95% voice consistency).
3. **Transparency Badge System**
   - Visual display of badge types (Human-Written, AI-Assisted, AI-Enhanced, AI-Generated).
   - Tooltips detailing disclosure expectations.
4. **Workflow Diagram**
   - Steps: Content creation → AI assistance → Voice & theology QA → Transparency tagging → Publication.
5. **Case Examples**
   - Show how badges appear on Alan’s article, Soul of Movement essay, Media Lab output.
6. **FAQ Section**
   - Questions on AI boundaries, human agency, theological review, disclosure policies.
7. **CTA Footer**
   - Links to `/transparency`, `/governance`, and contact for ethics inquiries.

## Data Needs

- Badge icons & color codes.
- QA metrics for voice/theology compliance.
- Examples from actual content pieces.

## Tone
- Transparent, confident, technically clear.
- Emphasize theological fidelity and scenius accountability.
