# Resources Page (`/resources`)

## Purpose
Serve as the public library and funnel into deeper Movemental education—AI briefs, SEO guides, transparency documentation, workshop replays, templates.

## Audience
- Current/prospective leaders seeking practical playbooks.
- Referral sources wanting proof of Movemental’s generosity.
- Media/investors evaluating thought leadership.

## Outcomes
1. Visitors browse by topic (AI, Content, SEO, Governance, Design).
2. Capture leads for high-value downloads/webinars (email capture for private content).
3. Reinforce cadence (weekly briefs, bi-weekly labs, quarterly playbooks).

## Component Map
1. **Intro Banner**
   - Copy: “Movemental equips movement leaders with actionable frameworks.”
   - Subcopy describing cadence + newsletter CTA.
2. **Topic Filters**
   - Chips for AI & Voice, Content Repurposing, SEO & Discovery, Governance & Transparency, Design & Customization.
3. **Featured Resources**
   - Hero card (latest AI brief), secondary cards (SEO framework, transparency badge guide).
4. **Resource Grid**
   - Card elements: title, summary, format (Guide, Video, Template), tags, CTA.
   - Example items: AI Transparency Guide, NotebookLM Workflow Deck, Movemental Gate explainer, customization checklist.
5. **Workshop & Event Replays**
   - Carousel of on-demand videos (Media Lab demo, Content OS walkthrough, AI ethics session).
6. **Template Library**
   - Download cards for sermon-to-article template, cohort curriculum outline, transparency disclosure templates.
7. **Newsletter & Lab Signup**
   - Embedded form for weekly AI briefs and bi-weekly platform labs.
8. **Deep Links**
   - Buttons to `/ai-principles`, `/transparency`, `/governance`, `/platform`.

## Data Needs
- CMS records for each resource (title, summary, tags, format, link, gated vs. open).
- Video hosting links (Vimeo/YouTube/private).
- Newsletter integrations (ConvertKit, Mailchimp, etc.).

## Tone
- Helpful, practical, no fluff.
- Reinforce Movemental voice (authoritative yet invitational).

## Open Questions
- Decide gating rules (e.g., certain PDFs require email).
- Determine if resources should surface recommended content based on visitor archetype (future personalization).
