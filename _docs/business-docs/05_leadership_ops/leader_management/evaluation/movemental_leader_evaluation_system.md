# Movemental Leader Evaluation System

## Overview

This evaluation system assesses potential movemental leaders for the Movemental publishing platform based on alignment with our mission, audience quality, revenue potential, and platform readiness. The system emphasizes both content quality and diversity of voices while maintaining high standards for theological depth and practical movement impact.

### **1. Movement Alignment & Theological Depth (25 points)**
**Weight: 25%** - Core compatibility with missional/incarnational theology and movemental practice

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **Exceptional (23-25 points)**: Published works in missional theology, recognized movemental leader, theological depth with practical application
- **Strong (18-22 points)**: Clear alignment with movemental principles, some published work, solid theological foundation
- **Moderate (13-17 points)**: General alignment with kingdom values, emerging movemental voice
- **Basic (8-12 points)**: Some connection to movement themes, limited theological depth
- **Weak (0-7 points)**: Minimal alignment or unclear theological positioning

### **2. Audience Size & Engagement (20 points)**
**Weight: 20%** - Platform reach and audience quality over quantity

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **Exceptional (18-20 points)**: 25K+ highly engaged followers, strong email list, proven audience loyalty
- **Strong (14-17 points)**: 10K-25K engaged followers, growing email list, good engagement rates
- **Moderate (10-13 points)**: 3K-10K followers, some email presence, moderate engagement
- **Basic (6-9 points)**: 1K-3K followers, limited email, basic engagement
- **Emerging (0-5 points)**: <1K followers but high potential for growth

### **3. Content Quality & Consistency (20 points)**
**Weight: 20%** - Demonstrated ability to produce valuable, consistent content

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **Exceptional (18-20 points)**: Regular high-quality content, unique voice, proven impact on audience
- **Strong (14-17 points)**: Consistent content production, clear voice, good audience response
- **Moderate (10-13 points)**: Sporadic but quality content, developing voice
- **Basic (6-9 points)**: Occasional content, inconsistent quality
- **Weak (0-5 points)**: Minimal content production or poor quality

### **4. Revenue Generation Potential (15 points)**
**Weight: 15%** - Current or demonstrated potential for monetization

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **Exceptional (14-15 points)**: Currently generating $50K+ annually from content/speaking/consulting
- **Strong (11-13 points)**: $25K-$50K annual revenue or strong monetization trajectory
- **Moderate (8-10 points)**: $10K-$25K revenue or clear monetization potential
- **Basic (5-7 points)**: Some revenue generation or early monetization attempts
- **Emerging (0-4 points)**: No current revenue but potential for development

### **5. Platform Readiness & Tech Adoption (10 points)**
**Weight: 10%** - Comfort with technology and readiness for platform migration

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **High (9-10 points)**: Tech-savvy, uses multiple platforms, ready for advanced features
- **Medium (6-8 points)**: Comfortable with basic tech, uses some digital tools
- **Basic (3-5 points)**: Limited tech comfort but willing to learn
- **Low (0-2 points)**: Resistant to technology or requires significant support

### **6. Diversity & Representation (10 points)**
**Weight: 10%** - Contributes to diverse voices and perspectives in the movement

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **High (9-10 points)**: Brings underrepresented perspective (ethnicity, gender, geography, generation, context)
- **Medium (6-8 points)**: Some diversity factors, adds unique perspective
- **Basic (3-5 points)**: Limited diversity contribution but valuable voice
- **Standard (0-2 points)**: Fits typical movement leader profile

## ðŸŽ¯ **Evaluation Process**

### **Research Phase**
1. **Digital Presence Audit**
   - Website, social media, content platforms
   - Audience size and engagement metrics
   - Content quality and consistency assessment

2. **Content Analysis**
   - Published works, articles, speaking
   - Theological depth and movemental alignment
   - Audience impact and transformation stories

3. **Network Assessment**
   - Movement leader connections and endorsements
   - Collaborative history and partnership potential
   - Reputation within movemental circles

### **Reference Verification**
1. **Movement Leader Input**
   - Alan Hirsch assessment and recommendation
   - Brad Brisco evaluation and network feedback
   - Movement Leaders Collective perspective

2. **Peer References**
   - Other movemental leaders' experiences
   - Collaborative project outcomes
   - Professional reputation verification

3. **Audience Feedback**
   - Comment quality and engagement depth
   - Transformation stories and testimonials
   - Community building effectiveness

### **Decision Framework**
1. **Minimum Thresholds**
   - Movement Alignment: Minimum 15/25 points
   - Content Quality: Minimum 12/20 points
   - Overall Score: Minimum 55 points for consideration

2. **Diversity Considerations**
   - Prioritize underrepresented voices meeting threshold criteria
   - Balance established and emerging leaders
   - Geographic and generational representation

3. **Strategic Alignment**
   - Network growth potential
   - Movement multiplication capacity
   - Long-term partnership viability

## ðŸŒ **Strategic Objectives**

This evaluation system serves multiple strategic objectives:

1. **Quality Assurance**: Maintain high standards for movemental alignment and content quality
2. **Diversity Promotion**: Actively seek underrepresented voices in movement leadership
3. **Network Growth**: Build sustainable, collaborative relationships among movemental leaders
4. **Movement Multiplication**: Select leaders with capacity to catalyze broader movement impact
5. **Platform Success**: Ensure each movemental leader has strong potential for platform success and revenue generation

The ultimate goal is building a thriving ecosystem of movemental leaders who own their platforms, amplify their impact, and multiply movements globally while maintaining the theological depth and practical wisdom that characterizes authentic movemental leadership.

This evaluation system ensures we maintain high standards while actively promoting diverse voices and perspectives within the movemental ecosystem.
