Movemental DNA: A Leadership Development Framework

"After a time of decay comes the turning point… The movement is natural, arising spontaneously." The question before us is whether we will design for propagation or settle for preservation. The problem we must solve before we offer the solution is simple to say and hard to face: five strong strands won't carry a movement; you need the whole mDNA—integrated, operative, and embodied—if you want sustained transformation that multiplies at the edges. Not programs but paradigm; not maintenance but movement.

Learning Architecture

Adult-Learning "Chunking" Approach

Two-phase journey for adult learners:

Phase 1: Core (14 weeks). Seven modules, some with sub-modules. Weekly 90-minute cohort sessions plus 30 minutes pre-work and a small field experiment. Cadence every week: Explore (dialogue the content) → Evaluate (prioritize options, name blockages) → Employ (try a field practice) → Journal (guided reflection) to prevent discussion-only drift and anchor action-learning.

Phase 2: Residency (12 weeks). Learners lead in-context experiments, coached through huddles and peer feedback; focus on reproduction and network effects (starfish logic) rather than central performance.

Micro-chunking inside each module:

  • Week A: paradigm framing + light tool
  • Week B: field lab + peer coaching

Short, simple, transferable tools favored over complex curricula (movement scales on simplicity).

Assessment-by-Evidence

mDNA diagnostic at start and at each module pivot to identify the "weakest strand" and focus effort; all six strands must be cultivated—resist silver-bullet fixes.

Artifacts: network maps, coaching plans, micro-expression stories, and APEST team exercises.

How Alan's Books Anchor Each Module

  • The Forgotten Ways (TFW): movement paradigm, mDNA, APEST Culture, liminality-communitas; establishes why a movemental imaginary must supplant an institutional lens in the West.
  • The Forgotten Ways Handbook: group process and the Explore/Evaluate/Employ/Journal rhythm; practical scaffolding for action groups and diagnostics.
  • Permanent Revolution: movements vs. institutions; centralization's inverse relationship to movement capacity; links to innovation and organic systems; APEST leadership ecology.
  • On the Verge: process for implementing movemental change in complex organizations; a companion for leaders shepherding larger systems.
  • The Starfish-and-network references: decentralized, regenerative logic for micro-expressions and equipping hubs; weekly coaching huddles that scale in crisis (KC Underground as a living example).
  • Right Here, Right Now; The Shaping of Things to Come: missional-incarnational practice and innovation; cited within the ecosystem to inform contextualization and culture shift.
  • Metanoia resources: ongoing un/learning posture, learning-organization dynamics that sustain long-term change in a movemental ecology.

Full Course Outline

Intro Module: mDNA Overview and Diagnostics

Big Idea

Movements require all six mDNA elements functioning together; five won't get you to full-scale movement. Avoid silver-bullet thinking; embed Jesus-centered DNA at the core and strengthen your weakest strand first.

Movements vs. institutions: centralization of power and function inversely correlates to capacity for spread; design for diffusion, not control.

A living U.S. example of a decentralized, equipping ecosystem is the Kansas City Underground (weekly leader equipping, coaching webs, microchurch emergence).

Outcomes

  • Explain why all six mDNA elements are necessary for genuine movement and diagnose your weakest strand
  • Describe how decentralization increases movement potential and identify one centralized bottleneck

Content

  • The Forgotten Ways paradigm shift and updated terms: APEST Culture; Liminality-Communitas; imagination and metanoia as prerequisites for change
  • Movement mental model for post-Christendom complexity (avoid copy-paste from premodern settings)
  • Explore/Evaluate/Employ/Journal group process to ensure action-learning

Actions/Exercises

  • Baseline mDNA self-diagnostic; pick the weakest strand to target this term
  • Draw your ministry as a network and mark centralized "red zones" that limit spread
  • Watch the starfish/microchurch case and note one coaching habit to adopt (KC Underground)

Module 1: Jesus is Lord

Big Idea

Indicative precedes imperative: we begin not with tasks but with the confession that orders reality—Jesus is Lord. From that center flows sentness: missio Dei as our identity, not an elective.

Outcomes

  • Articulate Jesus is Lord as the theological anchor that drives mission-as-identity
  • Identify one "queen piece" you will set aside this term to rediscover the body's latent capacities (remove the queen to learn the game)

Content

  • Reframing church from vendor-of-services to movement of disciples (reversing 20:80 passive:active to 80:20 active)
  • Missio Dei shapes method: sent as the Son was sent—missional-incarnational, not merely attractional

Actions/Exercises

  • Confess-in-place: a small public act that names Jesus' Lordship in your everyday context; capture the conversation it opens
  • Make a "queen removal" list: identify one centralized dependence (e.g., Sunday stage, single communicator) you will suspend or slim for 6 weeks and what you will learn in its absence

Module 2: Discipleship and Disciple-Making

Big Idea

Movements multiply because discipleship is the core process, not a department; keep it simple, reproducible, and obedience-oriented.

Outcomes

  • Design a simple, reproducible disciple pathway that embeds accountability and obedience
  • Launch a discovery group or micro-discipling triad with two people in your actual context

Content

  • TFW's insistence that movemental foundations reverse passive-to-active norms and internalize DNA for organic multiplication
  • Ecosystem journeys and simple frameworks (e.g., missionary pathway, harvest-to-church) as exemplars from KC Underground

Actions/Exercises

  • 4-week discovery Scripture dialog with two seekers/new believers; weekly obedience step and check-in
  • Map "from harvest to church, church to harvest" steps in your locale; identify next faithful step this week

Module 3: Incarnational Mission

Sub-module A: Contextualization & Culture Shift

Big Idea

We embed and extend the gospel into real places and peoples; attractional gravity is strong, so we must re-code for everyday mission in post-Christendom contexts.

Outcomes

  • Name your focus people/place; practice presence-proclamation patterns that fit your context
  • Draft a contextualization memo that translates movement dynamics into your culture (no copy-paste from premodern models)

Content

  • Missional-incarnational impulse as a hyphenated both/and, anchored in Jesus-is-Lord centeredness
  • Anchors and inspirations: Right Here, Right Now; The Shaping of Things to Come (as referenced) for everyday mission innovation

Actions/Exercises

  • Three presence reps: bless, eat, listen in your place; record names and next steps
  • Culture shift workshop: identify "default attractional scripts" and replace them with missional-incarnational scripts for three common scenarios

Module 4: Liminality–Communitas

Big Idea

Risk, marginality, and disorientation are God's gym for forming communitas; liminality provokes learning and mission courage.

Outcomes

  • Normalize liminal experiences and design safe-to-fail edge exposures that build communitas
  • Practice prayerful dependence as you enter liminality

Content

  • TFW language update: Liminality–Communitas as an inseparable pair; leaders curate environments of risk and solidarity
  • Scriptural sending into lack (Luke 9) as a pattern of empowered dependence; definition of liminality

Actions/Exercises

  • Liminality rep: try a new setting or practice beyond comfort (e.g., spiritual conversation in a third place) with a teammate; debrief with the Explore/Evaluate/Employ/Journal loop

Module 5: APEST Culture

Big Idea

APEST is an ecosystem for movement health—culture, not hierarchy. Unity-in-diversity mobilizes the whole body.

Outcomes

  • Identify APEST wirings on your team; run a rotation where each function leads a week's focus
  • Design a lightweight rhythm that keeps APEST voices in play without ossifying into roles-only

Content

  • Shift from "apostolic environment" to "APEST Culture" and why it matters for movemental leadership and ministry
  • Re-coding organizations around discipleship, incarnational mission, full APEST ministry, organic structures, and risky communitas

Actions/Exercises

  • APEST sprint: five weeks where each function proposes one practice and a success signal
  • Peer feedback: where did our ecology show imbalance this month?

Module 6: Organic Systems

Sub-module A: Movement Process & Systems

Big Idea

Organic, chaordic processes let intelligence flow from the edges; design feedback loops that learn and adapt.

Outcomes

Build two concrete feedback loops; host one cross-micro learning exchange.

Content

Collective intelligence and unseen processes; design for many-to-many learning; innovation cycles and "greenhouse" spaces for idea testing.

Actions

Launch a monthly learning exchange; harvest front-line insights and change one system in response.

Sub-module B: Coaching & Decentralized Leadership

Big Idea

Staff become equippers; coaching webs beat central programs at scale.

Outcomes

Redeploy 20–40% of staff/lead time to coaching; run weekly leader huddles with breakout problem-solving.

Content

KC Underground's equipping hub pattern; every micro leader has a coach; huddles work at 100% capacity even in crisis because they run on coaching logic.

Actions

Write a 6-week coaching plan for one emerging leader and run two huddles.

Sub-module C: Micro-Expressions & Networks (Starfish Logic)

Big Idea

Regenerative networks of microchurches spread further than centralized spiders; remove the "queen" to rediscover the rest of the body.

Outcomes

Launch/strengthen one micro-expression; set its DNA for multiplication.

Content

Reimagine church as decentralized micro expressions; starfish vs. spider; "remove the queen" analogy.

Actions

Host a meal-sized gathering with Word–table–witness; capture next steps and apprentice identification.

Sub-module D: Simple, Transferable Tools

Big Idea

Movement scales on simplicity; use practices that embed quickly and spread easily.

Outcomes

Adopt one habit set and one leadership rhythm; track adherence and fruit.

Content

BELLS/HUDDLE as examples of training tools; engage narrative, data, and theory to reach diverse learners.

Actions

Six-week BELLS sprint with weekly measurement; one HUDDLE launched for your micro team.

Overall Course Learning Outcomes

Graduating Learners Will Be Able To:

  1. Explain and assess all six mDNA elements; identify and strengthen their weakest strand via action-learning sprints (Explore/Evaluate/Employ/Journal)
  2. Confess and embody "Jesus is Lord" as the center that generates missional-incarnational identity and practice; reverse the passive/active ratio in their context toward 80% active engagement
  3. Design and run simple, reproducible disciple-making pathways that create micro-expressions and multiply at the edges; avoid premodern copy-paste and translate wisely into Western complexity
  4. Cultivate APEST culture in teams; lead through liminality toward communitas with courage and prayerful dependence
  5. Build organic systems that decentralize power, leverage coaching webs, and increase movement capacity; demonstrate understanding that centralization constrains spread
  6. Learn as a "movemental learning organization," practicing ongoing metanoia and iteration beyond the course window

Milestones and Assessment

Week 2: Baseline mDNA diagnostic complete; weakest strand identified; one bottleneck named
Week 4: First disciple-making micro-practice launched; evidence posted (names, next steps)
Week 6: Contextualization memo submitted; three missional-incarnational scripts rewritten for your locale
Week 8: Liminality rep completed and debriefed; communitas practices named
Week 10: APEST sprint completed with team reflections; imbalance identified and addressed
Week 12–14: Coaching huddles running; one micro-expression launched or strengthened; network map updated; starfish logic demonstrated in practice
Residency Finish: Re-diagnostic shows growth across all six strands; at least one reproduction outcome (apprentice identified or micro multiplied)

A Final Reframing

We will design the learning to act our way into a new way of thinking. The starfish beats the spider; hubs equip while edges extend; all six strands or it won't sing. Jesus is Lord—there's our center. From there, everything moves outward. The only way to fulfill a global commission is to become a movement; moving from one region to the next requires movement—so we will build for movement from the ground up.
