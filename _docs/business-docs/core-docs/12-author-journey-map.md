# Author Journey Map
## Entry Points, Assessments, Publishing, Formation, Monetization, Growth Pathways

**Purpose**: Align expectations for movemental leaders joining Movemental. Clear articulation of the complete journey from initial interest through sustainable digital publishing success.

**Audience**: Authors, internal support, strategic partners

**Status**: Foundational Document - MVP-Complete / Pre-Scale

---

## Overview: The Complete Author Journey

The Movemental author journey is designed to transform movemental leaders from initial inquiry to sustainable digital publishers who own their platform, amplify their impact, and generate sustainable revenue. This journey is structured, supported, and designed for long-term success—not quick wins.

**The Five-Phase Journey**:
1. **Entry & Assessment** - Qualification, invitation, and initial discovery
2. **Onboarding & Setup** - Platform deployment, voice capture, content strategy
3. **Content Launch & Formation** - First publications, rhythm establishment, audience building
4. **Monetization & Growth** - Revenue launch, audience expansion, network integration
5. **Scale & Amplification** - Sustainable practice, network leadership, legacy building

---

## Phase 1: Entry & Assessment

### Entry Points

**Primary Entry Paths**:

1. **Direct Invitation** (Primary Path)
   - Invitation from existing Movemental member
   - Referral through Movement Leaders Collective, 100Movements, or partner networks
   - Recognition through movemental leadership or content excellence
   - **Why**: Network vouching ensures quality and alignment

2. **Application Process** (Secondary Path)
   - Application form with basic qualification questions
   - Assessment consultation call
   - Evaluation against Movemental qualification criteria
   - **Why**: Allows for organic discovery of qualified leaders

3. **Cohort/Organizational Pathways** (Partnership Path)
   - Entry through seminary, publisher, or movement network partnership
   - Cohort-based onboarding for organizations training movement leaders
   - Coordinated launch with other cohort members
   - **Why**: Enables strategic expansion through institutional partnerships

### Assessment Process

**Qualification Criteria**: Movemental uses a 100-point evaluation rubric across six categories:

1. **Movement Alignment** (25 points)
   - Clear missional/incarnational theology and practice
   - Commitment to movemental values (scenius, collaboration, formation > growth)
   - Embodied practice over theory
   - Movement multiplication track record

2. **Content Depth** (20 points)
   - Published work demonstrating theological and practical depth
   - Substantive contribution to movemental thought or practice
   - Unique expertise and perspective
   - Archive of content available for repurposing

3. **Audience Development** (20 points)
   - Existing audience and platform (newsletter, social media, website)
   - Engaged community, not just large numbers
   - Audience quality aligned with movemental leaders
   - Growth potential and engagement patterns

4. **Revenue Potential** (15 points)
   - Ability to generate platform earnings through content, courses, speaking
   - Realistic revenue goals aligned with Movemental economics
   - Willingness to invest in digital publishing

5. **Network Value** (10 points)
   - Potential for network collaboration and cross-pollination
   - Connections to other movemental leaders
   - Willingness to participate in scenius model

6. **Platform Readiness** (10 points)
   - Capacity to commit to regular publishing (minimum monthly)
   - Willingness to be coached and learn
   - Understanding that this is a long game, not quick wins
   - Technical comfort level or willingness to learn

**Qualification Thresholds**:
- **90-100 points (Tier 1)**: Full Movemental access, $1,000-$5,000 pricing (tiered by network maturity), 12-week intensive onboarding
- **75-89 points (Tier 2)**: Standard onboarding, $2,000-$3,000 pricing, 8-week onboarding
- **60-74 points (Tier 3)**: Light-touch onboarding, $3,000-$4,000 pricing, group cohort model
- **Below 60 points**: Not yet ready, potential for future qualification

**Red Flags (Not Yet Ready)**:
- No existing audience (need to build that first)
- No content creation capacity (too busy with other priorities)
- Expecting magic ("I'll just set up a site and people will find me")
- Not willing to learn or be coached
- Misaligned values (just wanting a platform, not interested in movement)

**The Assessment Process**:
1. **Initial Inquiry** - Application form or referral
2. **Discovery Consultation** (30-60 minutes) - Understanding their calling, content, audience, goals
3. **Evaluation** - Scoring against 100-point rubric, reviewing existing content and platform
4. **Qualification Decision** - Tier assignment and pricing determination
5. **Invitation/Response** - Clear communication of qualification status and next steps

## Phase 2: Onboarding & Setup (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1: Vision & Voice (Kickoff)

**Goal**: Establish relationship, clarify vision, capture voice

**Deliverables**:
- Platform architecture and feature setup
- Voice and style guide documentation
- Content strategy framework
- Initial editorial calendar

**Key Activities**:
- **Discovery Conversation** (90 minutes): Movemental calling, vision, goals, audience, content archive
- **Voice Capture**: Analysis of existing content, identification of unique voice markers, style preferences
- **Platform Tour**: Customized dashboard walkthrough, feature overview, AI tool demonstration
- **Content Strategy**: First 3 articles identified, publishing cadence established, quick wins identified
- **Accountability Setup**: Weekly check-ins scheduled, Slack channel created, curriculum access granted

**Success Marker**: Clear vision, captured voice, platform ready, first article draft assigned

### Week 2: First Publication

**Goal**: Get first article published, build confidence

**Deliverables**:
- First published article
- Publishing workflow mastery
- Basic SEO optimization

**Key Activities**:
- **Article Workshop** (60 minutes): Review draft, live editing, headline refinement, SEO optimization
- **Publishing Process**: Workflow walkthrough, publish together (ceremonial moment)
- **Promotion Strategy**: Email to existing list, social announcement, network forum introduction
- **Next Steps**: Article 2 topic assigned, Module 2-3 curriculum assigned

**Success Marker**: First article live and promoted, confidence in publishing process

### Week 3: Rhythm & Repurposing

**Goal**: Establish sustainable content creation rhythm

**Deliverables**:
- Repurposed content from existing archive
- Editorial calendar for next 8 weeks
- AI repurposing workflow mastery

**Key Activities**:
- **Progress Review**: Analytics from first article, celebrate wins, address concerns
- **Repurposing Workshop**: Sermon → article transformation, AI tool mastery, 70% repurposing strategy
- **Editorial Calendar**: Next 8 weeks mapped, mix of repurposed and original content, publishing cadence locked
- **Content Strategy Refinement**: Based on early feedback and analytics

**Success Marker**: Sustainable content rhythm established, repurposing workflow mastered

### Week 4: Audience Building

**Goal**: Strategy for growing free subscriber base

**Deliverables**:
- Migration plan from existing platforms
- Email list import and segmentation
- Social promotion strategy
- Network integration

**Key Activities**:
- **Migration Plan**: Email to existing list, social announcement, personal invitations to key influencers
- **Audience Strategy**: SEO optimization, network discovery, content promotion framework
- **Network Integration**: Introduction to network members, cross-referencing opportunities, collaboration planning
- **Growth Metrics**: Subscriber goals, engagement targets, 6-month roadmap

**Success Marker**: Audience migration complete, growth strategy in place, network connections established

## What Authors Need to Know

**Commitment Required**:
- **Time**: 5-10 hours/week for content creation and platform management
- **Publishing**: Minimum monthly (ideally weekly or bi-weekly)
- **Learning**: 16-week curriculum, ongoing skill development
- **Network Participation**: Cross-referencing, collaboration, community engagement

**What Authors Get**:
- Complete digital publishing platform (fully owned)
- AI-powered content creation (tailored to voice and theology)
- Network amplification (28x-500x reach improvement)
- Strategic coaching and support
- 90% revenue retention (vs. 10-15% on other platforms)

**Realistic Expectations**:
- **Year 1**: Foundation building, audience growth, revenue launch
- **Year 2**: Growth, optimization, network leadership
- **Year 3+**: Scale, legacy, movement multiplication

**This is a long game, not quick wins**. Success requires consistent effort, strategic thinking, and commitment to movemental values. But the infrastructure, support, and network make it achievable for leaders who commit to the journey.
