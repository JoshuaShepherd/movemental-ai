# Movemental Author Maturity Model & Qualification
## Who We're Looking For and How We Decide

**Purpose**: Clear, accessible explanation of Movemental's maturity model and qualification process. Helps potential authors understand what makes someone a good fit, why gates exist, and how admittance works.

**Audience**: Potential authors, movemental leaders, strategic partners

**Status**: Foundational Document - MVP-Complete / Pre-Scale

## The Core Question: What Makes Someone a "Movemental Author"?

### The Simple Answer

**A movemental author is someone whose life, work, and communication catalyze visible, verifiable movement—transformation that multiplies beyond their direct oversight.**

This is not about:
- Platform size or follower count
- Topic choice (writing about movement vs. other subjects)
- Recognition or celebrity status
- Traditional credentials alone

This is about:
- **Visible movement**: Do people actually follow, replicate, or transform because of your work?
- **Multiplication**: Does your work spread beyond you, creating second-generation impact?
- **Verifiable credibility**: Can you point to real work, real followers, real transformation?
- **Embodied integration**: Is there a seamless connection between who you are, how you lead, and what you create?

### The Paradox: 80% Don't Write About Movement

Most movemental authors don't write directly about movement theory or missional practice. They write about:
- Parenting and family formation
- Counseling and mental health
- Medicine and healthcare
- Youth ministry and discipleship
- Business and leadership
- Education and learning

**Their movemental capacity comes not from their topic, but from how their content provokes transformation, how their leadership multiplies followers, and how their integration creates reproducible patterns.**

## The Three-Layer Maturity Model

Movemental uses a three-layer maturity model to assess authors. Think of it as building a house: you need a solid foundation before you can build the walls and roof.

### Layer 1: mDNA Foundation (The Foundation)

**What It Is**: The essential foundation of Jesus-shaped movement maturity. This measures whether you have mature discipleship, missional presence, organic systems, and all the foundational elements of movemental DNA.

**The Gate**: You must be **mDNA Level 3+ (Integrating)** to even unlock the movemental author assessment.

**Why This Gate Exists**: You cannot authentically lead movement or create movemental content without first embodying movemental DNA. Movemental authorship requires mature discipleship, missional presence, organic systems, and all mDNA elements working together. You cannot be a mature movemental author without a mature mDNA foundation.

**What This Means in Practice**:
- You have a track record of making disciples who make disciples
- Your faith is integrated into all of life, not compartmentalized
- You understand and practice missional presence
- You create reproducible patterns, not just individual impact
- You demonstrate mature Christian character and integrity

**The Assessment**: Uses the existing mDNA Individual Maturity Assessment (72 questions, 6 elements).

### Layer 2: Movemental Leadership (The Walls)

**What It Is**: The capacity to catalyze visible movement through your life, work, and communication. This measures whether people actually follow, replicate, or transform because of your work.

**The Gate**: You must be **Movemental Leadership Level 2+ (Awakening)** to be considered a movemental author at all.

**Why This Gate Exists**: Some visible movement is required. Theoretical knowledge or platform size alone doesn't qualify—there must be observable followers or replication. You cannot be a movemental author if no one is actually being transformed or replicating your approach.

**What This Means in Practice**:
- **Visible Followers**: Can you name 1-2 people who are actually following or adopting your approach? (Level 2)
- **Reproducible Patterns**: Are others able to adopt and adapt your methods without you?
- **Generational Impact**: Do you have second-generation fruit (your followers have followers)?
- **Contextual Influence**: Does your work shape the culture or context you're in?
- **Non-Anxious Presence**: Does movement continue without your direct control?

**The Levels**:
- **Level 1 (Dormant)**: No visible movement; work exists in isolation
- **Level 2 (Awakening)**: First followers emerging; sporadic influence (minimum gate)
- **Level 3 (Integrating)**: Consistent pattern of people being shaped by your work
- **Level 4 (Reproducing)**: Second-generation fruit; movement multiplies beyond direct oversight
- **Level 5 (Movemental)**: Contagious, culture-shaping, resilient influence

**The Assessment**: 12 questions measuring visible followers, reproducible patterns, generational impact, and contextual influence.

### Layer 3: Content Credibility & Embodied Integration (The Roof)

**What It Is**: Having something worth saying that's verifiable through past work, and seamless integration of mDNA foundation, movemental leadership, and content credibility.

**Content Credibility** measures:
- **Verifiable Track Record**: Can you point to specific work that demonstrates what you write about?
- **Domain Authority**: Are you recognized as having expertise in your field/context?
- **Story-Rich Content**: Does your content emerge from lived experience, not just ideas?
- **Accessible Communication**: Can you translate complex ideas for diverse audiences?
- **Integrity Alignment**: Does your content match your life?

**Embodied Integration** measures:
- **No Compartmentalization**: Are faith, leadership, and content integrated, not separate?
- **Authentic Voice**: Does your content reflect who you are, not who you think you should be?
- **Movemental Content**: Does your content provoke transformation, not just information?
- **Sustainable Practice**: Can you maintain integrity under pressure and visibility?
- **Generative Capacity**: Does your integration produce new insights, not just repetition?

**The Assessment**: 24 questions (12 for Content Credibility, 12 for Embodied Integration).

## How the Gates Work

### Gate 1: mDNA Foundation (Non-Negotiable)

**The Requirement**: Overall mDNA maturity must be **Level 3+ (Integrating)** to unlock the movemental author assessment.

**Why This Exists**: Movemental authorship is a specialized expression of mDNA, not a separate path. You cannot authentically lead movement or create movemental content without first embodying movemental DNA.

**What Happens if You Don't Meet This Gate**:
- The movemental author assessment is locked
- You cannot proceed until mDNA Level 3+ is achieved
- You receive formation recommendations to reach Level 3
- You can retake the assessment as you develop

**How to Meet This Gate**:
- Complete the mDNA Individual Maturity Assessment
- Focus on foundational discipleship and movemental practice
- Develop mature integration of faith and life
- Create reproducible patterns in your context
- Seek formation support and coaching

### Gate 2: Movemental Leadership (Minimum Threshold)

**The Requirement**: Movemental Leadership must be **Level 2+ (Awakening)** to be considered a movemental author at all.

**Why This Exists**: Some visible movement is required. Theoretical knowledge or platform size alone doesn't qualify—there must be observable followers or replication. You cannot be a movemental author if no one is actually being transformed or replicating your approach.

**What Happens if You Don't Meet This Gate**:
- You cannot be classified as a "movemental author"
- You still receive assessment results and recommendations
- You can work toward Level 2 by developing visible followers
- You can retake the assessment as movement develops

**How to Meet This Gate**:
- Develop at least 1-2 visible followers who are adopting your approach
- Create reproducible patterns that others can adopt
- Focus on transformation and multiplication, not just content creation
- Build second-generation fruit (your followers have followers)
- Develop non-anxious leadership that doesn't depend on control

### The Assessment Flow

**Step 1: mDNA Assessment (Prerequisite)**
- Complete mDNA Individual Maturity Assessment (72 questions)
- Receive mDNA maturity level (1-5)
- **Gate Check**: If mDNA Level 3+, proceed to Step 2. If not, assessment locked.

**Step 2: Movemental Author Assessment**
- Complete Movemental Author Assessment (36 questions)
- Three elements assessed:
  - Movemental Leadership (12 questions)
  - Content Credibility (12 questions)
  - Embodied Integration (12 questions)
- Receive element scores and overall maturity level

**Step 3: Gate Check**
- **Movemental Leadership Gate**: If Level 2+, classified as movemental author. If not, receive recommendations.
- Overall maturity level calculated using geometric mean (ensures all elements must be strong)

**Step 4: Results and Recommendations**
- Overall movemental author maturity level (1-5)
- Element scores breakdown
- Limiting factor identified
- Integration with mDNA results
- Personalized formation recommendations

## How Admittance Works

### The Qualification Process

**1. Initial Inquiry**
- Application form or referral
- Basic qualification questions
- Understanding of calling, content, audience, goals

**2. Discovery Consultation** (30-60 minutes)
- Conversation about their work and context
- Understanding their movemental alignment
- Assessing their content and audience
- Evaluating their readiness for partnership

**3. Assessment**
- mDNA Individual Maturity Assessment (if not already completed)
- Movemental Author Assessment (if mDNA Level 3+)
- Scoring against maturity model criteria
- Review of existing content and platform

**4. Qualification Decision**
- Tier assignment based on maturity levels
- Pricing determination (tiered by network maturity)
- Onboarding path recommendation
- Clear communication of qualification status

**5. Invitation/Response**
- For qualified candidates: Invitation to join Movemental
- For not-yet-qualified: Formation recommendations and pathways
- Relationship preservation and future opportunities

### Qualification Thresholds

**Tier 1: Fully Qualified** (mDNA Level 3+, Movemental Leadership Level 3+, Overall Level 3+)
- Full Movemental access
- $1,000-$5,000 pricing (tiered by network maturity)
- 12-week intensive onboarding
- Complete network integration

**Tier 2: Developing** (mDNA Level 3+, Movemental Leadership Level 2-3, Overall Level 2-3)
- Standard Movemental access
- $2,000-$3,000 pricing
- 8-week onboarding
- Network integration with development support

**Tier 3: Emerging** (mDNA Level 3+, Movemental Leadership Level 2, Overall Level 2)
- Light-touch onboarding
- $3,000-$4,000 pricing
- Group cohort model
- Network integration with mentorship

**Not Yet Ready** (mDNA Level 2 or below, or Movemental Leadership Level 1)
- Formation recommendations provided
- Pathways to qualification outlined
- Potential for future qualification
- Relationship maintained for future opportunities

### The 100-Point Rubric Integration

Movemental also uses a 100-point qualification rubric for initial screening, which complements the maturity model assessment:

**1. Movement Alignment** (25 points)
- Clear missional/incarnational theology and practice
- Commitment to movemental values (scenius, collaboration, formation > growth)
- Embodied practice over theory
- Movement multiplication track record

**2. Content Depth** (20 points)
- Published work demonstrating theological and practical depth
- Substantive contribution to movemental thought or practice
- Unique expertise and perspective
- Archive of content available for repurposing

**3. Audience Development** (20 points)
- Existing audience and platform (newsletter, social media, website)
- Engaged community, not just large numbers
- Audience quality aligned with movemental leaders
- Growth potential and engagement patterns

**4. Revenue Potential** (15 points)
- Ability to generate platform earnings through content, courses, speaking
- Realistic revenue goals aligned with Movemental economics
- Willingness to invest in digital publishing

**5. Network Value** (10 points)
- Potential for network collaboration and cross-pollination
- Connections to other movemental leaders
- Willingness to participate in scenius model

**6. Platform Readiness** (10 points)
- Capacity to commit to regular publishing (minimum monthly)
- Willingness to be coached and learn
- Understanding that this is a long game, not quick wins
- Technical comfort level or willingness to learn

**The Integration**: The rubric provides initial screening, while the maturity model provides deeper assessment of movemental maturity. Both are used together to make qualification decisions.

## Common Questions

### "I write about [domain], not movement. Can I still qualify?"

**Yes.** 80% of movemental authors don't write directly about movement. They write about parenting, counseling, medicine, youth ministry, or any domain where they have expertise. What matters is whether your content provokes transformation, your leadership multiplies followers, and your integration creates reproducible patterns—not whether you use movemental vocabulary.

### "I have a large platform but no visible movement. Can I qualify?"

**Not yet.** Platform size alone doesn't qualify. You need visible movement—people who are actually following, replicating, or transforming because of your work. A stay-at-home mom with 3 followers who are transforming their families is more movemental than an influencer with 100K passive followers. Focus on developing visible movement, then reassess.

### "I have visible movement but my mDNA foundation isn't mature. Can I qualify?"

**Not yet.** The mDNA foundation is non-negotiable. You cannot be a mature movemental author without mature mDNA foundation. Movemental authorship is a specialized expression of mDNA, not a separate path. Focus on foundational formation, develop mDNA maturity, then reassess.

### "How long does it take to qualify?"

**It depends on where you are in your journey.** If you already have mature mDNA foundation (Level 3+) and visible movement (Level 2+), qualification can happen quickly. If you're still developing, it may take months or years of formation. The assessment helps identify where you are and provides recommendations for development.

### "What if I don't qualify? Am I excluded forever?"

**No.** Qualification isn't permanent exclusion. Focus on the areas that need development:
- If mDNA foundation needs work: Focus on foundational formation
- If visible movement needs work: Develop reproducible patterns and followers
- If content credibility needs work: Ground content in lived experience
- Reassess when you're ready

### "What's the difference between the maturity model and the 100-point rubric?"

**The maturity model** provides deep assessment of movemental maturity (mDNA foundation, movemental leadership, content credibility, embodied integration). **The 100-point rubric** provides initial screening (movement alignment, content depth, audience, revenue potential, network value, platform readiness). Both are used together—the rubric for initial screening, the maturity model for deeper assessment.
