## Organizational Structure

### Legal Structure

**Entity Type**: Movemental LLC, Delaware Limited Liability Company
- **Formation**: Delaware LLC providing operational flexibility and tax efficiency
- **Operating Agreement**: Member-managed structure with defined roles and responsibilities
- **Fiduciary Standards**: Clear accountability and conflict of interest management
- **Decision Making**: Majority vote requirement for major decisions

### Management Structure

**Member-Managed LLC**:
- **Managing Member**: Josh Shepherd with operational control and day-to-day decision authority
- **Strategic Advisory**: Alan Hirsch, Brad Brisco, Rich Robinson providing guidance and network access
- **Quarterly Reviews**: Regular member meetings for strategic planning and performance assessment
- **Annual Planning**: Comprehensive strategic planning and goal setting process

## Decision Rights

### Operational Decisions (Josh Shepherd Authority)

**Day-to-Day Operations**:
- Platform development priorities and implementation
- Technical architecture and infrastructure decisions
- Hiring and team development decisions
- Customer acquisition and platform deployment
- Financial management and operational efficiency
- Client services and support operations

**Implementation**:
- Full authority for operational decisions
- No approval required for day-to-day operations
- Strategic consultation with advisory members as needed
- Quarterly reporting on operational decisions and outcomes

### Strategic Decisions (Majority Vote Required)

**Major Decisions Requiring Member Approval**:
- Sale of Company or substantially all assets
- Merger or acquisition transactions
- Changes to ownership structure
- Major partnership agreements and equity allocations
- Significant financial investments or expenditures over $25,000
- Market expansion into new verticals or geographic regions
- Exit strategy planning and major corporate transactions
- Amendment of Operating Agreement

**Implementation**:
- Majority vote of members required (70% + 30% = 100%)
- Strategic discussions and proposals at quarterly meetings
- Written documentation of major decisions
- Clear communication of decision rationale

### Advisory Input (All Members)

**Areas for Strategic Advisory Input**:
- Product development priorities and feature roadmap
- Market expansion opportunities and strategic partnerships
- Network development and relationship cultivation
- Quality assurance and client success initiatives
- Long-term vision and strategic direction
- Brand positioning and messaging

**Implementation**:
- Quarterly advisory meetings for strategic input
- Ongoing communication channels for advisory input
- Collaborative strategic planning process
- Advisory input informs but doesn't bind operational decisions

## Centralized vs. Distributed

### Centralized Functions

**Platform & Technology** (Centralized):
- Platform architecture and development
- AI agent development and optimization
- Technical infrastructure and hosting
- Security and data protection
- Platform maintenance and updates

**Business Operations** (Centralized):
- Financial management and accounting
- Client acquisition and onboarding
- Strategic planning and vision
- Quality assurance and standards
- Legal and compliance

**Network Infrastructure** (Centralized):
- Network platform and integration
- Cross-author discovery and amplification
- Network analytics and insights
- Network governance and curation
- Quality standards and qualification

### Distributed Functions

**Content Creation** (Distributed):
- Individual authors create their own content
- Authors control their content strategy and publishing
- Authors manage their audience and engagement
- Authors maintain their voice and theology

**Revenue Generation** (Distributed):
- Authors control their pricing and offerings
- Authors manage their monetization strategies
- Authors retain 90% of revenue
- Authors make independent revenue decisions

**Audience Engagement** (Distributed):
- Authors engage directly with their audiences
- Authors control their community and relationships
- Authors manage their email lists and subscribers
- Authors maintain direct audience relationships

**Network Participation** (Distributed):
- Authors choose network participation level
- Authors control cross-referencing and collaboration
- Authors manage their network relationships
- Authors participate in scenius model voluntarily

## Key Operating Principles

### Quality Over Quantity

**Principle**: We prioritize quality and user success over rapid growth.

**Implementation**:
- Rigorous qualification process
- High-touch onboarding and support
- Quality curation and standards
- Sustainable growth model

### Network-Driven Growth

**Principle**: Growth comes through network relationships and referrals, not marketing.

**Implementation**:
- Invitation-only access (primary path)
- Network vouching and referrals
- Strategic partnerships and relationships
- Organic, relationship-based expansion

### Founder-Led Operations

**Principle**: Founder leadership ensures vision alignment and quality standards.

**Implementation**:
- Founder control over operations and decisions
- Advisory input and strategic guidance
- Founder-led client relationships (early phase)
- Strategic delegation as team scales

### Scenius Over Solo

**Principle**: Collaborative genius and network participation create value.

**Implementation**:
- Network integration and collaboration
- Cross-author amplification and discovery
- Shared resources and intelligence
- Mutual elevation over individual competition
