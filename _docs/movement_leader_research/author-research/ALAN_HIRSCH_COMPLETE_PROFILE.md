## 1. Executive Summary

Alan Hirsch is a missiologist, author, and movement pioneer recognized as one of the leading voices in the missional church movement. Born and raised in apartheid South Africa, he immigrated to Australia in 1983 and has since become a key figure in shaping contemporary missional theology and practice.

**Key Accomplishments:**
- Founder of Forge Mission Training Network
- Co-founder of 100Movements
- Author of 15+ influential books on missional church and leadership
- Pioneer of the mDNA (missional DNA) framework
- Developer of the APEST (Five-fold ministry) framework
- Led South Melbourne Restoration Community for 15 years

**Current Status**: Active author, speaker, and movement catalyst focusing on missional discipleship and church transformation.

### Jewish Heritage
Alan Hirsch grew up in a Jewish family in apartheid South Africa. While he describes himself as "not an observant Jew by any stretch of the imagination," his Jewish heritage and background have significantly influenced his perspective and theological thinking.

**Source**: *Untamed* (00-intro.mdx): "I (Alan) grew up in a Jewish family and was born and raised in apartheid South Africa until age twenty-two."

### South African Context
- Born and raised in South Africa during apartheid
- Lived there until age 22
- Experienced the political and social dynamics of apartheid firsthand
- Developed early awareness of social justice issues

### Early Political Awareness
At age 13, Alan became politically active against apartheid. He describes having "a seemingly natural affinity with black people" and deciding early on that apartheid was "profoundly wrong and morally reprehensible."

**Source**: *Untamed* (00-intro.mdx): "Having a seemingly natural affinity with black people, I became politically active against apartheid as early as age thirteen. Nothing too hotheaded, but I had already decided that apartheid was profoundly wrong and morally reprehensible."

### Early Spiritual Awareness
Even as a young boy, Alan experienced moments of ecstatic worship and never doubted God's existence. He had what he describes as "an illicit fascination with Jesus" as a young Jewish teenager.

**Source**: *Untamed* (00-intro.mdx): "I can honestly say I don't remember a time when I ever doubted God's existence, and I can remember moments of ecstatic worship, even as a very young boy, where God seemed more real to me than my own self. My journey to explicit Christian faith started early, with what seemed to a young Jewish teenager as an illicit fascination with Jesus."

## 4. Conversion and Early Ministry

### Military Service
- Served mandatory two-year military service in South Africa
- Timeline: Approximately age 20-22 (before immigrating to Australia)
- During military service: first introduced to drugs, then to Jesus

### Conversion Experience
Alan met his first "real Christian" at about age 20, when one of his "drug buddies" was born again and began sharing the gospel with him. His conversion was a profound, overwhelming encounter with God.

**Key Quote**: "For me, coming to Jesus was coming home in every way. I had an overwhelming encounter with God and was totally and absolutely won over. It was the defining moment of my life: I gave my life fully to Jesus and I have never looked back."

**Source**: *Untamed* (00-intro.mdx): "As far as I can recall, I had never met a real Christian until I was about twenty, when one of my drug buddies was born again and began to share the gospel with me."

## 5. Immigration and Australian Ministry

### Immigration to Australia
- **Year**: 1983
- **Circumstances**: Immigrated with his family
- **Timeline**: Shortly after meeting his wife/partner

### Marriage
- Married his wife and ministry partner (Debra/Deb Hirsch)
- Timeline: Met shortly before immigrating to Australia in 1983

### South Melbourne Restoration Community

#### Overview
South Melbourne Restoration Community (affectionately called "South") was the church where Alan and Debra began their first "official" ministry.

#### Community Description
- Located in inner-city Melbourne, Australia
- Made up largely of people "on the fringes of society"
- Included: homosexuals, prostitutes, schizophrenics, drug addicts, and other marginalized individuals
- Described as: "the first port of call for those on their way into the church and the last port of call for those on their way out"

**Source**: *Untamed* (00-intro.mdx): "South Melbourne Restoration Community, the church where we began our first 'official' ministry (affectionately called South), was located in an inner-city context and made up largely of people who, in one way or another, were on the fringes of society."

#### Leadership Tenure
- Led the community for **fifteen years**
- Timeline: Approximately 1983-1998 (needs verification)

**Source**: *Untamed* (00-intro.mdx): "We led this wonderful community for fifteen years. We lived, loved, cried, and laughed with this bunch of people; and they, more than most, helped define who we are as people."

#### Ministry Activities
- Planted other communities of faith
- Initiated various outreach agencies and missional orders
- Alan involved in denominational leadership
- Debra led ministry to gays, lesbians, and transsexuals
- Debra served the poor through work with the Salvation Army

**Source**: *Untamed* (00-intro.mdx): "Throughout our time at South, we were privileged to be able to plant other communities of faith, and help in initiating various outreach agencies and missional orders. On top of this, Al was also involved in leadership of our denomination and Deb led a ministry to gays, lesbians, and transsexuals, and also served the poor through her work with the Salvation Army."

### Ministry Philosophy Development
The experience at South Melbourne deeply shaped Alan's understanding of:
- Missional church
- Discipleship
- Inclusion and ministry to marginalized communities
- The relationship between failure and learning
- The importance of authentic Christian community

## 10. Key Concepts and Contributions

### mDNA Framework (Missional DNA)
The six elements of apostolic genius that form the missional DNA of movements:
1. Jesus is Lord
2. Disciple Making
3. Missional-Incarnational Impulse
4. Apostolic Environment
5. Organic Systems
6. Liminality-Communitas

**Primary Source**: *The Forgotten Ways*

### APEST Framework (Five-fold Ministry)
Based on Ephesians 4:11, the five-fold ministry gifts:
- **A**postolic
- **P**rophetic
- **E**vangelistic
- **S**hepherding
- **T**eaching

**Primary Sources**: *5Q*, *The Permanent Revolution*

### Incarnational Mission
The concept that mission should follow the pattern of the incarnation - entering into culture rather than expecting culture to come to the church.

**Primary Source**: *The Shaping of Things to Come*

### Metanoia (Transformation)
Understanding repentance as fundamental transformation that affects individuals, churches, and organizations from the inside out.

**Primary Source**: *Metanoia*

### Other Key Contributions
- Movemental thinking vs. institutional thinking
- Organic systems over organizational structures
- Liminality and communitas
- Apostolic imagination
- Disciple-making movements
