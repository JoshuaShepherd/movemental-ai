## Executive Summary

Brad Brisco's audience consists primarily of church planters, pastors, and missional practitioners who are seeking sustainable, practical models for missional church life, covocational ministry, and neighborhood-based mission. The audience spans from new church planters exploring covocational models to established church leaders seeking missional transition, with strong concentration in Southern Baptist and evangelical church planting networks. This profile maps the Total Addressable Market (TAM), identifies key audience segments, documents content consumption patterns, and reveals unmet needs to inform content strategy and distribution channels.

**Key Outcomes:**
- Complete TAM analysis with market size estimates
- Detailed audience personas for each segment
- Content consumption preferences by segment
- Distribution channel mapping
- Unmet needs and content gap identification

## Total Addressable Market (TAM) Analysis

### Market Size Estimates

**Primary Market**: Church planters, pastors, and missional practitioners in North American contexts (US, Canada), with emphasis on Southern Baptist networks and evangelical church planting movements

**Conservative Estimates:**
- **Church Planters** (including covocational/bivocational): 50,000-100,000 in North America
- **Pastors Seeking Missional Transition**: 100,000-200,000 in North America
- **Denominational/Organizational Leaders**: 10,000-20,000 in North America
- **Lay Leaders & Active Disciples**: 500,000-1,000,000 in North America
- **Training Program Participants** (Forge, NAMB programs): 5,000-10,000 annually

**Total Addressable Market**: 665,000-1,330,000 individuals in North America

**Active Engaged Audience** (those actively seeking missional transition/covocational planting content):
- **Estimated**: 100,000-200,000 individuals
- **Characteristics**: Regular content consumption, curriculum users, training program participants, book purchasers

### Market Growth Trends

**Growth Indicators:**
- Increasing interest in covocational/bivocational church planting models (economic and cultural shifts)
- Growing need for existing church transition strategies (declining traditional church models)
- Rising interest in neighborhood-based mission (local, relational focus)
- Expanding online learning and digital content consumption
- Growing dissatisfaction with unsustainable church planting models

**Market Maturity**: Growing (covocational planting and missional transition have gained momentum over past 10 years, but digital access and practical resources create expanding opportunities)

**Market Positioning**: Bridge position between institutional church structures (NAMB/Send Network) and grassroots missional movements (Forge), serving both established denominational leaders and new church planters

## Primary Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Covocational/Bivocational Church Planters (30% of active audience)

**Demographics:**
- **Age**: 30-50 (primary: 35-45)
- **Education**: College-educated, many with M.Div. or theological training
- **Location**: North America (US, Canada primary)
- **Role**: Starting new churches while maintaining marketplace vocations
- **Experience**: 0-10 years in church planting (often transitioning from marketplace to ministry)

**Psychographics:**
- **Values**: Sustainable ministry, marketplace-mission integration, practical implementation, multiplication over addition
- **Goals**: 
  - Start sustainable church plants that don't require full-time salary
  - Integrate marketplace calling with ministry calling
  - Create multiplication-focused movements
  - Build organic, neighborhood-based communities
- **Pain Points**:
  - Need practical models for covocational planting
  - Balancing marketplace work and ministry
  - Feeling pulled between "sacred" and "secular" vocations
  - Need sustainable financial models for church planting
  - Isolation and lack of community/mentoring
- **Technology Comfort**: Moderate to high

**Content Consumption Patterns:**
- **Preferred Formats**: Books (practical guides), curriculum (structured learning), articles (quick access), podcasts (on-the-go learning), training programs (hands-on)
- **Preferred Channels**: Owned websites, email newsletters, podcasts, training programs (Forge, NAMB), conferences
- **Engagement Level**: High (active learners, implementers, seeking practical solutions)
- **Purchase Behavior**: Willing to invest in books ($15-$30), curriculum ($50-$200), training programs ($200-$500)

**Content Needs:**
- Practical models for covocational planting
- Financial sustainability frameworks
- Marketplace vocation integration strategies
- Multiplication and movement strategies
- Training and coaching resources

**How Brisco Serves Them:**
- *Covocational Church Planting* book (2021)
- Practical frameworks for marketplace-mission integration
- Training programs (Forge America, Forge Kansas City)
- NAMB/Send Network resources and support

### Segment 2: Pastors Seeking Missional Transition (35% of active audience)

**Demographics:**
- **Age**: 35-65 (primary: 45-60)
- **Education**: Advanced degrees (M.Div., D.Min. common)
- **Location**: North America (US, Canada primary)
- **Role**: Senior pastors, executive pastors, established church leaders
- **Experience**: 10+ years in established church leadership

**Psychographics:**
- **Values**: Transformation over maintenance, mission over programs, multiplication over addition, sustainable change
- **Goals**:
  - Lead church transformation from maintenance to mission
  - Shift church culture toward outward focus
  - Equip congregation for neighborhood mission
  - Implement sustainable missional change
- **Pain Points**:
  - Resistance to change in established churches
  - Need transition frameworks for existing churches
  - Balancing maintenance and mission
  - Lack of models for existing church transformation
  - Need practical, implementable strategies
- **Technology Comfort**: Moderate to high (varies by generation)

**Content Consumption Patterns:**
- **Preferred Formats**: Books (comprehensive guides), curriculum (congregational resources), articles (practical insights), conferences (inspiration and networking), training programs (transformation process)
- **Preferred Channels**: Conferences, email newsletters, owned websites, denominational networks, training programs
- **Engagement Level**: Medium to high (selective, strategic, seeking transformation resources)
- **Purchase Behavior**: Willing to invest in transformation resources ($50-$500 for programs, $20-$40 for books)

**Content Needs:**
- Church transformation frameworks
- Transition strategies (maintenance to mission)
- Congregational resources (curriculum, tools)
- Case studies from similar contexts
- Practical tools for cultural change

**How Brisco Serves Them:**
- *The Missional Quest* (long-term sustainability)
- *ReThink* (paradigm shifts)
- *Missional Essentials* curriculum (congregational resource)
- Forge training programs
- NAMB/Send Network support

## Unmet Needs & Content Gaps

### High-Priority Gaps

**1. Recent Digital Content**
- **Gap**: Limited recent articles, videos, podcasts
- **Impact**: Audience wants current, accessible content
- **Opportunity**: Regular blog posts, video teaching, podcast presence

**2. Practical Case Studies**
- **Gap**: Need more real-world examples of covocational planting and missional transition
- **Impact**: Leaders want to see frameworks applied
- **Opportunity**: Case study content, success stories, practical examples

**3. Video Content**
- **Gap**: Limited video teaching and framework explanations
- **Impact**: Missing engagement opportunities, especially for lay leaders
- **Opportunity**: Video series, teaching content, framework explanations

**4. Covocational Planting Resources**
- **Gap**: Need more practical resources for covocational planters (financial models, marketplace integration, sustainability)
- **Impact**: Growing segment needs more support
- **Opportunity**: Expanded covocational resources, tools, frameworks

**5. Transition Resources for Existing Churches**
- **Gap**: Need more practical tools and resources for existing church transition
- **Impact**: Large segment needs more implementation resources
- **Opportunity**: Expanded transition resources, congregational tools

### Medium-Priority Gaps

**6. Interactive Tools & Assessments**
- **Gap**: Need assessment tools for missional health, covocational readiness
- **Impact**: Leaders want diagnostic tools
- **Opportunity**: Interactive assessments, tools, resources

**7. Content for Younger Leaders**
- **Gap**: Limited content specifically for digital natives, younger planters
- **Impact**: Missing next generation of leaders
- **Opportunity**: Content tailored for younger audiences, digital-first formats

**8. Neighborhood Mission Resources**
- **Gap**: Need more practical resources for neighborhood engagement beyond *Next Door As It Is In Heaven*
- **Impact**: Lay leaders and congregations need more tools
- **Opportunity**: Expanded neighborhood mission resources, tools, case studies

## Distribution Channel Strategy

### Primary Channels (High Priority)

**1. Owned Website (Missional Church Network)**
- **Audience**: All segments
- **Content Types**: Articles, resources, book information, curriculum details
- **Strategy**: Central hub, SEO-optimized, comprehensive resource library

**2. Email Newsletters**
- **Audience**: All segments (high engagement)
- **Content Types**: Articles, updates, resource recommendations
- **Strategy**: Regular (weekly to bi-weekly), segmented by interest

**3. Conferences & Speaking**
- **Audience**: Planters, Pastors, Org Leaders
- **Content Types**: Keynotes, workshops, training sessions
- **Strategy**: High-value, in-person engagement, relationship building (Sentralized Conference, NAMB events)

**4. Training Programs (Forge, NAMB)**
- **Audience**: Planters, Pastors, Students
- **Content Types**: Structured courses, curriculum, coaching
- **Strategy**: High-value, transformation-focused, relationship-based

### Secondary Channels (Medium Priority)

**5. Podcasts (Guest Appearances)**
- **Audience**: Lay Leaders, Planters
- **Content Types**: Interviews, teaching, framework discussions
- **Strategy**: Strategic guest appearances on relevant podcasts

**6. Article Contributions (New Churches, ChurchLeaders.com)**
- **Audience**: All segments
- **Content Types**: Articles, practical insights
- **Strategy**: Regular contributions, value-focused content

**7. NAMB/Send Network Platforms**
- **Audience**: Org Leaders, Planters, Pastors
- **Content Types**: Resources, tools, support materials
- **Strategy**: Organizational reach, network distribution

### Emerging Channels (Opportunity)

**8. Video Content (YouTube, Owned Platform)**
- **Audience**: All segments (especially Lay Leaders, Planters)
- **Content Types**: Teaching series, interviews, framework explanations
- **Strategy**: Regular video content, series-based, SEO-optimized

**9. Regular Podcast (Owned)**
- **Audience**: All segments (especially Lay Leaders, Planters)
- **Content Types**: Teaching, interviews, framework discussions
- **Strategy**: Weekly or bi-weekly, consistent, value-focused

**10. Online Courses (Platform)**
- **Audience**: All segments
- **Content Types**: Structured learning, curriculum, certificates
- **Strategy**: Comprehensive courses on core frameworks, accessible pricing
