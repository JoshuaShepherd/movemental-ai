## Core Identity

Alan Hirsch is recognized as one of the leading voices in the missional church movement. Born and raised in apartheid South Africa, he immigrated to Australia in 1983 and has since become a key figure in shaping contemporary missional theology and practice through his writing, speaking, and organizational leadership.

---

## Early Life & Background

- **Heritage**: Grew up in a Jewish family in apartheid South Africa
- **Political Awareness**: Became politically active against apartheid at age 13
- **Education**: Attended university in Cape Town after military service
- **Military Service**: Mandatory two-year service in South Africa (age 20-22)
- **Conversion**: Converted to Christianity during military service (age ~20) through encounter with a "drug buddy" who was born again
- **Immigration**: Moved to Australia in 1983 at age 22, shortly after meeting his wife Debra

## Ministry Journey

### South Melbourne Restoration Community (1983-1998, 15 years)
- First "official" ministry with wife Debra
- Inner-city Melbourne church serving marginalized communities
- Ministry to homosexuals, prostitutes, schizophrenics, drug addicts

- Deeply shaped understanding of missional church and discipleship

### Forge Mission Training Network
- Founded to develop missional leaders for incarnational mission in Western contexts
- Networks in Australia, Canada, and USA
- Significant grassroots movement influencing church thinking

- Co-founded organization/publisher

## Key Contributions & Concepts

### mDNA Framework (Missional DNA)
Six elements of apostolic genius:
1. Jesus is Lord
2. Disciple Making
3. Missional-Incarnational Impulse
4. Apostolic Environment
5. Organic Systems
6. Liminality-Communitas

### APEST Framework (Five-fold Ministry)
Based on Ephesians 4:11:
- **A**postolic
- **P**rophetic
- **E**vangelistic
- **S**hepherding
- **T**eaching

### Other Key Concepts
- Incarnational mission
- Metanoia (transformation)
- Movemental thinking vs. institutional thinking
- Organic systems over organizational structures
