## Executive Summary

Michael Frost is a leading voice in the missional church movement, recognized internationally as an Australian Baptist minister, missiologist, and theologian. Born in 1961 and based in Sydney, Australia, Frost has authored or edited 20+ influential books, co-founded the Forge Mission Training Network (1999), founded the Tinsley Institute at Morling College, and established the missional Christian community smallboatbigsea (2002). His work focuses on practical missional living, cultural exegesis, post-Christendom ministry, and developing frameworks like BELLS (Bless, Eat, Listen, Learn, Sent) for missional living.

Frost's digital presence includes a personal website (mikefrost.net) with an active blog, 20+ published books, organizational platforms (Forge Mission Training Network, Tinsley Institute), and academic affiliations. His content spans books, blog articles, training materials, and international speaking, with strong thematic consistency around missional theology, practical frameworks, and cultural contextualization. Opportunities exist for greater content interconnection, digital amplification of extensive embodied work, and expansion into additional content forms.
