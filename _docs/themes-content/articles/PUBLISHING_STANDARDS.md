## Overview

This document defines the quality standards and requirements for publishing articles on the Alan Hirsch platform. All articles must meet these standards before publication to ensure consistency, quality, and effective portal discovery.

---

## Content Quality Standards

### Content Completeness

**Minimum Requirements**:
- ✅ **Content Length**: > 18,000 characters (substantial article)
- ✅ **Word Count**: 3,000+ words (comprehensive content)
- ✅ **Structure**: Well-organized with clear headings and sections
- ✅ **Completeness**: No placeholder or test content

**Quality Indicators**:
- Comprehensive coverage of topic
- Well-structured content (headings, paragraphs)
- Engaging introduction and conclusion
- Practical application included (40% of content)
- Framework integration (mDNA, APEST, etc.)

---

## SEO & Metadata Requirements

### Meta Title
- ✅ **Required**: Yes
- ✅ **Format**: Descriptive, 50-60 characters
- ✅ **Content**: Primary keyword in title
- ✅ **Example**: "Movement Intelligence: Understanding Movement Quality and Multiplication Dynamics"

### Meta Description
- ✅ **Required**: Yes
- ✅ **Format**: Compelling, 150-160 characters
- ✅ **Content**: Summary of article value proposition
- ✅ **Example**: "Movements are living organisms, not machines. They grow, adapt, and reproduce from within, following built-in patterns..."

### Additional SEO (Optional)
- Focus keyword (if applicable)
- Canonical URL (if applicable)
- Open Graph tags (for social sharing)
- Twitter Card tags (for social sharing)

## Portal Tagging Requirements

### Tagging Requirements
- ✅ **Required**: At least one portal theme
- ✅ **Accuracy**: Tagging must accurately reflect article content
- ✅ **Multiple Tags**: Appropriate when content spans multiple portals

## Content Formatting Standards

### Structure Requirements
- ✅ **Introduction**: 300-400 words (hook, context, overview)
- ✅ **Body Sections**: Well-organized with clear headings (H2, H3)
- ✅ **Practical Application**: 40% of content focused on practical application
- ✅ **Conclusion**: 200-300 words (summary, next steps, call to action)

### Formatting Standards
- ✅ **Headings**: Proper hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- ✅ **Paragraphs**: Clear, readable paragraphs (3-5 sentences)
- ✅ **Lists**: Use bulleted or numbered lists when appropriate
- ✅ **Links**: No broken links or references
- ✅ **Images**: Proper alt text and accessibility (if applicable)

## Voice & Style Consistency

### Alan Hirsch Voice Signature

**Required Elements**:
- ✅ **Direct Address**: Use "we," "you" (18.7 per 1,000 words)
- ✅ **Questions**: Questions for engagement (3.2 per 1,000 words)
- ✅ **Metaphors**: Movement/DNA metaphors (8.5 per 1,000 words)
- ✅ **Historical Examples**: Historical examples (4.8 per 1,000 words)
- ✅ **Epigraphs**: 2-4 epigraphs (biblical, historical, contemporary)
- ✅ **Biblical Integration**: Biblical integration throughout

**Prohibited Elements**:
- ❌ **Antithesis**: NO "not X but Y" constructions
- ❌ **Either/Or**: NO either/or constructions
- ❌ **Polemics**: Avoid polemical language
- ❌ **Institutional Language**: Avoid institutional jargon

### Style Guidelines
- ✅ **Tone**: Engaging, accessible, inspiring
- ✅ **Language**: Clear, direct, practical
- ✅ **Perspective**: Movement-oriented, missional, transformational
- ✅ **Integration**: Connect to frameworks (mDNA, APEST, etc.)

## Article Readiness Checklist

**Before Publishing, Verify**:

**Content Completeness**:
- [ ] Article has full content (not placeholder text)
- [ ] Content length > 18,000 characters
- [ ] Content is well-formatted (proper headings, paragraphs)
- [ ] No broken links or references
- [ ] Images/media are accessible (if applicable)

**SEO & Metadata**:
- [ ] Meta title is set and descriptive (50-60 characters)
- [ ] Meta description is set and compelling (150-160 characters)
- [ ] Focus keyword is appropriate (if applicable)
- [ ] Canonical URL is correct (if applicable)

**Portal Tagging**:
- [ ] Article is tagged with relevant portal themes
- [ ] Tagging is accurate (article matches portal theme)
- [ ] Multiple portal tags are appropriate (if applicable)

**Quality Assurance**:
- [ ] Content reviewed for quality
- [ ] No placeholder or test content
- [ ] Article follows content guidelines
- [ ] Voice and style consistency maintained
- [ ] Framework integration (mDNA, APEST, etc.)

## Publishing Decision Criteria

### Publish Now
✅ **Criteria**:
- All readiness checklist items complete
- Content quality meets standards
- Portal tagging accurate
- SEO metadata complete
- Voice and style consistent

### Needs Review/Revision
⚠️ **Criteria**:
- Missing required elements (metadata, tagging)
- Content quality issues
- Placeholder or test content present
- Voice/style inconsistencies

### Archive/Skip
❌ **Criteria**:
- Duplicate content
- Obsolete or outdated content
- Not aligned with platform strategy
- Significant quality issues

---

## Quality Assurance Process

### Pre-Publishing Review
1. **Content Review**: Verify content completeness and quality
2. **Metadata Review**: Check SEO metadata (title, description)
3. **Tagging Review**: Verify portal tagging accuracy
4. **Style Review**: Ensure voice and style consistency
5. **Technical Review**: Check formatting, links, images

### Post-Publishing Verification
1. **Portal Visibility**: Verify article appears in correct portals
2. **Public Access**: Test article URL and display
3. **Metadata Testing**: Verify SEO metadata works correctly
4. **Console Check**: Ensure no errors in browser console
