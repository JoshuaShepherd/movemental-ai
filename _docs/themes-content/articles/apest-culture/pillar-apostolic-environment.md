## Common Challenges to Apostolic Environment

The path of apostolic environment is not without obstacles. Cultural barriers represent perhaps the greatest challenge. We have tended to create cultures that favor stability over extension, recognizing maintenance (ST) while ignoring multiplication (APE). This imbalance makes apostolic environment impossible.

The institutional resistance challenge comes from our tendency to maintain existing structures rather than creating new ones. We have created hierarchies that elevate stability over extension, systems that favor maintenance over multiplication, and rewards that value addition over multiplication. This resistance makes apostolic environment impossible.

The activation challenge comes from our tendency to limit apostolic function to a select few rather than activating it throughout the organization. We have created clergy-laity divides that separate the "gifted" from the "ungifted," structures that limit apostolic function to leaders, and systems that prevent apostolic function from operating throughout the organization. This limitation makes apostolic environment impossible.

The measurement transformation challenges our assumptions about what success looks like. We have measured success by attendance, buildings, or budgets rather than by extension and multiplication. We have evaluated success by addition rather than by multiplication. We have assessed success by visible results rather than by apostolic function. This transformation requires us to rethink our metrics and our methods.

The system transformation challenge comes from our tendency to create systems that maintain rather than multiply. We have created systems that focus on addition rather than multiplication, on maintenance rather than extension, on control rather than release. This system transformation requires us to rethink our systems and our structures.
