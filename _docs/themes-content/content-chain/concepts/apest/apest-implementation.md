# APEST (Fivefold Ministry): Implementation Guide

**Concept**: APEST (Apostle, Prophet, Evangelist, Shepherd, Teacher) / 5Q  
**Purpose**: Step-by-step guide for implementing the fivefold ministry in churches and communities  
**Target Audience**: Church leaders, practitioners, implementers  
**Last Updated**: December 2025

---

## Overview

This implementation guide provides a practical, step-by-step approach to activating APEST (the fivefold ministry) in your church or community. Whether you're a church planter starting fresh, a leader in an established church seeking to recover the full fivefold ministry, or part of a denomination looking to distribute ministry to all believers, this guide will help you understand where you are and how to move forward.

The guide is based on Alan Hirsch's extensive research and practical experience, drawing from *5Q* and *The Permanent Revolution*. It recognizes that APEST is something we recover—it's the constitutional DNA of the church, always present in potential even when suppressed or unrecognized. The implementation process is about awakening latent potential and creating conditions where all five functions can be present and working together.

This is a journey that requires patience, prayer, and long-term commitment. The recovery of APEST, especially the often-suppressed apostolic, prophetic, and evangelistic functions, is essential for church maturity and mission effectiveness.

## Assessment

### Current State Assessment

**Questions to Consider**:

1. **Apostolic Function**: Do we have people pioneering new missional frontiers? Are we extending the gospel into new contexts? Is our church organized for multiplication?

2. **Prophetic Function**: Do we have people speaking truth to power? Are we calling our community to covenant faithfulness and justice? Is there space for challenging the status quo?

3. **Evangelistic Function**: Do we have people actively sharing the gospel and inviting others into the story? Are we creating receptivity to the good news? Is evangelism a natural part of our community life?

4. **Shepherding Function**: Do we have people building healthy, nurturing community? Are we caring for one another and developing spiritual maturity? Is there authentic pastoral care?

5. **Teaching Function**: Do we have people mediating wisdom and understanding? Are we integrating and passing on knowledge? Is there depth in our discipleship?

6. **Integration**: Are all five functions present and working together? Do we experience the symphonic integration of 5Q? Is there balance, or does one function dominate?

### Assessment Tools

#### Tool 1: 5Q Systems Test

- **Purpose**: Measures organizational awareness and practice of APEST functions
- **How to Use**: Complete online assessment at 5Qcentral.com or 5Qtests.com
- **Scoring**: Provides scores for each of the five functions and overall 5Q score
- **Interpretation**: Scores help identify strongest and weakest functions for strategic focus

#### Tool 2: 5Q Diagnostic Test

- **Purpose**: Straightforward assessment of current APEST capacities
- **How to Use**: Complete online diagnostic test (can be used multiple times)
- **Scoring**: Provides diagnostic overview similar to "APEST marks" diagrams
- **Interpretation**: Helps leaders assess strengths and weaknesses and develop strategies

#### Tool 3: APEST Inventory

- **Purpose**: Helps churches inventory activities and functions through fivefold lens
- **How to Use**: List all major church activities, tag each as A-P-E-S-T (primary and secondary)
- **Scoring**: Chart activities visually, assess concentrations and deficiencies
- **Interpretation**: Identify gaps and ways to fill them for balanced ministry

#### Tool 4: Personal APEST Portrait

- **Purpose**: Individual assessment of APEST calling
- **How to Use**: Take online test at aptest.org, create personal portrait, share with community
- **Scoring**: Shows primary and secondary callings
- **Interpretation**: Helps individuals understand their role in the Body

### Readiness Checklist

- [ ] Leadership team has read *5Q* or understands APEST framework
- [ ] Community recognizes need for full fivefold ministry, not just shepherd-teacher
- [ ] There is commitment to distributed ministry to all believers
- [ ] Resources (time, energy, support) are available for the journey
- [ ] There is willingness to let go of hierarchical leadership models
- [ ] Community is open to all five functions, especially apostolic, prophetic, and evangelistic
- [ ] There is prayerful commitment to recovering biblical ministry patterns

## Step-by-Step Implementation

### Phase 1: Recognition and Assessment (Months 1-2)

**Duration**: 1-2 months  
**Goal**: Recognize APEST is latent and assess current state

#### Step 1.1: Introduce APEST Framework

- **Action**: Share APEST concept with leadership team and core community through reading, teaching, or workshop
- **Resources Needed**: *5Q* book, summary materials, teaching time
- **Expected Outcome**: Community understands that APEST is constitutional DNA, always present in potential

#### Step 1.2: Complete Assessments

- **Action**: Have leadership team and core community complete 5Q Systems Test, Diagnostic Test, and APEST Inventory
- **Resources Needed**: Access to online assessments, evaluation tools, meeting time
- **Expected Outcome**: Clear picture of current state, identification of strongest and weakest functions

#### Step 1.3: Create Baseline Documentation

- **Action**: Document current state, scores, and observations for each function
- **Resources Needed**: Documentation tools, meeting space
- **Expected Outcome**: Baseline for measuring progress over time

#### Step 1.4: Identify Starting Point

- **Action**: Based on assessments, identify missing or weakest APEST function as starting point
- **Resources Needed**: Assessment results, prayerful discernment
- **Expected Outcome**: Clear focus for initial development efforts

### Phase 2: Individual Calling Discovery (Months 3-4)

**Duration**: 2 months  
**Goal**: Help individuals discover their APEST calling

#### Step 2.1: Personal APEST Assessment

- **Action**: Have all community members take APEST test at aptest.org
- **Resources Needed**: Access to online test, time for individuals
- **Expected Outcome**: Each person knows their primary and secondary callings

#### Step 2.2: Create Personal APEST Portraits

- **Action**: Each person creates visual portrait showing their APEST profile
- **Resources Needed**: APEST portrait templates, art supplies or digital tools
- **Expected Outcome**: Visual representation of each person's calling

#### Step 2.3: Community Confirmation

- **Action**: Share portraits in small groups, receive feedback and confirmation
- **Resources Needed**: Small group settings, facilitation
- **Expected Outcome**: Refined understanding of individual callings with community confirmation

#### Step 2.4: Begin Practicing Callings

- **Action**: Create opportunities for people to practice their APEST calling in community
- **Resources Needed**: Ministry opportunities, support, encouragement
- **Expected Outcome**: People begin operating in their calling

### Phase 3: Organizational Integration (Months 5-8)

**Duration**: 4 months  
**Goal**: Embed APEST into church structures and culture

#### Step 3.1: Restructure Leadership

- **Action**: Move from hierarchical to distributed leadership model based on APEST
- **Resources Needed**: Leadership commitment, structural changes, communication
- **Expected Outcome**: Leadership reflects all five functions, not just shepherd-teacher

#### Step 3.2: Use APEST Tools

- **Action**: Implement APEST Thinking Hats, APEST Perspectives, and other tools in decision-making
- **Resources Needed**: Tool training, facilitation, practice
- **Expected Outcome**: APEST becomes part of organizational culture

#### Step 3.3: Develop Missing Functions

- **Action**: Intentionally develop missing or weak APEST functions
- **Resources Needed**: Training, resources, people with those callings
- **Expected Outcome**: All five functions present and functioning

#### Step 3.4: Create APEST Pipeline

- **Action**: Develop processes for discipling people into each APEST calling
- **Resources Needed**: Curriculum development, apprenticeship structures
- **Expected Outcome**: Clear pathways for developing each calling

### Phase 4: Cultural Embedding (Months 9-12)

**Duration**: 4 months  
**Goal**: APEST becomes embedded in church culture

#### Step 4.1: Embed in Rhythms

- **Action**: Integrate APEST language and practices into regular church rhythms
- **Resources Needed**: Worship planning, small group structures, regular meetings
- **Expected Outcome**: APEST becomes natural part of church life

#### Step 4.2: Develop APEST Culture Levels

- **Action**: Work through levels: Grounded (all five understood), Holistic (all five present), Balanced (all five working together), Fruitful (all five accessing synergy), Multiplying (all five recreating themselves)
- **Resources Needed**: Assessment tools, intentional development
- **Expected Outcome**: Movement through culture levels

#### Step 4.3: Celebrate APEST Diversity

- **Action**: Regularly celebrate and affirm different APEST callings
- **Resources Needed**: Celebration events, storytelling, affirmation
- **Expected Outcome**: All callings valued and celebrated

#### Step 4.4: Ongoing Assessment

- **Action**: Regularly reassess using 5Q Systems Test and other tools
- **Resources Needed**: Assessment tools, evaluation time
- **Expected Outcome**: Continuous improvement and refinement

### Phase 5: Multiplication and Maturity (Months 13-24)

**Duration**: 12 months  
**Goal**: 5Q integration emerges and church multiplies

#### Step 5.1: Achieve 5Q Integration

- **Action**: Continue developing until all five functions work together in symphony
- **Resources Needed**: Continued commitment, integration practices
- **Expected Outcome**: Symphonic integration creating synergistic intelligence

#### Step 5.2: Multiply APEST Culture

- **Action**: Begin multiplying communities with full APEST expression
- **Resources Needed**: Multiplication structures, leadership development
- **Expected Outcome**: New expressions emerge with all five functions

#### Step 5.3: Sustain and Refine

- **Action**: Maintain APEST culture, refine practices, continue multiplication
- **Resources Needed**: Ongoing assessment, adaptation, community commitment
- **Expected Outcome**: Sustainable APEST culture and multiplication

#### Step 5.4: Measure Maturity

- **Action**: Evaluate progress toward "the whole measure of the fullness of Christ" (Ephesians 4:13)
- **Resources Needed**: Maturity indicators, community feedback
- **Expected Outcome**: Clear evidence of maturity and fullness

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Creating APEST Hierarchy

**Description**: Using APEST to create a new five-tier leadership structure rather than distributing ministry to all believers

**Why It Happens**: Institutional mindset, misunderstanding of Ephesians 4:7, desire for clear organizational structure

**How to Avoid**:
- Remember Ephesians 4:7: "to each one of us grace has been given"
- Emphasize ministry for all, not leadership for a few
- Leadership emerges from within distributed gifts, not imposed above them
- Continually shape APEST by humility and self-emptying of Christ

**If You're Already There**: Reassess structure, redistribute ministry, emphasize all believers' participation

### Pitfall 2: Focusing on Only One or Two Functions

**Description**: Developing only the functions that are already present (typically shepherd and teacher) rather than recovering all five

**Why It Happens**: Comfort with existing functions, resistance to change, lack of understanding of interdependence

**How to Avoid**:
- Remember all five functions are necessary for maturity
- Intentionally develop missing functions (especially apostolic, prophetic, evangelistic)
- Understand that each function brings something essential
- Resist the temptation to stay in comfort zone

**If You're Already There**: Assess what's missing, create plan to develop missing functions, seek people with those callings

### Pitfall 3: Treating APEST as Personality Test

**Description**: Reducing APEST to individual personality assessment without understanding corporate functions and integration

**Why It Happens**: Individualistic culture, misunderstanding of implicit vs. explicit APEST, focus on self-discovery

**How to Avoid**:
- Understand both implicit (corporate) and explicit (individual) APEST
- Emphasize how individual callings serve corporate functions
- Focus on integration and symphony, not just individual discovery
- Remember 5Q happens at corporate level

**If You're Already There**: Reintroduce corporate functions, emphasize integration, create practices that bring functions together

### Pitfall 4: Allowing One Function to Dominate

**Description**: One APEST function becomes dominant, creating precocious development and imbalance

**Why It Happens**: Natural tendency to rely on strengths, lack of intentional balance, groupthink

**How to Avoid**:
- Regularly assess balance using 5Q Systems Test
- Intentionally develop weaker functions
- Create structures that require all five functions
- Celebrate diversity and interdependence

**If You're Already There**: Identify dominant function, create plan to develop others, restructure to require balance

### Pitfall 5: Implementing Without Theological Foundation

**Description**: Using APEST as organizational tool without understanding biblical and theological foundations

**Why It Happens**: Pragmatic approach, desire for quick results, lack of theological training

**How to Avoid**:
- Ground implementation in Ephesians 4:1–16
- Understand APEST as constitutional theology
- Connect to Christology (J-APEST) and ecclesiology
- Study biblical foundations before implementing

**If You're Already There**: Return to biblical foundations, study theology, reconnect implementation to theology

## Timeline Expectations

**New Churches**: Can embed APEST from the beginning (6-12 months for full integration)

**Established Churches**: Transformation takes longer depending on starting point (12-24 months for significant change, 2-5 years for full integration)

**Denominations/Systems**: Systemic change requires longer timeframe (3-7 years for system-wide adoption)

**Key Factors Affecting Timeline**:
- Depth of institutionalization
- Commitment of leadership and community
- Resources available
- Resistance to change
- Starting point (how many functions already present)
