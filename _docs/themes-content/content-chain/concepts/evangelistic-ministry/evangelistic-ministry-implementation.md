# Evangelistic Ministry: Implementation Guide

**Concept**: Evangelistic Ministry  
**Purpose**: Step-by-step guide for implementing evangelistic ministry  
**Target Audience**: Church leaders, practitioners, implementers  
**Last Updated**: December 2025

---

## Overview

This guide provides practical steps for developing and implementing evangelistic ministry, reframed within discipleship. Evangelistic ministry is essential for the church to extend the gospel, but it must be understood within the context of disciple-making.

---

## Assessment

### Current State Assessment

**Questions to Consider**:
1. How are we currently extending the gospel?
2. Is our evangelism event-based or process-oriented?
3. Are we reframing evangelism within discipleship?
4. Do we have people with evangelistic gifting being released?
5. Are we balancing proclamation and presence?

## Step-by-Step Implementation

### Phase 1: Foundation

**Duration**: 1-3 months  
**Goal**: Establish understanding and commitment

#### Step 1.1: Leadership Education

**Action**: Educate leadership on evangelistic ministry reframed within discipleship

**Resources Needed**: Books, study guides, time

**Expected Outcome**: Leadership understands and commits

#### Step 1.2: Church-Wide Teaching

**Action**: Teach the church about evangelistic ministry and discipleship

**Resources Needed**: Teaching materials, time

**Expected Outcome**: Church understands reframed approach

#### Step 1.3: Assessment and Identification

**Action**: Identify people with evangelistic gifting

**Resources Needed**: Assessment tools, time

**Expected Outcome**: People with evangelistic gifting identified

### Phase 2: Creating Systems

**Duration**: 3-6 months  
**Goal**: Create systems for evangelistic ministry

#### Step 2.1: Develop Discipleship Framework

**Action**: Create framework where evangelism happens within discipleship

**Resources Needed**: Framework design, training, time

**Expected Outcome**: Discipleship framework established

#### Step 2.2: Create Spaces for Presence

**Action**: Create spaces and opportunities for relational presence

**Resources Needed**: Spaces, time, facilitation

**Expected Outcome**: Spaces for presence created

#### Step 2.3: Develop Proclamation Skills

**Action**: Train people in clear, compelling proclamation

**Resources Needed**: Training materials, time

**Expected Outcome**: People equipped for proclamation

### Phase 3: Functioning and Impact

**Duration**: 6-12 months  
**Goal**: Evangelistic ministry functioning and impacting

#### Step 3.1: Release Evangelistic Ministry

**Action**: Release people with evangelistic gifting to function

**Resources Needed**: Support, resources, space

**Expected Outcome**: Evangelistic ministry functioning

#### Step 3.2: Measure Impact

**Action**: Measure encounters with gospel and discipleship progress

**Resources Needed**: Metrics, evaluation, feedback

**Expected Outcome**: Impact measured and improved

#### Step 3.3: Multiply Evangelists

**Action**: Train and multiply people in evangelistic ministry

**Resources Needed**: Training systems, multiplication focus

**Expected Outcome**: Evangelists multiplied

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Event-Only Approach

**Description**: Reducing evangelistic ministry to events only

**How to Avoid**: Develop both proclamation and presence, event and process

**If You're Already There**: Expand to include presence and process

### Pitfall 2: Separating from Discipleship

**Description**: Treating evangelism as separate from discipleship

**How to Avoid**: Reframe evangelism within discipleship context

**If You're Already There**: Reintegrate with discipleship

### Pitfall 3: Only Proclamation

**Description**: Focusing only on verbal proclamation

**How to Avoid**: Develop both proclamation and presence

**If You're Already There**: Add presence dimension
