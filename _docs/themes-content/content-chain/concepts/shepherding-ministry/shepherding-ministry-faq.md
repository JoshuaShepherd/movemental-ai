## Introduction

Shepherding ministry is the most recognized in contemporary church, but it's often reduced to maintenance care. This FAQ addresses common questions, helping readers understand what shepherding ministry is, why it matters, and how it functions in missional contexts.

---

## Questions

### Q1: What is shepherding ministry?

**Answer**: Shepherding ministry is the caring, protecting, nurturing ministry that centers on nurturing healthy Christian community and caring for people's welfare. Shepherds are the church's relational foundation, fostering formation in Christ by building cohesive, inclusive, and stable communities.

**Key Points**:
- Nurtures healthy Christian community
- Cares for people's welfare
- Builds cohesive, inclusive communities
- Fosters formation in Christ

**Sources**: *The Permanent Revolution*, Chapter 3

---

### Q2: Is shepherding only about maintenance care?

**Answer**: No. While maintenance care is important, the shepherding function is about more than maintenance. It's about developing other shepherds and creating cultures of care that multiply. Missional shepherding is about care that multiplies, not just care that maintains.

**Key Points**:
- More than maintenance care
- Develops other shepherds
- Creates cultures of care that multiply
- Care that multiplies vs. care that maintains

**Sources**: Various sources

---

### Q3: What is the difference between maintenance care and missional shepherding?

**Answer**: Maintenance care is reactive, dependent, limited, and doesn't multiply. Missional shepherding is proactive, developmental, distributed, sustainable, and multiplies. Maintenance care keeps people healthy. Missional shepherding develops people so they can help others become healthy.

**Key Points**:
- Maintenance: reactive, dependent, limited
- Missional: proactive, developmental, distributed, multiplies
- Maintenance keeps healthy; missional develops capacity

**Sources**: Various sources

---

### Q4: How do we develop other shepherds?

**Answer**: Develop other shepherds by identifying people with shepherding capacity, creating opportunities for development, providing support and coaching, creating cultures of care, and multiplying communities of care.

**Key Points**:
- Identify shepherding capacity
- Create development opportunities
- Provide support and coaching
- Create cultures of care
- Multiply communities

**Sources**: Various sources

---

### Q5: What does missional shepherding look like?

**Answer**: Missional shepherding means caring for the flock so it flourishes and reproduces, participating in the extension of the church's mission. It extends beyond the community, caring for neighbors, welcoming the marginalized, serving the community, and developing leaders.

**Key Points**:
- Caring so flock flourishes and reproduces
- Extends beyond community
- Connects to God's broader mission
- Develops leaders for mission

**Sources**: *The Permanent Revolution*, Chapter 3
