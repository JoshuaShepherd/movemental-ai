## 1. Definition Report

### Alan's Definition

Teaching ministry is the equipping, explaining, grounding ministry that mediates practical wisdom through a biblical worldview, helping people see and experience their world as God intends. Teachers focus on both broad comprehension and systematic understanding, fostering lifelong learning rather than urgency or activism.

Teaching ministry is about mediating wisdom and understanding, bringing comprehensive insight to the church's revelation. It must move beyond speculation to "ideas in action"—discipleship and formation. Teachers' lives should reflect their message. When detached from life and obedience, teaching becomes abstract, speculative, and potentially controlling. True biblical teaching is life-oriented, ethical, and participatory.

Vibrant teaching is indispensable, but it must not dominate the community at the expense of apostolic, prophetic, evangelistic, or shepherding functions. Teaching should be resituated within a field of practice and with a keener concern to training in context. Mastery is bound up in reflective practitioners who engage in their disciplines accompanied with guided reflection.

### Common Misunderstandings

**Misunderstanding 1: Teaching is only about information transfer**

Many people think teaching ministry is primarily about transferring information—lecturing, explaining, or providing knowledge. While information is part of teaching, teaching ministry is broader. It's about mediating wisdom, helping people see their world as God intends, and fostering formation.

**Misunderstanding 2: Teaching should dominate the community**

Some churches elevate teaching to the point where it dominates all other ministries. However, teaching must not dominate at the expense of other APEST functions. Teaching is indispensable but should be resituated within a field of practice.

**Misunderstanding 3: Teaching is only for teachers**

Some believe that only people with the "gift of teaching" can do teaching ministry. However, Ephesians 4:7 says "to each one of us"—all believers are endowed with APEST gifting. While some are particularly gifted in teaching, all believers can participate in teaching ministry.

**Misunderstanding 4: Teaching is separate from practice**

Some separate teaching from practice, seeing teaching as theoretical and practice as practical. However, teaching must move beyond speculation to "ideas in action." True biblical teaching is life-oriented, ethical, and participatory.

### Why It Matters

Teaching ministry matters because the church needs wisdom and understanding to function faithfully. Without it, churches lack theological grounding, biblical understanding, and practical wisdom. Teaching ministry provides the foundation for all other ministries to function effectively.

The church's theological drift can often be traced to inadequate or distorted teaching ministry. When teaching is abstract, speculative, or controlling, it doesn't serve the church well. When it's life-oriented, ethical, and participatory, it equips the church for faithful mission.

Teaching ministry is essential for the church to fulfill its calling. It provides the wisdom and understanding necessary for the church to navigate complex issues, make faithful decisions, and live out the gospel in the world.

### Biblical Foundations

**Ephesians 4:7–11** establishes teachers as the fifth of five essential ministries given to equip God's people.

**Jesus as Exemplary Teacher**: Historically regarded as Rabbi and Teacher, Jesus embodies the Way, teaching through example and life. He is the Word made flesh, the fulfillment and redefinition of the Torah (Matthew 5:17; John 1:17, 14:6). In him "are hidden all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge" (Colossians 2:2–3).

**James 3:1**: "Not many of you should become teachers, my fellow believers, because you know that we who teach will be judged more strictly." This warning indicates the seriousness and responsibility of teaching ministry.

### Theological Framework

Teaching ministry is rooted in the doctrine of revelation. God has revealed himself, and teaching ministry helps people understand and apply that revelation. It mediates the wisdom of God to the people of God.

Teaching ministry is also rooted in the doctrine of formation. People need to be formed in Christ, and teaching ministry provides the wisdom and understanding necessary for this formation to happen.

Teaching ministry is Christological. Jesus is the exemplary teacher, and all teaching ministry flows from his pattern. The church is the Body of Christ, and as such, it must reflect the teaching dimension of Jesus' ministry.

### Historical Examples

**Jesus**: The exemplary teacher who taught through word and example

**Paul**: The apostle who was also a teacher, providing theological foundation

**Early Church**: Teachers who grounded communities in Scripture and tradition

**Contemporary Examples**: Teachers who mediate wisdom and foster formation
