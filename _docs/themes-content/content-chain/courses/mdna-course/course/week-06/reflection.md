# Reflection Questions

Take some time to reflect on these questions. You might want to journal your responses, or discuss them with others who are on this journey with you.

1. **What systems in your context enable multiplication?** What systems constrain it? How can you tell the difference?

2. **What would it look like to design simple, reproducible, distributed systems?** What simple rules could guide your community? What common language do you need? What reproducible practices could you develop?

3. **How can you be "organized but not institutionalized"?** What's the difference? How do you maintain organization without killing movemental energy?

4. **What would it look like to shift from mechanistic to organic thinking?** How would this change your approach to organization? What would need to change?

5. **How can you pass on DNA through relationships, stories, and practices?** What relationships are you building? What stories are you telling? What practices are you developing?

6. **What would it look like to create networks rather than hierarchies?** How would this change your structure? What obstacles stand in the way?

7. **How can you enable multiplication rather than addition?** What would need to change? What would multiplication look like in your context?

8. **How does organic systems connect to Jesus' lordship?** What does it mean that organic systems reflect the nature of the kingdom Jesus announced?

9. **How does the parable of the growing seed speak to you?** What does it mean that the seed grows "all by itself"? How does this shape your understanding of organization?

10. **What's your next step?** How can you begin creating organic systems? What support do you need? What questions do you want to explore further?
