## Introduction

This document provides comprehensive mapping of relationships between the 11 editorial portals and plans for cross-portal content integration. Cross-portal content recognizes that portals are interconnected and that understanding their relationships is essential for comprehensive learning.

**Cross-Portal Content Types:**
- **Connection Maps**: Visual and textual maps showing how portals relate
- **Integrated Framework Explanations**: How frameworks work together
- **Thematic Integration Guides**: How themes integrate across portals
- **Cross-Portal Q&A**: Questions that span multiple portals

**Central Hub:**
- **Portal 4**: Jesus is Lord (mDNA) - The foundation and central confession

**Framework:**
- **Portal 3**: The Forgotten Ways - The complete mDNA framework (all six elements)

**Elements:**
- **Portal 5**: Discipleship & Disciple-Making - Element 2: Disciple-Making
- **Portal 6**: Missional-Incarnational Impulse - Element 3: Missional-Incarnational Impulse
- **Portal 7**: APEST Culture - Element 4: APEST Culture
- **Portal 8**: Organic Systems - Element 5: Organic Systems
- **Portal 9**: Liminality & Communitas - Element 6: Communitas

**Key Relationships:**
- Portal 4 (Jesus is Lord) is the foundation for all other portals
- Portal 3 (The Forgotten Ways) provides the framework connecting all mDNA elements
- Portals 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 are the six elements of mDNA
- All elements connect through Portal 3 and Portal 4

**Cross-Portal Content Needed:**
- Connection map showing all six mDNA elements together
- Integrated explanation of mDNA as a system
- Guide on how mDNA elements work together
- Q&A on mDNA integration

## Key Portal Relationships

### Foundation Relationships

**1. Jesus is Lord (Portal 4) as Foundation**
- **Foundation for**: All other portals
- **Key Insight**: Christology determines missiology determines ecclesiology
- **Connection**: All portals flow from understanding "Jesus is Lord"
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps showing Portal 4 as foundation, integrated explanations of Christocentricity

**2. The Forgotten Ways (Portal 3) as Framework**
- **Framework for**: All mDNA element portals (5, 6, 7, 8, 9)
- **Key Insight**: Apostolic Genius as the system that connects all elements
- **Connection**: Portal 3 provides the framework, Portals 5-9 are the elements
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps showing mDNA framework, integrated explanations of Apostolic Genius

### Element Relationships

**3. APEST Framework and Culture (Portal 1 ↔ Portal 7)**
- **Relationship**: Framework → Application
- **Key Insight**: APEST/5Q as framework, APEST Culture as organizational application
- **Connection**: Portal 1 provides the framework, Portal 7 applies it organizationally
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, transformation guides

**4. Discipleship and Mission (Portal 5 ↔ Portal 6)**
- **Relationship**: Inseparable integration
- **Key Insight**: Discipleship and mission cannot be separated
- **Connection**: Portal 5 (discipleship) and Portal 6 (mission) work together
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, practice guides

**5. Movement and Systems (Portal 2 ↔ Portal 8)**
- **Relationship**: Quality and Structure
- **Key Insight**: Movement Quality requires organic systems
- **Connection**: Portal 2 (movement quality) requires Portal 8 (organic systems)
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, system design guides

**6. Transformation Vision and Process (Portal 10 ↔ Portal 11)**
- **Relationship**: Vision enables Process
- **Key Insight**: Reframation (seeing) enables Metanoia (changing)
- **Connection**: Portal 10 (reframation) enables Portal 11 (metanoia)
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, transformation journeys

**7. Liminality and Transformation (Portal 9 ↔ Portal 11)**
- **Relationship**: Context and Process
- **Key Insight**: Liminality creates the context for transformation
- **Connection**: Portal 9 (liminality/communitas) creates context for Portal 11 (metanoia)
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, journey guides

### Framework Integration Relationships

**8. mDNA and APEST (Portal 3 ↔ Portal 1)**
- **Relationship**: System integration
- **Key Insight**: APEST Culture is an element of mDNA
- **Connection**: Portal 1 (APEST) provides Portal 7 (APEST Culture), which is Portal 3 (mDNA element)
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, system guides

**9. Movement and Transformation (Portal 2 ↔ Portal 11)**
- **Relationship**: Capacity and Change
- **Key Insight**: Movement capacity requires organizational transformation
- **Connection**: Portal 11 (metanoia) enables Portal 2 (movement quality)
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, transformation strategies

**10. Mission and Formation (Portal 6 ↔ Portal 10)**
- **Relationship**: Practice and Vision
- **Key Insight**: Missional practice requires reframed formation
- **Connection**: Portal 10 (reframation) enables Portal 6 (missional-incarnational impulse)
- **Cross-Portal Content**: Connection maps, integrated explanations, practice guides

## Cross-Portal Reference Strategy

### Portal-Specific Content with Cross-References

Each portal-specific content file should include:

1. **Cross-Portal References Section**:
   - List related portals
   - Explain relationships
   - Link to related portal content
   - Note how portals connect

2. **Navigation Elements**:
   - "Related Portals" sidebar/navigation
   - "See Also" links to related portals
   - "Integrated Frameworks" links to cross-portal content

3. **Terminology Consistency**:
   - Use portal-specific terminology for primary portal
   - Reference related portals using their terminology
   - Note terminology relationships in cross-portal content

## Cross-Portal Content Creation Process

### For Each Cross-Portal File

1. **Identify portals** involved in the relationship
2. **Review source material** from all related portals
3. **Map relationships** clearly and accurately
4. **Use portal terminology** from each portal appropriately
5. **Create integrated content** that shows connections
6. **Add navigation** to related portal-specific content
7. **Verify completeness** against portal relationships map

### Cross-Portal Content Checklist

- [ ] All related portals identified
- [ ] Relationships clearly explained
- [ ] Portal terminology used correctly
- [ ] Source material referenced
- [ ] Navigation to portal-specific content included
- [ ] Visual/textual maps created
- [ ] Integrated explanations provided
- [ ] Cross-references added
