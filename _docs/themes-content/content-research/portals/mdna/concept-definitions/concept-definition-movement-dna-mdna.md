# Movement DNA (mDNA): The Genetic Code of Apostolic Movements

## The Essential Definition

Movement DNA (mDNA) consists of six essential elements that must be present and working together for Apostolic Genius to activate: Jesus is Lord (the central confession), Disciple-Making (multiplication through formation), Missional-Incarnational Impulse (going out and going deep), APEST Culture (the fivefold ministry environment), Organic Systems (movemental structures), and Communitas (liminal community). Like biological DNA, mDNA is found in every living cell of the church, contains all genetic information needed to reproduce the whole organism, is self-replicating, and can be suppressed or dormant but can be reactivated.

## The Full Explanation

What if I told you that every church, every believer, every expression of the people of God already contains within it the genetic code for world transformation? What if the potential for exponential growth and cultural impact isn't something we need to import or create, but something encoded in the very being of the church itself?

This is the revolutionary insight behind movement DNA, or mDNA. The DNA metaphor is intentionally chosen because, like biological DNA, mDNA functions in remarkably similar ways:

- **DNA is found in every living cell** (except the simplest viruses). In the same way, mDNA is present in every genuine expression of church—it's built into the system.

- **DNA contains all the genetic information needed to reproduce the whole organism.** Each element of mDNA carries within it the potential for reproducing the entire movement. Like a seed that contains the whole tree, each part carries the information for the whole.

- **DNA is self-replicating.** It can be passed from one generation to the next, from one context to another, maintaining the essential characteristics while adapting to new environments.

- **DNA carries vital information for healthy reproduction.** When mDNA is present and functioning, it ensures that new expressions of church maintain the essential characteristics that enable movement.

- **DNA can be suppressed or dormant but can be reactivated.** This is perhaps the most hopeful aspect: even if mDNA has been suppressed in favor of less demanding forms of church, it can be recovered and reactivated.

The "m" in mDNA is inserted purely to differentiate it from biological DNA, but the parallel is intentional. Just as DNA does for biological systems, mDNA does for ecclesial systems. It's the presence of a simple, intrinsic, reproducible, central guiding mechanism that is built into the very structure of authentic Jesus movements.

Each element of mDNA is an irreducible and essential component of the system. mDNA is a concentrated area of focus composed of a complex of theology, practice, and organization that, when working together, encodes movement into the very life and structures of the church. Think of each element as one of six originating instincts—built-in instincts that seem to guide the patterns of behavior of authentic Jesus movements.

But here's the crucial insight: mDNA is systemic. Each element is necessary but not sufficient on its own. You can enhance discipleship, but if you don't have APEST culture or organic systems, you won't get movement. You can have all five elements, but if you're missing even one, the system won't activate. This is why churches can be doing many good things—good discipleship, good mission, good community—and still not experience the transformative power of movement.

The six elements work together as an integrated system:

1. **Jesus is Lord** - The central confession that gives meaning to everything else
2. **Disciple-Making** - The irreplaceable task of becoming like Jesus
3. **Missional-Incarnational Impulse** - Going out and going deep, extending and embedding
4. **APEST Culture** - The fivefold ministry environment activating all functions
5. **Organic Systems** - Movemental structures that enable reproduction
6. **Communitas** - Liminal community formed in shared ordeal and purpose

When all six elements are present and functioning together, something remarkable happens: Apostolic Genius emerges. It's not just that you have six good things happening; it's that the system itself becomes something greater than the sum of its parts.

The evidence for mDNA comes from observing remarkable movements throughout history. Every transformative Jesus movement—the early church, the Chinese underground church, the Celtic missions, the Moravians, the Methodists—demonstrates all six elements. This consistency across vastly different times, cultures, and circumstances suggests that mDNA is not culturally bound but represents something inherent to the church's design.

Perhaps the most encouraging aspect of mDNA is that it's latent in every expression of church. The future lies in the womb of the present. The possibility of movement lies at the very heart of every local church, waiting to be awakened. We don't need to create something new; we need to recover what's been forgotten.

## Biblical Foundations

The concept of mDNA finds its roots in the New Testament understanding of the church as a living organism. The Bible uses organic metaphors throughout: the church as a body (1 Corinthians 12), as a vine (John 15), as a building made of living stones (1 Peter 2:5), as a tree (Romans 11), as a temple (Ephesians 2:21).

These organic images suggest that the church is designed to function like a living system, not a machine. The DNA metaphor captures this understanding—the church has genetic information encoded within it that determines how it functions and reproduces.

The six elements of mDNA are all present in the New Testament:
- **Jesus is Lord**: The central confession (1 Corinthians 8:6, Romans 10:9)
- **Disciple-Making**: The Great Commission (Matthew 28:18-20)
- **Missional-Incarnational**: "As the Father has sent me, so I am sending you" (John 20:21)
- **APEST**: The fivefold gifts (Ephesians 4:11-13)
- **Organic Systems**: Body imagery, organic metaphors throughout
- **Communitas**: The early church's experience of shared life and purpose (Acts 2:42-47)

The book of Acts shows mDNA in action—the early church demonstrates all six elements working together, resulting in exponential growth and cultural transformation.

## Historical Development

The concept of mDNA was first introduced in *The Forgotten Ways* (2006) as a way to understand what enables remarkable movements. The framework emerged from studying movements that experienced exponential growth:

- **Early Church**: Grew from 25,000 to 20 million in two centuries
- **Chinese Underground Church**: Grew from 2 million to 120 million in seventy years
- **Celtic Missionary Movement**: Spread Christianity throughout Europe
- **Moravian Movement**: Sent missionaries and formed transformative communities
- **Methodist Movement**: One in thirty English people became Methodists

Each movement, despite different contexts, demonstrated the same six elements. This consistency suggested a pattern—a genetic code that enables movement.

The framework has been developed and applied across multiple publications:
- *The Forgotten Ways* (2006): Initial framework and six elements
- *The Forgotten Ways Handbook* (2012): Practical application and assessment
- *On the Verge* (2011): Application to large churches
- *The Starfish and the Spirit* (2019): Movement dynamics and organic systems

## Theological Significance

mDNA matters because it reveals how God designed the church to function. It shows us that movement isn't an accident or a result of special circumstances, but the natural expression of the church when all elements are present and working together.

The systemic nature of mDNA teaches us that partial solutions won't work. We can't just improve one element and expect movement. We need the whole system functioning together, because it's the system that creates the movement, not individual elements.

mDNA also reveals that the church's potential is not external but internal. The power for transformation is encoded within the church itself, waiting to be activated. This has profound implications for how we understand ecclesiology, missiology, and the nature of Christian community.

The concept challenges us to think systemically rather than programmatically. Instead of asking "What program should we add?" we should ask "What elements of mDNA are missing or suppressed?"

## Practical Implications

For churches and leaders, understanding mDNA means:

1. **Assessment**: Using the mDNA framework to diagnose which elements are present, which are missing, and which are suppressed.

2. **Systemic Thinking**: Recognizing that all six elements must work together. You can't focus on just one or two.

3. **Recovery**: Understanding that mDNA is latent, not absent. The task is recovery and activation, not creation.

4. **Prioritization**: Knowing that while all elements are necessary, some may need more attention than others in your context.

5. **Patience**: Recognizing that activating mDNA takes time. It's not about quick fixes but systemic change.

6. **Simplicity**: Distilling to essence. Movements thrive when the message and structure are simple enough to reproduce.

For individual believers, mDNA means recognizing that you carry the genetic code for movement. Every believer has a role to play, and the DNA is in you, waiting to be activated.

## Common Misunderstandings

1. **"We can focus on just a few elements"**: No, all six are necessary. The system won't activate if any element is missing.

2. **"mDNA is a program we can implement"**: No, it's a genetic code that's already present. The task is recovery and activation.

3. **"Some elements are more important than others"**: While contexts may require different emphasis, all elements are necessary for the system to function.

4. **"mDNA only works in certain contexts"**: The evidence shows mDNA working across vastly different cultures, times, and circumstances.

5. **"We need to create mDNA"**: No, mDNA is latent in every church. We need to recover what's been forgotten.

6. **"mDNA is about church growth"**: While movements do grow, mDNA is about transformation and multiplication, not just numerical increase.
