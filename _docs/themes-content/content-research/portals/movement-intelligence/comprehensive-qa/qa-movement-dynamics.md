## Foundational Questions

### Question 1: How do movements work, and what enables exponential growth?

**Answer:**

Movements work differently from institutions. While institutions grow through addition (adding people to existing structures), movements grow through multiplication (creating new expressions that carry the DNA forward). Exponential growth happens when movements reproduce themselves, creating exponential rather than linear expansion.

**Key Movement Dynamics:**

1. **Multiplication vs. Addition:** Movements multiply by creating new expressions, communities, and contexts that carry the DNA forward. Each new expression can reproduce again, creating exponential growth. Institutions add to existing structures, creating linear growth.

2. **DNA Transmission:** Movements reproduce through DNA transmission—passing on core elements through relationships, stories, and practices rather than through policies and procedures. The DNA is embedded in simple, reproducible forms.

3. **Simple Rules:** Movements use simple rules (core principles) that guide behavior and enable reproduction. These are not complex systems but simple, clear principles that anyone can understand and apply.

4. **Distributed Leadership:** Movements have distributed rather than centralized leadership. Intelligence and capacity are throughout the system, not concentrated at the top. This enables reproduction and adaptation.

5. **Organic Systems:** Movements use organic systems—self-organizing, adaptive, reproducible structures that function like living organisms rather than machines. These systems enable scaling while maintaining DNA.

6. **Network Structure:** Movements function as networks rather than hierarchies. They're connected in distributed ways, enabling rapid communication and reproduction across the network.

7. **Reproducible Practices:** Movements develop practices that are simple, clear, and reproducible. Anyone can learn and pass them on. Complex practices don't reproduce well; simple ones do.

**What Enables Exponential Growth:**

- **DNA Completeness:** All elements of mDNA present and functioning
- **Reproducibility:** Simple, clear, transferable practices and principles
- **Multiplication Focus:** Intentional focus on multiplication, not just addition
- **Distributed Capacity:** Leadership and capacity throughout the system
- **Network Structure:** Connected network enabling rapid spread
- **Organic Systems:** Structures that enable reproduction and adaptation

**Key Points:**
- Movements multiply, institutions add
- DNA transmission enables reproduction
- Simple rules and practices enable multiplication
- Distributed leadership and network structure enable scaling
