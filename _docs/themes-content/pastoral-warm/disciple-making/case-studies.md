# Case studies & stories — Disciple Making

## 1. Jesus and the Twelve
- **Type:** Biblical.
- **Summary:** Jesus’ primary strategy was making disciples—teaching, modeling, sending. He did not build an institution first; he formed people who would carry the movement.
- **Source:** Gospels; *The Forgotten Ways* ch. 5.

## 2. Early church multiplication
- **Type:** Historical.
- **Summary:** Every significant missional movement (early church, China, Methodists) was built on rigorous disciple-making. Multiplication through formation, not only addition through attraction.
- **Source:** story-index 01-HISTORICAL-MOVEMENT-STORIES.

## 3. Contemporary disciplism
- **Type:** Contemporary.
- **Summary:** Churches that reframe evangelism within discipleship and design pathways for long-term formation see deeper transformation and multiplication. Use local or anonymized examples.
- **Source:** faq-017; contemporary examples.
