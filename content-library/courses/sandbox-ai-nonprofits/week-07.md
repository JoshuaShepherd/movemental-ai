## chat_dissonance

## reading

### The question

For every use case that scores well on the four dimensions, one more written question, by a **named senior** not in the experiment:

*What about this might cost us something humanly?*

Not structural risk—that was scoring. Something the dashboard cannot price: **authorship** (whose name is on it—could they defend it as theirs?), **voice** (does it sound like us or the sector median?), **trust** (if the recipient knew how this was made, would the relationship hold?), **formation** (what are we becoming at scale by doing this—either shape can be defensible; neither is neutral).

### Why the flag is separate from scoring

Merging human dimensions into extra columns lets greens **outvote** yellows on voice. The flag **sits on top** of the aggregate; it can reroute even when every scoring dimension is green. Mission-driven orgs fail most on failures scoring cannot see—**trust is not recoverable** on the same timeline as productivity.

### Who writes it

Not the experiment owner; not the peer quality reviewer. Someone with **distance, authority, and long memory**—typically the Safety Owner or executive for the domain. The flag is a **short paragraph**: specific reasons, or explicit “no flag, because …” Blanks are skips; skips recreate the donor-note failure mode.

### What red produces

Reroute: narrower scope, slower rollout, redesign with human-in-the-loop step, or **park**—not reject—until policy or formation catches up. Red does not require winning a debate with the team. The moment red becomes negotiable, the discipline becomes **theater**.

> The structural dimensions can all be green while the human dimensions are red. Merging them lets the greens outvote the reds.

### Handoff

Flagged and scored use cases feed the portfolio—next week you assemble the artifact leadership can stand behind in ten minutes with the board.

## case_study

**Witness — legal aid clinic**

Intake narrative summarization almost shipped. The Safety Owner’s flag named **narrative flattening** as relational violence: clients’ agency lives in nuance. Reroute: **internal** memos only; human-only intake preserved. A grant and a union partnership survived because the org refused a green-score story that could not survive the waiting room. The flag felt expensive for two quarters; it was cheaper than one headline.
