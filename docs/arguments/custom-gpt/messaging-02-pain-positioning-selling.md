# The credibility crisis — 40–60% of online content is AI-generated, and traditional credibility signals no longer work

**Category:** Pain Points | **Strength:** high | **Sources:** 3

## Core Claim

68% of internet users now struggle to distinguish human-created from AI-generated content. Volume, polish, and consistent online presence—traditional credibility signals—no longer indicate expertise because AI can replicate all of them. Movement leaders with hard-won expertise, theological depth, and tested frameworks find themselves invisible in a flood of plausible-sounding AI-generated content. The credibility mechanisms that used to help people find and trust you are breaking down, creating a crisis for anyone whose work depends on trust.

## Gut-Check Question

> Do I recognize this crisis in my own context? Am I seeing it in my audience and organization?

Movement leaders with decades of valuable teaching and proven impact are trapped in systems that don't serve their mission—forced to choose between extraction or invisibility.
