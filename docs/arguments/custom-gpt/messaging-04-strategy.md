# Content length: depth determined by intent, not arbitrary word count

**Category:** Strategy | **Strength:** medium | **Sources:** 1

## Core Claim

There is no magic word count. Pillar pages 2,000-4,000 words; cluster articles 1,200-2,000 words (4x more likely to rank per Ahrefs); quick-answer posts 300-800 words; thought leadership 800-1,500 words. The real rule: match the depth to the intent. A 'what is X' query needs a clear answer, not 3,000 words. A 'complete guide to X' query needs comprehensive coverage. Padding destroys quality signals. Truncating comprehensive topics destroys authority signals. Short content about complex topics makes you look shallow; long content about simple questions makes you look padded.

## Gut-Check Question

> What if you stopped trying to hit a word count and started asking: what's the minimum necessary depth to answer this question completely?

# EEAT as the foundation for discoverability in search and AI

**Category:** Strategy | **Strength:** high | **Sources:** 1

## Core Claim

E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) is a quality framework used by Google and increasingly by AI systems. Experience: first-hand, lived experience with the topic. Expertise: demonstrable knowledge and depth. Authoritativeness: recognition as a go-to source (citations, mentions, links from others). Trustworthiness: transparency, accuracy, clear purpose, secure site. Linking is one primary mechanism by which authoritativeness and trustworthiness become legible. Inlinks signal that other nodes vouch for your content. Outlinks signal you participate in a responsible information ecosystem. Internal links create coherent topology. Cross-domain links in a bounded network (scenius) make visible graphs of mutual vouching.
