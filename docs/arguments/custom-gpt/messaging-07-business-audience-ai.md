# The 90/10 revenue share — aligned incentives where network amplification multiplies everyone's upside

**Category:** Business Model | **Strength:** high | **Sources:** 5

## Core Claim

For movemental leaders: 90% revenue retention enables sustainable full-time movement work, platform ownership creates long-term asset building, network amplification multiplies individual revenue. For Movemental: $1,000 upfront covers initial development, 10% revenue share creates long-term sustainability, network effects multiply individual revenue enough that 10% of amplified revenue exceeds what could be charged upfront. Long-term partnership matters more than short-term fees. We succeed when creators succeed. Volume strategy enables sustainability at revolutionary pricing.

## Alternate Phrasings

### 90/10 revenue share—leaders keep majority of their earned income
**Source:** articles

Leaders keep 90% of their revenue. Movemental takes 10% to maintain and improve the platform. No upfront licensing fees. No per-user charges. No transaction percentages that climb with scale. Typical launch cost is $1,000 instead of $50K-$150K for a custom platform. This model aligns success: the platform only grows when leaders succeed. It refuses extraction; it pursues alignment. The revenue structure itself is a statement: movement work deserves to keep its economics.

### 90/10 Revenue Model: Aligned Incentives
**Source:** business

Movemental keeps 10% of all revenue generated through the platform; creators keep 90%. Revenue sources include book sales, course enrollments, subscriptions, speaking/consulting bookings, and donations. The split is automatic, transparent, and auditable. Why 90/10? Because aligned incentives matter: Movemental succeeds only when creators succeed. If a creator makes $100,000, Movemental makes $10,000. If a creator makes $1,000,000, Movemental makes $100,000. Long-term revenue from successful creators exceeds what could be charged upfront.

### Who Benefits from Network Growth: Everyone
**Source:** business

Network effects multiply everyone's revenue, not just Movemental's. When 100-user network forms, each individual member benefits from 28x amplification, not just Movemental getting 10%. New member joins network, everyone's reach amplifies. Revenue share model ensures this: 90% to leaders means when network grows, individual revenue grows. This is fundamentally different from platform extraction models. Growth benefits network members more than platform. Aligned incentives mean we're all invested in network success, not just platform success.

### Movemental's Economic Model: Day One Numbers
**Source:** business

After platform deployment, leader with $50K first-year revenue keeps $45,000 (90%). Movemental gets $5,000 (10%). Even in year one, both benefit. But network effects multiply: year two revenue might become $100K–$200K, so Movemental revenue becomes $10K–$20K. Long-term partnership creates sustainable economics. Early revenue from successful creators funds platform development and ongoing support. Volume of successful leaders creates profitability at 10%. Economics work through network effects and long-term thinking, not upfront extraction.
