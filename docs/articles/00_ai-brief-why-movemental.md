# AI Brief: Why Movemental Page

**Document type**: Agent and editor brief (not public copy).
**Companion**: `01_copy-deck-why-movemental.md` for section copy with three alternatives each.

---

## 1. What this page must do

- **Context**: The reader arrives skeptical but curious. Something — a conversation, a referral, the fit check — sparked interest. This page converts interest into conviction.
- **Job**: Answer "Why should I care?" and "What makes this different?" through **meaning first, mechanics second**. The reader should feel the weight of the problem before they hear the shape of the response.
- **Non-goals**: This is not pricing, onboarding, or team credibility. It can gesture toward those pages. It must not try to be them.

---

## 2. Success criterion

**If this page works, the reader thinks:**

> "Yes — this needed to exist."

Observable signals:

- They can articulate what is broken (in the world, in publishing, in leadership infrastructure).
- They feel the stakes as **spiritual and human**, not merely technical.
- They understand AI as a tool under authority — neither savior nor threat in isolation.
- They see Movemental as a **coherent ecosystem response**, not another app.
- They feel relief: someone built the thing they sensed was missing.

---

## 3. Voice and guardrails

| Dimension | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Tone** | Clear, unhurried, morally serious. Confident without swagger. Patient with complexity. |
| **Theological posture** | Jesus-shaped formation is load-bearing structure, not decorative trim. Avoid generic "faith-friendly" language that costs nothing. Ground claims in practices: peer verification, truth-telling, patience, embodied presence. |
| **AI posture** | AI intensifies both the crisis and the opportunity. Never imply AI replaces discernment, embodiment, or relational trust. Show it as instrument under communal authority. |
| **Economic claims** | Use "often," "typically," "many leaders" unless citing a single verified source. Revenue splits and build costs are illustrative patterns, not audited figures. |
| **Emotional register** | Name real pain without melodrama. Pair every crisis beat with a constructive counterpart. The reader should feel understood, not harangued. |

## 4. Narrative spine (recommended scroll order)

The page moves through four acts. Each act answers a cluster of questions.

### Act I — The fracture

1. **Open with tension** — Credibility and meaning are under pressure. No soft brand greeting.
2. **Name what changed** — The break is social and spiritual, not a gadget upgrade.
3. **Show why the usual path fails** — Publishing stacks, platforms, incentives are misaligned with movement work.

### Act II — The cost

4. **Name what has been lost** — Depth, coherence, trust, formation.
5. **Explain why it stays stuck** — Structural incentives, isolation, and lack of shared infrastructure.
6. **Raise the stakes** — Beyond efficiency. What happens to truth-telling, to formation, to generations if nothing changes.

### Act III — The reframe

7. **Right-size AI** — Part of the conversation, not the whole story. Opportunity, risk, and governed use.
8. **Ground it in humanity (and Jesus)** — What kind of people and communities are we forming? Formation is the point; technology is the servant.

### Act IV — The response

9. **Movemental's answer** — Infrastructure + network + integrity, in plain language.
10. **The ecosystem** — Properties of the world this creates (compounding credibility, content that travels, leaders who own their work).
11. **What others miss** — Contrarian truth: what we believe that the market doesn't.
12. **Why now** — The technological and cultural window.
13. **Close** — An invitation that feels like the honest next step after understanding, not a sales pitch.

## 6. Motifs to weave (not mandatory jargon — recurring threads)

- **Credibility crisis**: Volume without trust. Fluency mistaken for wisdom. Signals that used to mean something now mean nothing.
- **Scenius**: Genius as networked. Movements emerge in clusters of mutual sharpening, not solo platforms.
- **Content that moves**: Architecture for discovery, reference, and compounding — not one-hit publishing.
- **Ownership vs. extraction**: Leaders deserve agency over voice, audience, and economics. Ownership is stewardship, not empire.
- **Formation over performance**: Tools shaped for long obedience, not growth metrics alone.
- **The middle**: The space between hyperlocal trust and algorithm-mediated reach — where publication, wisdom, and movement work used to live. That middle collapsed. Rebuilding it is the work.

---

## 7. Pain point integration

Pain points should **power** the page, not become a marketing checklist.

**Principles:**
- Lead with 1–2 that land hardest, in the hero or immediately after.
- Weave the rest into narrative sections — not a standalone list.
- Surface three levels: **personal** ("I'm invisible online"), **relational** ("my people can't find what would form them"), and **collective** ("the movement's wisdom doesn't compound").
- Frame as identification, not accusation: "Many leaders find…" and "this is structural, not personal."

**Key pain points to surface:**
- My best work already exists. Almost nobody can find it.
- I have credibility in the room. Online I'm invisible.
- The people I'm called to form can't get what would form them.
- I don't want to create more. I want what I've created to do more.
- I can't afford a $50K platform. I shouldn't have to.

## 8. Risks for AI-generated drafts

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| **Feature dumping before meaning** | Keep §9–10 story-shaped. Defer specs to other pages. |
| **Apocalyptic tone without hope** | Pair every crisis paragraph with a constructive counterpart. |
| **AI as hero or AI as villain only** | Hold both — AI as force multiplier under human and communal authority. |
| **Generic "Christian brand" filler** | Tie theological claims to practices: peer verification, truth-telling, patient formation, embodied presence. |
| **Shame language** | Frame the problem as structural, not personal failure. Good people, bad scaffolding. |
| **Over-reliance on stats** | Numbers are illustrative. Lead with the human reality; let data support, not substitute. |
