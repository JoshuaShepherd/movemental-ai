# Onbuilding: the four-week cohort (author track)

> **Names:** *Onbuilding* is the **primary** program name. *Residency* is **deprecated** for this phase — use only in legacy quotes; new copy must say **onbuilding (cohort)**.  
> **Canon:** [docs/build/notes/onbuilding-4-week-course-SSOT.md](../build/notes/onbuilding-4-week-course-SSOT.md) (this playbook **defers to** the SSOT if they ever differ).

**Duration:** 4 weeks (21 working days) — **only** length. There is no 8- or 12-week variant of onbuilding.  
**Format:** **Cohort-first:** weekly **90-minute** cohort (facilitator-led) is the **spine**. Daily modules are **async** between sessions. **Not** a primarily 1:1 program; optional 1:1 is for unblockers.  
**Prerequisite:** The leader has **already said yes** and **completed pre-onboarding** (fully **provisioned** template-based platform — **not** a blank slate; see SSOT).  
**Outcome:** A **launched** tenant with: **sufficient** evergreen **pillar + cluster** articles, **at least one** full **12-section** primary **pathway**, a **usable** 8-week **course** arc with **at least** one week in **ship-ready** shape, **AI/voice** alignment, and a **coherent** plan — *not* an exhausted article farm. Strategy maps are **enough to launch**; the SSOT defines *enough* vs. post-launch work.

---

## Course philosophy

This is not an information dump about a CMS. It is a formation journey for authors — structured the same way we structure everything on this platform: dissonance first, then depth, then action, then reflection. By the end, you will not just know *how* the platform works. You will know *why* it works this way, and your content will prove it.

The Four Necessities that govern every Movemental course govern this one too:

1. **Dissonance** — Each week opens by surfacing a tension between how content platforms usually work and how this one is designed.
2. **Action** — You will write, publish, and ship real content every week — not hypothetical exercises.
3. **Reflection** — Weekly guided reflection on what you produced and what it taught you about your own voice.
4. **Community** — Cohort: **momentum, clarity, accountability, peer reinforcement**. The **group** is the primary spine, not 1:1.

## Week 1: Vision and Voice

**Theme:** Understand what Movemental is, why it exists, and how your voice fits inside a Christocentric, formation-first platform.

**Weekly dissonance question:** *What if the way you've been distributing your work online is fragmenting the very formation you're trying to catalyze?*

### Day 1 — The problem we're solving

- **Read:** The platform's origin story — why scattered PDFs, isolated YouTube channels, and disconnected course platforms fail movement leaders.
- **Explore:** Walk through the live reference tenant (alanhirsch.com) end to end. Note how content types connect: articles link to pathways, pathways link to courses, courses surface the AI Lab.
- **Concept:** The "digital layer movement leaders were missing" — one home for a leader's body of work where read, form, assess, and explore are unified.
- **Deliverable:** Write a one-paragraph description of *your* body of work as if it were a single library, not a list of products.

### Day 2 — The Christocentric spine

- **Read:** [06 — The Christocentric Spine](06-the-christocentric-spine.md)
- **Concept:** The six dimensions of the Christocentric spine (Core Confession, Allegiance, Gospel Fullness, Obedience, Communal Formation, Sentness). The watermark line: *"We learn this to follow Jesus more deeply and join his mission more faithfully."*
- **Discussion:** How does this theological load-bearing wall show up in *your* work? Where does your teaching naturally anchor to Jesus' lordship and mission?
- **Deliverable:** Draft your own watermark line — the single sentence that determines what belongs on your platform and what does not.

### Day 3 — The five pathways as organizing intelligence

- **Read:** [05 — Formation Journeys: The Pathway Architecture](05-formation-journeys-the-pathway-architecture.md)
- **Concept:** Pathways are not categories. They are thematic doorways — each carries a reframing question, a formation arc, and connects articles, books, courses, and assessments into a single narrative.
- **Study the reference pathways:** Reframation, Metanoia, mDNA (Forgotten Ways), Movement Intelligence, Discipleship. Note the 12-section architecture of each.
- **Deliverable:** Draft 3–5 candidate pathways for your body of work. For each: a name, a reframing question, and a one-sentence description of what it opens.

### Day 4 — Voice, not just content

- **Read:** The five voice markers and their weights:
  - Christocentric Anchoring (30%) — 2–3 explicit Jesus/Christ/Kingdom references
  - Prophetic Intensity (25%) — ~3 reframing questions per 1,000 words
  - Pastoral Warmth (20%) — "we" language; relational, invitational tone
  - Narrative Imagery (15%) — ~8–9 metaphors per 1,000 words (movement, organic, journey, water)
  - Theological Depth (10%) — real terms named and unpacked; historical specificity
- **Exercise:** Take 500 words of your existing published writing. Score it against the five markers. Where are you naturally strong? Where do you default to generic motivational language, corporate tone, or detached academic voice?
- **Deliverable:** Write a 300-word voice calibration memo: "My natural voice leans toward ___ and I need to be intentional about ___."

### Day 5 — The scenius and the business

- **Concept: Scenius** — Brian Eno's term for the intelligence and intuition of a whole cultural scene. Movemental is not a solo publishing platform; it is infrastructure for scenius. Your work joins a network of movement leaders (the M100 founding network).
- **Concept: The four pricing layers** — Subscriptions (Free / Reader / Leader / Scholar), e-commerce (individual books, courses, bundles, assessments), movement leader platforms (white-label multi-tenant), and institutional licensing.
- **Concept: Why this matters for authors** — Your content is not just published; it is *monetized, measured, and connected*. Subscriptions create recurring revenue. Courses create high-value formation products. Assessments create entry points. The AI Lab makes your corpus searchable and conversational.
- **Deliverable:** Draft a one-page content monetization map: which of your works become free (discovery), which become subscriber content, which become premium products (courses, assessments, bundles)?

### Weekly cohort session (90 min)

- Each author presents their watermark line, candidate pathways, and monetization map.
- Peer feedback: Does the watermark line actually filter? Do the pathways feel like doorways or just categories?
- **Week 1 reflection prompt:** *What surprised you about the difference between publishing content and building a formation platform?*

## Week 2: Articles — The Evergreen Architecture

**Theme:** Complete the **strategic article layer** for your body of work, then master the nine-section architecture and publish your first pillar and cluster articles.

**Weekly dissonance question:** *What if most of your "content" is invisible to both search engines and AI systems because it was never structured for discovery?*

### Day 6 — The informational layer (before you write)

**Introduce articles with strategy first.** You are not collecting random posts. You are building an **informational layer** — a full pillar-cluster map that gives search engines and AI systems enough interconnected surface to recognize topical authority.

- **Read:** [01 — Content Strategy for Movement Leaders](01-content-strategy-for-movement-leaders.md) — pillar-cluster model, ~63-article scale across five themes, why the graph matters.
- **Read:** [`_docs/_imported/06-content-leadership-marketing/seo-topic-clusters.md`](../_imported/06-content-leadership-marketing/seo-topic-clusters.md) — **reference inventory**: five pillar pages, cluster articles per pillar, cross-pillar bridge articles, phased rollout. Use it as a **template**, not a verbatim list; adapt titles and counts to *your* themes and corpus.
- **Concept:** A **reference** scale in the example docs can run to **dozens** of planned pieces; **onbuilding** only requires a **sufficient** strategic map for **launch** plus **one** pillar and **one** cluster **live** — *not* writing the entire long-tail library in four weeks. Your map should be **honest** and **complete enough to navigate**, not a commitment to every row of a 60+ row spreadsheet before launch.
- **Concept:** Types in the inventory — **Pillar**, **Cluster**, **Quick-Answer** (where intent fits), **Bridge** (cross-theme). Align tier/word count with [02 — The Evergreen Article Architecture](02-the-evergreen-article-architecture.md).
- **Deliverable:** **Full thematic article inventory** — For each of your candidate themes/pillars (typically 3–5): (1) working title for the **pillar page**, (2) **8–13** cluster/article working titles or clear intents, (3) noted **long-tail** or quick-answer gaps, (4) which **pathway** each pillar primarily feeds. Add a short **cross-theme / bridge** list where concepts connect. Format: table or spreadsheet is fine. This deliverable **completes the article strategy inside the residency**; later publishing is execution, not discovery.

### Day 7 — The evergreen article architecture

- **Read:** [02 — The Evergreen Article Architecture](02-the-evergreen-article-architecture.md)
- **Concept:** The nine mandatory sections in fixed order: Opening Hook, Definition Anchor, Why This Matters, Core Teaching, Common Misunderstandings, Biblical Foundation, What This Means in Practice, How This Connects, Formation Invitation.
- **Concept:** The tier system — Tier 1 Pillar (3,500–4,500 words), Tier 2 Cluster (2,200–3,200 words), Tier 3 Long-tail (1,500–2,200 words).
- **Exercise:** Read two published pillar articles on the reference tenant. Identify each of the nine sections. Note how the Definition Anchor is self-contained and quotable (this is what AI systems pull).
- **Deliverable:** Choose your **first pillar article topic** from your Day 6 inventory — the concept you are most known for, aligned to one row in your thematic map. One sentence on why this pillar must exist before any cluster content.

### Day 8 — Pillar-cluster execution slice (first pathway)

- **Concept:** Your Day 6 inventory is the **whole map**. Today you define the **first execution slice**: the pillar + clusters you will publish first for **one pathway**.
- **Concept:** GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — multiple interconnected pages on a topic increase citation likelihood. Internal links knit pillar ↔ cluster ↔ long-tail.
- **Deliverable:** From your Day 6 inventory, extract a **pillar-cluster execution plan** for your **primary pathway**: your first pillar title (same as Day 7 choice), **3–5 cluster titles** you will write next (after the residency), **2–3 long-tail questions**, and **planned links** between nodes. This connects Week 1 pathway candidates to Week 2–4 publishing order.

### Day 9 — Write the pillar article (draft)

- **Action:** Write the full draft of your pillar article following the nine-section architecture. Hit every section. Hit the word count (3,500–4,500 words).
- **Voice check:** Score your draft against the five voice markers. Ensure Christocentric Anchoring appears in the opening and closing. Ensure at least 3 reframing questions per 1,000 words.
- **AI Lab exercise:** Open the AI Lab and ask it about your topic using the reference tenant's corpus. Note how a well-structured Definition Anchor gets surfaced verbatim. This is what yours needs to do.
- **Deliverable:** Complete pillar article draft (all nine sections).

### Day 10 — Edit, refine, and the "what never appears" list

- **Concept:** The anti-patterns — what never appears in a Movemental article:
  - Corporate consultant tone
  - Detached academic voice
  - Antithesis as primary rhetorical move (always expand, not just negate)
  - Rushing to practice before theology earns it
  - Homiletical openings ("Imagine you're standing on a cliff...")
  - Generic motivational language
  - Stacked scripture (proof-texting)
  - Missing attribution
- **Action:** Edit your pillar article. Read it aloud. Cut every sentence that sounds like a TED talk summary or a seminary paper abstract. Add specificity: dates, numbers, locations, named people, named books.
- **Deliverable:** Revised pillar article ready for peer review.

### Day 11 — Write your first cluster article

- **Action:** Choose the most natural cluster topic from your Day 8 execution plan (aligned to your Day 6 inventory). Write it at Tier 2 (2,200–3,200 words), following the same nine-section architecture.
- **Linking:** Your cluster article should link back to the pillar article (How This Connects section). The pillar article should link forward to this cluster (also How This Connects).
- **Deliverable:** Complete cluster article draft.

### Weekly cohort session (90 min)

- Peer review: Exchange pillar articles. Each reviewer scores against the nine-section checklist and five voice markers. Specific feedback only — no "this is great."
- **Week 2 reflection prompt:** *What did the nine-section structure force you to write that you would have skipped in a blog post?*

## Week 3: Courses and Assessments — Formation, Not Information

**Theme:** Complete your **8-week course scaffold** (whole arc specified in this residency), draft a full transformation week, and understand how assessments create entry points — so the course design is not deferred to “after onboarding.”

**Weekly dissonance question:** *What if your audience doesn't need more of your content — they need a structured environment where your content can actually change them?*

### Day 12 — Transformation over information

- **Read:** [03 — Transformation Over Information](03-transformation-over-information.md)
- **Concept:** The Four Necessities — Dissonance, Action, Reflection, Community. Every week of every course must contain all four. Information alone does not form people. Environments form people.
- **Concept:** The difference between a content course (watch videos, take quiz) and a formation course (productive tension, concrete action in real life, guided reflection, cohort accountability).
- **Exercise:** Think of the last online course you took. Which of the Four Necessities were present? Which were missing? What happened to your formation as a result?
- **Deliverable:** Write a one-paragraph course thesis: "This course exists because ___ and by Week 8 participants will have ___."

### Day 13 — The eight-week formation scaffold

- **Read:** [04 — The Eight-Week Formation Scaffold](04-the-eight-week-formation-scaffold.md)
- **Concept:** The canonical 8-week structure:
  - Week 1: Orientation — context discovery, AI identifies learner situation, community formation
  - Weeks 2–7: Core transformation — one framework dimension per week, full transformation loop (dissonance → teaching → case study → action → reflection → cohort)
  - Week 8: Synthesis — integration, 30/60/90 day plan, commissioning liturgy
- **Concept:** The weekly content flow (Weeks 2–7): Opening video (5 min) → Dissonance (AI conversation) → Main teaching (2,000–3,500 words) → Case study (300–600 words) → Action step (AI-personalized) → Reflection (AI conversation) → Cohort meeting → Exit ticket
- **Study:** Walk through the reference course (The Forgotten Ways) week by week. Note how each module maps one dimension of the framework to the full transformation loop.
- **Deliverable:** Draft your 8-week course outline. For each week: title, the one framework dimension it covers, and the reframing question that opens it.

### Day 14 — The AI companion in courses

- **Concept:** Three AI conversations per week replace static worksheets:
  1. **Dissonance conversation** (before reading) — Opens the learner; surfaces tension between their assumptions and the week's content
  2. **Action conversation** (after reading) — Personalizes one concrete, time-boxed step for their actual context
  3. **Reflection conversation** (after action) — Integrates the experience; turns raw action into insight
- **Concept:** The AI Lab's eight-step context onboarding (geography, language, vocation, experience, tradition, APEST profile, learning style, current focus) means every conversation is contextualized — not generic.
- **Exercise:** Using the AI Lab, have all three conversations for one module of the reference course. Note how context changes the quality of the interaction.
- **Deliverable:** Write the dissonance prompts for Weeks 2–4 of your course — the specific tensions you want the AI to surface before the learner reads your teaching.

### Day 15 — Assessments as formation entry points

- **Concept:** Assessments are not quizzes. They are mirrors — "a way of seeing what God has already placed within you."
- **Study:** The three assessment types: mDNA (community health diagnostic), APEST Profile (fivefold gift identification), 5Q Intelligence Mapping (broader fivefold intelligences).
- **Concept:** Assessments create natural on-ramps into courses and pathways. A person who discovers their APEST profile naturally wants to understand the fivefold ecology — which leads to the 5Q course and the APEST pathway.
- **Exercise:** Does your body of work contain a diagnostic framework? A self-assessment? A typology? If so, it can become an assessment on the platform.
- **Deliverable:** If applicable, draft an assessment concept: what it measures, how many questions, what the results reveal, and which pathway/course it connects to. If not applicable, write a brief explaining why and how you would use existing assessments as entry points to your content.

### Day 16 — Course Week 2 deep draft

- **Action:** Write the complete content for Week 2 of your course:
  - Opening video script (5 min, conversational — not a lecture)
  - Main teaching (2,000–3,500 words, 2–3 subheadings, 1–2 direct quotations from your corpus)
  - Case study (300–600 words — a specific narrative showing the concept at work in a real community, organization, or life)
  - Action step description (what the learner will do, when, with whom)
  - Reflection prompt (what to notice after the action)
- **Deliverable:** Complete Week 2 module draft.

### Weekly cohort session (90 min)

- Each author presents their 8-week outline and reads their Week 2 teaching aloud.
- Cohort feedback: Does the outline cover the whole framework? Does each week have genuine dissonance, or does it ease the learner in too gently? Is the action step concrete enough to actually do?
- **Week 3 reflection prompt:** *What is the difference between teaching your framework and creating an environment where your framework can form someone?*

## Week 4: Integration, Pathways, and Launch Readiness

**Theme:** Connect everything — articles, courses, and assessments — through pathways. Prepare for launch.

**Weekly dissonance question:** *What if the most important thing you build this week is not a piece of content but the connective tissue between all the content you've already built?*

### Day 17 — Pathway architecture deep dive

- **Concept:** The 12-section pathway architecture:
  1. **Hero** — Image, title, tagline, anchor verse
  2. **Provocation** — Reframing question + productive tension
  3. **Overview** — 5 bullets of theological invitation (not feature list)
  4. **Core Model** — Framework presentation with visual
  5. **Scripture Thread** — 4–6 passages woven together (not proof-texted)
  6. **Case Studies** — Real-world narratives (historical + contemporary)
  7. **Formation Practices** — 10–15 minute actionable practice
  8. **Curated Resources** — Featured book + featured course from your corpus
  9. **AI Lab Invitation** — Explore conversationally with pathway-specific context
  10. **FAQs** — Common questions + "What this is NOT" distortion warnings
  11. **Distortion Warnings** — 3 ways the concept can be misappropriated
  12. **Invitation** — Warm, Christ-centered closing with personal, communal, and missional outcomes
- **Study:** Walk through one reference pathway (e.g., Reframation or mDNA) and identify all 12 sections. Note how sections 10–11 (FAQs and Distortion Warnings) protect the concept from misuse.
- **Deliverable:** Select your primary pathway (from Week 1 candidates) and draft sections 1–3 (Hero, Provocation, Overview).

### Day 18 — Build the pathway

- **Action:** Draft sections 4–12 of your primary pathway.
- **Key requirement for Section 8 (Curated Resources):** Link to your pillar article (from Week 2) and your course (from Week 3). This is where the platform's connective tissue becomes visible — a pathway is not a page, it is a hub that routes people to the right content for their formation stage.
- **Key requirement for Section 11 (Distortion Warnings):** Name three specific ways your concept gets co-opted, flattened, or weaponized. This is prophetic work — it protects your readers from the most common misreadings.
- **Deliverable:** Complete pathway draft (all 12 sections).

### Day 19 — The content ecosystem map

- **Action:** Create a visual map of your content ecosystem showing how everything connects:
  - Your pathways (the organizing intelligence)
  - Your **Day 6 thematic article inventory** and published pillar/cluster pieces (the discovery layer)
  - Your course (the formation journey)
  - Your assessment, if applicable (the entry point)
  - The AI Lab (the companion layer across everything)
- **Concept:** Each content type serves a different function in the formation journey:
  - **Articles** = discovery and depth (SEO/GEO surface area; the way new people find you)
  - **Books** = scholarship and reference (the canonical corpus; what the AI Lab is grounded in)
  - **Courses** = structured transformation (the environment where formation happens)
  - **Assessments** = self-knowledge and on-ramp (the mirror that creates motivation)
  - **Pathways** = integration and navigation (the story that connects everything)
  - **AI Lab** = personalized companion (the conversation layer that contextualizes everything to the individual)
- **Deliverable:** Completed content ecosystem map + a prioritized publishing roadmap: what ships first, second, third, and why.

### Day 20 — Platform tooling and publishing workflow

- **Concept:** The six-layer type safety chain — how content flows from database schema through Zod validation, services, API routes, React hooks, and UI components. You don't need to code this, but you need to understand that your content enters a system with integrity guarantees.
- **Concept:** The tenant config system — all tenant-specific strings live in `tenant.config.ts`. Your platform instance is configured here: name, tagline, themes, feature flags, chat text, hero content.
- **Hands-on:** Walk through the publishing workflow for articles (markdown ingestion → database → article detail page → AI Lab indexing). Understand how `pnpm ingest:articles` processes your markdown into the platform.
- **Hands-on:** Walk through the course publishing workflow (course scaffold → lesson content → cohort setup → AI conversation configuration).
- **Deliverable:** Publish your pillar and cluster to **production** (or your tenant’s live publish path). Verify they render correctly, link to each other, and appear in the **AI** layer’s awareness. **Onbuilding** ends with a **publicly usable** platform — *not* content trapped in staging.

### Day 21 — Launch plan and commissioning

- **Action:** Finalize all content for review:
  - Full thematic article inventory (final)
  - Pillar article (final, published)
  - Cluster article (final, published)
  - Course 8-week outline (complete, with Week 2 fully drafted)
  - Primary pathway (all 12 sections drafted)
  - Content ecosystem map and publishing roadmap
  - Monetization map (from Week 1, updated)
- **Concept: The M100 and scenius network** — You are not launching alone. Your platform instance joins a network of movement leaders. Cross-pollination between tenants (shared pathways, guest articles, co-taught courses) is part of the long-term vision. Your content ecosystem plugs into something larger.
- **Deliverable:** A one-page launch brief: what is **live**, what is **deferred** (with dates), and your **90-day** rhythm **post–onbuilding** (not a commitment to new volume for its own sake).

### Weekly cohort session (90 min) — Commissioning

- Each author presents their content ecosystem map and launch brief.
- Cohort celebration: read each author's watermark line (from Day 2) and opening promise (pillar article hook) aloud.
- Mutual commissioning: the cohort speaks over each author's work — "This is ready. This will form people. Go."
- **Final reflection prompt:** *What did you learn about your own body of work by being forced to organize it as a formation platform rather than a content archive?*

## Success criteria

An author **completes onbuilding** when:

1. **Vision fluency** — They can articulate the Christocentric spine, the Four Necessities, and the pathway architecture without notes, and explain why each matters for formation (not just content delivery).
2. **Voice calibration** — Their published articles score above threshold on all five voice markers, with no anti-pattern violations.
3. **Article strategy complete** — A **launch-sufficient** thematic plan is submitted; at least **one** pillar and **one** cluster are **published** and live on the tenant.
4. **Course designed** — A complete 8-week outline exists with at least one fully drafted module, including AI conversation prompts.
5. **Pathway integrated** — A primary pathway draft connects their articles, course, and (if applicable) assessment into a single formation journey.
6. **Business clarity** — They have a monetization map and 90-day publishing roadmap that aligns with the platform's pricing layers.
7. **Scenius orientation** — They understand their work as part of a network, not a solo act, and can name at least two cross-pollination opportunities with other tenants or authors.
