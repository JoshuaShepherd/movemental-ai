# Capture, transform, sustain? Repurposing, hour budgets, and what the evidence actually rewards

**Related:** [11-voice-preservation.md](./11-voice-preservation.md) (voice, disclosure, 70/30 ethics), Prompt **03** (publishing economics), Prompt **10** (fragmentation), Prompt **00** (corpus audit)  
**Date:** 2026-04-12

---

## Executive summary

Movemental’s core intuition is directionally sound: **movement leaders already produce high-value speech** that can seed **durable digital objects**—articles, curricula, discussion guides—if the pipeline respects **genre**, **rights**, and **editorial responsibility** (`C-F05`, `articles-039`). Industry practice and institutional publishers have long recycled primary material into derivative formats.

The harder public claims are **quantitative** and **total-workflow**. Orbit Media’s 2025 survey of **808** marketers finds a single typical blog article already takes **just under three and a half hours** to produce—before email, social, course maintenance, or community labor—while marketers who report using AI to write **entire** articles are the **least likely** cohort to report “strong results.” That single benchmark does not “disprove” Movemental, but it **tightens the margin** on any promise that **five hours** covers a **multi-channel platform** unless scope is aggressively defined.

The **70/30** split remains best understood as **governance** (“machines expand; humans judge and sign”) rather than an optimized ratio validated across domains; deeper treatment sits in [11-voice-preservation.md](./11-voice-preservation.md). **GEO** research shows why **visibility in generative answers** is now a **different game** from classic rankings: models synthesize across sources (`arxiv:2311.09735`). Movemental should market **stewardship of intellectual property and pastoral trust**, not **magic throughput**.

---

## 1. The promise in plain language

Movemental asks leaders to believe three overlapping things:

1. **Legitimacy** — Turning sermons, talks, and trainings into articles and learning artifacts is **faithful reuse**, not cheating (`articles-039`).  
2. **Efficiency** — The same underlying labor can **populate** a website, email, and courseware with **bounded weekly time** (`research-025`).  
3. **Quality at scale** — Assisted drafting—often narrated as **70% AI / 30% human** (`research-029`)—can hit **credible public bars** for SEO, teaching, and brand.

Those are **different claim classes**. The first is chiefly **ethical and vocational**. The second and third require **operational evidence** and **cohort metrics**, not analogy to consumer creators alone.

## 2. What “repurposing” means in the wild (and what it does not prove)

### 2.1 Strategy literature: modular assets, not copy-paste

The Content Marketing Institute’s long-running guidance treats repurposing as **planning problem**: build “Lego” assets, favor evergreen cores, and break silos so downstream channels inherit upstream thinking ([CMI, *Remix, recycle, repurpose*](https://contentmarketinginstitute.com/content-operations/remix-recycle-repurpose)). That is **sound operations design**. It is **not** a controlled study that repurposed posts **outrank** net-new essays on identical topics.

Influencer-era “content pyramids” (many derivatives from one pillar) are likewise **distribution heuristics**. They explain **reach**, not **theological proportion** or **homiletical fit**.

### 2.2 Performance data: time, AI, and “strong results”

Orbit Media’s 2025 blogger survey is unusually helpful because it links **time investment**, **AI use patterns**, and **self-reported performance** in one dataset ([Orbit Media, 2025 blogging statistics](https://www.orbitmedia.com/blog/blogging-statistics/)). Key lines for Movemental:

- **~3.5 hours** per typical article (single asset, writing-focused question).  
- **21%** of respondents report “strong results,” used as the report’s internal benchmark.  
- **AI to write complete articles** skews toward the **weakest** performance association in their narrative; **AI as editor** looks healthier.

**Interpretation:** assisted **revision** and **structuring** align with a Movemental-shaped pipeline; **unattended auto-publishing** does not.

### 2.3 Religious-leader specifics

Peer-reviewed tables on “sermon → article conversion rates” are scarce. Adjacent **vocational** data matter: NPR’s reporting on Barna figures notes **12%** of Protestant clergy comfortable with AI **writing** sermons versus **43%** seeing merit for **research and prep** ([NPR, Jul 17, 2025](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/17/nx-s1-5468637/clergy-grapple-with-the-ethics-of-using-ai-to-write-sermons)). That split supports Movemental **if** the product story stays on **capture, study, drafting support, and disclosure**—not silent substitution in **proclamation**.

## 3. The hour budget: where five hours collides with arithmetic

Five hours per week is plausible for a **narrowly scoped** program (for example: **one** polished longform piece plus **light** social/email touch-ups **if** transcription and CMS work are delegated). It is **not** a default realistic budget for **articles + courses + email + social + community + “AI Lab”** unless Movemental defines **minimum viable surfaces** upfront.

Industry surveys that ask only “how long did writing take?” systematically **omit**:

- consent and **rights** review for stories and music;  
- **fact-check** and Scripture accuracy;  
- **structural rewrite** from oral monologue to readerly essay;  
- **metadata**, internal links, accessibility, and **canonical** URL strategy;  
- **reply handling** and pastoral follow-up tied to public teaching.

ConvertKit/Kit’s recurring “State of the Creator Economy” reports and Buffer’s burnout resources both point to **multi-platform pressure** as a burnout driver ([Kit 2024 report hub](https://partners.convertkit.com/2024report); [Buffer on creator burnout](https://buffer.com/resources/creator-burnout/)). Movemental should therefore treat `research-025` as a **disciplined target** for a **defined slice** of work, backed by **instrumented pilot data**, not as a universal promise for every leader’s full stack.

## 4. The 70/30 rule: language that matches the evidence

See [11-voice-preservation.md §4](./11-voice-preservation.md#4-humanai-co-writing-and-the-7030-rule): there is **no** stable cross-domain optimum for “exactly 70% machine / 30% human.” There **is** a cluster of findings about **metacognitive laziness**, **motivation**, and **trust after disclosure** that rewards **human accountability** on publish-tier text.

Orbit’s 2025 correlation story adds a marketing-specific caution: **full AI drafting** tracks with **worse** self-reported outcomes in their sample. That does not condemn assisted workflows; it **warns** against marketing that sounds like **“the machine carries the load and you dabble.”**

**Public phrasing that fits the evidence:**

- **“AI proposes structure and variants; humans correct, thicken, and take responsibility.”**  
- **“Pastoral publish gate”** — nothing ships without a named human reviewer (`articles-019` family).

## 5. Theology and integrity: stewardship vs shortcut

### 5.1 Stewardship frame

Framing repurposing as **stewardship of teaching labor** is coherent in traditions that prize **faithful transmission** and **access**: spoken word reaches one room; careful writing can serve diaspora, newcomers, and asynchronous learners—**if** the written form is not a **degraded simulacrum** of the live event.

### 5.2 Homiletical realism: sermon ≠ essay

Homileticians from **Craddock** (inductive, listener-shaped discourse) through **Long** (witness of preaching) and **Taylor** (embodied, situational authority) are not a statistical panel, but they agree on a design fact easy to forget in product demos: **oral persuasion** and **readerly argument** differ in rhythm, repetition, and accountability. Turning a sermon into an article is often **re-authoring**, not **transcription**.

That supports Movemental editorially: charge for **theological edit**, not just **grammar edit**.

## 6. Discovery risks: SEO duplication, thin syndication, GEO

### 6.1 Duplicate and near-duplicate URLs

Google’s public documentation emphasizes **canonicalization** across similar URLs rather than campfire stories about automatic “duplicate penalties” ([Search Central, duplicate URL consolidation](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/consolidate-duplicate-urls)). Practical Movemental guidance:

- pick a **canonical** home for the core teaching;  
- make derivatives **meaningfully additive** (study questions, citations, tightened logic), not transcript spam;  
- align pages with **E-E-A-T** signals (authorship, bios, dated revisions, transparent sourcing).

### 6.2 GEO: synthesized answers change what “winning” means

Aggarwal et al.’s **GEO** framework (`arxiv:2311.09735`) models generative engines as **multi-source summarizers** and shows that **black-box optimization** tactics can move **measured visibility** substantially **within their benchmark**—domain dependent. For Movemental, the honest upshot is dual:

- **Owned longform** still matters for **depth**, **citations**, and **training corpora** you control.  
- **Referral traffic** from generative interfaces is **less deterministic** than classic SERP clicks; product and analytics need **new success metrics**.

## 7. Failure modes when scale outruns discernment

When organizations industrialize repurposing without standards, recurring patterns appear across sectors (and rhyme with AI-slop critiques):

- **Voice drift** and **generic theology** as prompts accumulate.  
- **Audience fatigue** when the same anecdote hits every channel in one week.  
- **Factual and citation errors** amplified by confident models.  
- **Trust events** when undisclosed assistance surfaces—Pew’s 2025 disclosure experiments are a cold shower for any “hide the AI” strategy ([Pew, Sep 17, 2025](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/09/17/from-political-speeches-to-songs-how-would-americans-react-if-they-found-out-ai-was-involved/)).

## 8. Recommendations: what to say where

### Marketing (top of funnel)

- Lead with **stewardship** and **editorial seriousness**, not throughput math.  
- Replace absolute “five hours for everything” with **scoped scenarios** (“one flagship essay + two touchpoints,” etc.).  
- Pair any demo (`business-082`) with **visible human edit marks** and **time-on-task** transparency.

### Onboarding (first 30 days)

- Require a **workflow map** that lists hidden hours; compare to **their** baseline, not a fictional average.  
- Set **minimum quality gates** per artifact class (article vs devotional vs social batch).

### Contracts / SLA language

- Bind deliverables to **reviewer roles**, **revision rounds**, and **incident response**—not raw word counts from transcripts.  
- Specify **canonical URL** policy and **rights** workflow.

### Instrumentation (replace story with cohort data)

Track **editorial minutes per publish**, **human edit delta**, **time from recording to live URL**, **GSC vs AI-referral splits**, and a lightweight **pastoral incident log**. After two quarters, Movemental can speak from **its own** distribution, not only from industry surveys.

---

## Closing posture

The **capture-and-transform** thesis is **credible** as vocational technology: leaders should not have to **re-invent** truth weekly for every file format. The **risk** is **over-quantified marketing** that collides with **single-channel benchmarks** (already ~3.5 hours per article), **multi-channel burnout dynamics**, and **generative search** economics. Keep the moral claim; **tighten** the hour claim; **reframe** 70/30 as **accountability design**; and **measure** the pipeline you actually run.
