Creative Approach

Most platform explainers open with the product. This one opens with a person — and that's the advantage. Alan Hirsch's body of work is staggering: 20 published books, 267 chapters, roughly one million words written before AI existed. That weight is the hook. The gap between that weight and its digital absence is the tension.

The video builds in four acts, each expanding the frame:

  • Act 1 — The Weight: Establish Alan's offline legacy. Counting-up numbers create visceral scale. Then the pivot: almost none of it was online.
  • Act 2 — The Build: Show what Movemental did with Alan's corpus. Product reveals earn more weight when they follow the gap — don't show the platform before the problem.
  • Act 3 — The Process: Zoom out. Brad Brisco as proof of repeatability. Walk through the 5-step process. This isn't a custom build — it's a platform.
  • Act 4 — The Network: Vision shot. Movement of movements. Land on economics last (90/10) — lead with mission, close with the model.

Visual language: Follows the established brand tokens — warm charcoal backdrop, gold accents, Inter/Lora typography, abstract silhouette figures. Running counters for statistics. No live action required (though Alan portrait stills or video clips would elevate Act 1 significantly).

Key creative decision: Alan's voice appears via on-screen quotes (Lora italic, gold) — same treatment as the course explainer. If audio of Alan reading any of these passages exists, swap one or two quotes from text to audio for maximum emotional impact.

21 seconds · 50 words

Narration
Then we learned how Alan writes. Not what he says — how he says it. We measured his metaphor density. His question frequency. His ratio of "we" to "you" to "I." We identified five voice markers with specific weights. This wasn't AI guessing. This was humans reading, measuring, and naming.

The rings are like a fingerprint — unique to this voice, measured from this corpus.

Production Notes

  • Narrator: Movemental voice (TBD). Documentary tone — measured, warm, confident. Not startup hype. Not pastoral. Think Vox explainer meets Criterion Collection voiceover.
  • Alan's presence: On-screen quotes only (2 across 13 scenes). If audio of Alan exists for either quote, use it in H02 for maximum emotional impact in Act 1.
  • Brad's presence: No quotes attributed to Brad. His story is told by the narrator. If Brad records a short testimonial clip ("Here's what changed for me..."), insert between H09 and H10.
  • Statistics: All numbers sourced from the Movemental content audit docs: 20 books, 267 chapters, 2,901 stories, 5,090 dates, 473 locations, 2,332 orgs, 16,762 Goodreads ratings. Verify latest figures before final script lock.
  • Act transitions: 1→2 needs a 2-second pause (the "locked library" beat lingers). 2→3 is a smooth zoom-out. 3→4 is the fastest transition — momentum building.
  • Color: Follow brand-tokens.md. Dark backgrounds, gold accent for quotes and stats, warm neutrals for narration text. Brad's platform uses teal accent to distinguish from Alan's gold.
  • Word count note: "Roughly one million words" is a conservative estimate. 20 books averaging 50,000–80,000 words each = 1.0–1.6M words from books alone, plus articles, talks, and assessments. The ~1M figure is defensible and understates rather than overstates.
  • What this video is NOT: Not a product demo. Not a feature tour. Not a pitch deck read aloud. It's a story that happens to explain a platform. If it doesn't feel like a documentary short, it's not right.
