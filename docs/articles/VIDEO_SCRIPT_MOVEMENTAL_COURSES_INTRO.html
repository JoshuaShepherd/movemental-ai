Animated Explainer · Video Script + Storyboard

Introduction to Movemental Transformative Courses

A 5-minute animated explainer. Provocative cold open, data-driven credibility beat, then the Movemental approach to formation. AI-generated female VO, motion graphics, NB2 key frames.
Duration: ~5 minutes
Format: Animated explainer
Narrator: AI female VO (ElevenLabs)
Word Count: ~870 words
Use: Courses landing / onboarding / social

Voice Direction

ElevenLabs voice: Use Rachel or Sarah — warm, clear, mid-range. Not corporate or newscaster. Think editorial documentary: confident and inviting, with enough warmth to feel personal but enough authority to carry hard data and framework explanations. Pacing is unhurried. Let the provocation land. Slight pauses before key phrases. The opening line should feel like a statement, not a shout — calm certainty.

Visual Style

Motion graphics + NB2 key frames. Clean kinetic typography for the cold open provocation, data stats, and framework concepts. NB2-generated atmospheric images at emotional beats — warm, painterly, desaturated gold/earth palette matching the Movemental brand. No stock footage. No talking head. Transitions: smooth cross-dissolves and gentle parallax movement on still frames. Ambient electronic underscore throughout (warm, not clinical).

Tone

This is the platform speaking, not Alan. The narrator is a guide — clear, warm, confident. She opens with a provocation that earns trust through honesty (we're a digital platform admitting digital courses don't work). She respects the theological weight of the content without performing it. No prophetic intensity; that's Alan's register. She's the person who names the problem, shows the data, then walks you to the door and shows you what's different inside.

Data Citations

Sources for the statistics used in Act 1. All are peer-reviewed or institutional research.

Stat Source Detail
"5% average completion rate" Jordan, K. (2015). IRRODL 16(1). "Massive Open Online Course Completion Rates Revisited: Assessment, Length and Attrition." Meta-analysis of 221 MOOCs. Median completion: 12.6%, but free/open courses average 5–7%.
"96% never finish" HarvardX / MITx Working Papers (2014–2018) Ho et al. "HarvardX and MITx: Four Years of Open Online Courses." Of 5.63M enrolments, ~4% earned certificates. 96% figure widely cited; exact rate varies by course type.
"Nobody measures transformation" Editorial observation No major MOOC platform (Coursera, edX, Udemy) publishes transformation or behavioural change metrics. Industry standard KPIs are: completion, NPS, knowledge assessment scores, re-enrolment. This is a verifiable gap in the literature.
