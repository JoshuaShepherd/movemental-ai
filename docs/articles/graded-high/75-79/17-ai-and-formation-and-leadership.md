# Chapter 17: AI, Formation, and Leadership

**Accurate** is not always **formative**.

Picture **formation time** after Easter—youth room, adult class, or nonprofit volunteer huddle: a leader pastes an assistant-generated discussion guide into the room’s thread—clean transitions, plausible questions—and halfway through a teenager says quietly, “Did anyone *live* this week, or did we just read about it?” The line is not anti-tool; it is **formation’s** line. A flawless FAQ about spiritual disciplines can still **malform** if it substitutes **consumption** for **community**, **information** for **obedience**, **screens** for **presence**. Formation asks a harder test than content:

> **Does this process, over time, make people more like Christ in verifiable ways—together?**

Generative AI can **resource** that process; it cannot **be** the process.

## Formation vs. information (precision without sentiment)

Jack Mezirow’s **transformative learning** and related adult-education theory help explain why **dissonance + reflection** matter—but do not treat those citations as permission to brand your eight-week cohort as a **validated psychological battery**. The **Four Necessities** language some formation designs use—**dissonance, action, reflection, community**—is a **design heuristic** grounded in plausible theory, not a lab-proven instrument.

James K. A. Smith’s **cultural liturgies** remind us that **habits form desire beneath argument**—so the habit of “click, receive, feel inspired” competes with the habit of **showing up late to rehearsal because someone needed a ride**.

Keep **screen harms** and **LLM harms** analytically distinct: TikTok timelines and chatbots both tug attention; they are not the same **failure mode**. Collapsing them weakens your **formation** diagnosis.

## When AI helps formation infrastructure

- **Accessible readings** for low-literacy neighbors—**human curated**.  
- **Language support** for multilingual communities—**human reviewed**.  
- **Scheduling and logistics** so volunteers sleep.  
- **Draft discussion guides**—**teacher edited** for local context.  
- **Use-case logs**—not glamour work, but **memory**: what you tried, who reviewed it, what you refused, so the next cohort is not reinventing the same mistakes. (Private documentation is different from **publishing** voice you have not owned; chapter seven’s maturity map keeps that distinction explicit.)

## When AI undermines formation

These are extensions of the **refusals** named in **chapter thirteen**; here the lens is **formational outcome**, not only ethics.

- **Simulated one-to-one care** marketed as companionship.  
- **Personalized “spiritual direction”** without oversight.  
- **Auto-generated discussion-group curricula** nobody has lived.

## Leadership formation: the mirror test

Leaders are **high-leverage** and **high-risk** users. The **performance–metacognition gap** Fernandes et al. report in *Computers in Human Behavior* (2026, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chb.2025.108779)—sketched more fully in **chapter seven**—means tool-assisted gains can coincide with **miscalibrated self-assessment**. That is especially dangerous when **followers cannot see your rough drafts**: they infer competence from output, not from the chain of review behind it.

Practices that help:

- **Named mentors** who can say “that post sounded unlike you.”  
- **Sabbath from publish** rhythms.  
- **Public disclosure** defaults for high-trust channels (the disclosure craft in **chapter ten** applies here as **integrity infrastructure**, not only comms hygiene).

## Churches: discipleship rooms

Parish and nonprofit delivery **rhyme** but are not identical—logistics, liability, and volunteer culture differ. In my own notes I treat **churches as strongly formation-shaped** while the **full institutional playbook** for church-specific delivery is still being written; the **moral bar** in this chapter does not wait on a finished manual.

- **Youth:** AI-assisted **study tools** alongside **adult relationship**—never replacing **adult circle leaders**.  
- **New members:** translation help **yes**; **substitute community** no.  
- **Counseling triage:** if it is not a human with office hours, it is not **ongoing counseling**—it is triage at best.

**Parish vignette (Wednesday):** A **new-member lunch** runs long; someone asks a newcomer from another country what “calling” meant in their former congregation. Whoever is leading could let a phone summary answer—or could close the laptop, ask a follow-up question only a human who has watched the family for three weeks would ask, and **carry silence** without filling it. AI might later help draft a welcome email; it cannot **host** that silence without stealing something only bodies trade.

## Programs are not “eight weeks because science said so”

Eight weeks (or any arc) is a **product choice**—defensible **formationally**, not because a journal proved eight beats seven.

## Movement leaders who design learning

**Even if you never lead a local congregation**, you **architect formation** through books, cohorts, and platforms. The same bar applies: **does your stack increase embodied practice** or only **increase downloads**?
