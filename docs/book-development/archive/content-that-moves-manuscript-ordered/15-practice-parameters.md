# Chapter 15: Practice Parameters

**Chapter fourteen** named what must stay human in formation and care. **This chapter** consolidates **operating defaults**—not eternal law. Models will change; your board might not. Good parameters are **written in pencil**, reviewed quarterly.

## 70 / 30 (governance heuristic, not science)

Treat **70/30** as a **fence**, not a finding: roughly **more human judgment than unreviewed machine text** on publish-tier material. I am not leaning on any single survey as scripture; for **time-on-task**, **full-model drafting**, and **self-reported outcomes** in one marketing-style blogging study (Orbit Media, 2025), see **chapter six**—where that evidence first appears in this book. For how the same discipline maps to a **repeatable publish chain**, see **chapter sixteen**. The point here is posture, not a statistic.

Public language: **AI expands and structures; humans judge, correct, and take responsibility.**

## Agent roles: assistants, archivists, translators—**not authors**

**Assistants** propose outlines, counterarguments, edits—you **own** acceptance.

**Archivists** index, tag, retrieve—truth still lives in **curated sources**, not embeddings alone.

**Translators** adapt format (sermon → article) while you **re-assert** thesis sentences in your voice.

**Authors** of moral and theological weight remain **human**. COPE-style norms are explicit: **AI tools are not authors**; people are accountable for what ships ([COPE guidance](https://publicationethics.org/guidance/cope-position/authorship-and-ai-tools)).

For movement leaders in public view, **approval** of AI-affected voice still belongs with the **leader** and whatever small circle they trust—tools do not inherit your reputation.

## A workable spectrum (compressed)

Instead of eight micro-levels, think **three bands**:

1. **Human-led, machine-assisted** — research, editing, structure; you are visibly the source.  
2. **Human-directed, machine-generated** — model drafts from your brief; heavy redraft; disclosure.  
3. **Machine-led** — **red zone** for ministry-publication defaults; at most R&D sandbox, never silent ship.

Different **domains** ride different bands: admin comms may tolerate band 2; **liturgy, pastoral care, counseling** stay band 1 or human-only—not because models are “wicked,” but because the goods at stake are **practice-shaped** in ways I named in **chapter fourteen** (prayer, presence, slow moral time).

One organizational parameter I keep seeing omitted: during **structured experimentation** (the phase I unpack in **chapter seven**), some teams adopt an explicit **moratorium on publishing** AI-affected or AI-generated content—internal drafts and documented learnings only—until governance and reviewer roles exist. Panic after a public mistake is expensive; a written publish rule is cheaper.

## Movement leaders: content flywheel (example only)

A **compressed** example only—the full capture → verify → publish chain lives in **chapter sixteen**:

Talk → transcript → **human** thesis → machine-assisted shape → **human** rewrite → named reviewers → publish → network amplification (mutual links, shared courses where integrity allows). The principle: **workflow > sparkle**.

## Churches: four rooms on one campus

- **Worship:** lyrics projection OK; **prayer voice** stays human.  
- **Discipleship:** AI-generated discussion prompts **reviewed** by a teacher who knows the group.  
- **Comms:** templated newsletters **checked** for tone-deaf fluency.  
- **Admin:** scheduling bots—**celebrate** them.

## Nonprofits: triage table

| Artifact            | Typical band | Non-negotiable reviewer      |
|---------------------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Grant narrative     | 1–2         | Program + finance            |
| Donor thank-you     | 1           | Relationship owner           |
| Impact blog         | 1–2         | M&E lead + comms             |
| Internal minutes    | 1           | Still: confidentiality       |

## Parameters expire

When vendors ship new models, **re-run** a red-team afternoon: hallucination checks, citation checks, voice drift checks. Parameters are **liturgy**, not wallpaper—they should **advance** with your maturity stage (again, **chapter seven**), not float as a one-off workshop poster.
