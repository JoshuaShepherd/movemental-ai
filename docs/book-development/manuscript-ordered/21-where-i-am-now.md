# Chapter 21: Where I Am Now

I want to end where the preface began: with the story, and with the fact that it is not over.

I do not have this figured out. I am still learning. I am still wrong about things. I am still discovering what I do not know. I said that at the start, and it is still true. The grappling is not finished. The trail keeps going.

**What I am holding to:** AI is both problem and solution; credibility in an AI age is rebuilt through **networks and relationships**, not through competing with machines on volume or polish; voice preservation, transparency, and boundaries are not optional extras; formation happens in embodied life, and what we do online should **support** that, not replace it; slowing down and discerning are responsible; you do not have to have it all figured out to lead well.

**What I am holding to behind the scenes:** the unglamorous work I described in the preface turned out to be **what everything else rested on**—consolidation, traceability, places where enthusiasm becomes something you can verify. I do not think you can argue for transparency in public while your own intellectual house stays permanently scattered. I am still building that house. I am still learning what “enough order” looks like without pretending the mess never existed.

**What I am still figuring out:** how fast the technology will change; how the maturity model lands in contexts unlike mine; how to serve movement leaders, nonprofits, and churches with real parity without flattening real differences in how formation shows up (especially in church-shaped spaces, where my honest answer is still forming); how to tell the Movemental and Alan Hirsch story in a way that serves you without collapsing into pitch or polish; how to keep this book honest and useful as the landscape shifts. I do not have final answers. I have a framework, a set of refusals and freedoms, and a commitment to keep learning **with** others—not only *about* them.

So I am not closing the loop by handing you a master key. I am closing it by admitting: **the story continues.** I am still staying. I am still learning. I invite you to do the same—not to have it all figured out, but to stay on the trail, to experiment within bounds, to discern, to slow down when you need to, to refuse what you must refuse, and to use what you are free to use, alongside others who are figuring it out too.

We are in this together. The work ahead belongs to leaders who embed their labor in **networks of verified humans** while using AI as **amplification, not replacement**. You do not have to do that alone. You do not have to do it perfectly. You do have to **stay**—and keep discerning.

The preface borrowed Frost to name what happened in my story. I will end here without repeating the poem: the road is still bending, I am still walking it with friends, and that companionship is still the part I do not want to pretend I can replace with a better interface.
