## How Trademark Overlap Actually Works

### The Legal Standard: "Likelihood of Confusion"

Trademark law doesn't prohibit all uses of similar names. It prohibits uses that create a **"likelihood of confusion"** among consumers. Courts analyze this using the **DuPont factors** (from *In re E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co.*):

1. **Similarity of marks** (sound, appearance, meaning)
2. **Similarity of goods/services** 
3. **Similarity of trade channels**
4. **Similarity of target customers**
5. **Strength of the prior mark**
6. **Evidence of actual confusion**
7. **Intent to trade on another's reputation**
