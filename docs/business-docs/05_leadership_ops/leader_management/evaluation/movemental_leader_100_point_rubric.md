# Movemental Leader 100-Point Assessment Rubric
## Comprehensive Evaluation Framework for Platform Inclusion

**Version**: 1.0  
**Date**: September 24, 2025  
**Based on**: Alan Hirsch (100/100) and Brad Brisco (88/100) strategic analysis

---

## Overview & Philosophy

This rubric identifies both **established movemental leaders** (like Alan Hirsch and Brad Brisco) and **emerging voices** who demonstrate movemental potential and could benefit from platform credibility to amplify their impact. The scoring framework balances proven track record with future potential, recognizing that platform ownership can catalyze movement multiplication.

### Scoring Philosophy
- **90-100 points**: Tier 1 - Immediate Platform Priority (Flagship Leaders)
- **75-89 points**: Tier 2 - High Value Platform Candidates (Proven Leaders)
- **60-74 points**: Tier 3 - Emerging Platform Candidates (Rising Voices)
- **45-59 points**: Tier 4 - Development Pipeline (Future Potential)
- **Below 45**: Not ready for platform investment

---

## Category 1: Movement Alignment & Theological Depth (25 points)

**Purpose**: Assess theological foundation and movemental practice alignment

### Subcategory 1A: Theological Foundation (15 points)
**Excellent (13-15 points)**
- Published theological work in missional/incarnational theology
- Seminary-level theological training or equivalent depth
- Recognized theological voice in movemental circles
- Integration of theology with practical movement application
- *Example: Alan Hirsch - multiple theological works, global recognition*

**Strong (10-12 points)**
- Clear theological grounding in movemental principles
- Some published work or recognized teaching
- Demonstrates theological thinking in practical contexts
- Shows growth in theological articulation
- *Example: Emerging leader with seminary training and growing voice*

**Moderate (7-9 points)**
- Basic understanding of movemental theology
- Limited but authentic theological expression
- Shows potential for theological development
- Aligns with core movemental principles
- *Example: Practitioner with solid foundation, limited publishing*

**Basic (4-6 points)**
- Minimal theological depth but genuine movemental heart
- Shows openness to theological development
- Basic alignment with movemental values
- *Example: Church planter with passion but limited formal training*

**Weak (0-3 points)**
- Unclear theological positioning
- Minimal movemental understanding
- Misalignment with core movemental principles

### Subcategory 1B: Movement Practice Integration (10 points)
**Excellent (9-10 points)**
- Proven track record of movement catalysis
- Multiplication mindset demonstrated through results
- Networks naturally around movemental leaders
- *Example: Alan Hirsch - founded multiple movement organizations*

**Strong (7-8 points)**
- Clear movemental practice with some multiplication results
- Connects with other movemental practitioners
- Demonstrates movement thinking beyond individual ministry
- *Example: Church planter who has launched multiple sites/plants*

**Moderate (5-6 points)**
- Movemental orientation with limited multiplication
- Shows potential for movement development
- Connects with movement concepts and language
- *Example: Pastor transitioning church toward movemental approach*

**Basic (3-4 points)**
- Interest in movement principles
- Limited movemental experience
- Shows teachable spirit and potential
- *Example: Recent seminary graduate with movemental calling*

**Weak (0-2 points)**
- No demonstrated movemental practice
- Unclear commitment to movement principles

## Special Considerations & Adjustments

### Diversity & Emerging Voice Bonus (up to +5 points)
To ensure platform diversity and emerging voice development:

**+5 points**: Leader from significantly underrepresented demographic who demonstrates clear movemental calling and platform potential

**+3 points**: Emerging voice (under 40, less than 10 years ministry experience) with exceptional potential and movemental alignment

**+2 points**: Geographic diversity (international, rural, or underrepresented regions) with strong movemental foundation

**+1 point**: Gender diversity with comparable qualifications to established leaders

### Strategic Value Multiplier
Certain combinations warrant special consideration:
- **Institutional Bridge**: Leaders who connect establishment and movemental streams (like Brad Brisco)
- **Global Influence**: International leaders providing global platform expansion
- **Network Multiplication**: Leaders whose participation could attract 5+ additional high-value candidates
- **Market Innovation**: Leaders pioneering new approaches to movemental practice

## Application Guidelines

### Scoring Process
1. **Research Phase**: Complete deep profile process for comprehensive assessment
2. **Evidence-Based Scoring**: Support all scores with specific examples and documentation
3. **Calibration**: Compare scores across similar leaders for consistency
4. **Strategic Review**: Consider special circumstances and strategic value

### Decision Matrix
**90-100 points (Tier 1)**: Immediate platform development with maximum investment
**75-89 points (Tier 2)**: High priority platform development with standard investment
**60-74 points (Tier 3)**: Selective platform development with targeted support
**45-59 points (Tier 4)**: Development pipeline with mentoring and growth focus
**Below 45**: Not ready for platform investment

### Quality Assurance
- **Peer Review**: Multiple evaluators for consistency
- **Reference Checks**: Verification of claims and assessments
- **Network Validation**: Confirmation from movemental leader references
- **Strategic Alignment**: Final review for platform mission alignment

## Conclusion

This rubric ensures we identify both proven movemental leaders and emerging voices who can benefit from platform credibility while maintaining high standards for theological depth, practical capability, and strategic value. The framework balances current achievement with future potential, enabling us to build a diverse, high-quality network of movemental leaders who advance God's kingdom through authentic digital presence and strategic platform ownership.

**Key Success Factors:**
- Evidence-based assessment with clear documentation
- Balance between proven track record and emerging potential
- Strategic consideration of network effects and collaboration opportunities
- Commitment to diversity while maintaining quality standards
- Focus on movemental alignment and kingdom impact over mere platform metrics

*This rubric serves as the foundation for systematic movemental leader evaluation and platform development decisions, ensuring consistent, strategic, and mission-aligned leader selection.*
