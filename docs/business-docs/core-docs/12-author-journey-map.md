**Purpose**: Align expectations for movemental leaders joining Movemental. The **onbuilding** phase (four weeks, cohort) is **canonical** — [docs/build/notes/onbuilding-4-week-course-SSOT.md](../../../build/notes/onbuilding-4-week-course-SSOT.md). This map situates onbuilding in the full journey.

**Audience**: Authors, internal support, strategic partners

**Status**: Foundational Document — **aligned to onbuilding SSOT (2026-04-23)**

## Overview: The Complete Author Journey

The Movemental author journey is designed to transform movemental leaders from initial inquiry to sustainable digital publishers who own their platform, amplify their impact, and generate sustainable revenue. **The intellectual build** for “what goes on the site” is **onbuilding** — **4 weeks, cohort, always** — and **not** a 8-, 9-, or 12-week *build* of the same thing.

**The journey (numbered for clarity; pre-onboarding + onbuilding are distinct):**
1. **Entry & assessment** — Qualification, invitation, initial discovery
2. **Pre-onboarding (only use “onboarding” for this)** — **Provisioned** **template** **Next.js**/**React** **tenant** (not a **blank** slate), design, handoff, corpus, **start** **date** for cohort
3. **Onbuilding (4 weeks, cohort only)** — Pillar+cluster, **at least** one **pathway** + **one** **course** arc, **AI**/voice, **public** **launch** — **SSOT**; **not** 8- or 12-week **build**
4. **Formation & reach (post-launch)** — Rhythm, audience, **SEO** (old **months 2–6** focus)
5. **Monetization & growth** — Paid tiers, revenue, expansion
6. **Scale, network & legacy** — Long horizon

## Phase 1: Entry & Assessment

### Entry Points

**Primary Entry Paths**:

1. **Direct Invitation** (Primary Path)
   - Invitation from existing Movemental member
   - Referral through Movement Leaders Collective, 100Movements, or partner networks
   - Recognition through movemental leadership or content excellence
   - **Why**: Network vouching ensures quality and alignment

2. **Application Process** (Secondary Path)
   - Application form with basic qualification questions
   - Assessment consultation call
   - Evaluation against Movemental qualification criteria
   - **Why**: Allows for organic discovery of qualified leaders

3. **Cohort/organizational entry** (partnership path)
   - Entry through seminary, publisher, or movement **networks**
   - The **onbuilding** work is still the **same** 4-week **cohort** model when they join the Movemental product track
   - **Why**: Strategic expansion while preserving a **single** onbuilding design

### Assessment Process

**Qualification Criteria**: Movemental uses a 100-point evaluation rubric across six categories:

1. **Movement Alignment** (25 points)
   - Clear missional/incarnational theology and practice
   - Commitment to movemental values (scenius, collaboration, formation > growth)
   - Embodied practice over theory
   - Movement multiplication track record

2. **Content Depth** (20 points)
   - Published work demonstrating theological and practical depth
   - Substantive contribution to movemental thought or practice
   - Unique expertise and perspective
   - Archive of content available for repurposing

3. **Audience Development** (20 points)
   - Existing audience and platform (newsletter, social media, website)
   - Engaged community, not just large numbers
   - Audience quality aligned with movemental leaders
   - Growth potential and engagement patterns

4. **Revenue Potential** (15 points)
   - Ability to generate platform earnings through content, courses, speaking
   - Realistic revenue goals aligned with Movemental economics
   - Willingness to invest in digital publishing

5. **Network Value** (10 points)
   - Potential for network collaboration and cross-pollination
   - Connections to other movemental leaders
   - Willingness to participate in scenius model

6. **Platform Readiness** (10 points)
   - Capacity to commit to regular publishing (minimum monthly)
   - Willingness to be coached and learn
   - Understanding that this is a long game, not quick wins
   - Technical comfort level or willingness to learn

**Qualification thresholds** (post–2026, **onbuilding** is the **same 4** **weeks** for all admitted authors — **not** 8, **not** 12):
- **Tiers (if we keep pricing tiers)**: differ by **coaching** **after** onbuilding and by **revenue** **/ network** access — *not* by a longer **build** phase. **Everyone** who ships goes through the **one** onbuilding cohort.
- **Below 60** on the **rubric** may still read “not yet ready” — in that case they **do** **not** **start** a cohort until the gap is **closed** (e.g. corpus or audience threshold).

**Red Flags (Not Yet Ready)**:
- No existing audience (need to build that first)
- No content creation capacity (too busy with other priorities)
- Expecting magic ("I'll just set up a site and people will find me")
- Not willing to learn or be coached
- Misaligned values (just wanting a platform, not interested in movement)

**The Assessment Process**:
1. **Initial Inquiry** - Application form or referral
2. **Discovery Consultation** (30-60 minutes) - Understanding their calling, content, audience, goals
3. **Evaluation** - Scoring against 100-point rubric, reviewing existing content and platform
4. **Qualification Decision** - Tier assignment and pricing determination
5. **Invitation/Response** - Clear communication of qualification status and next steps

## Phases 2 & 3: Pre-onboarding → onbuilding (single 4-week build)

**Do not** map “weeks 1–4” in this file to a **different** calendar than the **onbuilding** **SSOT**. The **author-track** **day**/**week** plan lives in [docs/articles/07-author-onboarding-course-outline.md](../../../articles/07-author-onboarding-course-outline.md) (onbuilding playbook).

**Phase 2 — Pre-onboarding (days, not a duplicate “4 weeks”):** Contract, **fully provisioned** **template** platform, design selection, access, **corpus** to ingest, prerequisites, **start date** of **onbuilding** cohort. **1:1** and ops touchpoints are **expected** *here* for logistics — not a substitute for cohort in the build.

**Phase 3 — Onbuilding (exactly 4 weeks, always cohort, never 12 as the build):**

| Week | Objectives (summary) | Spine |
| ---- | --------------------- | ----- |
| **1** | Voice, watermark, pathway *candidates*, **monetization** intent, corpus positioned | Weekly **90** **m** **cohort**; async **daily** work; **ingest** + **AI** **themes** |
| **2** | **Pillar + cluster** **in** **prod**; **launch-sufficient** article **map** | Cohort **peer** **rubric** **review**; **RAG** **drafts** |
| **3** | **8-week** **course** **arc** + **one** **module** **ship-** **ready** | Cohort read-through; **dissonance** / **action** **hooks** **as** **in** product |
| **4** | **One** **12-section** **pathway**; **ecosystem** **map**; **launch** | **Commissioning** cohort |

**Not** the old “week 4 = only audience” pattern — that work **largely** **moves** to **post-launch** **(Phase 4)** **unless** a campaign **explicitly** bundles it.

**Success (end of onbuilding):** **Live** tenant with **enough** evergreen links (see SSOT §3) + **at least** one **pathway** + at least one **course** in **usable** form + **AI** on **corpus** + **voice** **—** *not* “3 articles and a calendar” as the *only* bar.

## Phase 4: Content launch & formation (months 2–6; after onbuilding)

### Month 2-3: Building Foundation

**Goal**: Establish consistent publishing rhythm, build free audience

**Key Activities**:
- **Weekly Publishing**: 2-4 articles/month minimum
- **Content Refinement**: Voice consistency, format optimization, engagement analysis
- **Audience Growth**: SEO optimization, social promotion, network discovery
- **Network Participation**: Cross-references, collaboration, community engagement

**Support**:
- **Bi-weekly Coaching Calls** (45 minutes): Content review, strategy refinement, problem-solving
- **Curriculum Progress**: Modules 4-6 (SEO, audience building, engagement)
- **Slack Support**: Questions, feedback, community

**Success Markers**:
- 4+ articles published
- 500+ free subscribers
- Publishing rhythm sustainable
- Network engagement active

### Month 4-6: Establishing Authority

**Goal**: Build authority in movemental domain, expand content reach

**Key Activities**:
- **Content Depth**: Series development, signature frameworks, substantive pieces
- **SEO Optimization**: Page 1 rankings for target keywords, network authority growth
- **Network Amplification**: Cross-pollination, collaborative content, mutual elevation
- **Audience Engagement**: Comments, shares, email responses, community building

**Support**:
- **Monthly Coaching Calls** (45 minutes): Strategic planning, content review, growth optimization
- **Curriculum Progress**: Modules 7-9 (monetization foundations, advanced strategies)
- **Network Events**: Cohort calls, collaboration opportunities

**Success Markers**:
- 2,000+ free subscribers
- 15+ published articles
- Page 1 SEO rankings
- Network recognition and engagement

## Phase 5: Monetization & growth (months 7–12)

### Month 7-8: Monetization Launch

**Goal**: Launch paid subscription tier, generate first revenue

**Key Activities**:
- **Paid Tier Setup**: Pricing strategy, value proposition, member benefits
- **Launch Strategy**: Email sequence, social promotion, network announcement
- **Conversion Optimization**: Free-to-paid funnel, pricing experiments, value communication
- **First Revenue**: Track conversions, optimize pricing, celebrate milestones

**Support**:
- **Weekly Coaching** (first 4 weeks): Launch support, conversion optimization, problem-solving
- **Revenue Guidance**: Pricing strategy, member benefits, value communication
- **Technical Support**: Payment processing, member management, analytics

**Success Markers**:
- Paid tier launched
- 50-100 paid subscribers (5-8% conversion rate)
- $500-$1,000/month recurring revenue
- Sustainable conversion funnel

### Month 9-12: Revenue Diversification

**Goal**: Expand revenue streams, optimize monetization

**Key Activities**:
- **Course Development**: First premium course, pricing strategy, launch sequence
- **Speaking Integration**: Platform as booking tool, testimonials, speaking page
- **Consulting/Coaching**: Offer development, booking system, revenue tracking
- **Book/Resource Sales**: Digital products, affiliate opportunities, direct sales

**Support**:
- **Monthly Coaching Calls**: Revenue optimization, diversification strategy, growth planning
- **Curriculum**: Modules 10-12 (advanced monetization, revenue diversification)
- **Network Collaboration**: Joint courses, collaborative opportunities, cross-promotion

**Success Markers**:
- 150-300 paid subscribers
- $1,500-$3,000/month recurring revenue
- Additional revenue streams active
- 12-month revenue goal achieved

## Support & Resources

### Ongoing Support Structure

**Tiered support (after onbuilding — *not* longer onbuilding)**:
- **Tier 1 (High touch)**: **More** **1:1** and **frequent** check-ins in **months 1–3** **post-launch**; **not** “more weeks of build”
- **Tier 2 (Standard)**: Bi-weekly or monthly as contracted **after** the **4-week** **cohort**

**Onbuilding** itself is **one** **cadence** for all — **4** **weeks**, **cohort**; **onboarding** is **pre-cohort** only.

## What Authors Need to Know

**Commitment Required**:
- **Time**: 5-10 hours/week for content creation and platform management
- **Publishing**: Minimum monthly (ideally weekly or bi-weekly)
- **Learning**: 16-week curriculum, ongoing skill development
- **Network Participation**: Cross-referencing, collaboration, community engagement

**What Authors Get**:
- Complete digital publishing platform (fully owned)
- AI-powered content creation (tailored to voice and theology)
- Network amplification (28x-500x reach improvement)
- Strategic coaching and support
- 90% revenue retention (vs. 10-15% on other platforms)

**Realistic Expectations**:
- **Year 1**: Foundation building, audience growth, revenue launch
- **Year 2**: Growth, optimization, network leadership
- **Year 3+**: Scale, legacy, movement multiplication

**This is a long game, not quick wins**. Success requires consistent effort, strategic thinking, and commitment to movemental values. But the infrastructure, support, and network make it achievable for leaders who commit to the journey.
