## reading

### Cumulative learning, not amnesia with enthusiasm

Run two is not “try harder.” It is **controlled delta**: change one variable, pre-register what signal would confirm or disconfirm, keep the five fields intact. If run one’s recipe was sound but rubric weak, run two might hold the recipe and **tighten criteria**—that is a legitimate delta, named as such.

### Recipes + scoring together

The canon is explicit: **well-run experiments are prerequisite for honest scoring**; bad experiments cannot be rescued by careful scoring. Conversely, scoring inflation makes good experiments look like lottery tickets. This week you hold both: **hygiene** (fields, reviewers, yellows) and **iteration** (one delta, cumulative ledger).

### Mixed ledger as success signal

A season with five reds, seven yellows, and three high-confidence greens is **better work** than twelve greens—reds are reasons the sandbox exists; yellows are honest uncertainty the portfolio can respect.

### Bridge to judgment

You are approaching the flag: structural greens still miss donor notes that say *warmer and less specific—what changed?* Second-cycle discipline builds the **muscle** to welcome Week 7’s separate pass without treating it as an insult to the experiment team.

Deep reading: [Recipes Are Not Cooking](/articles/sandbox/recipes-are-not-cooking) · [Scoring Value Honestly](/articles/sandbox/scoring-value-honestly).

## case_study

**Witness — environmental nonprofit**

Run one: retrieval-augmented draft against internal memos. Run two: **only** the retrieval slice changed (memos in vs none). Scores moved modestly; **latency** and **citation presence** improved enough to justify a later Solutions spike. The board loved the boring graph because it was **comparable**—same recipe, one knob, honest time lines. The team’s temptation to also “fix tone” mid-run was refused; tone became run three’s explicit delta—scheduled, not smuggled.
