Syllabus

Overview

Every week carries the same weekly engine: Dissonance → Concept → Witness → Practice → Reflection → Cohort Learning → Integration. The topics below describe the concept anchor for each week.

Week 1 · Rediscovering Apostolic Genius

Orient to mDNA and why movement logic requires a different architecture from institutional maintenance. Establish cohort covenant, assess current mDNA state, and name the distortion you are most susceptible to.

Week 2 · Jesus and the Missional Church

Centering Christ's lordship across every call. Without this gravitational center the other five strands cannot hold.

Week 3 · The Heart of Christianity

Disciple-making through formation, not programs. Paul to Timothy. Timothy to faithful people. Four generations in a single verse.

Week 4 · Disciple Making

Apprenticeship in the way of Jesus that multiplies — the test is whether the person you are forming can form others.

Week 5 · Missional Incarnational Impulse

The church as simultaneously going out and going deep. The attractional model is a structural choice that makes movement hard to sustain.

Week 6 · APEST Culture

Recovering the fivefold intelligence (apostolic, prophetic, evangelistic, shepherding, teaching) distributed across the whole Body.

Week 7 · Organic Systems

The church as living organism, not machine. Simplicity is not a preference; it is a structural requirement for reproduction.

Week 8 · Communitas & Commissioning

Deep social bonding forged in shared risk. The commissioning rite sends you with a formation plan and a cohort at your back.

What each week contains

  • Opening devotional and anchor Scripture
  • One dissonance prompt and one concept reading
  • A recorded teaching from Alan Hirsch (15–25 minutes)
  • A practical exercise scoped to your ministry context
  • A 90-minute live cohort call with your facilitator
  • Two reflection questions and one exit ticket
  • An integration rhythm that carries the week forward
