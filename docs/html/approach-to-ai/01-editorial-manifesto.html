The editorial covenant

How Movemental approaches AI

Most public discourse splits into "AI is a savior" or "AI is a threat." We reject both. AI is infrastructure. The leaders are the work. This page outlines the framing, the tests, and the boundaries that govern how we use AI at Movemental.

Article i · Framing

The mirror and amplifier

AI is trained on the broad output of human writing, speech, and behavior. When you interact with it, it mirrors and amplifies what it has been trained on, and it mirrors and amplifies you specifically. The outputs you get back reflect both the training data and the human in the room.

People who are worried about AI are often worried about humanity itself, because AI surfaces and amplifies what humans have already produced. People who are hopeful about AI are often hopeful about humanity for the same reason.

If AI mirrors and amplifies the humans it is around, the most important thing an organization can do is deliberately gather the right humans. The thing people are saying yes to when they join Movemental is not the technology. It is each other. The platform exists to make that possible at a scale that was not possible before.

Article ii · The single test

The "take AI out of the story" test

The single most useful test for whether an AI use case is valid is portable and falsifiable.

"
Take the AI out of the story. Look at what remains.
Movemental editorial test · §ii

If what remains is people doing valuable human work that AI helped them do faster, larger, or more accessibly — the use is humanly valuable.

If what remains is just AI output dressed up to look like human work — the use is suspect, regardless of how impressive the output is.

Applied to our own work: what remains, when you take the AI out, is a group of practitioners who had been circling each other's work for years and who wanted to do something together. The technology made it possible to gather. The technology did not make the gathering valuable.

Article iii · The framework

Green, yellow, and red lights

We sort AI use cases into three distinct categories to govern our development and deployment.

Deploy readily

Green light

Clear human benefit, no meaningful negative consequence. The washing machine of AI applications. The easiest part of our work — we deploy these readily.

  • Making existing content searchable.
  • Translating between languages a person does not speak.
  • Surfacing patterns in data that would otherwise take weeks to find.
Deploy with guardrails

Yellow light

Real benefit, but only with sufficient guardrails. The majority of AI applications fall here.

  • Drafting communications that humans review before sending.
  • Research where citation and verification are required.
  • Facilitating group conversations — never substituting for participants' contributions.
Do not deploy

Red light

Harm, regardless of how carefully the use is deployed. These form our Named Refusals. We do not deploy these.

  • Authoring content under a human's name without disclosure.
  • Eligibility decisions affecting access to services.
  • Impersonating humans in pastoral or counseling contexts.
  • Surveillance that changes how people are served.

The traffic light is a working device, not a fixed list. Every new use case we consider is sorted against it. New Named Refusals are added when we encounter them.

Article iv · Position

We are not an AI company

We are a company of humans who use AI as carefully as we can, in service of a network of movement leaders whose work matters. AI is the infrastructure. The leaders are the work.

The reason we are doing this now is that AI cannot be put back. Every organization our network serves is going to face the same questions about AI, whether they want to or not. The vendors who will help them are mostly not the people who understand the substance of what they do.

We are a few years into wrestling with these questions in a field where the most experienced person could only have three years of experience. That head start is what we offer.
