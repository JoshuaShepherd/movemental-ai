The editorial covenant

How Movemental approaches AI

Most public discourse splits into "AI is a savior" or "AI is a threat." We reject both. AI is infrastructure. The leaders are the work. This page outlines the framing, the tests, and the boundaries that govern how we use AI at Movemental.

§ 01 · Framing

The mirror and amplifier

Input · what AI was given

The humans in the room

Broad human writing, speech, and behavior — plus you, specifically, in this conversation.

Output · what comes back

Reflection

A mirror of the training data and the human in the room — same scale.

AI is trained on the broad output of human writing, speech, and behavior. When you interact with it, it mirrors and amplifies what it has been trained on, and it mirrors and amplifies you specifically. The outputs you get back reflect both the training data and the human in the room.

People who are worried about AI are often worried about humanity itself — because AI surfaces and amplifies what humans have already produced. People who are hopeful about AI are often hopeful about humanity for the same reason.

If AI mirrors and amplifies the humans it is around, the most important thing an organization can do is deliberately gather the right humans. The thing people are saying yes to when they join Movemental is not the technology. It is each other.

§ 02 · The single test

The "take AI out of the story" test

Take the AI out of the story. Look at what remains.

The single most useful test for whether an AI use case is valid is portable and falsifiable. Apply it to the situation in front of you and see what's left.

What you should see: people doing work, a tool that helped, an outcome the people could have justified without the tool.

If what remains is people doing valuable human work that AI helped them do faster, larger, or more accessibly — the use is humanly valuable.

If what remains is just AI output dressed up to look like human work — the use is suspect, regardless of how impressive the output is.

Applied to our own work: what remains is a group of practitioners who had been circling each other's work for years and who wanted to do something together. The technology made it possible to gather. The technology did not make the gathering valuable.

§ 03 · The framework

Green, yellow, and red lights

We sort AI use cases into three distinct categories to govern our development and deployment. Tap any row to read the rationale.

The washing machine of AI applications. The easiest part of our work. Deployed readily, with normal engineering quality checks.

  • AI used to make existing content searchable.
  • AI used to translate between languages a person does not speak.
  • AI used to surface patterns in data that would otherwise take weeks to find.

Most of our applied work is here. The guardrail is non-negotiable — without it the use case graduates to red.

  • AI used to draft communications that humans review before sending.
  • AI used in research where citation and verification are required.
  • AI used to facilitate group conversations — never substituting for participants' contributions.

These form our Named Refusals. We do not deploy these. New refusals are added when we encounter them — the list is intended to grow.

  • AI used to author content under a human's name without disclosure.
  • AI used to make eligibility decisions that affect access to services.
  • AI used to impersonate humans in pastoral or counseling contexts.
  • AI used to surveil people in ways that change how they are served.
§ 04 · Position

We are not an AI company

We are a company of humans who use AI as carefully as we can, in service of a network of movement leaders whose work matters. AI is the infrastructure. The leaders are the work.

The reason we are doing this now is that AI cannot be put back. Every organization our network serves is going to face the same questions about AI, whether they want to or not. The vendors who will help them are mostly not the people who understand the substance of what they do.

We are a few years into wrestling with these questions in a field where the most experienced person could only have three years of experience. That head start is what we offer.
