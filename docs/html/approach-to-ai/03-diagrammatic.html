The editorial covenant

How Movemental approaches AI

Most public discourse splits into "AI is a savior" or "AI is a threat." We reject both. AI is infrastructure. The leaders are the work. This page outlines the framing, the tests, and the boundaries that govern how we use AI at Movemental.

Figure 01 · The architectural relationship.

§ 01 · Framing

The mirror and amplifier

AI is trained on the broad output of human writing, speech, and behavior. When you interact with it, it mirrors and amplifies what it has been trained on, and it mirrors and amplifies you specifically. The outputs reflect both the training data and the human in the room.

People who are worried about AI are often worried about humanity itself, because AI surfaces and amplifies what humans have already produced. People who are hopeful about AI are often hopeful about humanity for the same reason.

If AI mirrors and amplifies the humans it is around, the most important thing an organization can do is deliberately gather the right humans. The thing people are saying yes to when they join Movemental is not the technology. It is each other.

§ 03 · The framework

Green, yellow, and red lights

We sort AI use cases into three distinct categories to govern our development and deployment.

  1. Green light Deploy readily

    Clear human benefit, no meaningful negative consequence. The washing machine of AI applications.

    • AI to make existing content searchable.
    • AI to translate between unknown languages.
    • AI to surface patterns in data quickly.
  2. Yellow light Deploy with guardrails

    Real benefit, but only with sufficient guardrails. The majority of AI applications fall here.

    • Drafting communications that humans review.
    • Research where citation and verification are required.
    • Facilitating group conversations — never substituting for participants.
  3. Red light Do not deploy

    Harm, regardless of how carefully the use is deployed. These form our Named Refusals — and the list is intended to grow.

    • Authoring content under a human's name without disclosure.
    • Eligibility decisions affecting access to services.
    • Impersonating humans in pastoral or counseling contexts.
    • Surveilling people in ways that change how they are served.
§ 04 · Position

We are not an AI company

We are a company of humans who use AI as carefully as we can, in service of a network of movement leaders whose work matters. AI is the infrastructure. The leaders are the work.

The reason we are doing this now is that AI cannot be put back. Every organization our network serves is going to face the same questions about AI, whether they want to or not. The vendors who will help them are mostly not the people who understand the substance of what they do.

We are a few years into wrestling with these questions in a field where the most experienced person could only have three years of experience. That head start is what we offer.
