The editorial covenant · preamble

How Movemental approaches AI

Most public discourse splits into "AI is a savior" or "AI is a threat." We reject both.

AI is infrastructure. The leaders are the work. This document outlines the framing, the tests, and the boundaries that govern how we use AI at Movemental. It is intended to be read by anyone who wants to understand the position before they enter into work with us.

Article I. Framing

The premise on which everything else in this document depends — that AI mirrors and amplifies the humans it is around.

The mirror and amplifier

AI is trained on the broad output of human writing, speech, and behavior. When you interact with it, it mirrors and amplifies what it has been trained on, and it mirrors and amplifies you specifically. The outputs you get back reflect both the training data and the human in the room.

People who are worried about AI are often worried about humanity itself, because AI surfaces and amplifies what humans have already produced. People who are hopeful about AI are often hopeful about humanity for the same reason.

"The most important thing an organization can do is deliberately gather the right humans."

If AI mirrors and amplifies the humans it is around, the most important thing an organization can do is deliberately gather the right humans. The thing people are saying yes to when they join Movemental is not the technology. It is each other. The platform exists to make that possible at a scale that was not possible before.

Article II. The single test

A portable, falsifiable check we apply to every proposed AI use case before it ships.

The "take AI out of the story" test

The single most useful test for whether an AI use case is valid is portable and falsifiable.

"Take the AI out of the story. Look at what remains."

If what remains is people doing valuable human work that AI helped them do faster, larger, or more accessibly — the use is humanly valuable.

If what remains is just AI output dressed up to look like human work — the use is suspect, regardless of how impressive the output is.

Applied to our own work: what remains, when you take the AI out, is a group of practitioners who had been circling each other's work for years and who wanted to do something together. The technology made it possible to gather. The technology did not make the gathering valuable.

Article III. The framework

The traffic light. Three categories. Named permissions we keep on the shelf and Named Refusals we hold the line on.

Green, yellow, and red lights

We sort AI use cases into three distinct categories to govern our development and deployment. Specific entries are illustrative, not exhaustive — the framework is the operative thing.

§ Green · deploy readily — clear human benefit, no meaningful negative consequence
i. AI used to make existing content searchable. Permit
ii. AI used to translate between languages a person does not speak. Permit
iii. AI used to surface patterns in data that would otherwise take weeks to find. Permit
§ Yellow · deploy with guardrails — real benefit only with sufficient guardrails
iv. AI used to draft communications that humans review before sending. Guarded
v. AI used to help with research where citation and verification are required. Guarded
vi. AI used to facilitate group conversations where it never substitutes for the participants' actual contributions. Guarded
§ Red · named refusals — harm, regardless of how carefully deployed
vii. AI used to author content under a human's name without disclosure. Refused
viii. AI used to make eligibility decisions that affect people's access to services. Refused
ix. AI used to impersonate humans in pastoral or counseling contexts. Refused
x. AI used to surveil people in ways that change how they are served. Refused
Article IV. Position

Why we are doing this now, and what we offer that vendors with no substance can't.

We are not an AI company

We are a company of humans who use AI as carefully as we can, in service of a network of movement leaders whose work matters. AI is the infrastructure. The leaders are the work.

The reason we are doing this now is that AI cannot be put back. Every organization our network serves is going to face the same questions about AI, whether they want to or not. The vendors who will help them are mostly not the people who understand the substance of what they do.

We are a few years into wrestling with these questions in a field where the most experienced person could only have three years of experience. That head start is what we offer.

So adopted on behalf of Movemental, holding the line described above and committing the organization to its application in every product decision, every customer engagement, and every contracted relationship.

Editorial Council For Movemental
