The claim

Fragmentation is structural, not a content problem.

Every organization that carries meaning through time — a life’s work, a congregation, an institution, a mission — pays a tax it usually can’t see. Knowledge, relationships, decisions, and formation live in places that don’t agree with each other, and the cost of that disagreement accumulates.

The book argues the fix isn’t more content, more tools, or a different platform. The fix is integration — the load-bearing stage that turns a scatter field into a field. Activation, formation, multiplication, and movement only compound once that stage holds.
