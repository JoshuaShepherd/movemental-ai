Section 01

The Sequence.

The order is the work.

Why this order? Why not parallel?

Because the order is the work. Skipping Safety means deploying before you know what you are willing to defend. Skipping Sandbox means training judgment in production, on real people. Skipping Skills means importing someone else's. Solutions deployed without the prior three stages are technical answers to questions the organization has not yet asked itself.

What if our staff are already using AI on personal accounts?

Almost every organization is in this situation. It is the most common starting state we encounter. Naming it, on the record, is the first move of Safety — not a failure that needs hiding. We have never started an engagement where this was not already true.

Can stages overlap?

In practice, yes. The early Sandbox can run while Safety is being ratified, because the Sandbox is bounded precisely so it does not require the final posture to begin. But the order in which work is finished matters: nothing leaves the Sandbox into production before Safety is named, and no Solutions ship before Skills are formed. The order of completion is load-bearing.

How long does the Sequence take?

A focused Sandbox stage runs roughly eight to twelve weeks. A full Sequence engagement, end to end, runs four to nine months depending on board cadence, staff size, and how much of the human work was already in motion before we arrived. We will tell you, before you commit, if the timeline you are offering is too short for the work to hold. We have walked away from engagements over this.

What if we have already deployed a Solution?

Most organizations have. The Sequence is recursive, not linear-in-time. We start where you are: take the Solution as given, walk Safety in writing, run the Sandbox the deployment skipped, form the Skills the deployment assumed. The Solution stays. What changes is whether the organization can defend it.

Section 02

Money.

Direct answers, no public pricing.

What does this cost?

Engagements are scoped per organization. The most common shape — a Sandbox stage for a single audience — runs in the low five figures. A full Sequence engagement runs higher. We do not publish a price list because the price tracks the scope, the senior team's hours, and the depth of the human work involved, and any number we publish in advance will be wrong for half the conversations.

Why is there no public pricing page?

Two reasons. First, the price would be wrong. Second, public pricing on this kind of work attracts the wrong conversations — the ones that want to compress the Sequence into a number rather than walk it. We accept the cost of being harder to evaluate at a glance. If you need a budget number to take to your board, ask us in the first call and we will give you a good-faith range.

Will we know in the first call whether we can afford this?

Yes. If the budget you can hold is less than what the Sequence would cost in your context, we will tell you in the first call and recommend either a smaller starting move (often the Integrity Diagnostic, which is free) or a different organization. We will not let an engagement begin that we know you cannot afford to finish.

Do you offer reduced rates for smaller organizations?

Sometimes, on a case-by-case basis, and only when the reduced rate is honest about the smaller scope. We do not run a tiered pricing program. We do, occasionally, walk a Sandbox stage at cost when the partner organization is doing work the field needs to learn from. Ask if you think you fit that description.

Section 03

Proof.

Where the case studies are. Where the evidence is. Where the logos are not.

Where are your case studies?

We do not currently publish named case studies. Two reasons: most of our partners do not want their AI posture work narrated publicly while it is still in formation, and we do not write case studies for partners who haven't asked us to. We can share specific, anonymized stories on a call when the question is concrete. If you need a public case study before you can have a conversation, we are not yet the right fit.

Where is the evidence?

Two places. First, the field guide: it is the single most complete public account of the AI Stewardship Sequence, and is the document our partners read alongside us. Second, the named ecosystem on the Trusted voices page: practitioners whose own published work testifies to the framing, independent of any contractual relationship with Movemental.

Can we talk to a current partner?

On request, yes — in the second or third call, after we have read whether the question is concrete enough to be useful for them. We do not ask partners to take cold reference calls. We do connect partners to each other directly when we read that the conversation will help both of them.

Why no logos?

Logos do nothing for the kind of trust this work depends on. The relevant question is not "who else uses this" but "do the people in your seat trust the people in our seat to read this honestly." That question is answered by named voices, written work, and a read-back from the Integrity Diagnostic — not by a logo wall.

What about quotes?

On the Trusted voices page, yes — from the named ecosystem layer. Inside engagements, we use direct quotes only with explicit written permission from the partner, and only after the partner has seen them in the context where they will be published. We do not run quotes by partners post-publication.

Section 04

Posture and theology.

Is this only for Christian organizations?

No. The three implementation audiences are churches, nonprofits, and institutions, of which only the first is Christian by default. The Sequence does not depend on a particular theology. It depends on an organization having a mission worth protecting and a senior team willing to be in the room while AI changes how the mission gets done.

Your founders are theologically formed — does that show up?

Yes, it shows up in the vocabulary — posture, formation, witness, refusal — and in the editorial honesty about what AI is not. We do not bracket that language for non-faith partners. If the vocabulary is the wrong fit, the engagement will be the wrong fit. We have walked away on this.

Are you partisan? Politically, ecclesially?

We do not run on either axis. The Sequence holds across the whole sector. The Trusted voices page is deliberately curated to span streams and traditions. If you read the page and see only your tribe represented, we have made a curatorial mistake and want to hear about it.

What about denominations and ecclesial bodies?

We have worked alongside leaders inside several streams; we do not represent any. If your denomination or judicatory wants to translate the Sequence into its own polity, we are happy to be a resource and an editorial partner — not a vendor.

Section 05

AI itself.

The questions about the technology, the harms, and the tools.

Are you for or against AI?

Neither, on purpose. The question itself is part of the diagnosis. Organizations stuck on the for/against frame are usually stuck on Stage 1 — which is fine, that is where Safety lives — but the rest of the Sequence assumes you have already moved past the all-or-nothing posture and are ready to ask the harder, downstream questions.

What about a specific tool — ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini?

We do not endorse, sell, resell, or take referral fees from any specific AI vendor. The Solutions stage of an engagement may recommend specific tools — usually two or three options at the relevant tier — but only after the prior three stages have produced enough clarity that the recommendation can be defended in the room. We will name the tradeoffs. We will not pretend a single vendor is the answer.

What about the harms — labor, environment, copyright, hallucination, bias?

Real, named, and load-bearing. The Safety stage is where these are turned into specific organizational refusals — not abstract concerns. By the end of Safety, an organization typically has a written list of things it will not use AI for and a written list of things it will, with the harms it cares about most named explicitly in both.

What if the technology changes?

It will. The Sequence is designed to be more durable than any specific model or vendor — the artifacts it produces (a posture paragraph, named refusals, a Sandbox process, a formation curriculum) survive model upgrades and vendor changes. When the technology shifts, you re-walk the relevant stages with the new facts. You do not start over.

Section 06

Who we are.

Who founded Movemental?

Brad Brisco (CEO), Alan Hirsch (Chief Missiologist), and Joshua Shepherd (CTO). Bios on the team page. The Trusted voices page lists the named ecosystem layer that has signed on alongside the founders.

Who pays you?

Engagements are paid by the organizations we serve. We do not take vendor referral fees, sponsorships, or affiliate revenue from AI tool companies. If we recommend a tool inside an engagement, we do not get paid for the recommendation. Period.

How are you accountable?

Three ways. To the partner organizations directly, by the terms of each engagement. To the named Trusted voices, who criticize our work in public when we miss. To the field, by publishing the field guide and the editorial notes that document what we are claiming and how we got there. We are not a 501(c)(3); the field guide and the diagnostic are not behind a paywall.

Is Movemental a nonprofit?

No. We are a privately held company. The choice is deliberate: it lets us walk away from engagements that would not hold, refuse vendor money, and publish editorial work without grant cycles dictating what gets said when. It also means we are not tax-deductible and we are not pretending to be.

Section 07

What happens next.

How a first conversation actually runs.

What does a first call look like?

Thirty minutes. One of the founders. Not a sales call. The questions on our side are: what is the senior team's posture today, what is in motion, what is at stake, and what is the timeline that the rest of the organization can hold. By the end of the call, we will tell you whether the Sequence is a fit and, if it is, whether the timing is.

What does a "not a fit" answer look like?

A direct one. We will tell you what we read in the conversation, why we think the engagement would not hold, and what we would do in your seat instead. Sometimes the answer is "come back in six months." Sometimes it is "we are not the right organization for this and here are two who might be." We try to leave no one worse off than they came in.

What if we are not ready to call yet?

Read the field guide and take the Integrity Diagnostic. Both are free. Both are the right shape for an organization that is still reading. If, after both, the conversation feels real, write us.

What if you don't write back?

We always write back, usually within two business days. If you have not heard from us inside a week, the message did not reach the inbox. Write again, or use a different address — there is a backup address on the contact page.
