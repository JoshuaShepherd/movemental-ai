Files

What lives where.

The thirteen pages share three files in ./site-templates/ and one CSS layer at the repo root. Editing any of these affects the whole family.

  • ./site-templates/site-theme.css Canonical L0–L2 + L2b token system shared with the Next.js app. Cream paper, ink type, Midnight bands, hairlines, pill buttons. Authoritative — never edit without updating the React side.
  • ./mock-pages.css Thin layer of page-only recipes (problem grid, stage grid, outcomes grid, audience cards, versus columns, case block, profile cards, contact form, field guide, comparison grid for organizations, diagnostic preview for assess, plus the Instrument Serif emphasis layer). Loads after site-theme.css.
  • ./site-templates/site-shell.js Mobile nav toggle and active-state highlight. Identical across all thirteen mocks; shared with the rest of the static templates.
  • ./mock-home.html Home. Hero through final CTA. Trusted voices fold, diagnosis + path (grounded in core claims), final CTA; scorecard band internal.
  • ./mock-organizations.html Audience hub. What the three audiences share, what differs across four axes, three audience cards, and a note on why movement leaders are not a fourth card.
  • ./mock-churches.html · ./mock-nonprofits.html · ./mock-institutions.html The three audience pages. Audience-named hero, voice-of-customer problem grid, audience-tuned Sequence, artifact-shaped proof, differentiation, engagement, audience-specific FAQ.
  • ./mock-movement-leaders.html Definitional surface. Three-facet definition, an "ecosystem layer not a funnel" comparison, three signs of practitioner fit, pointer to Trusted voices, and the editorial boundary about what this page is not.
  • ./mock-voices.html The Movemental Scenius. Liz Rios, JR Woodward, Rowland Smith and beyond. Definition + practitioner-fit framing. No nominate-a-leader form, per the doctrine.
  • ./mock-about.html The thesis (AI is a human challenge first), three pillars, why-now, pointers to team, voices, and the field guide.
  • ./mock-team.html The three founders as profile cards. "Same Sequence we ask of you, we walk ourselves" band.
  • ./mock-field-guide.html Long-form article on the AI Stewardship Sequence. TOC pills, four stage-detail blocks, load-bearing-order coda.
  • ./mock-assess.html Integrity Diagnostic. Six dimensions, a styled question preview, the read-back shape, the "not a maturity model" editorial note, six-question FAQ.
  • ./mock-faq.html The full FAQ. Seven sections: Sequence, money, proof, posture, AI itself, who we are, what happens next. Absorbs questions previously held under /evidence and /case-studies.
  • ./mock-contact.html Contact. Form + editorial side panel. "What happens next" written as real expectations, not marketing.

When you have walked the surface

The mockups are not the page. They are the argument the page should be making.

Edit the copy directly in the HTML. Where a real partner quote, photo, or named case is needed, the text intentionally does not invent one — it points at where the proof actually lives. Promote sections into src/components/sections/ and src/app/(site)/<route>/ when ready.
