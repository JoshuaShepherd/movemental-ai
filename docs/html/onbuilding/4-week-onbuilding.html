Single source of truth

Onbuilding

The one structured, cohort-based build for committed movement leaders: from a fully provisioned template platform to a launched, navigable, formation-ready presence. Always four weeks. Cohort-based. After pre-onboarding — not instead of it.

Duration 4 weeks — no variants, not “12-week onboarding”
Cohort 1× 90 min / week (facilitator-led) + async; peers are the spine
Leader time ~5–8 hrs / week (one number published to the cohort)
Principle Launch > perfection. Integrity, clarity, real navigation — not volume

§1 The model (definitive)

What onbuilding is

The only structured, cohort-based build that turns a template-based, fully provisioned platform into something shipped for real people. Build focus: at minimum one pillar + one cluster of evergreen articles (and a strategy map sufficient to launch); at least one pathway; at least one course (usable, or with one week publishable and the rest specified — not “we’ll do the course later”). Intelligence: AI ingest, theme extraction, voice alignment, and very high quality drafts; humans lead, decide, edit, and sign what ships.

What onbuilding is not

  • Not open-ended “enablement” or a library of best-effort work.
  • Not a 12-week (or 8-week) onboarding program; those time horizons describe after the build, if at all — not the build.
  • Not the same as onboarding (all pre-cohort: contract, provisioned tenant, corpus, prerequisites).

Onboarding (contrast) — pre-cohort only

  • Agreement, template stand-up, design selection, corpus for ingest, AI access, pre-reading, prerequisites complete.
  • Does not require a “finished” site; does require a real product surface, not an empty project.
  • Everything before week 1 of the cohort.

After week 4

Ongoing publishing, audience work, optional stabilization or coaching — separate SKUs and separate language. Not a longer onbuilding.

Only do what is necessary; remove decision ambiguity step by step; default against overproduction and blank-page work.

§2 The four weeks

1

Foundation: voice, themes, and architecture

Name what must be true for this platform to form people, not just host files. Pathway candidates, corpus as one library, and what is free vs. gated — with no “we’ll pick themes later.”

Week 1 — dimension definitions
Objective Voice and watermark (what belongs / what does not); 3–5 pathway candidates; corpus as one library; monetization intent. No unfilled “later” placeholders.
Leader Contribute source material; watermark + pathway list; voice calibration; one-page monetization map.
AI / system Ingest and chunk corpus; surface themes, gaps, overlaps; draft memos and pathway blurbs; structural suggestions (no unapproved public copy).
Cohort Present watermark, pathways, and map; peers challenge vagueness; facilitator enforces enough to proceed.
Outputs Watermark; pathway candidates; voice memo; monetization map; peer feedback log.
2

Articles: pillar + cluster (launch-grade)

Ship one pillar and one cluster to production, on brand and interlinked, with a strategic map that is enough to launch — not a content farm.

Week 2 — dimension definitions
Objective One pillar and one cluster evergreen, in production, on voice, linked, RAG- and SEO-sensible. Strategic map of pillars and clusters enough to launch (not every future article written).
Leader Own editing and sign-off; reject generic tone; set one execution slice (which pillar first).
AI / system Topic cluster ideas; drafts in nine-section architecture; internal link suggestions; voice audit list.
Cohort Peer review of pillars — rubric: nine sections, five voice markers; no hollow praise.
Outputs Published pillar + cluster; strategic inventory; execution slice for the primary pathway.
3

Course: usable arc + one deep week

An 8-week learner course specified, with at least one week in teachable form — clear thesis, honest scope.

Week 3 — dimension definitions
Objective Transformatively scoped 8-week course specified. At least one week (e.g. week 2) fully drafted for use. Thesis clear. Assessment optional in concept only.
Leader Write or revise the load-bearing module; set dissonance stakes; sign off.
AI / system Scaffold from corpus; draft modules; optional assessment hooks.
Cohort Outline read-through; “Is the action doable in real life this week?”
Outputs 8-week outline; one week publishable; dissonance prompts; course in nav when applicable.
4

Pathway + integration + launch

One primary pathway that wires the work together; go-live to real people with a 90-day clarity plan.

Week 4 — dimension definitions
Objective One primary pathway (12-section model or approved) wiring pillar, cluster, course, and assessment. Ecosystem map + launch brief. Go-live to real users.
Leader Draft/approve pathway sections; click-through the live tenant; sign the launch checklist.
AI / system Links, curated resources block, AI Lab copy; last-mile ingest of published URLs.
Cohort Commissioning — read hooks aloud; accountability pairs for 30-day rhythm.
Outputs Live platform: pathway + articles (min) + course (usable) + AI in voice + 90-day post-launch plan (clarity, not more volume).

§3 What is “enough” to launch

Minimum viable surface — not maximum possible volume

Every line below is about integrity + clarity + navigation — not filling every slot in a notional 60+ article map.

Required outputs: enough vs. not required at launch
Output Enough to launch Not required at launch
Articles One pillar + cluster in production, on voice, interlinked. A thematic map of future work (strategy table). Filling an entire 60+ article map.
Pathway At least one complete primary pathway to product standard, routing to course and key articles. A pathway for every theme.
Course 8-week arc designed and one week in teachable shape. Other weeks specified with dates in the launch plan if still pending. Every module filmed or fully written.
AI / voice Ingested corpus, voice markers, RAG where supported, signed public copy only. Exhaustive custom agents per sub-task.
Platform Template tenant fully provisioned before week 1. Wholesale custom redesign during the cohort.

§4 The workflow (exact)

From corpus to a wired pathway

Rule: draft-heavy, not blank-page-heavy. The leader’s time goes to decision, deletion, and distinction, not the empty cursor.

corpus → ingest → theme extraction → structural map → AI drafts → human edit & sign → publish & wire pathway

Responsibility split
Stage Automated Human Optional
Ingest, chunk, embed Yes Curate what to exclude; legal / sensitivity —
Theme / pillars Suggest Approve and prune —
Drafts High-quality first draft Every public line earned by judgment Stylistic tone passes
Voice guard / audit Assist Overrule; fix edge cases —
Publish Pipeline Sign-off for go-live —
Cohort Scheduling nudges Facilitator sets pace, kills scope creep Short 1:1 for blockers

§5 Product model

Starting state vs. what the cohort adds

Starting state: a provisioned Next / React template (Movemental spine): auth where needed, article routes, course and pathway shells, AI integration, brand tokens.

Pre-built before onbuilding: design, empty shells of content types, ingest path — not a blank repo.

Built in four weeks: the minimum that makes the site unmistakably this leader’s — not every feature, not every page. AI agents do not replace human byline, governance, or formation judgment.

§6 Cohort model

Peers and rubrics — not a private tutoring queue

  • Peers hold you accountable on shipped work; they challenge fuzzy strategy.
  • Facilitator sets the edge of “done,” prevents tripling scope, runs rubric-based review, and keeps time to launch.
  • 1:1 is ancillary — short office hours or blockers, not the spine.
  • Cannot make the weekly cohort: you are in the wrong cohort — reschedule to the next slot, do not run a secret “12-week” shadow build.

§7 Constraints and principles

The guardrails in one glance

Start from drafts and maps No blank-page heroics. Theme extraction and structure first.

Every piece must earn its place If it does not serve pathway, course, or voice, it waits.

Refuse performative busyness No overproduction in four weeks — clarity beats volume.

Clarity over exhaustiveness; launch > perfection The cohort exists for momentum, clarity, accountability, peer reinforcement every week, in a different form.

§8 Naming standard

Say this; do not say that

Onbuilding
The 4-week cohort build — the primary name in product and comms.
Onboarding
Pre-cohort only: access, provisioned platform, corpus, handoff to week 1.
Residency
Deprecated as a program name. Historical prose may use it with a one-line map to onbuilding in a footnote. Do not introduce it in product as a new name.
“12-week onboarding”
Prohibited to mean the build. Say post-launch stabilization or a dated coaching window (optional) instead.
“2–4 week launch” (marketing)
If it appears, it must mean pre-on + 4-week onbuilding = launch — or remove it. Never imply the whole intellectual build is 24 hours.

§9 Product gaps revealed

What the honest product should ship

These are the capabilities that make the onbuilding story match the onbuilding experience — not a wish list for scope creep, but a checklist for integrity.

Capabilities

  • Onboarding → onbuilding: a gated checklist (corpus, legal, access, template live) before the cohort starts.
  • Cohort UI — rosters, session times, submission of pillars/clusters for peer review, facilitator visibility into build progress.
  • AI drafting on tenant RAG, drafts with citation to chunks, and no unapproved public publish.
  • Pathway + course builder: 12-section pathway, course week pattern, one end-to-end click pathway → content.
  • Onbuilding progress: weeks 1–4, separate from long-term analytics.
  • Launch toggle or readiness list for a public (even soft) URL.
