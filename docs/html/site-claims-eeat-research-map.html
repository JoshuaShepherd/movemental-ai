Claims, sources, and footnotes

This table is meant to read like the bottom of a serious nonfiction chapter: the claim is what we assert on the site; the source is styled the way a reader should see it in a bibliography or citation drawer; the footnote explains what the evidence actually shows and how it connects to the claim—no staff-only jargon.

Source column. Author or organization first, then title in italics (handled by CSS), then date and any reader-relevant detail line. When a public link exists, you get a single clear “view publication” action.

Footnote column. Written for the public: additional context, scope, and interpretation—like a book note—not instructions for editors.
