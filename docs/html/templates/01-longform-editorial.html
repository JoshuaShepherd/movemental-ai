How Movemental approaches AI

Most public discourse splits into “AI is a savior” or “AI is a threat.” We reject both. AI is infrastructure. The leaders are the work. This page outlines the framing, the tests, and the boundaries that govern how we use AI at Movemental.

Editorial Updated May 2026 14 min read

§ 01  ·  Premise

The mirror and the amplifier

AI is trained on the broad output of human writing, speech, and behavior. When you interact with it, it mirrors and amplifies what it has been trained on, and it mirrors and amplifies you specifically. The outputs you get back reflect both the training data and the human in the room.

People who are worried about AI are often worried about humanity itself, because AI surfaces and amplifies what humans have already produced. People who are hopeful about AI are often hopeful about humanity for the same reason.

If AI mirrors and amplifies the humans it is around, the most important thing an organization can do is deliberately gather the right humans. The thing people are saying yes to when they join Movemental is not the technology. It is each other. The platform exists to make that possible at a scale that was not possible before.

§ 02  ·  The Acid Test

The “take AI out of the story” test

“Take the AI out of the story. Look at what remains.”

The single most useful test for whether an AI use case is valid is portable and falsifiable. If what remains is people doing valuable human work that AI helped them do faster, larger, or more accessibly — the use is humanly valuable.

If what remains is just AI output dressed up to look like human work — the use is suspect, regardless of how impressive the output is. Applied to our own work: what remains, when you take the AI out, is a group of practitioners who had been circling each other’s work for years and who wanted to do something together. The technology made it possible to gather. The technology did not make the gathering valuable.

§ 03  ·  The framework

Green, yellow, and red lights

We make AI deployment decisions through a three-tier framework. It is deliberately coarse-grained because the goal is fast judgment in service of careful work, not bureaucratic adjudication.

Green Light

— Deploy readily

Clear human benefit, no meaningful negative consequence.

The washing machine of AI applications. AI used to make existing content searchable. AI used to translate between languages a person does not speak. AI used to surface patterns in data that would otherwise take weeks to find.

When the question is “does this expand human capacity without trading anything important away?” the answer is yes. We use it. We do not write a memo about it.

Yellow Light

— Deploy with guardrails

Real benefit, but only with sufficient guardrails.

AI used to draft communications that humans review before sending. AI used to help with research where citation and verification are required. AI used to facilitate group conversations where the AI never substitutes for the participants’ actual contributions.

The shape of the guardrail is always the same: a human with judgment is the last hand on the work before it leaves the building.

Red Light

— Do not deploy

Harm, regardless of how carefully the use is deployed.

AI used to author content under a human’s name without disclosure. AI used to make eligibility decisions that affect people’s access to services. AI used to impersonate humans in pastoral or counseling contexts. AI used to surveil people.

These are not edge cases we will figure out later. They are stop-work conditions.

§ 04  ·  Conclusion

We are not an AI company

We are a company of humans who use AI as carefully as we can, in service of a network of movement leaders whose work matters. AI is the infrastructure. The leaders are the work.

The reason we are doing this now is that AI cannot be put back. Every organization our network serves is going to face the same questions about AI, whether they want to or not. The vendors who will help them are mostly not the people who understand the substance of what they do.

We are a few years into wrestling with these questions in a field where the most experienced person could only have three years of experience. That head start is what we offer.
