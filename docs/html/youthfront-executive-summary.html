Executive Summary

Sandbox-first AI Stewardship Engagement for Youthfront

Prepared for: Youthfront

Primary contact: Natasha Nikkel, COO

Prepared by: Movemental

For faith-based non-profit organizations like Youthfront, AI is not primarily a technology challenge but a human, theological one. AI raises questions for how organizations communicate, plan, decide, relate, and build on the foundation of pre-existing trust they’ve already established with their community and constituents.

To do that well, there must be a foundation of clearly documented ground rules for Safety. My understanding is that Youthfront is developing this response. What comes next is thoughtful, wise experimentation in a safe sandbox so that valuable use cases can be discovered, while the human ethical questions each raises can be simultaneously flagged — for near-future resolution.

For mission-driven organizations like Youthfront, the best option is neither a full “No AI,” which is impossible, nor “Go AI” (as in full adoption without safety, training, and testing).

Safety is the first step, but it’s not the last. Once clear and complete safety documentation is in place, organizations must find out whether valuable AI applications do exist for them (wisely saving time, steward resources & revenue, and improving the quality of work and relationship) and what human concerns exist for each of them.

We provide a clear sequence and guidance through the process.

Where Youthfront is

Youthfront is not starting from zero.

You already have:

  • strong instincts around trust, formation, and relational responsibility
  • leadership awareness of data sensitivity and public credibility
  • internal momentum toward clarity
  • ongoing work to document safe AI usage and rules organization-wide

This is the crucial foundation.

What is missing—and what most organizations lack—is:

a structured way to discover value safely, while building shared skills & wisdom across the team

What We Propose

A Sandbox-first engagement, with Skills layered in.

This is not a general training program.

It is a structured discovery system that produces real organizational work product and results (safely).

The engagement centers on three outcomes:

1. Use Case Portfolio (the core outcome)

  • Movemental provides a curated library of nonprofit-relevant AI use cases
  • Youthfront tests these through structured experiments
  • The organization produces a portfolio of as many validated, valuable use cases as we can explore and document together — starting from the original work of Movemental to get us started

Each use case is documented with:

  • purpose
  • owner
  • inputs and boundaries
  • estimated time savings, revenue potential, or other quantitative/qualitative metric
  • review requirements

Then

  • flags for concern about potential ethical and trust violations
  • a clear process for flagging and discerning what is allowed and under what conditions

This portfolio becomes:

  • the leadership decision surface
  • the board-level narrative
  • the foundation for any future adoption

2. Structured Evaluation (value + ethics together)

Each use case is evaluated through:

A. Practical value

  • time saved
  • quality improved
  • repeatability
  • risk introduced

B. Ethical and relational alignment

  • authorship and voice
  • trust and disclosure
  • appropriateness within Youthfront’s mission

This produces:

  • clear green / yellow / red decisions
  • documented reasoning
  • confidence in what moves forward—and what does not

3. Skills in Context (not abstract training)

Skills are built inside real work, not in isolation.

Staff learn:

  • how to work with AI without losing human intelligence, authorship & creativity
  • how to verify and review outputs
  • how to operate within clear boundaries

This ensures:

capability grows alongside judgment—not ahead of it

What This Is (and Is Not)

This is:

  • a Sandbox season
  • focused on discovery, evidence, and shared judgment

This is not:

  • a full AI rollout
  • a tools-first implementation
  • a generic training program

Outcomes Youthfront Can Expect

  • A clear, defensible set of approved use cases
  • Reduced shadow experimentation and ambiguity
  • Staff capable of responsible, consistent AI use
  • Concrete work product & documentation for board, donor, and partner conversations
  • A Claude-centered approach to valuable, wise AI use at Youthfront

Most importantly:

Youthfront will be able to engage AI in a way that remains recognizable as Youthfront.

Engagement Structure

  • Modular timeline (typically 4-8 months)
  • Sandbox as the primary engine
  • Skills layered in as needed
  • Solutions (integration) only if warranted by evidence

Founding Partner Position

This engagement is structured as a founding partner relationship.

Youthfront will:

  • receive the full Sandbox program and infrastructure
  • help shape how this model is refined in practice

Investment

Youthfront pricing: $10,000 (fixed)

This reflects:

  • a first-season Sandbox engagement
  • full access to Movemental’s frameworks and use case library
  • direct collaboration in shaping the model

Future engagements of this scope will be priced at a higher standard rate.

Closing

The goal is not to move quickly.

The goal is to move clearly—so that any future adoption is:

  • evidence-based
  • ethically grounded
  • and led by humans, not technology
