# Part seven: Solutions Deployment
The Solutions stage is the technological deployment of what the upstream stages have made the organization ready to deploy. It is the building of agentic ecosystems, the optimization of existing tools like Claude Enterprise, the integration of AI capability into the organization's actual workflows, the construction of custom infrastructure where warranted, and the operational rhythm that sustains the deployed capability over time. It begins at $30,000 for focused engagements and scales by scope. The premise of the Solutions stage is that technology built on a coherent foundation compounds, while technology built on a fragmented foundation corrodes. The organization that arrives at Solutions has done the foundational work: Safety has been ratified, Sandbox has surfaced and adjudicated specific use cases, Skills has formed the staff who will operate and lead the deployed capability. Solutions is therefore not a build-from-scratch engagement. It is the deployment of clarity that has already been generated.

### Seven-A: What Solutions actually deploys

Solutions Deployment is not one offering. It is a family of offerings calibrated to what Sandbox surfaced as the right next investment for this specific organization. Five typical Solutions configurations cover the majority of engagements. Configuration A: Optimization of existing tools. The organization's green-light use cases are deployed through the AI tools the organization already has — typically Claude Enterprise, Microsoft Copilot, or the equivalent enterprise-tier configuration of a major model provider. Movemental builds custom skills, prompts, and integrations within the existing tool environment. Documentation, staff training, and a 90-day operational support window are included. Pricing typically lands in the $30,000-$50,000 range. This is the right configuration for organizations whose Sandbox surfaced strong use cases that do not require new infrastructure. Configuration B: Custom agentic ecosystem. The organization's green-light use cases require integration across multiple tools, custom data handling, or sustained agentic workflows that exceed what off-the-shelf enterprise tools deliver well. Movemental builds a custom agentic infrastructure — typically combining Claude or comparable models, the organization's data systems (CRM, member database, donor system, content libraries), and custom workflow orchestration. The build is hosted on Movemental's infrastructure or the organization's, depending on data sovereignty requirements. Pricing typically lands in the $80,000-$150,000 range. This is the right configuration for organizations with significant scale or substantial multi-system workflows. Configuration C: Content and intelligence platform. For institutions, networks, and content-heavy organizations (large churches with substantial sermon archives; nonprofits with extensive program documentation; seminaries with faculty writing and curriculum), the deployment is a content and intelligence platform — the organization's archive made queryable, searchable, and active rather than static. The platform supports staff workflows that draw on the archive, member or constituent workflows that access the archive in defined ways, and ongoing additions that keep the archive growing. Pricing typically lands in the $60,000-$120,000 range plus annual platform licensing. This is the configuration that converts an organization's accumulated content into an active asset. Configuration D: Network deployment (Institutions only). For denominational bodies, training networks, and multi-site institutions, the deployment extends across multiple member organizations under a shared governance framework and shared platform. Each member organization receives a configured deployment; the institution holds the framework and the platform; the cost is shared across the network. Pricing begins at $60,000 for the institutional engagement and scales with the number of member organizations. This is the configuration that lets an institution lead its network into AI capability rather than watching members navigate independently.

Configuration E: Hybrid and bespoke. Some organizations need a combination — optimization of existing tools for some workflows, custom agentic infrastructure for others, content platform integration for the archive, and network distribution for member organizations. These are scoped per conversation. Pricing reflects scope. Movemental does not over-engineer; we deploy what Sandbox actually surfaced as needed and supported by the organization's Skills capacity.

### Seven-B: The readiness threshold

Movemental does not deploy Solutions for organizations that have not crossed a threshold. The threshold is named explicitly because most consulting and technology firms do not name it, and the absence of clarity is a primary cause of the AI deployment failures the broader sector is currently experiencing. An organization is ready for Solutions Deployment when:

- Safety Documentation is in place — ratified by leadership, communicated to staff, and in active use as a reference for adjudications. Whether this work was done with Movemental or independently does not matter, but it must be done.

- Sandbox Discovery has surfaced specific, named, adjudicated use cases. Solutions does not build for hypothetical value. It deploys for documented value with documented integrity assessment.

Without Sandbox, Solutions is building on speculation.

- Skills Development has produced — or the organization already possesses — the staff capacity to operate and lead what is being deployed. Technology deployed without the human capacity to lead it does not produce value; it produces dependency on the vendor that built it. Movemental does not want this dependency, and we do not configure engagements that produce it.

- Leadership has clarity about what is being deployed and why. The Discernment Memo from

Sandbox is the typical artifact that establishes this clarity. Without it, Solutions becomes an exercise in shifting goals during the engagement — expensive for the organization and frustrating for everyone involved. Organizations that have done some but not all of this upstream work are not refused; they are routed. Movemental will help an organization complete the upstream work first, then deploy Solutions. The path is the path. The threshold is real.

### Seven-C: The Solutions process, by phase

Phase 1: Scoping (2-3 weeks). Movemental works with leadership to translate the Sandbox findings and Discernment Memo into a specific Solutions configuration. The scoping phase produces a Solutions Brief that names the configuration (A, B, C, D, or E), the specific deliverables, the integration points with the organization's existing systems, the staff who will operate the deployed capability, the timeline, and the price. The brief is signed before build begins. Phase 2: Build (4-12 weeks depending on configuration). Movemental engineers the deployment. For Configuration A (optimization), this is typically 4-6 weeks. For Configuration B (custom agentic), 8-12 weeks. For Configuration C (content platform), 8-12 weeks plus ongoing migration. For Configuration D (network), 12-16 weeks plus per-member rollouts. The build phase includes weekly check-ins with leadership and key staff, milestone reviews, and progressive testing against real workflows rather than artificial demonstrations. Phase 3: Deployment and training (2-4 weeks). The deployment goes live progressively rather than as a single launch event. Staff who completed Skills lead the rollout to additional staff. Movemental provides documentation, role-specific training materials, troubleshooting reference, and active support during the rollout window. The deployment is considered complete when the organization can operate the capability without daily Movemental involvement. Phase 4: 90-day operational support. After deployment, Movemental remains engaged for 90 days of operational support — addressing issues as they arise, adjusting configurations based on real-world friction, supporting the organization through the inevitable surprises that emerge once a deployment meets actual operational reality. After 90 days, the engagement transitions to either an ongoing platform subscription (for hosted infrastructure) or a defined annual retainer for periodic optimization (for organization-hosted deployments).

### Seven-D: Solutions technological architecture

Movemental's Solutions deployments are built on a consistent technical foundation that adapts to the configuration. Model layer. Anthropic's Claude is the default model. Other major-provider models can be integrated where the use case warrants, with the corresponding data handling and contractual review. Model choices are documented in the Solutions Brief and revisited as the field evolves.

Application layer. Movemental's existing React, Next.js, and Tailwind front-end stack. Supabase for authentication, database, and storage. Custom agentic orchestration built on Movemental's internal framework. The application layer is consistent across deployments, which is what allows Movemental to build deeply customized solutions in the timelines we name. Data layer. Each organization's data remains in systems the organization controls (their CRM, member database, donor system, content management system, etc.) with API integrations rather than data migration. This is a deliberate architectural choice: Movemental does not become the custodian of organizational data unless explicitly engaged to do so under a hosted-platform configuration. Most deployments leave data where it lives and integrate around it. Skill layer. The Claude skills (recipes) the organization developed and adjudicated through Sandbox become the operational layer of the deployment. Solutions does not invent new use cases; it deploys what Sandbox proved. The skills are versioned, documented, and tied to the Safety documentation that governs them. Governance layer. Every deployment includes a governance layer that ties the technical capability back to the Safety documentation. Acceptable Use Policy violations are detected and surfaced. Data Handling Standards are enforced at the technical level where possible. Disclosure standards are applied to outputs that warrant them. The governance layer is what makes Solutions sustainable: the deployment cannot drift from the foundational decisions because the foundation is wired into the system.
