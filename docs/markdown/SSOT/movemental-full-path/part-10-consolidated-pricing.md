# Part ten: consolidated pricing across the path
This part consolidates pricing across all four stages, all productization levels, and all segments into a single reference. The pricing replaces the scattered pricing references throughout the prior parts of the document. When pricing is updated — based on engagement learning, market positioning, or scope refinement — the update happens here first; downstream marketing, sales, and contractual references regenerate from this source. Pricing is presented in three sections. First, a master reference table covering every offering at every level. Second, segment-specific pricing notes that name where churches, nonprofits, and institutions encounter different price points or different configuration emphasis. Third, the bundling and progression pricing — the discounts that apply when an organization commits to multi-stage engagement.

### Ten-B: Segment-specific pricing notes

The master pricing reference applies across segments, but each segment has texture that affects which offerings are most relevant and where configurations land within their pricing ranges.

## Churches

Most churches engage at the Safety and Sandbox levels first. The Field Guide and Facilitated Safety MVP serve solo pastors and smaller congregations well; the AI-Assisted Safety Product serves mid-size churches with a small staff. Sandbox engagement at the facilitated level is appropriate for churches with budget for substantive discovery; the AI-Assisted Sandbox serves churches that want strong support without full facilitation. Skills cohort facilitation is well-positioned for multi-staff churches whose staff will benefit from formation as a group. Configuration A is the most common Solutions deployment for churches; Configurations C and D become relevant for large churches with substantial sermon archives or multi-site operations. Configuration E is the configuration for denominational bodies, networks of churches, and church-planting organizations engaging at the network level.

## Nonprofits

Nonprofits often arrive with stronger AI adoption than they have governance for, which makes the Safety stage particularly valuable as a corrective rather than as foundational work. The AI-Assisted Safety Product serves the typical mid-size nonprofit well; the Facilitated MVP serves nonprofits where the board needs the structured engagement to ratify governance with confidence. Sandbox at the facilitated level is high-value for nonprofits with operational complexity — multi-program organizations, advocacy groups, social-service nonprofits with beneficiary-facing programs. Skills serves nonprofit teams whose work spans development, programs, and operations. Configurations B and C are the most common Solutions deployments for nonprofits, with Configuration B serving focused workflow needs (donor stewardship, grant writing) and Configuration C serving organizations with substantial program documentation. Configuration D becomes relevant for large nonprofits with platform-scale operational complexity.

## Institutions: seminaries, training networks, denominational bodies

Institutions typically engage at the network level from the start, which positions them toward Network Sandbox, Network Skills, Train-the-Facilitator certification, and Configuration E from the Solutions menu. The institutional engagement model means Movemental is engaged not only by the institution but by the institution's member or partner organizations through the same framework. Pricing for institutions reflects the network-scale value: Configuration E in particular is priced to reflect that the institution is buying network platform value that compounds across member organizations rather than buying engineering hours for a single deployment. Train-the-Facilitator certification is highly relevant for institutions that want Skills capacity to live inside their walls and propagate through their formation programs over time.

### Ten-C: Bundling and full-path pricing

Movemental does not require organizations to commit to the full path at intake; most engagements begin at one stage and continue based on what emerges. When Movemental knows from contract signing that an organization will move through multiple stages, the team configures the engagement for continuity: the facilitator who runs Safety transitions to Sandbox; the recipe library configured during Sandbox feeds Skills directly; the Skills cohort produces the staff who will operate the Solutions deployment. The 10% discount captures the margin Movemental gains from this continuity and shares it with the customer who committed to the full path. Organizations that choose to engage stage-by-stage are not penalized; they pay standard pricing per stage. The choice between stage-by-stage and bundled is a customer choice based on their certainty about the path. Movemental does not pressure organizations into bundles before they are ready.

### Ten-D: Pricing posture

A note on the pricing posture overall, because it informs how the team should hold pricing in conversation with customers and prospects. Movemental's pricing reflects three convictions. First, that the work is real and the value is real, and pricing should reflect that rather than reflecting the discount-anxiety patterns that consulting and SaaS pricing often falls into. The Safety MVP is not priced at $5,000 because that's what the market will bear; it's priced at $5,000 because two weeks of facilitator time on an outcome that protects the organization is honestly worth that and is honestly worth more, and the lower-priced AI-Assisted product exists for organizations whose budget cannot reach $5,000. Second, that segment-specific pricing is reductive of the work and Movemental will not do it. A church and a nonprofit and an institution doing the same work receive the same price for the same work. Discounts exist for full-path commitment, for network-scale engagement, and for context where Movemental and the organization mutually agree that the work warrants it. Discounts do not exist by category of organization. Third, that pricing is honest about what Movemental does and does not provide. Configuration A does not pretend to be Configuration D. Configuration E does not pretend to be available to every organization. The pricing structure is transparent so that the conversation between Movemental and a prospective customer can focus on what the organization actually needs rather than on what the salesperson is trying to sell.

The path is the path. The pricing reflects the path. This document keeps both coherent.
