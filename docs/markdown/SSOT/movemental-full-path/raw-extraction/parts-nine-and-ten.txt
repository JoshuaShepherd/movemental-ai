Solutions Deployment as described in Part Seven assumes a technological foundation that the prior

Movemental was incorporated and began operating in late 2025. The technological foundation was built 
primarily by a single architect over approximately seven months, on top of agentic development

Nine-B: The content and data pipeline
The content pipeline is, in our judgment, Movemental's most distinctive technical asset. Most AI-for-
organizations companies are wrappers around a model provider. Movemental holds a substrate that 
converts organizational content from heterogeneous source forms into structured, queryable, AI-
grounded data. The substrate compounds: each piece of content added becomes part of an 
organization's active intelligence layer rather than a static archive.
The pipeline accepts:
•Books and long-form manuscripts (PDF or other source) converted to structured markup, stored as 
relational rows with chapter-level granularity, with pricing and commerce identifiers integrated 
where applicable.
•Articles in markdown with YAML frontmatter, deterministically validated and migrated as 
conventions evolve, ingested into content tables via scripts rather than model-based upload.
•Course content authored as markdown week-files that serve as single-source-of-truth slices, 
scaffolded into structured course/module/lesson rows with cohort and drip-schedule support.
•Video and audio inputs extracted and transcribed (FFmpeg, Whisper-class transcription), 
structured into searchable text grounded in courses and archive search.

Nine-C: The typed entity chain
The architecture is structured as a six-layer chain that runs from database to user interface, with five of 
the six layers generated and validated automatically and the sixth (the user interface) hand-built from 
design specifications. The discipline this enforces is that backend changes propagate predictably, 
schemas and services stay in sync, and the user interface remains under deliberate design control rather 
than being mechanically generated from data shapes.
The chain runs as follows. The PostgreSQL database schema is defined in Drizzle ORM as the system of 
record. From the schema, a Zod validation layer is generated. From Zod, a service layer is generated 
using a result-pattern that handles success and failure cases consistently. From the services, REST API 
routes are generated. From the routes, TanStack Query hooks are generated. The user interface is built 
by hand using semantic Tailwind tokens locked to a pinned design source, ensuring that the visual 
surface remains a deliberate craft layer rather than a mechanical projection of the schema.
The practical commercial implication: when Movemental builds a deployment for an organization, 
schema changes specific to their work propagate automatically through the validation, service, route, 
and hook layers. The hand-built user interface is the only surface that requires deliberate engineering 
attention per organization. This is what allows custom deployments to ship in weeks rather than months 
— the substrate is generated; the surface is composed from a designed component library that is itself 
accelerated by a Stitch-to-React workflow that locks visual design as a single source of truth.
Nine-D: The multi-tenant model
The architecture was built multi-tenant from inception rather than retrofitted. This is significant because 
retrofitting multi-tenancy into a single-tenant codebase is one of the most painful migrations in 
software, and the difficulty of it is the reason most platform offerings in adjacent sectors are built single-
tenant and then re-architected at substantial cost when the platform business model becomes the right 
business model.

The model is built on Supabase Postgres with row-level security and an organization-id-based isolation 
pattern. Every relevant table carries an organization scope. The organizations table itself includes plan, 
subdomain, custom domain, and settings configuration. A tenant-organization-id pattern allows single-
tenant deployments to use the same codebase as multi-tenant network deployments — meaning that 
the Configuration A tool optimization, the Configuration D platform tenant, and the Configuration E 
multi-node network all run on the same architectural foundation, with the network deployment being 
the multi-tenant case the architecture was designed for from day one.
What this means for Configuration E specifically: when an institution engages Movemental for a network 
deployment, each member organization receives its own tenant with its own data, its own 
configuration, its own user base, and its own user interface customization, while sharing the institutional 
governance framework, the cross-tenant content pipeline where authorized, and the SEO and 
discoverability propagation across the network. The platform was built for this case. The pricing for 
Configuration E reflects that an institution buying it is not buying engineering hours; it is buying a 
structural capacity for network-scale intelligence that compounds across the member organizations.
Nine-E: Agent inventory and governance integration
Movemental maintains an inventory of approximately twenty-two named AI agents documented with 
explicit behavior boundaries. The agents fall into five functional themes: search and discovery (SEO 
scoring, keyword research, schema generation, related content semantic discovery); credibility and 
voice (voice-and-vocation coaching, voice authenticity, credibility scoring against organizational rubrics, 
theological integrity review, writing assistance with human-in-the-loop control); writing and structure 
(content structure, article companion, sermon shaping with multi-sub-agent orchestration, translation, 
agent orchestration); learning and formation (formation companion for learners, formation coach for 
staff developers, course companion for in-course assistance, missional-DNA assessment, assessment 
interpretation); and research and lab (AI Lab agent with corpus-grounded vector store and themed 
instructions, archive explorer, contextual book reading help).
The agents operate against governance constraints documented at the system level: human publish 
required for any externally-facing content, theological integrity review for content touching tradition or 
doctrine, no silent strategy execution without authorization, credibility scoring against organization-
specific rubrics, and a system of explicit behavior boundaries that names what each agent will and will 
not do.
What this means in deployment: when a Solutions configuration is delivered, the agents that operate 
within it carry the organization's Safety documentation as constraints. The Acceptable Use Policy 
informs what the agents are permitted to do. The Data Handling Standards constrain what data the 
agents can touch. The Care Boundaries shape what work the agents are structurally prevented from 
entering. The Disclosure and Attribution Standard governs what the agents annotate as AI-assisted. The

Nine-F: CRM and voice intake
Movemental has solved the constituent-relationship-management layer through a combination of 
established CRM stack components and a cutting-edge capability that warrants specific naming: a voice-
based intake system that automatically sorts relational intelligence as it captures conversations.
The CRM stack covers the standard work of a constituent relationship system: pipeline for prospects, 
learners, donors; lifecycle email sequences with deliverability handling; segmentation; integration with 
existing organizational systems where the organization already operates a CRM (Planning Center for 
churches, Bloomerang or Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud for nonprofits, denominational membership 
systems for institutions). For organizations without an existing CRM, Movemental can deploy the CRM as 
part of the platform tenant; for organizations with an existing CRM, Movemental integrates rather than 
replacing.
The voice-based intake capability is the more distinctive part. The system captures voice conversations 
— discovery calls, member onboarding, donor stewardship, pastoral intake — and automatically 
extracts and structures the relational intelligence those conversations contain. Names, relationships, 
history, areas of interest, prayer requests where appropriate, donor capacity signals where appropriate, 
follow-up commitments, and the texture of the conversation itself become structured records that route 
to the right people in the organization with the right context. The capability addresses a real pain point 
— the relational intelligence inside ministry and nonprofit conversations is constantly lost because no 
one has time to take notes — and it does so in a way that preserves the human warmth of the 
conversation rather than imposing a clipboard between the conversational partners.
What this means commercially: Configurations D and E include CRM and voice intake as integrated 
capabilities rather than as components the organization must source separately and integrate 
themselves. This significantly compresses the time-to-value for the deployment and removes one of the 
most common failure modes in AI deployments at adjacent vendors — the gap between the AI capability 
and the relationship-management substrate it must operate within.

Nine-H: The Solutions configurations, refined
Part Seven of the prior document described five Solutions configurations. Given the technological 
foundation documented in this part, the configuration set is refined to six configurations that better 
describe the actual offering spectrum, with explicit acknowledgment that the architecture supports the 
network-platform configuration as a credible offering rather than as an aspirational claim.
Configuration A — Tool Optimization.  Claude Enterprise or comparable enterprise-tier AI tool, 
configured with custom skills, prompts, and Safety integration against the organization's 
Acceptable Use Policy and Data Handling Standards. Documentation, staff training, and 90-day 
operational support included. Suitable for organizations whose Sandbox surfaced strong use 
cases that do not require new infrastructure. Most organizations should consider this 
configuration first.
Configuration B — Composed Application.  A focused web application built on Movemental's 
typed-entity stack for one or two specific workflows, integrated with the organization's existing 
systems via API. The application is deployed to the organization's infrastructure or hosted by 
Movemental as preferred. Suitable for organizations with specific high-value workflows that 
exceed what tool optimization can deliver but do not require a full platform.

discoverability propagation. The institution becomes a platform for its own network, with the

organization deployment could achieve. This is the architectural innovation Movemental is 
structurally positioned to offer.
Configuration F — Hybrid and Bespoke.  Combinations of the prior configurations and custom

Nine-I: The network multiplication thesis
Configuration E deserves explicit framing because it is the configuration where Movemental's 
architecture delivers value that no alternative offering in the sector currently delivers.
The thesis is this. A denominational network with fifty member churches, each running its own AI tools 
independently, produces fifty parallel AI deployments that do not compound. Each church reinvents 
prompts. Each church builds its own recipes. Each church surfaces independently in search. The 
denomination, despite holding the relational and theological coherence of fifty congregations, gets no 
platform value from that coherence at the AI layer. The same is true for a training network with its 
institutes, a seminary with its partner programs, a multi-site church with its campuses, a nonprofit 
network with its chapters.
When the same network deploys Configuration E, the institution holds the platform; the member 
organizations operate as sub-tenants with their own configured slice of it; the institutional governance 
framework propagates across the network; content produced by any node becomes discoverable across 
the network where authorized; SEO and content optimization compound rather than competing; agent 
capability matures across the network as the network learns. The network self-perpetuates as a 
platform-scale intelligence rather than as fifty independent deployments.
This is the structural innovation Movemental is positioned to offer. It is not a feature; it is the 
architectural premise the platform was built on. Configurations D and E are priced accordingly because 
the value an institution receives is not engineering hours but the platform-scale advantage their network 
gains. An institution that deploys Configuration E for fifty member organizations is buying something 
that no off-the-shelf tool can deliver and that no consulting firm can build in a reasonable timeline 
starting from scratch. It is buying multi-tenant platform value that exists because the architecture was 
designed for it from inception.

the institutional level plus 50-150 hours per member organization.

They are not engagements an organization can run in the margins. They become possible when the 
organization has decided that platform-scale AI capability is part of their next chapter and is willing to 
invest the leadership attention deployment requires.

Ten-B: Segment-specific pricing notes
The master pricing reference applies across segments, but each segment has texture that affects which 
offerings are most relevant and where configurations land within their pricing ranges.
Churches
Most churches engage at the Safety and Sandbox levels first. The Field Guide and Facilitated Safety MVP 
serve solo pastors and smaller congregations well; the AI-Assisted Safety Product serves mid-size 
churches with a small staff. Sandbox engagement at the facilitated level is appropriate for churches with 
budget for substantive discovery; the AI-Assisted Sandbox serves churches that want strong support 
without full facilitation. Skills cohort facilitation is well-positioned for multi-staff churches whose staff 
will benefit from formation as a group. Configuration A is the most common Solutions deployment for 
churches; Configurations C and D become relevant for large churches with substantial sermon archives 
or multi-site operations. Configuration E is the configuration for denominational bodies, networks of 
churches, and church-planting organizations engaging at the network level.
Nonprofits
Nonprofits often arrive with stronger AI adoption than they have governance for, which makes the 
Safety stage particularly valuable as a corrective rather than as foundational work. The AI-Assisted 
Safety Product serves the typical mid-size nonprofit well; the Facilitated MVP serves nonprofits where 
the board needs the structured engagement to ratify governance with confidence. Sandbox at the 
facilitated level is high-value for nonprofits with operational complexity — multi-program organizations, 
advocacy groups, social-service nonprofits with beneficiary-facing programs. Skills serves nonprofit 
teams whose work spans development, programs, and operations. Configurations B and C are the most 
common Solutions deployments for nonprofits, with Configuration B serving focused workflow needs 
(donor stewardship, grant writing) and Configuration C serving organizations with substantial program 
documentation. Configuration D becomes relevant for large nonprofits with platform-scale operational 
complexity.
Institutions: seminaries, training networks, denominational bodies
Institutions typically engage at the network level from the start, which positions them toward Network 
Sandbox, Network Skills, Train-the-Facilitator certification, and Configuration E from the Solutions menu. 
The institutional engagement model means Movemental is engaged not only by the institution but by 
the institution's member or partner organizations through the same framework. Pricing for institutions 
reflects the network-scale value: Configuration E in particular is priced to reflect that the institution is

What the bundling discount reflects
The bundle discount is not a margin sacrifice; it reflects engagement efficiency. When Movemental 
knows from contract signing that an organization will move through multiple stages, the team 
configures the engagement for continuity: the facilitator who runs Safety transitions to Sandbox; the 
recipe library configured during Sandbox feeds Skills directly; the Skills cohort produces the staff who 
will operate the Solutions deployment. The 10% discount captures the margin Movemental gains from 
this continuity and shares it with the customer who committed to the full path.
Organizations that choose to engage stage-by-stage are not penalized; they pay standard pricing per 
stage. The choice between stage-by-stage and bundled is a customer choice based on their certainty 
about the path. Movemental does not pressure organizations into bundles before they are ready.
Ten-D: Pricing posture
A note on the pricing posture overall, because it informs how the team should hold pricing in 
conversation with customers and prospects.
Movemental's pricing reflects three convictions. First, that the work is real and the value is real, and 
pricing should reflect that rather than reflecting the discount-anxiety patterns that consulting and SaaS 
pricing often falls into. The Safety MVP is not priced at $5,000 because that's what the market will bear; 
it's priced at $5,000 because two weeks of facilitator time on an outcome that protects the organization 
is honestly worth that and is honestly worth more, and the lower-priced AI-Assisted product exists for 
organizations whose budget cannot reach $5,000.
Second, that segment-specific pricing is reductive of the work and Movemental will not do it. A church 
and a nonprofit and an institution doing the same work receive the same price for the same work. 
Discounts exist for full-path commitment, for network-scale engagement, and for context where 
Movemental and the organization mutually agree that the work warrants it. Discounts do not exist by 
category of organization.
Third, that pricing is honest about what Movemental does and does not provide. Configuration A does 
not pretend to be Configuration D. Configuration E does not pretend to be available to every 
organization. The pricing structure is transparent so that the conversation between Movemental and a 
prospective customer can focus on what the organization actually needs rather than on what the 
salesperson is trying to sell.
