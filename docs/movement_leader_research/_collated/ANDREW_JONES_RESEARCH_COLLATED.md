## Editorial bio

Andrew Jones, known online as Tall Skinny Kiwi, is a New Zealand-born missionary, church developer, and long-horizon chronicler of the emerging and missional church conversation. His corpus positions him less as a trade-book author and more as a connector and real-time narrator of movement life: decades of blog writing, podcasting, and field reflection across mission, church innovation, spirituality, and digital culture. Sources consistently describe his two-track vocation: embodied third-sector and mission work across 70+ countries over 30+ years, alongside continuous digital publishing that began in the early web era.

His formation arc runs from New Zealand and Western Australia into San Francisco, Prague, broader European work, and eventually the Orkney Islands. Across that arc he held organizational roles with BOAZ, Church Mission Society, NZCMS, and Dawn Ministries while maintaining a stable public identity under Tall Skinny Kiwi. Recurring framework-level emphases include rhizome-style church planting, mission beginning in local ordinary contexts, resistance to reducing "emerging church" to aesthetics, and treating digital/virtual culture as valid mission terrain rather than an external threat.

Current footprint: ongoing writing at tallskinnykiwi.com, active Tall Skinny Kiwi podcast distribution, and conference/festival speaking (including Greenbelt 2022, "In The Metaverse As In Heaven"), with a corpus that is broad and durable but distributed across multiple platforms and historical migrations.

Sources: `profile/biography.md`, `profile/identity.md`, `_staff/identity-verification.md`, `content/frameworks.md`, `digital-presence/platforms.md`, `digital-presence/websites.md`.
