## Editorial bio

Danielle Strickland is a Canadian author, speaker, and justice advocate whose ministry fuses Wesleyan-holiness spirituality with frontline social justice. Raised in Toronto by parents who were both orphaned, she came to faith out of a turbulent adolescence — addiction from age 12 and time in jail — with a pivotal turn during missionary work in Malawi, where she encountered "the Jesus who invited me to lose me in the midst of this massive vision he had to redeem the world."[^caring] Commissioned as a Salvation Army officer in 1995 (Canada & Bermuda Territory), she spent roughly 22 years in the Army, eventually reaching the rank of Major, with appointments across Canada, Russia, Australia, and the United States — co-founding The Salvation Army War College in Vancouver (2003), serving as Territorial Social Justice Director in Australia, running brothel chaplaincy and anti-trafficking work in Edmonton, and becoming Territorial Social Justice Secretary for the USA Western Territory in Los Angeles (2015).[^enacademic][^caring]

In December 2017 she resigned her officership to "mobilize *the whole church*," launching a portfolio of ventures under **Boundless Communications Inc.**: **Infinitum** (a monastic-style "rule of life"), **Amplify Peace** (Jesus-rooted peacemaking pilgrimages, co-founded with Lisa Jernigan), **Brave Global** (anti-trafficking prevention for at-risk girls, co-founded with Noemi Chavez), the **Women Speakers Collective**, **IMBY**, **Hagar's Voice**, and **Deep & Wide Academy**.[^initiatives] She briefly served as a Teaching Pastor at The Meeting House (Oakville, Ontario) from 2019, resigning in March 2022 in solidarity with an abuse survivor during the Bruxy Cavey misconduct case.[^chvn]

Her published voice spans more than a dozen books — among them *The Liberating Truth* (2011), *A Beautiful Mess* (2014), *The Ultimate Exodus* (2017), *The Zombie Gospel* (2017), *Better Together* (2020), *Human(Kind)* (with Ashlee Eiland), *Waging Peace* (with Diana Oestreich), and *The Other Side of Hope* (2022) — plus the *Right Side Up* podcast and a deep, reciprocal relationship with the Alan Hirsch / Michael Frost / 100 Movements world (the *Reframation* foreword, the *Need to Know* female-leadership volume she curated with Hirsch's introduction, and her endorsement of Kate Coleman's *Metamorph*).[^goodreads][^needtoknow]

### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not separate spirituality from justice, or comfort the reader into private piety. Strickland's most-corrected misreading is the assumption that the gospel is primarily about personal benefit ("When I first encountered Jesus, it was about what he could do for me") or that holiness can be lived comfortably. Her consistent correction: "we can't properly live out the gospel without it being uncomfortable," and discomfort itself is "an agent for change." Her voice fuses personal holiness with social reform and refuses a spirituality that does not "live differently." Likewise, do not flatten her gender argument into grievance — she frames it as hopeful ("we have the answer"). Derived from her Outreach and Caring Magazine interviews.[^outreach][^caring]
