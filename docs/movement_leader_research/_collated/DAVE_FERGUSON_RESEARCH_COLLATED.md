## Editorial bio

Dave Ferguson is a church planter, author, and movement leader who has spent more than three decades proving a single conviction: the church is built to multiply, not merely to add. On March 5, 1989, he and his brother Jon gathered five college friends in the cafeteria of Naperville Central High School and held the first service of what became Community Christian Church — one of the earliest multi-site churches in America and a Chicago-area community of roughly 5,500. His formation runs through a church-planting household (his father planted a church in the Restoration Movement tradition after the family moved from rural Missouri to Chicago when Dave was about four) and a Restoration Movement education at Lincoln Christian University (BA, 1986) and Wheaton College Graduate School (MA, 1991).[^bio][^identity]

In 2001 he launched NewThing to carry the reproducing-church vision beyond his own campuses; it grew into a network that planted thousands of churches across dozens of nations before merging into Exponential in 2025. As co-founder and CEO/President of Exponential, he convenes the world's largest church-planting conference and resource network, organized around the "16% Mission." His books trace the conviction maturing in public — from church-strategy method (*The Big Idea*, 2007) through movement vision (*Exponential*, 2010; *On the Verge*, 2011, with Alan Hirsch) and redefined metrics (*Keeping Score*, 2014), to relational evangelism (*Finding Your Way Back to God*, 2015; *B.L.E.S.S.*, 2021), to his defining work on leadership, *Hero Maker* (2018, with Warren Bird), and his 2026 turn toward sustainable leader health, *Multiplier*. Along the way he built a toolkit of named, teachable frameworks — the five hero-making practices, the BLESS rhythm, the Five Levels of a multiplying church, ICNU conversations, the apprenticeship ladder, and the "16% Mission."[^books][^theology]

In 2025 he enacted his own theology. He folded NewThing into Exponential under the banner "Kingdom over Castle," and after an eighteen-month public apprenticeship he handed the Lead Pastor role at Community Christian to Ted Coniaris and stepped back to Co-Founding Pastor. Colleagues describe him as the operator to Alan Hirsch's theologian — the practitioner who takes apostolic vision and turns it into a system a church can actually run. "My fruit grows on other people's trees," he likes to say. "I'm the platform; I'm not the show."[^cv][^voice]

## Theological positioning

| Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Evangelical; Restoration Movement heritage (independent Christian Churches / Churches of Christ); Lincoln Christian University; non-creedal, Scripture-centered, baptism-emphasizing |
| Ecclesiology | Apostolic-reproducing: the church is a sent, multiplying organism, not a gathered, attractional institution; multi-site and networked-church oriented |

| Christology | Reads the Gospels through Jesus's time-allocation (73% with the Twelve, 27% with the crowds) as the hermeneutical key for apprenticeship-based discipleship |

### Distinctive claims

- The leader's primary calling is to **make heroes of others**, not to be the hero — an identity inversion applied at every organizational level (ICNU, apprenticeship, permission-giving, succession, the NewThing–Exponential merger).
- Churches should measure a **kingdom scorecard** (disciples made, leaders released, churches planted), not attendance and budget.
- Multiplication is **possible for most churches**, not just pioneering ones — with the right framework and culture shift, churches can move toward Level 4–5.
- Organizational structures should be **dissolved** when kingdom mission requires it — "Kingdom over Castle."
- Sustainable multiplication requires **leader health first**: "you can't multiply something that's not first alive in you."
