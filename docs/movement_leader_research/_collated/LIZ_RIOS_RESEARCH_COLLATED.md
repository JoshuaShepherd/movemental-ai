## Editorial bio

Rev. Dr. Elizabeth "Liz" D. Rios is an Afro-Boricua (Nuyorican Afro-Latina) theologian, educator, and activist born in Brooklyn and raised on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. She discerned a call to leadership at age 11, was formed in the Latino Pentecostal (Assemblies of God) world of New York City — initially under Bishop Raymond Rivera and the Latino Pastoral Action Center, with early credentials through Primitive Christian Church, NYC — and is now ordained in the Christian Church (Disciples of Christ), where she serves on the Commission on Ministry. She writes and teaches from a mujerista frame (the Ada María Isasi-Díaz lineage) and a "barrio theology" rooted in urban Latinx neighborhoods, integrating Pentecostal/charismatic formation with a justice-oriented ecclesiology. (profile/identity.md, profile/biography.md)

Her institutional footprint runs simultaneously across denominational, academic, and movement spaces. She founded the Center for Emerging Female Leadership in NYC (1996–2001), served as Assistant Professor of Christian Education and Executive Director of Advancement at Nyack College (mid-2000s, where she launched the Urban Ministry Conferences), and has been Consulting Editor at *Outreach Magazine* since 2008. In 2020 she founded Passion2Plant — described in the dossier as the only national BIPOC-woman-led church planting network in the United States — and founded/curates The Passion Center, an online missional faith community and training platform. In 2024 she launched Púlpito Fellows, a Lilly Endowment–funded bilingual preaching fellowship run in partnership with Urban Strategies. She holds a BA, MA, and EdD (Nova Southeastern), a DMin (Southeastern), and a 2025 MA in Social Justice from Union Theological Seminary. (profile/biography.md, network/affiliations.md)

Her authored corpus includes the sole-authored *Don't Buy The Lie* (Ghetto Rose Publications, 2012), a contributor essay in 100 Movements Publishing's *Need to Know* (2023, curated by Danielle Strickland), co-curation of *Rhythms of Rest* (Our Daily Bread, with Amb. Suzan Johnson Cook), and the Oxford University Press chapter "The Women are Warriors." She sits on the board of Sojourners, on the executive team of the Latino Leadership Circle, and serves as Senior Consultant at Lisa Sharon Harper's Freedom Road and as adjunct faculty at Fuller Theological Seminary. In October 2025 she became the only woman inducted into Palm Beach Atlantic University's inaugural Florida College of Preachers. (content/content-audit.md, profile/biography.md)
