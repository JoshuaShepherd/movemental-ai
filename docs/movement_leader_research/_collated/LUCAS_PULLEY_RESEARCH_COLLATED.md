## Editorial bio

Lucas Pulley is one of the central operational figures in the North American microchurch movement. For fourteen-plus years he has planted and directed decentralized networks of grassroots churches — first founding the **Carbondale Movement** (later the Carbondale Underground) on the Southern Illinois University campus while on staff with InterVarsity Christian Fellowship, then moving south to inherit Brian Sanders' executive seat at the **Underground Network** in Tampa. He continues to pastor a neighborhood house church inside Tampa's inner-city subsidized housing projects with his wife Jami and their three children — a context that is not symbolic but is the soil from which all of his writing emerges.[^bio][^koinesune]

His path to ministry was unusual. Lucas began not in seminary but in **pure mathematics**: a BA in the Theory of Mathematics at SIU Carbondale and a stint on a mathematical research team at **Technische Universität Braunschweig** in Germany, before completing a **Master of Global Leadership** with a Missions & Missiology emphasis at Fuller Theological Seminary. The mathematician is still visible in the pastor — the repeated reach for systems-theory vocabulary (network effects, Granovetter thresholds, starfish vs. spider, two-entity models) and the willingness to treat ecclesiology as a hypothesis "constantly under investigation."[^identity][^voice] His public written corpus is lean — roughly ten Medium essays, one Missio Alliance article (*The Parable of the White Pastor*), one ~53-minute Teachable session, and **zero books** — while his embodied teaching output (conferences, cohorts, coaching, in-house podcast episodes, sister-movement consultation) is extensive and largely under-digitized.[^books][^articles]

In 2024–25 the Underground restructured its top layer: **Joel Repic** moved into the Executive Director seat, **Tomy Wilkerson** became Tampa Director, **Stacy Gaskins** became Managing Director, and **Lucas took the Movements Director lane** — the translocal, apostolic-team seat that supports the 100+ Tampa microchurches and dozens of sister movements worldwide. The line he repeats verbatim across podcasts from *Theology in the Raw* to *Shifting Culture* to *The Starfish and the Church* is the one he organizes his life around: **cities transformed by the priesthood of all believers activated in the mission of God.**[^identity][^bio]

## Theological positioning

| Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Non-denominational evangelical (mere-Christian); missional-incarnational stream, specifically the Underground / microchurch / Hubology lineage; outside seeker-sensitive / attractional evangelicalism |
| Ecclesiology | Decentralized microchurch ecclesiology; church = extended spiritual family (worship + community + mission); "ecclesial minimum" over ecclesial maximum; church as verb, not noun |
| Missiology | Margins-first ("start there"); missio Dei via Hirsch / Sanders / Fuller; mission-specific vs. distributive microchurch archetypes |
| Leadership | Servant-equipper / Hubology: local elders, equipping servants (*diakonoi*), apostolic teams; serve rather than consolidate power; accelerate readiness rather than gatekeep |
| Christology | Christocentric or meaningless — "without Jesus at the center, all of this is meaningless"; Jesus as the leader the spiritual family is committed to |

| Culture | Culture beats programs (Granovetter thresholds); leaders lower the behavioral threshold for missional practice; co-create culture rather than cast vision |

### Distinctive claims

- **Church as verb, not noun.** The institution is not the point; the gathered-and-sent family is.
- **The ecclesial minimum is worship + community + mission** — everything beyond that is discretionary scaffolding that must be justified.
- **Power redistribution is non-optional in multicultural church** — programmatic multiculturalism without surrendering second-order (narrative/budget/decision) power is hollow; the future "must also be minority led."
- **Calling is immediate and communal, not self-actualization** — "your conversion is your commission"; pipelines over-gatekeep readiness.
- **Imports secular *models* (not metaphors) into ecclesiology** — Granovetter's threshold model and Brafman's starfish are used as structural theology, his clearest differentiator from peers.
- **Even innovative microchurch leaders harbor celebrity-growth ambition** — a spiritual-formation problem ("I have one, too"), not a strategy problem.
