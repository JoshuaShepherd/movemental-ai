## Editorial bio

Meghan Larissa Good is a Mennonite pastor, theologian, and award-winning preacher whose entire body of work turns on one question: what happens when you read the Bible — and rebuild your faith — through the lens of Jesus? Trained at Gordon College, Duke Divinity School, and Portland Seminary, she became in 2018 the first-ever recipient of the Frederick Buechner Award for Excellence in Preaching, and her voice carries that lineage: literary, narrative-driven, honest about doubt, and quietly funny. After completing her M.Div. at Duke she worked at Duke's Center for Reconciliation and spent time teaching and preaching in Uganda before entering Mennonite pastoral ministry — orienting her early on toward the global church and the social-political dimensions of the gospel.[^bio][^identity]

Her first book, *The Bible Unwrapped: Making Sense of Scripture Today* (Herald Press, 2018, foreword by Gregory Boyd), translated her doctoral work on the dialogic shape of Christian authority into an accessible guide for ordinary readers and won Outreach Magazine's 2019 Resource of the Year in Theology and Biblical Studies. Her second, *Divine Gravity: Sparking a Movement to Recover a Better Christian Story* (Herald Press, 2023, foreword by Dennis R. Edwards, endorsed by Richard Mouw, Brian McLaren, Greg Boyd, and David Gushee), moved from how to read Scripture to what kind of church to become — a call for a Spirit-led, grassroots new reformation grounded in cross-shaped power and centered-set belonging.[^books]

After more than sixteen years in pastoral ministry — at Albany Mennonite Church in Oregon and then over a decade as Lead Pastor of Trinity Mennonite Church in Glendale, Arizona — she resigned her pastorate effective May 2, 2025, to take a movement-level role as Resource Development Manager at Jesus Collective, where she also chairs the international Theology Circle, and she continues to teach in AMBS's Doctor of Ministry program. She produces Jesus-centered devotionals on her "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel and a weekly devotional newsletter from a base in Dayton, Ohio.[^org][^resign]

## Frameworks

### The Jesus Lens (Christocentric Hermeneutic)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2018 (*The Bible Unwrapped*; DMin dissertation) |
| Components | (1) Jesus as the fullness of God (Col. 1:19, Heb. 1:1-3, John 1:1); (2) Jesus as Scripture's authoritative interpreter; (3) every text — including OT violence, difficult Pauline and apocalyptic passages — read through and toward its culmination in Christ; (4) Christ as the "canon within the canon" while honoring all of Scripture |
| Visual / model | "Putting on our Jesus glasses" (verbal metaphor) |
| Adoption evidence | *The Bible Unwrapped* (Outreach 2019 Resource of the Year; 150 Goodreads ratings); informed the Anabaptist Community Bible project (2022 keynotes); "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel (54+ Luke devotionals, 2026) |
| Source | [heraldpress.com](https://heraldpress.com/9781513802343/the-bible-unwrapped/); [anabaptistworld.org](https://anabaptistworld.org/planning-an-anabaptist-bible/) |

Reading all of Scripture through Jesus as the supreme interpretive authority: "Jesus is not the softer side of God that gets averaged" with divine judgment, but the fullness of God against which every text is evaluated.

### Dialogic Biblical Authority

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2018 (DMin dissertation *Between Breaths*; *The Bible Unwrapped*) |
| Components | Authority emerges from dynamic conversation among five dialogue partners — Scripture, tradition, Spirit, reason, and community/experience — rather than from a single static source (sola scriptura alone) |
| Visual / model | "Dialogical paradigm" (described, not diagrammed) |
| Adoption evidence | Dissertation publicly available in George Fox Digital Commons; underlies *The Bible Unwrapped*; informs the AMBS Teaching Leadership competency she oversees |
| Source | [digitalcommons.georgefox.edu/dmin/272](https://digitalcommons.georgefox.edu/dmin/272/) |

The dissertation argues Christian authority "cannot be possessed as a static and absolute quality of any single entity"; its shape is "fundamentally relational" and dialogic, positioning her against both fundamentalist biblicism and liberal dismissal.

### Divine Gravity / Centered-Set Ecclesiology

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*Divine Gravity*) |
| Components | Jesus as the gravitational center holding together diverse, naturally repelling people; belonging defined by trajectory of motion toward Jesus rather than by boundary positions; centered-set over bounded-set church (drawing on Mark D. Baker's application of Paul Hiebert's missiological model) |
| Visual / model | Gravity metaphor — objects drawn toward a common center |
| Adoption evidence | *Divine Gravity* (4.36–4.62 Goodreads; endorsed by Mouw, McLaren, Gushee, Boyd); featured on Jesus Collective's "Jesus Centered Foundations" resource page; MennoCon21 address |
| Source | [heraldpress.com](https://heraldpress.com/9781513813127/divine-gravity/) |

Christian community is defined not by doctrinal fences (who is in/out) but by orbital direction toward Jesus: "Individuals and groups who would naturally repel each other will be held together when they find a center of gravity in something bigger and better."

### The Better Christian Story (Eight Theses)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*Divine Gravity*) |
| Components | (1) Jesus as authoritative interpretive lens for God and Scripture; (2) salvation as setting all things right on earth; (3) discipleship as acknowledging and following Jesus; (4) Christian community as both means and goal of God's transformation; (5) God's people as reconciliation ambassadors; (6) the Spirit guiding and resourcing mission; (7) sacrificial love overcoming evil; (8) church unity centering on Jesus as gravitational focal point |
| Visual / model | — (organized in three parts: a dilemma, a rediscovery, a proposal) |
| Adoption evidence | The structural backbone of *Divine Gravity*; cited in Cramer's *Anabaptist World* review as "a manifesto for reforming" the faith |
| Source | [heraldpress.com](https://heraldpress.com/9781513813127/divine-gravity/); [anabaptistworld.org](https://anabaptistworld.org/starting-point-for-a-new-reformation/) |

Eight interlocking convictions that constitute a recovered Christian narrative, framed as the content of a Spirit-led "new reformation."

### Cross-Shaped (Kenotic) Power

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*Divine Gravity*); developed in "Jesus and the Devil's Bargain" (2024) |
| Components | God's kingdom advances through self-sacrifice, vulnerability, and suffering love, not coercive force; Jesus's Matthew 4 temptations were genuine (power for righteous ends was available) and refused; cross-shaped power "appears slower, more painful, seemingly like losing before winning" but alone defeats evil |
| Visual / model | — |
| Adoption evidence | Central to *Divine Gravity*; journal essay "Jesus and the Devil's Bargain" (2024); rooted in Anabaptist peace-church theology |
| Source | [meghanlarissagood.com](https://meghanlarissagood.com/2024/02/03/jesus-and-the-devils-bargain/) |

A theology of power: coercive force even for righteous ends is a "devil's bargain" rejected by Jesus; nonviolence flows from the very character of God revealed in Christ.

### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not render Jesus as a counterweight to be balanced against divine wrath, violence, or judgment — the "averaging" misreading is the one Good most consistently corrects. In her voice, Jesus is "not the softer side of God that gets averaged" with anything; he is the fullness of God against which every other text is read. Avoid framing her Christocentrism as a relativizing of Scripture (she insists on reading the whole text faithfully) and avoid stripping Jesus from her movement and peacemaking language (her named correction to her own tradition is that "reconciliation work that gives up on proclaiming Jesus loses its very ground"). Derived from her quoted positions in [anabaptistworld.org/planning-an-anabaptist-bible](https://anabaptistworld.org/planning-an-anabaptist-bible/) and the MennoCon21 coverage ([anabaptistworld.org](https://anabaptistworld.org/mennocon-question-do-peacemakers-need-jesus/)).

| Tradition | Anabaptist/Mennonite home base (MC USA, ordained); ecumenically engaged across post-evangelical, progressive evangelical, and mainline circles |
| Ecclesiology | Centered-set — belonging defined by trajectory toward Jesus, not boundary positions; movement-oriented over institution-preserving |
| Missiology | Reconciliation-centered; the church as ambassador community for God's cosmic restoration; peacemaking requires Jesus at the center |
| Leadership | Prophetic authority through cross-bearing and costly discipleship, not charisma or prestige; against Christian celebrity culture |
| Christology | High and normative — Jesus is the fullness of God (Col. 1:19), the exact imprint (Heb. 1:1-3), the Word (John 1:1); all Scripture reads toward him |
| Anthropology / formation | Many carry a "wounded image of God"; transformation of the God-image (through Jesus encounter) enables transformation of human identity |
| Culture | Strong contextual reading; reads the church as a contrast community advancing "from the margins" (Bethlehem and Capernaum, not Rome and Jerusalem), not a power center |

### Distinctive claims

- **"Jesus is not the softer side of God that gets averaged"** — Jesus reveals the fullness of God against which every text is evaluated, not one divine attribute balanced against wrath.
- **The "Divine Gravity" metaphor as ecclesiology** — Jesus as a gravitational center; belonging and unity emerge from shared orbital direction rather than boundary enforcement.
- **The wounded-image-of-God pastoral framework** — a specific spiritual injury (a violence-shaped God-image) met with a specific remedy (encounter with the Jesus-centered God).
- **Peace without Jesus loses its ground** — a prophetic corrective to her own peace-church tradition: reconciliation work needs an explicit Christ-center to hold together those who would naturally repel each other.
- **Christian authority is dialogic, not monolithic** — authority functions through living conversation among Scripture, tradition, Spirit, reason, and community.
