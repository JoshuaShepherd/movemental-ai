## Editorial bio

Neil Cole left a Grace Brethren pastorate in Alta Loma, California in 1999, started a church in the smoking section of a Long Beach coffeehouse called The Coffee Tavern, and named it Awakening Chapel. From that one community he and Phil Helfer built Church Multiplication Associates, the organization whose Life Transformation Group — a two-or-three-person Scripture, confession, and prayer unit — and Organic Church Planters' Greenhouse cycle shaped a generation of American church planters. Cole was born and raised in Los Angeles in 1960, came to faith while studying Fine Art at California State University, Long Beach, and worked as an ocean lifeguard before ministry — a frame that later became the controlling metaphor of *Search & Rescue* (Baker, 2008).

The nine-year publishing run that followed put him at the center of the missional conversation. *Organic Church* (Jossey-Bass, 2005), with a foreword by Leonard Sweet, named the category; *Church 3.0* (Jossey-Bass, 2010, foreword by Francis Chan), *Search & Rescue* (Baker, 2008), *Organic Leadership* (Baker, 2009), *Church Transfusion* (with Phil Helfer, Jossey-Bass, 2012), and *Primal Fire* (Tyndale Momentum, 2014) followed. In 2015 he co-founded 100 Movements with Alan Hirsch, Dave Rhodes, Will Mancini, and Jessie Cruickshank — the design team behind the six movement competencies.

Then, by his own account, God told him to stop. He kept the silence for roughly thirteen years — no public speaking in the U.S., no social media, no blogging — and re-emerged tentatively in 2024 on *Shifting Culture*, the *reChurch Podcast*, and KC Underground's *Upside Down Kingdom*. He continues to write quieter, more interior books — *Rising Tides*, *Pray*, *VIRAL*, *Journeys* — under his own Starling Initiatives imprint, narrating his own audiobooks, and teaches Church Development at Kairos Pacific University in Long Beach.

### Note on the silence period

In *Shifting Culture* Ep. 178 (Apr 2024) and KCU's *Upside Down Kingdom* (Aug 2025) Cole describes a ~13-year season in which God told him to stop speaking publicly in the U.S., refrain from social media, and cease blogging. The Cole-Slaw blog goes dormant after June 7, 2017; his last big-house trade release is *One Thing* (Thomas Nelson, Nov 2015). The exact start date is not stated; working hypothesis is ~2011–2012 to ~2024.

## Frameworks

### Life Transformation Group (LTG)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 1999 (*Cultivating a Life for God*); operational at Awakening Chapel from 1999 |
| Components | (1) 2-or-3-person same-gender cell; (2) weekly Scripture reading (large portions); (3) mutual confession/accountability; (4) prayer for non-Christians (named lost) |
| Visual / model | One-page card with reading plan, accountability questions, and prayer-for-lost list |
| Adoption evidence | CMA claim of "thousands of churches in 50 states and 50+ nations"; IMB adapted LTG into the **CHAT** discipleship tool; used in Saddleback's PEACE Plan church-planting work (Curtis Sergeant); cited across Steve Addison and DMM-stream literature |
| Source | [*Cultivating a Life for God* (ChurchSmart, 1999)](https://archive.org/details/cultivatinglifef0000cole) |

The LTG is Cole's foundational reproducible discipleship technology — the smallest viable cell that already contains the whole DNA of the church.

### DNA of the Body of Christ (D / N / A)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | early 2000s; codified in *Organic Church* (2005) |
| Components | **D**ivine Truth (faith); **N**urturing Relationships (love); **A**postolic Mission (hope) — all three must be present in every cell |
| Visual / model | The D/N/A "genetic code" present in every reproducible unit of the body |
| Adoption evidence | Core vocabulary of CMA / Greenhouse training; structurally parallel to Alan Hirsch's mDNA (mutually acknowledged in endorsements) |
| Source | [*Organic Church* (Jossey-Bass, 2005)](https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/249457.Neil_Cole) |

Cole insists the DNA must not be "supplanted, supplemented or separated" — the lowest reproducible unit (the LTG, two or three people) must already contain all three strands.

## Theological positioning

| Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Evangelical; origin in the Fellowship of Grace Brethren Churches (Charis Fellowship); now non-denominational / organic-church; Christocentric grammar closer to the Anabaptist/Mennonite stream than to Reformed-confessional |
| Ecclesiology | Organic, low-church, decentralized; the church is "the presence of Jesus among His people, a family on mission together"; actively "low-bar" — anyone can do church, but the bar of discipleship is raised |
| Missiology | Multiplication-first; disciple-multiplication is the irreducible unit; compatible with and a bridge into the DMM / CPM stream (LTG → IMB CHAT) |
| Leadership | Anti-hierarchy; leadership emerges naturally from the body, not credentialed from outside; "Senior Pastor" is not a biblical title; decentralized across the five APEST gifts |
| Christology | Radically Christocentric: "listen to Jesus and do what He says" is the irreducible operating principle |
| Anthropology / formation | Formation through small reproducing cells (the LTG); late-career emphasis on interior intimacy, listening, and obscurity over platform |
| Culture | "Church happens where life happens" — the coffeehouse, not the chapel; incarnational, going-to-people rather than attractional bring-them-in |

### Distinctive claims

- The smallest reproducible unit of the church (two or three people, the LTG) must already contain the whole DNA — Divine Truth, Nurturing Relationships, Apostolic Mission.
- "Lower the bar of how church is done so that anyone can do it; raise the bar of what it means to be a disciple so that not just anyone will do it."
- APEST gifts are **gifts of Jesus** (Eph 4:11), not interchangeable with the Spirit's gifts (Rom 12 / 1 Cor 12), and they decentralize rather than concentrate leadership.
- Legacy is measured in examples and cultural shifts, not in permanent institutional structures (2010 *Christianity Today* response).
- Obscurity is a positive spiritual category: intimacy with Christ produces fruit that platform-building cannot.
