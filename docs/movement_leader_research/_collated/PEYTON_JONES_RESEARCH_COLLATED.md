## Editorial bio

Peyton Jones is an American serial church planter, author, and trainer best known for reframing church planting as both "art and science" in his best-selling Zondervan textbook *Church Plantology* (2021). A native of Huntington Beach, California (born in Washington, D.C.), he entered ministry at nineteen and left a career as a psychiatric / drug-rehab nurse to spend roughly twelve years planting in Wales — by his own description "the most unchurched part of the UK, with less than 1% church attendance." He began as an evangelist at "Sandfields," the historic Aberavon church once pastored by D. Martyn Lloyd-Jones, before founding New Breed Church Planting, which pioneered unconventional planting locations. He holds an MA in Theology (Pastoral Studies, 2010) from W.E.S.T. (Wales Evangelical School of Theology).[^nbt][^gr]

Returning to the United States, Jones built a training and media footprint around an apostolic, first-century vision of church planting. He authored or rewrote church-planting curriculum for the North American Mission Board (NAMB), ran training centers across North America, and served as Content Director for Exponential and for the Through the Word Bible-teaching ministry. With Pete Mitchell he co-founded Church Planter Magazine and the long-running Church Planter Podcast (640+ episodes over roughly nine years), alongside the Hardcore Church Planting and Ministry Ninja podcasts.[^expo][^cpp]

His body of work runs from the "punk-rock" apostolic manifesto *Church Zero* (David C. Cook, 2013) through *Reaching the Unreached* (Zondervan, 2017, with Alan Hirsch) and the flagship *Church Plantology* (2021), to *Discipology: The Art and Science of Making Disciples* (Zondervan, 2026). A through-line unites them: church planting is not the goal but the *result* of disciple-making. He currently leads NewBreed Training and teaches church-planting courses at Calvary University.[^books][^discipology]

### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not frame **church planting as the goal**, or measure success by sites/attendance/buildings. Jones's most-corrected reader misreading is exactly this: his consistent correction is that "the first lesson of church plantology is that planting a church should never be our focus," and "if you plant churches, discipleship may or may not happen. Yet if you devote yourself to making disciples, churches will inevitably be planted." In his voice, planting is always downstream of disciple-making and apostolic, Acts-shaped mission. Derived from *Church Plantology* (via [Goodreads quotes](https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/5814494.Peyton_Jones)).

### Distinctive claims

- "Planting a church should never be our focus" — disciple-making is the focus; churches are the inevitable byproduct.
- Church planting is simultaneously an **art** and a **science** (the "Plantology" thesis); the same dual frame applies to disciple-making ("Discipology").
- The post-Christian West mirrors the apostles' **pre-Christian** world, so first-century methods are not nostalgia but the live solution set.
- Church success measured by gospel "penetration, saturation, and maturation," not attendance.
