## 3. Editorial bio

Dr. Leo Rowland Smith ("L. Rowland Smith") is a Colorado Springs–based pastor, missional practitioner, author, and adjunct educator who operates at the intersection of national mission training, regional micro-church networking, and local church culture. He serves as National Director of Forge America Mission Training Network (the US arm of the Forge movement Alan Hirsch co-founded in Australia in 1996), as Founder and Director of The Pando Collective (a Front-Range micro-church / Kingdom network seeded inside Pulpit Rock Church), and as Pastor of Missional Culture at The Church at Pulpit Rock. His prior chapters reportedly include church planting, worship pastoring, co-founding the missional coffee shop Third Space Coffee with his wife, and a career in portfolio management and investment analysis.

His published work includes *Life Out Loud: Joining Jesus Outside the Walls of the Church* (100 Movements Publishing, 2019), which carries a contribution from Michael Frost, and *Red Skies: 10 Essential Conversations Exploring Our Future as the Church* (100 Movements Publishing, 2022), which he curated and edited as a multi-author conversation with Alan Hirsch, Michael Frost, Debra Hirsch, Brian Sanders, Leonard Sweet, Mark DeYmaz, and eleven other contributors. His Fuller dissertation, *Missional Emergence*, sits in Google Books under the byline Leo Rowland Smith and codifies the action-reflection pedagogy that powers his Forge cohort design.

He teaches as adjunct faculty at Fuller Theological Seminary (DGL Program), Denver Seminary (Leadership and the Doctor of Ministry in New Missional Movements), and Grand Canyon University (cross-cultural classes), holding an MA in Global Leadership and a DMiss from Fuller. His public themes cluster around missional discipleship, action–reflection learning, micro-church multiplication, business-as-mission, and multi-ethnic / cultural posture in the church — articulated in print, on Missio Alliance, through Forge America cohorts, and on national conference stages (Exponential, Mosaix, Awakenings Gathering). He sits firmly within the Forge / Hirsch missional ecosystem as a practitioner–scholar translating missional ecclesiology into training systems and regional experiments.

### Distinctive claims

- Discipleship is best formed through an **action-reflection** cycle — doing mission and reflecting on it — rather than propositional teaching first (codified in *Missional Emergence* and the Missio Alliance essay).
- The future of the church is best approached as a **curated, multi-voice conversation** across ten specific domains (the *Red Skies* frame) rather than a single-author prescription.
- Missional discipleship is "joining Jesus **outside the walls**" — relocating the locus of mission from church programs to the everyday contexts where believers live, work, and play.
- Micro-church networks (the Pando model) can be **nested inside** an existing local church while launching Kingdom-focused businesses and ministries — a both/and rather than a church-replacement posture.
