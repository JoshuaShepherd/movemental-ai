## Editorial bio

Roy Moran pastored Shoal Creek Community Church in Kansas City for twenty-five years before he was willing to admit out loud that the disciples he was making were not making disciples. That recognition — and the long mentorship under David Watson that followed it — turned a Dallas Theological Seminary-trained, Willow Creek-shaped attractional pastor into the rare Western voice trusted equally by senior pastors of established congregations and by global Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) practitioners. He still leads Shoal Creek as its directional leader and spiritual literacy advocate, alongside his wife Candy, who co-founded the church with him in 1992.[^cv]

His books carry that double credibility. *Spent Matches* (Thomas Nelson, 2015) tells the story; *Hybrid Church* (DMM Press, 2017) systematizes it as a playbook for other senior leaders — the integrated "come and go" model that holds an attractional Sunday gathering and a multiplicative DMM track inside one congregation. He now serves as Global Team Leader, Platform at New Generations — the Jerry Trousdale-led network documenting millions of disciples and hundreds of thousands of churches across Africa, Asia, and Latin America — and as founder and Executive Director of Mercy Alliance International.[^cv]

His weekly *Movement Conversations — Powered by New Generations* podcast (since 2020) is one of only a handful of sustained DMM media properties in the English-speaking world. His footprint sits at the convergence of Western pastoral credibility, Global South movement data, and the David Watson / CityTeam / Beyond DMM lineage that mentored him.[^cv]

## Frameworks

### Hybrid Church

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2015 (*Spent Matches*); systematized 2017 (*Hybrid Church*) |
| Components | (1) Gathering / Attractional — Sunday services built on creative storytelling and cultural touchpoints, designed for the ~30% of Americans open to attending a church; (2) Network / Multiplicative — reproducible disciple-making deployed through neighborhoods, workplaces, schools, and prisons, designed for the ~70% who will never come on a Sunday |
| Visual / model | The hybrid car — two engines, one vehicle, switching modes by terrain |
| Adoption evidence | Lived 25-year case study at Shoal Creek Community Church (1,000+ attendees); Trout Lake Hybrid Church Consultation (ongoing training event, Pine River, MN); cited by Vision Clarity, Experience Life, More Disciples, Praxeis; 2025 "decade revisited" cycle (Nexus) |
| Source | [Spent Matches (Thomas Nelson, 2015)](https://www.amazon.com/Spent-Matches-Spiritually-Dissatisfied-Refraction/dp/0718030621), [Hybrid Church (DMM Press, 2017)](https://www.amazon.com/Hybrid-Church-disciple-making-movements-strategy-ebook/dp/B06XGR4L5Q) |

An integrated local-church strategy that holds two modes simultaneously — an attractional Sunday gathering *and* a multiplicative DMM track — in the same body, rather than treating them as rival paradigms or sequential phases. Governing principles drawn from the books: clarity of language prevents vision leakage; obedience over information; progress visibility through regular reporting; community precedes conversion; and "Sunday as launchpad, not destination" — "what you win people *with* is what you win them *to*."

### Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) — Western adaptation

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Applied through Shoal Creek (~2008–) and *Hybrid Church* (2017) |
| Components | (1) Discovery Bible Study (DBS) — simple inductive method: read, retell, observe, obey, share; (2) obedience-based metrics — measure "I will" statements + next-meeting accountability, not attendance; (3) "person of peace" discernment (Luke 10); (4) four-generation reproduction as the test of real multiplication |
| Visual / model | — |
| Adoption evidence | Deployed inside Shoal Creek's multiplicative track; taught at Mercy Alliance training events; recurring subject across *Movement Conversations* episodes |
| Source | [Hybrid Church (DMM Press, 2017)](https://www.amazon.com/Hybrid-Church-disciple-making-movements-strategy-ebook/dp/B06XGR4L5Q), [ROY_MORAN_AUTHOR_PROFILE.md](../roy-moran/ROY_MORAN_AUTHOR_PROFILE.md) |

Obedience-based, reproducible discipleship aimed at four generations of multiplication, applied in the North American context rather than only in the unreached-people-group contexts where DMM originated.

### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not write in an anti-institutional, "burn it down" register, and do not frame DMM as exotic foreign-mission strategy. Moran's most-corrected reader misreading is that disciple-making requires abandoning the established congregation; his consistent correction is the hybrid both/and — reform the attractional church from inside rather than reject it. Equally, do not slip into academic missiology jargon or Global-South-only framing: his voice is pastoral, story-led, metaphor-driven, and Kansas-City-specific, written senior-pastor-to-senior-pastor. He resists triumphalism, treating DMM as adaptive rather than magic and often naming what *didn't* work. | Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Evangelical Protestant; Baptist-evangelical formation (Baylor, Dallas Theological Seminary); Willow Creek attractional influence (early) |
| Ecclesiology | Hybrid — attractional Sunday gathering and multiplicative DMM track held inside one local-church body; reform-from-inside, not anti-institutional |
| Missiology | Disciple-Making Movement (obedience-based, reproducible, four-generation multiplication) adapted from Global South contexts to the Western congregation |
| Leadership | Practitioner over theorist; senior-pastor-to-senior-pastor; "directional leader" / spiritual literacy advocate rather than CEO-pastor |
| Christology | Christologically grounded; "obedience to Jesus" framing throughout; Jesus is Lord as the center |

### Distinctive claims

- The attractional church and DMM multiplication are not rival paradigms or sequential phases — they can and should be held together inside one congregation (the Hybrid Church thesis).
- Discipleship is measured by obedience ("I will" statements + accountability), not by attendance or information delivered.
- "What you win people *with* is what you win them *to*" — the means of attraction determine the kind of disciple produced.
- A Western senior pastor can lead an existing congregation into DMM without abandoning it — the hybrid is an on-ramp, not an exit.
- The church's win-condition is "spiritual literacy" (reproducible Scripture-obedience), not Sunday attendance.
