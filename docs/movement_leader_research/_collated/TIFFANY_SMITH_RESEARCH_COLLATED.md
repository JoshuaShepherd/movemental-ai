## Editorial bio

Tiffany D. Smith — who publishes and brands as **"TD Smith"** — is a Southern Baptist missiologist, missional strategist, writer, and podcast host who describes herself as a "movement catalyst" working in "movemental spaces." A Texas native, she began her career as a public-school English teacher before two formative years as an International Mission Board (IMB) missionary in **Japan** "ignited her lifelong commitment to multiplying disciples and engaging the nations."[^theorg] Her formation is thoroughly within the SBC academy and missions apparatus: a B.S. in Psychology from Texas A&M, an M.Div. from Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary, and a Doctorate in Missional Leadership from New Orleans Baptist Theological Seminary.[^theorg]

Her institutional center of gravity is the **North American Mission Board (NAMB) / Send Network**, where she has served as a Regional Equipper for Canada, National Mobilizer and Managing Editor for the Send Institute, a Pipeline Consultant, and — most recently — **Send Network Strategies Coordinator**.[^theorg] Out of that work she built **Missional Mayhem** (tagline "Perspectives on a Missional Life" / "where mission, innovation, and movement collide in a changing world"): a personal platform spanning a blog, a podcast (launched September 2025), a coaching-and-cohort practice for female missional leaders, and a live "Houston Immersion" intensive in diaspora missions.[^mm][^houston]

Her signature contributions are programmatic rather than book-length: the **"Missio Dei: SOUL" and "Missio Dei: SENT"** multi-intelligence women's discipleship curricula written for Send Network, and a widely-syndicated argument — **"We need more Lydias in church planting"** — that frames Lydia of Philippi as the model for women as co-laboring missional leaders within (not against) a complementarian-compatible SBC frame.[^lydia] She is a fresher, lower-public-profile addition to the movement-leader cohort; her verifiable ties to the broader scenius run through the Send Institute / Exponential-adjacent ecosystem (e.g., interviewing Rich Robinson of 100Movements/5Q) rather than direct co-authorship with its senior figures.[^podbean]

### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not frame women's missional leadership as a claim on senior-pastor office or as a polemic against complementarian structures. Smith's careful, repeatedly-threaded position is that women are "co-laborers and synergetic in community and mission" and that affirming female missional leaders should come "without diminishing… our church and marketplace leaders who have God-given roles, authority, and titles." Her voice elevates women through discipling, mobilizing, and church-planting *teams* — not through contesting office or title. Likewise, avoid a hard, metrics-only "strategy" register stripped of the devotional cadence (prayers, benedictions, abiding imagery) that is equally load-bearing in her voice. Derived from her "Women, Missional Leaders" post and the "Lydias" article.[^lydia][^women]
