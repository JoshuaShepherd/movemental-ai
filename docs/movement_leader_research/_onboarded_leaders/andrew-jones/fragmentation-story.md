## The Story

Andrew Jones launched one of the earliest Christian voices on the open web — **Tall Skinny Kiwi** — and spent more than twenty years narrating the emerging church movement from the inside. From San Francisco's Haight-Ashbury and the Celtic House intentional community, through Prague and a campervan across Europe, to the Orkney Islands today, his content followed a life that refused to sit still.

The blog (`tallskinnykiwi.com`, rooted in Blogger) is the spine: decades of essays on emerging church, simple churches, mission, travel, and digital culture. The **Tall Skinny Kiwi podcast** extends that voice into conversation format on Apple Podcasts and syndicated directories. Christianity Today called him "an irrepressible New Zealander who chronicles innovative efforts to proclaim the gospel."

But the corpus does not live in one place. Organizational seasons — **BOAZ**, **Church Mission Society**, **NZCMS** — produced work that may live on institutional sites rather than his personal archive. Books (if any) are not catalogued alongside the blog. Social handles are assumed but not fully verified. There is **no confirmed newsletter** capturing the blog's readership into an owned list.

Andrew Jones's fragmentation is the fragmentation of a **pioneer blogger whose primary asset is temporal depth** — 20+ years of posts — scattered across platform migrations (TypePad, Blogger, personal domain), podcast directories, festival talk recordings, and organizational footprints, without a single searchable hub that unifies the whole career for a new reader arriving in 2026.
