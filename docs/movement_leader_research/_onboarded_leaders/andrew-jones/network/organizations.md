# Organizations & Affiliations: Andrew Jones (Tall Skinny Kiwi)

Andrew Jones's affiliations span three decades of mission, church development, and social-enterprise work across more than seventy countries. The organizations below are recorded at the confidence level the available sources support.

**The Boaz Project / Boaz** is the work most closely associated with Jones's own initiative. He has been described as leading the Boaz Project, supporting church-planting movements within global emerging culture, and as founder of a Boaz organization focused on social transformation and "impact entrepreneurs," active across roughly 2000-2016. Earlier descriptions place him as director of The Boaz Project while based in Walthamstow, north-east London, consulting for mission organizations and denominations on emerging-culture and emerging-church issues. (CONFIDENCE: HIGH that Boaz is his signature initiative; MEDIUM on exact dates and legal structure, which vary across sources.)

**Church Mission Society (CMS)** — Jones served as a church developer associated with CMS, the historic Anglican mission agency, during the period he was based in the United Kingdom (sources place this roughly 2006-2010). CMS has long had a "pioneer" stream supporting exactly the kind of fresh-expressions and emerging-church work Jones championed, which fits his profile, though the precise terms of his relationship should be confirmed with CMS directly. (CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM.)

**NZCMS (New Zealand Church Missionary Society)** — Jones is recorded as having worked with NZCMS as an international aid worker in the period around 2014-2017, connecting his New Zealand roots to his later international mission work. (CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM — drawn from professional-profile sources.)

**Dawn Ministries** — Jones is recorded as a missions consultant with Dawn Ministries (a church-planting-movement-focused organization) in the period around 2001-2005. (CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM — professional-profile sourcing.)

**24-7 Prayer / New Monasticism networks** — Jones moved within the same circles as the 24-7 Prayer movement and the new-monastic and alternative-worship streams of the emerging church, and wrote about monastic-style and alternative-worship communities (for example his "Baptist Monastery" reflections). A formal organizational role in 24-7 Prayer could not be verified; the relationship is best described as movement-adjacent rather than employment. (CONFIDENCE: LOW on any formal role; MEDIUM on movement proximity.)

**Emerging / Emergent church networks** — Jones was a central connector in the broader emerging-church conversation and was, for a season, associated with Emergent Village before publicly stepping back from it in early 2010 ("Goodbyes to Emergent Village"). He was repeatedly described as a centrist, bridge-building voice across the movement's factions and was recommended by figures such as Brian McLaren. (CONFIDENCE: HIGH on movement role; HIGH on the 2010 Emergent Village departure as a documented event.)

**Earlier intentional community** — In the late 1990s Andrew and his wife Debbie led an intentional community known as "The Celtic House" in San Francisco's Haight-Ashbury district before relocating to Texas to start a ministry to people from alternative cultures. (CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM — single first-person recollection source.)

Where specific dates, titles, or legal structures matter for downstream use, they should be confirmed against the organizations' own records; the strongest, most consistently attested affiliations are Boaz (his own initiative) and his central, connecting role in the emerging-church conversation.
