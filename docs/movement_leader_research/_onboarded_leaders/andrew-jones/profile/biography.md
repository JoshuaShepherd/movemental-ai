# Andrew Jones (Tall Skinny Kiwi)

Andrew Jones, known across the internet as "Tall Skinny Kiwi," is a New Zealand-born missionary, church developer, and one of the earliest and most widely-read Christian bloggers, whose decades of writing helped shape the global conversation around the emerging and missional church. He grew up in Orewa, New Zealand, before moving to Perth in Western Australia, and from the early 1980s onward worked in and out of the United States, in Prague, across continental Europe from a campervan, and now from the Orkney Islands of Scotland. By his own account he launched his first space on the internet in 1997 and has been writing, teaching, and chronicling the renewal of the church online for more than two decades since — running, in the words of one observer, "one of the most read blogs in cyberspace" on matters of emerging church, mission, and culture. His online identity is consistent and distinctive: "Tall Skinny Kiwi" across his blog (tallskinnykiwi.com, formerly hosted on TypePad and Blogger), his podcast, and his social accounts. Across thirty years he has worked with third-sector social enterprises in more than seventy countries, variously as an international aid worker, a church developer, a pastor, and a mission consultant — discovering and empowering "impact entrepreneurs" for social transformation, with a recurring emphasis on justice, financial sustainability, and urban renewal. He is best understood not as an author of books but as a connector, a chronicler, and a sense-maker: someone who watched a global movement form in real time and narrated it for everyone else trying to find their footing in it. Christianity Today described him as "an irrepressible New Zealander who chronicles innovative efforts to proclaim the gospel," and Brian McLaren has recommended bookmarking his site. His themes — emerging and missional church, rhizomatic and grass-roots church planting, alternative worship, simple and house churches, digital storytelling, and the future of faith in a networked culture — have remained remarkably constant across the life of his blog.

## Origins and Formation

Jones grew up in Orewa, New Zealand, and moved to Perth, Western Australia, before beginning a peripatetic ministry life. In 1995 he and his wife Debbie were considering relocating to the San Francisco Bay Area; by 1997-1998 they were living in San Francisco's Haight-Ashbury district, leading an intentional community known as "The Celtic House" and engaging with emerging-culture ministries. In 1997 Andrew attended Leadership Network's "Emerging Urban Leaders" gathering. In mid-1998 the family moved to Texas to start a ministry to people from alternative cultures, traveling extensively. ## Pioneering Years

From New Zealand and the United States, Andrew and Debbie moved into a deeply mobile, international pattern of work — Prague, the United Kingdom, continental Europe, and eventually the Orkney Islands of Scotland. He served as a church developer with mission agencies, founded and led work focused on social transformation, and became a recognized "centrist" voice and connector within the emerging-church conversation rather than a partisan of any single camp.

## Present

Andrew now lives in the Orkney Islands, Scotland, where he continues to write at tallskinnykiwi.com, record the *Tall Skinny Kiwi* podcast, and speak at festivals and conferences. He appeared at the 2022 Greenbelt Festival, giving a talk titled "In The Metaverse As In Heaven." His present focus carries forward the same threads that have defined his work for thirty years: mission at the grass roots, social transformation, and the church learning to live faithfully inside a digital, networked culture.
