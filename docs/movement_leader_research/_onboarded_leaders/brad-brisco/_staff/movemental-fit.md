## What Movemental Would Change for This Author

### Problems Movemental Solves

- **Platform fragmentation**: His content lives on 10+ platforms he doesn't own (NAMB, New Churches, Amazon, Exponential, Vimeo, Foundry Publishing, Forge Teachable, various podcast hosts). Movemental provides a single owned hub.
- **No audience ownership**: He reaches thousands through NAMB/Send Network channels but has no subscriber list, no email capture, no way to communicate directly with his audience outside institutional channels.
- **Content format poverty**: He has deep expertise expressed only in books and blog posts. Movemental enables courses, video series, downloadable resources, and community -- formats his content is ready for but doesn't yet exist in.
- **Invisible teaching archive**: 18+ years of college teaching and decades of church planting coaching have zero digital footprint. Movemental provides the infrastructure to capture and distribute this knowledge.
- **No monetization beyond books**: Amazon royalties are his only direct content revenue. Movemental enables courses, premium content, and bundled offerings.
- **Discoverability ceiling**: His Squarespace blog has minimal SEO, no structured data, and limited interlinking. A Movemental profile would surface his content within a network designed for discovery by movement leaders.

### Content Movemental Would Surface

- **5 published books** -- cataloged with summaries, key themes, and purchase links in a unified author profile
- **25+ articles** from 7 platforms -- aggregated and organized by theme (covocational ministry, missional ecclesiology, church planting, discipleship)
- **25+ podcast episodes** -- indexed and searchable, with topic tagging and cross-references to his written content
- **25+ videos** -- conference keynotes (Exponential, CGGC Triennial), Missional Essentials curriculum, and podcast video versions, organized by topic
- **2 existing curricula** -- Missional Essentials (12-week) and Engage Your City (4-week), presented as structured learning paths
- **Network connections** -- his relationships with Lance Ford, Ed Stetzer, Alan Hirsch, Michael Frost, and others visualized as a collaborative map

### New Content Movemental Would Enable

- **Online courses**: "The Covocational Planter" (from his book + podcast episodes), "ReThinking Church" (from his CGGC keynotes), and digitized versions of his college courses
- **Email newsletter**: Platform-native newsletter tool to build his first owned subscriber list
- **Video content**: Short-form clips from existing keynotes, new video teachings recorded with minimal production overhead
- **Downloadable resources**: Study guides, framework diagrams (9 Paradigm Shifts, Centered-Set vs. Bounded-Set), neighborhood engagement toolkits
- **Audiobook editions**: Production pipeline for at least *Covocational Church Planting*
- **Community features**: Discussion spaces for cohort-based learning connected to his courses

## What We Will NOT Do

- **Replace NAMB/Send Network role**: Movemental is complementary to his institutional work, not competitive. His NAMB articles, Send Network resources, and New Churches contributions continue unchanged.
- **Remove content from existing platforms**: Articles on New Churches, NAMB, Missio Alliance, and Send Institute stay where they are. Movemental aggregates and links; it does not extract.
- **Change his voice or theological positions**: His missional theology (Lesslie Newbigin-influenced, incarnational, missio Dei-centered) is preserved exactly as is. Movemental doesn't edit or reframe.
- **Force social media activity**: If he prefers Twitter/X as his primary social channel, that's fine. Movemental enhances what exists rather than demanding new platform adoption.
- **Compete with Forge**: His Forge Kansas City and Forge America training network relationships remain independent. Movemental may host some of the same content types but does not replace the Forge relational model.
- **Pressure monetization**: Some authors prefer to keep content free and accessible. Movemental provides monetization tools but doesn't require their use. His free eBooks through NAMB remain free.
