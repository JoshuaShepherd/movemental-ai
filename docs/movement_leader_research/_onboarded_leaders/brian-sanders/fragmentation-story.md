## The Story

In the summer of 2006, roughly fifty people left their traditional churches in Tampa and formed seven house-based groups — the beginning of the **Underground**. Brian Sanders had spent years in inner-city intentional community and InterVarsity urban mission before helping design a church "structured and streamlined for mission." That experiment became **100+ microchurches in Tampa** and **15–30+ sister movements** worldwide, documented in *Underground Church* (Zondervan, 2018) and *Microchurches: A Smaller Way* (2019).

But the story and the tools do not live in one catalog. Books span **four imprints** (IVP, Underground Media, Zondervan, Moody). The network lives on **tampaunderground.com** and **undergroundnetwork.org**. Teaching continues on the **Microchurches podcast** and at the **MicroChurch Conference**. Essays appear on **Medium (@BSunderground)**. Social voice splits across **X, LinkedIn, and Facebook**. The Goodreads author page **conflates namesakes**, polluting bibliographic clarity.

Sanders' fragmentation is the fragmentation of a **movement architect whose proof-of-concept is distributed** — books on retailer shelves, network resources on org sites, podcast on platform directories, conference talks on event hosts — without a single owned index that tells a new leader where to start and how the pieces connect.
