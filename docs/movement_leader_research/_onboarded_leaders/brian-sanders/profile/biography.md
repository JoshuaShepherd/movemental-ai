# Biography: Brian Sanders

Brian Sanders is an American social entrepreneur, author, and the founder of the **Underground Network** (Tampa Underground) — a network of microchurches in Tampa, Florida that has become a reference model for the microchurch movement in the West. He and his family moved into the poorest urban neighborhood of his hometown, Tampa, to start an intentional community, and he has lived in inner-city intentional community with his wife **Monica** and their **six children** for more than two decades. ([Moody Publishers](https://www.moodypublishers.com/authors/s/brian-sanders/))

His formation was shaped by collegiate ministry: he earned a BA in Communication from the **University of Florida**, an MA in Religious Studies from the **University of South Florida**, and an MTh in Applied Theology from the **University of Wales / Spurgeon's College** (London). For years he served as the **State Director for InterVarsity Christian Fellowship in Florida** and founded/directed the **Tampa Urban Project** (InterVarsity, 1999–2025). ([Moody Publishers](https://www.moodypublishers.com/authors/s/brian-sanders/), [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bsunderground/))

In the **summer of 2006**, roughly 50 people left their traditional churches to form seven house-based church groups — the beginning of the Underground. Frustrated with attractional, program-centered church, Sanders helped design a structure "streamlined for mission," organized not around a weekly gathering but around the callings of individuals to outward ministry — to the poor, the lost, and biblical justice. What began with 50 people became a network of **100+ microchurches** in Tampa (earlier reports cited 136–200; cumulative founding totals run into the hundreds), with **15–30+ sister movements** in cities and countries around the world. ([Outreach Magazine](https://outreachmagazine.com/features/18604-tampa-underground.html), [Underground Network](https://www.undergroundnetwork.org/))

As a social entrepreneur, Sanders has helped start hundreds of missional enterprises — churches, nonprofits, and businesses — and built the Underground as a "network of relationship and resources" providing coaching, finance, media, and incubation services to small missional communities. He authored *The Underground Church* (Zondervan, 2018, with a foreword/introduction by **Alan Hirsch**), *Microchurches: A Smaller Way* (Underground Media, 2019), and *The 6 Seasons of Calling* (Moody, 2022), among earlier self-published works. He has since stepped out of the Executive Director seat (now held by Joel Repic), remaining a founder and board member, and has moved into venture/philanthropic roles including **Underground Venture Studio** and **VP of Impact Alliances at the National Christian Foundation Tampa Bay**. ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bsunderground/), [Underground Network](https://www.undergroundnetwork.org/who-we-are))
