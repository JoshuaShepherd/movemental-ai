# Brian Sanders: Calling Profile

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-06-05

---

## Calling statement

Brian Sanders is called to **reframe the church's basic unit downward** — to demonstrate that the microchurch is not a stepping-stone to something larger but the most common, most reproducible, and most potent expression of the church, and to build the lightweight network architecture that lets hundreds of such churches flourish without centralizing them. His vocation is practitioner-first: he moved into the poorest neighborhood of his hometown, raised six children in inner-city intentional community, and in the summer of 2006 helped fifty people leave traditional churches to form seven house-based groups that became the Underground Network. He is a movement reference-builder, not a denominational reformer — his significance is the pairing of a flagship story (*Underground Church*, 2018) with an operational ecclesiology (*Microchurches: A Smaller Way*, 2019) that cities worldwide have used as a template. His later work on *The 6 Seasons of Calling* (Moody, 2022) extends the same conviction that God calls ordinary disciples in every context, treating vocation as a lifelong developmental arc rather than a single vocational decision.

### Core calling elements

**1. Microchurch movement pioneer**
- Gave the Western microchurch movement both its flagship narrative and its operational primer
- Coined and popularized "microchurch" as an ecclesial category — "the simplest unit of the church"
- Built a reference model (100+ Tampa microchurches; 15–30+ sister movements worldwide)

**2. Inner-city intentional community practitioner**
- More than two decades living in Tampa's poorest urban neighborhood with Monica and their six children
- Community practice is the lived substrate beneath the ecclesiology — not a rhetorical backdrop

**3. Network architect**
- Designed a church "structured and streamlined for mission" organized around individuals' callings, not a weekly gathering
- Built a network of relationship and resources (coaching, finance, media, incubation) that serves small churches rather than centralizing them

**4. Calling-serving leader and equipper**
- Reframes leadership as serving members' God-given callings rather than recruiting them to the leader's vision
- Equips ordinary disciples to imagine themselves "responsible for the future formation of the church"
- Teaches through books, the Microchurches podcast, MicroChurch Conference, and Network Intensive cohorts

**5. Social entrepreneur**
- Has helped start hundreds of missional enterprises — churches, nonprofits, and businesses
- Current roles include Principal of Underground Venture Studio and VP of Impact Alliances at National Christian Foundation Tampa Bay

## Distinctive vocational signature

Sanders' calling is distinguished within the wider missional ecosystem by three features:

1. **Practitioner credibility** — he did not theorize microchurch from outside attractional megachurch culture; he lived the experiment in inner-city Tampa for two decades and narrates it as a sustained, structural prescription.
2. **Network inversion** — where many leaders build a church and add small groups, Sanders built a network that exists to serve many small churches — a structural inversion that sister movements have replicated globally.
3. **Calling-serving leadership** — his most-corrected misreading is equating church with a weekly meeting; his consistent correction is that leaders serve people's God-given callings rather than recruit them to the leader's vision.
