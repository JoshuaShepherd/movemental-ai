## Bob Buford Mentor Connection

Bob Buford (1939–2018), co-founder of Leadership Network and the Halftime Institute, was a personal mentor to Ferguson. Ferguson has stated publicly that Buford "taught me what it meant to be a hero maker" and described him as "one of the greatest positive influences of his generation on the North American church." The *Legacy of a Hero Maker* (co-authored with Todd Wilson) was written partly as a tribute to Buford, applying the Hero Maker framework to Buford's legacy.

## Key Relationship Clusters

### The Exponential / Church Multiplication Circle
Ferguson sits at the center of the North American church multiplication movement:
- **Zondervan Exponential Series**: His own books anchor the series; he is a peer contributor with Hirsch, Halter, Chan, Hatmaker, Carter, Patrick, and others
- **NewThing / Exponential merger (2025)**: Operational control of the world's largest church-planting conference and network
- **Exponential Conference**: Annual national and regional platform connecting him to hundreds of church-planting leaders

### The Missional Mainstream Circle
- **Alan Hirsch**: Deep co-authorship (*On the Verge*) and mutual endorsement relationship; shared emphasis on apostolic and multiplying movements
- **Warren Bird / Leadership Network**: Research credibility and institutional bridge to the broader evangelical leadership world
- **Craig Groeschel, Andy Stanley, Mark Batterson, Matt Chandler**: Endorsement network representing the largest North American megachurch ecosystem

### The Chicago / COMMUNITY Circle
- **Jon Ferguson**: Primary lifelong co-author and co-founder; all three WaterBrook books are co-authored with Jon
- **Eric Bramlett**: Long-tenured colleague at COMMUNITY who co-authored the foundational *Big Idea* book
- **Ted Coniaris**: Successor and embodiment of the Hero Maker succession model

### Mentor / Influence Lines
- **Bob Buford** (Leadership Network): Named personal mentor
- **Alan Hirsch**: Co-author and intellectual peer in the apostolic / multiplication movement
