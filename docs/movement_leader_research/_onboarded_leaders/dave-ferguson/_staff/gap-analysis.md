## Executive Summary

Dave Ferguson is one of the highest-profile voices in the North American church-multiplication movement — co-founder of Community Christian Church (5,500+), founder of NewThing (6,373+ churches planted), and CEO/President of Exponential (world's largest church-planting conference). His books have sold across 4 major publishers (Zondervan, WaterBrook/PRH, Salem Books, Exponential Resources), he has 7 commercial audiobooks, 15+ high-profile podcast appearances, and endorsements from Andy Stanley, Craig Groeschel, J.D. Greear, Christine Caine, and Alan Hirsch.

But beneath this surface, a structural fragility runs deep: **the overwhelming majority of Dave Ferguson's reach is institutionally owned, not personally owned.** Exponential owns the email list that reaches tens of thousands of church planters. Exponential owns the conference platform. Exponential owns the podcast. Community Christian Church owns the sermon library and the Sunday morning congregation. NewThing's network is now absorbed into Exponential. Ferguson's personally owned audience — the channels he controls, not his employer — amounts to approximately 42,000 social media followers, 511 Substack subscribers (on a platform with an awkward "3" URL), a sparse 17-post blog on a Squarespace site, and a handful of book-companion websites tied to publisher agreements.

If Dave Ferguson were to leave Exponential tomorrow, his personal digital footprint would be thin. This is the central gap: the gap between enormous institutional leverage and modest owned audience. The secondary gaps are specific actionable opportunities to convert that institutional influence into personally owned audience equity before any such transition becomes relevant.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
