# Biography: Dave Ferguson

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

## Narrative Biography

Dave Ferguson is a church planter, author, and movement leader who has spent more than three decades building one of America's most influential churches, launching an international church-planting network, and catalyzing a global movement of reproducing congregations. As CEO of Exponential, Co-Founding Pastor of Community Christian Church, and the founding visionary of NewThing, Ferguson has become one of the defining practitioner voices in the North American church-multiplication movement. His work is distinguished by a rare integration of local pastoral depth — he co-founded a single church in 1989 and watched it become a multi-site community of thousands — with global apostolic ambition: the NewThing network grew to nearly 1,700 church-planting networks in 47 countries by 2022 before merging with Exponential in 2025.

Ferguson's story begins in the Midwest. When he was approximately four years old, his parents moved from rural Missouri to the Chicago area to plant a church in the Restoration Movement tradition — the independent Christian Church network that traces its heritage to the 19th-century restoration of New Testament Christianity. Ferguson grew up in that church-planting household, watching his father establish and lead a congregation that eventually grew to roughly 1,000 members and expanded to multiple locations before "multisite" had become a recognized category in American church leadership. His father would eventually become a campus pastor at the church Dave himself founded — a remarkable multigenerational story of church-planting continuity. His brother Jon Ferguson — two years younger than Dave — shared that upbringing and would become his most consistent ministry partner across forty years, from a childhood lawn care business ("D & J Lawn Service") to college roommates at Lincoln Christian University to co-founders of Community Christian Church.

### Education

Dave Ferguson earned his Bachelor of Arts in Bible and Communications from Lincoln Christian University (Lincoln, IL) in 1986. Jon Ferguson graduated from the same institution the following year ("LCC '87 BA"), confirming the brothers' documented college overlap. After Lincoln Christian, Dave pursued graduate study at Wheaton College Graduate School, earning a Master of Arts in Educational Ministries with a specialization in Church Planting in 1991 (year per baseline documentation; year unconfirmed from a fetched primary source). His first full-time ministry role following his education was as a youth pastor — a formative context where his focus on developing leaders first took shape.

### The Founding of Community Christian Church (1989)

On March 5, 1989, Dave and Jon Ferguson gathered with five friends from college in the cafeteria of Naperville Central High School and held the first service of what would become Community Christian Church. The founding vision was simple but far-reaching: to create a community that would help people "find their way back to God." That phrase — "helping people find their way back to God" — has remained the organizational tagline and north star for more than three and a half decades.

The early years required the kind of patience and tenacity common to church-planting stories. From that high school cafeteria, the church grew gradually and consistently. Approximately eight years after launch, in the late 1990s, Community Christian Church established its first additional campus in Romeoville, Illinois — making it one of the early pioneers of the multi-site model in American evangelical churches, before that model had widespread recognition or a common name. The Romeoville launch established the church's identity not merely as a single congregation but as a network of connected communities sharing a common mission and culture.

Over the following decades, the church continued to expand across the Chicago suburbs. By the time Ferguson stepped out of the Lead Pastor role in 2025, Community Christian operated seven locations: Aurora, Carillon, Downers Grove, Lincoln Park, Naperville, Plainfield, and Yorkville. The church's three-fold vision — functioning as an impact church, a reproducing church, and a catalyst for global church movement — held together local presence and global ambition. The church has been consistently recognized by Outreach Magazine as one of America's most influential churches.

Dave's father eventually joined the CCC staff as a campus pastor, and Jon Ferguson has served as Co-Founding Pastor, teacher, and movement architect throughout. Sue Ferguson, Dave's wife, has been a grounding and formative presence throughout the pastoral journey; the couple has three children — Amy, Joshua, and Caleb.

### NewThing: From Local Vision to Global Network (2001–2025)

In 2001, twelve years after founding Community Christian Church, Ferguson launched NewThing — a church-planting mission designed to take the reproducing-church vision beyond CCC's own campuses and catalyze a broader movement. NewThing's mission was to be "a catalyst for a movement of reproducing churches," and its approach centered on equipping and networking leaders who could replicate church planting ecosystems across cities and nations.

The network's growth was significant. By 2022, NewThing comprised 1,696 church-planting networks operating in 47 countries. Ferguson served as the visionary and apostolic leader of the network throughout this period, working with Patrick O'Connell and others on practical implementation. The NewThing model emphasized relationality and reproducibility: rather than simply planting churches, it sought to build systems and cultures that could generate church-planting movements organically across diverse contexts.

The theological logic underlying NewThing is captured in the concept that shaped Ferguson's book *Together: The Great Collaboration* (2020, with Patrick O'Connell): the Great Commission (Go), the Great Commandment (Love), and the Great Collaboration (Together) are inseparable. Reproducing churches are not independent entrepreneurial ventures but interconnected communities advancing a shared kingdom agenda through networked relationships.

### Exponential: Building the Church-Planting Ecosystem

Alongside NewThing, Ferguson served as President/CEO and Co-Founder of Exponential — the world's largest church-planting conference and resource network. Exponential operates as a 501(c)3 nonprofit serving the evangelical church-planting community across more than 100 networks and denominations. Its organizing vision is what Ferguson calls the "16% Mission": currently only 7% of American churches are multiplying; the goal is to reach a tipping point where 16% of U.S. churches become reproducing or multiplying, generating movement dynamics across the broader culture.

Exponential provides conferences (both national and regional), learning communities, cohorts, resources, and research to equip church planters and multiplication-minded leaders. Ferguson's leadership of the conference has made him a central convener of the North American church-planting conversation.

### The 2025 Convergences

Two major transitions in 2025 marked a significant reshaping of Ferguson's institutional landscape.

First, on April 1, 2025, NewThing officially joined Exponential as a unified organization. NewThing ceased operations as a separate entity, and its staff, networks, and expertise were integrated into Exponential's structure. Patrick O'Connell became Executive Director of Practice within the merged organization. The merger was framed around three objectives: advancing the 16% Mission in the U.S., launching Exponential International (leveraging NewThing's relationships across 60+ countries across nine world regions), and strengthening financial development. Ferguson described the animating principle behind the decision as "Kingdom over Castle" — a phrase that captures his conviction that organizational structures should be subordinated to kingdom mission.

Second, on May 1, 2025, Ferguson transitioned from Lead Pastor to Co-Founding Pastor at Community Christian Church. Ted Coniaris — who had joined CCC as a leadership resident approximately eight years earlier, planted a location, and served as director of all location pastors — became Lead Pastor after an eighteen-month public apprenticeship process. The transition embodied the very hero-making and apprenticeship theology Ferguson has written and taught: an eighteen-month process using the five-step apprenticeship model that Ferguson himself had articulated in *Exponential* — "I do, you watch, we talk" through to "You do, someone else watches." Ferguson remains on the CCC staff team, focusing on teaching, coaching, and leading Exponential.

### Authorship Arc

Ferguson's books trace the evolution of his ministry thinking across nearly two decades. They fall into four thematic clusters:

**Church strategy and the "Big Idea" (2007–2011)**: *The Big Idea* (Zondervan, 2007, with Jon Ferguson and Eric Bramlett) introduced his philosophy of one laser-focused teaching theme per week, aligned across all church ministries through creative collaboration. *Exponential* (Zondervan, 2010, with Jon Ferguson) narrated the Community Christian story as a template for missional church movements. *On the Verge* (Zondervan, 2011, with Alan Hirsch) co-articulated the apostolic-movement framework with the foremost missional theologian of the era, grounding CCC's practice in Hirsch's "mDNA" and "Apostolic Genius" theology.

**Mission activation and multiplication metrics (2012–2013)**: *Discover Your Mission Now* (Missio Publishing, 2012, with Jon Ferguson and Tim Sutherland) laid out five missional practices for everyday Christians. *Keeping Score* (Exponential Resources, 2014) challenged churches to redefine success metrics around disciple-making and movement-making rather than attendance and budget alone.

**Relational evangelism and spiritual journey (2015–2016)**: *Finding Your Way Back to God* (WaterBrook/Multnomah, 2015, with Jon Ferguson) addressed the returning and spiritually wandering with the "Five Awakenings" framework drawn from the Parable of the Prodigal Son: Awakening to Longing, Regret, Help, Love, and Life. *Starting Over* (WaterBrook/Multnomah, with Jon Ferguson) addressed the recognize-release-redeem process for regret in five life domains.

**Leadership multiplication (2018–2026)**: *Hero Maker* (Zondervan, 2018, with Warren Bird) is Ferguson's defining work on leadership development, presenting the five practices of hero-making leaders — multiplication thinking, permission giving, disciple multiplying, gift activating, and kingdom building — as a holistic culture shift from individual heroism to leadership multiplication. *B.L.E.S.S.* (2020, with Jon Ferguson) distilled missional neighboring into five everyday practices. *Together* (Exponential, 2020, with Patrick O'Connell) articulated the networked-collaboration model underlying NewThing. *Multiplier* (Zondervan Reflective, March 2026) addresses sustainable leadership health through the RPMS framework and the external multiplication practices required for long-term kingdom impact.

Ferguson also co-authored the eBook *Becoming a Level Five Multiplying Church* (with Todd Wilson and Alan Hirsch), which introduced the five-level framework for diagnosing where a church falls on the growth-to-multiplication spectrum.

### Lincoln Christian University Recognition

In 2021, Lincoln Christian University honored both Dave (LCC '86 BA) and Jon Ferguson (LCC '87 BA) with its Restoration Award — the university's highest alumni recognition. The award cited their founding of Community Christian Church, the NewThing network, and the Exponential Conference as expressions of the university's Restoration Movement heritage applied to church-planting innovation.

## Origin & Formation

Dave Ferguson's formation was shaped by three convergent forces: a church-planting household, a Restoration Movement theological inheritance, and a Midwestern relational culture that prizes collaboration over celebrity.

Growing up watching his father plant and grow a church from scratch gave Ferguson an early, concrete picture of what it looks like to start a congregation from nothing and see it grow. The Restoration Movement heritage — with its emphasis on returning to New Testament Christianity, baptism, and non-creedal simplicity — provided a theological grammar that prized practicality, Scripture centrality, and ecclesial reproducibility over denominational distinctives. Lincoln Christian University, a Restoration Movement institution, deepened that formation.

The brother-partnership with Jon Ferguson is one of the most distinctive features of Ferguson's ministry ecology. The two have co-founded, co-written, and co-pastored for their entire adult lives. This relational co-leadership is not incidental but reflects Ferguson's core conviction: that the kingdom advances through collaboration, not individual heroism.
