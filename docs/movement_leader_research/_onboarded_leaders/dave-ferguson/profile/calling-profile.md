**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-06-05

## Calling statement

Dave Ferguson is called to **multiply leaders, churches, and movements** — to shift the North American church from addition to reproduction, from hero-pastor culture to hero-making culture, and from isolated congregations to networked kingdom collaboration. His vocation is practitioner-first: in March 1989 he and his brother Jon gathered five college friends in a high school cafeteria to plant Community Christian Church, watched it grow into a seven-location multi-site community, and used that local laboratory to launch NewThing (2001) and Exponential — building an international church-planting network that reached nearly 1,700 networks in 47 countries before merging with Exponential in 2025. Ferguson is not primarily a theologian or a conference personality; he is a multiplication architect who has spent more than three decades proving that the mission of Jesus is fulfilled when ordinary disciples, leaders, and churches reproduce at every level. His defining vocational signature is hero-making: "My fruit grows on other people's trees."

### Core calling elements

**1. Church multiplication movement catalyst**
- Convenes the world's largest church-planting conference (Exponential) and advances the "16% Mission" — the tipping point where reproducing churches become 16% of U.S. congregations
- Built NewThing as "a catalyst for a movement of reproducing churches" (2001–2025)
- Frames multiplication as the mission of Jesus: "Multiplication church planting leads to movement and movement is how we accomplish the mission of Jesus."

**2. Local church practitioner**
- Co-founded Community Christian Church in 1989; led it as Lead Pastor for 36 years before transitioning to Co-Founding Pastor in May 2025
- Pioneered multi-site expansion in the late 1990s (Romeoville campus) before the model was widely recognized
- Embodied his own succession theology: Ted Coniaris apprenticed for 18 months through the five-step model before becoming Lead Pastor

**3. Hero-maker and leadership multiplier**
- *Hero Maker* (2018) is his defining vocational articulation — five practices that shift leaders from heroism to multiplication thinking, permission giving, disciple multiplying, gift activating, and kingdom building
- "As a leader, you reproduce who you are" — internal health is vocational, not merely personal
- "Am I trying to be the hero, or am I trying to make heroes out of others?"

**4. Network architect and convener**
- Merged NewThing into Exponential (April 2025) under the principle "Kingdom over Castle" — organizational structures subordinated to kingdom mission
- Articulates the Great Collaboration: Great Commission (Go) + Great Commandment (Love) + Great Collaboration (Together)
- Bridges denominations and networks — Exponential serves 100+ networks across Baptist, Wesleyan, Reformed, Pentecostal, and non-denominational traditions

**5. Mission activator for ordinary Christians**
- BLESS framework makes incarnational neighboring accessible to every believer — begin with prayer, listen, eat, serve, share
- "It's God's job to judge people. It's the Holy Spirit's job to convict people. It's your job to love your neighbor."
- Consistently pushes against clergy-lay distinctions in mission

## Distinctive vocational signature

Ferguson's calling is distinguished within the wider church-multiplication ecosystem by three features:

1. **Practitioner credibility at scale** — he built one of America's most influential churches from a cafeteria, not from a think tank. Exponential and NewThing grew out of lived local practice, not theory imposed on others.
2. **Hero-making as vocational center** — where many multiplication leaders emphasize church planting math, Ferguson emphasizes leader development culture. His succession of Ted Coniaris is the lived proof of the model.
3. **Kingdom over Castle** — his 2025 institutional convergences (NewThing → Exponential; Lead Pastor → Co-Founding Pastor) embody the conviction that structures exist to serve multiplication, not to preserve organizational castles.
