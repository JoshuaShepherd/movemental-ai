## Audience Profile

Lewis's audience is primarily **pastors, church planters, and ministry practitioners** — especially those in urban, multiethnic, and majority-minority contexts — plus the broader Southern Baptist / evangelical church-planting ecosystem reached through NAMB, Send Network, and conferences (T4G, Mosaix, Verge, Exponential). A secondary audience is readers engaged with **racial reconciliation** from a theologically conservative, Christ-centered frame.

## Audience Personas

- **The urban church planter** — looking for a disciple-making model that works "among wolves" and a vision for staying in the city.
- **The multiethnic-church leader** — drawn to *Advocates* and the "narrow path" framing of reconciliation.
- **The SBC/evangelical practitioner** — encountering him through Send Network, conference platforms, and B&H/LifeWay resources.

## What the Audience Values

Practical, framework-driven teaching grounded in Scripture and personal testimony; a both/and posture that holds theology and context together; and credibility as a practitioner who has actually planted and multiplied churches in the urban context.

## Engagement Patterns

Reach is concentrated in **owned long-form** (books, podcasts) and **borrowed platforms** (denominational sites, conference stages) more than in large personal social followings — his social numbers are modest relative to his institutional influence, indicating a practitioner/insider audience rather than a mass-market one.
