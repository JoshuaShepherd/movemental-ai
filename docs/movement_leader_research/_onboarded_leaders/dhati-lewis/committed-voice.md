## Editorial bio

Dhati Lewis moved to Atlanta in 2009 with roughly twenty-five others to plant Blueprint Church on a single conviction: the church "is not like family; it is family," and disciple-making — not church-planting — is the true engine of multiplication. Raised in California as the son of an NFL player, he came to Texas on a football scholarship to the University of North Texas before ministry formation through Dallas Theological Seminary and a D.Min. at Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary turned him toward urban disciple-making.

Among Wolves: Disciple-Making in the City (B&H, 2017) traces eight movements in Matthew's Gospel to turn passive participants into active disciple-makers; Advocates: The Narrow Path to Racial Reconciliation (B&H, 2019) reads Philemon for a church that runs to tension rather than away from it. He launched the Rebuild Network in 2011, served as Vice President and then President of the North American Mission Board's Send Network (2018–2021), and departed NAMB at the end of 2021 to expand the Rebuild Initiative under the MyBLVD banner.

He continues as Blueprint's lead pastor alongside his wife Angie, hosts the Where Life Exists and MyBLVD podcasts with Missie Branch, and teaches across Exponential, Mosaix, Verge, and Together for the Gospel — still naming his calling as raising the last generation to leave the urban context for sound discipleship.
