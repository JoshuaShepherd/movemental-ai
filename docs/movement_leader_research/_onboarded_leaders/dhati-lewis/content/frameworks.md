# Frameworks: Dhati Lewis

### Advocate vs. Aggravator

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2019, *Advocates: The Narrow Path to Racial Reconciliation* |
| Components | (1) Rely on Christ; (2) Run to the tension; (3) Respond with dignity |
| Source text | Paul's letter to Philemon |
| Adoption evidence | Framing for *Advocates* (B&H, 2019); used in reconciliation teaching and conference talks |
| Source | [baptistpress.com](https://www.baptistpress.com/resource-library/news/path-to-reconciliation-focus-of-advocates/) |

An "advocate" pursues reconciliation; an "aggravator" deepens division out of self-interest, self-preservation, or self-vindication. The three marks of an advocate define a Christ-centered "narrow path" distinct from politically-driven aggravation.

### The Eight Movements in Matthew (disciple-making model)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2017, *Among Wolves: Disciple-Making in the City* |
| Components | Eight "movements" traced through Matthew's Gospel, from Jesus establishing His presence with His followers to mobilizing them to make disciples of all nations |
| Visual / model | Matthew's narrative arc |
| Adoption evidence | Basis of *Among Wolves* + the 8-session LifeWay Bible study + Leader Kit |
| Source | [bhpublishinggroup.com](https://www.bhpublishinggroup.com/product/among-wolves-2/) |

A disciple-making culture built by following eight movements in Matthew that turn passive participants into active disciple-makers in the city.

### Church as Family (ecclesiology)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | *Among Wolves* (2017); articulated in his Saturate essay (2017) |
| Components | Identity precedes activity; establish belonging/family before infrastructure; "make disciples and a church is the logical result" |
| Adoption evidence | Blueprint Church discipleship strategy: "develop healthy people who are growing in the Gospel, in the context of family, while living on mission" |
| Source | [saturatetheworld.com](https://saturatetheworld.com/2017/07/20/dont-just-start-church-establish-family/) |

"The church is not like family; it is family." Disciple-making, not church-planting, is the engine.
