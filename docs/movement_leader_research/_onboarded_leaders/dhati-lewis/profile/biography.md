# Biography: Dhati Lewis

## Narrative Biography

Dhati Lewis is the founding pastor of Blueprint Church in Atlanta, the founder and president of the Rebuild Initiative (MyBLVD), and the former Vice President and President of the North American Mission Board's Send Network. Born and raised in California as the son of an NFL player, he came to Texas on a football scholarship to the University of North Texas, where an emerging call to ministry — through football chaplaincy and college ministry — set him on a path toward urban disciple-making. After a Master's in Cross-Cultural Ministry at Dallas Theological Seminary and a Doctor of Ministry at Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary, he moved to Atlanta in 2009 with roughly twenty-five others to plant Blueprint Church on the conviction that the church "is not like family; it is family," and that disciple-making — not church-planting — is the true engine of multiplication.

## Origin & Formation

Raised in California in the home of a former NFL player, Lewis pursued football to the collegiate level at the University of North Texas. His ministry formation began in that collegiate world — serving as a chaplain to the UNT football team, coaching, and moving into college pastoring — before he planted a church in Texas and pursued graduate theological education (Dallas Theological Seminary; later Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary). ([blueprintchurch.org/dhati-lewis](https://www.blueprintchurch.org/dhati-lewis))

## Career & Ministry Arc

- **2009** — Moved to Atlanta with ~25 others; founded Blueprint Church (launched downtown 2010).
- **2011** — Launched the Rebuild Network to plant multi-ethnic churches in urban contexts; began partnering with NAMB's Send North America strategy.
- **2017** — Published *Among Wolves: Disciple-Making in the City* (B&H), with a companion LifeWay Bible study and leader kit.
- **Oct 2018** — Voted Vice President of Send Network by NAMB trustees, succeeding Jeff Christopherson.
- **2019** — Published *Advocates: The Narrow Path to Racial Reconciliation* (B&H); launched the Send Network–NAAF (National African American Fellowship) church-planting partnership.
- **~2019–2021** — Served as President of Send Network (~3 years leading the network).
- **End of 2021** — Departed NAMB to launch BLVD/MyBLVD under the Rebuild Initiative; succeeded as Send Network president by Vance Pitman.
- **2022–** — Continues as Blueprint's lead/vision pastor and founder/president of the Rebuild Initiative (MyBLVD Experience, MyBLVD Family, Known 360).

([baptistpress.com](https://www.baptistpress.com/resource-library/news/trustees-record-aaeo-new-vps-top-namb-agenda/), [namb.net](https://www.namb.net/news/send-networks-dhati-lewis-leaving-namb-for-new-project/))

## Current Focus

Lewis pastors Blueprint Church and leads the Rebuild Initiative / MyBLVD, an effort to "empower disciple-makers in majority-minority, multiethnic communities" and to develop and mentor young leaders who will plant churches in the urban context. He hosts the *Where Life Exists* and *MyBLVD* podcasts and teaches across the church-planting ecosystem (Together for the Gospel, Mosaix, Verge, Exponential). ([dhatilewis.com/about](https://www.dhatilewis.com/about))
