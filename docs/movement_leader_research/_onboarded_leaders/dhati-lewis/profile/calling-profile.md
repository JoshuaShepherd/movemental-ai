# Dhati Lewis: Calling Profile & Audit

## Executive Summary

Dhati Lewis is a movement-level church planter and disciple-making practitioner whose calling centers on keeping the church present in the urban, majority-minority context that it has too often abandoned. From founding Blueprint Church in Atlanta (2009) to leading the North American Mission Board's Send Network (2018–2021) to launching the Rebuild Initiative / MyBLVD, his vocational arc has consistently fused theology, mission, and context — refusing to choose between them. His calling statement, in his own words, is to help raise "the last generation to leave the urban context for sound discipleship."

## Calling Statement

To make disciples who make disciples in the city — establishing the church as family, multiplying multiethnic urban congregations, and mentoring the next generation of urban missionaries so that sound discipleship no longer requires leaving the urban context.

## Movement Leadership Map

- **Local:** Blueprint Church (founder/lead pastor) — a disciple-making, church-planting congregation.
- **Network:** Rebuild Initiative / Rebuild Network (founder) — multi-ethnic urban church planting.
- **National:** Send Network / NAMB (former VP → President) — national church-planting infrastructure; drove ethnic diversity and the NAAF partnership.
- **Formation:** BLVD / MyBLVD — mentoring and developing young urban-context leaders.

## Content-Calling Alignment Analysis

Lewis's published content maps tightly to his calling: *Among Wolves* (disciple-making in the city) and *Advocates* (reconciliation / multiethnic church) are direct expressions of his vocation, and his podcasts (*Where Life Exists*, *MyBLVD*) extend the same themes into ongoing teaching. The alignment between what he is called to and what he produces is high; the gap is one of *consolidation and reach* (see [../summary.md](../summary.md)), not of message coherence.

## Digital Calling Opportunity Map

His calling is well-served by a unified disciple-making/urban-mission body of work, but that work is currently scattered across a personal site, two org sites (Blueprint, MyBLVD), multiple podcast hubs, and several speaker pages. A consolidated, searchable home for the disciple-making frameworks, reconciliation teaching, and urban-missionary formation arc would most directly amplify the calling. (Factual fragmentation detail in [../fragmentation-story.md](../fragmentation-story.md).)

## Strategic Recommendations

- Consolidate the disciple-making and reconciliation corpus into one canonical, searchable surface.
- Make the named frameworks (Advocate/Aggravator, Eight Movements, 7 Stops of an Urban Missionary) first-class, linkable references.
- Capture the podcast/teaching catalogue (currently spread across Podbean, iHeart, Apple, YouTube) into a single discoverable archive.
