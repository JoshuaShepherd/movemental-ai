# Voice Analysis: Dhati Lewis

## Voice Summary

Dhati Lewis writes and speaks as an urban pastor-practitioner: framework-driven, exegetically anchored, and grounded in personal testimony. He packages teaching into memorable, often triadic schemes ("rely on Christ, run to the tension, respond with dignity"), builds whole books on a single biblical book (Matthew; Philemon), and addresses the reader directly as a fellow practitioner. His signature rhetorical move is to name a binary — context vs. theology, comfort vs. obedience, fleeing vs. staying — and refuse to resolve it by choosing a side, instead calling the listener to "run *to* the tension."

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tone | Earnest, pastoral, conviction-driven; warm but direct |
| Register | Accessible practitioner prose; code-switches into spoken Black-church cadence in interviews |
| Person | Second person ("you, pastor / leader / practitioner") and first-person testimony |
| Structure | Triadic, sequential, framework-scaffolded |

**Recurring phrases**: "disciple-making", "run to the tension", "the church as family", "the last generation to leave the urban context", "navigate the cultural divide", "respond with dignity"

**Favorite metaphors**: **family** (the church as literal family), **the city / boulevard ("where life exists")**, **the narrow path** (reconciliation), **wolves** (the hostile environment of urban disciple-making)

## Writing Characteristics

1. **Triadic, framework-driven structure** — teaching arrives as memorable three-part schemes (advocates "rely on Christ, run to the tension, respond with dignity").
2. **Exegetical anchoring** — a whole book built on one biblical book, with chapter-and-verse cited inline.
3. **Narrative / personal testimony** — opens from autobiography (NFL aspirations → conversion → call to the city) before moving to teaching.
4. **Direct second-person address** — speaks straight to "pastor, leader, practitioner."
5. **Conversational/vernacular register** — code-switches into spoken Black-church cadence in interviews, distinct from his more formal prose.

## Signature Patterns

The **"both/and tension as the doorway to mission"** move: he frames a binary, refuses to resolve it by picking a pole, and calls the listener to stay in / run toward the tension. Reinforced by the recurring "run to the tension" formula and alliterative triads.

## Representative quotes

> "Cities are not only diverse culturally, but also socioeconomically. In urban settings, in a one-mile area, one can see extreme poverty intertwined with extreme wealth…" — *Among Wolves*

> "And many of us define God's will by the path of least resistance… our North Stars are comfort, and we're addicted to our comfort." — *Where Ya From?* Ep. 56

> "It was like either I was around people who got my context but didn't have that same commitment to theology and mission, or I was around people who got my mission in theology, but they didn't get my context." — *Where Ya From?* Ep. 56

## Speaking vs. Writing

In print his register is more formal and exegetical; in interviews and from the platform he code-switches into a vernacular, spoken-Black-church cadence ("they ain't talking about this") and leans harder on testimony and call-and-response rhythm. The frameworks stay constant across both modes.
