# Biography: Hugh Halter

Hugh Halter is a missional church leader, author, speaker, and consultant based in Alton, Illinois, whose work over more than three decades has helped shape the North American conversation about incarnational community and the gathered-and-scattered church. With his wife Cheryl, he has been a church planter and missionary in North America for more than thirty years, pioneering missional expressions in Portland (Oregon), Denver (Colorado), and now Alton (Illinois). In Denver he founded and pastored Adullam, a congregational network of incarnational communities, and co-directed Missio — an apprenticing network for missional leaders — with his longtime co-author and collaborator Matt Smay. He served as the U.S. director of Forge America, the missional training network.

In 2016 Halter founded Lantern Network in Alton, and in 2018 he revived the city's shuttered federal post office building as Post Commons — a community hub for connection, food, coffee, and collaboration that functions as the innovation, enterprise, and mission center for Lantern. He also runs Rí Beag Refuge, an 80-acre equine-therapy farm. His most recent work is organized around the idea of "kingdom ecosystems," developed through the Brave Cities network and consultancy he co-leads with Taylor McCall.

He is the author or co-author of ten books published across multiple imprints over two decades, including *The Tangible Kingdom*, *AND: The Gathered and Scattered Church*, *Sacrilege*, *BiVo*, *Flesh*, *Brimstone*, *Righteous Brood*, and *Brave Cities*. His body of work is substantial and coherent, centered on incarnational community, bi-vocational ("BiVo") ministry, holy nonjudgment, family as mission, and kingdom ecosystems — though it is dispersed across several publishers, organizations, and platforms with no single owned hub.
