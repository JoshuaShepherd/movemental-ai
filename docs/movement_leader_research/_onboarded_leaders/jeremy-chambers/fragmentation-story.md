## The Story

Jeremy "Jemo" Chambers built his public corpus across **three geographies and two ecclesial experiments** — Arise RVA in Richmond (2013–2019), then Pando Denver with Rowland Smith (2020+), while ascending to **Training Director of Forge America**. His books with Monica live on **100 Movements Publishing** and Amazon. His essays live on **V3**, **Renovaré**, and **Missio Alliance**. His 2024 book launch scattered across **five podcasts** in four months.

The integrative thesis — missional spirituality as a single rhythm, not two sequential stages — is consistent. The **storage is not**. A reader who discovers *The Art of Missional Spirituality* on Amazon does not automatically find the V3 essay archive, the Forge training pathway, the Pando Denver network, or the Renovaré contributor page. There is no confirmed personal newsletter bridging these surfaces.

Chambers' fragmentation is typical of a **Forge-era practitioner-author**: deep institutional embed (Forge, Pando, 100 Movements, Renovaré), two co-authored books, a strong essay tail — but no owned hub that catalogs the whole body of work for a leader arriving cold.
