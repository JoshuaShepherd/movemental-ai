# Identity

Jeremy Chambers — known informally as "Jemo" (the source of his personal domain, jemochambers.com) — is an American church planter, spiritual director, and missional trainer based in Denver, Colorado. He was born around 1986 (the year is inferred from his account of praying about a move to Denver since age thirteen in 1999) and relocated to Denver from Richmond, Virginia in late 2019 or early 2020. He is married to Monica Paredes Chambers, born in Costa Rica, who is his co-author and ministry partner; the two operate as a writing-and-teaching dyad, and the majority of his published work is signed jointly with her.

## Roles

His primary roles are church planter, urban missionary, spiritual director, author, and trainer/coach. His primary organizational affiliations are Forge America (Training Director), The Pando Collective (Denver network leader), and the Arise RVA Church Network (founder, 2013). He is also a contributing author with the V3 Movement and a contributor to Renovaré.

## Disambiguation

There are several public figures named Jeremy Chambers. This profile concerns **only** the American missional practitioner: Forge America Training Director, co-author of *The Art of Missional Spirituality*, and leader within the Arise and Pando microchurch networks. He should not be confused with Capt. Jeremy Chambers of the U.S. Army, an indie musician of the same name with a Bandcamp release, an education-doctorate holder teaching in Japan, or the Australian jockey who is the subject of the *Jeremy Chambers* Wikipedia article. Reliable disambiguating modifiers include "Jemo Chambers," "Forge America," "Pando Collective," "Arise RVA," and "missional spirituality."

## Coined term

Chambers coined "Grativangelism" (gratitude + evangelism) in a 2018 V3 Movement essay — a practice-frame in which gratitude expressed publicly toward neighbors and strangers functions as an evangelistic posture.
