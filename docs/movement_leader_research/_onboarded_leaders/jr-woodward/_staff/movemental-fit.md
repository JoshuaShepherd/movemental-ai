## What Movemental Would Change for This Author

### Problems Movemental Solves

- **Platform fragmentation**: His content lives across 12+ platforms he doesn't own (V3 site, Seminary Now, Missio Alliance, Verge Network, Academia.edu, Amazon, Goodreads, IVP, 100Movements, multiple podcast hosts, conference archives, WordPress blog). Movemental provides a single owned hub with structured navigation.
- **No audience ownership beyond Substack**: He reaches thousands through V3 training, conference speaking, and institutional channels, but his only owned audience channel is a Substack with "hundreds" of subscribers. No email capture on his main website. No way to communicate directly with the 1,000+ church planters V3 has trained outside of organizational channels.
- **Invisible training archive**: 30+ weeks of Praxis Cohort curriculum, 4-6 week immersion courses, 12-week Reimagining Church program, Fuller DGL cohort content, and 4 seminary teaching positions generate zero publicly accessible digital content. This represents the largest untapped content reservoir.
- **Video content scattered**: Teaching content exists across Seminary Now, V3 media, Exponential recordings, Awakenings Gathering recordings, and third-party podcast video channels. No centralized personal video presence or YouTube channel.
- **No monetization infrastructure beyond books and Substack**: Amazon royalties, IVP/100Movements royalties, and Substack's optional $10/month tier are his only direct content revenue streams. No course platform, no downloadable resources, no community membership offering.
- **Domain and brand fragmentation**: jrwoodward.com, jrwoodward.net, jrwoodward.me, and a legacy Blogspot all create confusion. The personal website (WordPress/Astra) underperforms for an author of his stature -- no book showcase, no embedded media, no newsletter CTA.
- **Academic content locked in silos**: 477 Academia.edu followers, 8 papers, and ~274 Google Scholar citations represent significant scholarly credibility that is invisible to his practitioner audience. His PhD thesis-to-book pipeline (University of Manchester -> *The Scandal of Leadership*) is a model others would learn from, but nowhere is this journey made accessible.

### Content Movemental Would Surface

- **4 books** -- cataloged with summaries, key frameworks, purchase links, review excerpts, and cross-references to related courses/articles in a unified author profile
- **8+ academic papers** from Academia.edu -- organized by theme (digital culture and missiology, leadership, election doctrine, development) with accessible summaries for non-academic readers

## What We Will NOT Do

- **Replace V3 Movement**: Movemental is complementary to V3's training infrastructure, not competitive. V3's Praxis Cohorts, immersion courses, and coaching model continue unchanged. Movemental amplifies JR's personal voice and extends V3's reach, but does not replace the relational, cohort-based training model.
- **Remove content from existing platforms**: Articles on Missio Alliance, Verge Network, V3 blog, and other outlets stay where they are. Academic papers remain on Academia.edu. Movemental aggregates and links; it does not extract.
- **Compete with Substack**: "Faithful Resistance" is a distinct publication with its own voice (more politically engaged than his books). Movemental complements the Substack by providing the broader professional profile, course infrastructure, and community tools that Substack does not offer. The Substack remains the primary newsletter channel.
- **Dilute the academic voice**: His PhD research and scholarly credibility are core differentiators. Movemental will surface this work accessibly but will not dumb it down or strip the academic rigor from his content.
- **Change his theological positions**: His missional theology (Newbigin-influenced, incarnational, kenotic, justice-oriented) is preserved exactly as is. His Substack's political engagement ("Faithful Resistance") is his voice -- Movemental doesn't edit, soften, or reframe.
- **Force social media activity**: If he prefers Twitter/X and Substack as his primary channels, that's fine. Movemental enhances what exists rather than demanding Instagram Reels or TikTok content.
- **Pressure monetization**: Some of his best content (V3 training frameworks, academic papers, Substack posts) is free and accessible. Movemental provides monetization tools but doesn't require their use.
