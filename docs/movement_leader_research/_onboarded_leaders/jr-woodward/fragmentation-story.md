## The Story

Gailand A "JR" Woodward Jr. has been planting and training church planters for **more than 30 years**. He founded New Life Christian Fellowship at Virginia Tech in **1989** and grew it from 24 to ~1,200 people with 500+ new Christians and 40+ sent into full-time ministry. He co-founded **Kairos Los Angeles** (2002–2012) with three LA-area plants. He co-founded and served as West Coast then national director of **Ecclesia Network** (2007–2012), growing it from four plants to ~30 churches / ~4,000 people at ~30% annual growth. He co-founded the **Solis Foundation** in Turkana, Kenya (2007–2012), starting roughly ten new businesses per year. He co-founded **Missio Alliance** in 2012 (first national gathering drew 700+ leaders). Since 2013 he has served as **National Director of the V3 Church Planting Movement**, which has trained **600+ church planters in its first ten years** with a stated goal of 600 more in the next five. Also in 2013 he co-founded the **annual Praxis Gathering**. And in June 2020, at age 50-plus, he was awarded a **Ph.D. from the University of Manchester / Nazarene Theological College**, with an 80,385-word dissertation titled *Missional Leadership, Mimetic Desire, and a Theology of the Powers* — supervised by Dr. Phil Meadows, with Dr. Joseph Wood as internal examiner and Prof. Wilbert Shenk as external examiner, dedicated "To the V3 Church Planting Movement."

That dissertation became his 2023 book **The Scandal of Leadership: Unmasking the Powers of Domination in the Church** (100 Movements Publishing), which won Joel Wentz's "Books and Big Ideas" Book of the Year and was a runner-up at Englewood Review of Books' Readers Best Awards. It is his third book, following *Creating a Missional Culture* (IVP, 2012) and the IVP Reader's Choice Award–winning *The Church as Movement* (IVP, 2016, co-authored with Dan White Jr.). The 2023 book joined a shelf of writing that combines two strands most practitioners never combine: **Walter Wink's theology of the Powers** and **René Girard's mimetic theory**, synthesized through William Stringfellow into a framework for missional leadership formation.

And here is where the fragmentation becomes visible. The CV on file (`pdf/cv-jr-woodward.pdf`) enumerates a **"large" set of book endorsements and forewords** that existing web-only research had never catalogued. His Academia.edu profile (`JRWoodwardPhD`) exists. His Ph.D. thesis (`pdf/phd-thesis-missional-leadership-mimetic-desire-theology-of-the-powers.pdf`) exists on file. He teaches as adjunct at **Fuller (MAGL directed studies + Doctor of Global Leadership cohort starting Spring 2025), America Evangelical University, Capital Seminary and Graduate School (DC), Central Seminary, Missio Theological Seminary, and SOMA University** — including a 2023 DM747 lecture at World Mission University & SOMA. He serves on **10 boards / advisory bodies** — Reliant Mission (2004–), Manchester Wesley Research Centre (Associate Fellow, 2015–), Gospel and Our Culture Network (2016–), Colloquium on Violence and Religion (2018–), Fuller Church Planting Initiative (2020–), Kineo Center (2020–), Movement Leaders Collective Advisory Board (2021–), Fuller Global Mission Advisory Council (2021–), Missio Alliance Governing Board (2014–), American Society of Missiology (2023–). He is a confirmed Exponential speaker.
