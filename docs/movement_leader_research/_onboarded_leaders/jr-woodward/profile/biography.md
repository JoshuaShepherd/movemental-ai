**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-04-13

## Narrative Biography

JR Woodward is a church planter, missiologist, and movement leader who has spent more than three decades establishing missional-incarnational communities across the United States. As the National Director of the V3 Church Planting Movement, co-founder of Missio Alliance and the Praxis Gathering, and author of three books on missional culture, movement ecclesiology, and leadership, Woodward has become one of the most trusted practitioner-scholars in the North American missional church movement. His work is distinguished by the rare integration of grassroots church planting experience with rigorous academic research -- his most recent book, *The Scandal of Leadership* (2023), is a direct outgrowth of his PhD dissertation at the University of Manchester.

Woodward's church planting journey began in 1989 when he founded New Life Christian Fellowship (NLCF) at Virginia Tech in Blacksburg, Virginia, as part of Great Commission Ministries. In the first five years of that plant, he experienced both the loneliness of pioneering leadership and the difficulty of cultivating missional hearts in an inherited congregation. NLCF grew to become a significant campus and community presence; by 2007, the church had branched into two congregations -- one serving Virginia Tech students and another serving the broader New River Valley community. During the Virginia Tech shooting of 2007, the church played a visible role in the campus community's response, with two members among those killed and ten others directly connected to the church.

Around 2003, Woodward and other NLCF members planted Kairos Los Angeles, a network of neighborhood churches in the East Hollywood area. This move to Los Angeles deepened Woodward's commitment to incarnational, neighborhood-rooted ministry. He lived in East Hollywood and served on the East Hollywood Neighborhood Council, embodying his conviction that the church should be embedded in the fabric of its local community rather than operating as an institution set apart from it. He co-founded several additional organizations during this period: the Solis Foundation (focused on walking alongside the poor in Lodwar, Kenya), the Ecclesia Network, and eventually Missio Alliance and the Praxis Gathering -- an annual conference of over three hundred on-the-ground missional practitioners.

Woodward pursued academic work alongside his planting. He holds a B.S. in psychology from Radford University (1987), an M.A. from Fuller Theological Seminary in missional leadership (2011) with a thesis titled *Equippers as Environmentalist: Re-imaging Leadership in Today's Western Church*, and in 2020 completed a PhD at the University of Manchester (research based at Nazarene Theological College) supervised by Phil Meadows, examined internally by Joseph Wood and externally by Wilbert Shenk. His dissertation, *Missional Leadership, Mimetic Desire, and a Theology of the Powers* (~80,400 words), synthesized Walter Wink, René Girard, and William Stringfellow to develop an imitation-based framework for domineering leadership and Christ-shaped alternative. Colleagues including Dwight Swanson at NTC gave formative feedback on the project. The work was published in adapted form as *The Scandal of Leadership* in 2023. His first book, *Creating a Missional Culture* (IVP, 2012), introduced the concept of polycentric leadership and the five "culture creators" -- Dream Awakeners, Heart Revealers, Story Tellers, Soul Healers, and Light Givers -- as a practical application of Ephesians 4 ministry. His second, *The Church as Movement* (IVP, 2016), co-authored with Dan White Jr. and with a foreword by Alan Hirsch, outlined eight competencies for creating movemental churches rooted in neighborhoods.

Today, Woodward serves as National Director of the V3 Church Planting Movement, where he trains church planters across North America and internationally in what he calls "grounded spirituality, missions theology, and movement ecclesiology." He leads V3 collaboratively with Dan White Jr. and Jesse Eubanks, embodying the shared leadership model he advocates. He serves as an adjunct professor at Central Seminary, Missio Seminary, and American Evangelical University, and sits on multiple boards including Missio Alliance, Reliant Mission, Fuller's Global Mission Advisory Council, Fuller's Church Initiatives Board, and KINEO. He currently serves locally with the District Church in Washington, DC. Outside of ministry, Woodward is a surfer, skateboarder, photographer, and film enthusiast who attends the Sundance Film Festival whenever possible.

## Origin & Formation

JR Woodward's full legal name is Gailand Woodward Jr., as recorded in his University of Manchester PhD thesis. No public information is available about his birth date, birthplace, or early life. His career in ministry began in 1989 with the founding of NLCF at Virginia Tech, suggesting he was likely in his twenties at that time. His about.me profile and self-description consistently emphasize his identity as someone who "encourages people to participate in God's renewal of all things" -- a missional and activist orientation that has been consistent from his earliest public work.

His formative years as a church planter at Virginia Tech shaped his understanding of the difficulties of cultivating missional culture. He has spoken publicly about the loneliness of those early years and the challenge of inheriting a congregation without a missional heart. This experience became the laboratory for the frameworks he would later develop in *Creating a Missional Culture*.
