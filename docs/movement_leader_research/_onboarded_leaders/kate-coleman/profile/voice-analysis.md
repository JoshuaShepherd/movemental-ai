# Voice Analysis

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Tone | warm, candid, encouraging, pastoral, quietly authoritative |
| Register | practitioner / pastoral — a coach and minister speaking to leaders as peers |

Kate Coleman's voice combines the directness of an executive coach with the warmth of a pastor. She names hard things plainly — self-defeating behaviours, the cost of people-pleasing, the limits leaders place on themselves — but always toward transformation rather than rebuke. She speaks from Scripture and from decades in the room, moving easily between biblical narrative and the lived realities of leading across sectors. Her questions do as much work as her statements: she reorients leaders by asking rather than telling.

**Recurring phrases**:
- "Why me? Why here? Why this? Why now?"
- "self-defeating behaviour"
- "the disease to please"
- "transforming your life and leadership"
- "godly self-discovery, healthy relationships, and Spirit-led mission"
- "extraordinary, ordinary people of the Bible"
- "Stay focused"

**Favorite metaphors**:
- **Metamorphosis / transformation** — leadership as a metamorphic change of the whole person, not just acquired skill.
- **The seven deadly sins** — naming self-defeating leadership patterns through the inherited language of "sins" turned inward and diagnostic.
- **Baptism of fire** — formation forged through resistance and trial.
- **Drawing the line** — boundaries as a leadership discipline.
- **The inner circle** — the protected core of family and key relationships a leader must not neglect.
