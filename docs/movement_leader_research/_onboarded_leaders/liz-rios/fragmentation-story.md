## The Story

Rev. Dr. Elizabeth "Liz" D. Rios has carried thirty-five-plus years of ministry through pastoring, planting, teaching, consulting, and editing — and into infrastructure. She founded **Passion2Plant** in 2020, the only national BIPOC-woman-led church planting network in the United States, with three named cohort tracks (Just Church, Trauma, Púlpito Fellows). She directs **Púlpito Fellows**, a three-year Lilly Endowment–funded bilingual preaching fellowship at Urban Strategies, running 10-week cohorts (one 1.5-day in-person launch plus eight Zoom sessions) coordinated with Rev. Aracelis Vasquez Haye, whose explicit deliverable is "three digital products per fellow." She founded and curates **The Passion Center**, an online missional community and training platform, and her earliest flagship — the Center for Emerging Female Leadership in NYC — ran from 1996 to 2001. She holds a BA, MA, EdD from Nova Southeastern (dissertation: *Evaluating Transformative Spirituality in an Organizational Leadership Program at a Christian University*), a DMin from Southeastern (dissertation: *Developing Civically Engaged, Justice Oriented Churches Right From the Start*), and a 2025 MA in Social Justice from Union Theological Seminary. She is ordained Christian Church (Disciples of Christ), serves on its Commission on Ministry, was previously credentialed Assemblies of God through Primitive Christian Church, NYC. She sits on the board of Sojourners, on the executive team of the Latino Leadership Circle (with Rev. David Ramos, Rev. Dr. Luis Alvarez, Rev. Dr. Orlando Crespo, Elder Belinda Passafaro), as senior consultant at Lisa Sharon Harper's Freedom Road, as adjunct faculty at Fuller Theological Seminary, as senior mentor in the R.E.A.L. Black Women in Ministry THRIVE initiative, as consulting editor at *Outreach Magazine* since 2008, and as a co-vocational leader at Urban Strategies for 20+ years. In October 2025 she became the only woman inducted into Palm Beach Atlantic University's inaugural Florida College of Preachers.

That is a stunning amount of codified, durable work. And yet the picture that emerges from the dossier is of a Latina pastor-scholar-operator whose **owned digital surface area is split across three domains, two newsletters, two YouTube channels, one hosted podcast, and an audience that does not yet have a first-class author platform representing Afro-Boricua mujerista voice at production-quality scale**. She runs `elizabethrios.com` (personal brand, reads like a static portfolio), `thepassioncenter.org` (training center), and `passion2plant.com` (cohort network) — three sites, no canonical hub. She writes under two flags — Substack *Mujerista in Motion* (handle `drlizrios`) and Medium `drlizrios.medium.com` — without cross-post automation. She runs two YouTube presences — The Passion Center channel (`@thepassioncenter`) and the separate **Púlpito Voices** channel for the fellowship — both low-production, mostly event capture, neither editorially sequenced. Her hosted podcast work is the Latino Leadership Circle Podcast (moderator role); her guest velocity is high — *Be the Bridge* with Latasha Morrison, *The Mestizo Podcast* with Emanuel Padilla, *The Protagonistas* with Kat Armas, *Right Side Up* with Danielle Strickland, *And the Church Said*, *The Church Planting Podcast* with Greg Nettle and Len Tang, *Ephesiology*, the COGIC Hispanic Initiatives Podcast with John Edward Perez and Dr. Eli Valentín, and more — but the canonical archive of her own hosted feed is under-developed.

And even the public-facing layer is fractured. She is a contributor to **100 Movements Publishing**'s *Need to Know* (2023, curated by Danielle Strickland, alongside Alan Hirsch, Scot McKnight, Jo Saxton, Lisa Rodriguez-Watson, Beth Allison Barr, Mandy Smith, Bob Ekblad, and Lori Anne Thompson). She is sole author of *Don't Buy The Lie: Eradicating False Belief Systems That Keep You From Your Destiny* (Ghetto Rose Publications, 2012). She is co-curator with Amb. Suzan Johnson Cook on *Rhythms of Rest: 40 Devotions for Women on the Move* (Our Daily Bread). She wrote "The Women are Warriors" for Oxford University Press's *Latino Religions and Civic Activism in the United States*, reprinted in the *Azusa Street and Beyond* centennial volume. She publishes columns in Sojourners, Christianity Today, Influence, Called Magazine, PCPJ, Red Letter Christians, Latin Business Today, *The Atlantic*, *The Washington Post*, and Religion News Service — none of it indexed back to a single canonical hub. Her bilingual register (Púlpito, Cafecito, Madrinas, Latinas of Legacy, Mujerista) is a primary brand signal, but no platform she uses today treats Spanish-English code-switching as a first-class concern. Her audience is being served everywhere and nowhere.

## Synthesis

Liz Rios's fragmentation is **not** the generic shape of "your work is scattered." It is the precise shape of **an Afro-Boricua mujerista pastor-scholar-operator running a national BIPOC-woman-led church planting network and a Lilly-funded bilingual preaching fellowship from three uncoordinated owned domains, two newsletters, two YouTube channels, one moderator-only podcast, and a guest-velocity speaking calendar that everyone else's audience benefits from before her own does**. The Púlpito Fellows program already has the cohort architecture, the bilingual register, the funding structure, the explicit deliverable ("three digital products per fellow"), and the editorial coordinator (Rev. Aracelis Vasquez Haye) — but the artifacts those fellows produce do not yet roll up into a searchable, sequenced, mujerista-voiced corpus indexed at `elizabethrios.com` or `thepassioncenter.org` or `passion2plant.com` or any fourth canonical hub. Her authored corpus — *Don't Buy The Lie*, the *Need to Know* contribution alongside Alan Hirsch and Danielle Strickland and Lisa Rodriguez-Watson, the *Rhythms of Rest* co-curation with Suzan Johnson Cook, the Oxford "Women are Warriors" chapter, the two dissertations, the Sojourners and CT and PCPJ and Influence columns, the *Mujerista in Motion* Substack, the *Cafecito Conversations* and *Latinas of Legacy* and *Passion2Plant Mentor Moment* video series, the Mama Leo Lecture at Union — has the volume to make RAG and voice-fidelity tooling materially useful. She is past the cold-start problem. What she does not yet have is **one canonical Latina Pentecostal mujerista author platform that treats Spanish-English code-switching as first-class, indexes the corpus across the three domains and two newsletters, and turns Púlpito Fellows' "three digital products per fellow" into a sequenced public library** rather than a per-cohort scatter.

The diagnosis is precise: a diasporic Afro-Boricua bilingual cohort-operator's authority is scattered across institutional pulpits she does not own — Sojourners, Freedom Road, Outreach, *Need to Know*, Union, Fuller, Palm Beach Atlantic, Lilly, Urban Strategies, the Latino Leadership Circle, Disciples of Christ — with no central searchable hub that her own audience can return to in either language.
