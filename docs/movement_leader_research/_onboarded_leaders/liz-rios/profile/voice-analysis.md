# Voice analysis — Rev. Dr. Liz Rios

Preliminary voice markers extracted from corpus surface (essays, interviews, keynote titles). Confirm with primary-source sampling before using for any AI voice-fidelity work.

## Register
- **Personal + pastoral + prophetic** — she slides between first-person memoir, pastoral care-language, and prophetic rebuke in a single piece.
- **Bilingual / code-switching** as identity performance, not ornamentation. Spanish titles (*Púlpito*, *Cafecito*, *Madrinas*, *Latinas of Legacy*) frame English content.
- **Named accountability** — she *names* people and institutions (both as honor and as critique): Samuel Rodriguez, Christian Nationalism, specific networks that failed her, specific mentors (Mama Leo, Bishop Rivera, Suzan Johnson Cook).

## Recurring vocabulary
`global majority` · `BIPOC` · `mujerista` · `barrio` · `justice-oriented` · `holistic` · `prophetic` · `emerging (female) leaders` · `digital pulpit` · `oppressive systems` · `cultivate` · `disrupt` · `legacy` · `madrinas` · `cafecito` · `warriors`

## Argument patterns
1. **"I was trained in X, and X failed me. Here is what is needed instead."** — origin story of Passion2Plant.
2. **"Before me, there was her."** — positions herself in continuity with unnamed / under-named Latina foremothers (Mama Leo, Ada María Isasi-Díaz).
3. **"Latina/o Christians are X% of the church — ignoring them is ecclesial malpractice."** — demographic framing (Atlantic 2021, Axios 2024).
4. **Justice as formation** — refuses the separation between "spiritual formation" and "civic praxis."
5. **Naming the cost** — explicit about what it has cost her personally (losing a child, being the only woman in rooms, credentialing as double/triple-doctored woman of color to be heard).
