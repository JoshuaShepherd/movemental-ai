# Content Analysis: Meghan Larissa Good

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## Executive Summary

Meghan Larissa Good is a narrative theologian-preacher whose body of work moves along a single deepening trajectory: the Western church has inherited a distorted image of God — one shaped by coercive power, fear, and cultural accommodation rather than by the person of Jesus Christ — and the remedy is not merely theological correction but a movement-level recovery of what she calls "a better Christian story." Her distinctive contribution is the **synthesis of Anabaptist Christocentrism, award-winning narrative homiletics, and centered-set movement ecclesiology** expressed in accessible, literary prose that holds together rigorous theology and pastoral warmth. Where JR Woodward builds diagnostic frameworks for leadership failure and Alan Hirsch constructs theories of apostolic movement, Good occupies a third position: the **narrative theologian-preacher** who helps individuals and communities feel the beauty and coherence of the Jesus-centered God before they can name the theology. Her work progresses from biblical interpretation (*The Bible Unwrapped*) to movement manifesto (*Divine Gravity*), each layer adding scope while keeping Jesus at the center. At a career inflection point — just leaving a 12-year pastorate for full-time network and resource work — her intellectual output and vocational focus are converging toward what could be her most formative and widely-reaching decade.

## Theme Map

### Primary Themes (Core to their identity)

| Theme | Description | Key Works | Centrality |
|-------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| The Jesus Lens / "Jesus Glasses" Hermeneutic | Jesus is not "the softer side of God that gets averaged" with divine violence or judgment; he is the fullness of God (Col. 1:19), God's exact imprint (Heb. 1:3), and the supreme interpretive authority for all of Scripture. Reading the Bible through Jesus means every text — including OT violence, difficult Pauline passages, and apocalyptic material — must be read through and toward its culmination in Christ, not alongside him as one data point among many. | *The Bible Unwrapped* (2018); DMin dissertation *Between Breaths* (2018); "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel (2026); Anabaptist Study Bible Conference (2022); throughout teaching | 10 |
| The Better Christian Story (Movement Framing) | Western Christianity has been telling "too small" a story — too narrow, too fearful, too focused on individual escape rather than cosmic restoration. Recovering the "better Christian story" requires a movement-scale reformation analogous to the Protestant Reformation: a Spirit-led, grassroots rediscovery of Jesus-centered faith that can hold together a fractured, polarized church. Eight interlocking theses form the content of this recovered story. | *Divine Gravity* (2023); Anabaptist World review (Cramer, 2024); Shifting Culture Podcast Episode 148; Jesus Collective resource work | 10 |
| Divine Gravity / Centered-Set Ecclesiology | Jesus is the gravitational center that holds together diverse, naturally repelling individuals and communities. Borrowing from centered-set theory (Mark D. Baker), Good argues that Christian community is not defined by its boundary positions (who is in/out based on doctrinal fences) but by trajectory of motion toward Jesus. Those drawn toward the divine grace embodied in Jesus are the church — regardless of their position on peripheral questions. This reframes both evangelism and ecclesial unity. | *Divine Gravity* (2023); MennoCon21 address (2021); speaking on depolarizing the church | 9 |
| Cross-Shaped Power | The Jesus way of exercising power is categorically different from worldly power. The temptations of Matthew 4 were genuine — using power for righteous ends (liberation of the oppressed) was available to Jesus — and he refused. God's kingdom advances through self-sacrifice, vulnerability, and suffering love, not military, political, or institutional force. This "cross-shaped power" appears "slower, more painful, seemingly like losing before winning" but alone genuinely defeats evil. This is not passivity but the most radical form of agency. | *Divine Gravity* (2023); "Jesus and the Devil's Bargain" (journal essay, 2024); Anabaptist peace-church theological framework | 9 |
| Wounded Image of God | Many contemporary Christians carry "a profound wound in their image of God" — shaped by distorted theologies of divine violence, wrath-as-punishment, and coercive power that contradict what is revealed in Jesus. The healing of this wound requires not better arguments but direct encounter with the Jesus-centered God. "As our image of God is transformed by Jesus, we are set free to experience deeper transformation in our own humanity." This is the pastoral-therapeutic dimension of her hermeneutical framework. | meghanlarissagood.com/about/; throughout her work; *Divine Gravity* (2023) | 8 |

### Has their thinking shifted?

Good's thinking has deepened in scope without shifting in center. The unchanging center is Jesus — who he is, what he reveals about God, and what it means to follow him. The progression moves from incarnational ethics (phase 1) to hermeneutical framework (phase 2) to movement theology (phase 3) — each layer adding scope while keeping Jesus at the center. The theological content of phases 2 and 3 was always implicit in phase 1; Good has made explicit what was tacit.

The most significant development is the move from congregational to movement-level thinking: *The Bible Unwrapped* was a resource for individuals and study groups; *Divine Gravity* is a manifesto for a multi-denominational reformation. This is not a shift in theology but a shift in scope and vocation — confirmed by the resignation from Trinity Mennonite and the assumption of the Jesus Collective role.

## Unique Contribution

### What only this author says

- **"Jesus is not the softer side of God that gets averaged"**: This formulation — that Jesus does not balance out divine violence by averaging it with divine love, but rather reveals the fullness of God against which everything else must be evaluated — is Good's signature hermeneutical claim. Other christocentric scholars come close, but her formulation of it in accessible, memorable language is distinctive.

- **The "Divine Gravity" metaphor as ecclesiological model**: While centered-set theory (Hiebert, Baker) is not unique to Good, her deployment of gravity as the organizing metaphor — Jesus as a gravitational center, belonging defined by trajectory toward him, unity emerging from shared orbital direction rather than boundary enforcement — is her original contribution. It is accessible, counterintuitive enough to be memorable, and theologically substantive enough to do real work.

- **The wounded-image-of-God pastoral framework**: Good names a specific spiritual injury — a distorted image of God shaped by violence, fear, and coercive power — and offers a specific remedy: encounter with the Jesus-centered God. This is not merely "people need to know Jesus better" but a targeted diagnosis and a targeted prescription. No other voice in the progressive-evangelical or Anabaptist space articulates this as clearly.

- **Narrative preaching in service of movement theology**: Good is perhaps the only voice in the "movement Christianity" conversation who is also an award-winning preacher in the literary-narrative tradition of Frederick Buechner. Movement leaders (Hirsch, Woodward, McLaren) are not primarily preachers in this sense. Good brings a preacher's instinct for the specific, concrete, embodied moment that carries abstract truth — and applies it to movement-scale theological claims.

### Where they disagree with peers

| Topic | Good's Position | Mainstream/Peer Position |
|-------|----------------|------------------------|
| Centered-set vs. bounded-set church | Centered-set: belonging defined by trajectory toward Jesus, not boundary doctrinal positions | Most evangelical ecclesiologies assume bounded-set (statement of faith, membership process, doctrinal agreement as criteria) |
| Peace without Jesus | "Reconciliation work that gives up on proclaiming Jesus loses its very ground" | Many Mennonite social justice practitioners have increasingly bracketed explicit Christ-proclamation in favor of broader coalition work |
| OT violence and divine character | Must be read through and in light of Jesus; cannot be "averaged" with Christ's revelation | Conservative biblical scholarship holds to "progressive revelation" where both OT and NT are equally direct revelation; liberals dismiss OT violence as primitive religion |
| Christian celebrity culture | Authentic authority is marked by cross-bearing, not charisma or "resume points" | Much Christian media culture evaluates leaders by platform size, rhetorical performance, and institutional prestige |

### What would be lost without their work

Without Good's work, the deconstruction-to-reconstruction conversation in progressive-evangelical and Anabaptist Christianity would lack its most theologically coherent, narratively compelling, and pastorally warm Christocentric voice. *The Bible Unwrapped* remains one of the most accessible and theologically serious introductions to Jesus-centered hermeneutics in English — many readers call it "the best book I've read on the Bible." *Divine Gravity*'s eight-thesis framework for a "better Christian story" provides movement leaders with language for articulating a positive vision, not merely a critique of Christendom. And the "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel's daily engagement with the Gospel of Luke represents a sustained, publicly available catechesis in Christocentric biblical interpretation that no other voice is currently providing at this scale and format.

## Content Interconnections

### How themes connect across works

Good's body of work follows a clear deepening progression: **how to read Scripture** (hermeneutics) leads to **what the story says** (theology and movement thesis) leads to **daily companionship through the text** (devotional practice).

- *The Bible Unwrapped* (2018) provides the foundational method — the Jesus lens, dialogic authority, genre sensitivity. This is the hermeneutical toolkit.
- *Divine Gravity* (2023) applies the method to its largest possible object: the whole Christian story, the nature of God, the character of salvation, the structure of the church, the shape of mission. This is the movement manifesto.
- "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel (2026) practices both — reading Luke chapter by chapter through the Jesus lens, applying the "better story" framework to specific pericopes. This is the daily catechetical extension of the first two works.
- Journal essays (2024–2026) deepen specific themes: cross-shaped power ("Jesus and the Devil's Bargain"), prophetic authority ("The Test of a Prophet"), divine engagement with history ("Four Forgotten Truths"), spiritual formation ("Always Hungry," "Healing Religion"). Each essay is an expansion of a theorem from the books into a specific pastoral application.

### Recommended engagement order

| Order | Work | Why this sequence |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1 | *The Bible Unwrapped* (2018) | Foundational method; most accessible entry; establishes the Jesus-lens hermeneutic that everything else builds on |
| 2 | *Divine Gravity* (2023) — or its self-narrated audiobook | Applies the method to the whole story; the movement manifesto; best understood after the hermeneutical framework is in place |
| 3 | "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel — Luke series (2026) | Daily practice of the framework; seeing the books enacted in real-time biblical engagement |
| 4 | Journal essays (meghanlarissagood.com/journal) | Specific pastoral and theological applications; best read after the books establish the framework |
| 5 | Podcast interviews (ReKnew, Red Letter Christians, Shifting Culture) | Conversation-mode expression of the same ideas; accessible entry points for audio learners |

## Movemental Content Strategy Implications

- **Pillar mapping**: Good serves the **Biblical Interpretation**, **Movement Ecclesiology**, **Deconstruction/Reconstruction**, **Preaching and Homiletics**, and **Jesus-Centered Theology** content pillars. Her work also feeds into **Pastoral Formation**, **Gender and Ministry**, and **Ecumenism/Unity** categories.

- **Cross-author connections**: Strong alignment with **Greg Boyd** (shared Christocentric hermeneutic, mutual forewords, Jesus Collective; Boyd is the most natural platform pairing). Meaningful connection with **Dennis Edwards** (foreword to *Divine Gravity*; Missio Alliance bridge). Thematic alignment with **Brian McLaren** (post-evangelical reform, better story framing) though McLaren operates at larger scale and different theological register. Conceptual overlap with any Anabaptist-tradition authors on platform.

- **Content gaps Movemental can fill**: (1) No course derived from *The Bible Unwrapped* despite the book's proven reception as an "accessible primer" for study groups — the free study guide is the foundation of a structured course; (2) The "Reading Jesus" YouTube series has no companion guide, no cross-referencing to the books, and no community context — a Movemental hub could provide all three; (3) 12 years of Trinity Mennonite sermons are unarchived and largely inaccessible — this represents the largest untapped content reservoir; (4) No single place where her books, YouTube channel, newsletter, speaking calendar, and Jesus Collective resources connect; (5) No preaching workshop or homiletics course despite the Buechner Award credential.

- **Series potential**: (1) "Reading Jesus" — the YouTube Luke series extended to other Gospels, with companion reading guides linking to *The Bible Unwrapped*; (2) "The Jesus Lens: Reading Scripture Through Christ" — a structured course drawn from *The Bible Unwrapped* for study groups, seminary courses, or individual learners; (3) "The Better Christian Story" — *Divine Gravity*'s eight theses as eight standalone modules or discussion sessions; (4) "Cross-Shaped Power" — a series on kenotic theology and Christian ethics for leaders; (5) "Preaching with the Grain of the Gospel" — a workshop-style series on narrative homiletics in the Buechner tradition.
