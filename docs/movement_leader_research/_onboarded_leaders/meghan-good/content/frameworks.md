# Frameworks: Meghan Larissa Good

**Status**: Complete (grounded in published corpus)
**Last updated**: 2026-06-05
**Confidence**: HIGH on framework existence and naming; framework dates anchored to the source book's publication year.

Good's frameworks form one coherent argument: God looks like Jesus, so all of Scripture and the whole life of faith is read *through the lens of Jesus*, and the church recovered on that center is held together not by boundaries but by a shared gravitational pull toward Christ — advanced through cross-shaped rather than coercive power.

---

### The Jesus Lens (Christocentric Hermeneutic)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2018 (*The Bible Unwrapped*; DMin dissertation) |
| Components | (1) Jesus as the fullness of God (Col. 1:19, Heb. 1:1-3, John 1:1); (2) Jesus as Scripture's authoritative interpreter; (3) every text — including OT violence, difficult Pauline and apocalyptic passages — read through and toward its culmination in Christ; (4) Christ as the "canon within the canon" while honoring all of Scripture |
| Visual / model | "Putting on our Jesus glasses" (verbal metaphor) |
| Adoption evidence | *The Bible Unwrapped* (Outreach 2019 Resource of the Year; ~150 Goodreads ratings); informed the Anabaptist Community Bible project (2022 keynotes); "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel (54+ Luke devotionals, 2026) |
| Source | [heraldpress.com](https://heraldpress.com/9781513802343/the-bible-unwrapped/); [anabaptistworld.org](https://anabaptistworld.org/planning-an-anabaptist-bible/) |

The Jesus Lens reads all of Scripture through Jesus as the supreme interpretive authority: Jesus is "not the softer side of God that gets averaged" with divine judgment, but the fullness of God against which every text is evaluated.

### Dialogic Biblical Authority

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2018 (DMin dissertation *Between Breaths*; *The Bible Unwrapped*) |
| Components | Authority emerges from dynamic conversation among five dialogue partners — Scripture, tradition, Spirit, reason, and community/experience — rather than from a single static source (*sola scriptura* alone) |
| Visual / model | "Dialogical paradigm" (described, not diagrammed) |
| Adoption evidence | Dissertation publicly available in George Fox Digital Commons; underlies *The Bible Unwrapped*; informs the AMBS Teaching Leadership competency she oversees |
| Source | [digitalcommons.georgefox.edu/dmin/272](https://digitalcommons.georgefox.edu/dmin/272/) |

Dialogic authority argues that Christian authority "cannot be possessed as a static and absolute quality of any single entity" — its shape is "fundamentally relational" and dialogic, positioning her against both fundamentalist biblicism and liberal dismissal.

### Divine Gravity / Centered-Set Ecclesiology

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*Divine Gravity*) |
| Components | Jesus as the gravitational center holding together diverse, naturally repelling people; belonging defined by trajectory of motion toward Jesus rather than by boundary position; centered-set over bounded-set church (drawing on Mark D. Baker's application of Paul Hiebert's missiological model) |
| Visual / model | Gravity metaphor — objects drawn toward a common center |
| Adoption evidence | *Divine Gravity* (4.36–4.62 Goodreads; endorsed by Mouw, McLaren, Gushee, Boyd); featured on Jesus Collective's "Jesus Centered Foundations" resource page; MennoCon21 address |
| Source | [heraldpress.com](https://heraldpress.com/9781513813127/divine-gravity/) |

Divine Gravity defines Christian community not by doctrinal fences (who is in or out) but by orbital direction toward Jesus: "Individuals and groups who would naturally repel each other will be held together when they find a center of gravity in something bigger and better."

### The Better Christian Story (Eight Theses)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*Divine Gravity*) |
| Components | (1) Jesus as authoritative interpretive lens for God and Scripture; (2) salvation as setting all things right on earth; (3) discipleship as acknowledging and following Jesus; (4) Christian community as both means and goal of God's transformation; (5) God's people as reconciliation ambassadors; (6) the Spirit guiding and resourcing mission; (7) sacrificial love overcoming evil; (8) church unity centering on Jesus as gravitational focal point |
| Visual / model | — (organized in three parts: a dilemma, a rediscovery, a proposal) |
| Adoption evidence | The structural backbone of *Divine Gravity*; cited in Cramer's *Anabaptist World* review as "a manifesto for reforming" the faith |
| Source | [heraldpress.com](https://heraldpress.com/9781513813127/divine-gravity/); [anabaptistworld.org](https://anabaptistworld.org/starting-point-for-a-new-reformation/) |

The Better Christian Story is eight interlocking convictions that constitute a recovered Christian narrative, framed as the content of a Spirit-led "new reformation."

### Cross-Shaped (Kenotic) Power

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*Divine Gravity*); developed in "Jesus and the Devil's Bargain" (2024) |
| Components | God's kingdom advances through self-sacrifice, vulnerability, and suffering love, not coercive force; Jesus's Matthew 4 temptations were genuine (power for righteous ends was available) and refused; cross-shaped power "appears slower, more painful, seemingly like losing before winning" but alone defeats evil |
| Visual / model | — |
| Adoption evidence | Central to *Divine Gravity*; journal essay "Jesus and the Devil's Bargain" (2024); rooted in Anabaptist peace-church theology |
| Source | [meghanlarissagood.com](https://meghanlarissagood.com/2024/02/03/jesus-and-the-devils-bargain/) |

Cross-shaped power is a theology of power in which coercive force even for righteous ends is a "devil's bargain" rejected by Jesus; nonviolence flows from the very character of God revealed in Christ.
