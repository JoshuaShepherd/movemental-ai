### Score Interpretation

Meghan Larissa Good has a **practitioner-first, digital-second presence** that is coherent but underdeveloped relative to her influence, institutional positioning, and content production volume. Her influence is felt most strongly through:

- **Organizational role**: Jesus Collective (Resource Development Manager + Theology Circle Chair) extends her reach into a cross-denominational network beyond Mennonite contexts
- **Published books**: Two Herald Press titles with strong ratings (4.31 and 4.36 on Goodreads); *The Bible Unwrapped* recognized by Outreach Magazine
- **Teaching/speaking**: Active at AMBS (DMin faculty), Northern Seminary, Jesus Collective gatherings, cross-denominational retreats (Church of the Brethren)
- **"Reading Jesus" channel**: The highest-volume content asset she owns, with 54+ episodes in 2026 — but largely disconnected from her website and social strategy

### Key Gaps for Movemental to Address

1. **"Reading Jesus" YouTube is her biggest disconnected asset**: 54+ video episodes in 2026, but no embed on homepage or Journal page, no cross-posting to Instagram Reels or Shorts, no dedicated "Watch" section on the website. This is her best content and least visible.
2. **All social follower counts are unknown**: Foundational audience data is missing. Needs direct platform access to record Instagram, Twitter/X, Facebook, and YouTube subscriber counts.
3. **No Substack or public newsletter archive**: Her email newsletter is invisible from the outside. No subscriber count, no past-issue archive, no discovery layer. Substack would give her free public discoverability and a network of theological readers.
4. **No LinkedIn presence**: For a DMin faculty member at AMBS and Resource Development Manager at Jesus Collective, LinkedIn is an obvious professional platform gap.
5. **No newsletter CTA in journal posts**: Organic search visitors read a single article and have no clear path to email signup unless they navigate elsewhere.
6. **No Wikipedia article**: She has the credentials (two published books, DMin, Outreach Resource of the Year, 15+ years pastoral ministry, institutional faculty role) but no Wikipedia entry. This is a discoverable gap.
7. **No course platform**: Despite 15+ years of pastoral experience and DMin faculty credentials, no Teachable/Thinkific/self-hosted course exists. She teaches students at AMBS and Northern Seminary but there is no public-facing "course" product.
8. **No Threads/Bluesky/Emerging Platform**: Consistent with peer set in Mennonite/Anabaptist space; less critical than YouTube integration.
9. **Email delivery platform unknown**: Whether she uses Mailchimp, ConvertKit, or another ESP affects how sophisticated the audience funnel is. This is an ops gap, not a visibility gap, but matters for Movemental integration conversations.
