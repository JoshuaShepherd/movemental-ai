### Site Structure

meghanlarissagood.com functions as a personal author-speaker-theologian hub. The homepage leads with the theological tagline, prominently features *Divine Gravity* with a group-purchase Zoom Q&A offer, and presents "Latest stories" from the journal in a grid layout. Two newsletter signup CTAs appear above and below the fold, both offering the free eBook as an incentive — the clearest conversion mechanic on the site.

The six-page navigation (Home / Books / Speaking / Journal / About / Contact) is lean and purposeful. The Books page presents both titles with Amazon purchase links and notable endorsements (Greg Boyd on *The Bible Unwrapped*; Richard J. Mouw on *Divine Gravity*). The Speaking page is the most detailed secondary page, listing topics, booking logistics, upcoming engagements through 2027, and past speaking history. The About page confirms current roles (Jesus Collective Resource Development Manager; AMBS DMin faculty; "Reading Jesus" YouTube host) and educational background without listing any social media links.

The Contact page uses WPForms Lite for a simple name/email/message form, with a second newsletter signup embedded below. No social handles are displayed anywhere in the primary navigation or footers based on pages accessed.

### Jesus Collective Relationship

Meghan Larissa Good chairs the Jesus Collective Theology Circle — a body providing theological direction and resources for the Jesus Collective network and mentoring theological leaders. Her book *Divine Gravity* is featured on the organization's "Jesus Centered Foundations" resources page as an extended written companion to their Five Markers video/discipleship content. After resigning from Trinity Mennonite Church on May 2, 2025, she joined Jesus Collective full-time as **Resource Development Manager**. Jesus Collective is a Canadian-registered charitable organization (CRA: 716049739RR0001) founded in association with Greg Boyd (Woodland Hills Church / ReKnew), oriented toward a "Jesus-Centered, Spirit-Empowered" movement theology.

Source: [Anabaptist World, "Pastor resigns to shift attention to broader network"](https://anabaptistworld.org/pastor-resigns-to-shift-attention-to-broader-network/) | Confidence: High
