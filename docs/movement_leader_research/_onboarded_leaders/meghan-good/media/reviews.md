### Reception Summary — *The Bible Unwrapped*

Readers consistently praise two qualities: **accessibility** (non-specialist language that does not sacrifice scholarship) and **practical utility** (useful for church study groups, people deconstructing faith, and students alike). The Outreach Magazine award signals acceptance by evangelical gatekeepers across denominational lines. The Goodreads rating of 4.31 across 150 readers is strong for a niche Christian title. No critical or negative published reviews have been found. The main implicit critique in endorser language is that it is intentionally introductory rather than academic — a feature, not a bug, given the book's stated audience.

### Reception Summary — *Divine Gravity*

*Divine Gravity* has a smaller but highly enthusiastic readership. The single formal review (Cramer in *Anabaptist World*) is intellectually engaged and offers the book's most substantive published critique: it is most useful for those deconstructing faith rather than for committed Scripture-readers, and lacks a bibliography for deeper engagement. Goodreads reviewers use language like "prophetic," "gold," and "reformational" — suggesting strong resonance with its target audience of pastors and leaders seeking a compelling Jesus-centered narrative. The weight of endorsers (Mouw, McLaren, Gushee, Boyd) is exceptional for a first-time book from a Mennonite trade press.
