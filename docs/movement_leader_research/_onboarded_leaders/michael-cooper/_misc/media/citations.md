## Book-level citations

No systematic full-text citation search was possible. **"Ephesiology" as a coined term is a category of one** — Cooper invented it and uniquely occupies the Ephesus/Acts 19 movement-theology niche — which means term-level diffusion is easy to track but currently small: the word travels almost exclusively within his own ecosystem (podcast, Master Classes, Mission India MA) and the Farah/Vaughn engagements, rather than appearing in others' book titles or indexes.

## Citation pattern

- **Engaged more than indexed.** Like other practitioner-scholars, Cooper is *engaged in networks and reviews* more visibly than he is *quantifiably cited* — but unlike pure practitioners he has a genuine peer-reviewed publishing record that supports real (if unmeasured) academic citation.
- **A coined term that hasn't yet escaped its origin.** "Ephesiology" is distinctive and ownable, but it has not yet entered general missiological vocabulary the way "CPM" (Garrison) or "APEST" (Hirsch) have. It is pre-diffusion.
- **Lineage-by-endorsement.** The repeated Roland Allen comparison from credentialed endorsers functions as a positioning citation, locating him in the *Missionary Methods: St. Paul's or Ours?* tradition.

## Implication

The single highest-leverage citation move is **establishing a verified scholar profile** (Google Scholar + ORCID) to make the existing journal record discoverable and citable, and **driving "Ephesiology" as a term into others' work** through the Motus Dei / EMS / Missio Nexus channels where the appetite already exists. The intellectual capital is real; the measurement infrastructure and term-diffusion are the gaps.
