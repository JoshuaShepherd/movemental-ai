## Assessment

- **His teaching cadence is institutional and digital, not conference-stage.** Cooper's "events" are overwhelmingly *recurring teaching posts* (East-West, Mission India, Kairos, Torch Trinity, AGST Nepal) plus his *own owned surfaces* (Master Classes, Areopagus Symposium, the 227-episode podcast) — not a circuit of named conference keynotes. He is a professor-podcaster more than a conference speaker.
- **The podcast is the flywheel.** With 227 weekly episodes since 2019 and a co-host (Andrew Johnson), the podcast is the most consistent, highest-volume public-teaching artifact he has — a far richer corpus than his thin conference record.
- **Academic-venue range is unusually broad but undated.** LSE, Bordeaux, Loyola (Jesuit), Baylor, Utah, Craiova span secular, Catholic, and evangelical institutions across three continents — a credibility signal, but not a live calendar Movemental can plug into.
- **Reach is niche but vertically deep.** Small public numbers, but each surface ladders toward accredited credentials — engagement runs deep (degree-seeking cohorts) rather than wide.
