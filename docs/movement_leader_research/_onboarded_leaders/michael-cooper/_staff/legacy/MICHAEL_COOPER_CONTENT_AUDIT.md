## Executive Summary

This audit inventories Michael T. Cooper's content across all formats and channels. The headline finding inverts the usual pattern: Cooper is **content-rich and structurally dispersed**, not content-poor. He has ~17 books across at least six imprints, a 227-episode hosted podcast, an own accredited LMS with 8+ courses, a directed seminary MA, two owned blogs, and a real journal-article history — yet the corpus is scattered across six publishers, three brand identities (Ephesiology / TELOS. / personal byline), and several platforms, sitting atop a paper-thin owned-audience layer (~120–175 social followers, 12 podcast ratings, ~40 Goodreads ratings). The editorial issue is **discoverability and consolidation, not production or voice quality.** The overall digital presence score is **3.1 / 5** (strong content + commerce + owned hub; weak audience capture).

**Key Outcomes:**
- Complete content inventory by format and topic
- Content performance analysis and opportunities
- Content gap identification (dispersion-centric)
- Repurposing roadmap
- Content architecture and SEO/GEO recommendations (own the "Ephesiology" category)

## Content Gap Analysis

This is the inverse of a thin-surface leader. The gaps are about **concentration and audience**, not content volume.

### High-Priority Gaps
1. **No unified, discoverable corpus hub.** A reader who finds *Ephesiology* on Amazon has no path to *Make Disciples*, *Letters to Churches*, or the 2026 volume. Single largest value-destruction in the dossier.
2. **No owned audience layer.** Signup form only; no Substack; no list of scale; ~120–175 brand-social followers.
3. **Reader-footprint under-capture.** ~40 Goodreads ratings for a bestseller + award winner; 0 Goodreads followers.
4. **Discoverability / SEO.** No Wikipedia article; no verified Scholar profile; misspelled Patheos slug; corpus not aggregated.

### Medium-Priority Gaps
5. **Imprint / brand consolidation.** Six imprints + three brand identities (Ephesiology / TELOS. / personal byline) with no single masthead.
6. **Format gaps.** No *Ephesiology* audiobook; no translations/oral modules for the Global South audience.
7. **Course on-ramp.** No free/low-cost top-of-funnel course routing podcast listeners and readers into the paid Master Classes pipeline; Master Classes pricing/value-prop surface is gated.

### SEO / GEO Optimization — Own the "Ephesiology" Category

The single largest SEO/GEO opportunity in the dossier: **"Ephesiology" is a category of one** that Cooper coined and uniquely occupies. The work is to make the canonical surface unambiguous.

- **Establish the canonical entity** — one authoritative hub URL; pursue a Wikipedia article and a verified Google Scholar/ORCID profile so generative-answer engines resolve "Ephesiology" and "missiological theology of the Ephesian movement" to Cooper.
- **Resolve brand drift** — redirect the misspelled Patheos slug (`ephesilogy`) and the `untiltheyallhearblog` handle toward one public-facing brand; reconcile Ephesiology / TELOS. / personal byline.
- **Framework pages** — canonical, structured pages for Ephesiology, missiological theology, disciple-making-over-church-planting, healthy DNA, and Christoform leadership (each cross-linking the pillar book + podcast series + essays), so the framework set is machine-extractable.
- **Aggregate the corpus** for discoverability — the six imprints become source links from one indexed hub; consolidate Goodreads/Amazon/Smashwords references.
- **Capture the audience** — a real newsletter is the missing layer that explains why ~17 books draw only ~40 Goodreads ratings.
