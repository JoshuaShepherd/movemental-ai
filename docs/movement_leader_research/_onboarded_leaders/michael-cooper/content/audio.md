## Assessment

Cooper's audio profile is the **strongest and most consolidated dimension of his digital presence** — and it is the exact opposite of a guest-only profile.

- **He owns a 227-episode, 7-year, weekly hosted show.** That is a mature, sustained audio asset most movement leaders never build.
- **The owned-audience layer is thin.** 4.8/5 on only 12 Apple ratings means the show is good but under-discovered — the same dispersion/thin-capture pattern as the books and blogs.
- **The guest circuit is under-documented.** Unlike a leader whose strength is appearing on others' shows, Cooper's borrowed-audience footprint is sparse and largely UNKNOWN — his energy goes into his own feed, not into cross-pollinating onto larger shows.
- **No audio archive off-platform.** The 227 episodes live on Apple/Spotify/YouTube feeds; there is no canonical episode index on his own domain that a researcher (or new listener) can navigate.

The fragmentation story for audio is **distribution and discovery**, not production: the content exists in volume; the audience-capture and cross-promotion layers are missing.
