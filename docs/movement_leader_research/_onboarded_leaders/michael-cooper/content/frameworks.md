# Frameworks: Michael T. Cooper

**Status**: Complete (grounded in published corpus)
**Last updated**: 2026-06-02
**Confidence**: HIGH on framework existence and naming; framework dates anchored to the source book's publication year.

Cooper's frameworks form one coherent argument: mission is the center of theology, the measure of mission is multiplying disciples who identify with the life of Christ, and the model for both is the first-century Ephesian movement read with a scholar's questions and a planter's stake.

---

### Ephesiology

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Ephesiology: A Study of the Ephesian Movement*) |
| Components | A discrete discipline ("the study of the Ephesian movement"); the first-century Ephesian church-planting movement treated as a normative model; explicitly *not* a commentary on Ephesians and *not* a biblical theology of missions |
| Source | [*Ephesiology* (William Carey, 2020)](https://missionbooks.org/products/ephesiology) |

Ephesiology is Cooper's signature move: he names a category of one — the study of the Ephesian movement as a discrete field — and treats it as a normative model for catalyzing contemporary movements.

---

### Missiological Theology

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Ephesiology*) |
| Components | Mission as the *center* of theology rather than a subset; the Bible read as "a missiological theology"; Paul and John read as missiological theologians who connected Jesus to the cultural narrative of Ephesus |
| Source | [*Ephesiology* (2020)](https://www.everand.com/book/547780170/Ephesiology-A-Study-of-the-Ephesian-Movement) |

Missiological theology inverts the usual order — instead of filing "missions" as one applied chapter downstream of systematic theology, Cooper reads the whole Bible as a sustained account of God's movement to the nations.

---

### Disciple-Making Over Church-Planting

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Ephesiology*); expanded 2023 (*Make Disciples*) |
| Components | "Making disciples is the mandate while church planting is a result"; church planting is nowhere commanded in Scripture; "disciples multiply while volunteers keep busy" |
| Source | [Patheos / Jackson Wu (2020)](https://www.patheos.com/blogs/jacksonwu/2020/10/28/is-church-planting-a-biblical-mandate/); [*Make Disciples* (2023)](https://www.amazon.com/Make-Disciples-Spiritual-Christ-Follower-Ephesiology/dp/B0CPNTVDXB) |

Cooper's most-cited corrective recenters the movement conversation on disciple-multiplication: the mandate is to make disciples, and churches are the result that follows, never the goal that drives.

---

### The Five Analytical Questions

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Ephesiology*) |
| Components | (1) How did the movement start? (2) What did its adherents believe? (3) Who led it? (4) How did it multiply and sustain growth? (5) Why did some defect into false teaching? |
| Source | [*Ephesiology* (2020)](https://missionbooks.org/products/ephesiology) |

The Five Analytical Questions are the diagnostic lens Ephesiology applies to any movement — the same questions a new-religious-movements ethnographer asks of a living movement, turned on the first-century Ephesian church.

---

### Healthy DNA

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Ephesiology*) |
| Components | Replicable spiritual "DNA" that sustains or fractures churches, denominations, and ministries; multiplication-over-addition; DNA degradation as the systems-health failure mode (the fifth analytical question) |
| Source | [*Ephesiology* (2020)](https://missionbooks.org/products/ephesiology) |

Healthy DNA frames movement health as a property of replicable spiritual code — movements multiply when the DNA is sound and fracture into false teaching when it degrades.

---

### Christoform Leadership

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2025 (*Letters to Churches: Christoform Leadership Lessons*) |
| Components | Christ-shaped, communal, theological, and missional leadership recovered from the Apostolic Fathers (1 Clement, Ignatius of Antioch, Polycarp); set against hierarchical and novelty-driven leadership |
| Source | [*Letters to Churches* (2025)](https://www.amazon.com/Letters-Churches-Christoform-Leadership-Disciples/dp/B0G3HRPWXT) |

Christoform leadership retrieves a Christ-shaped pattern of leading from the disciples of the apostles, offered as a corrective to the celebrity and innovation models of contemporary leadership culture.

---

### Lead Like Paul — The Five C's

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2017 (*Lead Like Paul*) |
| Components | Five empowering leadership traits drawn from the Apostle Paul: Call, Character, Charismata, Confidence, Commitment |
| Source | [*Lead Like Paul* (2017)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/38191926-lead-like-paul) |

The Five C's distill the Apostle Paul's empowering, world-upending leadership into five reproducible traits — Cooper's earliest, most compact leadership framework, later deepened by the patristic Christoform material.

---

### TELOS. — The Three Pillars

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | (TELOS. / Ephesiology ministry) |
| Components | Three ministry pillars: plant churches, care for the marginalized, defend the faith — the operational expression of the Ephesiology vision |
| Source | [ephesiology.com](https://ephesiology.com/) |

The TELOS. three pillars translate Cooper's missiological theology into ministry practice — planting churches, caring for the marginalized, and defending the faith as the lived shape of the Ephesian movement today.
