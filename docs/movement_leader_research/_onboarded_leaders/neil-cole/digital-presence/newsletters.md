## Volumetric Estimate

If we use an order-of-magnitude estimate from the Cole-Slaw blog activity (250 posts over ten years, with the post-2014 cadence drop) and the modest Starling Initiatives community footprint, the total subscriber-list reach Cole personally controls in 2026 is **almost certainly under 5,000** — probably substantially under. This is the single largest under-leveraged asset in the dossier:

- Cole has **20+ years of working relationships** with Greenhouse training alumni (Frisco, Virginia Mennonite, Long Beach cohorts, etc.).
- He has **17+ books' worth of buyers** scattered across Amazon, Barnes & Noble, Christianbook, Audible, and library systems.
- He has **direct access** to the 100 Movements / Movement Leaders Collective audience as a co-founder.
- He has the **post-silence interview audience** (KCU, *Shifting Culture*, *reChurch*) — a few thousand additional listeners per appearance.

A consolidated Cole newsletter (Movemental-hosted or Starling-hosted) could plausibly seed at 3,000–8,000 subscribers within 90 days of launch. **It does not currently exist in any visible form.**

## Implications for Movemental

1. **A newsletter is the single highest-leverage move Movemental could make on Cole's behalf.** A weekly or monthly cadence is not necessary; a quarterly cadence ("From Neil's desk") with a single story, a single short framework recap, and a single recommended LTG/Greenhouse practice would be more than sufficient.
2. **The launch lead-magnet is obvious: the silence story.** A polished essay or short PDF — *"13 Years of Silence: What I Heard When I Stopped Speaking"* — would be a near-guaranteed signup driver, and would also serve as the doorway essay that introduces him to the post-2015 generation of practitioners (Wegner, Pulley, Brian Johnson, the post-Sanders Underground / Tampa cohort) who built on top of his work without ever working with him directly.
3. **A "best of Cole-Slaw" archive newsletter** (1 post per week from the legacy blog, curated and lightly re-edited with a 2026 framing introduction) would convert the dormant 250-post archive into a 250-week / ~5-year automated content engine.
