## Platform Architecture Diagnosis

Cole's platform architecture is the **mirror image of a contemporary "build the platform" author's**. He has:

- **Many small surfaces, no central one.** 17+ books across 5 publishers; 250+ blog posts on a dormant blog; multiple YouTube channels; conference talks scattered across Verge, Exponential, KCU, DTS; podcast appearances on 8+ different shows. There is no `neilcole.com` that ties any of this together.
- **Owned-domain leakage.** The most-cited domain (`cmaresources.org`) is not under his control anymore. The personal blog (`cole-slaw.blogspot.com`) is on Google's free infrastructure and dormant. Starling Initiatives is small and quiet by design.
- **Borrowed-platform dependence for retail and audio.** Books live on Amazon / Goodreads / publisher sites; audiobooks live on Audible. None of these are surfaces Cole shapes.
- **No central email list.** Removing the highest-leverage direct-relationship asset.
- **Episodic re-emergences** (KCU 2025, *reChurch* 2024–2025, *Shifting Culture* 2024, DTS Lectureship 2022) that succeed *despite* the absence of a feeder platform — because the strength of the underlying work and the network density of the host shows do the routing.

This pattern is consistent with a leader whose career arc was front-loaded into the trade-publishing era (2005–2014) and whose late career has deliberately rejected platform-building for theological reasons (the silence period). It produces **maximum fragmentation per unit of intellectual depth** — exactly the case Movemental's ecosystem layer is designed to serve.

## Where Cole's Platform Most Dramatically Falls Short of His Influence

1. **No author site under his own name.** Hirsch has alanhirsch.org. Frost has michaelfrost.net. Brisco has bradbrisco.com. Cole has nothing comparable.
2. **The CMA archive is bleeding.** Every CMA Resources URL cited in the 2005–2018 missional literature now either 404s or routes to a gambling site. This is reputational decay that compounds with time.
3. **The Cole-Slaw archive has no SEO upkeep.** Blog posts are findable only via direct search; Google has nothing recent to index.
4. **No central catalog of his books.** The Goodreads page is the closest thing, and even Goodreads under-counts (60 distinct works listed; only ~17 are major books).
5. **No way for a stranger to subscribe to anything.**

These are precisely the things a Movemental-hosted Cole Hub solves.
