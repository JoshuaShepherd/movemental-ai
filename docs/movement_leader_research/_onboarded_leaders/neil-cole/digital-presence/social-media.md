## Cross-Platform Analysis

### What we can say with confidence

- Cole's **historical** social presence (2007–~2014) was non-trivial: a 250-post blog, a Twitter handle still showing 10K+ posts, a Facebook page with several thousand likes, and an active CMA Resources YouTube embed pattern.
- Cole's **current** social presence is **deliberately small and quiet.** Starling Initiatives is the operational hub but its public-facing footprint is intentionally modest — a private community group and a Zoom-training cadence rather than a follower-and-engagement game.
- The **CMA Resources** social handles still exist but appear effectively dormant. They map to a now-lapsed domain.

### What we can't say without login

- Current follower counts on Twitter, Facebook (personal), LinkedIn, and YouTube.
- Most-recent-post dates on Twitter, Facebook personal, and LinkedIn.
- Engagement rates on any platform.
- Whether the silence period (~2012–2024) included or excluded social media. Cole has explicitly said it included social media; whether he has resumed posting since the 2024 *Shifting Culture* re-emergence is UNKNOWN.

### Theology-of-obscurity caveat

Cole's own framing on KCU (Aug 2025) — *"God told me to stop speaking publicly in the U.S., refrain from social media, and cease blogging"* — is one of the strongest pieces of evidence that the social-media silence has been deliberate and coherent with his theology, not accidental. Movemental copy that benchmarks Cole against "leaders should have 50K Twitter followers" is mis-fitting the subject. **Treat the small, quiet social footprint as a feature, not a bug.**

## Implications for Movemental

1. **Don't chase a handle takeover or growth play.** The lane that fits is "trusted-voice elder, episodic feature," not "build the brand."
2. **The 2024–2025 podcast cluster (Shifting Culture / reChurch / KC Underground) is the right kind of surface for Cole's voice now.** Movemental's own podcast / video / column program should pattern after those — long-form, listening-shaped, host-led.
3. **The most valuable single asset Movemental could host on his behalf is a curated archive of his canonical content** (Cole-Slaw blog selections; CMA Resources rescue; book excerpts; the silence-story interviews). Not a new social campaign.
4. **A respectful "where to find Neil Cole" page** is high-value low-effort. There currently is no single URL on the internet that lists his Twitter, his Starling Initiatives YouTube, his Cole-Slaw archive, his Goodreads, his Audible, his books-by-publisher, and his current podcast appearances.
