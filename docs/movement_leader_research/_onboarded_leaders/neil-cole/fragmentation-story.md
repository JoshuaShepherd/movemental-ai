## The Story

Neil Cole spent the late 1990s pastoring a Grace Brethren church in Alta Loma, California; in 1999 he walked away from that pastorate, walked into the smoking section of a Long Beach coffeehouse called The Coffee Tavern, and started **Awakening Chapel**. Out of that first congregation he built two operational technologies — the **Life Transformation Group (LTG)**: 2 or 3 people, weekly, Scripture and accountability and prayer for non-Christians; and the **Organic Church Planters' Greenhouse**, a two-stage cohort training. He wrapped them inside an organization called **Church Multiplication Associates (CMA)** and spent the next nineteen years sending the LTG card to every church planter who would take one. By the organization's own count it had helped start "thousands of churches in 50 states and 50+ nations." The IMB adapted his LTG into a tool called CHAT. Curtis Sergeant carried the framework into Saddleback's PEACE Plan. Steve Addison cited him as a foundational practitioner. Alan Hirsch wrote "Neil Cole is one of the foremost thinkers and practitioners [of] organic forms of church" on the back cover of *Organic Church*, and a year later Cole returned the favor on the back cover of *The Forgotten Ways*.

Between 2005 and 2015 he published **twelve trade-house books** across **five publishers** — Jossey-Bass, Baker, Tyndale Momentum, Thomas Nelson, and ChurchSmart. *Organic Church* (2005, foreword by Leonard Sweet); *Search & Rescue* (2008); *Organic Leadership* (2009); *Church 3.0* (2010, foreword by Francis Chan); *Journeys to Significance* (2011); *Ordinary Hero* (2011); *Church Transfusion* with Phil Helfer (2012); *Primal Fire* (Tyndale, 2014, with four co-thinkers); *One Thing* (Thomas Nelson, 2015). He spoke at Verge and Exponential and the Simple Church Conference. He ran Greenhouse cohorts in Frisco TX, Virginia Mennonite Conference, and across Southern California. He maintained the **Cole-Slaw blog** ("Cole — raw, shredded, mixed-up, and served cold") and produced ~250 posts. In November 2015 he co-founded **100 Movements** with Alan Hirsch, Dave Rhodes, Will Mancini, and Jessie Cruickshank. That is the part of the story that is searchable.

The rest is not. There is no `neilcole.com`. The `cmaresources.org` domain — for two decades the central content hub — has lapsed and now serves a Sydney lottery-prediction page. The Cole-Slaw blog goes dormant after a single post on June 7, 2017. His big-house publishing run ends with *One Thing* in November 2015 and there are no Jossey-Bass or Baker or Tyndale or Thomas Nelson titles between 2015 and 2026. The reason, as Cole has explained on three different podcasts in 2024 and 2025: God told him to stop speaking publicly in the U.S., to refrain from social media, and to cease blogging. He calls it his **13-year season of silence**. *"I never felt like Jesus left me. Everyone else did — but He never did."*

What he did publish in that silence window — *Rising Tides* (2018), *Pray* (2019/2022), *VIRAL* (2021), *Journeys* (2024) — appears under his own self-publishing imprint, **Starling Initiatives**, on Amazon. He narrates the audiobooks himself. He teaches at Kairos Pacific University as Professor of Church Development. He delivered three chapel sessions at Dallas Theological Seminary's 2022 Missions and Evangelism Lectureship. In April 2024 he returned to *Shifting Culture*; in December 2024 and January 2025 he sat down with the *reChurch Podcast*; in August 2025 he sat down with Brian Johnson and Cory at **KC Underground's *Upside Down Kingdom*** podcast — Rob Wegner's organization receiving him as the elder voice of the framework Wegner inherited.

Neil Cole's fragmentation is not the fragmentation of a 30-book author whose work is scattered across 7 publishers — though the publisher dispersion is real (5 publishers in 10 years). It is the fragmentation of **a movement architect whose own primary domain has died, whose 250-post blog is frozen, whose CMA Resources canon is now Wayback-Machine-only, whose 17+ books have no central catalog page, whose social handles have unknown activity status, whose new home (Starling Initiatives) is a small private community group, and whose late-career theology of obscurity has structurally prevented him from solving any of this himself.** The corpus is large; the surface is small; the bridge is broken.

This is precisely what Movemental's ecosystem layer is built to repair.

The tertiary trapped pool is **the Greenhouse / LTG alumni network** — practitioners across 50+ countries whose ministries were shaped by Cole's frameworks, whose names are in his email and his memory, and who have no shared post-CMA home.
