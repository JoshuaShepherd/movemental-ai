# Theology: Neil Cole

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-04-29
**Confidence**: HIGH on framework structure (the public corpus is large and consistent); MEDIUM on Cole's positions on contested doctrinal questions outside the ecclesiology lane; LOW on systematic theology proper (Cole rarely operates in dogmatic categories).

---

## Christological Center

Cole's theological frame is **radically Christocentric** in a way that is closer to the Anabaptist / Mennonite stream than to the Reformed-confessional stream of his Grace Brethren origin. His most-repeated definition of the church names this directly:

> "Church is not an event, a place or an organization; it is a family on mission together."
> "The presence of Jesus among His people, called out as a spiritual family to pursue His mission on this planet."
> ([Frank Viola joint interview, 2010](https://www.frankviola.org/2010/09/07/missional-organic-church-an-interview-with-neil-cole-frank-viola/))

He distills the entire ministry program of his organization down to one sentence: **"Listen to Jesus and do what He says."** This is the foundational rule of Starling Initiatives ([starlinginitiatives.com](https://starlinginitiatives.com/)) and is repeated nearly verbatim across the Cole-Slaw blog and his recent podcast interviews.

## The DNA Framework — D, N, A

Cole's most cited contribution to missional vocabulary is the **DNA of the Body of Christ**:

- **D**ivine Truth (faith) — Scripture-based revelation, learned through participatory Bible engagement. He was an early adopter of what is now widely called Discovery Bible Study.
- **N**urturing Relationships (love) — authentic family-level community and accountability.
- **A**postolic Mission (hope) — sent-ness; outward orientation; multiplication.

Cole insists that all three must be present in every cell of the body: *"all components of the DNA must be in every part of the church from the smallest unit of disciple in relation to another disciple… the components should not be supplanted, supplemented or separated"* (paraphrased from *Organic Church*). This is structurally parallel to Alan Hirsch's **mDNA** framework in *The Forgotten Ways* — and the parallel is mutually acknowledged in their endorsements of each other's books.

## Multiplication-First Ecclesiology

The single most-quoted Cole sentence in the missional canon:

> "If we cannot multiply disciples, we will never multiply leaders. If we cannot multiply leaders, we will never multiply churches. If we cannot multiply churches, we will never see a movement of God." (*Organic Church*, 2005, repeated in *Search & Rescue*, 2008, and *Cultivating a Life for God*, 1999)

Three theological commitments are embedded in that sequence:

1. **Disciples are the irreducible unit.** Programmatic church-growth language ("more attenders," "more giving units") is not the right level of analysis. Without multiplied disciples, every other layer is hollow.
2. **Leadership emerges, it is not appointed from outside.** *Organic Leadership* (2009) is a sustained argument that Christian leadership is best understood as a function that emerges naturally inside the body, not a credential certified by an institution. ("Leadership that rests on title is weak. Leadership that rests in a cause and inspires others to follow is strong." — *Organic Church*.)
3. **The lowest reproducible unit must already contain the whole DNA.** This is why the Life Transformation Group is two or three people: the smallest viable cell that contains all of D, N, and A.

This makes Cole's ecclesiology **actively low-bar**. *Church 3.0* (2010) is upfront about this:

> "I have intentionally lowered the bar of how church is done so that anyone can do it, and raised the bar of what it means to be a disciple so that not just anyone will do it." (paraphrased; cited in 9Marks review)

## APEST / Five-Fold Ministry

*Primal Fire: Reigniting the Church with the Five Gifts of Jesus* (Tyndale Momentum, 2014) is Cole's APEST book — the apostle/prophet/evangelist/shepherd/teacher framework derived from Ephesians 4:11. The book's structural conceit is that the five gifts of Jesus are explicitly modeled by Cole and his four co-thinkers:

- **Apostle** — Neil Cole
- **Prophet** — Dezi Baker
- **Evangelist** — Ed Waken
- **Shepherd** — Phil Helfer
- **Teacher** — Paul Kaak

This is structurally parallel to (and slightly distinct from) Alan Hirsch's APEST treatment in *The Permanent Revolution* and *5Q*. Cole and Hirsch's APEST treatments are mutually compatible, which is part of why 100 Movements (2015) was able to draw on both authors' frameworks without conflict.

Cole's posture on APEST distinctives:

- He affirms all five as ongoing offices/functions in the contemporary church (against cessationist readings).
- He treats them as **gifts of Jesus, not gifts of the Spirit** — they remain Christ's gifts to the body (Eph 4:11), not interchangeable with the Romans 12 / 1 Corinthians 12 spiritual gifts inventories.
- He frames them as **decentralizing** — they are gifts, not titles, and they emerge across the body rather than concentrating in a senior pastor.

## The Black Swan Effect — Gender Hierarchy

Cole is a **named contributor** to *The Black Swan Effect: A Response to Gender Hierarchy in the Church* (Felicity Dale, ed., 2014), alongside Hirsch, Frost, Ferguson, McClung, and the Dales. The book argues, from inside the missional / simple-church / organic-church stream, **for women's full participation and leadership** in the body of Christ — including in apostolic, leadership, and teaching capacities. Cole's contribution to that volume is consistent with his broader anti-hierarchical posture in *Organic Leadership*.

## Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) and Church Planting Movement (CPM) Compatibility

Cole's LTG and Greenhouse training pre-date the contemporary DMM / CPM vocabulary by nearly a decade. He is consistently cited as a **bridge** between the simple/organic-church stream and the DMM / CPM stream:

- The IMB (International Mission Board) adapted LTG into their **CHAT** discipleship tool.
- **Curtis Sergeant** ("The Only One"; former IMB strategist) treats Cole's work as foundational.
- **Steve Addison** (*Pioneering Movements*, *The Rise and Fall of Movements*) consistently includes Cole in the lineage.
- **Jim Montgomery's DAWN movement** — Michael Steele, the North American director of DAWN, recommended *Organic Church*.

Cole's compatibility with the DMM stream is not a footnote — it is the reason his work has had as much global reach as it has, especially through the IMB and Saddleback's PEACE Plan ecosystem (Curtis Sergeant was Saddleback's director of church planting).

## Theology of Obscurity

This is the distinctive late-career addition. In *Shifting Culture* Ep. 178 (2024) and KC Underground's *Upside Down Kingdom* (2025), Cole describes a 13-year season of imposed silence — no public speaking in the U.S., no blogging, no social media. The theological grammar he uses for that season:

> *"You'd be amazed what people will do for Jesus that they won't do for your mission statement."* (*Upside Down Kingdom*, KCU, Aug 2025)
>
> *"I never felt like Jesus left me. Everyone else did — but He never did."* (same episode)
>
> The cumulative argument of his late writings (*Pray*, *VIRAL*, *Journeys*) is that **intimacy with Christ produces fruit that platform-building cannot** — and that obscurity is a positive spiritual category, not a failure mode.

This sits in a recognizable theological lineage — Henri Nouwen, Eugene Peterson, the desert tradition — but Cole arrives at it from the inside of the high-output church-multiplication and platform-author world, which makes the witness particularly weighty. It is also the reason the Movemental fit must not be framed as "build his platform back."

## Sacraments, Polity, and Doctrinal Commitments

This is where Cole is **least developed** as a public theologian, and where his Reformed critics (notably **9Marks**: Mark Dever / Jonathan Leeman) push hardest:

- **Polity / governance**: Cole's ecclesiology is loose on formal eldership, ordination, and church discipline. *Organic Church* dedicates a full chapter to leadership without engaging 1 Timothy 3 or Titus 1 substantively (per the 9Marks review).
- **Sacraments**: he affirms the practice of communion and baptism, but treats them as embedded in the life of the LTG and the household-church gathering rather than as sacraments administered by ordained ministers. The Reformed critique is that this risks a sacramental thinness.
- **Doctrinal definition of the church**: his "presence of Jesus among His people" definition is Christocentric and warm, but does not include the classical Protestant marks (right preaching of the gospel, right administration of the sacraments, church discipline). This is a deliberate prioritization — not an oversight — but it is what the 9Marks reviews flag.

Cole's response posture to this critique is consistently **patient and irenic**, not polemical. He has not, to public-record knowledge, written a sustained reply to 9Marks; instead, his approach has been to keep doing the work and let the body of practice speak.

## Distinctive Theological Vocabulary

A non-exhaustive glossary that recurs across Cole's corpus:

- **Organic** — life-shaped, not factory-shaped; growing from a seed rather than being assembled from a kit.
- **DNA** — the irreducible D/N/A package present in every cell.
- **LTG / Life Transformation Group** — the 2-or-3-person reproducing discipleship cell.
- **Greenhouse** — the training environment that prepares organic church planters (Story 1 = "Foundations for Organic Church Planting"; Story 2 = field application).
- **Movement** — the network-of-networks effect when disciples, leaders, and churches all multiply.
- **Apostolic / Apostolic Mission** — sent-ness; the "A" in the DNA framework; also the apostolic gift in *Primal Fire*.
- **Cultivating** — the dominant agricultural metaphor; *Cultivating a Life for God* — discipleship as farming, not factory.
- **Family on mission** — the relational category Cole uses for the church.
- **Listen to Jesus and do what He says** — the irreducible operating principle of Starling Initiatives.
- **Church 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0** — Cole's eras: 1.0 = pre-Constantinian organic church; 2.0 = post-Constantinian institutional church; 3.0 = the contemporary recovery of organic patterns.
- **From institution to organic** — the transition movement; *Church Transfusion* is the practical guide.

## Where Cole Differs from Adjacent Voices

| Voice | Point of agreement | Point of difference |
|-------|--------------------|---------------------|
| **Alan Hirsch** | Fully aligned on mDNA / DNA, APEST, missional-incarnational, decentralization | Hirsch is more explicitly system-theory/missiological; Cole is more practitioner/coffee-shop-grade |
| **Frank Viola** | Christocentric, organic, anti-institutional | Viola = mature-disciples-first, slow multiplication; Cole = rapid multiplication. Public friction in 2010 |
| **Felicity & Tony Dale (House2House)** | Simple/house church, women's leadership, decentralized | Dales emphasize the local home as the irreducible unit; Cole emphasizes the LTG / 2-or-3 cell as the irreducible unit |
| **Mark Dever / 9Marks** | Christ-centered preaching, costly discipleship | 9Marks holds tightly to formal ecclesiology, sacraments, and Reformed polity; Cole intentionally leaves these loose |
| **Curtis Sergeant / Steve Addison** | DMM/CPM compatible, multiplication-shaped | Sergeant/Addison frame from missions strategy; Cole frames from local Long Beach pastoring outward |
| **Robert (Bob) E. Logan** | Coaching, church-planting catalysis, mentor relationship | Logan is more institutional / Fuller Seminary / coach-ed; Cole is more pioneer / artist-pastor |
| **Brian Sanders (Underground Network)** | Decentralized microchurch, apostolic, sending-shaped | Sanders organizes around city-microchurch ecosystems; Cole organizes around LTG-as-the-cell |
| **Rob Wegner (KC Underground)** | Microchurch multiplication, decentralized leadership | Wegner is downstream of Cole and explicitly receives him as an elder voice in 2025 |

## Bottom Line

Cole is a **practitioner-theologian of decentralized discipleship multiplication**. His theological grammar is Christocentric, agricultural, low-hierarchy, and small-cell. He is doctrinally conservative (orthodox Christology, biblical authority, complementarian-and-egalitarian coexisting in his work but personally landing in the egalitarian-friendly camp via *Black Swan Effect*), but ecclesially low-church to the point of irritating Reformed critics. The late-career theology of obscurity is what makes him an unusually credible voice now — he has spent 13 years living the anti-platform argument his books made all along.
