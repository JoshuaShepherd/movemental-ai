## Editorial bio

Peyton Jones left a career as a psychiatric and drug-rehab nurse to enter full-time ministry at nineteen, then spent roughly twelve years planting churches in Wales — by his own description "the most unchurched part of the UK, with less than 1% church attendance." He began as an evangelist at Sandfields, the historic Aberavon church once pastored by D. Martyn Lloyd-Jones, helped plant its first daughter church in 2001, and founded NewBreed Church Planting across a post-Christian frontier before returning to the United States.

His books share one through-line: make disciples first, and churches will follow. Church Zero (David C. Cook, 2013) was the punk-rock apostolic manifesto; Reaching the Unreached (Zondervan, 2017, with Alan Hirsch) named the recovery of a lost art; Church Plantology (Zondervan, 2021) became a best-selling planting textbook; and Discipology (Zondervan, 2026) applies the same art-and-science architecture to disciple-making itself.

He founded NewBreed Training in Southern California, wrote and rewrote church-planting curriculum for the North American Mission Board, served as Content Director for Exponential and Through the Word, co-founded Church Planter Magazine and the Church Planter Podcast with Pete Mitchell, and now teaches church planting at Calvary University while training planters worldwide through Jump School and the CityWide regional-team model.
