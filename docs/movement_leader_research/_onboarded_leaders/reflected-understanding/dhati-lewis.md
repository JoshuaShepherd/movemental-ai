# A Letter to Dhati Lewis

Dhati — this is our attempt to reflect back what we understand about your calling and your work, in your own terms, before we ever talk about building anything. If we've misread you, the gaps are ours to fix.

## Calling

You are called to make disciples who make disciples in the city — and to keep the church *in* the city rather than fleeing it. From the beginning you've insisted that disciple-making, not church-planting, is the engine: plant a church and you're not guaranteed disciples, but make disciples and a church — a family — is the logical result. You founded Blueprint Church in Atlanta in 2009 on exactly that conviction, and you've said you want it to be part of "the last generation to [have to] leave the urban context for sound discipleship."

That calling has worn several institutional shapes. You built the Rebuild Network to plant multi-ethnic urban churches. You led the North American Mission Board's Send Network — as Vice President and then President — and used that seat to diversify who gets to plant, including the partnership with the National African American Fellowship. And then you stepped *out* of that national seat, at the end of 2021, to do the more particular work: developing and mentoring young leaders who will plant in the urban context, through BLVD and MyBLVD. The through-line is unmistakable — disciple-making and church-as-family, carried into the multiethnic city, and now poured into the next generation.

Your reconciliation work is part of the same calling, not a separate one. In *Advocates* you argued that Christians must become advocates rather than aggravators — relying on Christ, running to the tension, responding with dignity — a Christ-centered "narrow path" distinct from politically-driven division. It's the same posture as your disciple-making: refuse the easy binary, run toward the hard thing, stay.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

You've already made a great deal. There are the books — *Among Wolves: Disciple-Making in the City* (2017), built on eight movements through Matthew, with a LifeWay Bible study and leader kit; *Advocates: The Narrow Path to Racial Reconciliation* (2019), built on Philemon; and the earlier primer *In Plain Sight*, which seeded *Among Wolves*. There are the podcasts you host — *Where Life Exists*, the *MyBLVD Podcast* with Missie Branch and its "7 Stops of an Urban Missionary," and *We Are Send Network*. There are articles across ERLC, Saturate, Outreach, and the Christian Post, and years of teaching from the stages of Together for the Gospel, Mosaix, Verge, and Exponential. And there are the frameworks people quote you for: Advocate-versus-Aggravator, the eight movements, the church as family, the seven stops.

The honest observation is that this body of work doesn't live anywhere as one thing. The books are at two publishers; *In Plain Sight* has effectively disappeared; the podcasts are split across four platforms; the frameworks live buried inside long-form works rather than standing as references. The message is coherent. The *surface* is scattered.

## Constraints

We want to name the constraints as we understand them, because they shape what's worth building. First, your instinct is to run to people, not to build infrastructure that sits between you and them — you've warned that overhead can mean "a lot of overhead and little impact," and that gifts can take a person where their character can't follow. Anything built around your work has to serve presence, not replace it. Second, you operate as a practitioner and a pastor, not an academic or a brand — your reach is through trusted networks and stages more than through a large personal platform, and that's a feature of the calling, not a deficit. Third, the work is unfinished in a specific way: some of it — most notably the "7 Stops" — isn't fully written down anywhere, and an early foundational piece is no longer findable.

If we've understood you, the question worth asking together is simply this: could the work be gathered — frameworks, catalogue, the pieces still in your head — so that the next urban missionary doesn't have to leave to find sound discipleship? That's your sentence, not ours. We just want to help make it true.

— The Movemental team
