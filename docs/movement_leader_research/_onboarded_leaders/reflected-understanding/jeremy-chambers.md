## Calling

You're called to **refuse the dichotomy** — to hold intimacy with God and engagement with neighbors as one rhythm rather than two stages. Not contemplation first and mission later, not mission for God set beside time with God as separate items on a list, but a single integrated way of life. Your own words carry the whole conviction: a spirituality that "doesn't draw you away from your neighbors but propels you into the lives of others and the needs of your city." That's the seam you stand in — between the Renovaré contemplative tradition you were formed by (Foster, Willard, Hudson) and the Hirsch–Frost, Forge, and 100 Movements missional stream you now help steward. Most voices live on one side of that seam. You live on the seam itself, and you've been building tools to help others stand there too: the six paradigms of *Kingdom Contours*, the thirty-one daily practices of *The Art of Missional Spirituality*, the calling-versus-assignment distinction that guards a missionary's longevity.

There's a pastoral steadiness in how you carry this. You name **distraction**, not opposition, as the dominant threat to the inner life. You resist method absolutism — microchurch is *a* form, not *the* form — even within your own ecosystem. You teach people to put on their own oxygen mask before helping others, and you model service that needs no credit: "Goodness can be beautiful in its own right." And you do almost all of it as a dyad with Monica — a marriage-as-writing-partnership that is itself a quiet argument for the integrated life you teach. The language you already use — *rhythm*, *missional spirituality*, *micro-expressions*, *cross-pollinate*, *sustainable mission* — is the right language. It's practitioner-pastoral, formed by Scripture and by years of actually planting, and it names a way of being rather than a strategy to execute.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

**Where it lives.** Your content lives in **books** — *Kingdom Contours* (2020; revised 2024–25) and *The Art of Missional Spirituality* (2023), both co-authored with Monica, both in the 100 Movements Publishing orbit, with forewords from Alan Hirsch, Debra Hirsch, and Michael Frost. It lives in a sustained **essay archive** at the V3 Movement (2018–2021), Renovaré (2022–2023), and Missio Alliance (2025). It lives in a 2024 **podcast circuit** built around the book launch (Pivotal People, Dandelion, Renovaré's *Life With God*, Everyday Disciple, Shifting Culture). And it lives in **organizational and training contexts** — Forge America, where you serve as Training Director; The Pando Collective, where you co-lead the Denver expression; and Arise RVA, the microchurch network you founded. Your personal site (jemochambers.com) anchors you and the books; the rest is spread across those other domains.

**Credibility.** It's strong and well-attested. Your books carry forewords and endorsements from the central voices of two traditions at once — Hirsch, Debra Hirsch, and Frost on the missional side; Richard Foster, Carolyn Arends, and Renovaré on the contemplative side — alongside Forge (Rowland Smith, Terry Ishee), V3 (JR Woodward), and Tampa Underground (Brian Sanders). Your Training Director role gives you genuine curricular stewardship over Forge's national pipeline. The authority is real; the issue isn't credibility — it's **visibility, connection, and reuse**.

**How it lives online now.** As **silos**. The books sit on retailer and publisher pages; the essays sit on three different organizational blogs; the podcast appearances sit on five different hosts' feeds; the training material sits inside Forge's platform and is team-credited rather than connected to your name. There's no single place where "everything Jeremy has said about missional spirituality" or "the six paradigms" is gathered, structured, and easy to find. So the work doesn't **move** in the sense of circulating, compounding, and pointing back to itself — it stays where it was first put.

**The NOTs this addresses.** Your content is largely **not** gathered under one surface you control; **not** structured and repurposed so talks become articles and book themes become courses; **not** interconnected, so the books, essays, podcasts, and training visibly reference each other; **not** optimized for discoverability, so a searcher for "missional spirituality," "microchurch," or "sustainable mission" lands on your material first; and **not** translated, despite Monica's Costa Rica heritage and your shared Latin America work. A unified, connected home is built to speak to each of these — gathering, structuring, repurposing, interconnecting, and amplifying what you already do — so your integrated way can actually circulate.

## Constraints

The constraints you live under are emblematic of movement leaders, not personal failure. You're directing training for Forge nationally, co-leading a Pando network in Denver, pastoring and coaching microchurches, writing, and traveling a speaking and podcast circuit — much of it as a two-person team with Monica, which deepens the work but doesn't multiply the hours. Time for content strategy, for turning talks into articles, for building and maintaining a unified digital home, is scarce precisely because the work is in the room — with planters, neighbors, and the practices themselves. Your own teaching on compassion fatigue and the Cycle of Grace makes this sharper: you of all people know the cost of reversing grace's order and chasing fruitfulness before rest. A platform that reduces the lift — that helps gather, structure, repurpose, connect, and amplify what already exists — honors those constraints rather than adding to them, and lets your message reach more people without asking you to become a different kind of leader.
