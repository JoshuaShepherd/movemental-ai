## Calling

You're called to be a **bridge** — to carry obedience-based, reproducible disciple-making across a divide that most leaders treat as uncrossable. On one side stands the classically trained Western senior pastor: seminary-formed, responsible for a Sunday gathering, accountable to a congregation that exists. On the other stands the Disciple-Making Movement world — the obedience-focused, discovery-based, four-generations practice that has produced movements across Africa and Asia. Almost no one is trusted on both sides at once. You are, because you've lived both: twenty-five years as a senior pastor of the church you and Candy co-founded, and a long apprenticeship in DMM under David Watson after you were finally willing to say out loud that the disciples your church was making were not making disciples.

Your calling is not to plant the next great Sunday service, and not to abandon the local church for pure movement work. It's to **hold the two together** until the wider Western church can do the same — to keep an attractional gathering and a multiplicative track inside one body without forcing a leader to tear down what they already have. The language you already use is the right language: *Hybrid Church*, *spiritual literacy*, *obedience over information*, *the disciples we're making aren't making disciples*. It's pastoral and practitioner-grounded rather than theoretical — you're not selling a system, you're handing over a field manual you wrote because you needed it yourself. And it stays tethered to Scripture, to a reproducible practice ordinary people can carry, and to the real cost a senior pastor's identity pays to lead this shift.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

**Where it lives.** Your content lives in **two books** — *Spent Matches* (Thomas Nelson, 2015), the narrative on-ramp, and *Hybrid Church* (DMM Press, 2017), the how-to companion; in **roymoran.com**, where an archive of a hundred-plus posts spans Disciple-Making Movements, Hybrid Church, DBS practice, and lessons from twenty-five years at Shoal Creek; in the **weekly *Movement Conversations* podcast** you've hosted since 2020, one of only a handful of sustained DMM media properties in English; in **conference and consultation teaching** (iDisciple 2014, the Multiply DMM Summit alongside Jerry Trousdale and Mike Moran, the Trout Lake Hybrid Church Consultation, Mercy Alliance training); and in the **organizations you lead** — Mercy Alliance International and your Platform role at New Generations, which keeps you connected weekly to global movement data.

**Credibility.** It's high, and it's unusual. You hold Dallas Theological Seminary credentials and a lived twenty-five-year hybrid case study at one congregation — a combination that makes your turn toward DMM credible to the very pastors who would dismiss a foreign-mission theory. The issue was never authority. It's **visibility, structure, and reuse.**

**How it lives online now.** As **scattered pieces.** Your books sit on retailer and publisher pages. Your blog holds real topical depth but under-surfaces for the searches it should own — "hybrid church," "DBS in a Western setting," "DMM in America." Your podcast is distributed across Apple, Spotify, and others with no single searchable home you control. Your teaching videos are uploaded by event hosts to third-party channels, with no central library; there is no one place where "everything Roy has said and written about the Hybrid Church" is gathered, linked, and findable. So the work doesn't **move** the way it could — it stays where it was first put.

**The NOTs this platform addresses directly.** Your content is largely **not** structured and repurposed so it lives as findable digital content (talks → articles, book themes → a cohort); **not** gathered into a single owned home for the podcast archive and the video library; **not** optimized so searchers for hybrid church, DBS, or DMM in the West land on your material first; **not** connected to an AI that reflects your voice and corpus — which fits you especially well, since your "obedience over information" pedagogy wants an interactive coach more than a static library; and **not** owned and unified under one surface you control rather than platforms you rent. This platform is built to speak to each of these — structure and repurpose, a single owned home, discoverability, an AI that serves your voice, and ownership — so your content can move.

## Constraints

The gap in your work is not output — it's **capture and packaging.** Almost everything you do is already aligned to your calling: you preach, you train, you record a weekly podcast, you coach pastors, you carry New Generations and Mercy Alliance. The constraint is the same one every working movement leader lives under: the hours are spent in the room — with pastors mid-transition, with catalysts, with the congregation that still looks to you as directional leader — not on content strategy, SEO, or building a unified digital home. The teaching gets done; the recording gets left with whoever hosted the event; the archive never gets organized. The desire to reach every leader God puts in front of you is there; the time to structure, translate, and amplify all of it at scale typically is not. A platform that reduces that lift — that captures, structures, connects, and surfaces what you're already producing — meets the constraint without asking you to become a different kind of leader than the practitioner-pastor you are.
