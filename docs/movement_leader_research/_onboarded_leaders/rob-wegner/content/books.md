## Why this matters

The existing flat-file research (January 2026) recorded **1 co-authored book** for Wegner. The actual count is **8 books across roughly 15 years**. This is a substantial miss.

The implications:

- **Wegner is not an under-published practitioner.** He has more retail-distributed titles than Lucas Pulley (0), Joel Repic (UNKNOWN — likely 0–1), or Brian Sanders (~3) — and roughly comparable volume to Lance Ford and Brad Brisco.
- **His co-author network is a publishing ecosystem in itself.** Eight books across seven different co-author groupings — Hirsch + Ford + Brafman; Wilson; Phipps; Magruder; Brian Johnson (twice); Walkemeyer; Michelle Wegner. This is rare.
- **The publishing arc traces his ecclesial arc.** *Share the Well* (India / Granger era) → *Missional Moves* (paradigm-shift / 2012 Granger era) → *Made for More* + *Find Your Place* (mobilization / 2018–19 Westside-to-KCU transition) → *Starfish and the Spirit* (decentralized leadership / 2021 KCU keystone) → *Extraordinary Prayer* + *Deeply Rooted* (Missionary Pathway / 2024–25 KCU + Exponential merger era).
- **The publishers signal the network.** Three Zondervan titles (in the Exponential Series) tie him to the major-publisher Christian leadership market. Five Exponential titles tie him to the Exponential / Leadership Network ecosystem. Self-published *Share the Well* opens the corpus.

**Onboarding implication**: do not treat Wegner as a "1 book" author. The bibliography is substantial, the co-author network is unusually wide, and there is enough material across 15 years to anchor multiple Movemental learning paths (microchurch starter, decentralized leadership, mobilization-to-mission, missionary pathway, India-to-North-America movements). The largest miss in the existing dossier was the volume of work; the second-largest was the co-author network density.
