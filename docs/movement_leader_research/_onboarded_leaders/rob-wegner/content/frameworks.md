# Frameworks: Rob Wegner

The signature frameworks, operating models, and conceptual structures that recur across Rob Wegner's corpus. Each is grounded in his published work and KCU practice; see `profile/theology.md`, `profile/voice-analysis.md`, and `content/books.md` for sources.

### A Missionary on Every Street, a Microchurch in Every Network

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2019 (Kansas City Underground founding mission) |
| Components | Mission horizon: 21,000 microchurches arising from 42,000 missionaries over 40 years; "extended spiritual family led by ordinary people, not paid professionals" |
| Source | [Exponential — KC Underground](https://exponential.org/kc-underground/) |

The organizing horizon of all Wegner's work: a city saturated with the gospel when ordinary people become missionaries on their actual streets and microchurches emerge from the networks of relationships they already have.

### The Two-Entity Structure

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2019 (Kansas City Underground operating model) |
| Components | A mission agency that equips ordinary people to live as missionaries **and** a decentralized network of microchurches that emerges from new disciples — working as one organism |
| Source | [Exponential — KC Underground](https://exponential.org/kc-underground/) |

KCU runs as two coupled entities — a mission agency and a microchurch network — so that mobilization and multiplication reinforce each other rather than compete.

### The Missionary Pathway

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | ~2024 (*Extraordinary Prayer*, Exponential; *Five Provocative Questions: Part Two*) |
| Components | Five named phases: prayer/fasting → live as a missionary → plant the gospel → a microchurch emerges → multiplication |
| Source | *Extraordinary Prayer: Tools, Movements, Microchurches, and the Missionary Pathway* (Exponential, ~2024) |

The Missionary Pathway is KCU's training pipeline that moves an ordinary person, step by step, from prayer into missionary identity until a reproducing microchurch emerges.

### Starfish vs. Spider (with Lance Ford and Alan Hirsch)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2021 (*The Starfish and the Spirit*, Zondervan; foreword by Ori Brafman) |
| Components | Decentralized starfish structures outperform centralized spider hierarchies in adaptive, multiplicative environments; the Spirit is what distinguishes Christian starfish from secular ones |
| Source | *The Starfish and the Spirit: Unleashing the Leadership Potential of Churches and Organizations* (Zondervan, 2021) |

Wegner, Ford, and Hirsch argue that decentralized, regenerative "starfish" organizations multiply where centralized "spider" hierarchies cannot — and that the Holy Spirit is the difference between a Christian starfish and a merely organizational one.

### Find Your Place — Gifts × Passions × Story

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2018 (*Find Your Place*, Exponential; Zondervan re-release 2019) |
| Components | A "GPS for the soul": three personal-discovery axes — Gifts, Passions, and Story — triangulated by the Spirit; productized as a 4-week Huddle, a self-paced webinar, and the book |
| Source | *Find Your Place: Locating Your Calling Through Your Gifts, Passions, and Story* (Zondervan/Exponential, 2018–19) |

Find Your Place is a "GPS for the soul" that helps a person locate their calling where their gifts, passions, and story intersect — the on-ramp into KCU's Missionary Pathway.

### Made for More — Six Essential Shifts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2018 (*Made for More*, Exponential; theme of Exponential 2019) |
| Components | Six paradigm shifts for a culture of mobilization, drawn from Ephesians 1–6, beginning with "from more effort to more Jesus" |
| Source | *Made for More: Six Essential Shifts for Creating a Culture of Mobilization* (Exponential, 2018) |

Made for More names six paradigm shifts from Ephesians that move a church from striving programs to a culture where every person is mobilized into God's mission.

### Five Provocative Questions

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2024 (*Five Provocative Questions*, Parts 1 & 2, Exponential) |
| Components | Vision (definition of a disciple) → Voice (who listens to you) → Vocabulary (language and tools) → Vehicles (five relational social spaces) → Voyage (maturity / missionary pathway) |
| Source | *Five Provocative Questions* Parts 1–2 (Exponential, Nov–Dec 2024) |

The Five Provocative Questions are a diagnostic for any disciple-making environment, beginning with the most basic and most often skipped: "What is your definition of a disciple?"

### Five Ingredients for Disciple-Making Environments

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2024 (*Five Ingredients*, Exponential; maps to chapters 10–14 of *Starfish and the Spirit*) |
| Components | Outcome-Focused · Habit-Fueled · Community-Forged · Mission-Fixated · Content-Flavored |
| Source | *Five Ingredients* (Exponential, May 2024) |

The Five Ingredients name the conditions that make a disciple-making environment actually form disciples — practice and habit over information alone, because "practice makes permanent."

### Family of Families / Hubology

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2024 (*Navigating Change*, Exponential; Hubology series with Lance Ford) |
| Components | KCU's restructure from a centralized founder team to peer Hubs operating as a "family of families"; servant-equipper leadership that supports microchurch leaders without controlling them |
| Source | *Navigating Change: Reimagining the Future of KC Underground as a Family of Families* (Exponential, Oct 2024) |

The Family of Families restructure redistributes power from a founding team to peer leadership Hubs, modeling a servant-equipper "hubology" that supports leaders without controlling them.
