## Personal newsletter

**None found under `robwegner.org` or any Wegner-owned domain.**

`robwegner.org` is a Tumblr-powered hub with no email-capture form, no ESP integration, and no archive of past newsletter issues. Search for "Rob Wegner Substack," "Rob Wegner newsletter," "Rob Wegner Mailchimp," "Rob Wegner Beehiiv," "Rob Wegner ConvertKit," "Rob Wegner Kit" returned no Wegner-personal hits. The only Substack he is associated with is the joint *Starfish and the Church / Starfish yoU* Substack with Lance Ford (see below).

This is a strikingly under-leveraged surface for an 8-book co-author and three-organization principal.

## Assessment

**Newsletter is one of the largest holes in Wegner's personal digital surface.**

Specifically:

1. **No Wegner-personal newsletter exists** at all.
2. The joint *Starfish yoU* Substack is co-branded and primarily a podcast feed.
3. KCU and Exponential org newsletters carry Wegner content but route subscribers to those orgs, not to Wegner.
4. There are no Wegner-personal lead magnets that capture email; *Find Your Place Guide* is KCU-branded.
5. For an 8-book author, the absence of any retailer-funnel-into-newsletter mechanism is unusual.

The 2026 baseline: a reader who finishes *The Starfish and the Spirit* and goes looking for "what's next from Rob Wegner" lands on a stale `robwegner.org` Tumblr blog or a co-branded Substack — neither of which is a clear "follow Rob Wegner" path.

## What Movemental would change

- A genuine personal newsletter under `robwegner.org` (or `wegner.movemental.ai`), seeded from Goodreads followers, Instagram (1,867), KCU subscriber list, *Starfish* book buyers, and Substack co-subscribers.
- Standalone lead magnet: *Five Provocative Questions for a Disciple-Making Ecosystem* as a polished PDF, capturing email at every retailer-page click and conference appearance.
- Cross-link into the joint Substack so the "Starfish ecosystem" carries through both surfaces without flattening into one.
- Capture every appearance — podcast guest spots, conference sessions, KCU stories — into the newsletter as a "voice across surfaces" digest.
