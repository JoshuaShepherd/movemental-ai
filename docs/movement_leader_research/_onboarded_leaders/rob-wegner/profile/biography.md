## Narrative Biography

Rob Wegner is one of the most networked figures in the contemporary North American microchurch and church-multiplication conversation. For roughly **a quarter-century at Granger Community Church** in northern Indiana — under founding pastor Mark Beeson — he served as **Teaching Pastor / Pastor of Life Mission**, with a brief from the senior team to "call the people of God to get out of their seat and into the Story" (per [robwegner.org/about](https://robwegner.org/about/)). During that period he co-led EnterMission, partnered with **Alan Hirsch** to start the **Future Travelers** coaching program for megachurches becoming missional movements, co-authored *Missional Moves* (Zondervan, 2012) with Granger colleague Jack Magruder, and went to India repeatedly with his wife **Michelle Wegner** and their three daughters to document a Granger-seeded grassroots church-planting movement that grew to over 120,000 disciples in 950+ churches across southern India. That India experience reshaped his ecclesial vision and produced *Share the Well*, the photo-essay book he and Michelle co-authored.

Around 2015 Wegner moved with the family from Granger, Indiana to the Kansas City area to join the staff of **Westside Family Church**, where he served four years as a teaching pastor whose team — by Westside's own account — "trained leaders who planted over 700 churches internationally in four years" ([KCU staff bio](https://www.kcunderground.com/staff/wegners)). The Westside era is the bridge: he had moved out of Granger's attractional megachurch model but had not yet started what would become his own decentralized work.

In **2019**, with a starter group of about 80 disciples — and partnering with **Brian Johnson** and Michelle — Rob founded **Kansas City Underground (KCU)**: "a mission agency and decentralized network of microchurches and missional leaders committed to filling Kansas City with the beauty, justice, and Good News of Jesus." The organization's stated 40-year horizon is **21,000 microchurches arising from 42,000 missionaries**. As of 2025–26 KCU operates ~95 microchurches across 7 Collectives, has activated 110+ missionaries (with 300+ in training), runs 5 Hubs operational or in development, and has launched 120+ Discovery Bible Studies ([Exponential KCU page](https://exponential.org/kc-underground/)). The architecture is explicitly two-entity: a mission agency (Missionary Pathway, training, Hubs, coaching) plus a decentralized network of microchurches (Collectives of 4–8 microchurches, with shared leadership).

Concurrent with KCU, Wegner picked up two more national-scale roles. He became **North American Director of NewThing** (Dave Ferguson's church-multiplication network, ~1,600 churches across 177 local networks), and **Director of Microchurch NEXT for Exponential** (later re-titled Co-Director with **Brian Johnson** after 2024). He has remained on the Global Leadership Team of NewThing through the **April 1, 2025 NewThing-into-Exponential merger** announced at `exponential.org/bettertogether`. By April 2026 his Outreach Magazine byline reads: "Mobilization and Practice Catalyst for Exponential." He is also a **co-host (with Lance Ford)** of *The Starfish and the Church* podcast — 43 episodes — and of the parallel *Starfish yoU* Substack at `starfishinitiatives.substack.com`. The podcast and Substack are the public faces of **Starfish Initiatives**, a coaching/training brand that lists Wegner, Ford, **Alan Hirsch**, and **Rich Robinson** as principal leaders ([starfishinitiatives.com](https://starfishinitiatives.com/)).

His written corpus is larger than the public conversation suggests. *Missional Moves* (Zondervan 2012) was the first book; **eight books in total** are confirmed by 2026, spanning Granger-era megachurch mobilization (*Made for More*, *Find Your Place*) through the KCU microchurch era (*Extraordinary Prayer*, *Deeply Rooted*, both 2024–25) and culminating in the keynote *The Starfish and the Spirit* (Zondervan, March 30, 2021), co-authored with **Lance Ford** and **Alan Hirsch**, with a foreword by **Ori Brafman**. *Starfish and the Spirit* is the book that places Wegner inside the Hirsch–Ford–Brafman intellectual lineage and is the bridge edge connecting two otherwise separate author clusters: the Forge / 100Movements / Movement Leaders Collective wing on one side, and the Exponential / NewThing / Underground city-movement wing on the other.

## Origin & Formation

Wegner is, by his own framing, **a megachurch insider who became a microchurch evangelist**. He has stated explicitly that "early success in large, attractional churches led to internal conflict and a complete overhaul" of his outlook on church (per [Roys Report](https://julieroys.com/podcast/going-from-megachurch-to-microchurch/)). The transformation happened **in India**, working alongside grassroots indigenous church planters who were multiplying at speeds and densities the American megachurch couldn't match. The intellectual companions of that transformation are visible across his books and podcasts:

- **Alan Hirsch** — APEST, *The Forgotten Ways*, mDNA. Hirsch wrote the foreword to *Missional Moves* (2012), co-authored *The Starfish and the Spirit* (2021), and co-founded Future Travelers with Wegner. Hirsch is the longest-running intellectual influence in Wegner's bibliography.
- **Ori Brafman** — *The Starfish and the Spider* (2006). Brafman wrote the foreword to *The Starfish and the Spirit*. The starfish/spider metaphor is the sustaining frame across the second half of Wegner's career.
- **Lance Ford** — co-author and primary co-host. The two have been collaborating publicly since at least 2021.
- **Brian Sanders** — Tampa Underground founder. Wegner appears with Sanders on the *Starfish and the Church* podcast and KCU's "Underground" naming explicitly riffs off the Tampa Underground lineage. (Wegner has confirmed familiarity with Sanders' *Underground Church* paradigm; whether KCU is a formally-affiliated Underground sister is not fully clarified in public materials.)
- **Indian disciple-making movements (DMM/DBS)** — Discovery Bible Study (DBS) is a documented KCU practice (120+ DBS launched per Exponential profile). The movement-multiplication math underlying KCU's 21,000-microchurch / 42,000-missionary horizon is borrowed from Indian and African disciple-making movement practitioners.

His midwestern-evangelical instincts (Taylor University; 21 years at Granger; a non-denominational megachurch sensibility) sit on top of those imports, which keeps his vocabulary plain and his distribution channels evangelical-friendly. He is **not** academically trained; the formation is practitioner-driven and read-everything.

## Career & Ministry Arc

Wegner's career splits cleanly into three eras:

**Era 1: Granger / EnterMission / India (~Early 1990s → ~2015, 21 years).** Teaching Pastor / Pastor of Life Mission at Granger Community Church under Mark Beeson. Co-leader of EnterMission (Granger's community-transformation initiative). Repeated India trips to document the Granger-seeded movement. Co-founder of Future Travelers with Hirsch. Author of *Share the Well* (with Michelle) and *Missional Moves* (with Magruder). The frame is "an attractional megachurch becoming missional" — Wegner was inside the megachurch but pushing it outward.

**Era 2: Westside Family Church (Kansas City, ~2015–2019, 4 years).** Teaching pastor at a second large non-denominational megachurch in Kansas City. Notable claim: trained a team that planted 700+ churches internationally in four years. Less of his public writing is dated to this era; the books that bracket it are *Made for More* (Exponential 2018, with Todd Wilson) and *Find Your Place* (Exponential 2018; Zondervan rerelease 2019, with Brian Phipps). Wegner has said publicly that he was already in conflict with megachurch leadership form during this era.

**Era 3: Kansas City Underground (2019 → present).** The microchurch network. Two-entity structure (mission agency + decentralized network). Concurrent with: Microchurch NEXT (Exponential), North American Director of NewThing, Mobilization & Practice Catalyst at Exponential, Global Leadership Team at NewThing, co-host of *Starfish and the Church*, co-author of *Starfish and the Spirit* (2021), *Extraordinary Prayer* (2024), *Deeply Rooted* (2025). This is the era where Wegner stops being "a teaching pastor inside a large institution" and becomes "a translocal apostolic-team-member serving the ecosystem."

## Current Focus

- **Founder & Director, Kansas City Underground.** Operational lead of a ~95-microchurch network in Kansas City; mission agency continues to recruit and train missionaries via the "Missionary Pathway."
- **Microchurch NEXT Co-Director, Exponential** (with Brian Johnson). Hosts the Microchurch NEXT podcast; runs Microchurch Pre-Conferences at Exponential national conferences; produces the BLESS Rhythms and Church Expressions NEXT video series with Bill Couchenour and Carrie Williams.
- **Co-host, *The Starfish and the Church* podcast** (with Lance Ford) — 43 episodes; the **Hubology series** (with Lance Ford, Brian Johnson, Lucas Pulley) is the highest-leverage thread in 2024–2025.
- **Co-leader, Starfish Initiatives** (consulting / coaching brand with Ford, Hirsch, Robinson).
- **NewThing Global Leadership Team member** (post April 2025 merger into Exponential).
- **Future Travelers** (with Hirsch) — status in 2026 UNKNOWN, but presence on `robwegner.org/about` page suggests still affiliated.
- Annual presence at **Exponential Global, Central, and West conferences** plus **Microchurch Conference** (KCU's annual event; Wegner's KCU team co-hosted the Tampa Microchurch Conference 2026 with 550+ attendees).
