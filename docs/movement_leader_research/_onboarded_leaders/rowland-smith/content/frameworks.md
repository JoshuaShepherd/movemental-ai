# Frameworks: L. Rowland Smith

**Status**: Complete (grounded in published corpus)
**Last updated**: 2026-06-05
**Confidence**: HIGH on framework existence and naming. Smith has no formally diagrammed or trademarked framework comparable to APEST / mDNA; these are named, recurring constructs documented in his corpus.

Smith's constructs form one coherent practice: disciples are formed by *doing* mission and reflecting on it (action-reflection), ordinary believers are commissioned to join Jesus outside the walls, the church's future is best discerned as a curated multi-voice conversation, and micro-church networks like Pando carry it into a region.

---

### Action-Reflection Discipleship

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Codified in the Missio Alliance essay *An Action-Reflection Approach to Discipleship unto Jesus* and his Fuller dissertation *Missional Emergence* |
| Components | "Learning by doing" before propositional knowledge; an action → reflection learning cycle driving missional formation |
| Visual / model | — (no published diagram located) |
| Adoption evidence | Powers the Forge America cohort curriculum and his Denver Seminary DMin (New Missional Movements) teaching weeks |
| Source | [missioalliance.org](https://www.missioalliance.org/an-action-reflection-approach-to-discipleship-unto-jesus/) |

An action-reflection pedagogy holding that disciples are formed by doing mission and then reflecting on it, rather than by acquiring propositional knowledge first — the methodological backbone of his Forge cohort design.

### Joining Jesus Outside the Walls (Life Out Loud thesis)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2019 (*Life Out Loud: Joining Jesus Outside the Walls of the Church*) |
| Components | Every Christ-follower invited into adventurous, everyday-missionary living in the places they live, work, and play |
| Visual / model | — |
| Adoption evidence | Reinforced by his Pulpit Rock role title "Pastor of Missional Culture"; anchors his missional-discipleship teaching |
| Source | [100mpublishing.com](https://100mpublishing.com/product/life-out-loud-joining-jesus-outside-the-walls-of-the-church/) |

The central thesis of *Life Out Loud*: ordinary believers are commissioned as everyday missionaries in their own contexts, not spectators inside church walls.

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | The Pando Collective (Front-Range network seeded inside Pulpit Rock Church) |
| Components | A regional Kingdom network connecting, activating, and launching micro-expressions of the church — house churches, ministries, and Kingdom-focused businesses |
| Visual / model | — |
| Adoption evidence | Operates across Colorado Springs, Denver, and the Front Range; programs include coaching/training and "Kingdom Dream Grants"; cited inspiration: Tampa Underground (Brian Sanders) |
