## The Story

Dr. Leo Rowland Smith runs four institutions at once. He is the **National Director of Forge America Mission Training Network**, the **Founder/Director of The Pando Collective** on the Front Range, the **Pastor of Missional Culture at The Church at Pulpit Rock** in Colorado Springs, and **adjunct faculty at three accredited seminaries** — Fuller (DGL), Denver Seminary (DMin in New Missional Movements), and Grand Canyon University. He has written one book (*Life Out Loud*, 100 Movements Publishing, 2019), curated another (*Red Skies*, 100 Movements Publishing, 2022, with Alan Hirsch, Michael Frost, Debra Hirsch, Brian Sanders, Leonard Sweet, and eleven other contributors), and defended a Fuller dissertation (*Missional Emergence*) now sitting in Google Books under the byline "Leo Rowland Smith." And in 2026 alone he is listed as a speaker at Exponential Global, Exponential West, Mosaix, and the Awakenings Gathering.

None of that lives in one place. His personal site at [rowlandsmith.net](https://www.rowlandsmith.net) has a `/bio` page and a `/speaking` page and effectively nothing else — during the April 2026 research pass it returned `ECONNREFUSED` to programmatic fetches, meaning even the crawlers that try to find him bounce off. His books are sold through Amazon, Goodreads, Christianbook, AbeBooks, BooksRun, eBay, MX Platform, RJ Julia, and 100mpublishing.com — nine retail surfaces — but there is no canonical "where to start with Rowland's work" page on any site he controls. The *Red Skies* companion site [redskiesfuture.com](https://redskiesfuture.com) hosts a sample chapter and contributors page; it does not link back to his hub. His Missio Alliance essay *An Action-Reflection Approach to Discipleship unto Jesus* — which codifies the methodology that powers his entire Forge America cohort curriculum — lives at `missioalliance.org/author/rowland-smith/` and is unlinked from both books. The two organizations he leads, [forgeamerica.com](https://www.forgeamerica.com) and [thepandocollective.com](https://thepandocollective.com), are both built on Wix and render heavily client-side, which means search engines see the chrome and miss the content.

He does not have a newsletter. He does not have a personal podcast — he guests on at least four (Forge America Episode 73, Curious Friends September 2022, the Missio Alliance *Red Skies* series, and Koinesúnē) but hosts none of them. He does not have a personal YouTube channel — his teaching video lives on Pulpit Rock's sermon archive at `/messages/` and in Forge America's Wix-embedded library, neither of which is keyed to his name. His LinkedIn and personal Instagram cannot be verified at URL level because Google collisions with a retired MLB pitcher (Ryan Rowland-Smith, `@hyphen18`), a UK author (Dr. Robert Rowland Smith, `@dr_robert_rowland_smith`), and the hosts of "The Smith and Rowland Show" (Alan Smith + Jeff Rowland) swamp the results.

The defining feature of his fragmentation is not absence — it is that every embodied activity he runs has a digital surface *somewhere*, and no two surfaces point at each other. A reader who finishes *Life Out Loud* cannot find his dissertation from it. A DMin student at Denver Seminary cannot find his *Red Skies* conversations from the seminary page. A pastor who hears him at Mosaix on "A Needed Posture of the Church for Our Culture Today" cannot find the Missio Alliance essay where he lays out the action-reflection frame that posture rests on. A Forge America cohort alumnus cannot follow him into Pando; a Pando network leader cannot follow him into the book. The work is everywhere. The through-line is nowhere.

## Synthesis

Rowland Smith's knowledge is retrievable in pieces and unretrievable as a whole. Roughly two-thirds of his externalized output is book-shaped and therefore indexed well by retailers — Amazon, Goodreads, 100mpublishing.com, Christianbook, and six more — but the other third (the Missio Alliance essay, the Mosaix talk, the Awakenings workshops, the Forge Episode 73 interview, the Curious Friends appearance, the Koinesúnē conversation, the dissertation, the Pulpit Rock sermon archive) is scattered across partner domains with no canonical index on any site he controls. The single largest pool of trapped value is not the public-but-unlinked content — it is the **Forge America cohort curriculum, the Pando Collective field knowledge, and the multi-seminary DMin teaching weeks**: years of codified missional-training material that lives only in cohort rooms, private LMS surfaces, and the facilitator's head. His personal site [rowlandsmith.net](https://www.rowlandsmith.net) could be the hub that ties it all together and currently ties none of it together, which is why a Google search for his own name is still disambiguated by a retired baseball player.
