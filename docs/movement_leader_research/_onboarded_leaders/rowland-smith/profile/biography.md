## Narrative biography

Dr. **Leo Rowland Smith** ("L. Rowland Smith") is a Colorado Springs–based pastor, missional practitioner, author, and adjunct educator. He leads at the intersection of national mission training, regional micro-church networking, and local church culture: as **National Director of Forge America Mission Training Network**, **Founder and Director of The Pando Collective** (Front-Range micro-church network nested in Pulpit Rock Church), and **Pastor of Missional Culture at The Church at Pulpit Rock**. He teaches adjunctively at **Fuller Theological Seminary** (DGL), **Denver Seminary** (including the Doctor of Ministry in New Missional Movements), and **Grand Canyon University**.

His published work includes *Life Out Loud: Joining Jesus Outside the Walls of the Church* (100 Movements Publishing, 2019; with Michael Frost's contribution on the volume) and *Red Skies: 10 Essential Conversations Exploring Our Future as the Church* (100 Movements Publishing, 2022), which he curated as a multi-author conversation with leaders such as Alan Hirsch, Michael Frost, Debra Hirsch, Brian Sanders, Gregory Coles, Mark DeYmaz, Rich Robinson, Oneya Okuwobi, Tyler Kleeberger, Lisa Rodriguez-Watson, Jay Y. Kim, Leonard Sweet, Michael Adam Beck, and Anna Robinson — with a companion site and Missio Alliance podcast tie-in. His Fuller dissertation, *Missional Emergence*, appears under the byline Leo Rowland Smith in Google Books metadata.

Earlier chapters of ministry reportedly include church planting, worship pastorship, co-founding the missional coffee shop **Third Space Coffee** with his wife, and a prior career in portfolio management and investment analysis. His public themes cluster around missional discipleship, action–reflection learning, micro-church multiplication, and multi-ethnic / cultural posture in the church — articulated in print, on Missio Alliance, through Forge America cohorts, and on national conference stages (Exponential, Mosaix, Awakenings).
