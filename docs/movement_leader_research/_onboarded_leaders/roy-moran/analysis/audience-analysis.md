# Audience Analysis: Roy Moran

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-06-03

---

## Overview

Roy Moran's audience sits across a line that is usually hard to cross: U.S. seminary-trained senior pastors and global obedience-based disciple-making movement (DMM) practitioners. His credibility in both worlds comes from 25 years leading Shoal Creek Community Church in Kansas City while building a parallel multiplicative track inside the same congregation, then publishing that journey in *Spent Matches* (2015) and *Hybrid Church* (2017).

Most missional voices reach pastors *or* practitioners, not both. Moran can address attractional megachurch leaders and interview a Nepali DMM catalyst in the same week — and both audiences tend to receive him as one of their own.

---

## Primary Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Convicted Senior Pastors of Established Churches

**Who they are**

- Typically 40–65 years old, predominantly male, seminary-trained
- Pastoring congregations of roughly 250–2,500 attendees; often founder or tenured 10+ years
- Theologically evangelical; not anti-institutional but increasingly aware the attractional model is fragile
- May have read Frost, Hirsch, or Stetzer but find purely missional theology too theoretical
- Often describe a "spent matches" feeling — best ideas burned through without disciples reproducing disciples

**What they need**

- An on-ramp from attractional to multiplicative ministry without abandoning the existing congregation
- Story-driven, first-person guidance with "Monday morning" specificity
- A sequenced multi-year transition pattern (language → structures → metrics)
- Peer testimony from other pastors who made a similar shift

**How Roy serves them**

He writes as the slightly-ahead colleague who walked the same path inside the same kind of church. *Spent Matches* is the narrative entry; the Hybrid Church framework is the operating manual.

**Preferred channels**

Books → podcast → conference stages → cohort-style learning (when available)

---

### Segment 2: North American DMM Practitioners and Catalysts

**Who they are**

- Typically 30–60 years old, mixed denominational background (Baptist, nondenominational, charismatic)
- Often bivocational; many connected to Mercy Alliance, Beyond, New Generations, or CityTeam-legacy networks
- Already convinced of DMM; seeking sustained peer ecosystem and Western-context wisdom
- Heavy podcast consumers; lighter book buyers than the pastor segment

**What they need**

- Field intelligence and peer connection
- Contextualization help for suburban and urban North American settings
- Practitioner interviews, case studies, and obedience-based pedagogy
- Failure narratives — what did not work and why

**How Roy serves them**

He is among the most consistent North America–located DMM media voices: weekly *Movement Conversations* podcast, blog cadence, and conference presence.

**Preferred channels**

Podcast, retreats, in-person training, practitioner networks

---

### Segment 3: Global DMM Practitioners

**Who they are**

- DMM catalysts across Indonesia, India, Sub-Saharan Africa, Latin America, and Europe
- Affiliated with New Generations, 24:14 Coalition, Beyond, and related networks
- Their movements often document multi-generation reproduction
- Roy functions as a U.S. pastoral voice advocating for the model in Western church contexts

**How Roy serves them**

As podcast host, board member, and convening figure — amplifying Global South practice and translating lessons for U.S. pastoral language.

**Preferred channels**

New Generations organizational channels, donor-network meetings, WhatsApp groups, in-person gatherings

---

### Segment 4: Lay Disciple-Makers

**Who they are**

- Everyday Christians seeking a simple, reproducible way to disciple a neighbor or coworker without seminary training
- Often arrive through friend recommendation or a church handoff

**What they need**

- Short, visual, immediately usable tools (Discovery Bible Study starters, simple obedience frameworks)

**Gap**

Much of Roy's published content is pastor-pitched rather than lay-pitched. The "Read *Spent Matches* in 20 Minutes" lead asset on roymoran.com is one bridge toward this segment.

**Preferred channels**

Friend recommendation, church handoff, simple book or PDF tools

## Audience Bridge

Roy's unusual strength is holding pastoral and practitioner audiences together. They want different language, formats, and proof points — but he is a rare leader through whom either segment can be invited into the other's frame.

For content planning: treat the segments as distinct channels and tones, not one collapsed funnel. Senior pastors respond to first-person congregational narrative; practitioners respond to field interviews and Western contextualization; global partners respond to convening and amplification rather than product sales.
