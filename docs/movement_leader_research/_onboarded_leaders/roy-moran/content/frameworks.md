# Frameworks

Signature models and practices Roy Moran teaches and has published, grounded in *Spent Matches* (Thomas Nelson, 2015), *Hybrid Church* (DMM Press, 2017), his roymoran.com archive, and his Disciple-Making Movement lineage under David Watson.

### Hybrid Church

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2017 (*Hybrid Church: How the local church can engage disciple-making movements strategy*) |
| Components | Attractional Sunday gathering held together with a multiplicative DMM track inside one body; mode-switching by terrain; "come and go" |
| Core convictions | Clarity of language · obedience over information · visible progress · community before conversion · Sunday as launchpad not destination |
| Audience logic | A gathering for the minority of Americans open to attending church; a multiplicative network for the majority who will never come on a Sunday |
| Source | [Hybrid Church (roymoran.com)](https://www.roymoran.com/hybrid-church-can-the-local-church-and-dmm-strategy-co-exist/) |

Moran's signature model holds an attractional weekend service and a reproducing disciple-making network inside the same congregation, switching modes by context like a hybrid car so that one church can both gather a crowd and multiply disciples.

### Disciple-Making Movements (DMM)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Adapted for the West c. 2015 (*Spent Matches*); lineage from David Watson / CityTeam (Beyond) |
| Six characteristics | God-ordained · Spirit-dependent · Bible-centered · obedience-focused · discovery-based · disciple-driven |
| Aim | Reproducible discipleship reaching four generations of multiplication without professional-clergy mediation |
| Source | [Spent Matches (Goodreads)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/22574737-spent-matches) |

DMM is the obedience-based, reproducible disciple-making strategy — flourishing across Africa and Asia — that Moran translated into the language and structures of the North American church.

### Discovery Bible Study (DBS)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Taught throughout *Spent Matches* (2015) and his DMM training |
| Process | Read / retell the passage · observe what it says about God and people · identify an "I will" obedience response · share with someone · report back next time |
| Distinctive | Self-feeding, leaderless-reproducible group process; no professional teacher required |
| Source | [Read Spent Matches in 20 Minutes (roymoran.com)](https://www.roymoran.com/read-spent-matches-in-20-minutes/) |

DBS is the simple, reproducible inductive group process at the engine of Moran's practice, where ordinary people discover Scripture together and commit to obey it rather than merely accumulate information.

### Obedience-Based Discipleship

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Central thesis of *Spent Matches* (2015) |
| Mechanism | "I will" obedience statements · accountability and report-back · obedience tracked as the metric of growth |
| Contrast | Obedience over information; formation measured by what disciples do, not by attendance or knowledge |
| Source | [Spent Matches notes (Justin Gravitt)](https://www.justingravitt.com/booknotes/spentmatches) |

Moran's diagnosis is that Western Christianity is starved of obedience, so he organizes discipleship around acted-on "I will" commitments and report-back accountability rather than information transfer.

### Person of Peace

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Taught in *Spent Matches* and DMM training; drawn from Luke 10 |
| Function | Discern the receptive "person of peace" who opens a household or network to the gospel |
| Role | Entry point for a Discovery group; locus of multiplication into a new relational web |
| Source | [Spent Matches (Goodreads)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/22574737-spent-matches) |

The "person of peace" is the receptive individual — drawn from Jesus' sending instructions in Luke 10 — whom Moran teaches catalysts to look for as the natural doorway into a new household or social network.

### Spiritual Literacy

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Framing tied to Moran's post-pastoral role as Shoal Creek's "spiritual literacy advocate" |
| Goal | Believers able to read, obey, and reproduce Scripture on their own — without depending on a professional teacher |
| Relation | The maturity outcome DBS and obedience-based discipleship are designed to produce |
| Source | [Roy Moran (roymoran.com)](https://www.roymoran.com/) |

Spiritual literacy is Moran's name for the end goal of his discipleship: ordinary Christians who can feed themselves on Scripture and pass it on, rather than remaining dependent on clergy to interpret and apply the Bible for them.
