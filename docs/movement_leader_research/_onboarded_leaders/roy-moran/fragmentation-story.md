## The Story

Roy Moran has spent thirty-plus years building one of the most unusual ministries in the North American church — a 1,000-attendee attractional congregation in Kansas City that also runs an obedience-based Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) track inside the same body. He co-founded **Shoal Creek Community Church** in 1992 with his wife **Candy Moran** and four others, originally on the Willow Creek attractional template, and served as senior pastor for twenty-five years before transitioning to the title "directional leader and spiritual literacy advocate." Somewhere in the mid-2000s he hit the existential crisis that organizes the rest of his work — *the disciples we are making are not making disciples* — and the long mentorship under **David Watson** (CityTeam International, now Beyond) that followed pulled him into the global obedience-based DMM lineage. He now serves as **Global Team Leader, Platform at New Generations** — the **Jerry Trousdale**-led network that has documented millions of disciples and hundreds of thousands of churches across Africa, Asia, and Latin America — and as **Founder and Executive Director of Mercy Alliance International**, a Kansas City hub for DMM training and hybrid coaching. Across the *Movement Conversations — Powered by New Generations* podcast (weekly since 2020), *Spent Matches* (Thomas Nelson / Refraction, 2015), and *Hybrid Church* (DMM Press, 2017), he has built the only widely-published Western on-ramp into DMM that doesn't require pastors to abandon their congregation.

That is an unusually coherent body of work — a published framework, a lived 25-year case study, a global ecosystem role, and a sustained weekly media property. And yet the picture that emerges from the dossier is of a high-output, low-capture leader whose actual influence sits at least an order of magnitude larger than the surface anyone can search. The 25-year Shoal Creek hybrid experiment — the single most valuable thing he owns — has never been written up as a longitudinal case study with real metrics (baptisms, DBS groups, four-generation depth, attendance vs. dispersion ratios). The weekly podcast catalog of 100+ episodes is scattered across Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Audible, iHeart, Stitcher, Deezer, and Podcast Addict with no canonical web home, no searchable archive, no topical index. The blog at roymoran.com is 100+ posts deep and active since ~2013 but under-traffics relative to its content depth. There is no newsletter. There is no flagship cohort or course. There is no human-narrated audiobook of *Spent Matches* — the trade-published title with the broadest reach is audio-illegible — and the 2024 *Hybrid Church* audiobook is AI-narrated through Audible's Virtual Voice rather than recorded.

In short: Roy Moran is the credentialed Western pastor with a globally-validated framework and a clear convictional center, whose expertise is distributed across at least eight platforms he doesn't own. The Movemental thesis — *fragmentation is solved by infrastructure, not new content* — describes his situation precisely. He already has the content, the network, and the conviction. What he lacks is the owned platform, the course architecture, the longitudinal data capture, and the unified video library that would let his actual influence be seen.

## Synthesis

Roy Moran's fragmentation is not a **content** problem — it is an **infrastructure and capture** problem inside a body of work that is already unusually coherent. His calling, his content, and his ecosystem all point in the same direction (Hybrid Church + obedience-based DMM in Western contexts, with global movement data flowing through New Generations); the Calling profile rates content-calling alignment at 90%. What is missing is the infrastructure that would let the body of work be seen, sequenced, and acted on: a unified owned audience (no newsletter on a 13-year content footprint), a flagship cohort (no Hybrid Church Lab despite the demand and the curriculum being mostly written across his two books, his blog, and his Mercy Alliance training), a canonical podcast archive (100+ weekly episodes scattered across at least seven distribution platforms with no searchable topical home), a video library (a single personal YouTube channel does not exist), and — most consequentially — a written longitudinal case study of the 25-year Shoal Creek hybrid experiment with the actual metrics that would convert the framework from anecdote to validated practice.

The single largest pool of trapped value is the **25-year Shoal Creek hybrid case study itself** — it is the credibility anchor of every other piece of his work, it is referenced everywhere, and it has never been quantitatively documented. The second-largest is the **100+ *Movement Conversations* episode archive** — sustained weekly DMM content for five-plus years with no canonical web home and no searchable topical index. The third is the **embodied pastoral coaching wisdom** that lives in 1:1 conversations through Mercy Alliance and informal pastor-to-pastor coffees. Roy is precisely the kind of practitioner Movemental's infrastructure is designed to consolidate: he is producing the content already, the framework is already published, the network is already built — what is missing is the owned platform layer that turns scattered output into a compounding, captured, ownable body of work.
