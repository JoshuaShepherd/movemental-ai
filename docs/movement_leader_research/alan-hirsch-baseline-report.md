# Alan Hirsch: Baseline System Analysis

A structured assessment of Alan Hirsch's current influence system — before Movemental platform moves are fully implemented. Based on quantitative audit of three repositories: docs (content corpus), alan-hirsch (platform build), and movemental-ai (platform infrastructure and research).

**Date**: 2026-04-05
**Method**: Cross-repository evidence synthesis with explicit confidence labeling
**Convention**: Evidence is cited. Estimation is labeled. Assumptions are named.

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Alan Hirsch operates one of the most structurally significant influence systems in the Christian movement/missional space — but it is almost entirely embodied, fragmented, and offline. The gap between his actual influence and his digital infrastructure is extreme.

**What exists:** 20 books across 7 publishers (21 years), 150,000+ APEST assessments taken, 7 organizations founded, 4 seminary affiliations, a network that touches virtually every node in the movement ecosystem. He is the gravitational center of this field.

**What doesn't exist:** A centralized digital platform. A content journey from assessment to formation. An email list of meaningful scale. A YouTube channel. Audiobooks for flagship titles. Courses on accessible platforms. Structured pathways. Any digital revenue infrastructure.

**The core finding:** Hirsch's system currently converts intellectual authority into embodied outcomes (speaking, consulting, organizational leadership) but leaves ~90% of his potential influence unrealized because there is no structured, accessible, retrievable digital layer. The 150,000 people who took the APEST assessment walked away with a score and no onward journey. That is the single most telling data point in this analysis.

## 2. TAM ANALYSIS

### 2.1 Current TAM (Realized)

**Estimate: 50,000 - 80,000 people**

This represents individuals who have meaningfully engaged with Hirsch's body of work — not just encountered his name, but been influenced by his frameworks or formed through his content.

**Evidence basis:**

| Signal | Count | What It Represents |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| APEST assessments completed | 150,000+ | But most are single-interaction — the assessment itself is entry-level engagement, not formation. Estimate ~30-40% engaged beyond the score = 45,000-60,000 |
| Goodreads ratings (all works) | 16,762 across 92 editions | Industry standard: 1-5% of readers rate on Goodreads. Implies 335,000-1.6M copies read across all titles over 21 years. But "read" ≠ "formed." Estimate 10-20% of readers deeply engaged = 33,000-320,000 |
| Conference attendance (cumulative) | 50,000+ | But most attend one talk, not a sustained journey. Estimate ~20% return or continue = 10,000 |
| Social media (combined) | ~58,700 | Low engagement rate; mostly broadcast. Unlikely to represent deep formation |
| Seminary course adoptions | 4 institutions | Conservatively 50-100 students/year/institution x 10 years = 2,000-4,000 seminary students |
| Forge network (active) | Unknown precisely | 30+ US cities + international. Estimate 2,000-5,000 active practitioners |
| 5Q Collective / MLC | Small, curated | Estimate 500-1,000 active participants |
| Substack subscribers | 1,500 | Negligible as formation channel at current scale |

**Synthesis:** Triangulating across these signals, the current realized TAM — people who have been meaningfully influenced by or formed through Hirsch's work — is in the range of **50,000-80,000**. The uncertainty is primarily in how many book readers move from "read" to "applied."

**Confidence: MEDIUM.** The APEST number is verified. The Goodreads-to-readers inference is standard but imprecise. Conference and organizational data is estimated from partial evidence.

### 2.2 Latent TAM (Potential)

**Estimate: 500,000 - 1,500,000 people**

This represents individuals who would meaningfully engage with Hirsch's work if it were fully structured, accessible, and activated — but who currently cannot find it, cannot access it in usable form, or do not know it exists.

**Evidence basis:**

| Signal | Count | What It Represents |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| Global search volume for movement/missional keywords | Not measured, but: "missional church" + "APEST" + "fivefold ministry" + "church multiplication" + "apostolic leadership" collectively represent a substantial long-tail search universe | People actively looking for what Hirsch has already written about — who find other sources instead |
| APEST assessment completers who received no onward journey | ~110,000-120,000 (150K minus those who continued engaging) | Demonstrated interest, zero formation pathway |
| Non-English potential | Spanish: 580M speakers, Portuguese: 260M speakers globally; these are the two highest-translation-coverage languages in the corpus | Partial translations exist but are not accessible on any platform |
| Book readers who never encountered courses, pathways, or structured engagement | 200,000+ (estimated total readership minus realized TAM) | Read the book, had no structured next step |
| Adjacent domain practitioners | ~5,000-15,000 (soul care, justice, worship, marketplace leaders who use movemental principles without knowing Hirsch's frameworks) | Would benefit from the vocabulary and structure Hirsch provides |
| Seminary students and faculty globally | Missiology is a global discipline; Hirsch is foundational reading in the field. Non-US seminaries multiply this | Currently accessible only through books — no digital course, no structured curriculum |
| Movement leaders in Global South | CPM/DMM practitioners in India, Africa, China who are doing what Hirsch describes but haven't encountered his frameworks | Hirsch co-authored the Lausanne "Reactivating the Church" paper with Victor John (Bhojpuri movement, 100M+ population). The intersection exists but is unserved |

**Synthesis:** The latent TAM is large because Hirsch's frameworks address a question millions of Christians are asking ("how do we move from institutional church to missional movement?") but his answers are locked in books, scattered across organizations, and invisible to search engines. The constraint is not demand — it's accessibility and structure.

**The clearest latent TAM signal is the 150,000 APEST assessments.** These people demonstrated behavioral interest (took a 15-30 minute assessment) and received nothing afterward. Even a 10% conversion to a structured pathway = 15,000 new engaged users. A 30% conversion = 45,000.

**Confidence: LOW-MEDIUM.** The latent TAM is inherently speculative. The APEST conversion opportunity is high-confidence. The global non-English estimate is low-confidence. The search volume inference is reasonable but unmeasured.

### 2.3 Key Constraints Suppressing TAM

Ranked by impact:

1. **No post-assessment journey.** 150,000+ people completed APEST and hit a dead end. This is the single largest TAM suppression factor.
2. **Content is locked in books.** 165 chapters across 13 titles exist as static text. Not indexed, not searchable, not recombineable, not available for AI retrieval.
3. **No owned digital platform.** alanhirsch.org is a static Squarespace site. No courses, no pathways, no structured engagement.
4. **No email infrastructure.** 37,900 Twitter followers → 1,500 Substack subscribers = 4% conversion (industry standard: 15-25%). There is no email capture, no nurture sequence, no content funnel.
5. **No audio layer.** Only 3 of 20 books on Audible. 38+ videos scattered across 20+ third-party YouTube channels with no centralized channel.
6. **Translation inaccessibility.** Spanish translations exist for 10 books, Portuguese for 4+, German/French for 1 each — but none of this is accessible on a platform. It exists as files in a repo.
7. **No AI retrieval layer.** Hirsch's ideas are not represented in any AI system. When someone asks ChatGPT about "missional church" or "APEST," Hirsch's voice is absent.

## 3. SYSTEM ANALYSIS (BY VARIABLE)

### 3.1 Access & Retrieval

**Current State: 1.5 / 5 — Severely Constrained**

The degree to which Hirsch's ideas can be found, searched, queried, recombined, and surfaced in context is extremely low.

**What exists:**
- Books on Amazon/Goodreads (discoverable but not searchable at the idea level)
- alanhirsch.org with a flat blog archive (no pillar pages, no SEO structure for mDNA, APEST, missional church)
- 5qcentral.com with the APEST assessment (functional but isolated)
- 13 articles on the alan-hirsch platform build (not yet deployed)
- Scattered articles on Saturate, Verge Network, Exponential, personal blog

**What exists in repos but is not deployed:**
- 165 book chapters digitized and structured as markdown (docs repo)
- 67 articles created (docs repo)
- 902 course files with weekly structures, assessments, playbooks (docs repo)
- 223 pathway files across 7 learning pathways (docs repo)
- 132 AI agent definitions including voice agents, formation companions, and a concierge system (docs repo + alan-hirsch repo)
- A full Next.js platform with article reader, book library, course LMS, assessment pipeline, and AI Lab chat — **all built but not yet live** (alan-hirsch repo)
- OpenAI-based chat agent with access to Hirsch's collected works, persona-aware responses, and 11 visitor persona types (alan-hirsch repo)

**Key limitations:**
- Google search for "APEST framework" or "mDNA" does not lead to any Hirsch-owned property with structured content
- No semantic indexing of the corpus — ideas cannot be cross-referenced or queried
- No AI agent is live — Hirsch's voice is absent from every AI system
- The platform that would solve all of this exists as built software but has not been deployed

**Evidence:** The alan-hirsch repo contains 73 live pages, a complete LMS, AI Lab chat, APEST assessment integration, Stripe commerce, and analytics — all production-ready on Vercel. The gap is deployment, not development. Digital presence score assessed internally at 3.2/5.

**Confidence: HIGH.** The content exists. The platform exists. The deployment gap is documented.

### 3.2 Engagement Depth

**Current State: 1.0 / 5 — Almost Nonexistent**

The degree to which a user can spend meaningful time, move through content progressively, and be transformed (not just informed) is nearly zero in Hirsch's current digital system.

**What exists:**

- APEST assessment (single interaction, ~15-30 minutes, then nothing)

- 5QCast podcast (~30 episodes, seasonal, niche — listening is passive)

- MLC gatherings (invitation-only, not digital)

**What exists in repos but is not deployed:**

- 7 learning pathways: pastoral-warm (76 files), mDNA (48), movement-intelligence (33), discipleship (22), metanoia (18), reframation (18) — each with hero statement, core question, overview, model, scripture, case studies, FAQ, practices, reflection, linked courses, resources, and glossary
- Certificate generation and CE credit tracking (built in platform)
- Cohort management with discussion threading (built in platform)
- Drip scheduling for time-gated content release (built in platform)
- Formation practices hub (built in platform)

**Key limitations:**
- There is no structured digital journey available to anyone, anywhere, right now
- A person who reads *The Forgotten Ways* and wants to go deeper has no structured next step
- The 150,000 APEST assessment takers received a score and nothing else
- Engagement is entirely event-driven (conferences, consulting) or self-directed (books)
- The courses and pathways that would change this are built but not live

**The starkest gap:** The platform contains an "Apostolic Genius" 8-week formation course (announced on the hero page of the alan-hirsch build), a complete LMS with video player, journal prompts, cohort discussions, progression rules, and certificates. But it is not deployed. A user searching for "APEST course" or "mDNA training" today will find nothing from Hirsch.

**Confidence: HIGH.** The absence of digital engagement depth is verifiable. The existence of the built-but-undeployed system is documented in the alan-hirsch repo.

**Confidence: HIGH** for organic network strength (thoroughly documented). **HIGH** for structural absence.

## 4. STRUCTURAL GAPS

The biggest gaps between current system and fully realized system, ranked by impact on TAM realization:

### Gap 1: No Post-Assessment Pathway (Impact: Critical)

**Current:** 150,000+ people take the APEST assessment → receive a score → leave
**Realized:** Assessment → personalized pathway recommendation → course enrollment → formation journey → community → ongoing engagement

This single gap represents the largest unrealized conversion in the system. The behavioral data (someone spending 15-30 minutes on an assessment) indicates genuine interest. The absence of a follow-on journey wastes this signal entirely.

**What exists in the repo:** Full assessment pipeline with dimension scoring + 7 learning pathways + 4 course structures + cohort management + formation practices hub. Everything needed to close this gap is built.

### Gap 2: Corpus Not Indexed for Retrieval (Impact: Critical)

**Current:** 165 chapters across 13 books exist as static text. Ideas cannot be searched, queried, or recombined.
**Realized:** Full corpus semantically indexed → searchable by concept, framework, and question → retrievable by AI agents → surfaceable in context for any user query

This gap suppresses both Access & Retrieval and Network Amplification simultaneously. When someone searches "what is mDNA?" or asks an AI assistant about missional church frameworks, Hirsch's corpus should be the authoritative source. Currently it is invisible.

**What exists in the repo:** The corpus is digitized (markdown files). AI agent architecture is built. The chat agent with corpus access exists. The gap is deployment + vector indexing.

### Gap 3: No Owned Digital Platform (Impact: High)

**Current:** alanhirsch.org is a static Squarespace site. Content is scattered across publisher sites, organizational sites, and third-party platforms.
**Realized:** Single owned platform with articles, books, courses, pathways, assessments, AI chat, community, and commerce — all interconnected and under one roof.

**What exists in the repo:** A complete Next.js platform with 73 pages, full LMS, AI Lab, APEST assessment, Stripe commerce, analytics, and multi-tenant architecture. Production-ready on Vercel. Not deployed.

### Gap 4: No Translation Accessibility (Impact: High for Latent TAM)

**Current:** Spanish translations of 10 books, Portuguese of 4+, German/French of 1 each — all exist as files in a repo. None accessible on any platform.
**Realized:** Multilingual content accessible through a platform with language detection, structured reading experience, and localized pathways.

The Spanish-speaking TAM alone (580M speakers, significant evangelical population in Latin America) represents a latent audience that may rival the English TAM.

### Gap 5: No Email Infrastructure (Impact: High for Engagement Depth)

**Current:** 37,900 Twitter followers → 1,500 Substack subscribers (4% conversion vs. 15-25% industry standard). No email capture, no nurture sequence, no content funnel.
**Realized:** Assessment → email capture → welcome sequence → weekly formation content → course promotion → community engagement. Target: 5,000-10,000 subscribers within 6-12 months of launch.

### Gap 6: No Audio/Video Layer (Impact: Medium-High)

**Current:** 3 of 20 books on Audible. 38+ videos on 20+ third-party channels. No personal YouTube channel. No personal podcast.
**Realized:** Centralized video library, all flagship titles on Audible, weekly podcast, short-form clips from conference archive.

## 5. SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

Which variables, if improved, would most increase TAM realization, engagement, and network effects?

### Rank 1: Access & Retrieval (Highest Sensitivity)

**Why:** This is the bottleneck. Hirsch's content exists (165 chapters, 67 articles, 902 course files, 223 pathway files) but cannot be found, searched, or retrieved. Improving access unlocks every downstream variable:
- TAM increases because new people can discover the work
- Engagement depth increases because content can be structured into journeys
- Output velocity increases because existing content can be repurposed/recombined
- Network amplification increases because structured content can be linked, cited, and indexed

**Specific highest-impact move:** Deploy the platform. This single action would take Access & Retrieval from 1.5/5 to ~3.5/5 overnight, because the platform contains articles, books, courses, pathways, assessment, AI chat, and search — all already built.

### Rank 2: Engagement Depth (High Sensitivity)

**Why:** The 150,000 APEST assessment takers represent a proven, interested audience with zero engagement pathway. Closing the assessment-to-journey gap converts latent TAM to realized TAM at a rate no other variable can match. Even a 10% conversion = 15,000 newly engaged users — roughly doubling the current realized TAM.

**Specific highest-impact move:** Connect the APEST assessment to a personalized pathway recommendation engine using the 7 built pathways and 4 course structures.

### Rank 3: Output Velocity (Medium-High Sensitivity)

**Why:** Hirsch's personal bandwidth is the production bottleneck. He cannot scale himself. But the voice emulation system (10-level tested, 5 weighted markers, rubric-scored) and content generation framework exist in the repo. Activating system-enabled production would shift output from ~1 book/2 years + occasional posts to: weekly articles derived from corpus + localized content for pathways + AI-generated responses in chat.

**Specific highest-impact move:** Activate the article generation engine (SUPPORTING_ARTICLE_GENERATION_FRAMEWORK.md) to produce 2-4 articles/week from the existing 165-chapter corpus. This alone would transform SEO discoverability.

### Rank 4: Network Amplification (Medium Sensitivity, Compounding)

**Why:** Organic network is already strong (3.5/5). The missing piece is structural legibility — making the network visible and linkable. This variable has the lowest immediate sensitivity but the highest compounding effect over time. When Hirsch's platform exists and contains structured content, every leader in the ecosystem can link to it. Every AI system can reference it. Every search query can surface it.

**Specific highest-impact move:** Launch the Movemental scenius — the multi-leader network where Hirsch's content links to Frost, Ferguson, Brisco, etc. and vice versa. This turns a hub-and-spoke personal network into a legible, searchable, compounding knowledge graph.

## 7. SYNTHESIS

Alan Hirsch's influence system is characterized by an extreme asymmetry: **massive embodied authority with near-zero digital infrastructure**.

The data reveals a system where:
- **The content exists** (165 chapters, 67 articles, 902 course files, 223 pathway files, translations in 5 languages)
- **The platform exists** (73-page Next.js application with LMS, AI, commerce, analytics — production-ready)
- **The audience exists** (150,000+ demonstrated interest via APEST assessment alone)
- **The network exists** (10+ co-authors, 7+ forewords given, 7 organizations, 4 seminary positions)

But none of these are connected to each other in a digital system.

The baseline reality is not "needs to be built." The baseline reality is "built but not deployed." This is an unusual and significant finding. The transformation moves described in the conceptual model (corpus ingestion, translation, content engine, courses, pathways, network integration) have largely been **pre-executed at the content and infrastructure level**. The gap is activation, not creation.

The single most impactful action available — deploying the existing platform and connecting the APEST assessment to the existing pathway/course infrastructure — would simultaneously improve Access & Retrieval (from 1.5 to ~3.5), Engagement Depth (from 1.0 to ~3.0), and begin generating data that would inform every subsequent move.

This is not a content problem. It is not a technology problem. It is a deployment problem.
