## Executive Summary

Alan Hirsch is one of the most influential missiologists alive, with 20 books, 7 founded organizations, 150K+ APEST assessments taken, and a network spanning 22+ podcast appearances and 24+ documented speaking events. Yet his personal digital presence scores only 3.2/5. The core gap is structural: Hirsch's enormous embodied influence -- conferences, consulting, seminary teaching, organizational leadership -- is poorly captured, centralized, or monetized digitally. He has no personal YouTube channel despite 38+ videos scattered across third-party platforms, no hosted podcast despite being a sought-after guest, a Substack with only 1,500 subscribers against 37.9K Twitter followers, and a website that lacks modern engagement and conversion features. The opportunity is not to create something from nothing -- it is to surface, connect, and amplify a massive body of existing work that is currently fragmented and under-leveraged.

## Embodied Work vs. Digital Expression

### Strong embodied work NOT captured digitally:

- **Seminary teaching**: Adjunct faculty at 4 institutions; course content is not publicly available or repurposed
- **Consulting with Redeemer City to City**: Embedded consultant work; insights stay private
- **Future Travelers cohort work**: Multi-year megachurch transformation processes; no public case studies or frameworks published from this work
- **Movement Leaders Collective gatherings**: Annual events for invitation-only network; rich content not captured publicly
- **Forge training residencies**: In-person formation experiences; the "Forge way" is experiential and difficult to capture digitally
- **One-on-one mentoring**: Hirsch mentors movement leaders through CRM and NewThing; this relational work is invisible online
- **The Tribe of LA participation**: Active community engagement among artists/creatives in Los Angeles; no digital footprint
- **International consulting**: Work with denominations (Assemblies of God NZ, Mennonite Church USA, Foursquare, UMC) generates institutional change but no public content

### Digital content NOT connected to embodied work:

- **Substack posts** exist in isolation; not linked back from organizational sites or cross-promoted through organizational channels
- **5QCast podcast** is not prominently featured on alanhirsch.org or cross-promoted on social media
- **Free eBooks** on alanhirsch.org are not connected to any email nurture sequence or content journey
- **Blog posts on Saturate, Verge, Exponential** -- content on third-party sites not aggregated or linked from personal site

### Integration opportunities:

1. **Post-event content capture**: After every conference talk, publish a summary article + video clip + social media thread within 48 hours
2. **Consulting-to-content pipeline**: Anonymize and publish frameworks, case studies, and insights from Future Travelers and Redeemer City to City consulting work
3. **Assessment-to-learning pathway**: After APEST assessment, route users into a content journey (articles, videos, courses) based on their profile
4. **Forge formation content**: Capture and publish elements of the Forge training experience as digital content (video series, reflection guides, cohort curricula)
5. **MLC gathering content**: Record and publish selected sessions from Movement Leaders Collective gatherings
6. **Seminary lecture capture**: Record and adapt seminary lectures as publicly available teaching content (video series, articles)
