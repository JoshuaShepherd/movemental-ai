## Key Observations

1. **No personal YouTube channel**: Unlike many contemporary Christian leaders, Alan Hirsch does not maintain a personal YouTube channel. His video presence is entirely through third-party platforms and conference recordings.

2. **Verge Conference dominance**: The Verge Network/Conference is the single largest source of his video content, with 10+ clips from conference appearances (~2010-2013 era).

3. **Exponential as recurring platform**: Hirsch has spoken at Exponential conferences repeatedly (2014, 2016, 2017, 2019, 2020, 2022), making it his most consistent conference relationship.

4. **Podcast era shift**: Post-2020, his video appearances have shifted heavily toward podcast video formats (Fresh Expressions, Church Digital, Everyday Disciple, Ephesiology, Canadian Church Leaders, Microchurches).

5. **alanhirsch.org/videos**: His personal website aggregates video content but does not appear to host original video content directly.

6. **5Q Central / theforgottenways.org**: These owned platforms contain video resources but much content is behind registration or purchase.

7. **Signature themes across all videos**: Missional church, APEST/5Q fivefold ministry, discipleship vs. consumerism, communitas, apostolic movements, Ephesians 4, Reframation/Metanoia.

8. **International reach**: Content appears on Australian (Exponential Australia, Morling College), UK (Anthony Delaney/LAUNCH), Canadian (Canadian Church Leaders), and US platforms.

9. **Morling College video series**: The 6-session "Missiology To Go: Fivefold Leadership" course with the Tinsley Institute represents his most structured video teaching content.

10. **Awakenings 2023 plenary**: The "Metanoia" talk is notable as a major recent conference appearance, with video available for purchase through Missio Alliance.
