## The Story

Alan Hirsch has published **20 books across 7 publishers over 21 years**. His [Goodreads author page](https://goodreads.com/author/show/56757.Alan_Hirsch) lists **92 editions** with **16,762 total ratings** — which, by the industry heuristic that 1–5% of readers rate, implies somewhere between 335,000 and 1.6 million copies read. Over **150,000 people have taken his APEST assessment**. He has founded or co-founded **seven organizations** (100 Movements, Forge, Forge America, 5Q Collective, Movement Leaders Collective, Future Travelers, Shapevine) and holds adjunct or associate faculty appointments at **four accredited seminaries** (Wheaton, Fuller, Asbury, George Fox). By any measure of reach, he is the gravitational center of the global missional movement.

And virtually none of that is retrievable as a system. His books live on Amazon, Goodreads, IVP, Baker/Brazos, Audible, Everand, OverDrive, Google Books, Christianbook, and seven more retailers — but the books themselves are static text; there is no AI retrieval layer, no chapter-level search, no index of the ideas inside them. The [alanhirsch.org](https://www.alanhirsch.org) homepage is a Squarespace site with a blog, a books page, a free-ebooks page, a tests page, and an FAQ — no prominent newsletter signup, no video hub, no course library, no structured pathway connecting his 20 books to anything a reader could do next. The APEST assessment, which 150,000 people have completed, lives on [theforgottenways.org](https://theforgottenways.org) and [apest.5qcentral.com](https://apest.5qcentral.com) — and when someone finishes it, they get a score and no onward journey. **That single dead end is the most telling data point in the entire system.**

His audio presence is 35+ items scattered across 22+ unique podcast feeds — **5QCast is his only hosted podcast**; the other 28+ appearances are guest spots on Everyday Disciple, Shifting Culture, Fresh Expressions, Ephesiology, The Church Digital, Stetzer ChurchLeaders, DTS Table, Venture 12, Godspacelight, C4SO, Voxology, LAUNCH, Post-Christian Podcast, and more. His video presence is 38+ clips distributed across **20+ third-party channels** — Verge Network (10+ clips), Exponential (5+), ChurchLeaders (4+), Missio Alliance, Anthony Delaney, Fresh Expressions, Canopy International, Morling College, Asbury Seminary, and on. **He has no personal YouTube channel.** His social footprint is substantial on the two platforms he cares about (Twitter/X: ~37,900 followers, Facebook: ~20,800 combined across two pages), effectively absent on the two he doesn't (Instagram: one unverified handle, TikTok: nothing). His email list is **1,500 subscribers on Substack at [alanhirsch1.substack.com](https://alanhirsch1.substack.com)** — 4% of his Twitter follower count, against an industry baseline of 15–25% — and the newsletter posts irregularly. Audiobooks exist for **3 of his 20 books**; *The Forgotten Ways*, *5Q*, *The Shaping of Things to Come*, *ReJesus*, and *The Faith of Leap* — his most important titles — are print and e-book only.

So the portrait is this: a founding figure of modern missional ecclesiology whose 20 books are indexed everywhere and searchable nowhere, whose 150,000 assessment completions produced no onward pathway, whose 38+ videos have no home, whose audiobook catalogue is 15% complete, whose email list is a tenth of what it should be, and whose Spanish, Portuguese, German, and French translations exist as files in a repo rather than content on a platform. The fragmentation is not caused by absence of work — it is caused by the work being trapped in book-shaped, retailer-shaped, and organization-shaped containers that don't talk to each other.

## Synthesis

Alan Hirsch converts intellectual authority into embodied outcomes — speaking, consulting, organization-founding — at near-singular scale. But the system leaks **~90% of its potential reach** before it gets to anyone who could be formed by it. A concrete picture: 150,000 APEST completions leading to 1,500 Substack subscribers (a 1% onward-journey conversion); 37,900 Twitter followers leading to 1,500 Substack subscribers (4% vs. 15–25% industry baseline); 38+ videos distributed across 20+ channels with zero personal YouTube home; 20 books of which only 3 have Audible audiobooks; translations in four languages existing as files, not content. The single largest pool of trapped value is the **APEST dead-end** — 150,000 people who demonstrated behavioral interest and received no structured pathway; a 10% conversion would be 15,000 new engaged users, a 30% conversion would be 45,000. The second-largest pool is his **cohort-and-seminary teaching content** — years of material at Wheaton, Fuller, Asbury, George Fox, and inside MLC / 100M / Forge / Future Travelers that is visible only to the people in the room.
