## Key Observations

1. **Founder-leader pattern**: Alan Hirsch's primary organizational mode is founding and leading organizations rather than serving on external boards. His network is built around organizations he created.

2. **Ecosystem coherence**: The founded organizations form a coherent ecosystem -- 100 Movements provides the vision, MLC develops leaders, 5Q provides the APEST framework, Forge provides training, Future Travelers targets large churches, and Shapevine provides the resource platform.

3. **Rich Robinson partnership**: Rich Robinson is a key co-leader across multiple Hirsch organizations (100M, MLC), serving as the operational leader while Alan provides thought leadership.

4. **CRM as institutional home**: Church Resource Ministries serves as Alan's formal US employer and fundraising vehicle, enabling his work across all other organizations.

5. **Academic legitimacy**: The Wheaton M.A. program co-founding and adjunct positions at three seminaries provide academic credibility for his practitioner-oriented work.

6. **Publisher depth**: Alan has published with at least five publishers and edits two book series (IVP Forge line, Baker Shapevine series), indicating significant influence in Christian publishing.

7. **Australian roots, US base**: Born and formed in Australia (Churches of Christ, SMRC, Forge Australia), now primarily US-based through CRM and American expressions of his organizations.
