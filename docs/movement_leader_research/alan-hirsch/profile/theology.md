## Core Thesis

The Western church has lost the essential DNA of apostolic movements -- what Hirsch calls "Apostolic Genius" -- and must recover these forgotten ways to become again the dynamic, multiplying, Jesus-centered movement it was designed to be. The church is not fundamentally an institution but a movement, and recovering its missional DNA requires a paradigm shift from Christendom-mode maintenance to apostolic-mode multiplication.

## Theological Tradition

**Primary**: Missional-Incarnational, with roots in the Stone-Campbell Restoration Movement (Churches of Christ in Australia)

**Secondary influences**: Anabaptist-influenced ecclesiology, Jewish-Hebraic thought (from personal heritage), post-Christendom missiology (Newbigin school), charismatic/Pentecostal movement dynamics (studied rather than claimed as personal tradition)

**Denominational context**: Ordained/credentialed within the Churches of Christ in Australia (Victoria and Tasmania Conference). Not bound to a single tradition; works across denominational lines.

## Key Frameworks & Models

| Framework | Description | Where Developed |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **mDNA (Missional DNA)** | Six interdependent elements of "Apostolic Genius" that characterize every significant Jesus movement: (1) Jesus is Lord, (2) Disciple Making, (3) Missional-Incarnational Impulse, (4) Apostolic Environment (APEST), (5) Organic Systems, (6) Communitas (Liminality-Communitas). "Jesus is Lord" gates overall maturity. | *The Forgotten Ways* (2006), *The Forgotten Ways Handbook* (2009) |
| **APEST / 5Q** | Five-fold ministry gifts from Ephesians 4:11 -- Apostles, Prophets, Evangelists, Shepherds, Teachers -- understood not just as leadership roles but as the "original intelligence and capacity" of the Body of Christ. All five are needed for a fully functioning church. | *The Permanent Revolution* (2012), *5Q* (2017), *Activating 5Q* (2018) |
| **Incarnational Mission** | Mission follows the pattern of the Incarnation: God enters culture rather than extracting people from it. Missional engagement means "living among" not "reaching out to." Contextual, relational, embedded, everyday. | *The Shaping of Things to Come* (2003) |
| **Metanoia** | Repentance understood as fundamental paradigm-level transformation that changes everything -- individuals, churches, organizations -- from the inside out. Not merely moral reform but cognitive-spiritual revolution. | *Metanoia* (2019) |
| **Movemental Thinking** | The distinction between institution and movement as organizing paradigms for the church. Movements are organic, multiplicative, network-structured, virus-like in spread. Institutions are centralized, controlling, maintenance-focused. | Throughout all works; central to *The Forgotten Ways*, *On the Verge* |
| **Christology > Missiology > Ecclesiology** | A cascading theological sequence: who Jesus is determines how we do mission, which determines what the church looks like. Get Christology wrong and everything downstream is distorted. | *ReJesus* (2008/2009) |
| **Theo-genetic codes** | The concept that ministry types (APEST) function like genetic codes within the Body of Christ -- each containing distinct "intelligence" that the church needs to be healthy and complete. | *The Permanent Revolution* (2012) |
| **Reframation** | The process of changing mental frameworks to transform understanding of God, people, and mission. Re-enchanting worldview through fresh theological lenses. | *Reframation* (2018) |

## Distinctive Positions

- **APEST is for all Christians, not just leaders**: The five-fold gifts are not merely leadership offices but callings distributed across the entire Body of Christ. Every believer has an APEST profile.
- **The Western church has essentially exiled three of five ministries**: Apostolic, Prophetic, and Evangelistic functions have been marginalized in favor of Shepherd and Teacher, creating an imbalanced, maintenance-oriented church.
- **"Jesus is Lord" gates everything**: In the mDNA framework, no element can rise above the church's Christological maturity. If "Jesus is Lord" is at Level 2, the whole system is capped at Level 2.
- **Movement over institution is normative**: The New Testament church was a movement, not an institution. Institutionalization is deviation from the apostolic norm, not maturation toward it.
- **Every believer is a missionary**: Mission is not a department of the church or a role for specialists; it is the fundamental identity of every follower of Jesus in their sphere of influence.
- **Hebraic vs. Hellenistic thought**: Hirsch consistently distinguishes between Hebraic (holistic, embodied, dynamic) and Hellenistic (dualistic, abstract, static) modes of thinking, arguing the Western church has been too shaped by the latter.
- **Organic systems over organizational complexity**: Simple, reproducible patterns that can multiply are preferred over complex, centralized structures that require professional management.
- **Communitas through liminality**: Authentic community (communitas) is forged through shared ordeal and risk, not through comfort and programs. The church needs adventure and liminality to experience true fellowship.

## Intellectual Genealogy

**Shaped by**:
- **Lesslie Newbigin** -- Missional theology, the church as sign/instrument/foretaste of the kingdom, post-Christendom missiology
- **Roland Allen** -- Organic, Spirit-led mission; indigenous church principles; critique of Western missionary methods
- **Dietrich Bonhoeffer** -- Costly discipleship, religionless Christianity, the church's role in the world
- **Victor Turner** -- Anthropological concepts of liminality and communitas (adapted for theological use)
- **Howard Snyder** -- Radical renewal movements in church history
- **Chinese house church movement / early church movements** -- Studied as living examples of Apostolic Genius in action
- **Jewish-Hebraic tradition** -- Personal heritage; Shema spirituality; embodied faith; Jesus as Jewish Messiah

**In conversation with**:
- Michael Frost (closest collaborator; incarnational mission)
- Tim Catchim (apostolic ministry and APEST theory)
- Dave Ferguson (megachurch transformation; multiplication)
- Ed Stetzer (missiological research; church planting)
- Lance Ford (practical missional living; decentralized leadership)
- Neil Cole (organic church; simple church movements)
- Reggie McNeal (missional leadership; church culture change)
- Todd Hunter (Anglican missional bishop; C4SO network)

**Pushes back against**:
- **Christendom model**: The assumption that the church occupies a central, privileged position in culture. Hirsch argues this era is over and the church must learn to be missional rather than attractional.
- **Attractional church paradigm**: The "come and see" model where the church builds programs to attract people. Hirsch advocates "go and be" incarnational presence instead.
- **Shepherd-Teacher dominance**: The reduction of church leadership to only pastoral care and teaching, which Hirsch argues truncates the Body of Christ and eliminates apostolic, prophetic, and evangelistic energy.
- **Institutional preservation**: The tendency to make the institution's survival the primary goal, rather than multiplication of mission.
- **Hellenistic dualism**: The separation of sacred/secular, spiritual/physical that Hirsch sees as a distortion imported from Greek philosophy rather than reflecting biblical (Hebraic) holism.

## Theological Evolution

Hirsch's theology has been remarkably consistent in its core convictions since his first major publication in 2003, but has deepened and expanded in specific areas:

1. **Early phase (2003-2006)**: Foundational missional-incarnational theology developed with Michael Frost in *The Shaping of Things to Come*. *The Forgotten Ways* established the mDNA framework as the core intellectual contribution.

2. **APEST deepening (2012-2018)**: From general mention of "apostolic environment" as one mDNA element, Hirsch developed APEST into a comprehensive framework with its own assessment tools, training programs, and organizational applications. The shift from *The Forgotten Ways* to *5Q* represents a significant deepening of what was originally one element among six.

3. **Transformation/inner change emphasis (2018-2020)**: *Metanoia* and *Reframation* signal a turn toward the interior dimensions of transformation -- how paradigm shifts actually happen at the cognitive and spiritual level. This complements the earlier structural/organizational focus.

4. **Movement scaling (2016-present)**: The founding of 100Movements represents a shift from theoretical writing to active organizational catalysis of movements globally.

Throughout, "Jesus is Lord" has remained the non-negotiable center, and the fundamental conviction that the church is a movement (not an institution) has never wavered.
