# Books & Authored Contributions: Andrew Jones (Tall Skinny Kiwi)

Andrew Jones has no verified solo-authored trade book. His primary corpus is his blog (tallskinnykiwi.com), supplemented by his podcast and his speaking. This file records authored contributions — chapters and substantial standalone works — that can be reasonably grounded. Where a contribution rests on a single secondary source, it is marked MEDIUM or LOW confidence and should be confirmed against the publisher before being treated as fact. No ISBNs are asserted, because none could be verified for his contributions.

> NOTE: Do not attribute *The Prodigal Project: Journey into the Emerging Church* (SPCK, 2000) to Andrew Jones. That book was written by Mike Riddell, Mark Pierson, and Cathy Kirkpatrick. It is part of the same New Zealand emerging-church milieu Jones came out of, but he is not a credited author.

### *Tall Skinny Kiwi* — the blog as primary corpus

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Type | Long-form blog corpus (treated here as his principal authored body of work) |
| Published | 2003 |
| Confidence | HIGH (corpus exists; 2003 is the earliest archived blog year located, not a definitive launch date for all writing) |

**Description**: Jones's two-decade blog is his real book. Begun in the early 2000s (his first internet space dates to 1997; the earliest archived blog posts located run from 2003), it amounts to a serialized, real-time history of the emerging and missional church told through essays, field reports, recommendations, and reflection. Landmark entries include his multi-part "Emerging Church Definition" essays (2004), his retrospective "Emerging Church Movement (1989-2009)?", and his 2010 "Goodbyes to Emergent Village." Treated as a corpus, it is among the most-read and most-cited bodies of emerging-church writing online and is the natural candidate for being aggregated, curated, and republished as authored work.
