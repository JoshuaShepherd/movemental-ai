# Frameworks: Andrew Jones (Tall Skinny Kiwi)

These are the recurring conceptual frameworks that run across Andrew Jones's two-decade blog corpus, podcast, and speaking. Each is grounded in his documented writing and talks; where a single canonical permalink could not be verified the Source link is left as `—`.

### Rhizome Church Planting

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Recurring across the *Tall Skinny Kiwi* blog (2000s–present) |
| Components | Horizontal root-system spread; "spreading wide rather than building high"; diffusion of ideas, values, beliefs and action across a network; the World Wide Web as architectural analogue (drawing on Deleuze and rhizome biology) |
| Source | — |

The church is better understood as a rhizome than a hierarchy — multiplying by spreading wide across a network rather than by building high around a single center.

### Emerging Church as Mindset (not Style)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2004 (*"Emerging Church Definition 1.0 / 2.0"*) |
| Components | "A radically different mindset, value system and worldview"; resistance to tidy definitions; emerging culture as the context, not a worship aesthetic |
| Source | [Emerging Church Definition 1.0](https://tallskinnykiwi.typepad.com/tallskinnykiwi/2004/02/emerging_church.html) |

Emerging church was never primarily a style or a worship format but a radically different mindset, value system, and worldview — and the people inside it often neither want nor need a neat definition.

### Mission Begins at Home

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Recurring; tied to his "The Church Comes Home" chapter contribution |
| Components | Home countries treated as mission fields; the prodigal / journey-home motif; the ordinary and local as the starting point of mission |
| Source | — |

Mission begins in the local and the ordinary — one's "home" country is a mission field no less than any overseas one.

### Blogging and Digital Culture as Native Mission Terrain

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Recurring across the blog from 1997/2003 onward |
| Components | Real-time chronicling of a movement; link-rich, generous narration; treating the internet as habitable terrain rather than a threat to be managed; the blog itself as serialized authored corpus |
| Source | [Tall Skinny Kiwi blog](https://tallskinnykiwi.com/) |

Digital culture is not a threat to be managed but native terrain for the gospel, to be inhabited generously, creatively, and in public.

### The Metaverse as Heaven's Frontier

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2022 (Greenbelt talk + blog series) |
| Components | Virtual, augmented and meta "realities" as sites of spiritual experience and religious rite; Web3 and the metaverse as new mission opportunity; "in the metaverse as in heaven" |
| Source | [In The Metaverse As In Heaven (Greenbelt)](https://www.greenbelt.org.uk/talks/in-the-metaverse-as-in-heaven/) |

Virtual and metaverse spaces are an emerging frontier for incarnational mission — a place where the church should be present and prepared rather than absent or afraid.

### The Centrist Connector

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Recurring; crystallized in his 2009–2010 retrospectives |
| Components | Non-partisan bridge across a fractious movement; candid narration of a movement's history and decline; pointing readers to others' work; "Emerging Church Movement (1989–2009)?" and the 2010 departure from Emergent Village |
| Source | [Emerging Church Movement (1989–2009)?](https://tallskinnykiwi.typepad.com/tallskinnykiwi/2009/12/emerging-church-movement-1989---2009.html) |

His distinctive posture is the centrist connector — holding relationships across factions and narrating a movement's whole arc, including its decline, candidly rather than defensively.
