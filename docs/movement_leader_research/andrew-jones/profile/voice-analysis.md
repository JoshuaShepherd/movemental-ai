# Voice Analysis: Andrew Jones (Tall Skinny Kiwi)

| Attribute | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Tone | Witty, curious, mischievous, prophetic, generous |
| Register | Conversational blogger / practitioner-storyteller |
| Posture | Centrist connector — non-partisan bridge across a fractious movement |
| Stance | Exploratory and forward-looking; allergic to dogmatism and tidy definitions |
| Audience | Practitioners — church planters, mission workers, emerging-culture pioneers, fellow bloggers |

His prose is informal, fast, and link-rich — written in the native idiom of early blogging, where a post might mix theology, travel, a recommended read, and a joke in the same breath. He writes from concrete places (a campervan, a flat in Haight-Ashbury, an island in Orkney) and lets the global vantage do the work rather than abstract argument. He is more likely to point you toward someone else's work than to centre his own, and he narrates movements — including their decline — candidly rather than defensively.

**Recurring phrases**: "tall skinny kiwi", "emerging church", "impact entrepreneurs", "third sector", "grass roots", "spreading wide rather than building high", "innovations in church, mission and religion", "in the metaverse as in heaven", "a radically different mindset, value system and worldview"

**Favorite metaphors**:
- **The rhizome** — the church as a horizontally-spreading root system rather than a vertically-built hierarchy (drawing on Deleuze and on rhizome biology, and likened to the architecture of the World Wide Web)
- **The prodigal / the journey home** — mission and faith as a journey back, and "home" as itself a mission field
- **Roots spreading wide rather than building high** — multiplication by diffusion and network rather than by central scale
- **The metaverse as heaven's frontier** — digital and virtual space as native terrain for incarnational mission
- **The traveler / nomad** — the mobile, campervan-and-island life as a posture toward ministry and culture
