## Executive Summary

Brad Brisco is a practitioner-theologian whose work centers on a single animating conviction: the church is missionary by nature, not merely by activity, because it participates in the mission of a missionary God (missio Dei). Currently serving as Director of Multiplication Strategies for NAMB/Send Network, he operates at a unique intersection — translating post-Christendom missional theology (Newbigin, Bosch, Frost, Hirsch) into the language and systems of mainstream evangelical church planting within the Southern Baptist Convention.

His distinctive contribution is the **covocational framework** — a theological and practical reframing of marketplace work as a primary vehicle for mission rather than a compromise or stepping stone to fully-funded ministry. Across 5 books published between 2012 and 2021 (3 co-authored with primary collaborator Lance Ford), Brisco has built a progressive body of work: from foundational missional vocabulary (*Missional Essentials*) through sustainable practice (*The Missional Quest*) to neighborhood theology (*Next Door as It Is in Heaven*) to systematic paradigm shifts (*ReThink*) to his mature synthesis (*Covocational Church Planting*).

Despite strong institutional reach through NAMB/Send Network, Forge America, and the Sentralized Conference, Brisco's personal digital presence is notably underdeveloped. He has no newsletter, no personal YouTube channel, no online courses, no audiobooks, and no owned audience list. His expertise is distributed across 10+ platforms he doesn't control. The gap between his embodied influence and digital expression is precisely what Movemental solves.

His network — anchored by Lance Ford (co-author on 3 books), Ed Stetzer (foreword writer, New Churches platform), and Alan Hirsch (Forge network, endorser) — represents a direct pipeline to additional Movemental authors. The covocational ministry space is growing rapidly with no dominant digital platform, positioning Brisco to own that niche.
