## Executive Summary

Brad Brisco is a deeply credentialed missional theologian (doctorate, 5 books, 18+ years teaching, NAMB director) whose digital presence dramatically under-represents his embodied expertise. His content is fragmented across 10+ organizational platforms he does not own, he has no email newsletter, no personal YouTube channel, no online course on any major platform, and no audiobooks. The single biggest structural gap is **audience ownership** -- his reach flows almost entirely through institutional channels (NAMB, Send Network, New Churches, Exponential), meaning he does not control the relationship with the people consuming his work. The second is **format poverty** -- despite a rich corpus of conference keynotes, 18+ years of college lectures, and 25+ podcast appearances, almost none of this has been repurposed into scalable digital formats (courses, short-form video, newsletters, audiobooks).

## Embodied Work vs. Digital Expression

### Strong embodied work NOT captured digitally:

1. **18+ years of college-level teaching** -- Courses on History of Christianity, Religion in America, Life of Paul, Theology of Place, Launching Apostolic Movements, and Worship have no digital footprint whatsoever. This is his single largest untapped content asset.
2. **20+ years of church planting strategy in Kansas City** -- Decades of hands-on church planting coaching and strategy work with zero case studies, testimonials, or documented stories published digitally.
3. **Forge Kansas City training cohorts** -- In-person missional training content delivered through Forge KC has minimal digital capture beyond the single "Engage Your City" course listing.
4. **Sentralized Conference content** -- A recurring conference he co-founded with Lance Ford, but no recordings, recaps, or content from these events appears online.
5. **Pastoral mentoring and relationships** -- His relational ministry (evidenced by endorsements and collaborator network) is entirely invisible digitally.

### Digital content NOT connected to embodied work:

1. **Twitter/X quotes** -- His quote-sharing habit (Steinbeck, L'Engle, Wendell Berry, Carl Jung) reflects his reading life but is disconnected from any structured content strategy or call to action.
2. **NAMB organizational articles** -- Written for the organization's content needs, not always connected to his personal teaching narrative or growth.
3. **Free eBooks** -- Distributed through NAMB channels without connecting readers to his personal ecosystem.

### Integration opportunities:

1. **Teaching-to-course pipeline**: Record current or future semesters of his college courses and package them as Movemental courses with his existing syllabi.
2. **Conference recording strategy**: Ensure all future Sentralized Conference and speaking engagements are recorded for digital distribution.
3. **Church planting case studies**: Document the Kansas City church planting stories as a written/video series that brings his practitioner credibility online.
4. **Quote curation with context**: Turn his Twitter quote-sharing into a newsletter feature where he adds 200 words of personal reflection to each quote -- bridging his reading life with his teaching voice.
5. **Forge cohort digitization**: Record one full Forge KC cohort as a model for replicable online training.
