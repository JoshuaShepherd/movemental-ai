## Audience Profile

### Who Is the Audience?

**Primary audience**: Church planters and pastors exploring bivocational/covocational ministry models -- particularly those within the Southern Baptist Convention and broader evangelical tradition who are planting or leading smaller, mission-oriented churches without full-time funding.

**Secondary audience**: Established church leaders and pastors seeking to shift their existing congregations toward a more missional posture -- those wrestling with post-Christendom realities and looking for theological frameworks and practical tools for transformation.

**Tertiary audience**: Seminary students, denominational strategists, and missional training leaders who consume academic-practitioner content on missional ecclesiology, church multiplication, and neighborhood engagement.

### Audience Demographics (Estimated)

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Primary role | Church planter / Bivocational pastor / Missional strategist |
| Age range | 28-55 (core), skewing slightly older than typical church planting audiences due to covocational focus |
| Education | Seminary-educated or in seminary (M.Div. or equivalent); many with additional marketplace degrees |
| Denominational breadth | Moderate -- anchored in SBC/Send Network but extends to Nazarene (Foundry Publishing), CGGC, and broadly evangelical/missional traditions |
| Geographic concentration | US-centric, with strongest concentration in the South and Midwest (SBC footprint); some international reach through Forge network |
| Gender split | Predominantly male (~75-80%), consistent with church planting demographics in SBC-adjacent contexts |

## Movemental Audience Implications

- **Content format priority**: Free e-books and short-form written content (blog-length articles) should be prioritized first -- this is how Brisco's audience already consumes his work at scale. A hosted podcast or audio series would fill the largest content gap. Video courses are a secondary priority.

- **Topic focus**: Covocational/bivocational church planting is his signature differentiator and should anchor his Movemental presence. Missional ecclesiology and neighborhood engagement are strong secondary topics. Microchurches is a growing topic with rising audience interest.

- **Cross-pollination**: Strong overlap with Alan Hirsch's audience (Forge network connection, shared missional theology vocabulary). Lance Ford's audience is nearly identical. Hugh Halter and JR Woodward share significant audience segments. Ed Stetzer provides upstream amplification through NAMB/Send Network.

- **Growth opportunity**: The bivocational/covocational church planting niche is growing rapidly -- NAMB data shows 60%+ of new church planters are bivocational. Brisco is the leading voice in this space with limited competition. His audience is likely to grow as this trend accelerates. An audiobook of "Covocational Church Planting" and a dedicated podcast would significantly expand his reach.

- **Monetization signal**: Moderate willingness to pay. His core audience buys books ($15-25 range) and attends conferences ($100-300 range). However, his two most-distributed works (ReThink, Covocational Church Planting e-book) are free via NAMB, which sets an expectation of free content. A course or membership model ($50-150/year) is viable if it provides community access and practical tools beyond what free resources offer. The SBC/NAMB pipeline of 200+ newly endorsed church planters per year represents a built-in acquisition channel.
