### Key Observations

1. **Organizational distribution model**: Brad Brisco's content primarily flows through organizational channels (NAMB/Send Network, New Churches, Exponential, Outreach Magazine) rather than owned platforms. This is effective for reach but means he doesn't own the audience relationship.

2. **Book-centric thought leadership**: His 5 books (3 co-authored with Lance Ford) are well-distributed across Amazon, Goodreads, and Christian retailers. The free e-books (ReThink, Covocational Church Planting) through Send Network serve as effective lead magnets even without a personal newsletter.

3. **Podcast circuit presence**: Very active as a podcast guest with recent appearances in 2025. This is his strongest "media" channel beyond his blog.

4. **Newsletter opportunity**: The absence of an email newsletter is the most notable gap. His blog content and quote-sharing style on Twitter would translate well to a newsletter format.

5. **No video or course strategy**: Despite 18+ years of teaching experience and doctoral-level expertise, there is no discoverable online course or video content strategy. This represents both a gap and an opportunity.
