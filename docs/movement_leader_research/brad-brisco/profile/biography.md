## Narrative Biography

Brad Brisco is one of the leading voices in the North American missional church movement. As Director of Multiplication Strategies for the Send Network (a ministry of the North American Mission Board), he shapes church planting strategy at a national level, with a particular emphasis on missional communities, covocational ministry, and incarnational presence. He is a prolific author, having co-written several foundational books on missional living with Lance Ford, and he runs the influential Missional Church Network website and blog.

Brisco's path to ministry was unconventional. Before entering seminary, he worked in the restaurant industry — an experience that would later inform his deep conviction that the marketplace is not secondary to ministry but an essential context for mission. He earned an undergraduate degree in health care administration before pursuing a Master of Divinity and eventually a doctorate in missional ecclesiology, with his doctoral thesis focused on helping existing congregations transition toward a missional identity. As of 2025, he is also pursuing a Doctor of Ministry at Kairos University.

Over a career spanning more than two decades in church planting, Brisco has served as a church planting strategist for a network of churches in Kansas City, co-founded Forge Kansas City (a missional training center), co-founded the Sentralized Conference with Lance Ford, and served on the national leadership team for Forge America Mission Training Network. He has also taught college-level courses for more than eighteen years across subjects including History of Christianity, Religion in America, Life of Paul, Discipleship and Evangelism, Theology of Place, and Launching Apostolic Movements.

His intellectual awakening to the missional movement came in 2003, when he read "The Shaping of Things to Come" by Michael Frost and Alan Hirsch — a book he credits with fundamentally changing the way he thought about church, mission, and church planting. That pivotal encounter set him on a trajectory that would lead to five co-authored and solo books, dozens of articles across platforms like New Churches, ChurchLeaders, Send Institute, and Missio Alliance, and frequent podcast appearances where he articulates a vision of the church as inherently missionary.

## Origin & Formation

Brad Brisco's early professional life was spent in the restaurant industry, a background he rarely discusses in detail but which clearly shaped his later emphasis on the dignity of marketplace callings and the covocational model of ministry. He holds an undergraduate degree in health care administration — another indication that his path to vocational ministry was neither linear nor expected.

His seminary training (Master of Divinity) and subsequent doctoral work in missional ecclesiology grounded him theologically, but it was the encounter with Frost and Hirsch's work that catalyzed his distinctive missional orientation. His doctoral thesis — on assisting existing congregations in transitioning toward a missional direction — reflects the practical, change-oriented bent that characterizes all his work.

## Career & Ministry Arc

Brisco's career has been defined by two parallel tracks: church planting practice and missional thought leadership. On the practice side, he spent over twenty years as a church planting strategist in Kansas City, recruiting, training, and coaching planters. He co-founded Forge Kansas City as a local missional training hub, and co-founded the Sentralized Conference with Lance Ford as a gathering point for the missional movement.

On the thought leadership side, his partnership with Lance Ford proved extraordinarily productive. Together they authored "Missional Essentials" (a widely-used 12-week small group curriculum), "The Missional Quest: Becoming a Church of the Long Run" (InterVarsity Press, part of the Forge Partnership Books series with a foreword by Ed Stetzer), and "Next Door As It Is In Heaven" (NavPress). As a solo author, he wrote the influential free e-book "ReThink: 9 Key Paradigm Shifts for Activating the Church" (later expanded to "12 Missiological Principles for Church Multiplication") and "Covocational Church Planting: Aligning Your Marketplace Calling & the Mission of God."

His role at the North American Mission Board evolved from Director of Bivocational Church Planting to Director of Multiplication Strategies for the Send Network, reflecting both a personal promotion and a broadening of his strategic portfolio.

## Current Focus

Brisco now lives in Treasure Island, Florida, with his wife and three children. His current work centers on multiplication strategies for the Send Network, with a strong emphasis on covocational church planting — the model where a church planter's primary vocation is in the marketplace, and they never intend to leave it. He continues to write for his Missional Church Network blog, contribute articles to platforms like Send Institute and New Churches, and appear on podcasts. He is pursuing a Doctor of Ministry degree at Kairos University. In late 2024, his family was impacted by Hurricane Helene in Treasure Island.
