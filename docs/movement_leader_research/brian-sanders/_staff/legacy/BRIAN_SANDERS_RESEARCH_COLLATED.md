## Editorial bio

Brian Sanders is an American social entrepreneur, author, and the founder of the **Underground Network** (Tampa Underground) — a network of microchurches in Tampa, Florida that has become a leading reference model for the microchurch movement in the West. He and his family moved into the poorest urban neighborhood of his hometown, Tampa, to start an intentional community, and he has lived in inner-city intentional community with his wife **Monica** and their **six children** for more than two decades. His formation ran through collegiate ministry: a BA in Communication from the University of Florida, an MA in Religious Studies from the University of South Florida, and an MTh in Applied Theology from the University of Wales / Spurgeon's College, alongside years as the **State Director for InterVarsity Christian Fellowship in Florida** and founder/director of the InterVarsity **Tampa Urban Project**.[^ivp][^moody]

In the **summer of 2006**, roughly 50 people left their traditional churches to form seven house-based church groups — the beginning of the Underground. Frustrated with attractional, program-centered church, Sanders helped design a structure "streamlined for mission," organized not around a weekly gathering but around the callings of individuals to outward ministry — to the poor, the lost, and biblical justice. What began with 50 people became a network of **100+ microchurches** in Tampa (earlier reports cited 136, "over 200," and cumulative totals into the hundreds), with **15–30+ sister movements** in cities and countries around the world.[^outreach][^ugn]

His magnum opus is the pairing of *The Underground Church* (Zondervan, 2018, with a foreword/introduction by **Alan Hirsch**) — the narrative of the ten-year experiment — and *Microchurches: A Smaller Way* (Underground Media, 2019), the operational ecclesiology that defines a microchurch as "the coalescing of three intentional practices: worship, community, and mission." He has since stepped out of the Executive Director seat (now held by Joel Repic), remaining founder and board member, and moved into venture/philanthropic roles including **Underground Venture Studio** and **VP of Impact Alliances at the National Christian Foundation Tampa Bay**.[^linkedin][^whoweare]

## Frameworks

### Microchurch ("a smaller way")

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2019 (*Microchurches: A Smaller Way*); developed from the Underground experiment begun 2006 |
| Components | A microchurch is "the coalescing of three intentional practices: **worship, community, and mission**" — the "ecclesial minimum"; the smallest and most common expression of the church, reproducible and mission-organized |
| Visual / model | — (no formal published diagram located) |
| Adoption evidence | 100+ microchurches in Tampa; 15–30+ sister movements worldwide; MicroChurch Conference (biennial); Microchurch Network Intensive; Microchurches Podcast |
| Source | [Microchurches: A Smaller Way (Goodreads)](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/53009657-microchurches), [undergroundnetwork.org](https://www.undergroundnetwork.org/) |

The microchurch reframes the church's basic unit downward: not a weekly congregational gathering but a small, intentional community defined by the simultaneous practice of worship, community, and mission. Sanders argues the microchurch is "not just the smallest expression of the church, it is the most common."

### The Underground model (network-of-microchurches ecclesiology)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2018 (*Underground Church*); operative since 2006 |
| Components | (1) Church "structured and streamlined for mission" rather than weekly gathering; (2) ministry organized around the callings of individuals to outward mission; (3) a large support **network** ("relationship and resources" — coaching, finance, media, incubation) serving small microchurches; (4) priority of the poor, the lost, and biblical justice |
| Visual / model | — |
| Adoption evidence | Tampa Underground (Outreach 100 listing); sister movements (Kansas City Underground, Ignite Dublin, etc.); the book is a Zondervan Exponential Series title |
| Source | [undergroundnetwork.org/undergroundchurchbook](https://www.undergroundnetwork.org/undergroundchurchbook), [tampaunderground.com/who-we-are](https://www.tampaunderground.com/who-we-are) |

The Underground inverts the usual church-resource relationship: a lightweight network exists to *serve and incubate* many small mission-organized churches, rather than a central church running programs people attend.

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2022 (*The 6 Seasons of Calling*) |
| Components | Six life seasons, each with a developmental task: **Childhood** (bonding), **Adolescence** (learning), **Early Career** (serving), **Mid-Career** (creating), **Late Career** (giving), **Transition** (leaving) |
| Visual / model | Life-stage roadmap (Scripture + psychology + practitioner experience) |
| Adoption evidence | Moody Publishers trade title; Goodreads 4.16 (57 ratings) |
| Source | [everand.com](https://www.everand.com/book/545673190/The-6-Seasons-of-Calling-Discovering-Your-Purpose-in-Each-Stage-of-Life) |

A framework treating vocation/calling as ongoing and dynamic across six divinely-ordained life seasons rather than a single fixed decision.

### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not frame church success in attractional, gathering-centric, or leader-vision-centric terms — Sundays, weekly service attendance, programs, or "casting the leader's vision" as the organizing aim. Sanders' most-corrected misreading is that the church is primarily a weekly meeting; his consistent correction is that the church is empowered individuals on mission, and that leaders serve people's God-given callings rather than recruit them to the leader's vision ("the best way to love and lead our people is not to call them to our vision"). Derived from quotes in *Underground Church* and *Microchurches: A Smaller Way* ([Goodreads author quotes](https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/276141.Brian_Sanders)).

## Theological positioning

| Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Evangelical Protestant; missional / simple-church stream; non-denominational network ecclesiology; collegiate-ministry (InterVarsity) formation |
| Ecclesiology | Microchurch / network ecclesiology — the church's basic unit is small (worship + community + mission); a lightweight network serves many small mission-organized churches |
| Missiology | Mission as the organizing center of church; church "structured and streamlined for mission"; priority of the poor, the lost, and biblical justice |
| Leadership | Empowering, calling-serving leadership; leaders serve members' God-given callings rather than recruit members to the leader's vision |
| Christology | Jesus' lordship as the substance of worship ("not necessarily about singing or liturgy") |
| Anthropology / formation | Every committed disciple is responsible for the future formation of the church; formation happens in intentional community and outward mission |
| Culture | Contextualization, culture/ethnicity, and biblical justice held among the network's core values |

### Distinctive claims

- The microchurch is **not merely the smallest** expression of the church but **the most common** — reproducible and primary, not a stepping-stone to a larger gathering.
- A church can be **structured and streamlined for mission** rather than for a weekly gathering, and still be the church "in its most potent form."
- The proper relationship is a **network serving small churches**, inverting the usual "central church runs programs people attend."
- Leading people means serving their callings, **not calling them to the leader's vision**.
- Anonymity is incompatible with witness: "any place where we can go consistently and remain anonymous is not a witnessing church."
