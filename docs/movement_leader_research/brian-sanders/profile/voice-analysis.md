# Voice Analysis: Brian Sanders

## Voice summary

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tone | visionary, pastoral, direct |
| Register | practitioner / apostolic |

**Recurring phrases**: "a smaller way", "every Christian in every context", "the most common, not just the smallest", "structured and streamlined for mission", "worship, community, and mission"

**Favorite metaphors**: **the ecclesial minimum** (the church reduced to its smallest viable unit); **a smaller way** (scale inverted, small as most potent); **holding our place in the wall together** (the people of God as a shared, load-bearing structure); **the church underground** (a hidden, decentralized, mission-organized body beneath the visible institution)

## Register
Pastoral-practitioner with a manifesto edge. Sanders writes as a builder reporting from a sustained experiment, not as a detached theologian. He moves between narrative ("the true story of a ten-year experiment") and structural prescription (how to lead, fund, and organize for mission). The tone is invitational and democratizing — he wants ordinary disciples to see themselves as agents of the church's future.

## Hallmark moves
- **Smaller-is-most-potent inversion**: the smallest expression of the church is the most common, most reproducible, and most potent — a deliberate reversal of attractional, mega-scale assumptions.
- **Mission-first ecclesiology**: church is defined by mission and the callings of individuals, not by a weekly gathering or program.
- **The "ecclesial minimum"**: a microchurch is the coalescing of three intentional practices — **worship, community, and mission**.
- **Empowerment / activation language**: "every Christian in every context is being called by God"; "ordinary people activated as Kingdom agents."
- **Anti-anonymity**: "Any place where we can go consistently and remain anonymous is not a witnessing church."

## Antithesis prohibition
Do not frame church success in attractional, gathering-centric, leader-vision-centric terms — Sundays, programs, weekly service attendance, "casting the leader's vision" as the organizing aim. Sanders' most-corrected misreading is that church is primarily a weekly meeting; his consistent correction is that the church is empowered individuals on mission, and that leaders serve people's callings rather than recruit them to the leader's vision ("the best way to love and lead our people is not to call them to our vision").
