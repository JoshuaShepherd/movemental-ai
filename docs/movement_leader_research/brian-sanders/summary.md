# Brian Sanders

Brian Sanders is an American social entrepreneur, author, and the founder of the Underground Network — the Tampa, Florida network of microchurches widely regarded as a leading reference model for the microchurch movement in the West. More than two decades ago, he and his wife Monica moved into one of the poorest urban neighborhoods of his hometown to begin an intentional Christian community, and they have raised their six children in inner-city Tampa ever since. His formation ran through collegiate ministry — a BA in Communication from the University of Florida, an MA in Religious Studies from the University of South Florida, and an MTh in Applied Theology from the University of Wales / Spurgeon's College — alongside years as State Director for InterVarsity Christian Fellowship in Florida and founder of the Tampa Urban Project. In the summer of 2006, roughly fifty people left their traditional churches to form seven house-based church groups, the beginning of the Underground. What started as a frustration with attractional, program-centered church became a network "structured and streamlined for mission," organized not around a weekly gathering but around the callings of individuals to the poor, the lost, and biblical justice. That experiment grew into 100+ microchurches in Tampa and 15–30+ sister movements in cities and countries around the world. His central contribution is the pairing of *Underground Church* (Zondervan, 2018, with a foreword by Alan Hirsch), the narrative of the ten-year experiment, and *Microchurches: A Smaller Way* (Underground Media, 2019), the operational ecclesiology that defines a microchurch as "the coalescing of three intentional practices: worship, community, and mission." His significance lies in reframing the church's basic unit downward — arguing the microchurch is not merely the smallest expression of the church but the most common, most reproducible, and most potent — and in modeling a lightweight network that serves and incubates many small churches rather than running programs people attend.

## Body of work

- **Underground Network (Tampa Underground)** — founder and board member; former Executive Director. A network of 100+ Tampa microchurches with 15-30+ sister movements worldwide.
- **Books** — *Underground Church* (Zondervan, 2018), *Microchurches: A Smaller Way* (Underground Media, 2019), *The 6 Seasons of Calling* (Moody, 2022), plus earlier titles *Life In Intentional Christian Community*, *Different* (with Mike Patz), and *Life After Church* (IVP, 2007).
- **Current roles** — Principal, Underground Venture Studio; VP of Impact Alliances, National Christian Foundation Tampa Bay.

## Significance

Sanders gave the microchurch movement both its flagship story and its operational primer. He inverts attractional assumptions about church success, defining the church by mission and the callings of ordinary disciples rather than by a weekly gathering, and he models a network that exists to serve small mission-organized churches rather than to centralize programs.
