# Danielle Strickland — Fragmentation Story

## The Story

Danielle Strickland has spent more than two decades teaching, writing, and organizing across the seam where faith meets justice. Roughly twenty-two years of that began inside The Salvation Army, where she was a commissioned officer — planting churches, founding training schools, and helping stand up Justice Departments and global anti-trafficking work. When she stepped out of that institutional frame, she did not stop building; she multiplied. Today her work lives under Boundless Communications Inc. and fans out into Brave Global, Amplify Peace, the Women Speakers Collective, Infinitum, Hagar's Voice, and IMBY. She has written or curated roughly nine books across at least five publishers, hosts a podcast that has been renamed twice and carries half a million downloads, and appears as a main-stage voice at the Global Leadership Summit. The reach is real. The problem is that none of it sits in one place. Every venture she founded solved a real-world problem and, in doing so, created a new silo of content and a new list of people. The body of work is one of the most distributed in its field — and that distribution is precisely the issue.

## Informational Fragmentation

- **Books are spread across five-plus publishers with no unified reading surface.** *Just: Imagine* (2009) and *The Liberating Truth* (2011) sit on early Salvation Army / Monarch imprints; *Boundless: Living Life in Overflow* (2013, Monarch, with Stephen Court) and *A Beautiful Mess* (2014, Monarch/Lion Hudson) on Lion Hudson; *The Zombie Gospel* (2017) and *The Ultimate Exodus* on InterVarsity Press (https://www.ivpress.com/danielle-strickland); *Better Together* on W Publishing Group; *The Other Side of Hope* and the curated *Need to Know* elsewhere again. Author pages exist independently at InterVarsity Press, HarperCollins Australia, HarperChristian Resources (https://harperchristianresources.com/strickland/), Kregel, and SPCK.
- **Retail is scattered.** The same catalog is sold across Amazon (author id B00699MY3M, https://www.amazon.com/Danielle-Strickland/e/B00699MY3M), Audible, Barnes & Noble, Christianbook, Thriftbooks, Waterstones, Book Outlet, Everand/Scribd, and Logos — with no canonical "buy" page on her own site.
- **The podcast has fractured under three names.** It began as the *DJ Strickland Podcast*, became the *Boundless Podcast* (Apple Podcasts id 1166362366), and is now *Right Side Up* with co-host James Sholl (https://www.daniellestrickland.com/podcast). It lives simultaneously on Apple, Spotify (show 2eC9qeq5yobaI5FJhPvjMP), YouTube, Buzzsprout (id 59759, https://www.buzzsprout.com/59759), and Substack (https://daniellestrickland.substack.com) — different feeds, different back-catalogs.
- **Articles live on partner publications, not her site.** Global Leadership Network (https://globalleadership.org/author/danielle-strickland/), DTS Voice (https://voice.dts.edu/contributor/danielle-strickland/), Premier Christianity, Outreach Magazine, and Praise.com each host pieces under her name with no cross-link home.
- **Talks live on conference platforms.** GLS18 "Better Together" main-session notes and the "Leading Through the Dip" co-session with Craig Groeschel sit on globalleadership.org and Willow Creek's archive — not on daniellestrickland.com.
- **Guest interviews are uncollected.** Appearances on *Shifting Culture* (Ep. 250), *L3 Leadership* (Ep. 229), *TheoDisc* (Ep. 46), *Gather:Women* (Ep. 002), *Thrive Leadership Podcast* (Ep. 14), *The Follower Podcast*, and Rusty George's show (Ep. 155) exist only on each host's feed.
- **Named gaps:** no durable personal email newsletter (the Substack carries the podcast, not a sustained letter); no archived talk library under her own name; no single hub linking books, podcast, ventures, and talks.

## Relational Fragmentation

Six ventures means at least six separate relational systems, none of which talk to each other. Brave Global holds its own list of young women and partner churches. Amplify Peace holds the rosters of everyone who has traveled on a global immersion (Rwanda, the Middle East). The Women Speakers Collective holds a roster of developing communicators. Infinitum holds a rule-of-life community. Hagar's Voice holds survivors and advocates. IMBY holds housing partners. Boundless Communications sits over all of it but does not consolidate the contacts. Layered on top: the World Vision / Strong Women Strong World partnership behind *Right Side Up*, the Stop The Traffik ambassador relationship, and the Global Leadership Network speaker roster. Her co-author and husband, Stephen Court, is the most durable collaborator; through the *Need to Know* collection she is one co-author degree from Scot McKnight, Jo Saxton, and Mandy Smith — a direct line into the broader missional and justice scenius. But the relational rolodex that actually connects these worlds — who introduced whom, which Amplify traveler should meet which Brave Global organizer — lives in Danielle's phone, her memory, and the inboxes of a handful of staff.

## Embodied / In-Their-Head layer

This is the largest pool and the least written down. Twenty-two years of Salvation Army officership taught her how to plant a church, stand up a Justice Department, and run an anti-trafficking initiative on the ground — the pragmatic wisdom of the practice she lived long before she wrote *The Ultimate Exodus* about freedom. The curation logic behind *Need to Know* — why those particular women's voices, in that order — exists mostly in her head. The judgment calls behind an Amplify Peace immersion (which leaders to visit, how to hold grief and hope in the same week, why the Rwanda community's domestic-violence rate fell) live in trip debriefs and her own notebooks. The decision rules behind Brave Global — how you talk to a teenage girl about trafficking without re-traumatizing her — live in coaching conversations and the same talk given a thousand times from a thousand stages. The connective tissue between all six ventures is a single person's accumulated discernment, currently stored in conversation, memory, and the margins of a calendar.

## Synthesis

Danielle Strickland's fragmentation pattern is **the serial founder's sprawl**: every problem she encountered became a new organization, and every new organization became a new silo of content, contacts, and embodied know-how. She does not lack output — she has a catalog most authors would envy and a justice footprint most activists never reach. She lacks a layer above the output. There is no single surface where a reader who finds *The Zombie Gospel* can be carried to *Right Side Up*, then to Brave Global, then to an Amplify Peace immersion, then to the next book. The work is everywhere and findable from nowhere in particular. The most acute loss is the embodied layer — the practitioner wisdom from twenty-two years of frontline officership and six founded ventures — which has never been systematically captured and is the part most likely to evaporate unwritten.
