# Biography

Danielle Strickland is a communicator, author, justice advocate, and peacemaker who spent roughly twenty-two years as a commissioned officer in The Salvation Army, where she planted churches, founded training schools, and helped establish Justice Departments and global anti-trafficking initiatives. She held the rank of Major before stepping out of that institutional frame to build a portfolio of justice- and faith-driven ventures under Boundless Communications Inc. Her work sits at the intersection of spiritual leadership, women's empowerment, anti-trafficking, peacemaking, and abuse-survivor advocacy.

She has written or curated roughly nine books across five-plus publishers — among them *The Ultimate Exodus*, *Better Together*, *Boundless: Living Life in Overflow*, *A Beautiful Mess*, *The Zombie Gospel*, *The Other Side of Hope*, and the curated collection *Need to Know* — and hosts the *Right Side Up* podcast (formerly the *Boundless Podcast* and, before that, the *DJ Strickland Podcast*), a collaboration with World Vision and Strong Women Strong World that has reportedly passed half a million downloads.

She is the founder or co-founder of Brave Global, Amplify Peace, the Women Speakers Collective, Infinitum, Hagar's Voice, and IMBY (In My Backyard), and serves as an Ambassador for Stop The Traffik. She is a recurring main-stage voice at the Global Leadership Summit (Global Leadership Network / Willow Creek), where she delivered the GLS18 "Better Together" main session and a "Leading Through the Dip" conversation with Craig Groeschel.

Based in Canada (Toronto / Vancouver), she is married to Stephen Court, also a Salvation Army officer and her co-author on *Boundless: Living Life in Overflow*. Her primary online hub is daniellestrickland.com.
