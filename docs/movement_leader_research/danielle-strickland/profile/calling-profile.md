# Calling Profile

Danielle Strickland's vocation begins on the frontline, not at the desk. Roughly twenty-two years as a commissioned officer in The Salvation Army taught her to read a city the way a field general reads terrain — where the trafficking happens, who the church is failing to reach, what a Justice Department would need to actually function. She planted churches, founded training schools, and helped stand up anti-trafficking initiatives long before she wrote a sentence about freedom. The practice came first; the books are the record of it.

When she stepped out of the institutional frame of the Army, she did not narrow her calling — she multiplied it. Every problem she encountered became a new organization. Confronting trafficking and mobilizing young women became **Brave Global**. The conviction that grief and hope can share a room became **Amplify Peace** and its immersions into Rwanda and the Middle East. The under-platforming of women's voices became the **Women Speakers Collective** and the curated collection *Need to Know*. A longing for rule-of-life depth became **Infinitum**; survivor advocacy became **Hagar's Voice**; the housing crisis became **IMBY**. Above all of it sits **Boundless Communications Inc.**, the umbrella that launches and runs the ventures.

The through-line is a refusal to keep faith and justice in separate departments. For Strickland, freedom from what enslaves (*The Ultimate Exodus*), healing the gender divide (*Better Together*), and rediscovering humanity against cynicism (*The Other Side of Hope*) are not three causes but one calling viewed from three angles. She is, in the truest sense, a serial founder — but the founding is downstream of a single conviction: that the gospel is meant to liberate, and that liberation has to be built, organized, and embodied, not merely preached.

Her present season is less about authoring more and more about translation — making the body of work she has already built, and the embodied wisdom underneath it, reach the practitioners it was always written for.
