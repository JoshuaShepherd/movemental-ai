# Theology

Danielle Strickland's theology is shaped by the Salvation Army tradition — a holiness-rooted, justice-saturated evangelicalism that has never separated personal salvation from social action. The defining move of her work is to collapse the false distance between the spiritual and the social: the gospel is not adjacent to justice; it produces it.

## Emphases

- **Liberation / freedom from enslavement.** *The Ultimate Exodus* reads the Exodus narrative as a pattern for personal and structural freedom — naming what enslaves and walking out of it. Freedom is the governing image of her body of work, applied equally to addiction, oppression, and systemic injustice.

- **Anti-trafficking as gospel work.** Her trafficking-prevention advocacy (Brave Global, Stop The Traffik) is framed not as a humanitarian add-on but as the church living out its liberating mandate.

- **Female leadership and the gender divide.** *Better Together* and the curated *Need to Know* argue that empowering women's leadership is essential to the future of the church — and that men and women working together is a theological necessity, not a cultural concession.

- **Peacemaking.** Amplify Peace frames reconciliation as embodied immersion: holding grief and hope together, going to the places of conflict (Rwanda, the Middle East) rather than theorizing about them.

- **Hope against cynicism.** *The Other Side of Hope* treats cynicism and despair as spiritual conditions to be resisted, and the recovery of hope as a discipline central to rediscovering our shared humanity.

- **Incarnational / embodied practice.** Across ventures the pattern is the same: theology must be built and lived on the ground (church planting, Justice Departments, immersions, survivor advocacy), not held as abstraction. Infinitum's rule-of-life community extends this into the formation of leaders.
