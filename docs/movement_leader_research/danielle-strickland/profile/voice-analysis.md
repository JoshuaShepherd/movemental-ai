# Voice Analysis

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tone | Warm, urgent, prophetic, disarmingly funny |
| Register | Practitioner-activist / pastoral — frontline storyteller, not academic |
| Posture | Invitational and provocative at once; challenges comfortable audiences without shaming them |
| Pacing | Punchy, story-driven, builds to a clear call to act |

Strickland speaks the way a field practitioner writes — concrete, embodied, anchored in real people and real cities rather than abstraction. She moves fluidly between hard justice realities (trafficking, abuse, oppression) and an irrepressible, often humorous hope. She consistently reframes despair into agency: naming what is broken, then handing the listener something to do about it.

**Recurring phrases**: "living life in overflow", "the ultimate exodus", "better together", "the other side of hope", "freedom from what enslaves you", "brave", "need to know"

**Favorite metaphors**:
- **Exodus / liberation** — naming bondage and walking out of it
- **Overflow** — a life and a faith that spill past their own borders into the world
- **The divide and the bridge** — men and women, conflict and reconciliation, healed by working together
- **Flipping the script** — turning cynicism and despair inside out into hope
- **The frontline** — justice as terrain to be entered, not a topic to be discussed
