# A Letter to Danielle Strickland

## Calling

You have spent more than two decades teaching, writing, and organizing along the seam where faith meets justice — and you built most of your work twice, once as practice and once as text. Roughly twenty-two years of that began inside The Salvation Army, where you were a commissioned officer who planted churches, founded training schools, and helped stand up Justice Departments and anti-trafficking initiatives. You learned to read a city the way a field general reads terrain: where the harm is happening, who the church is failing to reach, what an institution would actually need to function. The practice came first. The books came after, as the record of what you already knew with your hands.

When you stepped out of the Army's institutional frame, you did not narrow the calling — you multiplied it. Every problem you met became an organization. Confronting trafficking and mobilizing young women became Brave Global. The conviction that grief and hope can share a room became Amplify Peace and its immersions into Rwanda and the Middle East. The under-platforming of women's voices became the Women Speakers Collective and the curated collection *Need to Know*. A longing for depth became Infinitum; survivor advocacy became Hagar's Voice; the housing crisis became IMBY. Boundless Communications sits over all of it.

The through-line is a refusal to keep faith and justice in separate departments. Freedom from what enslaves in *The Ultimate Exodus*, healing the gender divide in *Better Together*, and choosing hope against cynicism in *The Other Side of Hope* are not three causes but one calling seen from three angles. You are a serial founder, but the founding is always downstream of a single conviction: the gospel is meant to liberate, and liberation has to be built, organized, and embodied — not merely preached.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

The reach is real, and so is the scatter. You have written or curated roughly nine books across five-plus publishers — *Just: Imagine*, *The Liberating Truth*, *Boundless: Living Life in Overflow* with Stephen Court, *A Beautiful Mess*, *The Zombie Gospel*, *The Ultimate Exodus*, *Better Together*, *The Other Side of Hope*, and *Need to Know*. InterVarsity Press carries some, Monarch and Lion Hudson others, Thomas Nelson and W Publishing the rest. A reader who buys any of them lands on Amazon, Audible, Christianbook, or Waterstones — not on a page you control.

The podcast has been renamed twice. What began as the *DJ Strickland Podcast* became the *Boundless Podcast* and is now *Right Side Up* with James Sholl, a collaboration with World Vision and Strong Women Strong World. It lives at once on Apple Podcasts, Spotify, YouTube, Buzzsprout, and a Substack — different feeds, different back-catalogs, half a million downloads spread thin across all of them.

Your articles sit on other people's mastheads — Global Leadership Network, DTS Voice, Premier Christianity, Outreach Magazine — with no cross-link home. Your talks live on conference platforms: the GLS18 "Better Together" main session, "Better Together Without Fear," "Use Your Power to Empower Others," and "Leading Transformational Change" all sit on globalleadership.org rather than under your name. Your guest interviews — *Shifting Culture*, *L3 Leadership*, *TheoDisc*, *Gather:Women*, and more — exist only on each host's feed.

And the largest pool is the part that was never written down at all. Your officership taught you how to plant a church and run an anti-trafficking initiative long before you wrote a sentence about freedom. The curation logic behind *Need to Know* — why those particular women, in that order — lives mostly in your head. So do the judgment calls behind an Amplify Peace immersion, and the decision rules behind Brave Global: how you speak to a teenage girl about trafficking without re-traumatizing her. That embodied wisdom lives in trip debriefs, coaching calls, and the same talk given from a thousand stages.

## Constraints

Three things shape what is realistic for this season.

First, your time is the scarcest resource you have, divided across six ventures, a podcast, a speaking calendar, and the people each of those serves. Anything we build has to surface your work without asking you to become its archivist. The inventorying and the connective tissue are the platform's job, not yours.

Second, the embodied layer — the most valuable and the most at risk — only surfaces on your schedule, not a publisher's. It has to come out in your own voice, at your own pace, through conversation and dictation that you remain free to edit or refuse. None of it replaces your authorship; every draft stays yours.

Third, you lead Boundless Communications, and these ventures are live, not archived. The work ahead is not writing more. It is translation — making the body of work you have already built reach the practitioners it was always written for: the young women, survivors, and communicators you serve through Brave Global, Hagar's Voice, and the Women Speakers Collective. Right now the work is everywhere and findable from nowhere in particular. That is the constraint worth solving.
