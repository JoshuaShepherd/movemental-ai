## Bio synthesis

Danielle Strickland is a communicator, author, and global social-justice advocate who spent roughly 22 years as a commissioned officer in The Salvation Army, where she planted churches, founded training schools, and helped establish Justice Departments and global anti-trafficking initiatives. She has since built a portfolio of justice- and faith-driven ventures under Boundless Communications Inc. Her work sits at the intersection of spiritual leadership, women's empowerment, anti-trafficking, peacemaking, and abuse-survivor advocacy. She is an Ambassador for Stop The Traffik and a recurring main-stage voice at the Global Leadership Summit (Global Leadership Network / Willow Creek).
