## Executive Summary

Dave Ferguson is one of the highest-profile practitioner voices in the North American church-multiplication movement. As CEO and President of Exponential — the world's largest church-planting conference and resource network — Co-Founding Pastor of Community Christian Church (a Chicago-area multi-site community of ~5,500), and the founding visionary of the NewThing church-planting network, Ferguson has spent more than three decades translating a single animating conviction into systems, books, and institutions: that the church is called not merely to *add* but to *multiply* — disciples who make disciples, leaders who make leaders, churches that plant churches.

His distinctive contribution is **"hero making"** — the reframing of leadership from being the hero to making others the hero of God's story. Articulated most fully in *Hero Maker* (Zondervan, 2018, with Warren Bird, foreword by J.D. Greear), this is the maturation of a body of work that began with church-strategy method (*The Big Idea*, 2007), moved through movement vision (*Exponential*, 2010; *On the Verge*, 2011, co-authored with Alan Hirsch), reframed church metrics (*Keeping Score*, 2014), reached the spiritually searching (*Finding Your Way Back to God*, 2015), distilled missional neighboring into a portable rhythm (*B.L.E.S.S.*, 2021), and now turns inward toward sustainable leader health (*Multiplier*, March 2026, and its RPMS framework). Across this arc Ferguson has produced a deep toolkit of named, teachable frameworks — the Five Levels of multiplying church, the BLESS practices, ICNU conversations, the five-step apprenticeship model, and the "16% Mission" — that function as deployable language for tens of thousands of church planters.

In 2025 Ferguson did something most movement leaders only write about: he embodied his own theology in two public transitions. On April 1, 2025, he dissolved NewThing as a separate entity and merged it into Exponential under the banner **"Kingdom over Castle."** On May 1, 2025, after an eighteen-month public apprenticeship, he handed the Lead Pastor role at Community Christian Church to Ted Coniaris and stepped into a Co-Founding Pastor role — a lived case study of the apprenticeship and succession model he has taught for years.

Despite this outsized institutional influence — three organizations founded, a conference that convenes the North American church-planting conversation, a network that planted thousands of churches across ~70 nations, top-tier endorsements from J.D. Greear, Andy Stanley, Craig Groeschel, Mark Batterson, Ed Stetzer, and Carey Nieuwhof — Ferguson's *personally owned* digital presence is the thinnest part of his footprint (3.3/5). His reach is disproportionately **institutionally owned**: Exponential controls the conference email list, the podcast feeds, and the free-ebook distribution channel through which most of his content travels. His personal owned audience — ~511 Substack subscribers (on an awkward `daveferguson3.substack.com` URL), ~42K social followers, a small personal blog, and an underdeveloped YouTube channel — is strikingly modest relative to his stature. There is no email capture on daveferguson.org and no Wikipedia article despite his being president of the world's largest church-planting conference.

That gap is precisely the problem Movemental solves. The monetization opportunity is estimated at **$77K–$398K/year** through digitized courses (Hero Maker, Five Levels, BLESS, Multiplier/RPMS), a Five-Level church diagnostic assessment tool (analogous to Hirsch's APEST assessment), a paid Hero Maker practitioner community, owned-list email capture, and audiobooks/toolkits. His network — anchored by brother and lifelong co-author Jon Ferguson, intellectual peer Alan Hirsch, Exponential co-founder Todd Wilson, and successor Ted Coniaris — is a direct pipeline to additional Movemental authors, and a Hirsch–Ferguson co-produced multiplication track is the single most natural flagship collaboration in the pipeline.

## Top 5 Opportunities

1. **Owned email list with an RPMS lead magnet**: There is no email capture on daveferguson.org, and his ~42K social audience has no personal email pathway — most of his list lives inside Exponential. The *Multiplier* (2026) launch and its RPMS framework is a natural moment to gate a daily-tracking template and convert podcast-driven traffic into a personally owned list. (HIGH priority, LOW effort)

2. **"Hero Maker in 5 Sessions" online course**: His most widely deployed framework has no digital course expression. Five modules on the five practices, ICNU conversations, and the apprenticeship model would serve the thousands who buy the book at Exponential and then have nowhere to go. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort)

3. **Five-Level church diagnostic assessment tool**: "What level is your church?" is a question his framework already answers, but no interactive quiz exists. Built well, it becomes a viral, shareable lead-gen instrument analogous to Hirsch's APEST assessment. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort)

4. **Hero Maker practitioner network (paid community)**: A 52-week extension of the Exponential conference experience — peer cohorts and periodic Ferguson coaching for leaders actively working the five practices. (MEDIUM priority, MEDIUM effort)

5. **Fix the owned-channel basics + audiobooks/centralized video**: Migrate off the credibility-undermining `daveferguson3` Substack URL, centralize scattered conference video, and confirm/produce audiobook editions for the few titles still missing them. (MEDIUM priority, LOW effort)
