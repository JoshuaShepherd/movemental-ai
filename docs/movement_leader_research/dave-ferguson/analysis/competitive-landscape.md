# Competitive Landscape Analysis: Dave Ferguson

**Research Date**: 2026-05-27
**Subject**: Dave Ferguson — Co-Founding Pastor, Community Christian Church; CEO/President, Exponential; Founder, NewThing Network (now merged)
**Primary Niche**: Church multiplication, leadership multiplication, missional living, movement building
**Confidence Level**: High

---

## 1. Direct Competitors

These authors occupy substantially overlapping territory — church multiplication and movement leadership — with shared or directly competing audiences.

### Todd Wilson

- **Overlap**: Highest of any peer. Wilson co-founded Exponential with Ferguson. They co-authored *Becoming a Level Five Multiplying Church* (eBook, with an Alan Hirsch chapter). Wilson has been Ferguson's closest organizational and intellectual partner across two decades. The Five-Level church typology is a joint Ferguson-Wilson framework. Both lead within the same institutional ecosystem (Exponential) and address the same denominational and pastor audience.
- **Distinction**: Wilson is primarily a researcher and systems thinker. He has published fewer books than Ferguson and has a more behind-the-scenes organizational posture. He does not have Ferguson's public-facing platform or pastoral storytelling voice. Ferguson is the movement catalyst and communicator; Wilson is the researcher and data analyst. Wilson's *Becoming a Level Five Multiplying Church* is widely cited in academic/denominational contexts; Ferguson's *Hero Maker* drives the practitioner conversation.
- **Books**: 3–5 (including Exponential imprint publications, *More: Find Your Personal Calling and Live Life to the Fullest Measure*, and co-authored works)
- **Digital Presence**: Low-Moderate. Works primarily through Exponential's platform rather than a personal brand.
- **Audience**: Medium (10K–30K estimated). Overlaps almost entirely with Ferguson's.
- **Unique Angle**: Research-driven church multiplication analysis; multiplication as systems change.
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Very High** — co-founder, co-author, co-framework creator. Organizational partners, not competitors.

### Alan Hirsch

- **Relationship**: The most important upstream influence on Ferguson's work. Hirsch and Ferguson co-authored *On the Verge* (Zondervan, 2011) — among the most substantive co-authorships in the multiplication space. Hirsch endorsed both *Hero Maker* and *Multiplier*. Ferguson integrates Hirsch's mDNA, APEST, and "Apostolic Genius" language throughout his writing and identifies himself as "high on the apostolic gifting" using Hirsch's typology.
- **Distinction**: Hirsch operates at the meta-framework level — movement theory, APEST, mDNA, the forgotten ways of apostolic genius. Ferguson applies and deploys Hirsch's frameworks in operational church-planting reality. Hirsch is more prophetically urgent, more academically grounded, and more internationally positioned. Ferguson is more accessible, more motivationally invitational, and more North American-centric in his primary audience.
- **Books**: 15+ including *The Forgotten Ways* (2006), *The Permanent Revolution* (2012), *5Q* (2017), *Reframation* (2019), *Metanoia* (2024)
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. alanhirsch.org, 100Movements, Forge, 5Q Collective. Multiple organizational platforms. ~37,900 X followers, ~14,000 Facebook. Wikipedia page. Substack (1,500+).
- **Audience**: Large (50K–100K+ estimated). Movement leaders, denominational strategists, seminary students, global missional practitioners.
- **Unique Angle**: Apostolic movement meta-theory; APEST/5Q system; apostolic imagination; Forgotten Ways diagnostic.
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **High** (theological framework) / **Low** (operational implementation). Hirsch provides the theory; Ferguson provides the practice. Best understood as complementary rather than competitive — Hirsch's *Forgotten Ways* is the "why"; Ferguson's *Hero Maker* is the "how." Their 2011 co-authorship demonstrates this: Hirsch's chapters provide theological framework; Ferguson's chapters provide church-planting practice.

### Ed Stetzer

- **Relationship**: Close peer and mutual endorser. Stetzer holds the Billy Graham Distinguished Chair at Wheaton College (where Ferguson also teaches as adjunct). Stetzer has endorsed Ferguson's books (*Hero Maker*, *Multiplier*), and Ferguson has appeared on Stetzer's ChurchLeaders podcast (Oct 2024). Both serve the evangelical church-planting community.
- **Distinction**: Stetzer is a research-driven public intellectual with vastly larger institutional reach — Editor-in-Chief of Outreach Magazine, frequent Moody Radio presence, Lausanne leadership, Biola/Talbot dean's office. He covers church planting but also church culture, evangelism, and social commentary broadly. He is a generalist megaphone where Ferguson is a multiplication specialist practitioner. Stetzer's audience is significantly larger (~200K+ combined social) but less specifically focused on the multiplication practitioner niche.
- **Books**: 12+ including *Planting Missional Churches* (2006, 2016 ed.), *Christians in the Age of Outrage* (2018)
- **Digital Presence**: Very Strong. 100K+ on X; Outreach Magazine platform; Moody Radio; ChurchLeaders.com; Biola/Talbot.
- **Audience**: Very Large (200K+). Broad evangelical audience.
- **Unique Angle**: Research-backed public evangelical intellectualism; institutional megaphone; culture commentary; Lausanne global.
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Moderate** — church planting only. Different scale, different scope, different role. Stetzer amplifies Ferguson's work more than he competes with it (Outreach Magazine has featured Ferguson 10+ times).

### Neil Cole

- **Relationship**: Parallel track. Cole's Organic Church movement (2005) pioneered the simple/house church model that overlaps with Ferguson's multiplication vision — both advocate decentralized reproduction. Both speak (or have spoken) at Exponential.
- **Distinction**: Cole is far more radical — fully decentralized, house-church first, no paid clergy as starting assumption, DNA groups as the basic multiplication unit. Church Multiplication Associates (CMA) claimed 700+ churches in 32 states and 23 nations at peak. Ferguson's model is more institutionally structured: multi-site, formal conference infrastructure, Exponential as a centralized convening body. Cole's platform has declined significantly from its 2005–2012 peak.
- **Books**: 10+ including *Organic Church* (2005), *Church 3.0* (2010), *Organic Leadership* (2009), *Primal Fire* (2014)
- **Digital Presence**: Low — blog-based; declining from peak influence.
- **Audience**: Medium-declining (10K–25K estimated). Simple/organic church practitioners.
- **Unique Angle**: Radical decentralization; DNA groups; fully organic church multiplication with no institutional overhead.
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Moderate** — both advocate multiplication; very different structural philosophy (Ferguson is more institutionally structured; Cole is radically anti-institutional). Their audiences partially overlap among practitioners committed to decentralized multiplication.

### Hugh Halter

- **Relationship**: Adjacent peer. Halter's *Tangible Kingdom* (2008) and his Lantern Network / Brave Cities work overlaps with Ferguson's BLESS and incarnational neighboring content. Both speak at Exponential and similar conferences. Both advocate for everyday Christians as missionaries.
- **Distinction**: Halter is a practitioner-storyteller (house painter turned church planter) whose Brave Cities and Lantern Network position him as a bivocational pioneer. His voice is more embodied and experiential, less framework-heavy than Ferguson's. Halter's audience is smaller and more specifically focused on urban incarnational community. Ferguson's BLESS audience is broader and more suburban.
- **Books**: 7+ including *The Tangible Kingdom* (2008), *AND: The Gathered and Scattered Church* (2010), *BIVO* (2013), *Flesh* (2014)
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. Personal website, Brave Cities network, Outreach Magazine contributor.
- **Audience**: Medium (15K–30K estimated). Incarnational community practitioners, bivocational ministers.
- **Unique Angle**: Incarnational embodiment as apologetic; bivocational lifestyle; tangible community presence.
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Moderate-High** on the BLESS/neighboring content; **Low** on the multiplication-systems and Five-Level framework content. Halter is a complement to Ferguson's BLESS work more than a competitor.

Nieuwhof has hosted Ferguson on the Carey Nieuwhof Leadership Podcast three times (CNLP 169, 415, 805) — one of the few repeat guests at this frequency. Nieuwhof endorsed *BLESS* and *Multiplier*. He operates as a convener and amplifier of the evangelical leadership space.
- **Distinction**: Nieuwhof is a content and podcast platform, not primarily a church-multiplication practitioner. His focus is leadership health, personal effectiveness, and the declining church — adjacent to but distinct from Ferguson's multiplication-systems focus. Nieuwhof's newsletter and podcast audience is very large (est. 500K+); he is a distributor for leaders like Ferguson rather than a competitor.
- **Books**: Multiple leadership books

- **Audience**: Very Large (500K+). Broad evangelical leadership audience.

But they operate at different scales and with different organizational credibility claims. Nieuwhof is primarily a media platform; Ferguson is primarily a movement builder.

## 2. Adjacent Voices

These authors cover related territory with a different angle, audience, or institutional context.

### JR Woodward

- **Relationship**: Adjacent in the missional-ecclesiology and Exponential ecosystem. Woodward has spoken at Exponential conferences; both are connected through Alan Hirsch's network. Both engage church multiplication and the APEST framework, though from very different postures.
- **Distinction**: Woodward is a practitioner-scholar (PhD from University of Manchester, 4 seminary adjunct roles) whose approach is diagnostic-constructive and academically grounded. Ferguson is a practitioner-catalyst whose approach is motivational-operational. Woodward addresses *why* churches default to domination (Powers theology, mimetic desire); Ferguson addresses *how* to multiply leaders and churches (five practices, apprenticeship model). Their content is complementary in tone and target but very different in register.
- **Books**: 3 authored (+1 edited), all through IVP or 100Movements Publishing
- **Digital Presence**: Growing (25K–40K estimated reach). V3 Movement (1,000+ trained planters), Substack "Faithful Resistance," 4 seminary adjunct positions.
- **Unique Angle**: Powers theology and leadership failure diagnosis; polycentric leadership; Girard-Wink-Stringfellow synthesis applied to church leadership.
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Moderate** — both advocate multiplication; both speak at Exponential; both in the Alan Hirsch ecosystem. Woodward is at the theological-diagnostic layer; Ferguson is at the operational-catalytic layer. A Movemental-hosted pairing would be highly complementary.

### Brad Brisco

- **Relationship**: Parallel. Brisco is Director of Multiplication Strategies at NAMB/Send Network. Both operate in the missional church-planting space. Brisco has contributed to Exponential resources; both engage the IVP/Forge Partnership publishing ecosystem (Ferguson through *On the Verge*).
- **Distinction**: Brisco is denominationally embedded (SBC/NAMB) where Ferguson is broadly interdenominational (Exponential serves 100+ networks). Brisco's breakout niche is covocational/bivocational church planting within a major denomination. Ferguson's niche is multiplication systems and leader culture across a broader evangelical spectrum.
- **Books**: 5 — *Missional Essentials* (2012), *The Missional Quest* (2014), *Next Door As It Is in Heaven* (2016), *Covocational Church Planting* (2021), *ReThink* (eBook)
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Moderate** — church planting + multiplication focus. Different denominational context; different leadership emphasis (Brisco = vocational strategy; Ferguson = leader culture and movement systems).

### Craig Groeschel

- **Relationship**: Peer at scale. Groeschel's Life.Church is among the most significant multi-site expressions of what Ferguson has championed. Groeschel has endorsed three Ferguson books (*Hero Maker*, *BLESS*, *Multiplier*) and represents the "large church doing multiplication" model that Ferguson commends. The Life.Church Open Network (free resources for all churches) is the kind of kingdom-over-castle organizational posture Ferguson advocates.
- **Distinction**: Groeschel is himself a very large content platform (Craig Groeschel Leadership Podcast, large social following, NYT bestselling author). He is not a multiplication theorist so much as a multiplication practitioner at Hyper-scale. Unlike Ferguson, he does not run a conference or lead a formal network — he leads one very large church.
- **Books**: Multiple leadership books including *Winning the War in Your Mind*, *Dangerous* series
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Moderate-High** — the Life.Church/Open Network model is a peer expression of Ferguson's multiplication vision. But Groeschel's primary content is personal leadership development (Craig Groeschel Leadership Podcast); Ferguson's is church multiplication systems. Their audiences share evangelical senior pastor/leader demographics.

### Ralph Moore (How to Multiply Your Church)

- **Distinction**: Moore is a generation older than Ferguson (Hope Chapel movement predates Exponential's founding). He operates at smaller scale and with less institutional infrastructure. ### Steve Addison

- **Distinction**: Addison is an Australian missiologist and practitioner focused on church-planting movements in the global mission context. His research covers historical and contemporary movements (CPMs) across the Global South. He is more globally oriented and missiologically academic than Ferguson.
- **Books**: Multiple including *Movements That Change the World* (2011), *Pioneering Movements* (2015)
- **Overlap with Ferguson**: **Moderate** — shared multiplication theology; Ferguson has endorsed Addison; their audiences intersect but Addison's is more internationally mission-focused.

## 4. Differentiation: What Makes Dave Ferguson Unique

### The Operational Proof Point at Maximum Scale

Ferguson occupies a position that no other peer holds: he is the only multiplication-movement author who has simultaneously (1) co-founded a multi-site church that grew to 5,500+ attendees across 7 locations over 35 years, (2) founded a church-planting network that grew to 6,373+ churches in 47 countries, and (3) co-founded and leads the world's largest church-planting conference. This three-way operational credential is unique:

- **Hirsch** provides the movement theory but has not led a church-planting network at this scale personally
- **Stetzer** has the largest media platform but has moved away from hands-on movement leadership toward academic and journalistic roles
- **Cole** built CMA but at significantly smaller scale; his platform has declined
- **Nieuwhof** has the largest personal digital audience but is primarily a media platform, not a movement builder
- **Wilson** co-built Exponential but does not have the pastoral (CCC) or network-founder (NewThing) credentials

### The Five-Level Framework as Category Leader

The Five-Level church typology (Declining → Plateaued → Growing → Reproducing → Multiplying) has become the dominant diagnostic framework in North American evangelical church planting. It appears in denominational training materials, seminary syllabi, and pastor conferences across 100+ networks. No other framework in the peer group has achieved this level of category saturation. "What Level is your church?" is a question Ferguson essentially wrote into the vocabulary of the entire movement. The APEST assessment (Hirsch) and the Praxis Cohort (Woodward) are comparably influential in their niches, but neither has penetrated as many denominational contexts.

### The BLESS Framework as the Most Widely Adopted Missional Neighboring Tool

*BLESS* (4.20/5, 500 ratings) is Ferguson's highest-rated book and arguably his most impactful democratizing contribution. The BLESS acronym (Begin with prayer, Listen, Eat, Serve, Share) has been adopted as a small-group outreach curriculum by thousands of churches across evangelical traditions. No other peer author has created a missional neighboring framework that is simultaneously this accessible (no seminary training required), this memorable (acronym structure), and this theologically grounded (rooted in Genesis 12 blessing language and a prevenient grace theology). *The Tangible Kingdom* (Halter) and V3's training (Woodward) address similar territory but for different audiences at different depth levels.

### Strategic Sequencing Recommendation

**Phase 1 (Foundation)**: Ferguson + Hirsch on the platform together. This creates the core multiplication learning ecosystem — the theorist (Hirsch) and the operator (Ferguson). Their *On the Verge* co-authorship is the natural entry point: "Start with *Forgotten Ways*, then see how Ferguson built it." This pairing is the most natural and most powerful in the multiplication space.

**Phase 2 (Depth)**: Add Woodward. Position as "the three layers of multiplication": Hirsch provides the apostolic movement framework (the vision), Ferguson provides the operational systems (the how), Woodward provides the diagnostic theology (the why it fails). This three-author ecosystem serves every type of multiplication leader — the visionary, the practitioner, and the diagnostic thinker.

**Phase 3 (Breadth)**: Add Brisco, Halter, and Cole (carefully). Brisco extends into the denominational/SBC context. Halter extends into the BLESS/neighboring space. Cole provides the organic-church perspective as an alternative on the multiplication spectrum.

**Avoid**: Positioning Nieuwhof or Groeschel alongside Ferguson as multiplication practitioners — their scale advantages would overshadow Ferguson's specialist credibility. Better to use their platforms (Nieuwhof's podcast, Groeschel's Open Network) for outbound distribution of Ferguson's Movemental content than to platform them directly alongside him.

## 7. White Space: Ferguson's Distinct Competitive Positions

The following positions are either uniquely Ferguson's or largely unclaimed by any peer:

### White Space 1: The "World's Largest Conference CEO" Practitioner Voice

No other voice in the church-multiplication space simultaneously holds a CEO role at the world's largest church-planting conference AND writes accessible practitioner books AND has a 35-year pastoral track record. Stetzer is the closest analogue institutionally but is primarily a researcher and journalist, not a church-planter. This combination is Ferguson's alone.

### White Space 2: The Hero-Maker Cultural Paradigm as Universal Leadership Language

"Hero Maker" has crossed denominational lines and even begun crossing into secular organizational culture (workplaces.org podcast; Lochhead "Follow Your Different" podcast). No other multiplication-specific framework in the peer group has achieved this level of secular crossover. The BLESS framework is similarly positioned for general Christian adoption. Ferguson's frameworks are uniquely accessible to leaders outside the church-planting specialist community.

### White Space 3: The "Kingdom over Castle" Organizational Ethics Voice

Ferguson's decision to dissolve NewThing into Exponential — framing it explicitly as "Kingdom over Castle" — gives him a credibility claim that no peer can match: the organizational leader who actually dissolved what he built for a larger kingdom purpose. This is an extremely rare and credible voice for the multiplication community's recurring temptation toward empire-building.

### White Space 4: Everyday Mission for the Suburban Evangelical Layperson

*BLESS* (4.20/5, 500 ratings) fills a gap between the academic missional-incarnational literature (Hirsch, Frost, Woodward — complex, ecclesiologically demanding) and the programmatic evangelism tradition (too transactional, relationship-bypassing). Ferguson's BLESS framework is the most successful translation of missional-incarnational theology into everyday Christian practice. No peer occupies this specific position at his scale.

### White Space 5: The Multiplication Leader Who Demonstrates His Theology Through Succession

Ferguson's 18-month apprenticeship of Ted Coniaris as his Lead Pastor successor is a public demonstration of hero-making theology that most multiplication writers describe but few embody. This embodied witness — stepping down from his own Lead Pastor role to release someone else into leadership — is a compelling and underutilized narrative asset that distinguishes him from every peer who writes about hero-making but remains in a senior platform role indefinitely.

## 8. Key Takeaway

Dave Ferguson's competitive advantage is the **operational proof point at maximum institutional scale** — the only multiplication author who has built a 5,500-person multi-site church, a 6,373-church global network, and the world's largest church-planting conference, and who writes from that experience with frameworks accessible enough for suburban lay Christians and powerful enough for denominational executives. His Five-Level typology and Hero Maker practices are the dominant vocabulary of the multiplication movement across 100+ denominations.

His competitive vulnerability is that this scale is largely institutionally housed. His personal digital footprint — 511 Substack subscribers, a sparse blog, an underused personal YouTube channel — does not match his operational stature. Movemental's opportunity is to close this gap: give Ferguson the personal owned platform that his institutional influence warrants, converting his enormous conference-and-network reach into an audience that follows the person, not just the conference.

The competitive white space is clear: Ferguson as a personally owned voice — not the CEO of Exponential, but the pastor-movement-builder who proved that ordinary Christians can bless their neighbors, ordinary leaders can make heroes of others, and ordinary churches can multiply exponentially if they embrace the right culture. That voice exists. It needs a personally owned digital home.
