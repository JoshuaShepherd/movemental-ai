## Key Framing

Dave Ferguson is a **practitioner-leader and popular author**, not an academic theologian. He holds no doctorate (honorary or earned) and has produced no peer-reviewed journal articles or dissertation. His intellectual contribution is in the practitioner space: developing frameworks for church multiplication (the Five Levels, Hero Maker practices, BLESS), leading one of the most influential church planting movements in North America (NewThing), and translating missional theology into accessible tools for local church leaders.

This mirrors the profiles of leaders like Alan Hirsch's practitioner-facing output alongside, but distinct from, his more academic work — except that Ferguson sits entirely on the practitioner side with no academic publication track.

## Research / Framework Contributions (Practitioner)

Dave Ferguson's primary "research" output is practitioner-focused. He has developed several frameworks that have achieved wide adoption in the North American church planting space:

| Framework | Development Context | Adoption |
|---|---|---|
| **Five Levels of Church Multiplication** | Co-developed with Todd Wilson and Alan Hirsch; based on Exponential's research into church reproduction rates | Widely adopted across multiple denominations and networks; referenced in Exponential's research reports and widely cited in church multiplication literature |
| **Hero Maker Five Practices** | Research and writing with Warren Bird (Leadership Network researcher); empirical observations from Community Christian Church and NewThing | Adopted by Acts 29, EFCA, United Methodist, and other denominations via webinar series |
| **BLESS Framework** | Co-developed with Jon Ferguson; drawn from Community Christian Church's evangelism practices | Used by hundreds of churches as a small-group evangelism curriculum |
| **Church Scorecard / Scorecard vs. Scoreboard** | Exponential eBook *Keeping Score* (2014) | Foundational to Exponential's diagnostic framework; referenced in subsequent publications |
