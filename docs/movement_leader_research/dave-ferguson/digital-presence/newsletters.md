### Content Focus

The newsletter covers leadership multiplication, church planting, organizational health, missional living, and spiritual encouragement — directly aligned with Ferguson's books (*Multiplier*, *Hero Maker*, *B.L.E.S.S.*) and his Exponential CEO role. Posts are practical and pastoral in tone: short-form leadership frameworks, theological reflection on movement growth, personal stories from travel and ministry, and calls to action for church planters.

The newsletter is an extension of his book-and-platform brand rather than a distinct content voice (compare with JR Woodward's "Faithful Resistance," which is an intentionally different and more politically-edged publication). Ferguson's Substack is more tightly aligned with his established thought-leader identity.

## Observations

1. **Substack is active but young** — 511 subscribers after ~4 months of operation is a reasonable start, but well behind peers: JR Woodward ("hundreds," also newer) is similar scale; Alan Hirsch had 1,500+ on a more established Substack
2. **URL fragmentation is a problem** — daveferguson3.substack.com is an awkward URL; the "3" suffix signals that Ferguson was late to claim his primary Substack handle; the clean jrwoodward.substack.com or alanhirsch.substack.com equivalents weren't available when he signed up
3. **No personal email list outside Substack** — the personal website sends readers to Substack rather than capturing email addresses directly; this creates platform dependency
4. **multiplierbook.com is the best-functioning email capture** — the bonus-content registration after book purchase is smart book-launch email capture, but it only works for buyers of *Multiplier*
5. **Weekly cadence is strong** — posting every Sunday/Monday is a consistent, sustainable rhythm that signals commitment to the channel
6. **Engagement per post is low** — 5–20 likes on ~511 subscribers = ~1–4% like rate per post; commenting is present (2–16 per post) which is a healthy signal for a new publication
7. **Content brand consistency** — the newsletter voice is directly continuous with his books and Exponential mission; no audience confusion about who this is for
8. **No paid tier** — unlike JR Woodward's $10/month Substack option, Ferguson has not introduced a paid tier; this may reflect the newsletter's newness or a deliberate free-access strategy
9. **Institutional vs. personal** — the Exponential newsletter/podcast infrastructure is his most significant email/audio reach, but it is institutionally owned; if Ferguson left Exponential, he would not take that list
10. **Gap: no free lead magnet funnel** — no downloadable resource (PDF framework, mini-course, assessment) linked to an email capture on daveferguson.org to grow the Substack organically
