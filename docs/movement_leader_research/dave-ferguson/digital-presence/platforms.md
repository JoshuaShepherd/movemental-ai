### Score Interpretation

Dave Ferguson has a **broad institutional footprint with a developing personal content voice**. His influence is felt most strongly through:

- His **CEO/President role at Exponential** — the world's largest church planting conference organization, with 106+ podcast episodes, an active email list, conference events in multiple U.S. cities, and a growing international arm (Exponential International)
- His **published books** — 9+ books since 2007, most recently *Multiplier* (Zondervan, 2025/2026); Hero Maker remains the flagship brand (737 Goodreads ratings, 4.02 avg)
- His **Twitter/X presence** (@daveferguson, ~22.7K) — the most established personal social channel with a premium clean handle
- His **Substack newsletter** — newly launched (early 2026) but already at weekly cadence with 511 subscribers, showing intent to build a personal owned-audience channel separate from Exponential's institutional reach

The key structural vulnerability: a significant portion of his digital reach lives in institutional channels (Exponential's podcast, email list, social accounts) that he does not personally own. If his role at Exponential changed, his digital footprint would shrink considerably. His personally-owned assets (website, Substack, personal social) are currently insufficient to sustain that reach independently.

### Key Gaps for Movemental to Address

1. **Substack URL problem** — daveferguson3.substack.com is an embarrassing URL for a leader of his stature; the "3" suffix undermines personal brand authority
2. **No email capture on daveferguson.org** — website visitors are sent to Substack but there is no native email capture; opportunity for a free lead magnet (PDF framework, self-assessment, mini-course) to drive list growth
3. **YouTube personal channel underdeveloped** — channel UCr4AirrEAgHM_mIdcebNm-w exists but metrics are unknown and presumably modest; Ferguson's video content is scattered across Exponential's channel, COMMUNITY's @cmmntycc channel, and dozens of third-party conference recordings
4. **No Wikipedia article** — no Wikipedia page found for Dave Ferguson; a significant discoverability gap given his stature as a major conference president and bestselling author
5. **Institutional dependency** — too much of his digital reach (podcast, email, social) lives in Exponential org channels, not personally owned properties
6. **No personal podcast** — appears only as guest; lacks a dedicated channel for his voice and ideas
7. **Blog is effectively dead** — daveferguson.org blog has 17 total posts since 2015 and the newsletter is doing the work instead; the blog should either be retired/redirected or revived
8. **Facebook fragmentation** — two Facebook presences (@PastorDaveFerguson page + dave.ferguson personal) split following; needs consolidation around the page
9. **New platforms absent** — no Threads, Bluesky, or TikTok presence; depending on platform trajectory in 2026, this could become a meaningful gap
10. **Goodreads author engagement is near-zero** — ~11 Goodreads followers for an author with 3,045+ total ratings suggests he has never actively engaged the Goodreads community
