## The Story

Dave Ferguson has been planting churches and multiplying leaders for **more than 35 years**. On **March 5, 1989**, he and his brother Jon gathered five college friends in the cafeteria of Naperville Central High School and held the first service of **Community Christian Church** — a community founded to help people "find their way back to God." It became one of the earliest **multi-site churches** in America (its Romeoville campus launched in the late 1990s, before "multisite" was a recognized category) and grew to roughly **5,500 weekly attendees across seven Chicago-area locations**. In **2001** he launched **NewThing**, a church-planting network that grew to plant **6,373+ churches across nearly 70 nations** (982 in 2020 alone) before it merged into Exponential in 2025. As co-founder and **CEO/President of Exponential**, he convenes the **largest church-planting conference in the world**, organized around what he calls the **"16% Mission"** — moving the share of multiplying U.S. churches from roughly 7% to a 16% cultural tipping point.

He has written **ten trade books** across nearly two decades, from *The Big Idea* (Zondervan, 2007) through the movement vision of *Exponential* (2010) and *On the Verge* (2011, with **Alan Hirsch**), the redefined metrics of *Keeping Score* (2014), the relational evangelism of *Finding Your Way Back to God* (2015) and *B.L.E.S.S.* (2021), the flagship *Hero Maker* (2018, with Warren Bird, foreword by **J.D. Greear**), and his 2026 turn inward toward leader health, *Multiplier*. His frameworks — the **five hero-making practices**, the **BLESS** rhythm, the **Five Levels of a multiplying church**, **ICNU** conversations, the **five-step apprenticeship** ladder — have become working vocabulary for tens of thousands of church planters. In 2021 Lincoln Christian University gave Dave and Jon its highest alumni honor, the Restoration Award.

In 2025 he did what most leaders only write about. He folded NewThing into Exponential under the banner **"Kingdom over Castle,"** and after an **eighteen-month public apprenticeship** he handed the Lead Pastor role at Community Christian to **Ted Coniaris** and stepped back to Co-Founding Pastor — a lived case study of the succession theology he had taught for years.

And here is where the fragmentation becomes visible. For a leader of this institutional stature, **almost none of his reach is personally owned**. His most-read books are distributed and his conference audiences are convened through **Exponential** — which owns the conference email list, the podcast feeds, and the free-ebook channel through which most of his content actually travels. His **personal owned audience** is strikingly thin: roughly **511 Substack subscribers** on an awkward `daveferguson3.substack.com` URL, **~42,000 social followers** with no email pathway behind them, a small personal blog on daveferguson.org with **no email capture at all**, and a **personal YouTube channel that exists but is essentially undeveloped**. There is **no Wikipedia article** for the president of the world's largest church-planting conference. The gap between the embodied and institutional influence on one side and the personally owned digital expression on the other is among the widest in the corpus — not because the work doesn't exist, but because the work lives on rails Dave doesn't own.

## Synthesis

Dave Ferguson's fragmentation signature is unusual in the corpus: **his embodied and institutional influence is enormous and well-organized — inside institutions he built — while his personally owned digital layer is the thinnest part of his footprint.** He runs the world's largest church-planting conference, but the conference owns his email list, his podcast feeds, and the channel through which his free ebooks travel. His most-deployed frameworks (Hero Maker, Five Levels, BLESS) have no personal course or assessment expression. His ten books sit across four publishers with no connective hub. His ~42K social following has no email pathway behind it, his only owned list is ~511 free Substack subscribers on a credibility-undermining URL, and his YouTube presence is undeveloped despite 80–120+ videos scattered across institutional channels. The largest trapped value is not missing content — it is the **absence of any owned surface** that lets a reader who discovers *Hero Maker* walk into the whole body of work, and that lets the leaders he has formed stay connected to him rather than to the institutions that introduced them. For the leader who coined "Kingdom over Castle," the irony is sharp: the castle that holds his reach is one he doesn't personally own.
