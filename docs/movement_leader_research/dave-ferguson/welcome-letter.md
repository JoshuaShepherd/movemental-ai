Dave

You have built one of the most consequential bodies of work in North American church multiplication, and almost none of it is yours to reach. On March 5, 1989, you and Jon started Community Christian Church with five friends in a high school cafeteria; it became one of the first multisite churches in the country and a community of roughly 5,500. In 2001 you launched NewThing, which planted more than 6,300 churches across nearly seventy nations. You co-founded Exponential and made it the largest church-planting conference in the world. Across nearly two decades you wrote ten books — from *The Big Idea* through *Exponential* and *On the Verge* with Alan Hirsch, through *Finding Your Way Back to God* and *B.L.E.S.S.*, to *Hero Maker*, the book that gave a generation of leaders the language of permission-giving and multiplication, and now *Multiplier* in 2026. And in 2025 you did the thing you have taught for years: you folded NewThing into Exponential under "Kingdom over Castle," and you handed Community Christian's lead role to Ted Coniaris after an eighteen-month apprenticeship. You didn't just write the succession; you ran it.

All of it is somewhere. Almost none of it is in one place that belongs to you. *Hero Maker* sits on its own Zondervan product page and on heromakerbook.org; *B.L.E.S.S.* on a Salem page and bless-book.org; *Multiplier* on a Zondervan page and multiplierbook.com; *Finding Your Way Back to God* on WaterBrook; the free titles — *Keeping Score*, *Discover Your Mission Now*, *Together*, the *Lost Cause* workbooks — on Exponential's resource shelf. Four publishers, a dozen product pages, three book microsites, and no single surface where a reader who finishes *Hero Maker* can walk into the Five-Level framework, the BLESS practices, or *Multiplier*. Your articles are on Outreach, on Christianity Today, on the Global Leadership Network, on the old Verge site. Your talks are inside fifteen years of Exponential session archives. Your two podcasts were campaign series on Exponential's feed, and your fifteen-plus guest appearances — three of them with Carey Nieuwhof — live on other people's shows. There is a personal YouTube channel that is essentially empty, a personal blog with no way to capture an email, and a Substack at daveferguson3.substack.com with about five hundred free subscribers and a URL that undercuts the man who runs the world's largest planting conference. There is no Wikipedia page. The pattern is unmistakable: the reach is real and enormous, and it runs almost entirely on rails Exponential owns, not rails you own.

The relational layer fragments the same way, and more painfully, because relationship is your whole thesis. Exponential holds the conference email list and the attendee history. NewThing's records — networks and planters across seventy nations — now sit inside Exponential too. Community Christian holds thirty-five years of congregation and the apprentices you formed. The leaders you have personally raised up, the planters you have coached, the network heads you recruited — they are connected to the institutions that introduced them, not to you. You coined "Kingdom over Castle" to describe giving up a structure for the sake of the mission. The irony is that the castle holding your reach is one you don't personally own, and the people you formed can't find their way back to you without an institution in between.

The largest pool of value, and the one most at risk of going uncaptured, is in your head. The judgment behind multiplication — which leader to apprentice, which campus to launch, which network to back, when to merge and when to wait — has shaped tens of thousands of churches and is written down almost nowhere. The reasoning behind "Kingdom over Castle," the actual mechanics of the eighteen-month handoff to Ted, the relational architecture that let NewThing reproduce across nine world regions — these are the things planters most need and the things least likely to make it into a book. Your own frameworks prove the point: Hero Maker, the Five Levels, BLESS, ICNU, the apprenticeship ladder — each is a teachable system, and not one of them has a course, an assessment, or a practitioner community under your name. The "what level is your church?" question your framework already answers has no tool to answer it.
