## 3. Editorial bio

Dhati Lewis was born and raised in California, the son of an NFL player, and came to Texas on a football scholarship to the University of North Texas. Out of his collegiate years — chaplaincy to the UNT football team and early college ministry — came a vocation toward urban disciple-making, formalized through a Master's in Cross-Cultural Ministry at Dallas Theological Seminary and later a Doctor of Ministry in Great Commission Mobilization at Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary.

In 2009 he and roughly twenty-five others relocated to downtown Atlanta to found Blueprint Church, built on a conviction that the church "is not like family; it is family" and that disciple-making — not church-planting — is the engine of multiplication. He launched the Rebuild Network in 2011 to plant multi-ethnic churches in urban contexts, and articulated his disciple-making theology in *Among Wolves: Disciple-Making in the City* (B&H, 2017) and his approach to racial reconciliation in *Advocates: The Narrow Path to Racial Reconciliation* (B&H, 2019).

From 2018 he led the North American Mission Board's Send Network — voted Vice President in October 2018 and later serving as President — before departing at the end of 2021 to focus on Blueprint and a new initiative, BLVD/MyBLVD, under the Rebuild Initiative, aimed at empowering disciple-makers in majority-minority, multiethnic communities. He hosts the *Where Life Exists* and *MyBLVD* podcasts and teaches widely across the church-planting ecosystem (T4G, Mosaix, Verge, Exponential).

## 5. Frameworks

### Advocate vs. Aggravator

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2019, *Advocates: The Narrow Path to Racial Reconciliation* |
| Components | (1) Rely on Christ; (2) Run to the tension; (3) Respond with dignity |
| Visual / model | — |
| Adoption evidence | Framing for *Advocates* (B&H, 2019); used in his reconciliation teaching and conference talks |
| Source | [baptistpress.com](https://www.baptistpress.com/resource-library/news/path-to-reconciliation-focus-of-advocates/) |

An "advocate" pursues reconciliation by relying on Christ, running toward (not away from) racial tension, and responding with dignity; an "aggravator" deepens division out of self-interest, self-preservation, or self-vindication. The schema is drawn exegetically from Paul's letter to Philemon.

#### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not collapse Lewis's deliberately-held both/and tensions into a one-sided "not X but Y" resolution. His signature move is to name a binary — context vs. theology, comfort vs. obedience, fleeing vs. staying — and then refuse to choose a side, calling the listener to "run *to* the tension" rather than past it. Writing in his voice that dissolves a tension by picking the "correct" pole misreads him; he holds the tension as the doorway to mission. Derived from his *Where Ya From?* Ep. 56 framing ("either I was around people who got my context but didn't have that same commitment to theology and mission, or… they didn't get my context"). Source: [whereyafrom.org](https://whereyafrom.org/episode/episode-56-a-blueprint-for-discipleship-in-an-urban-context-with-dhati-lewis/).
