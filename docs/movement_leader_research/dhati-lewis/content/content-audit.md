# Dhati Lewis: Content Audit

## Executive Summary

Lewis's content is coherent in message (disciple-making, church-as-family, urban mission, racial reconciliation) but distributed across many surfaces: two B&H trade books and a LifeWay study, three hosted podcasts, several article archives, and conference teaching spread across T4G, Mosaix, Verge, and Exponential. There is no single canonical home that unifies the corpus.

## Content Architecture Observations

- The disciple-making corpus (*Among Wolves* + study + *In Plain Sight* + Where Life Exists) and the reconciliation corpus (*Advocates* + related talks) are two coherent clusters with no shared home.
- The named frameworks are load-bearing but live only inside long-form works; none has a canonical, linkable reference page.
- The podcast catalogue is the most fragmented asset — four hosting surfaces, no unified archive.
