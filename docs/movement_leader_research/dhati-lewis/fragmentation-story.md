# Dhati Lewis: The Fragmentation Story

## The Story

Dhati Lewis has built a coherent body of work — disciple-making in the city, the church as family, and a Christ-centered path to racial reconciliation — but that work does not live anywhere as one thing. It is scattered across two publishers, a self-published primer that has effectively disappeared, three podcasts on four different hosting platforms, a personal site, two organizational sites, half a dozen contributor archives, and years of conference talks held on other people's stages. A pastor looking for "the Dhati Lewis framework for disciple-making" has no single place to find it; they have to reassemble it from fragments.

## Informational Fragmentation

Where his content actually lives:

- **Books** — *Among Wolves* and *Advocates* on B&H / Amazon / retail; the *Among Wolves* Bible study and leader kit on LifeWay; *In Plain Sight* (his 2013 primer) on no retail surface at all — reportedly a free e-book with no findable ISBN or product page. ([content/books.md](./content/books.md))
- **Frameworks** — the Advocate/Aggravator triad, the Eight Movements in Matthew, "church as family," and the "7 Stops of an Urban Missionary" are all load-bearing, but each lives buried inside a book or a podcast series. None has a canonical, linkable reference. ([content/frameworks.md](./content/frameworks.md))
- **Audio** — *Where Life Exists* on his site/YouTube, *MyBLVD Podcast* on Podbean and iHeart, *We Are Send Network* on Apple Podcasts. Three shows, four hubs, no unified archive. ([content/audio.md](./content/audio.md))
- **Articles** — pieces spread across ERLC, MyBLVD, Saturate, Outreach, and Christian Post; several archives are not even crawlable (HTTP 403), so the full catalogue is effectively invisible. ([content/articles.md](./content/articles.md))
- **Teaching** — conference sessions on T4G, Mosaix, Verge, Exponential, and Moody Audio — all on borrowed platforms. ([digital-presence/platforms.md](./digital-presence/platforms.md))
- **Web** — a personal site, Blueprint Church, MyBLVD, plus publisher and contributor pages: 5+ domains, no hub. ([digital-presence/websites.md](./digital-presence/websites.md))
- **In his head / undocumented** — the components of the "7 Stops of an Urban Missionary" aren't fully written down anywhere public; the *In Plain Sight* material predates and underlies *Among Wolves* but is inaccessible.

## Relational Fragmentation

His relational capital is real but dispersed: a national church-planting network he once led (Send Network), his own network (Rebuild/MyBLVD), a congregation (Blueprint), and a web of peers and endorsers (J.D. Greear, Derwin Gray, Kevin Ezell, Daniel Im, Daniel Yang, Michael Stewart, Larry Grays, Vance Pitman, Missie Branch). These relationships are visible only in scattered traces — an endorsement here, a co-hosted episode there, a succession announcement — never assembled into a single map of who he is connected to and how. ([network/organizations.md](./network/organizations.md))

## Synthesis

The problem is not coherence of message — Lewis is unusually consistent. The problem is **consolidation and discoverability**: the corpus is fragmented across surfaces he doesn't own, the named frameworks have no canonical home, the podcast catalogue is split four ways, and an entire early work has fallen off the map. The disciple-making vision that animates everything he does is, ironically, hard to disciple-make *from*, because no one place holds it.
