# Theological Profile: Dhati Lewis

## Core Thesis

Disciple-making — not church-planting — is the engine of the church's mission, and the church is best understood as *family*. "If you plant a church you are not guaranteed to make disciples, but if you make disciples the logical result is" a church/family. Out of this flows a vision for the urban context: to be "the last generation to leave the urban context for sound discipleship." ([saturatetheworld.com](https://saturatetheworld.com/2017/07/20/dont-just-start-church-establish-family/), [namb.net](https://www.namb.net/news/send-networks-dhati-lewis-leaving-namb-for-new-project/))

## Theological Tradition

Southern Baptist (SBC). Trained at Dallas Theological Seminary (M.A.) and Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary (D.Min.); leadership tenure at NAMB/Send Network situates him squarely in conservative evangelical SBC church-planting streams. No explicit "Reformed" self-identification was found in sources reviewed. ([newchurches.com](https://www.newchurches.com/contributor/dhati-lewis/))

## Key Frameworks & Models

- **Advocate vs. Aggravator** (*Advocates*, 2019): an advocate pursues reconciliation by (1) relying on Christ, (2) running to the tension, (3) responding with dignity; an aggravator deepens division out of self-interest. Drawn exegetically from Philemon.
- **Eight Movements in Matthew** (*Among Wolves*, 2017): a disciple-making culture built by tracing eight movements in Matthew's Gospel.
- **Church as Family**: identity and belonging precede activity; "the church is not like family; it is family."
- **The 7 Stops of an Urban Missionary** (MyBLVD Podcast): a teaching arc on the formation of an urban missionary.

Full detail in [../content/frameworks.md](../content/frameworks.md).

## Distinctive Positions

- The church *is* literally family, not merely *like* family.
- Disciple-making is primary; church-planting is its logical result, not its precondition.
- Racial reconciliation is a Christ-centered "narrow path" of advocacy, distinct from politically-driven "aggravation"; heart posture precedes political positioning, and all parties share responsibility for reconciliation regardless of blame distribution.
- Mission happens "where life exists" — in the majority-minority urban context that the church has too often abandoned.

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Upstream:** Jeff Christopherson (Send Network missiology, served under him); Kevin Ezell (NAMB Send North America strategy). Theological training at Dallas Theological Seminary and Southeastern. Named authorial mentors are not documented in available sources.
- **Downstream:** Rebuild Network church plants; ethnic diversification of Send Network church planters; Send Network–NAAF partnership; mentoring of urban church planters via BLVD/MyBLVD.

## Theological Evolution

His thought moves from a primer on reclaiming local-church discipleship (*In Plain Sight*, reported 2013) → a developed disciple-making ecclesiology rooted in Matthew (*Among Wolves*, 2017) → an applied theology of racial reconciliation rooted in Philemon (*Advocates*, 2019) → an institutional expression in Send Network leadership and now a mentoring/formation focus through BLVD/MyBLVD. The through-line is constant: disciple-making and church-as-family carried into the urban, multiethnic context.
