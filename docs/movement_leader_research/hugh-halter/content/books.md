# Books: Hugh Halter

### The Tangible Kingdom: Creating Incarnational Community

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Published | 2008 |
| Co-author | Matt Smay |
| Publisher | Jossey-Bass / Wiley (Leadership Network Series) |
| URL | https://www.amazon.com/Tangible-Kingdom-Creating-Incarnational-Community/dp/0470188979 |

**Description**: Halter and Smay's foundational work on making the reign of God visible and touchable through incarnational community rather than attractional programming — the book that introduced "the tangible kingdom."

### Tangible Kingdom Primer

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Published | 2009 |
| Co-author | Matt Smay |
| Publisher | Missio Publishing |
| URL | https://www.amazon.com/Tangible-Kingdom-Primer-Missio-Publishing/dp/B002PJARWU |

### AND: The Gathered and Scattered Church

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Published | 2010 |
| Co-author | Matt Smay |
| Publisher | Zondervan (Exponential Series) |
| URL | https://www.amazon.com/Gathered-Scattered-Church-Exponential/dp/0310325854 |

**Description**: Argues the church must be both gathered and scattered — refusing the either/or between attractional and missional models and holding the two in creative tension.

### Sacrilege: Finding Life in the Unorthodox Ways of Jesus

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Published | 2011 |
| Publisher | Baker Books |
| URL | https://bakeracademic.com/authors/hugh-halter/9494 |

**Description**: A provocative re-reading of Jesus' unorthodox, boundary-crossing ways as a pattern for a church that has grown too tidy and respectable.

### BiVo: A Modern-Day Guide for Bi-Vocational Saints

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Published | 2013 |
| Publisher | Missio Publishing |
| URL | https://www.amazon.com/Bivo-Modern-Day-Guide-Bi-Vocational-Saints/dp/0983086443 |

### Brimstone: The Art and Act of Holy Nonjudgment

|-------|-------|
