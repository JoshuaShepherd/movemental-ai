# Frameworks: Hugh Halter

### The Tangible Kingdom (Incarnational Community)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2008 (*The Tangible Kingdom*, with Matt Smay) |
| Components | Incarnational presence; communities built around homes, tables, and neighborhoods; making the reign of God visible and touchable rather than abstract |
| Source | [The Tangible Kingdom](https://www.amazon.com/Tangible-Kingdom-Creating-Incarnational-Community/dp/0470188979) |

The conviction that the church is most faithful when it makes the kingdom of God tangible and local — embodied in ordinary community rather than confined to attractional programming.

### The Gathered and Scattered Church (AND)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2010 (*AND: The Gathered and Scattered Church*, with Matt Smay) |
| Components | Refusal of the attractional/missional either/or; rhythm of gathering for worship and scattering as sent people; holding both modes in tension |
| Source | [AND](https://www.amazon.com/Gathered-Scattered-Church-Exponential/dp/0310325854) |

The church is not gathered *or* scattered but both — a sent people who breathe in worship and formation and breathe out into the everyday world.

### BiVo (Bi-Vocational Saints)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2013 (*BiVo: A Modern-Day Guide for Bi-Vocational Saints*) |
| Components | Bi-vocational ministry as calling not compromise; staying woven into ordinary work and economy; Pauline pattern of self-supporting mission |
| Source | [BiVo](https://www.amazon.com/Bivo-Modern-Day-Guide-Bi-Vocational-Saints/dp/0983086443) |

A reframing of bi-vocational ministry as a positive calling that keeps leaders embedded in the economic and relational life of their place.

### Holy Nonjudgment

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2015 (*Brimstone: The Art and Act of Holy Nonjudgment*) |
| Components | A posture toward outsiders that refuses condemnation; truth held without softening; modeled on how Jesus made room for the religiously excluded |
| Source | [Brimstone](https://www.amazon.com/Brimstone-Art-Act-Holy-Nonjudgment/dp/1434706532) |

A discipline of moving toward people the religious world dismisses — neither condemning nor diluting truth, but practicing the welcome Jesus practiced.

### Family as Mission (Righteous Brood)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*Righteous Brood*, with Dave & Sue Ferguson); expands a 2012 ebook *A Righteous Brood* |
| Components | The family as a unit of mission rather than private retreat; the mission of God as a shared family story |
| Source | [Righteous Brood](https://www.amazon.com/Righteous-Brood-Making-Mission-Family/dp/1955142297) |

The household becomes a sent community in its own right, making the mission of God a story the whole family lives together.

### Kingdom Ecosystems (Brave Cities)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2024 (*Brave Cities*, with Taylor McCall) |
| Components | Whole-city renewal; archaeology, artistry, and architecture of place; coalescence of homes, reconciliation, discipleship, worship, enterprise, activism, and justice |
| Source | [Brave Cities](https://www.100movements.org/books/brave-cities-taylor-mccall-hugh-halter) |

A vision for building kingdom ecosystems — whole cities where worship, enterprise, justice, and reconciliation coalesce into biblical community rather than fragment.
