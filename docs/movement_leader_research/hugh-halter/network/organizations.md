# Organizations & Networks: Hugh Halter

## Lantern Network (Alton, Illinois, founded 2016)

The organization Halter founded and currently leads as president. Lantern Network is based in Alton, Illinois, and serves as the umbrella for his current season of work in regenerating a place — with Post Commons as its innovation, enterprise, and mission hub.

## Post Commons (Alton, Illinois, opened 2018)

A revived federal post office building that Halter renovated into a community hub for "connection, food, coffee and collaboration" — described as the living room of Alton. It functions as the physical center of Lantern Network's enterprise and mission activity.

## Brave Cities

A network and consultancy co-led by Halter and Taylor McCall, organized around "Building Kingdom Ecosystems." It coaches apostolic leaders to build whole-city expressions of the gospel and is the home of his most recent body of work, anchored by the 2024 book *Brave Cities* (100 Movements Publishing). Web: https://www.bravecities.com/

## Adullam (Denver, Colorado)

The congregational network of incarnational communities Halter founded and pastored in Denver — built as a network of small sent communities rather than a single Sunday service. Now part of his earlier season; legacy team and cohort information persists on dated SnapPages-hosted pages.

## Missio

An apprenticing network for missional leaders that Halter co-directed in Denver with longtime co-author and collaborator Matt Smay. Missio Publishing (the related imprint) published the *Tangible Kingdom Primer*, *BiVo*, and the original 2012 *A Righteous Brood* ebook. Legacy site: http://missio.snappages.com/

## Forge America

The missional training network for which Halter served as U.S. director. Through Forge, his relational reach extends into the broader missional training and church-planting ecosystem.

## Rí Beag Refuge

An 80-acre equine-therapy farm Halter operates, extending his incarnational, place-based practice into a ministry of presence and healing.

## Life as Mission

A publishing and content series (https://lifeasmission.co/) under which *Happy Hour* and *Righteous Brood* were released.

## Relational orbit

Halter's collaborations place him within wider missional networks: co-author Matt Smay (the early trilogy, plus Missio and Forge ties), Dave and Sue Ferguson (*Righteous Brood*, Exponential), Taylor McCall (*Brave Cities*), and connections to Exponential, Missio Alliance, and the 100 Movements / Movement Leaders Collective orbit through his publishers and podcast appearances.
