# Identity: Hugh Halter

## Who he is

Hugh Halter is a North American missional church leader, author, speaker, and consultant based in Alton, Illinois. With his wife Cheryl, he has planted churches and served as a missionary across three cities — Portland, Denver, and Alton — over more than thirty years.

## Roles

- **Founder and president, Lantern Network** (Alton, Illinois; founded 2016)
- **Founder, Post Commons** (Alton, Illinois; opened 2018) — a renovated federal post office building serving as a community hub for connection, food, coffee, and collaboration
- **Co-director, Brave Cities** — a network and consultancy building "kingdom ecosystems," co-led with Taylor McCall
- **Operator, Rí Beag Refuge** — an 80-acre equine-therapy farm
- **Former founder and lead pastor, Adullam** (Denver) — a congregational network of incarnational communities
- **Former co-director, Missio** (Denver) — an apprenticing network for missional leaders, co-directed with Matt Smay
- **Former U.S. director, Forge America** — the missional training network

## Traditions and lineage

Halter works within the missional and incarnational church streams, with ties to the Forge, Exponential, Missio Alliance, and 100 Movements / Movement Leaders Collective orbits. His thinking is shaped by the conviction that the church is most itself when it lives among, rather than apart from, the people and places it is sent to.

## What he is known for

- Incarnational community and "the tangible kingdom"
- The gathered-and-scattered ("AND") church
- Bi-vocational ("BiVo") ministry
- Holy nonjudgment
- Family as mission
- Kingdom ecosystems and the renewal of place
