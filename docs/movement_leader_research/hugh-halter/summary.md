# Digital Reality Profile: Hugh Halter

Hugh Halter is an Alton, Illinois-based missional church leader, author, speaker, and consultant — founder of Lantern Network (2016) and Post Commons (2018), former U.S. director of Forge America, former national director of Missio, and former lead pastor/architect of Adullam, the congregational network of incarnational communities in Denver, Colorado. His body of work centers on incarnational community, the gathered-and-scattered church, bi-vocational ("BiVo") ministry, holy nonjudgment, family-as-mission, and most recently "kingdom ecosystems." The work is substantial and coherent across two decades, but it is dispersed across multiple publishers, organizations, and platforms with no single owned hub.

## Bio Synthesis

Halter and his wife Cheryl have been church planters and missionaries in North America for more than thirty years, pioneering missional church expressions in Portland (Oregon), Denver (Colorado), and now Alton (Illinois). In Denver he founded and pastored Adullam and co-directed Missio (an apprenticing network for missional leaders) with longtime co-author and collaborator Matt Smay. He served as U.S. director of Forge America (the missional training network). In 2016 he founded Lantern Network in Alton, and in 2018 he revived the city's shuttered federal post office building as Post Commons — a community hub for "connection, food, coffee and collaboration" that functions as the innovation/enterprise/mission hub for Lantern. He also runs Rí Beag Refuge, an 80-acre equine-therapy farm. His most recent work is built around "kingdom ecosystems" through the Brave Cities network/consultancy, co-led with Taylor McCall.

## Current Role

Founder and president of Lantern Network and founder of Post Commons (Alton, Illinois); co-director of Brave Cities. Author, speaker, and consultant.
