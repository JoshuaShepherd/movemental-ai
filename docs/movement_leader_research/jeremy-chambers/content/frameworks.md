# Frameworks

Jeremy Chambers' frameworks are co-developed with Monica Paredes Chambers and sit at the seam of contemplative formation and missional movement. Each is grounded in his published books and documented teaching; borrowed frameworks are labeled as such.

### Six Foundational Paradigms (Kingdom Contours)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Kingdom Contours*, 1st ed.); refined 2024–25 (Revised Edition) |
| Components | (1) Christocentric and Trinitarian; (2) Discipleship — being and making Jesus followers; (3) Incarnational Living — a missional strategic mindset; (4) Movement Wisdom — being movemental; (5) Organic Systems — structuring for multiplication; (6) Deep Community — taking on risks by faith |
| Adoption | Curricular spine of Arise RVA, Arise Church Denver, and Pando Collective microchurch coaching; paired with a "Baby Steps: How to Start a Micro-Church" appendix |
| Source | [100 Movements — Kingdom Contours](https://www.100movements.org/books/kingdom-contours-jeremy-chambers-monica-paredes-chambers) |

Six planter-facing paradigms that extend and reframe Alan Hirsch's mDNA into an equipping toolkit, foregrounding Deep Community as the relational substrate the other five depend on.

### The Art of Missional Spirituality — 31 Sacred Practices

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*The Art of Missional Spirituality: 31 Sacred Practices for Jesus-Followers*) |
| Components | Thirty-one daily practices — one per day for a month — fusing classical disciplines (Praying the Hours, silence, solitude, immersion in Ecclesiastes) with missional-contemplative hybrids ("Secret Acts of Kindness," neighborhood blessing, hospitality, walking prayer) |
| Structure | Calendar-month progression; the recurring image is "rhythm" holding the contemplative and missional poles together |
| Source | [jemochambers.com — The Art of Missional Spirituality](https://www.jemochambers.com/the-art-of-missional-spirituality) |

A month-long, calendar-structured rule of life whose core thesis is the integration of mission and contemplation — "spirituality that doesn't draw you away from your neighbors but propels you into the lives of others and the needs of your city."

### Calling vs. Assignment

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2025 ("A Few Solutions for Compassion Fatigue," *Missio Alliance*) |
| Components | (1) **Calling** — "overall trajectory oriented by God"; (2) **Assignment** — "temporary action highlighted by the Holy Spirit"; companion discernment tool is Alan Hirsch's APEST assessment |
| Use | Pastoral diagnostic for missional practitioners experiencing burnout from over-attachment to a specific assignment at the expense of their broader calling |
| Source | [Missio Alliance — A Few Solutions for Compassion Fatigue](https://missioalliance.org/a-few-solutions-for-compassion-fatigue/) |

A two-term distinction that guards missional longevity by separating the durable trajectory God orients (calling) from the temporary tasks the Spirit highlights (assignment).

### Microchurch as a Form (not the form)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Operative across Arise RVA (2013–) and Pando Collective coaching; articulated in *Kingdom Contours* and 2024 interviews |
| Components | Network-of-microchurches as the most adaptive ecclesial form for movement — "nimble, very adaptable" — coupled with explicit anti-absolutism ("it's not the only way") |
| Posture | Coaches a shift from attractional, event-based models toward dispersed, community-focused micro-expressions of the church, without treating microchurch as the only valid form |
| Source | [The Pando Collective — What We Do](https://thepandocollective.com/what-we-do); [Pivotal People (2024)](https://pivotalpeople.buzzsprout.com/1909281/episodes/14107088) |

Microchurch is held as the most adaptive ecclesial form for movement while explicitly resisting method absolutism — a distinctive posture within microchurch advocacy.

### Forge / Pando Apprenticeship and Training

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Forge America training pipeline (Training Director role, 2024–) and Pando Denver coaching (2020–) |
| Components | Trainer-of-trainers apprenticeship — equipping leaders to network, resource, and launch missional micro-expressions; modules include *Who is Forge America?*, *Introduction to Mission*, and *Mobilizing Everyday Disciples* (team-credited) |
| Lineage | Builds on Rowland Smith's Pando model and Hirsch's Forge formula; APEST-aligned, coaching-based, distributed leadership |
| Source | [Forge America — Our People](https://www.forgeamerica.com/our-people); [The Pando Collective](https://thepandocollective.com/) |

An apprenticeship-based, trainer-of-trainers framework that forms everyday disciples and planters to launch and resource missional micro-expressions across a region.

### Cycle of Grace (adopted — Hudson / Haas)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | Not original to Chambers; used as his core compassion-fatigue diagnostic |
| Components | Acceptance → Sustenance → Significance → Fruitfulness; the **reversed cycle** (Fruitfulness → Significance → Acceptance) is the anti-pattern that depletes ministers |
| Use | Taught in his 2025 Missio Alliance essay and referenced in "Finishing Well" (Renovaré, 2022) as a guard for sustainable mission |
| Source | [Missio Alliance — A Few Solutions for Compassion Fatigue](https://missioalliance.org/a-few-solutions-for-compassion-fatigue/) |

A borrowed four-stage diagnostic (Trevor Hudson / Jerry Haas) that Chambers uses to name how ministers deplete themselves by reversing grace's order — chasing fruitfulness before resting in acceptance.
