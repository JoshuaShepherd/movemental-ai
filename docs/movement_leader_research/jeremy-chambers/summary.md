# Jeremy Chambers

Jeremy "Jemo" Chambers is an American church planter, spiritual director, and missional practitioner whose distinctive calling is to refuse the dichotomy between intimacy with God and engagement with neighbors — to hold contemplative formation and missionary sentness as a single rhythm rather than two sequential stages. Working almost always as a writing-and-teaching dyad with his wife Monica Paredes Chambers, he occupies a rare seam in the contemporary missional church: the intersection of the Renovaré contemplative tradition (the Foster, Willard, and Hudson lineage) and the Hirsch-Frost, Forge, and 100 Movements missional-movement stream. After undergraduate study at Johnson University and two master's degrees from Trinity Evangelical Divinity School, Jeremy and Monica founded the Arise RVA Church Network in Richmond, Virginia in 2013, growing a network of microchurches before relocating to Denver in late 2019 to help launch the Denver expression of The Pando Collective alongside Rowland Smith. He now serves as Training Director of Forge America, the U.S. arm of Alan Hirsch's globally networked missional training movement, making him a curricular steward for the next generation of Forge-formed practitioners. His body of work — the two co-authored books *Kingdom Contours* (2020; revised 2024–25) and *The Art of Missional Spirituality* (2023), a sustained essay archive across the V3 Movement, Renovaré, and Missio Alliance, and a 2024 podcast circuit — consistently presents missional life as a way of being rather than a strategy to execute. His significance lies in carrying a genuinely integrative thesis into a movement tradition that has often treated spiritual formation as supportive rather than constitutive of mission.

## Calling

Chambers' load-bearing conviction is that spirituality "doesn't draw you away from your neighbors but propels you into the lives of others and the needs of your city." He operationalizes this as rhythm — daily practices that fold formation and sentness together — rather than as program or campaign.

## Body of work

- **Books:** *The Art of Missional Spirituality: 31 Sacred Practices for Jesus-Followers* (2023) and *Kingdom Contours* (2020 original; revised edition 2024–25), both co-authored with Monica Paredes Chambers and published in the 100 Movements Publishing orbit, with forewords from Alan Hirsch, Debra Hirsch, and Michael Frost.
- **Frameworks:** the Six Foundational Paradigms of *Kingdom Contours*; the 31 Sacred Practices; the Calling-vs-Assignment diagnostic; the adopted Cycle of Grace (Hudson/Haas); and the coined term "Grativangelism."
- **Essays and audio:** an essay archive at the V3 Movement (2018–2021), Renovaré (2022–2023), and Missio Alliance (2025), plus a 2024 book-launch podcast circuit.

## Significance

Chambers is a practitioner-theologian who pairs microchurch planting with contemplative discipline, resists method absolutism ("microchurch is *a* form, not *the* form"), names distraction rather than opposition as the dominant spiritual threat, and models a marriage-as-writing-dyad rare in the missional publishing space.
