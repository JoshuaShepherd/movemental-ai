## Executive Summary

Joel Repic is, as of **August 16, 2025**, the new Executive Director of the **Underground Network** (Tampa, FL) — a 20-year-old family of **100+ microchurches** with **30+ sister movements worldwide**, founded by Brian Sanders and stewarded for five years under Lucas Pulley. Repic succeeds Pulley and follows Sanders' founding generation, inheriting one of the most theologically distinctive, institutionally mature microchurch networks in North America.

He arrives in Tampa after **20 years embedded in Aliquippa, Pennsylvania** — a post-industrial Ohio River Valley community where he and his wife Chelsea moved onto a "drug and prostitution corner" and began inviting people to live with them. Out of that soil Joel founded **Aliquippa Impact** (2005), a youth development organization; pastored at **The Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance**; helped catalyze what became **The Greenhouse Network** (formalized 2018) — a decentralized family of nonprofits, social enterprises, missional communities, businesses, and church plants; and served as **Church Planting Director for the Christian and Missionary Alliance, Western Pennsylvania District**.

His body of public-facing content is **conspicuously thin for someone of his ministry weight**: no books, no course, no newsletter of record, a barely-used personal site at **hopeisprotest.com** (two indexed posts), a handful of sermons on a church podcast feed, and a small but active Instagram. Meanwhile he has spent 20 years generating the kind of embodied, place-based, indigenous-leadership wisdom that the microchurch and movement-multiplication ecosystem is hungry for. **The gap between his embodied work and his digital expression is enormous** — which is exactly the fragmentation Movemental exists to resolve.

Repic's network is strategically placed: **Brian Sanders** (Underground founder, author of *Underground Church* and *Microchurches: A Smaller Way*), **Lucas Pulley** (Movements Director, existing Movemental research subject), **Stacy Gaskins** and **Tomy Wilkerson** (Underground executive team), **Bill Couchenour** (board chair, also in Movemental research pipeline), plus the **Exponential** and **C&MA** ecosystems and a visible intersection with **Hugh Halter**, **Bre Wiebe**, and **Dori Yuen** via the Microchurch Summit. Onboarding Joel is also an adjacent onboarding lever for the rest of the Underground executive team and board.

A structured long-form interview series (10–15 conversations) with Movemental's editorial team would surface the frameworks he has formed but never codified. (HIGHEST priority, MEDIUM effort)

An embedded Movemental newsletter ("Hope Is Protest" retained as the masthead) converts his Instagram + X + Underground reach into a subscriber list he owns for the first time in his ministry career. (HIGH priority, LOW effort)

3. **Co-build a Microchurch Movement Leader pathway with Brian Sanders and Lucas Pulley.** Joel is the third Underground leader in Movemental's pipeline. A coordinated three-voice pathway — Sanders (founding theology of *Microchurches: A Smaller Way*), Pulley (movement multiplication + coaching), Repic (neighborhood incarnation + marginal communities) — would be the most credible microchurch offering on the internet. (HIGH priority, HIGH effort — requires 3-way coordination)

4. **Digitize the Greenhouse Network methodology.** The Greenhouse Lab (coaching + finance + marketing + training incubator formalized in 2018) is one of the most under-published indigenous-leadership frameworks in American church planting. A Movemental course — "Greenhouse: Indigenous Leadership for Post-Industrial Communities" — is latent and waiting. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort)

5. **Open the sermon archive.** 6+ confirmed Gospel Tabernacle sermons (2018–2021) live in a church-podcast feed that is invisible under Joel's name. Migrating those to a searchable Movemental author hub with transcript + topical tagging triples their discoverability overnight. (MEDIUM priority, LOW effort)
