## Who actually listens to Joel

Joel's audience is best understood as **three overlapping concentric circles**, each tied to one of his career seasons — and each of which Movemental can help consolidate into a single owned audience for the first time.

### Circle 1: The Tampa Underground inheritance (primary, growing)

- **100+ microchurches** in the Tampa Bay area
- **30+ sister movements worldwide** (Cape Town, Manila, Dublin, Dallas, others)
- **Underground Network staff, board, governing elders, lead team**
- **Microchurches Podcast listeners** (institutional podcast listeners — volume UNKNOWN but likely in the low thousands)
- Attendees of Tampa Underground gatherings, training cohorts, telehealth clients, and downstream community
- **Growing**, because Joel has just begun this role

### Circle 2: The Aliquippa / Pittsburgh legacy (primary, mature)

- **Aliquippa Impact alumni and staff** (20 years of cohorts = hundreds of young people now in their 20s, 30s)
- **Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance congregation** (regular sermon audience 2018–2021+)
- **Greenhouse Network leaders** (dozens of indigenous leaders across Aliquippa and broader Pittsburgh region)
- **CMA Western PA District church planters** (dozens coached and developed)
- **Christian Counselors Collaborative community** (Pittsburgh mental-health / counseling network)
- **Regional donor base** supporting Aliquippa Impact + Greenhouse Network
- **Mature**, relationally deep, less likely to scale dramatically but highly loyal

### Circle 3: The ecosystem-reach audience (secondary, latent)

- **Exponential / church-planting conference attendees** (national evangelical audience)
- **Leadership Network followers** (leadership practitioner audience)
- **CMA national denominational audience**
- **Microchurch Summit audience** (practitioner microchurch ecosystem)
- **Brian Sanders' / Lucas Pulley's / Hugh Halter's audiences** (via adjacency)
- **Latent**, large in potential, currently reached only sporadically through single-moment exposures (one podcast appearance, one feature article, etc.)

## What his audience wants from him

Based on his voice profile and his current moment:

1. **Permission to stay small and faithful.** His audience is full of leaders who are *not* trying to build megachurches and who need ongoing theological encouragement that the small, place-specific work matters. "Hope is protest" scratches this itch directly.
2. **A framework for indigenous leadership** among communities most pastors would write off.
3. **Modeling for parenting in microchurch life** (evident in the Ep. 101 format).
4. **Ongoing ED-level teaching** to the Underground family.
5. **A voice that intersects community development and ecclesiology** — a blend rarer than either alone.
6. **Companionship for post-industrial / decline / loss contexts.** Aliquippa-shaped communities exist across the US Rust Belt, Appalachia, and beyond; his voice speaks to those contexts.

## Growth trajectory

Joel's audience is in a **rare inflection moment**:

- His Tampa Underground role (Aug 2025 →) effectively doubles or triples his natural audience overnight vs. his Aliquippa tenure.
- He is **less than a year** into this expanded reach.
- He has **no owned capture mechanism** (newsletter) to convert new reach into durable relationship.

A Movemental-hosted newsletter launched in Month 1 of onboarding could plausibly accumulate:

- **250–500** subscribers from existing Underground / CMA / Greenhouse warm list
- **1,000–2,500** by Month 6 if the Underground cross-promotes
- **3,000–5,000** by Year 1

The audience is not theoretical. It is warm, pre-existing, already reading and listening to Joel — just not in any single place.

## Implications for content strategy

1. **Lead with story, not framework.** His audience came to him through neighborhood narrative, not through systematic theology.
2. **Honor the cross-class composition.** Do not write content that is implicitly middle-class-only; many of his readers are leaders among the poor.
3. **Feature Chelsea and family rhythms where appropriate.** Ep. 101 suggests this is natural to his audience and genuine to his voice.
4. **Treat the Underground family as primary, ecosystem audience as secondary.** Don't over-optimize for Exponential / Leadership Network reach at the expense of Underground intimacy.
