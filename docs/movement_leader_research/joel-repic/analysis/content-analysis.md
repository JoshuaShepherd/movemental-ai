## Editorial structure of his typical teaching

### Sermon structure (inferred from titles and available material)

Joel's sermon titles reveal a narrative + topical pattern:

- "Acts: Jesus Uses The Pain" — narrative text, redemptive pain motif
- "Sharing the Gospel: Matthew 10" — specific text, evangelism theme
- "Biblical Justice: It Matters to God" — topical, theological claim as title
- "Reconciliation: Dividing Walls Falling" — topical, Ephesian imagery
- "Generational Transfer" — topical, ecclesial/leadership theme
- "Giving God Our Limitations" — topical, lament/humility

He does not appear to operate from a strict verse-by-verse lectio continua model. His preaching is **topically themed but text-anchored**, which aligns with his narrative-first voice profile and his convictional (rather than systematic) theology.

### Podcast interview structure (Exponential FCP #22)

Joel's Exponential interview shape follows what sounds like a standard practitioner-podcast arc:

1. **Origin story** — Aliquippa post-industrial context, early Aliquippa Impact
2. **Growth narrative** — organic multiplication over a decade
3. **Framework naming** — three core insights (growth with values integrity, indigenous leadership, innovation as curation)
4. **Strategic forward look** — four partnership models, Greenhouse Lab capacity, new church plants
5. **Theological anchors** — "Jesus works best in weakness," "our community hustles"

This is a serviceable arc that can be **re-run across different episodes and different audiences** with different emphases. For Movemental oral-history capture, this structure can be adapted across 10–15 interviews by holding the origin constant and rotating the emphasis (community development / ecclesiology / formation / reconciliation / prayer / etc.) per session.

### Written structure (Hope Is Protest posts)

Both indexed posts ("Jesu, Joy of Man's Desiring"; "Jesus in the Garbage") appear to be **short reflective essays** built on a specific scene:

- Opening scene (thunderstorm / garbage conversation)
- Movement into reflection
- Theological / spiritual interpretation
- Quiet closing (not a resolution, not a summary — a settled presence)

This is essayistic, not argumentative. It rewards close reading rather than scannability. It is also a form that **scales modestly** — meaning any single post reaches hundreds rather than thousands of readers, but the depth of engagement per reader is high. This is the right form for a trusted-voice layer; it is the wrong form if the goal is broadcast virality.
