## Summary

**Joel Repic has not published any online courses.** He has, however, spent 20 years operating **embodied training programs** that function like curricula — most notably:

1. **Aliquippa Impact's cohort-based mentoring model** (2005 →) — a 13+ year formation pathway for young people tracked from elementary through high school and beyond.
2. **The Greenhouse Lab** (2018 →) — an incubator-as-service offering coaching, financial services, marketing, and training to network leaders and to poor communities starting businesses.
3. **CMA Western PA church-planting development** (pre-2025) — denominational training of planters inside the C&MA structure.

None of these exist as **publicly accessible, structured digital courses**. They are all delivered in-person and relationally.

## Embodied training programs

### Aliquippa Impact (youth development)

- **Format**: Cohort-based, 8–10 young people per cohort
- **Duration**: Elementary school → high school and beyond
- **Content**: Mentoring + school-based programming + community-based programming + after-school programming
- **Instructor model**: Staff live as neighbors in the community
- **Three individual goals per participant per year**
- **Differentiator**: "90% of what we do is show up"

**Digital translation potential**: A Movemental-hosted module titled something like *"Cohort Formation for Post-Industrial Community Youth"* could capture the methodology as a guide for other urban youth workers. Medium effort.

### The Greenhouse Lab (incubator-as-service)

- **Format**: Service platform for network leaders
- **Services**: Coaching, financial support, marketing support, training
- **Purpose**: Help people imagine and start missional initiatives; provide pathways for the poor to create their own businesses
- **Formalized**: 2018

**Digital translation potential**: The most promising single course asset in Joel's portfolio. A Movemental-hosted course titled *"The Greenhouse Method: Indigenous Leadership for Post-Industrial Communities"* (6–8 modules) could draw from:

- The Exponential FCP #22 podcast
- The Leadership Network feature
- Oral-history interviews
- New written modules under Joel's byline

Estimated readiness: **V1 deliverable in 3–6 months** of Movemental onboarding.

### CMA Western PA church-planting development

- **Format**: Denominational training context; specifics UNKNOWN
- **Content**: Coaching and development of CMA church planters in Western PA

**Digital translation potential**: Low in the short term — this material may be bound by CMA institutional rights, and the specific curriculum is not publicly visible. Long-term, elements could inform a general "church planting in post-industrial communities" offering.

## Recommendation

Launching a Joel Repic course on Movemental is **a 6–12 month, not a Month-1 goal**. The right sequence is:

1. Build the owned audience (newsletter) — Month 1–3
2. Capture oral history and produce written content — Month 2–6
3. Extract frameworks from that material — Month 4–8
4. Produce Course v1 — Month 6–12

The premature path (ship a course in Month 1) would either misrepresent his readiness or force him into a framework-first voice that isn't his.
