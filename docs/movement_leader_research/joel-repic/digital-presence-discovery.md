## Top-level finding

Joel Repic's digital footprint is **dramatically smaller than his 20-year ministry impact would lead you to expect**. His public presence is best described as:

1. **An institutional bio page** at the Tampa Underground (his new employer as of Aug 16, 2025).
2. **A dormant personal reflective blog** at hopeisprotest.com (2 indexed posts).
3. **A handful of sermon audio files** on The Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance podcast feed (Aliquippa).
4. **A modest social presence**, most active on Instagram (~934 followers).
5. **A network of institutional platforms** he appears on but does not own — Leadership Network, Exponential, Christian Counselors Collaborative, CMA archive.

There is **no newsletter, no personal podcast, no personal YouTube channel, no course, no book page, no Amazon author page** — a configuration that is vanishingly rare among leaders of Underground Network's scale.

## What this tells us

The thin digital presence is **not evidence of thin ministry**. It is evidence of a leader whose 20 years have been oriented to relational, incarnational, place-specific work — exactly the kind of body of work that is both most valuable and most at risk of evaporating when the practitioner moves on. Joel's transition from Aliquippa to Tampa in 2025 is the textbook moment to begin externalizing that body of work before the Aliquippa chapter ends and the Tampa chapter overwrites it.
