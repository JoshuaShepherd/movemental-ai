## The strategic implication

This is arguably the single most concrete gap in Joel's digital infrastructure and the single highest-leverage Movemental intervention.

**He has never had an owned email list.** Twenty years of ministry have produced hundreds of relationships — CMA planters, Aliquippa Impact cohorts, Greenhouse Network leaders, Underground peers — none of which are consolidated into an email channel he controls. A Movemental-hosted newsletter ("Hope Is Protest," repurposed as the masthead) would:

1. Give him the first owned audience of his career.
2. Convert institutional reach (Underground, CMA) into personal reach.
3. Preserve the Hope Is Protest brand (already distinct) rather than abandoning it.
4. Provide the distribution layer for everything else Movemental helps him build — long-form interviews, essays, oral-history episodes, the eventual book.
5. Set a 2–4 issue/month cadence well-matched to his story-first voice.

Estimated realistic growth targets:
- **End of Month 1**: 250–500 subscribers (pulled from Underground / CMA / Greenhouse warm network)
- **End of Month 6**: 1,000–2,500 subscribers
- **End of Year 1**: 3,000–5,000 subscribers (if Tampa Underground actively cross-promotes)
- **Year 2+**: Tied to whether he launches a podcast or course; 5,000–10,000 realistic ceiling without dramatic external PR

None of those numbers require virality. They require consistent shipping from a known-voice practitioner into a warm, pre-existing ecosystem.

## Recommendation

Newsletter launch is **Priority #1** in a Movemental onboarding plan for Joel. It is the lowest-effort, highest-leverage move in the entire playbook and should precede oral-history capture, course development, or any other asset creation.
