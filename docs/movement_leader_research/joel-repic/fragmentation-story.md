## The Story

Joel Repic has spent **twenty years in one Pennsylvania zip code** — Aliquippa, a post-industrial Ohio River Valley town that lost its steel economy in the 1980s and never got another one. He moved there with his wife Chelsea onto a drug-and-prostitution corner and started having people live with them. Out of that twenty-year cohabitation with a neighborhood he didn't choose and couldn't leave, he **founded Aliquippa Impact in 2005**, pastored **The Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance Church**, helped birth **a decentralized family of nonprofits, businesses, missional communities, and church plants** that formalized in 2018 as **The Greenhouse Network**, served as **Church Planting Director for the Christian and Missionary Alliance's Western Pennsylvania District**, and then — in the summer of 2025 — relocated his family of five to Tampa, Florida, to become **Executive Director of the Underground Network**, a 20-year-old family of **100+ microchurches** with **30+ sister movements worldwide**, officially starting August 16, 2025.

That is a twenty-year ministry arc larger than many published authors' entire body of work. And in terms of publicly retrievable content under Joel Repic's own name, it amounts to: **zero books**, **two indexed blog posts** at [hopeisprotest.com](https://www.hopeisprotest.com/) (the most recent from September 2022), **six sermons** preserved on The Gospel Tabernacle's podcast feed (a small-church distribution that could disappear any time), **one Exponential FCP podcast interview** from October 12, 2022, **one bonus-episode teaching** and **one family-focused interview** on the Underground's own Microchurches Podcast, **one "Special Guest" sermon link** on Gospel Alliance Church's site, **one CMA feature quote** in the "Not Done Yet" article, **one Leadership Network Greenhouse profile**, and **a denominational byline archive at `legacy.cmalliance.org/news/author/joel-repic/`** that now 301-redirects to the CMA homepage — meaning whatever Joel wrote for the CMA across his years as Church Planting Director is either not recoverable or only recoverable via direct outreach.

He has an Instagram (~934 followers, 489 posts), an X account with a verified handle, a personal Facebook, and a LinkedIn whose title is still "Minister for Youth and Community Development" — the role he held years ago at Crestmont Alliance, not the Underground ED role he just assumed. **He has no newsletter. He has no course. He has no published book. He has no personal YouTube channel. He has no Amazon author page, no Goodreads entry, no publisher author page. He has no hosted podcast.** There is no single URL in the world that, if a stranger typed it, would show them who Joel Repic is and what he has spent two decades building.

The fragmentation here is not that his content is scattered across too many platforms. **The fragmentation is that almost none of his work has been externalized at all.** The 20 years in Aliquippa produced frameworks, methods, relationships, formation stories, theology, and movement wisdom. Those things live in him, in Chelsea, in a few dozen leaders he has formed, in the neighbors he has invited to live with his family, and in the memory of a community. They do not live on the internet. The Tampa Underground has just hired an Executive Director whose digital record represents perhaps **5% of what he actually knows**. And if no one helps him externalize the rest before the Aliquippa chapter fades behind the Tampa chapter, the 95% will simply be lost.
