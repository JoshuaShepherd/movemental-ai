## The four asymmetries

Every Movemental author has a gap between embodied work and digital expression, but the **shape** of the gap varies. Joel's shape:

1. **Volume asymmetry**: extreme. Probably the largest embodied-vs-digital ratio of any subject in the research folder to date.
2. **Format asymmetry**: nearly everything that exists is audio (sermons, podcast guest appearances). Written material is almost non-existent. Video is almost non-existent.
3. **Ownership asymmetry**: nothing he has produced publicly lives on a platform he controls except the two posts on hopeisprotest.com.
4. **Narrative asymmetry**: the pieces that exist are fragmentary — one podcast, one feature, one sermon — with no connective tissue between them. There is no "Joel Repic's body of work" that can be encountered whole.

## What we cannot see from public research

Because the public record is so thin, we are necessarily missing:

- His teaching frameworks as they have evolved over 20 years
- His coaching methodology for new church planters
- His community-reading practices (what does he notice in a neighborhood that others miss?)
- His internal language for theology of place, theology of suffering, theology of hope
- His rhythms of prayer and discernment
- His editorial taste in books / influences
- The names of the leaders he has formed

These can only be captured through relationship — sustained conversation with Joel himself.

## Implications for Movemental

The gap analysis points cleanly to the onboarding shape:

1. **Lead with oral history, not asset migration.** Brisco's plan is 80% "aggregate what exists." Joel's plan is 80% "create what should exist, from what has already been lived."
2. **Prioritize retention-focused capture from the Gospel Tabernacle feed and legacy CMA URL.** Before they decay further.
3. **Start with voice-and-story content** (long-form interviews, reflective essays) rather than framework-and-course content. Let the frameworks emerge from the narratives.
4. **Use his Instagram as the first growth surface.** It is his most active platform (~934 followers, 489 posts) and the format (reflective + visual + community-oriented) matches his voice.
5. **Honor the ecosystem frame.** Per canonical doctrine, Joel is a trusted-voice / ecosystem layer, not a sales target. The gap here is not a conversion opportunity — it is a stewardship opportunity. The posture has to be "we will help you honor 20 years of faithful work," not "we will monetize your audience."
