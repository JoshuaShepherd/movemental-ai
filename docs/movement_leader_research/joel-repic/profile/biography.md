## Narrative biography

Joel Repic is the Executive Director of the Underground Network, a 20-year-old family of **100+ microchurches** in the Tampa Bay Area with **30+ sister movements worldwide**, a role he officially began on **August 16, 2025**. His appointment marks the second generational leadership transition in the Underground's history — following founder Brian Sanders and the five-year executive tenure of Lucas Pulley — and he arrives in Tampa with the kind of practitioner credentials that are difficult to manufacture and easy to recognize.

Repic is a **Pittsburgh-area native** who, with his wife Chelsea — now a licensed therapist — spent **twenty years in Aliquippa, Pennsylvania**, a post-industrial Ohio River Valley community shaped by the 1980s collapse of the American steel industry. Their earliest Aliquippa story, repeatedly cited in regional coverage, is a concrete one: they moved onto what was then a drug-and-prostitution corner and almost immediately began having people live with them. Out of that daily cohabitation with a neighborhood they had not inherited and could not escape, a twenty-year ministry arc unfolded.

In **2005**, Joel founded **Aliquippa Impact**, a youth development organization birthed from the renewal season of The Gospel Tabernacle — a 100+ year-old Christian and Missionary Alliance congregation in Aliquippa. Aliquippa Impact began as a summer day camp and grew into a cohort-based mentoring program tracking groups of 8–10 young people from elementary school through high school and beyond, with staff who lived in the community as neighbors rather than commuting in from outside. Joel's operating language — "We like to say that 90% of what we do is show up. Every time we show up in the life of a kid, we are protesting the hopelessness they feel" — became the philosophical anchor that would later resurface as the title of his personal reflective blog, **Hope Is Protest**.

In parallel with founding Aliquippa Impact, Joel served as **Minister for Youth and Community Development** and eventually **pastor** at The Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance Church, preaching regularly through the late 2010s and early 2020s. The small surviving sermon archive from this era — Acts, biblical justice, reconciliation, generational transfer, sharing the gospel — reveals a pastor willing to address race, systemic injustice, lament, and evangelism in the same series, from the same pulpit, in a racially diverse, economically distressed community.

Over the next decade after 2005, what Joel described as a largely **organic** phenomenon emerged: a "family of non-profit organizations, social impact projects, missional communities, businesses, and church plants" multiplied across Aliquippa and the broader Pittsburgh region, with a special focus on post-industrial river communities. In **2018**, this ecosystem was formalized as **The Greenhouse Network**, with Joel as co-director / coordinator, and an accompanying **Greenhouse Lab** incubator-as-service providing coaching, financial services, marketing support, and training to network leaders and to poor communities launching their own businesses. Joel also stepped into denominational leadership as **Director of Church Planting for the Christian and Missionary Alliance's Western Pennsylvania District**, bringing the Greenhouse ethos into a wider denominational structure.

His most substantive single public articulation of this work came in **October 2022**, when he appeared as a guest on the **Exponential For the Church Planter Podcast (FCP Episode 22)**, where he offered a compact theology of the Greenhouse story: **growth with values integrity**, **indigenous leadership priority** ("local neighborhood leaders must drive development efforts"), and **innovation as curation** (contextualizing learning from other movements rather than purely creating new initiatives). He also sketched the Greenhouse's strategic goals at that moment: piloting four church-network partnership models, doubling the Greenhouse Lab's capacity, planting three new churches, and providing evangelism training across the network's missional outposts.

In the summer of **2025**, the Underground Network's board — a body including Bill Couchenour (Chair), Brianna Wilkerson (Treasurer), and founder Brian Sanders — named Joel its next Executive Director. The announcement language emphasized his posture: "he arrives without predetermined solutions, prioritizing listening and learning from the network's existing stories and leaders." Joel's own comment: "I've long admired the Underground, personally learned from its story and leaders, and resonated with its Manifesto. To be called into this role feels both humbling and hopeful. This is a movement I believe in deeply, and I'm excited to walk with, serve, and champion the leaders who make it what it is."

Joel relocated his family — Chelsea, Levi (16), Jade (14), and Isla (8) — from Aliquippa to Tampa ahead of his August 16, 2025 start date. He also serves as a board member of the Christian Counselors Collaborative in Pittsburgh. His personal reflective blog, **hopeisprotest.com**, remains live but dormant, with the most recent publicly indexed post dating to September 2022.

## Origin & Formation

Joel's pre-Aliquippa life is not publicly documented. He describes himself as a "native of the Pittsburgh area," and the arc of his public ministry begins essentially from his arrival in Aliquippa and the founding of Aliquippa Impact in 2005. This is unusual — most thought leaders have a traceable pre-vocational history (university, prior career, formative mentors) that predates their public ministry. Joel's public record essentially begins with the Aliquippa corner.

Whatever formation preceded his arrival in Aliquippa — theological training, denominational apprenticeship, family lineage, earlier ministry seasons — is not part of the currently available record. This gap is notable and, for a potential Movemental author, worth filling directly rather than inferring.

## Career & Ministry Arc

Joel's ministry arc is compact and coherent:

1. **Arrival in Aliquippa** with Chelsea. Beginning of the 20-year embed.
2. **Aliquippa Impact (founded 2005)** — youth development organization, cohort-based mentoring, community embedding.
3. **Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance** — pastoral role, preaching, part of a congregation undergoing renewal.
4. **Organic multiplication (2005–2018)** of nonprofits, businesses, missional communities, and church plants through Aliquippa and the Pittsburgh region.
5. **Greenhouse Network (formalized 2018)** — the above multiplication, given a name and a formal infrastructure, with the Greenhouse Lab as incubator-as-service.
6. **CMA Western PA Church Planting Director** — denominational stewardship of church-planting work in his district.
7. **Underground Network Executive Director (Aug 2025 →)** — national / global movement leadership; 100+ microchurches, 30+ sister movements.

## Current focus

Joel's Tampa Underground role is three months old as of this research (April 2026). His stated posture is explicitly non-prescriptive: listening first, learning from existing leaders, refusing to arrive with a "fixed agenda." His first public teaching moment in the role — delivered to the Underground community and preserved as Microchurches Podcast Ep. 88 bonus episode — was titled **"The Manifest Presence of God"**, signaling the spiritual (not strategic) register he wants to set at the outset.

His ongoing responsibilities include:

- Stewarding the Underground's ~18-value Manifesto (including Jesus, the poor, the lost, microchurches, biblical justice, prayer)
- Leading the Underground executive team (with Lucas Pulley as Movements Director, Stacy Gaskins as Managing Director, Tomy Wilkerson as Tampa Director)
- Reporting to a board including founder Brian Sanders
- Championing the 100+ Tampa microchurches and 30+ global sister movements

He also continues to sit on the Christian Counselors Collaborative board, and — based on family visibility in Ep. 101 — appears to be actively integrating his family into microchurch life in Tampa.
