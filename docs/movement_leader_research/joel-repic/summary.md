# Executive Summary: Joel Repic

**Research Date**: 2026-04-22
**Confidence Level**: MEDIUM-HIGH (identity and ministry record HIGH; personal biographical details LOWER due to thin digital footprint)

## The short version

Joel Repic is a 20-year practitioner of neighborhood-embedded, poverty-contextualized, microchurch-shaped ministry who — as of **August 16, 2025** — became Executive Director of the **Underground Network** in Tampa, FL, succeeding Lucas Pulley and following founder Brian Sanders. He inherits **100+ microchurches in the Tampa Bay area** and a family of **30+ sister movements worldwide**. His public-facing digital body of work is dramatically smaller than his embodied ministry impact — which is exactly why he is such a high-value Movemental candidate.

## Who he is

Repic is a **Pittsburgh-area native** who, with his wife Chelsea (now a licensed therapist) and eventually three children (Levi 16, Jade 14, Isla 8), moved into Aliquippa, Pennsylvania — a post-industrial Ohio River Valley community leveled by the 1980s collapse of the steel industry. There, starting around 2005, he:

1. **Founded Aliquippa Impact** (2005), a youth development organization birthed out of The Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance Church (C&MA) — cohort-based mentoring of 8–10 young people tracked from elementary through high school, with staff living in the community as neighbors.
2. **Pastored** at Gospel Tabernacle / Crestmont Alliance through the late 2010s and early 2020s, preaching sermons on Acts, Biblical Justice, Reconciliation, Generational Transfer, and Sharing the Gospel.
3. **Helped catalyze what became The Greenhouse Network** — a decentralized family of nonprofits, social enterprises, missional communities, businesses, and church plants that multiplied "organically for more than a decade" through Aliquippa and the broader Pittsburgh region before being formalized into a network in 2018. The **Greenhouse Lab** incubator, launched alongside the network, offers coaching, financial services, marketing, and training to support indigenous leaders.
4. **Served as Church Planting Director for the Christian and Missionary Alliance, Western Pennsylvania District** — stewarding denominational church-planting work from inside the C&MA structure.
5. **Founded / runs a dormant personal reflective blog** at [hopeisprotest.com](https://www.hopeisprotest.com/), subtitled *"reflections on relentless hoping"* — only 2 posts indexed in public search.

He now takes over the Underground Network with the stated posture of "arriving without predetermined solutions, prioritizing listening and learning from the network's existing stories and leaders."

## What makes him theologically distinctive

1. **Hope-as-protest framing.** His signature phrase — "Every time we show up in the life of a kid, we are protesting the hopelessness they feel" — reframes consistent presence as an active act of defiance against structural despair. The hopeisprotest.com domain makes the phrase a brand.
2. **Indigenous leadership conviction.** In his Exponential interview he explicitly rejects "savior" models of external leadership — "local neighborhood leaders must drive development efforts" — and frames communities as possessing inherent creativity rather than needing to be rescued.
3. **Diverse and decentralized expressions of church.** He was shaped in a context that explicitly rejected a single dominant congregational model in favor of a family of non-profits, businesses, missional communities, and church plants, with no hierarchical center. This aligns seamlessly with the Underground's microchurch theology but brings a different angle — post-industrial community development — than the Underground's Tampa context has emphasized.
4. **"Everyone gets to play" ecclesiology.** Cited in the CMA "Not Done Yet" feature: the conviction that every person called by God has something to bring, and the goal is a "radical yes" to that call.
5. **Prayer-centered, Spirit-led culture.** Explicitly named as one of his 20-year Aliquippa focus areas.
