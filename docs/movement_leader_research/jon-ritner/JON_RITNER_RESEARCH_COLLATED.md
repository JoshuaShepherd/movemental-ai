## Editorial bio

Jon Ritner is an American missional pastor, church-planting strategist, and author whose ministry centers on re-evangelizing the post-Christian West. He earned a B.S. from the College of William and Mary (1997) and an M.Div. from Trinity International University (2002), then spent roughly a decade pastoring a megachurch in Williamsburg, Virginia — a season he later described self-critically as being "a program director for a cruise ship for Christians."

Seeking a different model, Ritner and his wife Kristyn moved to Brussels, Belgium to help plant micro-churches through Communitas International's project "The Well." (Communitas was formerly named Christian Associates.) That immersion in a deeply secular European context reshaped his missiology around "sentness" and incarnational presence. In 2015 he returned to the United States to apply those post-Christendom lessons, becoming Lead Pastor of Ecclesia Hollywood and re-missioning the existing congregation.

In November 2020, 100 Movements Publishing released his book *Positively Irritating*, with a foreword by Alan and Debra Hirsch. His current footprint spans his church (Ecclesia Hollywood), an organizational-strategy role at Communitas International, missional training through Forge, contributing authorship at 5Q Central, and a coaching/consulting practice via jonritner.com.

## Frameworks

### Positively Irritating (oyster / sand / pearl)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Positively Irritating*) |
| Components | (1) The cultural "irritant" of secularism; (2) the defensive "eye" response (expel it); (3) the generative "oyster" response (embrace and transform it into a pearl) |
| Visual / model | Oyster/pearl master metaphor; recurs across book and interviews |
| Adoption evidence | Title concept; basis of the "Positively Irritating Accelerator" 8-session cohort | 
| Source | [everand.com](https://www.everand.com/book/507033451/), [Englewood Review](https://englewoodreview.org/jon-ritner-positively-irritating-review/) |

The Western church should respond to post-Christian culture not as a threat to expel but as an irritant to embrace — the means by which something prized and beautiful is formed.

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Positively Irritating*) |
| Components | Invert "worship → community → mission" to "mission → community → worship" |

| Source | [Englewood Review](https://englewoodreview.org/jon-ritner-positively-irritating-review/) |

Discipleship in a post-Christian context begins with sent mission, not with the Sunday gathering.

### Disruptive disciplemaking (vs. "industrial" disciplemaking)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (*Positively Irritating*) |

Disrupt a person's preconceived notions about Christianity so they reconsider Jesus, instead of processing consumers through a program funnel.

#### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not frame post-Christian culture with alarm, lament, or culture-war defensiveness ("the church is under attack / in decline and must resist or retreat"). Ritner's signature move is the inverse: the defensive "eye" expels the irritant; the church must instead be the "oyster" that embraces it. Voice generated in his register must treat secularization as generative opportunity, not threat. Derived from the oyster-not-eye metaphor in [everand.com preview](https://www.everand.com/book/507033451/) and [Word on the Streets](https://www.wordonthestreets.net/Articles/618896/Mobilising_missional_disciples.aspx).

## Theological positioning

| Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Non-denominational evangelical (Communitas maintains a Statement of Faith; not tied to one denomination). A speculated Grace Communion International affiliation is likely overstated — guest content only |
| Ecclesiology | Decentralized, distributed disciple-making; churches as training centers equipping all believers; against "industrialized" church reliant on property/programs/professionals; "skeleton" (biblical) vs. "skin" (cultural) |
| Missiology | Incarnational and "sent"; attractional ("come to us") → missional ("go to them"); contextual "narratives of good news" rather than a single shame-based template; lead from *imago Dei* (Gen 1) over sin (Gen 3) |
| Leadership | APEST / 5Q practitioner; argues post-Christian contexts especially need apostle/prophet/evangelist "innovator" giftings |
| Christology | Jesus "himself is always good news"; gospel proclamation reframed around the person of Jesus rather than transaction |
| Anthropology / formation | Humans as image-bearers; disciplemaking as disruption of preconceptions toward reconsidering Jesus |
| Culture | Post-Christendom embrace: secularization is an "irritant" to transform, not a threat to resist |

#### Distinctive claims

- Reframes cultural hostility/secularization not as decline to resist but as a generative "irritant" the church should embrace to become *more* faithful and innovative (the oyster/pearl thesis).
- The discipleship sequence should be reversed — mission precedes community precedes worship — in a post-Christian context.
- Lead evangelism with Genesis 1 (image-bearing dignity) before Genesis 3 (sin).
