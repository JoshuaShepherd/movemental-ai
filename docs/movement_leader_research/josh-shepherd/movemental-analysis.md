## What Movemental Already Provides Him

Because he sits inside the platform, every capability the platform offers tenants is also available to him at zero marginal cost:

- **Owned platform** — `movemental.ai` itself
- **Voice-preserving AI agents** — same toolset he has built for tenants
- **Content/LMS/e-commerce stack** — full
- **Network discoverability** — once tenants are live and cross-linked
- **Network verification** — implicit through the founding team and tenant roster

So the question is not "what does Movemental change for him?" — it's "**what posture does he take on his own platform?**"

---

## Posture Options

### Option A — Invisible System (Current Default)
- What it looks like: One founder card on the about page. No essays, no public artifacts.
- Strength: Maximally consistent with scenius-over-genius. Tenants get all the foreground.
- Risk: Loses the credibility lift of "the builder is named, accessible, and in-character." Prospective tenants and partners may have to take Movemental's quality on faith with no human anchor.

### Option B — Named Anchor, Quiet Voice (Recommended Posture)
- What it looks like: Slightly upgraded founder presence on the about page, a single-paragraph "why this exists" essay, and a small but real `/team/josh` page. No external broadcasting.
- Strength: Names the author, supplies a credibility anchor, preserves scenius posture, requires no external surface area.
- Risk: Negligible. The work is already done in the SSoT — surfacing it on the platform itself costs nothing.

### Option C — Builder-in-Public (Not Recommended at This Stage)
- What it looks like: Public build log, podcast appearances, LinkedIn cadence, conference talks.
- Strength: Could draw mission-tech / AI builder peers and accelerate hiring/partnership.
- Risk: Direct contradiction with the platform's stated values about voice, scenius, and not performing. Easy to drift into thought-leader brand-building. Would be hard to row back.

**Recommendation: Option B.**

---

## Concrete Surface Changes (If Option B Is Chosen)

These are all *on* the Movemental platform, not external:

1. **Strengthen the about-page founder card** with a 60–80 word bio that names the formation lineage (Mission House, missional ecosystem) alongside the technical credentials.
2. **Add a short "Why I built this" paragraph** signed by Josh in first person, sourced from `09-founder-narrative.md`. Two short paragraphs, not an essay.
3. **Add a `/team/josh` page** with bio, contact (`josh@movemental.ai`), and 2–3 pinned artifacts (the Maturity Model, the founder narrative excerpt, the about-page quote).
4. **Make the founding team visible together** — a single image or layout that shows Hirsch, Brisco, Robinson, Shepherd as a genuine four-way team. The founder narrative explicitly frames this as "Why Together"; the site doesn't carry that yet.
5. **Reserve the public-build-log idea for later.** Not zero, but not now.

Each of these reuses material already authored. None of them require new surface area outside Movemental.

## What Stays the Same

- **The product is the brand.** Tenants and the platform remain the foreground.
- **Voice preservation applies to Josh too.** Whatever he ships in his own voice should be consistent with how he writes in the founder narrative — concrete, builder-pragmatic, formation-flavored.
- **No content arms race.** No newsletter cadence, no podcast, no daily LinkedIn.
- **No external SSoT exposure.** Personal SSoT (career/personal/) stays private.
- **No equity-style self-promotion.** Movemental's economic-justice posture (90% to leaders) and scenius framing must not be undermined by founder-as-celebrity moves.

---

## Risks to Manage

1. **Single-founder concentration.** 70% equity + sole technical owner + sole operator = a real single-point-of-failure risk. Worth noting even if not addressed by this report.
2. **Brand-vs-platform drift.** Surfacing Josh too much makes Movemental feel like a personal consultancy; surfacing him too little makes it feel ownerless.
3. **Cross-tenant signaling.** If/when Josh writes anything personal on the platform, tenants may read it as competing for attention. Posture and frequency matter.
4. **Recovery surface.** If Josh ever does need to job-search again (e.g., the LinkedIn SSoT shows live-positioning planning), an under-built personal surface is a soft cost. Option B mitigates that without requiring a brand pivot.
