## Executive Summary

JR Woodward (legal name: Gailand Woodward Jr.) is a church planter, missiologist, and movement leader who has spent more than three decades establishing missional-incarnational communities across the United States. As National Director of the V3 Church Planting Movement, co-founder of Missio Alliance and the Praxis Gathering, and author of three books on missional culture, movement ecclesiology, and leadership, Woodward has become one of the most trusted practitioner-scholars in the North American missional church movement. His work is distinguished by the rare integration of grassroots church planting experience with rigorous academic research -- his most recent book, *The Scandal of Leadership* (2023), is a direct outgrowth of his PhD dissertation at the University of Manchester.

His distinctive contribution is the **synthesis of continental philosophy (Girard, Wink, Stringfellow) with hands-on church planting praxis** -- a combination virtually no other voice in the missional-incarnational movement offers at this depth. Operating as the architect to Alan Hirsch's prophet, Woodward builds carefully named frameworks (Five Culture Creators, Four Spaces of Belonging, Three Contours of Leadership, Eight Movement Competencies) that translate abstract theology into deployable training systems. His body of work progresses from practical culture-building (*Creating a Missional Culture*, IVP 2012) to movement-catalyzing (*The Church as Movement*, IVP 2016, co-authored with Dan White Jr.) to deep diagnostic theology (*The Scandal of Leadership*, 100 Movements 2023), each layer adding sophistication to the same fundamental vision: the church as a neighborhood-rooted, discipleship-fueled, boundary-crossing movement led by many centers rather than one.

Despite his outsized influence -- 7 organizations founded, 9+ board or fellowship roles (per CV), 600+ leaders trained in V3's first decade (CV) with **1,000+** often cited in broader marketing, adjunct teaching across multiple seminaries, IVP Book of the Year 2017 for *The Church as Movement* (also IVP Reader's Choice Award 2016 per CV), ~274 Google Scholar citations -- Woodward's personal digital presence is notably underdeveloped (3.3/5). He has no personal YouTube channel (despite 20-30 videos scattered across third-party platforms), no hosted podcast (despite 11+ guest appearances), a basic WordPress site with no email capture, dormant Instagram, and no online courses on any discoverable platform. His influence is disproportionately embodied -- V3 training cohorts, seminary classrooms, conference stages, and organizational leadership -- rather than digitally captured and centralized. His Substack "Faithful Resistance" (launched 2025) represents an emerging owned-audience strategy but serves a narrower political-prophetic niche rather than his full intellectual range.

The monetization opportunity is estimated at $33K-$118K/year through scaled online courses (digitizing V3 Praxis Cohort curriculum), missing audiobooks for all titles, a centralized YouTube channel, premium Substack tiers, and downloadable frameworks. His audience -- primarily church planters, seminary students, denominational strategists, and abuse-aware communities -- already pays for content through V3 training fees, seminary tuition, conference registration, and book purchases. The large pool of V3-trained planters (600+ in the first decade on CV; **1,000+** in cumulative messaging) represents a warm audience that could be routed into structured content journeys.

The core opportunity for Movemental is to surface, connect, and amplify a substantial body of work that is currently fragmented across 12+ platforms Woodward does not own. His network -- anchored by Alan Hirsch (forewords for 2 books, published through 100Movements), Dan White Jr. (co-author, co-leader of V3), David Fitch (Missio Alliance colleague), and the broader Missio Alliance board -- represents a direct pipeline to additional high-value authors. His frameworks (Five Culture Creators, APEST as cultural environments, Theology of the Powers, mimetic leadership) complement and deepen Hirsch's foundational work, making them a natural paired offering.
