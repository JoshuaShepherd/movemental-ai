# Competitive Landscape Analysis: JR Woodward

## 1. Direct Competitors

These authors cover substantially identical territory -- missional ecclesiology and church planting -- with overlapping audiences.

### Dan White Jr.

- **Overlap**: Highest of any peer. Co-authored *The Church as Movement* (IVP, 2016). Co-leads V3 alongside Woodward. Both train church planters in the same eight competencies. Shared theological framework, shared audience, shared publisher relationships. Woodward wrote the foreword for White's *Subterranean* (2015).
- **Distinction**: White tilts toward the hyperlocal and the underground. *Subterranean: Why the Future of the Church Is Rootedness* (Cascade, 2015) and his Syracuse, NY context position him as a parish-scale practitioner-poet. Woodward is the systems thinker and academic; White is the storyteller and place-keeper.
- **Books**: 2 -- *Subterranean* (2015), *The Church as Movement* (2016, with Woodward)
- **Digital Presence**: Low. No prominent personal website, limited social media. Relies on V3 and Missio Alliance platforms.
- **Audience**: Small (3K-8K estimated). Overlaps almost entirely with Woodward's.
- **Unique Angle**: Rootedness theology, hyperlocal incarnational practice, the "underground" church metaphor.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Very High** -- co-author, co-leader. They are collaborative partners, not competitors.

### David Fitch

- **Overlap**: High. Fellow Missio Alliance board member, wrote foreword for *The Scandal of Leadership*. Both address missional theology, the Powers, and church culture. Fitch's *Faithful Presence* (2016) covers similar ground to Woodward's *Creating a Missional Culture* -- how should the church be organized for mission?
- **Distinction**: Fitch is a systematic theologian with an academic chair (B.R. Lindner Chair of Evangelical Theology, Northern Seminary). His work is more theologically dense and politically analytical (*The Church of Us vs. Them*, *Reckoning with Power*). He has a stronger Substack ("Fitch's Provocations") and a more public-facing intellectual voice. Fitch diagnoses ideology; Woodward diagnoses power structures and imitation patterns.
- **Books**: 7+ -- *The Great Giveaway* (2005), *The End of Evangelicalism?* (2011), *Faithful Presence* (2016), *The Church of Us vs. Them* (2019), *Reckoning with Power* (2024), plus edited volumes.
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. Active Substack, Northern Seminary platform, Missio Alliance contributor, podcast appearances. Stronger personal digital presence than Woodward.
- **Audience**: Medium (15K-30K estimated). Seminary students, progressive evangelicals, pastors wrestling with political theology.
- **Unique Angle**: Political theology, ideology critique, "faithful presence" framework, post-Christendom evangelical identity.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **High** -- shared theological ecosystem, shared board service, shared concern with power and mission. Fitch is more ideological analysis; Woodward is more leadership praxis.

### Brad Brisco

- **Overlap**: High. Both operate within missional-incarnational church planting networks. Brisco is Director of Multiplication Strategies at NAMB/Send Network. Both published through IVP's Forge Partnership line. Both engage Alan Hirsch's frameworks.
- **Distinction**: Brisco is denominationally embedded (SBC/NAMB) where Woodward is interdenominational (V3, which has Baptist roots but ecumenical practice). Brisco's breakout niche is covocational/bivocational church planting. Woodward is more focused on organizational culture and leadership formation.
- **Books**: 5 -- *Missional Essentials* (2012, with Ford), *The Missional Quest* (2014, with Ford), *Next Door As It Is in Heaven* (2016, with Ford), *Covocational Church Planting* (2021), *ReThink* (eBook).
- **Digital Presence**: Low. missionalchurchnetwork.com blog, modest social media (~132 Instagram followers), no newsletter, no courses, no YouTube.
- **Audience**: Small (5K-15K estimated). SBC church planters, bivocational ministers, Send Network trainees.
- **Unique Angle**: Covocational ministry as institutional strategy within a major denomination. The SBC-missional bridge.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **High** -- church planting + missional culture. Different denominational context and different leadership focus (Brisco = vocational strategy, Woodward = organizational culture + powers).

### Hugh Halter

- **Overlap**: Moderate-High. Halter's *Tangible Kingdom* (2008) and incarnational community work overlap with Woodward's *Church as Movement*. Both advocate for neighborhood-rooted, non-institutional church forms. Both speak at Exponential and similar conferences.
- **Distinction**: Halter is a practitioner-storyteller (house painter turned church planter). His Lantern Network and Brave Cities give him a tangible movement infrastructure focused on post-Christian contexts. More embodied and experiential in tone; less systematic and academic than Woodward.
- **Books**: 7+ -- *The Tangible Kingdom* (2008), *Tangible Kingdom Primer* (2009), *AND: The Gathered and Scattered Church* (2010), *BIVO* (2013), *Flesh* (2014), *Righteous Brood*, *Happy Hour*.
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. hughhalter.com, Brave Cities network, Outreach Magazine contributor, social media presence.
- **Audience**: Medium (15K-30K estimated). Church planters, bivocational ministers, missional community practitioners.
- **Unique Angle**: Incarnational embodiment, bivocational as lifestyle, tangible community as apologetic.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Moderate-High** -- incarnational community focus. Halter is more experiential/story-driven; Woodward is more systematic/framework-driven.

## 2. Adjacent Voices

These authors cover related territory but with a different angle, audience, or institutional context.

### Alan Hirsch

- **Relationship**: Mentor-tier figure. Hirsch wrote forewords for both *Creating a Missional Culture* and *The Church as Movement*. Woodward published *The Scandal of Leadership* through Hirsch's 100Movements Publishing. Both serve in the Movement Leaders Collective ecosystem. Hirsch praised Woodward's work as containing "missiological insights that only an apostolically inclined leader can bring."
- **Distinction**: Hirsch operates at the meta-framework level (APEST/5Q, movements theory, mDNA). Woodward applies and extends Hirsch's frameworks at the congregational and organizational level. Hirsch is the theorist; Woodward is the implementer-scholar.
- **Books**: 15+ including *The Forgotten Ways* (2006), *The Permanent Revolution* (2012, with Catchim), *5Q* (2017), *Reframation* (2019), *Metanoia* (2024).
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. alanhirsch.org, 100Movements, Forge, 5Q Collective. Multiple organizational platforms. ~37,900 X followers, ~14,000 Facebook, Substack (1,500+). Wikipedia page.
- **Audience**: Large (50K-100K estimated). Movement leaders, denominational strategists, seminary students, global missional practitioners.
- **Unique Angle**: Movements meta-theory, APEST/5Q system, apostolic imagination.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **High** (theological foundations) / **Low** (practical application). Hirsch is upstream -- Woodward applies his frameworks.

### Michael Frost

- **Relationship**: Adjacent thought leader and endorser. Frost endorsed *The Scandal of Leadership* ("Every Christian leader should read this") and *Creating a Missional Culture*. Both operate in the missional-incarnational stream.
- **Distinction**: Frost is Australian, global, and more prolific (20+ books). He co-founded the missional-incarnational paradigm with Hirsch (*The Shaping of Things to Come*, 2003). He is a missiologist-provocateur; Woodward is a missiologist-practitioner. Frost operates from Morling College's Tinsley Institute; Woodward from V3.
- **Books**: 20+ including *The Shaping of Things to Come* (2003, with Hirsch), *Exiles* (2006), *The Road to Missional* (2011), *Incarnate* (2014), *Surprise the World* (2016), *To Alter Your World* (2017).
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. mikefrost.net. Migrating from X to Bluesky. Global conference circuit. Required reading in seminaries worldwide.
- **Audience**: Large (50K-100K estimated). Global seminary students, missional practitioners, Australian/international church leaders.
- **Unique Angle**: Post-Christendom missiology, incarnational provocation, global perspective, prolific output.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **High** (theological) / **Low** (institutional). Frost is a tier above in visibility and breadth; Woodward is more embedded in North American movement infrastructure.

### Ed Stetzer

- **Relationship**: Institutional adjacent. Both serve the church planting practitioner community. Stetzer has endorsed and amplified voices in Woodward's network (foreword for Brisco/Ford's *The Missional Quest*). Both speak at Exponential.
- **Distinction**: Stetzer is a research-driven public intellectual with massive institutional reach (Dean at Talbot/Biola, Editor-in-Chief of Outreach Magazine, Moody Radio host, Lausanne regional director). He covers church planting but also culture, politics, and evangelicalism broadly. He is a generalist megaphone where Woodward is a specialist practitioner-scholar.
- **Books**: 12+ including *Planting Missional Churches* (2006, 2016 2nd ed.), *Breaking the Missional Code* (2006), *Christians in the Age of Outrage* (2018).
- **Digital Presence**: **Strongest of any peer.** @edstetzer on X (100K+ followers), Outreach Magazine platform, Moody Radio, ChurchLeaders.com.
- **Audience**: Very Large (200K+). Broad evangelical audience, church planters, pastors, seminary students.
- **Unique Angle**: Research-driven public evangelical intellectualism, institutional megaphone, culture-and-church commentary.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Moderate** -- overlaps on church planting only. Different scale, different scope, different theological posture.

### Neil Cole

- **Relationship**: Parallel track. Cole's "Organic Church" movement (2005) pioneered the simple/house church model that overlaps with Woodward's emphasis on smaller, neighborhood-rooted communities. Both advocate decentralized, multiplication-oriented ecclesiology.
- **Distinction**: Cole is more radical -- fully decentralized, house-church focused, no paid clergy as starting assumption. His Church Multiplication Associates claims 700+ churches in 32 states and 23 nations. Woodward works within more structured (though still non-traditional) movement frameworks through V3.
- **Books**: 10+ including *Organic Church* (2005), *Church 3.0* (2010), *Organic Leadership* (2009), *Primal Fire* (2014).
- **Digital Presence**: Low. Blog-based. Declining from peak influence years (2005-2012).
- **Audience**: Medium-declining (15K-30K estimated). Organic/simple church practitioners, house church networks.
- **Unique Angle**: Radical organic church, fully decentralized multiplication, no institutional overhead.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Moderate** -- both advocate multiplication and decentralization, but Woodward's model is more structured (V3 cohorts, polycentric leadership within community, not independent house churches).

### Mike Breen

- **Relationship**: Tangential. Breen's discipleship-first approach (*Building a Discipling Culture*) feeds the same formation pipeline. Both prioritize discipleship as the engine of mission.
- **Distinction**: Breen built a proprietary system (3DM, LifeShapes, Huddles) with its own publishing arm, training pipeline, and certification. More systemized and franchise-like than Woodward's organic, cohort-based V3 model. Breen's system has faced significant controversy regarding organizational culture -- ironic given Woodward's PhD work on leadership abuse and the Powers.
- **Books**: 6+ including *Building a Discipling Culture* (3 editions), *Multiplying Missional Leaders*, *Family on Mission*.
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. 3dmovements.com, own publishing arm. Training-system focused.
- **Audience**: Medium (20K-40K estimated). Megachurch-adjacent leaders, discipleship program adopters.
- **Unique Angle**: Proprietary discipleship system, LifeShapes visual learning tools, structured multiplication.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Low** -- different system architecture, different ecclesial context, different leadership philosophy (Breen = centralized system; Woodward = polycentric, distributed).

### Dave Ferguson

- **Relationship**: Adjacent. Ferguson operates the church multiplication infrastructure (Exponential, formerly NewThing) that many of Woodward's V3 planters interface with. Both speak at Exponential conferences.
- **Distinction**: Ferguson is a mega-church pastor (Community Christian Church) turned movement CEO. His 2025 merger of NewThing into Exponential makes him the gatekeeper of the largest church planting conference ecosystem (~3,000 churches in network). More organizational/systems, less theological/missional than Woodward.
- **Books**: 8+ including *Exponential* (2010), *Hero Maker* (2018), *B.L.E.S.S.* (2021).
- **Digital Presence**: Strong. daveferguson.org, Exponential.org platform. Major conference circuit presence.
- **Audience**: Large (75K-150K estimated). Church planting practitioners across the spectrum.
- **Unique Angle**: Multiplication systems, movement infrastructure at scale, conference ecosystem gatekeeper.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Moderate** -- both advocate multiplication, but Ferguson operates at the systems/conference level while Woodward operates at the theology/formation level.

### Jeff Vanderstelt

- **Relationship**: Parallel. Vanderstelt's "Saturate" framework (missional communities, gospel fluency, everyday discipleship) overlaps substantially with Woodward's missional ecclesiology.
- **Distinction**: Vanderstelt is a church pastor (Doxa Church, Bellevue WA) with a tighter theological framework (gospel-centered, more Reformed). His Soma Family of Churches is a network of missional communities. More gospel-identity focused; Woodward is more missiology/movement-structure focused.
- **Books**: 3 -- *Saturate* (2015), *Gospel Fluency* (2017), *Making Space* (2019).
- **Digital Presence**: Moderate. saturatetheworld.com with training resources, podcast, field guides. Already has a unified content platform.
- **Audience**: Medium (20K-40K estimated). Reformed-leaning missional practitioners, Soma network churches.
- **Unique Angle**: Gospel fluency, missional communities within Reformed theological framework, Saturate platform.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Moderate** -- missional communities focus. Different theological home (Reformed vs. ecumenical missional) and different leadership model (Vanderstelt = gospel-centered pastoral; Woodward = polycentric APEST).

### Tim Catchim

- **Relationship**: Indirect collaborator through APEST ecosystem. Catchim co-authored *The Permanent Revolution* (2012) with Alan Hirsch, which is the foundational text on the apostolic function that Woodward's "Five Equippers" framework extends.
- **Distinction**: Catchim is focused specifically on APEST theory, typology development, and the apostolic/prophetic functions. More narrowly focused than Woodward, who applies APEST within a broader missional culture and movement ecclesiology framework.
- **Books**: 2 -- *The Permanent Revolution* (2012, with Hirsch), *The Permanent Revolution Playbook* (2014, with Hirsch).
- **Digital Presence**: Low-Moderate. Writing and consulting focused. Less public-facing than Woodward.
- **Audience**: Small (5K-10K estimated). APEST practitioners, 5Q enthusiasts, movement theorists.
- **Unique Angle**: APEST typology development, apostolic function theory.
- **Overlap with Woodward**: **Moderate** -- both extend APEST frameworks. Catchim is more theoretical; Woodward is more applied to organizational culture.

## 4. Differentiation: What Makes JR Woodward Unique

### The Practitioner-Scholar at the Powers Intersection

Woodward occupies a position no other peer holds: he is the **only voice who has a PhD specifically on leadership abuse and the theology of the Powers, AND has 30+ years of grassroots church planting experience, AND leads a national church planting movement**. This three-way integration is unique:

- **Hirsch** provides the movement theory but does not lead a church planting movement directly
- **Fitch** has the academic chair and political theology but has not led a national movement infrastructure
- **Brisco** has the denominational church planting role but lacks the doctoral research on power and leadership
- **Halter** has the incarnational practice credibility but lacks the academic framework
- **Stetzer** has the institutional megaphone but has moved away from hands-on movement leadership
- **Breen** built a discipleship system but his organizational culture has been questioned -- making Woodward's *Scandal of Leadership* a direct response to the problems Breen's model exemplified

### The APEST Implementer

*Creating a Missional Culture* is recognized as "one of the most thorough developments" (Dr. Carlus Gupton) of the Ephesians 4:11 fivefold ministry framework for practical congregational application. While Hirsch and Catchim provide the APEST theory, Woodward provides the congregational implementation with his Five Equippers model (Dream Awakeners, Heart Revealers, Story Tellers, Soul Healers, Light Givers). No other author has made APEST as practically accessible for church teams.

### The Mimetic Theory Bridge

Woodward is the only author in this peer group who synthesizes Rene Girard's mimetic desire theory with Walter Wink's theology of the Powers and William Stringfellow's structural analysis, and then applies this synthesis to church leadership. This interdisciplinary move gives *The Scandal of Leadership* a unique intellectual foundation -- it is not just another "leadership ethics" book but a systematic diagnosis of why leaders fail, rooted in imitation theory.

### The Movement Infrastructure Leader

As National Director of V3, Woodward has direct training relationships with 1,000+ church planters. This is not a publishing relationship or conference-speaking relationship -- it is a cohort-based, 30-week formation relationship. V3's Praxis Community model is one of the most intensive church planter training pipelines in North America. Combined with his 7 co-founded organizations and 8 board roles, Woodward's network connectivity is among the highest in this peer group.

### Strategic Sequencing Recommendation

**Phase 1 (Foundation)**: Woodward + Hirsch + Dan White Jr. on the platform together. This creates the strongest V3/100Movements content ecosystem -- the movement theorist (Hirsch), the culture designer and leadership scholar (Woodward), and the hyperlocal practitioner (White). **Phase 2 (Expansion)**: Add Brisco, Frost, and McNeal. These voices expand the denominational range (Brisco brings the SBC bridge), international scope (Frost brings the global perspective), and generational wisdom (McNeal brings the senior prophetic voice) without competing for Woodward's specific niche. Better to leverage their platforms (Exponential conference, Outreach Magazine, Moody Radio) for outbound promotion of Woodward's Movemental content. Do not platform Breen alongside Woodward given the direct tension between Woodward's *Scandal of Leadership* thesis and Breen's organizational history.

## 7. Key Takeaway

JR Woodward's competitive advantage is not breadth of output or platform size -- on those metrics, he trails Hirsch, Frost, Stetzer, and Ferguson. His advantage is **unique intellectual depth combined with movement infrastructure**: he is the only voice who has produced a PhD-level diagnosis of leadership failure (theology of the Powers + mimetic theory), developed a practically implemented APEST framework (Five Equippers), co-founded 7 organizations, and currently trains 1,000+ church planters through a 30-week cohort model.

Movemental's opportunity is to give Woodward the digital infrastructure that matches his institutional and intellectual stature -- unified content hub, course delivery for V3's currently locked-away training content, audience building tools to scale beyond "hundreds" of Substack subscribers, and network amplification that makes visible the enormous collaborative ecosystem he has spent 30+ years building. The gap between Woodward's actual influence (7 organizations, 8 boards, 1,000+ trained planters, ~274 citations, IVP Book of the Year) and his digital visibility (3.3/5 Digital Presence Score, basic WordPress site, no podcast, no courses) is one of the largest in the Movemental research pipeline. Closing that gap is the opportunity.
