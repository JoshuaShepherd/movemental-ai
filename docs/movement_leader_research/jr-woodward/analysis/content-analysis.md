## Executive Summary

JR Woodward is a practitioner-scholar whose work moves along a single deepening trajectory: the Western church has absorbed worldly patterns of power that distort its leadership, cripple its mission, and produce an epidemic of fallen leaders -- and the remedy is a polycentric, kenotic, movement-oriented ecclesiology grounded in the imitation of Christ rather than the imitation of the Powers. His distinctive contribution is the **synthesis of continental philosophy (Girard, Wink, Stringfellow) with hands-on church planting praxis** -- a combination virtually no other voice in the missional-incarnational movement offers at this depth. Operating as the architect to Alan Hirsch's prophet, Woodward builds carefully named frameworks (Five Culture Creators, Four Spaces of Belonging, Three Contours of Leadership) that translate abstract theology into deployable training systems. His body of work progresses from practical culture-building (*Creating a Missional Culture*) to movement-catalyzing (*The Church as Movement*) to deep diagnostic theology (*The Scandal of Leadership*), each layer adding sophistication to the same fundamental vision: the church as a neighborhood-rooted, discipleship-fueled, boundary-crossing movement led by many centers rather than one.

### Has their thinking shifted?

Woodward's thinking has not shifted so much as deepened in concentric layers. His core commitment -- neighborhood-rooted, incarnational, movement-oriented ecclesiology -- has remained constant since the NLCF founding in 1989 and the move to Los Angeles in 2003. What has evolved is the diagnostic depth and philosophical sophistication applied to the same fundamental vision.

The most significant intellectual development is the turn toward mimetic theory and Powers theology during his PhD research (2016-2020). This added a dimension absent from his earlier work: a diagnostic framework for *why* churches default to domineering leadership, not just *what* healthy leadership looks like. The earlier work was constructive-practical (build this); the later work is diagnostic-prophetic (understand this before you build).

The 2025 Substack represents a further extension: applying the Powers framework to contemporary political dynamics (authoritarianism, concentrated tech power, oligarchy). This is his most publicly engaged writing and suggests the next phase may involve Woodward as a prophetic public intellectual, not just an ecclesiological trainer.

## Unique Contribution

### What only this author says

- **The Girard-Wink-Stringfellow synthesis applied to church leadership**: Woodward is the only voice in the missional-incarnational movement who has synthesized Girard's mimetic theory, Wink's Powers trilogy, and Stringfellow's prophetic resistance into a single diagnostic framework for understanding leadership failure in the church. Others cite one or two of these thinkers; Woodward integrates all three at PhD level and applies them practically.

- **"Not to imitate Christ is to imitate the Powers"**: This binary formulation -- that leaders face an inescapable either/or between imitating Christ's kenosis and unconsciously replicating the Powers' domination -- is Woodward's signature thesis. No other author in this space has framed the leadership crisis in these terms.

- **APEST as culture creators, not just roles**: While Hirsch developed the APEST framework, Woodward extended it into a culture-shaping model. The renaming (apostles as Dream Awakeners, prophets as Heart Revealers, etc.) and the mapping of each gift to a specific cultural environment (learning, healing, welcoming, liberating, thriving) is Woodward's original contribution. Dr. Carlus Gupton called it "one of the most thorough developments" of the Ephesians 4:11 framework.

- **The Cultural Web as ecclesiological tool**: Woodward's six-dimension cultural web (Language, Artifacts, Rituals, Ethics, Institutions, Guiding Narratives) applied to congregational culture is a unique diagnostic tool not found in other missional ecclesiology literature.

- **Domineering leadership as a theological problem**: Where others diagnose leadership failure through psychology, management theory, or moral categories, Woodward insists it requires a theology of the Powers. The systemic, spiritual, and mimetic forces that shape leadership desire cannot be addressed by better accountability structures alone.

### Where they disagree with peers

| Topic | Woodward's Position | Mainstream/Peer Position |
|-------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Root cause of leadership failure | Theological-systemic: mimetic desire + Powers of domination operating through institutional systems | Psychological-moral: individual character deficiency, lack of accountability, or burnout |
| How to fix toxic leadership | Kenotic spiritual formation that reshapes desires through the imitation of Christ; systemic dismantling of domination structures | Better governance, accountability structures, psychological screening, and mentoring |
| Flat vs. hierarchical leadership | Neither -- polycentric leadership as a third way. Multiple interrelated centers, not undifferentiated flatness or top-down hierarchy | Most reformers default to either stronger hierarchy (governance) or flatter structures (shared leadership). The "polycentric" category is Woodward's distinctive alternative. |
| APEST framework application | Each gift creates a distinct cultural environment; all five are needed to cultivate a full missional culture | APEST is primarily about identifying leadership roles and filling gaps on a team (Hirsch's more common application) |
| Source of desire | Desires are socially formed through mimetic models (Girard); leaders must consciously choose their models | Desires are largely internal; leadership formation focuses on character, skill, and calling |
| Church and powers of domination | The church itself can become a vehicle for the Powers when it adopts corporate structures and worldly power models | The church is generally seen as a force against darkness; internal corruption is treated as deviation rather than systemic capture |

### What would be lost without their work

Without Woodward's work, the missional-incarnational movement would lack its deepest diagnostic framework for understanding why churches default to domination despite good intentions. The Girard-Wink-Stringfellow synthesis applied to ecclesiology would remain in academic journals rather than reaching church planters through V3 training. The APEST framework would remain primarily a role-identification tool rather than a culture-shaping model. And the emerging conversation about faithful resistance to concentrated power (technological, political, economic) would lack a theologian who connects it to both historic Powers theology and practical missional praxis.

## Content Interconnections

### How themes connect across works

Woodward's body of work follows a clear deepening progression: **culture** (what shapes a church) leads to **movement** (how churches multiply) leads to **power** (what distorts both).

- *Creating a Missional Culture* (2012) provides the foundational framework -- polycentric leadership, five culture creators, the cultural web. This is the constructive vision: what a healthy missional community looks like from the inside.
- *The Church as Movement* (2016, with Dan White Jr.) expands the frame from one community to a movement of communities. Eight competencies for multiplication. The shift is from culture to catalysis.
- *The Scandal of Leadership* (2023) diagnoses what goes wrong. Why do churches that aspire to the vision of the first two books end up reproducing domination? The Powers theology provides the answer: mimetic desire captures leaders unconsciously, and institutional systems embody fallen principalities.
- *Faithful Resistance* (Substack, 2025-present) extends the diagnosis to the broader cultural moment. If Powers operate through institutional systems, what happens when political, technological, and economic powers converge toward authoritarianism?

Each work builds on and presupposes the previous. The early work is constructive; the later work is diagnostic. Together they form a complete arc: vision, multiplication, diagnosis, resistance.

### Recommended reading order

| Order | Work | Why this sequence |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 1 | *Creating a Missional Culture* (2012) | Foundational frameworks: polycentric leadership, five culture creators, cultural web. Most accessible entry point. |
| 2 | *The Church as Movement* (2016) | Expands from one community to a movement. Interactive format with exercises. Best read after understanding the cultural framework. |
| 3 | *The Scandal of Leadership* (2023) | The diagnostic layer. Best appreciated after understanding what Woodward is trying to protect (the vision of books 1-2). Dense but essential. |
| 4 | Substack "Faithful Resistance" (2025-present) | The contemporary application. Extends Powers theology to current political dynamics. Best read after the theological grounding of book 3. |
| 5 | PhD thesis / Academia.edu papers | For those wanting the full academic treatment behind *The Scandal of Leadership*. |

## Movemental Content Strategy Implications

- **Pillar mapping**: Woodward serves the **Missional Leadership**, **Movement Ecclesiology**, **Power & Institutional Critique**, and **Discipleship Formation** content pillars. His work also feeds into **Church Planting**, **Spiritual Formation**, and **Cultural Analysis** categories.

- **Cross-author connections**: Strong alignment with **Alan Hirsch** (APEST, apostolic movement -- Hirsch is the direct upstream influence; Woodward extends and applies Hirsch's frameworks). Natural pairing with **Dan White Jr.** (co-author), **David Fitch** (Powers theology, mission), **Brad Brisco** (missional church planting, post-Christendom). Conceptual overlap with **Michael Frost** (incarnational theology). Potential contrast/complement with authors representing more institutional or attractional ecclesiology.

- **Content gaps Movemental can fill**: (1) No audiobooks for any of his 3 authored books -- significant production opportunity; (2) No centralized video presence -- his 25-40 scattered videos could be aggregated and supplemented; (3) No online course on any discoverable platform despite running V3 training for 10+ years; (4) The Girard-Wink-Stringfellow synthesis is his most distinctive intellectual contribution but exists only in a 418-page book -- needs shorter-form expression; (5) The "Five Culture Creators" framework has strong assessment/diagnostic potential (like Hirsch's APEST assessment) but no digital tool exists.

- **Series potential**: (1) "Five Culture Creators" -- each equipper as a standalone lesson/article/video; (2) "Unmasking the Powers" -- a course translating the *Scandal of Leadership* thesis into accessible modules; (3) "Polycentric Leadership" -- practical guide to shared leadership models; (4) "Prophetic Discipleship" -- the Substack Beatitudes series repackaged as a formation course; (5) "Church as Movement" -- the eight competencies as an online cohort curriculum; (6) "Faithful Resistance" -- Powers theology applied to contemporary culture.

- **Searchable concepts**: polycentric leadership, missional culture, five equippers, APEST leadership, powers and principalities, mimetic desire, kenotic leadership, church as movement, missional community, dream awakener, domineering leadership, theology of the powers, Walter Wink church, Rene Girard leadership, scapegoating, self-emptying leadership, neighborhood church planting, four spaces of belonging, missional-incarnational, post-Christendom ecclesiology, faithful resistance, prophetic discipleship.

- **Distinctive positioning for Movemental**: Woodward's unique value is the **scholar-practitioner bridge at the deepest level** -- integrating continental philosophy with 30+ years of church planting. No other author in the missional movement offers both PhD-level Girardian analysis and street-level V3 church planting experience. This combination positions him as the go-to voice for leaders who want to understand *why* churches default to domination, not just *how* to build better ones.
