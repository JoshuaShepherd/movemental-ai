### Abstract (from thesis; abridged)

All ecclesial leadership exercises power — either legitimately (Christ-like holy love) or illegitimately (selfish ambition). The thesis addresses leaders’ “fall” into **domineering** patterns (pride, manipulation, status, empire-building), which wound people and the church’s witness, especially where leaders claim a **missional** identity. Missional literature has surfaced imitation, desire, and Powers language, but not integrated them. **Wink** links leaders who mimic the Powers to the demonic and the “domination system”; **Girard** ties imitation and desire to the Powers. Together they yield an **imitation-based framework** connecting missional leadership, mimetic desire, and a theology of the Powers. Redeemed leadership reshapes desire toward discipleship, community, and mission through **positive mimesis** of Christ.
