### Polycentric Leadership

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2012 (*Creating a Missional Culture*) |
| Components | Leadership as "many centers that interrelate" — neither hierarchical (top-down CEO-pastor) nor flat (undifferentiated); reflects Trinitarian community; activates the priesthood of all believers |
| Visual / model | Analogies: jazz ensemble, geese in flight formation, Swiss government |
| Adoption evidence | Core of V3 training curriculum (one of the eight competencies); MaryKate Morse: "the chapters on polycentric leadership alone are worth the price of the book" |
| Source | [IVP — Creating a Missional Culture](https://www.ivpress.com/creating-a-missional-culture) |

Leadership organized around multiple interrelated centers as a third way between single-leader hierarchy and structureless flatness, grounded in the communal nature of the triune God.

### Five Culture Creators (Five Equippers)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2012 (*Creating a Missional Culture*) |
| Components | Ephesians 4:11 gifts reframed as culture-shaping vocations: Dream Awakener (apostle), Heart Revealer (prophet), Story Teller (evangelist), Soul Healer (pastor), Light Giver (teacher) |
| Visual / model | Each culture creator cultivates one of Five Environments (Learning, Healing, Welcoming, Liberating, Thriving) |
| Adoption evidence | "Five Equippers" Vimeo teaching series; recurring in V3 training, Missio Alliance and Verge Network talks; Carlus Gupton called it "one of the most thorough developments" of the Eph. 4:11 framework |
| Source | [IVP — Creating a Missional Culture](https://www.ivpress.com/creating-a-missional-culture) |

A reframing of APEST roles as culture-shaping vocations rather than hierarchical offices, each generating a distinct environment a fully missional community requires.

### Theology of the Powers

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (PhD thesis); 2023 (*The Scandal of Leadership*) |
| Components | Synthesis of Walter Wink (powers embodied in systems / the Domination System), René Girard (mimetic desire, scapegoating), William Stringfellow (Image, Institution, Ideology); principalities created good but fallen and redeemable, Satan/demonic as emergent and unredeemable |
| Visual / model | Stringfellow's image / institution / ideology mapped to identity / praxis / telos |
| Adoption evidence | PhD thesis (~80,385 words); 418-page trade book; Missio Alliance 3-part series; Substack "Faithful Resistance"; ~274 Google Scholar citations across works |
| Source | [100Movements — The Scandal of Leadership](https://www.100movements.org/books/scandal-of-leadership-jr-woodward) |

A diagnostic theology arguing that domineering church leadership results from unconsciously mimicking the fallen Powers; the alternative is positive mimesis of Christ — "not to imitate Christ is to imitate the Powers."

### Imitation-Based Leadership Framework

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2020 (PhD thesis); 2023 (*The Scandal of Leadership*) |
| Components | Leaders are inescapably imitative (Girard); imitating worldly power = domineering; imitating Christ's kenosis = life-giving servant leadership; no neutral ground |
| Visual / model | Oscar Romero as the primary positive exemplar of kenotic leadership |
| Adoption evidence | Central thesis of *The Scandal of Leadership*; carried through podcast circuit (Theology in the Raw, Faith in a Fresh Vibe) |
| Source | [Manchester Research Explorer](https://research.manchester.ac.uk/en/studentTheses/missional-leadership-mimetic-desire-and-a-theology-of-the-powers) |

A binary leadership formation model: leaders either reshape their desires toward Christ's self-emptying example or default to replicating the Powers' domination.

### Three Contours of Leadership

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2023 (*The Scandal of Leadership*) |
| Components | Identity (who leaders are), Praxis (how they lead and relate), Telos (what they value and pursue); domineering leaders malform across all three |
| Visual / model | Maps to Stringfellow's image / institution / ideology |
| Adoption evidence | Diagnostic structure within *The Scandal of Leadership* |
| Source | [Amazon — The Scandal of Leadership](https://www.amazon.com/Scandal-Leadership-Unmasking-Powers-Domination/dp/1955142246) |

A three-part diagnostic for leadership health across who a leader is, how they lead, and what they pursue.
