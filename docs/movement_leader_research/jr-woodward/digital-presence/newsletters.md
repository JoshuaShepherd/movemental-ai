### Content Focus

The newsletter explores faithful Christian witness and resistance in an era of concentrated power. Core themes include:

- Grounded spirituality and hope-filled imagination
- Naming and unmasking the Powers (drawing from Walter Wink, William Stringfellow)
- Discerning how to embody the Way of Jesus amid technocratic, autocratic, and oligarchic pressures
- Learning from historical examples of faithful resistance
- Cultivating courage for present-day action
- Prophetic discipleship and justice

## Observations

1. **Substack is the primary newsletter channel** — "Faithful Resistance" is a substantive publication with bi-weekly cadence and a clear theological-political thesis
2. **Clean handle**: jrwoodward.substack.com — secured the primary vanity URL (unlike Alan Hirsch who has alanhirsch1)
3. **Paid tier available** — $10/month or $100/year indicates intent to monetize, though most content appears free
4. **Modest but growing audience** — "hundreds of subscribers" is early-stage but the newsletter launched relatively recently (~2025)
5. **Distinct content niche** — the newsletter's focus on Powers, resistance, and justice is more politically engaged than his books, which focus on missional ecclesiology and leadership
6. **PhD-powered content** — the newsletter directly leverages his doctoral research on Powers and Principalities, giving it academic depth unusual for Substack
7. **Cross-platform amplification** — Substack posts are shared on X/Twitter and Facebook
8. **Recommendation network** — his Substack recommendations reveal his intellectual community: David Fitch, Danielle Strickland, A.J. Swoboda, Word&Way
9. **Gap**: The personal website (jrwoodward.com) does not prominently feature newsletter signup, creating a missed opportunity to convert website visitors to email subscribers
10. **Gap**: No free lead magnet or downloadable resource identified to drive email signups
