# Frameworks

### The Seven Self-Defeating Behaviours

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2010 (*7 Deadly Sins of Women in Leadership*) |
| Components | Limiting self-perceptions; failure to draw the line (boundaries); inadequate personal vision; an unhealthy work-life rhythm; the "disease to please"; colluding instead of confronting; neglecting the inner circle / family |
| Source | [7 Deadly Sins of Women in Leadership](https://www.amazon.com/Deadly-Sins-Women-Leadership-Self-Defeating/dp/0310119979) |

A diagnostic naming the seven internal patterns that most often hold leaders back, surfaced so they can be owned and transformed rather than hidden.

### Metamorphic Leadership

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2024 (*Metamorph*) |
| Components | Godly self-discovery; healthy relationships; Spirit-led mission — read through biblical figures such as Moses, Esther, and the Woman at the Well |
| Source | [Metamorph](https://www.amazon.com/Metamorph-Transforming-Leadership-Inspired-Extraordinary/dp/1955142602) |

A model of leadership as whole-person transformation, formed through Scripture's ordinary, extraordinary people rather than acquired as technique.

### The Four Questions

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | — (used in talks and coaching) |
| Components | "Why me?" · "Why here?" · "Why this?" · "Why now?" |
| Source | [The Profile — Premier Plus](https://www.premier.plus/the-profile/podcasts/episodes/leadership-special-kate-coleman-transforming-into-leaders-for-tomorrow) |

A re-orienting diagnostic Coleman uses to anchor leaders back in calling and context rather than in role or achievement.
