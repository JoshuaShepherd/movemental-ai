# Biography

Rev Dr Kate Coleman is a British Baptist minister, author, and leadership development specialist who has spent more than thirty-five years equipping leaders across the church, charity, voluntary, and business sectors. Born in Ghana before moving to England, she came to Christian faith while studying biochemistry and pharmacology at Southampton University, and was drawn into leadership soon afterward — first as a church elder in a congregation that initially opposed women leaders, an experience she has described as a "baptism of fire." She became the first Black woman accredited as a Baptist minister in the United Kingdom, the first Black woman President of the Baptist Union of Great Britain (2006–2007), and the first Black woman Chair of the Evangelical Alliance Council (2012–2014).

Her first pastorate was at Chalk Farm Baptist Church in London, where she gained her ministerial accreditation. In 2000 she moved to Birmingham and planted The Regeneration Centre, an innovative local church plant. She has preached at the opening services of both Labour and Conservative Party conferences and has worked with the United Nations on leadership development curriculum for women leaders.

Coleman is the author of *7 Deadly Sins of Women in Leadership: Overcome Self-Defeating Behaviour in Work and Ministry* — first self-published and later issued in a revised edition by Zondervan — and *Metamorph: Transforming Your Life and Leadership*, her reading of leadership through biblical figures such as Moses, Esther, and the Woman at the Well. She was named among the 20 most influential Black Christian women leaders in the UK (2008) and one of the top 25 Women Racial Justice Champions by Churches Together in Britain & Ireland (2021).

Today she is the founding director of Next Leadership, a leadership development organization equipping leaders in the public, private, voluntary, and church sectors, where she works as a strategic advisor, mentor, and coach to leaders and organizations at local, national, and international levels. She is a Certified Stakeholder Centered Coach and a former Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts (FRSA), with executive education from Oxford's Saïd Business School.
