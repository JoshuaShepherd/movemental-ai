# A Letter to Kate Coleman

## Calling

You have spent more than thirty-five years on a single question: what is it that holds leaders back, and what would it take to set them free? It surfaced early and against resistance. You came to faith as a science student at Southampton, were drawn into leadership soon after, and took your first real role as an elder in a church that did not at first welcome women in leadership — a season you have called a "baptism of fire." That fire set the work of your life. You have carried the question from the pew to the pulpit to the page, and you have answered it not with theory but with formation: naming the patterns that defeat leaders, and walking with people through the change.

The calling has repeatedly put you first across a threshold so that others could follow. First Black woman accredited as a Baptist minister in the UK. First Black woman President of the Baptist Union of Great Britain. First Black woman Chair of the Evangelical Alliance Council. You planted The Regeneration Centre in Birmingham and learned the costly work of building a church from nothing. You preached at the opening of national party conferences and helped shape leadership curriculum for the United Nations. None of these were trophies you set out to collect; each was a place the calling would not let you stop short of.

In *7 Deadly Sins of Women in Leadership* you wrote the calling down — the seven self-defeating behaviours named so plainly the book outgrew its self-published run and was taken up by Zondervan. In *Metamorph* you carried it from diagnosis to transformation, reading godly self-discovery, healthy relationships, and Spirit-led mission through Moses, Esther, and the Woman at the Well. And in the coaching chair, conference after conference, you have returned to four questions — why me, why here, why this, why now — that re-anchor a leader in calling rather than role. Your vocation now is as much curatorial as instructional: you hold a vast store of judgment about who is ready for which room, and the task of this season is to hand it on.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

You have built a real and durable body of work, and almost all of it is public — but it is scattered, and it is filed under the organization rather than under you.

Two books carry the core. *7 Deadly Sins of Women in Leadership* sits on Amazon UK and US, Barnes & Noble, Christianbook, the Church House Bookshop, Blackwell's, and on Audible and Everand as an audiobook. *Metamorph* is spread across Amazon, Apple, Google Play, Spotify, Chirp, Barnes & Noble, and Waterstones, in print, ebook, and audio.

Around the books sit two signature programs, each on its own company landing page: the **7DS** book-and-video study you co-created with Cham Kaur-Mann, with its Breakthrough one-day events, peer-led book clubs, and the "My Deadly Sins" self-assessment; and **Ellevation**, the immersive learning experience for women built on *Metamorph*.

Your recorded voice lives inside other people's shows. You talked through *Metamorph* and the four questions with Andy Peck on *The Profile* (Premier Plus, also on YouTube), went deeper on the *TheoDisc Podcast*, and walked through the seven behaviours again on *The Women Scholars and Professionals Podcast* and the *Recovering God Podcast*. Your interviews and profiles sit on partner publications — Premier Christianity, Church Times, Woman Alive, the Evangelical Alliance's *idea*, CBE International, the Baptist Union — with no thread tying them together.

The relational layer is fragmented the same way. The people you have led are held inside the institutions that introduced them: the Baptist Union, the Evangelical Alliance Council you chaired, Churches Together in Britain & Ireland, the UN curriculum work. The cohort lists for Breakthrough and Ellevation live in Next Leadership's own systems. The leaders you coach across business, charity, church, and government live in your phone and your memory. You already sit one endorsement degree from the wider missional network — Alan Hirsch and Christine Caine both put their names on *Metamorph* — a connection currently recorded only on a book's back cover.

And the largest pool of value you hold is the one least likely to be written down: why those seven behaviours and not eight, how the Breakthrough day is sequenced so it lands, the case studies behind thirty-five years of coaching, the wisdom of planting and leading The Regeneration Centre. That material has barely left the room.

## Constraints

A few realities shape the season ahead, and they are yours to set.

Your name resolves to Next Leadership, not to a hub of your own — there is no personal author site, no newsletter under your name, no hosted podcast, no personal video channel, and no archive linking book to program to interview to talk. That is the gap to close, but closing it should not contradict who you are: being founding director of Next Leadership is the reason this work matters now, not an obstacle to it.

The deepest material is embodied, and surfacing it must happen at your pace. The aim is to capture judgment and case wisdom on your terms — through assisted interviews, dictation, and capture you control — never to manufacture a voice that is not yours. Every draft remains yours to edit, refuse, or rewrite. The authorship stays with you; the work is to gather and connect what you have already built so the practitioners it was written for can finally reach it.
