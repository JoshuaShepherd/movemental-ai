## Snapshot
Afro-Boricua theologian-practitioner with 35+ years in ministry. Founded **Passion2Plant** (2020), the only national BIPOC-woman-led church planting network in the U.S., and directs **Púlpito Fellows**, a Lilly-funded three-year preaching fellowship at Urban Strategies. Adjunct faculty at Fuller Seminary. Board member, Sojourners. Executive team, Latino Leadership Circle. Only woman inducted into Palm Beach Atlantic's inaugural Florida College of Preachers (Oct 2025). Contributor to *Need to Know* (100 Movements Publishing, 2023) alongside Alan Hirsch.

## Why she matters for Movemental
1. **Hirsch network adjacency** — already co-authored into a 100 Movements Publishing title with Alan Hirsch, Scot McKnight, Jo Saxton, Lisa Rodriguez-Watson, Mandy Smith, Beth Allison Barr, Danielle Strickland. Known quantity to Alan's readership.
2. **Network she anchors is under-served by existing platforms** — BIPOC women planters, Latina Pentecostals, bivocational/co-vocational leaders — a distinct audience from Alan's core, Brad's Exponential-adjacent audience, or Jo's majority-culture women-in-leadership audience.
3. **She's already building her own digital platform** (Substack "Mujerista in Motion", Medium, The Passion Center, Púlpito Voices YouTube, elizabethrios.com). She is exactly the kind of operator who would benefit from the Content System Build and AI-enabled RAG/voice agent — but she's doing it by hand today.
4. **Justice + mujerista theology** is an intellectual tradition largely absent from the current movemental leader roster; adding her diversifies the platform's theological surface area.
