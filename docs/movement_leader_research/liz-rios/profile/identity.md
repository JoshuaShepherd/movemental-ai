# Identity — Rev. Dr. Liz Rios

## How she describes herself
- "Afro-Boricua theologian, educator, and activist"
- "Nuyorican Afro-Latina writer/activist"
- "Multi-hyphenate creative"
- "Mujerista in motion" (Substack handle / self-naming)

## Theological coordinates
- **Mujerista theology** (Ada María Isasi-Díaz lineage) — theology from the lived experience of Latinas
- **Barrio theology** — contextual theology rooted in urban Latinx neighborhoods
- **Pentecostal / Charismatic** formation (AG roots) integrated with Disciples of Christ ecclesiology
- **Justice-oriented ecclesiology** — her DMin thesis and public teaching argue civic engagement and justice are constitutive of church planting, not add-ons
- **Egalitarian / women-in-leadership** — core conviction; anchored the Center for Emerging Female Leadership (1996–2001) and still the drumbeat of Passion2Plant and *Need to Know*

## Signature framings she returns to
- **"Global majority" planters** — preferred term over "minority" or "ethnic"
- **"Holistic, justice-focused churches"** — contra the church-growth / attractional model she was trained in
- **"The digital pulpit"** — Púlpito Fellows thesis: social media and streaming are not adjuncts to ministry, they *are* ministry surface area for this generation of Latina/o leaders
- **"Prophetic legacy"** — her Mama Leo Lecture keynote title at Union (2024); Leoncia Rosado Rousseau (NYC's first Latina pastor) as formational figure
- **"Women are warriors"** — chapter title in *Latino Religions and Civic Activism* (Oxford UP); recurring motif

## Rhetorical posture
- **Autobiographical-theological** — routinely grounds argument in her own lived experience (Lower East Side, Afro-Boricua, mother of a child with special needs, widowed-in-grief narrative arc around Daniel)
- **Naming exclusions explicitly** — her own founding of Passion2Plant is narrated as a response to church planting networks that "did not prepare me for the real challenges I faced as a BIPOC woman planting a church in a community marked by poverty, systemic neglect, and food insecurity"
- **Disrupt/cultivate binary** — Passion2Plant mission: "disrupt oppressive systems and cultivate faith spaces of transformation"
- **Bilingual register** — Spanish-English code-switching is a consistent content signal (Púlpito, Cafecito Conversations, Mujerista, Latinas of Legacy)

## Core convictions (inferable from corpus)
1. The future of U.S. Christianity runs through Latina/o, Black, and bivocational BIPOC women.
2. Church planting training as currently designed centers white, male, attractional assumptions; retraining the field requires new curricula authored from the margins.
3. Justice praxis (civic engagement, anti-Christian-Nationalism, anti-death-penalty, pro-immigrant) is theologically non-negotiable.
4. Spiritual formation and trauma-informed pastoral care are inseparable from church planting health.
5. Digital tools are liberation tools when stewarded by those previously excluded from the pulpit.
6. Women's leadership is not an HR question; it is an ecclesiological and eschatological one.
