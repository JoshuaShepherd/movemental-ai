## Executive Summary

Lucas Pulley is a second-generation microchurch leader who has spent **14+ years** planting and multiplying decentralized church in two cities — founding the **Carbondale Movement** (now Carbondale Underground) while on InterVarsity campus staff in Southern Illinois, then moving to Tampa to become the operational heir of **Brian Sanders'** Underground Network. When the Sanders family left Tampa for Ireland in January 2019 to plant internationally, Lucas stepped into the Executive Director role (May 2020 per prior public descriptions). In 2025–26 the executive team was restructured: **Joel Repic is now Executive Director**, with Lucas identified on the Tampa Underground public staff page as **Movements Director** (Underground Network / UG Movements — the translocal arm that supports the 100+ Tampa microchurches *and* dozens of sister movements around the world). This is a promotion of focus, not a demotion — Lucas now sits at the apostolic-translocal layer of the movement while Tomy Wilkerson leads Tampa day-to-day and Joel runs the executive team.

Lucas is a **practitioner first, theorist second**. He still pastors a neighborhood house church in Tampa's subsidized housing projects alongside his wife and three kids. His distinctive frame — repeated verbatim across podcasts — is **"cities transformed by the priesthood of all believers activated in the mission of God."** His intellectual scaffolding draws from Alan Hirsch (APEST, *The Forgotten Ways*), Brian Sanders' *Underground Church* paradigm, Ori Brafman's *Starfish and the Spider*, Lance Ford and Rob Wegner's Hubology framework, and a math-and-systems sensibility inherited from his BA in Pure Mathematics (Southern Illinois University) and mathematical research stint at **TU Braunschweig (Germany)** before he ever went into full-time ministry.

Digitally he is **under-leveraged by roughly an order of magnitude relative to his embodied influence.** He has:

- 10 Medium posts total (2019–2026) on `lucaspulley.medium.com` — 128 followers.
- 1 Missio Alliance article (*The Parable of the White Pastor*, April 2019) — prescient and widely underread.
- 1 long-form teaching segment (53 minutes) inside the Underground's in-house *Underground Essentials 2020* Teachable course ("Underground as Creedal Community").
- No books (authored, co-authored, edited, foreword, or chapter contribution that the audit could surface).
- No newsletter other than a simple Airtable signup on his homepage.
- No dedicated podcast that he hosts solo; he is a recurring voice on the Tampa Underground *Microchurches Podcast*.
- No personal Substack, Patreon, or course under his own name.

He is, in other words, **the exact kind of apostolic-practitioner trusted voice Movemental's canonical doctrine names** — not an implementation buyer, but a person whose fragmented public corpus is precisely what Movemental's ecosystem layer exists to serve (see [docs/build/strategy/movement-leaders-as-ecosystem-layer.md](../../build/strategy/movement-leaders-as-ecosystem-layer.md)).

His network density is his largest unpriced asset: **Brian Sanders** (mentor / predecessor, now 100 Movements-adjacent), **Joel Repic, Tomy Wilkerson, Stacy Gaskins, Jeremy Stephens** (Tampa Underground Executive Team), **Rob Wegner and Lance Ford** (Starfish and the Church / Hubology co-conspirators), **Preston Sprinkle** (Theology in the Raw), **Joshua Johnson** (Shifting Culture), **Brian Johnson and Rob Wegner** (Exponential NEXT Microchurch track), and indirectly **Alan Hirsch / Michael Frost / Hugh Halter** via the Underground / 5Q / Forge orbit.
