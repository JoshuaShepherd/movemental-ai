## Cross-disciplinary framing — his signature intellectual move

Lucas is the one voice in the microchurch cluster who **imports secular frames into ecclesiology at the systems level, not the illustration level**. Others in his tribe reach for metaphor; he reaches for *model*:

- **Mark Granovetter's threshold model** — applied to cultural change in church communities (*Granny Shot*)
- **Ori Brafman's starfish-vs-spider** — applied as structural theology, not decoration
- **APEST (from Hirsch)** — compared cross-domain against corporate org-design (*Does the Corporate World Use APEST Better?*)
- **Amanda Palmer's *Art of Asking*** — interdependence as a theological practice
- **Joseph Myers / Mike Breen's four spaces of belonging** — operationalized as microchurch gathering strategy
- **Mathematical / network-theory sensibility** — showing up in language like "synapses," "nodes," "network effects," "two-entity models"

This is the clearest differentiator of his voice relative to adjacent authors.

## Argument architecture — his typical essay / talk shape

1. **Observation** — lived practitioner data (14 years, 100+ microchurches, specific stories).
2. **Reframe** — a secular framework from outside the usual theological toolbox.
3. **Application** — a concrete change in church practice.
4. **Self-implication** — *"I have one, too"*-type admission.
5. **Return to Jesus** — usually a single closing sentence; never decorative.

This is visible in *The Granny Shot*, *5 Ways Calling Is Being Hijacked*, *10 Thoughts After 10 Years*, and *The Parable of the White Pastor*.

## What the corpus notably does *not* do

- Does not engage in **polemical denominational-border-setting** (unlike 9Marks or Together for the Gospel material).
- Does not engage in **verse-by-verse exposition** or systematic theology.
- Does not **self-promote**; almost never mentions his own accolades or platform growth.
- Does not engage in **political commentary** (no discernible partisan posture in public writing).
- Does not use **seeker-sensitive vocabulary** — no "target audience," "brand," "growth strategy," "ROI," "reach."
- Does not elevate **entrepreneurship** per se; the social-enterprise framing is missional, not business-heroic.
