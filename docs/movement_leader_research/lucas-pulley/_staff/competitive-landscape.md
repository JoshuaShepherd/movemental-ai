## Framing

Lucas does not have *competitors* in the commercial sense — the microchurch / decentralized-church movement is collaborative by design, and his network is more accurately a co-laboring ecosystem. The right question is: **in the space of "voices readers / practitioners turn to for microchurch / decentralized movement leadership," who else occupies that attention? And what is Lucas's distinct position?**

## Lucas's distinct position in this landscape

**1. He is the most-deeply-embedded active operator on this list.**
Sanders moved to Ireland. Wegner is conference-convenor-first. Ford is author-first. Cole is elder statesman. Hirsch is thinker-teacher. Brisco is denominational-strategy. Only Lucas still pastors a neighborhood house church in a housing project *while* running the Movements Director seat of a 100+ microchurch network. That density is rare.

**2. He has the mathematician's sensibility.**
The systems / networks / thresholds framing is not performative; it is how he actually thinks. No one else on the list argues with Granovetter. No one else pairs inner-city Tampa with TU Braunschweig. The combination is strategically distinctive.

**3. He is explicitly anti-celebrity.**
Cole, Halter, Ferguson, Frost, Chan, Hirsch have all functioned as named authorial figures in the tradition. Lucas consciously decenters his own name. That works against personal brand growth, but for *team-brand* growth it is precisely right for how Movemental should onboard him.

**4. He bridges the Hubology cluster and the Underground cluster.**
Sanders is the Underground origin; Wegner and Ford are the Hubology articulators; Lucas is the only figure embedded in both conversations as a first-class voice. Movemental onboarding therefore reaches both clusters through one relationship.

**5. He is pre-book in a post-book field.**
Everyone else on the direct-peer list has at least one book. Lucas does not. That is either a competitive disadvantage (not yet citable in the book-authoring tradition's vocabulary) or an extraordinary opportunity (the first-book market in this niche is wide open for him, and Movemental can be the platform that ships it).

## Competitive positioning for Movemental

Movemental should position Lucas as:

- **The practitioner's practitioner** in the microchurch / decentralized-church conversation.
- **The systems-theorist inside the movement** — not by credential but by native intellectual pattern.
- **The embedded Tampa voice** — inside housing projects, inside a 100+ microchurch network, not in the stadium.
- **The emerging first-book author** in the Exponential / Hubology / Underground cluster.

Movemental should **not** position Lucas as:

- A solo thought-leader / keynote-celebrity.
- The next Alan Hirsch (different altitude, different role).
- A denominational strategist (he is not; this is Brisco's lane).
- An academic theologian.
