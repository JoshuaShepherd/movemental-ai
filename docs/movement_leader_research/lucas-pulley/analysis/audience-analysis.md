## Primary audience segments

### 1. Microchurch practitioners (core audience)

**Who**: leaders (ordained and lay) currently planting or directing microchurches or house-church networks across North America.
**Size estimate**: the Exponential / Underground / KC Underground / Sentralized / 5Q cluster reaches **tens of thousands of practitioners**; the active-planter subset is smaller (low thousands).
**Where they find him**: in-house Microchurches Podcast; Tampa Underground Microchurch Conference; Exponential NEXT Microchurch track; peer word-of-mouth.
**Why they engage**: he is one of the few voices combining 14 years of operator experience with articulate framework language (ecclesial minimum, servant-equipper, hubology).
**What they want more of**: practical governance, leadership-development pipelines, covocational financial models, coaching rhythms.

### 2. Missional-church pastors thinking about decentralization (adjacent / curious audience)

**Who**: conventional-church pastors (20–1,500 attendance) who are reading Frost, Hirsch, Brisco, and Sanders and wondering whether to restructure.
**Size estimate**: the Missio Alliance / Exponential / Forge / NAMB-adjacent audience numbers in the **tens of thousands**.
**Where they find him**: Theology in the Raw, Shifting Culture, The Church Digital, Missio Alliance, Exponential.
**Why they engage**: he is a post-attractional voice without the edge of some decentralized-church advocates; he pastors real people daily; he doesn't pitch.
**What they want more of**: a clear transition map from conventional church → microchurch network; sober practitioner risks and failure modes.

### 3. Seminary students and young ministry practitioners (pipeline audience)

**Who**: Fuller / Trinity / Gordon-Conwell / Regent / Bethel / Asbury students and post-grads exploring alternatives to mainline or megachurch pastorate.
**Size estimate**: low thousands actively paying attention; much larger passive pool.
**Where they find him**: professors who assign Hirsch / Brisco / Sanders; Theology in the Raw (strong with this cohort); Exponential NEXT cohorts.
**Why they engage**: he is a Fuller MGL alum, not an outsider to their tradition; his math-to-mission arc is usable identity narrative for pivoters.
**What they want more of**: *a first book*; a cohort-based onboarding path; a clear sense of how to move from "I'm interested" to "I'm doing this."

### 4. Cross-cultural / multicultural ministry leaders (values-aligned audience)

**Who**: leaders (often BIPOC, often bivocational) committed to multicultural and multi-ethnic ministry, wrestling with power dynamics in historically-white-led institutions.
**Size estimate**: smaller audience, high engagement density; Missio Alliance, Red Letter Christians, Sojourners, and progressive-evangelical adjacent outlets.
**Where they find him**: *The Parable of the White Pastor* is the entry point.
**Why they engage**: Lucas is a white male leader who publicly argues minorities must lead multicultural community and who has redirected significant organizational budget (the $50K → $200K Minority Leadership Fund) in that direction.
**What they want more of**: sustained writing on multicultural power redistribution; second essay on how the fund has actually performed; case studies on minority-led microchurches in the Underground.

### 5. Covocational / marketplace Christians (adjacent audience)

**Who**: working professionals (often 25–45) integrating marketplace vocation with gospel witness.
**Size estimate**: growing rapidly; the covocational audience is the fastest-growing adjacent audience in the missional space.
**Where they find him**: via Brisco's world (one hop via Lance Ford); via Exponential's covocational track.
**Why they engage**: 90%+ of Underground microchurch leaders are covocational by design; Lucas has lived and taught this model for 14 years.
**What they want more of**: a Brisco-Pulley collaboration; explicit teaching on covocational financial architecture and social enterprise (the 20 Underground microchurches using social-enterprise models).

### 6. Tampa Underground partners and donors (institutional audience)

**Who**: existing givers, ministry partners, and microchurch leaders inside the Tampa Underground network.
**Size estimate**: low thousands; donors may be in the hundreds.
**Where they find him**: directly — undergroundnetwork.org, tampaunderground.com, monthly Sunday gatherings, email communications.
**Why they engage**: they are the home base; his credibility with them is pastoral, not performative.
**What they want more of**: steady, personal, not-hyped communication; honest metrics; story-led updates.
