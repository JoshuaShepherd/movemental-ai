## Strategic frame

Lucas is not a marketing subject to be "promoted." He is an apostolic-translocal practitioner whose **existing body of work is the asset**; the playbook's job is to *aggregate, connect, and distribute* it — not to manufacture new persona. Four guiding principles:

1. **Voice first, platform second.** His anti-celebrity posture is real and theological; any campaign that hero-frames him will land wrong. Framings that center team + ecosystem will land right.
2. **Owned corpus before amplification.** Every investment starts by converting lent audience (podcasts, features) into owned audience (newsletter, Movemental profile). Amplification without ownership compounds fragmentation rather than solving it.
3. **Convert existing artifacts before authoring new ones.** He has 10 essays, 14 frameworks, 8+ podcast appearances, a 53-min Teachable session, and 4+ conference talks already — a year of playbook activity can be done entirely on the existing corpus before asking him to produce a single new asset.
4. **Leverage network effects.** His single-hop relationships to Sanders, Wegner, Ford, Brisco-via-Ford, and the Tampa Underground executive team are the highest-ROI distribution surface available.

## What NOT to do

1. **Do not hero-brand Lucas.** His theology decenters celebrity. Platform framing must center team and ecosystem.
2. **Do not migrate content away from Underground surfaces.** Tampa Underground staff page, undergroundnetwork.org, Microchurches Podcast, Underground Essentials Teachable course — all continue.
3. **Do not force platform adoption.** If he prefers Twitter/X over threading Bluesky, that is fine. Amplify where he is.
4. **Do not publish his *Can we talk about false accusations yet?* (Oct 2021) essay** without pastoral review. Context sensitivity required.
5. **Do not promote him as a standalone figure** when Tampa Underground executive peers (Joel Repic, Tomy Wilkerson, Stacy Gaskins, Jeremy Stephens) are co-laboring at the same level. Co-onboard them where possible.
6. **Do not compete with Exponential, Starfish Initiatives, or the Microchurch Conference.** Those are assets; Movemental amplifies them.
7. **Do not monetize content aggressively.** Lucas's economic instincts are missional (social-enterprise, donor-supported, covocational), not commercial. Pricing must match that posture.
