## Recurring thematic spine across his podcast appearances

Patterns show up across nearly every episode:

1. **The ecclesial minimum** (worship, community, mission).
2. **Priesthood of all believers activated in the mission of God** (recurring tagline).
3. **Mission-specific vs. distributive microchurch distinction.**
4. **Four spaces of belonging** (intimate / personal / social / public).
5. **Covocational default** (90%+ of network leaders work multiple income streams).
6. **Governance approaches that prevent heresy and abuse** while preserving grassroots leadership.
7. **Transitioning from unconscious to conscious competence** in ministry.
8. **Hubology vocabulary** (equipping servants, local elders, apostolic teams).
9. **His own house church in the Tampa housing projects** — near-universal grounding anecdote.

## Assessment

- **Guest-circuit strength is the strongest dimension of his digital presence.** 8+ distinct shows, anchored by top-tier hosts (Sprinkle, Johnson, Wegner, Ford), is exactly what a mature practitioner circuit looks like.
- **No personal show.** A second-order opportunity: Lucas could host a *Microchurch Movements* podcast interviewing sister-movement leaders globally — it would match his Movements Director lane perfectly.
- **No dedicated audio archive.** His Medium writing has a URL home; his podcast appearances do not — they live on the hosts' feeds, and tracking them requires this dossier.

1. **Aggregate the 8+ appearances** into a "Lucas Pulley on Podcasts" page on `lucaspulley.com` (or his Movemental profile).
2. **Standardize the bio** he sends to podcast hosts — the inherited Executive Director vs. current Movements Director language is creating SERP drift.
