## Collaborator-density observation

Lucas occupies an **unusually central position in the microchurch / decentralized-church cluster**. The single-hop reach from Lucas to the primary authors of that tradition (Sanders, Hirsch, Frost, Halter, Cole, Dale, Ford, Wegner, Brisco one hop removed, Stetzer two hops) is wider than the reach from almost any of them to the others. That makes him a high-leverage onboarding target — onboarding Lucas predictably opens conversations with several additional voices.

## Collaborator-density observation — the cautionary version

The *same* fact means Lucas's audience is mostly composed of people already known to the network. His platform growth will not come from expanding within the Underground / Hubology orbit (saturated) but from **pushing into adjacent audiences**: post-evangelical urban practitioners, multicultural-church leaders (via *Parable of the White Pastor*), covocational entrepreneurs (via Brisco overlap), and academic-missiology-curious seminary students.
