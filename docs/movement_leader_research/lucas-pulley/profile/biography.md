## Narrative Biography

Lucas Pulley is one of the central operational figures in the North American microchurch movement. For 14+ years he has planted and directed decentralized networks of grassroots churches, first in Carbondale, Illinois, and then — and still — in Tampa, Florida, where he succeeded Brian Sanders as the lead executive of the Underground Network and now (as of 2025–26) sits on the Executive Team as **Movements Director** of Underground Network's translocal arm, supporting the 100+ microchurches in Tampa Bay and dozens of sister movements around the world. He continues to pastor a neighborhood house church inside Tampa's inner-city subsidized housing projects with his wife Jami and their three kids.

His path to ministry was unusual. Lucas began not in seminary but in **pure mathematics**. He earned a Bachelor of Arts in the Theory of Mathematics at Southern Illinois University, Carbondale, and spent time on a mathematical research team at **Technische Universität Braunschweig** in Germany. He converted to, and was formed in, a particular strand of missional Christianity in his SIU years — shaped early by Alan Hirsch's *The Forgotten Ways* — and joined the staff of **InterVarsity Christian Fellowship** on the SIU Carbondale campus from 2011 to 2014. During that window he founded and directed the **Carbondale Movement** (later the Carbondale Underground), a small, decentralized cluster of microchurches that operated as a sister network to Brian Sanders' Tampa Underground.

In the mid-2010s Lucas made the geographic and vocational move south. He joined the Tampa Underground staff as **Tampa Movement Director**, and by late 2018 he was writing publicly as an executive of the Underground Network. In January 2019 the founder, **Brian Sanders**, relocated his family to Ireland to plant internationally — a leadership handoff that Lucas himself documented in real time in his Medium post *"I'm HYPED About 4 Changes at Tampa Underground in 2019"* (December 2018). By May 2020 Lucas was publicly described as **Executive Director** of Underground Network, a role he held through at least 2024. In 2024–25 the Underground restructured its top layer: **Joel Repic** moved into the Executive Director seat, **Tomy Wilkerson** became Tampa Director, **Stacy Gaskins** became Managing Director, and **Lucas took the Movements Director lane** — effectively the apostolic-translocal seat that serves sister movements globally. This realignment matches Lucas's consistent language in 2023–24 interviews about Underground Network being "over 160 microchurches in Tampa Bay and serving dozens of similar decentralized and empowering movements around the world."

Alongside his operational work, Lucas earned a **Master of Global Leadership** with an emphasis in Missions & Missiology from Fuller Theological Seminary. He is, by description and by self-identification, a **"writer. speaker. trainer. coach."** The description is accurate but understated — his public written corpus is lean (10 Medium posts, 1 Missio Alliance article, 0 books), while his embodied training and teaching output — conferences, cohorts, coaching calls, in-house podcast episodes, 53-minute Teachable lectures, hub-team consultations for sister movements — is extensive and under-digitized.

## Origin & Formation

The **math → mission pivot** matters more than it first appears. Lucas did not come up through a seminary-pastor pipeline. He came up through problem-solving, formal proof, and research collaboration, and then converted that sensibility into ecclesial architecture. It shows in his writing: the repeated reach for systems-theory vocabulary (network effects, thresholds, starfish vs. spider, ecosystems, two-entity models, decentralized graphs), the impatience with unexamined program-logic, the willingness to question "our ecclesiology" as something that "should be constantly under investigation." His rural-central-Illinois roots — GCMS High School, SIU — give him a midwestern plainness that cuts through the Silicon-Valley-ish jargon of the decentralized-church conversation; the pairing of that plainness with a trained mathematical sensibility is his signature voice.

His InterVarsity years on the SIU Carbondale campus (2011–2014) are the bridge. Campus ministry taught him how to build communities from scratch without infrastructure, how to equip student leaders in APEST-style distribution, and how to treat evangelism and community-formation as one problem rather than two. The Carbondale Movement / Carbondale Underground that he founded in parallel was the first outing of his microchurch instinct — a network rather than a church, a training hub rather than a stage.

## Career & Ministry Arc

The Tampa move is the hinge. Under Brian Sanders, Tampa Underground had been running as a microchurch network since 2006. When Lucas arrived as Tampa Movement Director he inherited — and then had to steward through — the 2019 handoff when Sanders relocated to Ireland. Lucas's Medium post about that transition reads as both a status update and a love letter to Sanders' role ("Brian Sanders has been the media team, finance team, coaching team, has negotiated every contract, project managed every Hub, and designed every budget while faithfully leading a microchurch"), alongside a sober inventory of the systems Tampa Underground would need to replace him at scale — counseling care for leaders, a rebuilt Sunday gathering ("From Crucible to Catalyst"), a $50,000 (growing to $200,000 by 2025) Minority Leadership Fund to address racial inequities in missionary fundraising, and four new executive hires (Tomy & Brianna Wilkerson, Marcus Ramsay, George Wood).

Between 2020 and 2024 Lucas became the primary external face of Underground Network. He appeared on **Theology in the Raw with Preston Sprinkle (#1022)**, **Shifting Culture with Joshua Johnson (#175)**, **The Church Digital Podcast with Jeff Reed (#227)**, **For Everyday Leaders (#21)**, **Exponential NEXT Podcast** with Rob Wegner and Brian Johnson, **The Starfish and the Church** with Rob Wegner and Lance Ford (the "Hubology: Rise of the Servant Equippers" episode), and **KC Underground** with Rob Wegner — a substantial guest circuit that tracks his expanding centrality in the Hubology / decentralized-church cluster. He spoke at Exponential 2023 Orlando and is on the Exponential Global 2025 speaker roster alongside Jeremy Stephens, Stacy Gaskins, and Tomy Wilkerson. He teaches annually at Tampa Underground's **Microchurch Conference** (next edition March 12–14, 2026). His single long-form article on **Missio Alliance** — *The Parable of the White Pastor* (April 2019) — is one of the most theologically serious pieces in his corpus and foreshadowed a decade of conversation about power redistribution in multicultural church.

In 2024–25 Underground restructured the top, and Lucas moved into Movements Director. This is not a step back; it matches where his convictional gravity has been all along — on the *network of networks*, not on running a local org. The Hubology framework (developed jointly with Rob Wegner and Lance Ford) clarifies the lane: Lucas is an **equipping servant / translocal apostolic-team member**, not a local elder.
