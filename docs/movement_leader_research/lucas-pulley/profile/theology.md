## Core Thesis

The church is not a building, a service, or a program — it is **an extended spiritual family committed to the leadership of Jesus and pursuing some piece of the mission of God in the world**. That family is the *irreducible unit* of ecclesial life (the "ecclesial minimum"), and therefore the job of leaders is not to produce bigger services but to *cultivate the conditions* in which ordinary believers are activated, empowered, and sent as the priesthood of all believers into their cities, neighborhoods, and networks.

Lucas is a **Jesus-movements theologian of the decentralized church** — apostolic in orientation, pneumatological in practice, neighborhood-anchored in embodiment, and explicitly critical of attractional, celebrity, and power-consolidating models.

---

## Theological Tradition

**Primary**: Missional-Incarnational ecclesiology, specifically the **Underground / microchurch / Hubology** strand (Brian Sanders, Alan Hirsch, Lance Ford, Rob Wegner, Michael Frost).
**Secondary influences**: Neo-Anabaptist praxis; theology of place; APEST ministry-gifts framework; contextual missiology shaped by Fuller's Missions and Missiology faculty; practical sociology (Mark Granovetter's threshold model; Ori Brafman's *Starfish and the Spider*).
**Tradition-neutral in denominational terms**: Underground Network is non-denominational; Lucas's theology is evangelical in the mere-Christian sense but deeply outside seeker-sensitive / attractional / programmatic evangelicalism.

Lucas functions as a **practitioner-articulator** rather than an academic theologian. He does not argue from the biblical languages or from historical-critical scholarship. He argues from lived practice, sociological data, and a confident reading of the movement narrative in Acts as a blueprint for the decentralized priesthood of all believers.

## Distinctive Positions

- **Church as verb, not noun.** Headlined by Koinesúnē's 2024 feature ("The Underground Network: Church as a verb"). The institutional church is not the point; the gathered-and-sent family is.
- **Ecclesial minimum over ecclesial maximum.** Church is worship + community + mission; anything beyond that is discretionary and should be justified.
- **Calling is not self-actualization.** Strong pushback against the *Eat-Pray-Love* framing of calling as personal optimization; calling is submission, outward orientation, communal confirmation.
- **Power redistribution is not optional in multicultural church.** Programs are insufficient; the question is whether language, narrative, budget, and decision-making are actually relinquished.
- **The celebrity pastor model is idolatry-adjacent.** Even innovative microchurch leaders harbor secret growth-focused ambitions ("I have one, too"); this is a spiritual-formation challenge, not a strategy problem. ("Small and Significant Microchurches in a Celebrity Age," Jan 2020.)
- **Accelerate, don't gatekeep.** Most pipelines over-screen; God's people are ready far sooner than traditional systems permit.
- **Start at the margins.** Not as sentiment but as strategy — the homeless, trafficked, incarcerated, immigrant, and systemically excluded are not mission objects; they are the starting point of Jesus's movement.
- **Microchurches are Christologically-centered or they are meaningless.** Thought #10 of the 10 Thoughts: without Jesus at the center, all models collapse into organizational novelty.
- **Entropy toward comfort.** Organizations drift toward predictability; the leader's job is repeated conscious re-risking. "Change is the only constant" in Jesus's ongoing movement beyond institutional control.
- **Emotionally healthy spirituality is not optional for directors.** Cross-cultural competency, systems-differentiation understanding, and personal emotional health are required for network-director longevity.

## Theological Evolution

Lucas's arc runs: (1) **mathematics and campus ministry** at SIU Carbondale and TU Braunschweig — network-and-systems thinking + InterVarsity's student-led apostolic sensibility; (2) **Carbondale Movement (2009–2014)** — first outing of a decentralized microchurch network; (3) **Tampa Movement Director** — inheriting Sanders' decade-old experiment and running operational scale; (4) **Executive Director transition (2019–2020)** — from operator to principal; the *HYPED About 4 Changes* post is the best window into how he theologized that moment (counseling care, Minority Leadership Fund, restructured Sunday gathering, new executive hires); (5) **Missio Alliance / Parable of the White Pastor (2019)** — his clearest theological essay, on power redistribution in multicultural community; (6) **10 Thoughts After 10 Years (May 2020)** — the consolidated manifesto moment, written during the first months of COVID, crystallizing a decade of practice into 10 durable convictions; (7) **Hubology framework (2024–2025)** — external collaboration with Wegner and Ford, moving the conversation from "what is a microchurch" to "what kind of hub and servant-equipper leadership scales them"; (8) **Movements Director role (2024–26)** — the vocational shift from running a local-plus-translocal organization to serving primarily the *translocal network of networks*, which mirrors the Hubology-era theology.

The through-line is consistent: *the center of the church is not the professional pastor or the Sunday service — it is a family-sized community on Jesus's mission in a specific place, networked with many others doing the same, supported by translocal equippers who serve rather than consolidate power.*
