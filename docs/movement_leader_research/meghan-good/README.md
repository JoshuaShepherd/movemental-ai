## Executive Summary

Meghan Larissa Good is a Mennonite pastor, theologian, author, and award-winning preacher whose work centers on a single animating project: recovering a *better Christian story* by reading all of Scripture — and the whole life of faith — through the lens of Jesus. Across sixteen-plus years of pastoral ministry (Albany Mennonite Church in Oregon, then more than a decade as Lead Pastor of Trinity Mennonite Church in Glendale, Arizona), two books with Herald Press, a Doctor of Ministry from Portland Seminary, and a faculty role at Anabaptist Mennonite Biblical Seminary, she has built one of the most theologically serious and pastorally warm voices in the progressive/post-evangelical reconstruction space.

Her distinctive contribution is a **Christocentric hermeneutic** — the "Jesus lens" or "Jesus glasses" through which she teaches readers to interpret Scripture — paired with a constructive ecclesial vision. Her first book, *The Bible Unwrapped: Making Sense of Scripture Today* (Herald Press, 2018, foreword by Gregory Boyd), translated her DMin work on the *dialogic shape of Christian authority* into an accessible lay guide and won **Outreach Magazine's 2019 Resource of the Year** in Theology/Biblical Studies. Her second, *Divine Gravity: Sparking a Movement to Recover a Better Christian Story* (Herald Press, 2023, foreword by Dennis R. Edwards), moved from *how to read* to *what kind of church to become*, calling for a Spirit-led, grassroots "new reformation" rooted in cross-shaped power and centered-set belonging. The endorsement roster for *Divine Gravity* — Richard Mouw, Brian McLaren, Greg Boyd, David Gushee — punches far above the weight of a small denominational press, with Gushee naming her "a rising star of the next generation."

She is also an unusually credentialed preacher: in 2018 she became the **first-ever recipient of the Frederick Buechner Award for Excellence in Preaching**, and her voice — literary, narrative-driven, honest about doubt, and quietly funny — sits squarely in the Buechner tradition. She has preached at MennoCon (MC USA's national convention) in 2019 and 2021, lectured on Christ-centered hermeneutics for the Anabaptism-at-500 project, and teaches narrative-preaching workshops.

In May 2025, Good did something that reframes her entire trajectory: she **resigned her twelve-year pastorate at Trinity Mennonite** to take a movement-level role as **Resource Development Manager at Jesus Collective** (the Jesus-centered network associated with Greg Boyd), where she also chairs the international Theology Circle. She is now, by her own design, a movement builder rather than a single-congregation pastor — and she is producing daily Jesus-centered devotional content on her "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel (54+ Luke episodes in the first months of 2026 alone).

That combination — a movement thesis literally titled *Divine Gravity: Sparking a Movement*, a career inflection into network-building, a daily content engine, and exceptional theological IP — meets a strikingly underdeveloped owned-platform layer (Digital Presence Score 2.7/5). Her channels operate as disconnected islands: the YouTube engine isn't surfaced on her website, follower counts are small/unknown, there's no online course, no centralized hub, and sixteen years of award-caliber preaching sit almost entirely un-archived. The monetization opportunity is modest but real — estimated **$15K–$48K/year** — and the strategic opportunity is unusually well-timed: she is reorganizing her public life around movement work at the exact moment an owned platform would matter most.

## Top 5 Opportunities

1. **Centralize the disconnected content ecosystem**: Her YouTube engine (54+ episodes), website/newsletter, two books, speaking calendar, and Jesus Collective work operate as separate islands. A hub that cross-links every channel — especially YouTube → books → newsletter — is the highest-leverage, lowest-cost intervention. (HIGH priority, LOW effort)

2. **A course from *The Bible Unwrapped***: The book already ships with a free study guide that is effectively the skeleton of an 8–10 module course; reviewers repeatedly call it the best book they've read on reading Scripture. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort)

3. **Unlock the sermon archive**: Sixteen-plus years of narrative preaching by the inaugural Buechner Award winner is almost entirely inaccessible. Requesting and thematically organizing 15–20 sermons is the single largest untapped content reservoir. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort)

4. **"Reading Jesus" companion infrastructure**: The daily YouTube series already has momentum. Companion study guides (as email lead magnets), consistent description templates linking to books and newsletter, and an audio-only podcast extraction extend reach without new production. (HIGH priority, LOW effort)

5. **A narrative-preaching workshop course**: Her Buechner credential is an unused platform asset. A "Narrative Preaching in the Buechner Tradition" course, marketed to seminary preaching programs and pastor networks, reaches a high-value professional audience her current channels can't. (MEDIUM priority, MEDIUM effort)
