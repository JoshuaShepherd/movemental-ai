## Audience Profile

### Who Is the Audience?

**Primary audience**: Pastors, ministry leaders, and theologically educated laypeople in Mennonite Church USA and adjacent Anabaptist/Brethren traditions who are wrestling with how to articulate and embody a Jesus-centered faith in a post-Christendom, post-scandal ecclesial environment. Typically ages 30–55, seminary-educated or self-educated theologically, often bi-vocational or in small-to-mid-size congregations. They encounter Good through MC USA denominational channels (Anabaptist World, MennoCon, conference circuits), AMBS, and Herald Press.

**Secondary audience**: Post-evangelical and progressive-evangelical Christians — many in deconstruction or reconstruction — who are looking for a credible, intellectually honest, Jesus-centered alternative to both fundamentalism and theological liberalism. They are drawn to Good through Jesus Collective, the Boyd/ReKnew network, Brian McLaren adjacent communities, Red Letter Christians, and her "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel. Ages 25–45; often digitally native; may have no Mennonite background at all but resonate with her Christocentric hermeneutic and "better story" framing.

**Tertiary audience**: Preachers and homiletics students — across traditions — who discovered Good through her Frederick Buechner Award for Excellence in Preaching, her preaching workshop offerings (listed on her speaking page), or referrals from Portland Seminary. They are interested not just in her theology but in her craft of narrative preaching. This is a smaller but highly influential audience: preachers who adopt her approach can multiply her reach through their own congregations.

**What Hannah values in Good**: The Christocentric hermeneutic of *The Bible Unwrapped* gives her congregation a framework that holds together scriptural authority and honest engagement with hard texts. *Divine Gravity*'s eight-thesis "better story" gives her language for articulating why Jesus-centered faith is not just defensible but beautiful. Good's Anabaptist credential matters: Hannah trusts that Good is speaking from inside the tradition, not reforming it from outside.

**What Hannah struggles with**: Wishes Good had more sermon resources — specific preaching helps, not just principles. The "Reading Jesus" YouTube series on Luke is useful but she wishes for more denomination-specific formation content and study guides linked to the lectionary.

### Positioning Summary

Good's audience is substantially smaller in raw count than her peer comparables but unusually concentrated at the intersection of (a) pastoral/theological credibility in Anabaptist traditions and (b) the post-evangelical deconstruction/reconstruction audience seeking a Jesus-centered anchor. She is the most credentialed voice in the Mennonite tradition on the themes of biblical interpretation and movement ecclesiology, and among the most articulate voices in the Jesus Collective orbit. Her audience compares favorably to JR Woodward's in engagement quality — pastoral, formation-oriented, high-trust — though smaller in absolute terms.

The most important audience signal: her "Reading Jesus" YouTube devotional series (54+ episodes in 2026 alone, focused on Luke) represents a daily-content relationship with an audience that is choosing to accompany her through an entire Gospel. This level of sustained engagement is rare and suggests a highly committed viewer base, even if subscriber counts are unknown.

## Movemental Audience Implications

- **Warm audience routes**: Jesus Collective (she already chairs the Theology Circle and develops resources there); Herald Press/MennoMedia readers; MC USA convention attendees; Ohio Mennonite Conference network; Church of the Brethren clergywomen; Boyd/ReKnew audience; Red Letter Christians podcast listeners; Shifting Culture Podcast listeners. Each of these represents a pre-warmed audience that already trusts Good's voice.

- **Content format priority**: Books and devotional video are her audience's primary modes of engagement. The "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel is the clearest growth vector — it already produces content at scale and has an audience relationship that no other channel of hers matches. A Movemental-hosted hub should function as the connective tissue that links the YouTube series to the books, the newsletter, the speaking calendar, and course opportunities.

- **Underserved audience segment**: Preachers and homiletics students represent a high-value, underserved audience for Good. The Buechner Award is a credential that should unlock speaking and teaching opportunities in seminary preaching programs — but this audience cannot easily find her online. A Movemental profile with a dedicated preaching-craft content track could unlock this audience segment.

- **Cross-pollination with Boyd/McLaren**: The highest-value cross-referencing on the platform would pair Good with Boyd (shared Christocentric hermeneutic, mutual forewords) and, more cautiously, with McLaren (shared deconstruction-reconstruction audience, overlapping theological concerns). Good provides the Anabaptist tradition depth and pastoral credibility that McLaren lacks; McLaren provides the scale and name recognition.

- **Honest audience sizing**: This is a micro-to-growing leader by any standard digital metric. Her audience is not comparable in size to JR Woodward's (25K–40K estimated) or Alan Hirsch's (50K–100K estimated). Movemental should set realistic expectations: the opportunity here is not to amplify an already-large platform but to help a high-quality, under-leveraged voice reach the audience her work warrants — particularly through centralizing the "Reading Jesus" channel, connecting it to the books and newsletter, and unlocking new audiences through preaching networks and the broader post-evangelical/Jesus Collective ecosystem.
