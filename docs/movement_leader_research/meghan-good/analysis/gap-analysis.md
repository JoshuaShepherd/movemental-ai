# Gap Analysis: Meghan Larissa Good

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## Executive Summary

Meghan Larissa Good is a deeply credentialed narrative theologian-preacher (Frederick Buechner Award winner, Portland Seminary DMin, Duke Divinity MDiv, 16+ years of pastoral ministry, 2 Herald Press trade books, Jesus Collective Theology Circle Chair, AMBS Faculty of Record) whose digital presence significantly under-represents her embodied gifts. The gap is structural and specific: her strongest single asset — the "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel (54+ devotionals on Luke in 2026 alone) — is disconnected from her books, newsletter, speaking calendar, and organizational affiliations. Her 16 years of Trinity Mennonite sermons are unarchived. Her award-winning preaching is almost entirely undocumented online. And she is at the precise career inflection point — just stepped out of the pastorate into full-time network/resource work — where building owned digital infrastructure is unusually timely and unusually productive.

The monetization opportunity is calibrated realistically: this is a micro-to-growing voice with a modest but high-trust audience, not a megachurch digital platform. Revenue estimates reflect that reality. The opportunity is genuine, proportional, and well-matched to Movemental's capacity to add infrastructure to gifted leaders who need it.

## Embodied Work vs. Digital Expression

### Strong embodied work NOT captured digitally

1. **16+ years of narrative preaching** — Trinity Mennonite (12 years) and Albany Mennonite (5 years): weekly sermons by an award-winning preacher in the Buechner tradition, almost entirely inaccessible online.
2. **Portland Seminary preaching formation** — the DMin program that produced the Buechner Award included 3 years of intensive homiletical formation in *Bone Gardener* (12 selected sermons as DMin artifact). These sermons are accessible in limited form through George Fox Digital Commons but not surfaced from any of her channels.
3. **AMBS DMin teaching** — years of Zoom-based mentoring and teaching leadership formation for doctoral ministry students. No syllabi, session recordings, or teaching content is publicly accessible.
4. **Jesus Collective Theology Circle work** — chairing a 13-person international theological body whose work directly shapes Jesus Collective resources, curriculum, and network theology. The outputs of this work are invisible beyond what appears in Jesus Collective's published resources.
5. **Northern Seminary "Formation for Ministry" instruction (2025)** — entirely without digital footprint.
6. **Mennonite Education Agency webinars (2016)** — three webinars on Anabaptist biblical interpretation that may have reached a significant denominational audience; current accessibility of recordings unknown.

### Digital content NOT connected to embodied work

1. **"Reading Jesus" YouTube series** — the most substantive digital content production, but it functions in isolation from the books, newsletter, and organizational affiliations that give it context and depth.
2. **Journal essays** — strong standalone theological content (published approximately monthly) but not connected to the YouTube series, not organized into thematic collections, and not capturing organic search traffic for the topics they address.
3. **Newsletter** — private and non-archival; its content and value cannot be evaluated from outside, and it does not systematically direct readers to YouTube or other content.
