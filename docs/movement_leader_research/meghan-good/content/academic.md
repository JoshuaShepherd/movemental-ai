## DMin Dissertation

### "Between Breaths: The Dialogic Shape of Christian Authority"

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Full Title** | Between Breaths: the Dialogic Shape of Christian Authority |
| **Author** | Meghan Larissa Good |
| **Institution** | Portland Seminary at George Fox University |
| **Program** | Doctor of Ministry (DMin) |
| **Year** | 2018 |
| **Repository** | George Fox University Digital Commons: https://digitalcommons.georgefox.edu/dmin/272/ |
| **Lead Mentor / Advisor** | Dottie Escobedo-Frank, DMin |
| **Committee Members** | Jamie Noling-Auth, DMin; Nijay Gupta, PhD |
| **Published Book Artifact** | *The Bible Unwrapped: Making Sense of Scripture Today* (Herald Press, 2018) — the lay-accessible version of the dissertation's core argument |
| **Sermon Portfolio Artifact** | *Bone Gardener: Selected Sermons* (George Fox University, 2017) — 12 sermons embodying the dialogic homiletical approach |
| **Award** | Frederick Buechner Award for Excellence in Preaching (Portland Seminary, May 2018) — **first recipient** of this award |

**Abstract Summary**: Good's dissertation examines how Christian authority functions across Protestant traditions in a post-Reformation context. She argues that "the authority of the Bible functions dialogically" — not residing in a single static source (sola scriptura alone) but through a complex conversation among Scripture, tradition, Spirit, reason, and experience, with different Protestant branches prioritizing these sources differently. She introduces a **dialogical paradigm** as a fresh framework for understanding religious authority as relational rather than hierarchical. The dissertation includes a practical case study on gay marriage as an example of dialogic authority in practice. The accompanying book (*The Bible Unwrapped*) provides a lay introduction to core questions of biblical interpretation and authority.

**Key Themes**:
- Dialogic authority vs. monologic (static) biblical authority
- Post-Reformation Protestant epistemology
- Scripture, tradition, Spirit, reason, experience as dialogue partners
- Practical hermeneutics for lay communities
- Preaching as embodied, relational dialogic event

**Research Question**: How does Christian authority function dialogically, and what does that look like in contemporary Protestant practice?

## Research Interests and Theological Position

Based on confirmed publications, teaching, and interviews:

1. **Dialogic hermeneutics** — Scripture's authority functions relationally through multiple dialogue partners (Bible, tradition, Spirit, reason, community)
2. **Jesus-centered interpretation** — The person and teaching of Jesus as the hermeneutical key to Scripture, including the Old Testament ("Jesus glasses")
3. **Homiletics** — Narrative preaching; story and metaphor; dialogic sermon construction; preaching as communal discernment
4. **Ecclesiology** — Centered-set church; movement ecclesiology; the "new reformation" thesis
5. **Christian authority post-Reformation** — Navigating Protestant fragmentation; reclaiming coherent authority without fundamentalism
6. **Women in Scripture and ministry** — Implicit through her Luke work, interview with Barbara Brown Taylor, and role as a female DMin award recipient in homiletics

**Theological tradition**: Anabaptist-Mennonite (primary), with engagement across post-evangelical, progressive evangelical, and mainline ecumenical networks. Affiliated with Jesus Collective, which is explicitly cross-denominational and Jesus-centered rather than confessionally Anabaptist.
