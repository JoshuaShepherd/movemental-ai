## Summary

Good's teaching footprint spans four distinct contexts: (1) her primary academic role as Faculty of Record at **AMBS** (Doctor of Ministry program, Teaching Leadership competency); (2) adjunct / guest teaching at **Northern Seminary** (Formation for Ministry, 2025) and **Canadian Mennonite University** (21st Century Preaching, 2021); (3) **preaching workshops** offered directly through her personal website for emerging and experienced preachers; and (4) **retreat and conference teaching** for denominational gatherings. She does not appear to have a commercially available online course platform (e.g., Teachable, Thinkific) as of May 2026 — but her speaking/workshops page and the AMBS role represent her primary training delivery.

**Program Structure**: The DMin has five competency tracks. Students select their competency emphasis and work with a Faculty of Record in a directed study course. Good oversees the **Teaching Leadership** track, which focuses on developing leaders' capacity to teach, preach, and communicate effectively in ministry contexts — an area directly aligned with her DMin specialization (dialogic preaching authority) and the Buechner Award for Excellence in Preaching.

**Curriculum context**: The five competencies are Anabaptist Leadership, Intercultural Leadership, Teaching Leadership, Change Leadership, and Resilient Leadership. Core leadership courses are offered as January intensives on campus, with online coursework throughout.

**Faculty of Record peer group** (DMin program, also listed): Deborah K. Byler, and at least six others. Good is specifically named for Teaching Leadership in AMBS's faculty descriptions.

## Key Teaching Frameworks

Good's teaching, whether at AMBS, in workshops, or in conferences, consistently deploys these frameworks:

| Framework | Source | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Dialogic Authority** | DMin dissertation ("Between Breaths," 2018) | Scripture's authority functions dialogically through tradition, Spirit, reason, and experience — not in a single static source |
| **Jesus-centered Hermeneutics** | *The Bible Unwrapped* (2018) | Reading Scripture through the "Jesus lens"; moving beyond biblicism or skepticism |
| **Centered-set Ecclesiology** | *Divine Gravity* (2023) | Community defined by trajectory toward Jesus rather than bounded membership; draws on Mark Baker's Anabaptist centered-set model |
| **The New Reformation** | *Divine Gravity* / speaking | Western Christianity entering a reform period; identifying and aligning with what the Spirit is doing |
| **Story and Metaphor in Preaching** | Preaching workshops / DMin | Craft elements: narrative construction, metaphor, dialogic sermon form |
