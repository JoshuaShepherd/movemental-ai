## Overview

Meghan Larissa Good is not an academic publishing scholar in the traditional sense. Her two books — *The Bible Unwrapped* (Herald Press, 2018) and *Divine Gravity* (Herald Press, 2023) — are trade-level Christian titles written for pastors, laity, and thoughtful churchgoers, published through the Mennonite denominational press (MennoMedia / Herald Press). Her DMin dissertation (Portland Seminary, 2018) is accessible via George Fox University's Digital Commons but has not been published as a monograph.

As a result, her academic citation footprint is modest compared to publishing scholars. Her influence operates primarily through **practitioner channels**: denominational speaking circuits, pastoral training, seminary DMin work, YouTube/podcast teaching, and endorsement by prominent public theologians. This is not unusual for her type of output.

## Dissertation

| Title | Institution | Year | URL |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Between Breaths: the Dialogic Shape of Christian Authority" | Portland Seminary, George Fox University | 2018 | [digitalcommons.georgefox.edu/dmin/272/](https://digitalcommons.georgefox.edu/dmin/272/) |

**Abstract summary**: Good argues that "Christian authority cannot be possessed as a static and absolute quality of any single entity. Its shape is fundamentally relational." The dissertation traces historical shifts in Protestant views on authority, proposes a dialogical paradigm, includes a case study on gay marriage, and accompanies the artifact of *The Bible Unwrapped* as its practical output. The dissertation is classified under "Christianity Commons."

**Citation status**: No confirmed academic citations of the dissertation have been identified. It is publicly accessible in the George Fox University Digital Commons, which makes it findable by researchers. **UNKNOWN — needs Google Scholar/ProQuest search.**
