# Biography: Meghan Larissa Good

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-05-27

---

## Narrative Biography

Meghan Larissa Good is a pastor, theologian, author, and award-winning preacher who has spent more than sixteen years in local church ministry while simultaneously developing a significant national and international teaching platform in Anabaptist/Mennonite Christianity. Her core conviction — that "God looks like Jesus, and that changes everything" — anchors every dimension of her work: her preaching, her two books with Herald Press, her faculty role at Anabaptist Mennonite Biblical Seminary (AMBS), her leadership of Jesus Collective's theology circle, and her growing movement work to help individuals and churches recover what she calls "a better Christian story." By the time she resigned her pastoral charge in May 2025 to pursue broader network-building, she had become one of the most prominent voices in Mennonite-Anabaptist biblical theology in North America.

## Formation: Gordon College and Duke Divinity (2002–2009)

Good earned a B.A. in Biblical and Theological Studies and Philosophy from Gordon College in Wenham, Massachusetts (2006). Gordon, an evangelical liberal arts college, gave her a rigorous grounding in both Scripture and philosophical reasoning — a pairing that would become characteristic of her later writing, which moves fluidly between biblical exegesis and theological argument without surrendering accessibility.

From Gordon, Good went to Duke Divinity School in Durham, North Carolina, where she earned her M.Div. in 2009. Duke's divinity school is shaped by figures such as Stanley Hauerwas and the moral theology tradition; its culture of demanding theological seriousness would sharpen Good's intellectual instincts. After completing her MDiv, she worked at Duke University's Center for Reconciliation — an ecumenical initiative focused on building communities that embody reconciliation across lines of race, ethnicity, and conflict — and spent time teaching and preaching in Uganda. This formative post-graduate period oriented her toward the global church and the concrete political and social dimensions of the Christian gospel, themes that would resurface in her later theology of reconciliation.

## Pastoral Career: Oregon (c. 2008–c. 2013)

Good's pastoral ministry began at Albany Mennonite Church in Albany, Oregon, where she served as lead pastor. In October 2010, she was invited to be the featured speaker for Hesston College's annual Anabaptist Vision and Discipleship Series, speaking on the theme "The Inside-Out Kingdom" — an early indication that her ministry gifts were already drawing wider denominational attention. By this point she had established herself as a Mennonite pastor committed to Jesus-centered spirituality and the life of the local congregation. Her precise start and end dates at Albany Mennonite are not publicly confirmed (UNKNOWN — needs research), but available sources place her there from approximately 2008 to approximately 2013.

## Pastoral Career: Arizona (c. 2013–2025)

Good relocated to the Phoenix/Glendale, Arizona area and joined Trinity Mennonite Church in Glendale as teaching pastor, later serving as lead pastor. The church appears in sources under both "Glendale" and "Phoenix" addresses, consistent with the Glendale suburb's proximity to Phoenix. Trinity Mennonite is a congregation within Mennonite Church USA. Good's titles in different published sources vary between "teaching pastor" and "lead pastor" during this period, suggesting a possible title evolution; the Anabaptist World resignation article (2025) identifies her title at departure as "lead pastor."

During her time at Trinity Mennonite, Good developed her preaching voice, completed her DMin, published two books, and became a significant presence in Mennonite Church USA. She gave the Friday morning worship address at MennoCon19 (Kansas City, July 2019), preaching from John 20:19-23 in a sermon titled "He Breathed on Them" — drawing on the pneumatological imagery of the ruach of God to challenge churches running "on empty" with a call to receive fresh divine breath for mission. Two years later, at MennoCon21 (July 2021), she gave the keynote address "Do Peacemakers Need Jesus?", arguing before approximately 1,044 attendees that reconciliation without a center of gravity in Jesus loses its very ground: "Reconciliation work that gives up on proclaiming Jesus loses its very ground." She also contributed regularly to Anabaptist World and preached at Mennonite conventions across North America.

Good resigned from Trinity Mennonite Church effective May 2, 2025, citing a sense of call to broader network work. She stated: "I don't know yet exactly what this might look like, but I have a number of ideas that seem worth exploring, and I believe that stepping down from my current pastoral role is one of the necessary steps to create time and space for this larger sense of calling." — [Anabaptist World](https://anabaptistworld.org/pastor-resigns-to-shift-attention-to-broader-network/)

## Academic Work: Portland Seminary DMin (2014–2018)

While serving at Trinity Mennonite, Good pursued doctoral work at Portland Seminary at George Fox University in Portland, Oregon, completing her Doctor of Ministry in 2018. Her dissertation, *Between Breaths: The Dialogic Shape of Christian Authority*, argued that biblical authority is not a static property of a single source but functions dialogically — through living conversation among Scripture, tradition, Spirit, reason, and community. She challenged the Protestant tendency to anchor authority in Scripture alone, proposing instead a relational and contextual understanding more consonant with Anabaptist practice. Her committee included Dottie Escobedo-Frank, Jamie Noling-Auth, and Nijay Gupta. *The Bible Unwrapped* was produced as the accompanying lay resource designed to make the dissertation's insights accessible to congregations.

Upon completion of the DMin, Portland Seminary named Good the inaugural recipient of the **Frederick Buechner Award for Excellence in Preaching** — an award that crystallized her reputation as not only a theologian but an exceptional preacher in the literary-narrative tradition of Frederick Buechner himself.

## Academic Affiliations: AMBS and Jesus Collective

Good serves as **Faculty of Record for Teaching Leadership** in AMBS's Doctor of Ministry in Leadership program in Elkhart, Indiana. In this role, she connects regularly with DMin students over Zoom to help them develop personalized learning plans and integrate academic knowledge into their ministry contexts. She also served on the AMBS Board of Directors (confirmed as of 2019). Earlier in her AMBS involvement, she delivered the commencement address at AMBS's 73rd graduation ceremony (May 4, 2019), speaking to 20 graduates on the theme of "ministry of celebration" and urging them not to bear the burden of "saving the world": "The broken world is not out there waiting for you to go and save it. It has been saved and it will be saved by Jesus Christ." — [AMBS](https://ambs.edu/graduates-urged-to-pursue-ministry-of-celebration)

Good has also served as **Theology Chair** (later Resource Development Manager) of **Jesus Collective**, a North American organization amplifying and resourcing a movement toward more hopeful, Jesus-centered Christianity. She participated in Jesus Collective's North American tour as a keynote speaker. As of 2025, she holds the title Resource Development Manager, indicating her transition to a more operationally central role in the organization as her pastoral and movement work converge.

## Authorship

Good has published two books through Herald Press (the Mennonite/MennoMedia publisher) and contributed to a third:

**The Bible Unwrapped: Making Sense of Scripture Today** (Herald Press, 2018) grew directly from her DMin work. Drawing on contemporary biblical scholarship and the ancient Christian tradition, it addresses questions twenty-first-century readers bring to Scripture: Why does the Bible contain violence? Are there textual discrepancies? How do we handle passages with no clear contemporary application? Good calls readers past both "knee-jerk biblicism" and "skeptical disregard" toward a Christ-centered hermeneutic — reading all of Scripture through the lens of Jesus. Gregory Boyd wrote the foreword. The book was named one of **Outreach Magazine's Resources of the Year (2019)** in the Theology/Biblical Studies category — a significant evangelical recognition for a Mennonite author.

**Divine Gravity: Sparking a Movement to Recover a Better Christian Story** (Herald Press, 2023) is Good's most ambitious work. Drawing the analogy of a gravity that draws diverse objects toward a common center, she argues that Jesus himself is the gravitational center capable of holding together a fractured church and catalyzing a new reformation. The book diagnoses the contemporary crisis of Christianity — ecclesial scandals, biblical authority questions, post-pandemic deconstruction, polarization — and argues that a grassroots movement is already emerging as the Spirit draws people back to a Jesus-centered vision of God and salvation. Good employs "centered-set" ecclesiology, arguing that belonging is defined not by boundary positions but by trajectory of motion toward Jesus. Endorsers include Richard J. Mouw, Brian McLaren, David P. Gushee ("a rising star of the next generation"), Gregory Boyd, and Oneya Okuwobi. Dennis R. Edwards contributed the foreword chapter.

In 2024, Good wrote the foreword to **God Looks Like Jesus: A Renewed Approach to Understanding God** (Herald Press, 2024) by Gregory Boyd and M. Scott Boren — a Jesus Collective–affiliated resource that extends the theological vision she and Boyd share.

## The "Better Story" Vision and Resignation

Good's decision to leave Trinity Mennonite in May 2025 reflects a consistent pattern in her career of following the call to broader scope. Her entire body of work can be read as a sustained argument that the Western church has been operating with a distorted image of God — shaped more by power, fear, and cultural accommodation than by the person of Jesus — and that recovering "a better Christian story" requires concerted movement-level effort. She has spoken of many Christians carrying "a profound wound in their image of God that will only be healed through direct encounter with Jesus" and argued that "as our image of God is transformed by Jesus, we are set free to experience deeper transformation in our own humanity."

By 2025–2026, Good was based in Dayton, Ohio, conducting the Jesus Collective's resource development work, maintaining her AMBS teaching role, speaking widely (the Church of the Brethren Clergywomen's Retreat in February 2025; the Annual Mennonite Bible Study Series in Mountain Lake, Minnesota in January 2026; a California lecture series scheduled for 2027), hosting the "Reading Jesus" YouTube channel, and writing a weekly devotional newsletter for meghanlarissagood.com.
