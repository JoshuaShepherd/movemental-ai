## Identity resolution

Meghan Larissa Good is a preacher, author, and biblical teacher in the
Anabaptist/Mennonite tradition (USA). Identity is confirmed by multiple
independent sources: her authored books *The Bible Unwrapped* and *Divine
Gravity* (both Herald Press / MennoMedia), her personal site
meghanlarissagood.com, the Herald Press and MennoMedia author pages, the
Anabaptist Mennonite Biblical Seminary (AMBS) faculty page, and the Jesus
Collective leadership listing. The combination of distinctive book titles,
publisher, denomination, named institutions, and matching personal domain
removes ambiguity. There is no name-collision risk material to this dossier.

## Bio synthesis

Good holds a BA in Biblical & Theological Studies and Philosophy from Gordon
College (2006), an MDiv from Duke Divinity School (2009), and a DMin from
Portland Seminary at George Fox University (2018), where she was the first
recipient of the Frederick Buechner Award for Excellence in Preaching. She
served roughly sixteen years as a lead and teaching pastor in churches in
Oregon and Arizona — most notably as lead/teaching pastor at Trinity
Mennonite Church in Glendale, Arizona, the role still cited in older
publisher bios. Her work centers a Jesus-centered hermeneutic, captured in
her site tagline: "God looks like Jesus. And that changes everything." She is
in demand as a speaker on biblical hermeneutics, Anabaptism, contemporary
preaching, depolarizing the church, and the work of the Holy Spirit.

## Current role

Good's role has shifted from local-church pastoring toward network and
teaching work. Current roles, per her own About page and the AMBS faculty
page:

- **Resource Development Manager, Jesus Collective** — and chair of the
  Jesus Collective Theology Circle.
- **Faculty of Record for the Teaching Leadership component of the Doctor of
  Ministry in Leadership program at Anabaptist Mennonite Biblical Seminary
  (AMBS).**
- **Host of *Reading Jesus*** (podcast / YouTube; in 2026 working through
  the Gospel of Luke).

The publisher (Herald Press) bio still lists her as "lead pastor at Trinity
Mennonite Church, Glendale, AZ"; the AMBS page treats the pastorate in the
past tense ("for 15 years … served as lead and teaching pastor"). For this
dossier the current role used is **Resource Development Manager and Theology
Circle chair at Jesus Collective**, with the AMBS DMin faculty role and the
Trinity Mennonite history both named as supporting context.
