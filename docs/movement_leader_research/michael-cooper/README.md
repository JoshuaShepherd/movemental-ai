## Executive Summary

Michael T. Cooper is an American evangelical missiologist and missiological theologian who coined and built out **"Ephesiology"** — "the study of the Ephesian movement" — as a framework for understanding and catalyzing contemporary movements. He is both a scholar and a field practitioner: more than thirty years in missions, roughly ten of them as a pioneer church planter in post-communist Romania, plus a PhD in Intercultural Studies from Trinity Evangelical Divinity School and guest lectures at the London School of Economics, the University of Bordeaux, Loyola, and Baylor. His career closed the loop from practice to theory to institution to proof — the Romania decade supplied credibility, his new-religious-movements scholarship supplied method, *Ephesiology* (William Carey, 2020) supplied a brand, the TELOS. ecosystem supplied distribution, and the 2026 Asia Minor archaeology turn supplies material-culture evidence.

His signature intellectual move is **reframing missiology AS theology** rather than as a subset of it: he reads the Bible as "a missiological theology," treats Paul and John as missiological theologians, and refuses CPM/DMM operational jargon ("people of peace," "T4T," site metrics) as a deliberate theological position. His most-cited corrective — "making disciples is the mandate while church planting is a result" — anchors a coherent, scholarly, ten-framework corpus (Ephesiology, missiological theology, disciple-making-over-church-planting, healthy DNA, Christoform leadership, the Five C's, and more). *Ephesiology* won the 2020 Catalyst Services Reader's Choice Award for best book in Missiology and carries 13 named endorsers, including **Alan Hirsch**.

Cooper's gap is the **inverse** of the typical pre-platform movement leader: not absence but **dispersion**. He is extraordinarily prolific — ~17 books across at least six imprints (William Carey, Wipf & Stock, Sacred Roots / Samuel Morris, Sacred Tribes Press, and self/Ephesiology lines), a 227-episode weekly podcast, an accredited LMS (Ephesiology Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch, partnered with Kairos University), and a directed MA in Missiology of Movements at Mission India Theological Seminary — yet sits on a paper-thin owned-audience layer (~120–175 brand-account followers, ~40 Goodreads ratings, 12 Apple podcast ratings, no newsletter of scale) and severe informational dispersion with no single discoverable hub for the corpus. The enormous institutional reach his work commands (East-West's ~$33M base, Kairos, Mission India) routes audiences through institutions he does not own and converts almost none of them into an owned relationship.

That asymmetry is precisely what Movemental solves. The content asset base is exceptional and finished — Movemental does not need to commission a corpus, only to **surface, consolidate, index, and cross-link** one, then stand up a real owned-audience layer. The network compounds: onboarding Cooper opens single-hop conversations with Alan Hirsch, Kurt Nelson / East-West, the EMS / Missio Nexus missiology world, the Motus Dei CPM network (Warrick Farah), Gailyn Van Rheenen, John W. Morehead, and the GACX alliance — plus a possible cross-tie to Tampa Underground via Bill Couchenour.
