## Cross-disciplinary framing — his signature intellectual move

Cooper's defining move is **reframing missiology AS theology rather than as a subset of it**. Where most evangelical writers treat "missions" as an applied department downstream of systematic theology, Cooper inverts the order: the Bible is read as "a missiological theology" with mission at the center, and Paul and John are read not as theologians who happened to do mission but as **missiological theologians** who connected Jesus to the cultural narrative of Ephesus.

Three things make this distinctive in the field:

- **He coins a category and treats it as a discipline.** "Ephesiology — the study of the Ephesian movement" is presented (with a mock-dictionary pronunciation, *i-fē-zē-ä-lə-jē*) as a discrete object of study, not a metaphor. He claims roughly 40% of the New Testament relates to this single movement.
- **He refuses CPM/DMM jargon.** Unlike the disciple-making-movement practitioner literature, Cooper avoids "people of peace," "T4T," "generational mapping," and the metrics-of-sites vocabulary. His corrective — "making disciples is the mandate while church planting is a result" — is a deliberate theological repudiation of method-first movement language.
- **He grounds movement claims in material culture.** The 2026 Asia Minor archaeology volume and its photographic companion move the argument from exegesis-plus-illustration to inscriptions, architecture, and the archaeological record. No one else in the practitioner-movement space anchors movement theology in the physical remains of Ephesus and the Roman East.

This is the clearest differentiator of his voice relative to adjacent authors: he is the scholar-practitioner who **theologizes "movement" itself**, refusing both the academic detachment of pure NT commentary and the technique-driven register of CPM how-to.

## Argument architecture — his typical essay / talk shape

1. **Received assumption named** — e.g., "church planting is the biblical mandate," or "missions is a topic in theology."
2. **Provocative inversion** — "Nowhere in the Bible is the church commanded to plant churches."
3. **First-century grounding** — Acts 19, the Ephesian movement, Asia Minor material culture, or the Apostolic Fathers.
4. **Contemporary application** — interleaved church-planting-movement examples and the "healthy DNA" concept.
5. **Theocentric return** — disciples "committed to a missiologically theocentric passion to declare the glory of God to the nations."

This is visible across *Ephesiology*, *Make Disciples*, the Patheos church-planting piece, and the podcast's "10-Minute Ephesiologist" segment.

## What the corpus notably does *not* do

- Does not use **CPM/DMM operational jargon** — no "people of peace," "T4T," "generational mapping," "streams," or site-counting metrics. This is a deliberate refusal, not an omission.
- Does not frame mission in **pragmatic, method-driven, church-planting-as-metric terms** — his most-corrected reader misreading is the assumption that church planting is the mandate.
- Does not engage in **partisan political commentary**, though "Faith and Politics" appears as a podcast series and *Social Injustice* engages justice from a "biblical standpoint."
- Does not pursue **trade-press popularization** — the register stays scholarly; he writes for missiologists, seminarians, and serious practitioners, not a mass devotional market.
- Does not market through **personal-brand celebrity** — the public surface runs through the "Ephesiology" / TELOS. brand, not a named-founder personality.
- Does not produce a **single unified, discoverable hub** for the 17-title corpus — output is dispersed across at least six imprints (see gap-analysis.md).

## Strategic implication

Cooper's content is **scholarly-Movemental-ready** in substance: a coined category, a multi-book corpus, a 227-episode podcast, and a vertically integrated LMS. The editorial issue is not voice quality (it is high and coherent) but **discoverability and consolidation** — a 17-title corpus scattered across imprints with no canonical hub. His register must be respected, not flattened into trade-devotional; the work to be done is aggregation, indexing, and cross-linking, not rewriting.
