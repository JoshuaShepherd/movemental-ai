## Executive Summary

Michael T. Cooper is an American missiologist and missiological theologian who coined and built out **"Ephesiology"** — the study of the first-century church-planting movement centered in Ephesus — as a framework for understanding and catalyzing contemporary movements. ## Core Frameworks & Concepts

### Ephesiology

**Definition**: "The study of the Ephesian movement" — the first-century Ephesian church-planting movement treated as a discrete object of study and a normative model for contemporary movements. Explicitly *not* a commentary on Ephesians and *not* a biblical theology of missions, but **"a missiological theology of the Bible."**

**Five analytical questions**: (1) How did the movement start? (2) What did adherents believe? (3) Who led it? (4) How did it multiply and sustain growth? (5) Why did some defect into false teaching?

**Signature claim**: Roughly **40% of the New Testament** relates to the Ephesian movement.

**Key works**: *Ephesiology* (2020, William Carey — 2020 Catalyst Reader's Choice Award for best Missiology book); *Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement* (2026, Wipf & Stock).

### Missiological Theology

**Definition**: Mission as the **center** of theology rather than one topic within systematic theology. Paul and John are read as "missiological theologians" who connected Jesus to the cultural narrative of Ephesus. His densest coinage — **"missiologically theocentric"** — is the grounding test for any mission claim.

### Disciple-Making Over Church-Planting (the signature inversion)

**Definition**: "Making disciples is the mandate while church planting is a result." Church planting is *demoted from mandate to result*; disciple-multiplication is *promoted from method to mandate*. "Disciples multiply while volunteers keep busy."

**Key works**: *Make Disciples* (with Bill Couchenour, 2023); "Is Church Planting a Biblical Mandate?" (Patheos / Jackson Wu, 2020).

### Healthy DNA

**Definition**: Churches, denominations, and ministries are sustained — or fractured — by their underlying replicable spiritual "DNA." Movement health is a DNA property; movements fail when that DNA degrades into false teaching.

### Christoform Leadership

**Definition**: Christ-shaped, communal, theological, and missional leadership drawn from the Apostolic Fathers (1 Clement, Ignatius, Polycarp) — recovered *against* hierarchical or novelty-driven leadership.

**Key work**: *Letters to Churches* (2025).

### Lead Like Paul — the Five C's

**Definition**: Five empowering leadership traits derived from the Apostle Paul — **Call, Character, Charismata, Confidence, Commitment.**

**Key work**: *Lead Like Paul* (2017).

## Voice Patterns & Linguistic Signatures

### Hallmark Lexicon

Ephesiology • the Ephesian movement • missiological theology / missiological theologian • "a missiological theology of the Bible" • missiologically theocentric • make disciples / disciple-making / disciples multiply • multiply / multiplication • healthy DNA • Christoform • contextualization (policed against *syncretism*) • church-planting movement (CPM).

### Tone Markers

- **Definitional**: coins or sharpens a term, fences off what it is *not*, then builds on the cleaned-up definition.
- **Provocative inversion (constructive)**: flips a near-universal evangelical assumption ("our job is to plant churches") to recenter, not demolish.
- **Scholar-practitioner register**: interleaves first-century textual/archaeological analysis with contemporary CPM examples, then lands a compressed aphorism.
- **Confident, declarative, mildly contrarian** — a teacher's authority, not a provocateur's edge.
- **Doxological close**: returns the practical argument to "the glory of God to the nations."

### Sentence Structure Patterns

- **Coined term + mock-dictionary pronunciation gloss** (*Ephesiology [n. i-fē-zē-ä-lə-jē]*) as a branding device.
- **"Not-this-but-that" definitional fence** — defines by exclusion before content.
- **Quantified claim as hook** ("roughly 40% of the New Testament…").
- **Aphoristic contrast pair** ("Disciples multiply while volunteers keep busy.").
- **Enumerated frameworks** (the five C's; the five analytical questions).

**Pointed avoidances** (themselves a theological statement): CPM/DMM operational jargon ("people of peace," "T4T," "generational mapping," site-counting metrics); attractional / growth-hacking / platform-building vocabulary; personal-brand celebrity framing (the surface runs through the "Ephesiology" / TELOS. brand, not a named-founder personality).
