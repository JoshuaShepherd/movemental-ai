## Summary gap statement

Cooper's gap is the **inverse** of the typical pre-platform movement leader (and the precise inverse of Lucas Pulley). Pulley has book-length thinking with no books and one Airtable form; Cooper has the *opposite* problem. He is **extraordinarily prolific** — 17+ books across at least six imprints, 227 podcast episodes, his own accredited LMS, and seminary degree partnerships — yet sits on a **paper-thin owned-audience layer** (~120–175 followers across brand social accounts, no Substack, ~40 Goodreads ratings) and **severe informational dispersion** (no single discoverable hub for a 17-title corpus spread across William Carey, Wipf & Stock, Sacred Roots, Sacred Tribes Press, and multiple self/Ephesiology imprints).

The problem is not production. The problem is that a serious, award-winning, vertically integrated body of work is **scattered and undiscoverable**, and the large institutional reach it should have converted into an owned relationship (East-West, Kairos, Mission India) was never captured.

## Informational fragmentation

The defining gap. Cooper's 17-title corpus is split across **at least six publishers/imprints** — William Carey Publishing, Wipf & Stock, Sacred Roots / Samuel Morris, Sacred Tribes Press, and self/Ephesiology imprints ("Defense of the Faith from Ephesiology," "Social Justice from Ephesiology"). His writing also lives on the ephesiology.com "Until They All Hear" blog, a Patheos column (under a *misspelled* slug, `/blogs/ephesilogy/`), and Academia.edu. There is **no single discoverable surface** that consolidates more than a fraction of this output. A reader who finds *Ephesiology* on Amazon has no easy path to *Make Disciples*, *Letters to Churches*, or the 2026 archaeology volume. For an author of this volume, the absence of a unified, indexed, SEO-coherent hub is the single largest value-destruction in the dossier.

## Relational fragmentation

Cooper's audience relationship is fragmented in the inverse pattern from his content. He has **enormous institutional trust without owned access**: East-West's ~$33M missionary/donor base, the Kairos University degree funnel, the Mission India MA, Knox, Nepal Ebenezer, and Torch Trinity all route audiences *through institutions he does not own*. The 227-episode podcast has built sustained engagement (4.8/5) but converts to almost no owned list. The William Carey bestseller and the 2020 Reader's Choice Award moved through the missions world, but Cooper cannot email the people it reached. He has institutional reach where Pulley had institutional trust — and the same inability to follow up directly.

## Canonical-doctrine framing

Per `docs/build/strategy/movement-leaders-as-ecosystem-layer.md`: movement leaders are **trusted voices and an ecosystem layer**, not implementation audiences or a parallel funnel segment. Cooper is a near-archetypal trusted voice — a credentialed missiologist whose work the rest of the ecosystem cites and platforms (Hirsch endorses him; Missio Nexus and EMS carry him; Motus Dei engages him). Movemental does not "customize for Cooper" and does not absorb his institutions. Movemental provides the **owned corpus / voice / relational layer** that lets an already-substantial body of work become discoverable, coherent, and connectable to its audience — while East-West, Kairos, Mission India, and his own TELOS./Master Classes LMS remain his native surfaces.

## Why this gap is an opportunity, not a risk

Three reasons.

1. **The content asset base is exceptional and finished.** 17+ books, a coined category, 227 podcast episodes, a vertically integrated LMS, and an award-winning flagship. Movemental does not need to commission a corpus — it needs to *surface* one. That is a faster, lower-risk onboarding than any pre-book leader.

2. **The owned-layer gap is the easiest gap to close.** Aggregation, indexing, cross-linking, a real newsletter, and a single canonical hub are exactly what the Movemental platform is. The work is consolidation and discoverability, not production or editorial overhaul; his voice is coherent and platform-ready (see content-analysis.md).

3. **The network compounds.** Onboarding Cooper opens single-hop conversations with Alan Hirsch (lead endorser), Kurt Nelson and East-West, the EMS / Missio Nexus missiology world, the Motus Dei CPM network (Warrick Farah), Gailyn Van Rheenen, John W. Morehead, and the GACX church-multiplication alliance — plus a possible cross-tie to Tampa Underground via Bill Couchenour.

## Risks to note

- **Do not compete with his LMS.** TELOS. / Ephesiology Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch is his own accredited revenue and credential engine. Movemental must complement, not cannibalize (see movemental-fit.md).
- **Do not strip the scholarly register.** His value is exegetical-theological depth; flattening him into trade-devotional content would destroy his differentiation.
- **Respect East-West / Kairos / Mission India relationships.** These are institutional anchors with their own governance; Movemental aggregates and links, it does not reroute their audiences.
- **Coordination cost is real.** He already runs his own brand, LMS, and seminary partnerships — onboarding requires alignment, not a blank slate.
- **Unverified entities.** No "Ephesiology Coaching Network" is confirmed; Michael Frost's role in the 2026 book is unconfirmed; treat both as UNKNOWN in any public framing.
