## What Movemental would change for Michael

### Problems Movemental solves

- **Informational fragmentation.** A 17-title corpus split across William Carey, Wipf & Stock, Sacred Roots, Sacred Tribes Press, and multiple self/Ephesiology imprints collapses into one owned, indexed, cross-linked, SEO-coherent corpus hub.
- **Owned-audience near-zero.** ~120–175 brand-account followers and a no-scale signup form convert into a real email list with a discoverable archive.
- **Lent-to-owned conversion.** East-West (~$33M base), Kairos, and Mission India route audiences through institutions Cooper doesn't own; the 227-episode podcast converts almost none of its reach to an owned relationship. Movemental closes that loop.
- **Discoverability.** No Wikipedia article, no verified Scholar profile, a misspelled Patheos slug, ~40 Goodreads ratings for an award-winning author. Movemental's discovery surface and framework-tagging resurface the corpus.
- **Bio drift.** Movemental becomes the canonical bio source propagating correctly to podcast hosts, conference programmers, and partner institutions.

### Content Movemental would surface

- **The 17+ book corpus** — organized by the seven themes (see content-analysis.md), tagged, cross-linked, with *Ephesiology* as the pillar entry point and the 2026 Asia Minor volume + *Photographing the Movement of God* as the visual showcase.
- **The 227-episode Ephesiology Podcast** — indexed by series ("Christ: The Foundation of Mission and Church," "Reimagining our Ecclesiology," "Faith and Politics," "The 10-Minute Ephesiologist") with topical tags and cross-references to the books.
- **The named-framework set** — Ephesiology, missiological theology, disciple-making-over-church-planting, healthy DNA, Christoform leadership, the Five C's — as canonical framework pages.
- **Network map** linking Cooper's endorsers and collaborators to their Movemental profiles as they are onboarded (Hirsch first).

### New content Movemental would enable

- **A unified "Ephesiology" corpus hub** — the single discoverable home the 17-title corpus has never had.
- **The "Movemental Theology" newsletter** — periodic, scholarly-but-accessible, seeded from the podcast and the corpus, with one curated source per issue.
- **Audio essays / narrated corpus** — there is no audiobook of *Ephesiology*; narrated versions of the flagship chapters and top podcast segments fill an obvious format gap.
- **Translated / oral-format modules** — for the Global South audience (India, Nepal, Romania) his contextualization theme already serves.
- **Short-form video** — the visual companion (116 photographs + 7 maps) and "Let the Stones Speak" material are unusually well-suited to a short-form Asia-Minor archaeology series.

## What We Will NOT Do

- **Compete with TELOS. / Ephesiology Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch.** His accredited LMS and Kairos degree ladder are his own revenue and credential engine. Movemental hosts public-facing corpus and audience; the LMS keeps the cohorts and credentials. Complement, never cannibalize.
- **Strip his academic / scholarly register.** His differentiation *is* exegetical-theological depth and material-culture grounding. No trade-devotional flattening.
- **Reroute East-West / Kairos / Mission India audiences.** Those are institutional anchors with their own governance; Movemental aggregates and links, it does not redirect their relationships.
- **Adopt CPM/DMM jargon on his behalf.** His refusal of "people of peace," "T4T," and site-metrics language is a theological position; Movemental copy must respect it.
- **Publish unverified entities.** No "Ephesiology Coaching Network" (unverified); flag Michael Frost's 2026-book role as UNKNOWN until confirmed.
- **Flatten him into a single segment.** He is missiologist, NT-movement theologian, patristics retriever, archaeologist of Asia Minor, and leadership writer — not just "the Ephesiology guy."
