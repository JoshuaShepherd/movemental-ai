## In brief

Cooper's published writing, teaching, and podcasting cluster into **seven recurring themes**:

1. **Missiological theology of the Ephesian movement** (center of gravity) — *Ephesiology* (2020); *Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement* (2026).
2. **Disciple-making over church-planting** (the signature inversion) — *Make Disciples* (2023); "Is Church Planting a Biblical Mandate?" (Patheos/Jackson Wu).
3. **Early-church / Apostolic-Fathers retrieval** — *First Christian Voices* (2023); *Letters to Churches* (2025); *A Faithful Witness*.
4. **Contextualization & evangelical identity** — *Contextualization and Syncretism* (EMS 13); *When Evangelicals Sneeze* (2020).
5. **Material culture / archaeology of Asia Minor** (emerging frontier) — the 2026 volume + *Photographing the Movement of God* (116 photographs + 7 maps).
6. **Christoform / Pauline leadership** — *Letters to Churches*; *Lead Like Paul* (the Five C's).
7. **Justice, peace, and new religious movements** — *Social Injustice* I & II; *The Peaceable Christian*; *Contemporary Druidry* (2010).

**Signature intellectual move**: reframing missiology **AS** theology rather than as a subset of it — coining a category ("Ephesiology") and treating it as a discipline, refusing CPM/DMM operational jargon as a deliberate theological position, and grounding movement claims in material culture.

**Frameworks**: ten distinct, anchored in a coined, ownable category — Ephesiology, missiological theology, disciple-making-over-church-planting, healthy DNA, Christoform leadership, the Five C's, the contextualization/syncretism boundary, the TELOS. three pillars, and more.

**Strategic implication**: the content is scholarly-Movemental-ready in substance. The editorial issue is **discoverability and consolidation**, not voice quality — aggregation, indexing, and cross-linking, not rewriting. His scholarly register must be respected, never flattened into trade-devotional.
