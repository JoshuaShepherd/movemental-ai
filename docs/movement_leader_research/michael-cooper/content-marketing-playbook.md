## Strategic frame

Cooper is the inverse onboarding case from most movement leaders. He is not a thin-surface practitioner who needs a corpus built; he is an **extraordinarily prolific scholar-practitioner whose finished, award-winning corpus is scattered and undiscoverable, sitting on a paper-thin owned-audience layer.** The playbook's job is therefore **consolidation and audience capture**, not production. Four guiding principles:

1. **Own the "Ephesiology" category.** Cooper coined a field — "the study of the Ephesian movement" — and uniquely holds the Ephesus / Acts 19 movement-theology niche. The single most valuable strategic act is making Movemental the canonical, discoverable home of that category. Everything else hangs off it.
2. **Consolidate before amplifying.** His 17-title corpus is split across 6+ imprints with no unified hub; his 227-episode podcast converts almost no listeners to an owned relationship. Aggregation, indexing, and a real email list come before any new content. Amplifying a dispersed estate just compounds the dispersion.
3. **Respect the scholarly register.** His differentiation *is* exegetical-theological depth and material-culture grounding. No trade-devotional flattening, no CPM/DMM jargon imposed on his behalf. Convert and surface existing artifacts in his own voice before authoring new ones.
4. **Complement, never cannibalize.** He runs his own accredited LMS (TELOS. / Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch), a 501(c)(3), a podcast, and seminary partnerships (Kairos, Mission India, East-West). Movemental hosts the public-facing corpus and the owned-audience layer; the LMS keeps the cohorts and credentials.

## Positioning

**Own the Ephesiology category.** Cooper is the only person who can credibly hold "Ephesiology" — he coined it, defined it, and built the only vertically integrated estate around it (podcast → blog → LMS → Kairos degrees → East-West anchor). Movemental's positioning move is to make his profile the **canonical front door to the category**: the single place a stranger who hears the word "Ephesiology" lands, finds the pillar book, the framework explainer, the 227-episode podcast, the 17-title corpus, and the path into his Master Classes.

Sub-positioning to carry across surfaces:
- **The scholar-practitioner who theologizes "movement" itself** — practice first (Romania decade), theory second (TEDS PhD), then the framework, the institution, and now the archaeological proof.
- **"Making disciples is the mandate; church planting is a result."** His signature inversion is the clearest, most shareable thesis statement and should anchor the framework page and the lead magnet.
- **Material culture as evidence.** The 2026 Asia Minor archaeology turn (*Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement* + *Photographing the Movement of God*) is a differentiator no one else in the practitioner-movement space owns.

(Contextualization & evangelical identity, and justice/peace/NRM, remain in the corpus index but are secondary publishing pillars.)

## Owned-audience build — the core fix

This is the single highest-leverage work in the engagement. ~17 books and 227 podcast episodes have produced ~120–175 brand-account followers, ~40 Goodreads ratings, 12 Apple podcast ratings, and no newsletter of scale. The corpus, the LMS, and the institutional reach all exist; what is missing is the **owned relationship**. The conversion plan:

1. **Replace the bare signup form with a real ESP** on ephesiology.com and his Movemental profile.
2. **Convert the 227-episode podcast into a list.** Every episode page and show-notes block carries a newsletter CTA + the Ephesiology framework lead magnet. The "10-Minute Ephesiologist" segments become the recurring on-ramp.
3. **Convert the Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch funnel.** The free public teaching (hosted on Movemental) gates into the paid cohorts (kept on his LMS) — and captures email at the public layer Movemental owns.
4. **Convert the 17-book corpus.** Each book's hub entry offers a chapter excerpt or the framework explainer in exchange for email; *Ephesiology* (the bestseller + award winner) is the primary magnet.
5. **Lead magnet**: a designed **"Ephesiology in 20 Minutes"** PDF explainer of the framework — "the study of the Ephesian movement," the disciple-making-over-church-planting inversion, healthy DNA, and the seven-theme map — with a path into both the podcast and the Master Classes.
6. **Welcome sequence (5 emails)**: *(1)* Welcome + Ephesiology explainer PDF; *(2)* "Nowhere in the Bible is the church commanded to plant churches" (the inversion); *(3)* Healthy DNA & how movements multiply; *(4)* The 2026 archaeology turn — letting the stones speak; *(5)* What's next + the Master Classes path.
7. **Seed list**: podcast listeners, ephesiology.com signups, and — with permission — any East-West / Kairos / Mission India lists he is permitted to use.

**Targets**: 1,500 subscribers in 6 months; 4,000 by 12 months.

## What NOT to do

1. **Do not compete with his LMS.** TELOS. / Ephesiology Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch is his own accredited revenue and credential engine. Movemental hosts public-facing corpus and audience; the LMS keeps the cohorts and credentials. Complement, never cannibalize.
2. **Do not strip the scholarly register.** His value is exegetical-theological depth and material-culture grounding. No trade-devotional flattening.
3. **Do not reroute East-West / Kairos / Mission India audiences.** Those are institutional anchors with their own governance; Movemental aggregates and links, it does not redirect their relationships.
4. **Do not adopt CPM/DMM jargon on his behalf.** His refusal of "people of peace," "T4T," and site-metrics language is a theological position; Movemental copy must respect it.
5. **Do not publish unverified entities.** No "Ephesiology Coaching Network" (unverified); flag Michael Frost's role on the 2026 book as UNKNOWN until confirmed.
6. **Do not chase vanity social growth.** The brand accounts are small and inactive; the win is email capture and discoverability, not follower counts.
7. **Do not flatten him into "the Ephesiology guy."** He is missiologist, NT-movement theologian, patristics retriever, archaeologist of Asia Minor, and leadership writer.
