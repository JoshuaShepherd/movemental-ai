## Assessment

Cooper is **content-rich in short form but structurally dispersed.** He maintains two owned blogs, contributes guest posts, and has a real journal-article history — yet the writing is fragmented across three brand identities (Ephesiology / TELOS. / personal byline) and two platforms, with his single most-quoted essay living on a third party's blog.

- **No unified essay archive.** A reader cannot find "everything Cooper has written" from any one URL.
- **Brand drift at the URL level.** The Patheos blog slug is literally misspelled (`/blogs/ephesilogy/`), and the own-domain blog handle (`untiltheyallhearblog`) matches neither the site brand ("TELOS.") nor the flagship brand ("Ephesiology").
- **Academic articles are under-surfaced.** Three of four journal placements have UNKNOWN titles/years and appear nowhere on his own properties.

The fragmentation problem here is **the same as the books**: high output, weak indexing, no canonical surface, thin owned-audience capture.
