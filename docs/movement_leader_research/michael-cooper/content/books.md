## Self-published imprints (the dispersion problem)

Cooper runs at least two house imprints under the Ephesiology brand, alongside trade publishers (William Carey, Wipf & Stock) and academic presses (Sacred Roots / Samuel Morris, Sacred Tribes Press):

- **"Defense of the Faith from Ephesiology"** — apologetics / early-church titles (*A Faithful Witness*, *First Christian Voices*–adjacent material).
- **"Social Justice from Ephesiology"** — *Social Injustice* Vol. I & II, *The Peaceable Christian*.

The catalogue therefore spans **at least six imprints across 2010–2026**, with the self-published tier carrying the most metadata gaps (missing ISBN-13s, unconfirmed years). This is the central bibliographic finding: Cooper is prolific, but his output is **dispersed across imprints and weakly indexed**, so no single retail surface represents the whole body of work.

## Assessment

Cooper is a **prolific scholar-practitioner author** — the opposite profile from a leader with no book at all. The problem is not absence; it is **dispersion**:

- **23 works across at least six imprints**, with the most numerous (self-published) tier carrying the weakest metadata, no consistent ISBN coverage, and the thinnest distribution.
- One trade-published flagship (*Ephesiology*, 2020) carries the brand, the award, and the endorser slate — but the long tail of self-published titles is functionally invisible in retail discovery, indexed almost entirely on `ephesiology.com/library-2` rather than on a high-traffic surface.
- **~40 total Goodreads ratings against 23 works** confirms a thin owned-audience layer relative to output: he ships faster than his platform converts readers.
- No audiobook of the flagship, no single canonical "collected works" page off his own infrastructure, and missing identifiers on roughly a third of the catalogue.

**Onboarding implication**: do not treat Cooper as needing a first book. Treat him as needing **consolidation and re-indexing** — a single canonical bibliography surface, clean ISBN/metadata hygiene on the self-published tier, an *Ephesiology* audiobook, and a curated reading-order path that routes new readers from the flagship into the long tail.

## Recommended bibliography strategy (for Movemental onboarding)

1. **Build one canonical works page** that supersedes the scattered imprint listings — the single authoritative bibliography surface, newest-first, with covers, ISBNs, and buy links.
2. **Close the metadata gaps** — assign/recover ISBN-13s and confirm publication years for the six unconfirmed self-published titles so they are discoverable in library and retail catalogues.
3. **Commission the *Ephesiology* audiobook** — the flagship has an award and an endorser slate but no audio format; this is the highest-leverage single addition.
4. **Publish a curated reading order** (Ephesiology → Make Disciples → Lead Like Paul → Letters to Churches → First Christian Voices → Gods, Emperors, Philosophers) as the on-ramp from the flagship into the long tail.
5. **Consolidate the two house imprints** under one clearly branded publishing identity so the self-published tier reads as a coherent line rather than scattered one-offs.
