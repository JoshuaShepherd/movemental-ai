## Assessment

Cooper has **more codified course infrastructure than almost any peer** — a stackable certificate program, a university accreditation partnership, and a directed master's degree. The problem, consistent with the rest of his portfolio, is **discovery and consolidation, not absence**:

- **The Master Classes are real and ambitious** — four certificates, three delivery modes, laddering into five accredited degrees — but pricing, enrollment numbers, and a public "what you'll learn" surface are all UNKNOWN/gated. A prospective learner cannot easily evaluate or enter the funnel.
- **The MA in Missiology of Movements is institutionally significant** but lives on a separate seminary's site (Mission India, Nagpur), disconnected from the Master Classes brand and from ephesiology.com.
- **Teaching is dispersed across at least five institutions** (East West, Kairos, TIU, Torch Trinity, AGST Nepal) with no single "where Cooper teaches" surface.
- There is **no low-cost / free top-of-funnel course** that would route the podcast's audience and the books' readers into the paid Master Classes pipeline.

The fragmentation story for courses is **a strong product hidden behind weak packaging**: the educational depth is there; the on-ramp, the pricing transparency, and the cross-surface integration are not.

---

## Recommended course strategy (for Movemental onboarding)

1. **Build a single "Study with Michael Cooper" surface** that unifies the Master Classes, the four certificates, the five-degree ladder, and the Mission India MA into one navigable funnel with transparent pricing.
2. **Create a free / low-cost intro course** — "Introduction to Ephesiology" or "Reading the New Testament as a Missiological Theology" — as the top-of-funnel asset that converts podcast listeners and *Ephesiology* readers into Master Class prospects.
3. **Surface the value proposition** of the Kairos ladder (certificate credits → accredited MA/MDiv/DMin/PhD) prominently — this stackability is a genuine differentiator that is currently buried.
4. **Cross-link the Mission India MA** from ephesiology.com so the degree and the Master Classes read as one coherent learning pathway rather than two disconnected programs.
5. **Verify or retire the "coaching network"** language — either stand up a named coaching tier or remove the unverified reference to avoid funnel confusion.
