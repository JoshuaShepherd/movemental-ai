## Headline finding

**His problem is dispersion, not absence.** Cooper has a genuine owned hub, an accredited LMS, a 227-episode podcast, and ~17 books — but those assets are spread across **6+ publisher imprints, two brand domains, a misspelled-slug hosted blog, three retail profiles, and several podcast platforms**, sitting on top of a **thin owned-audience layer**. No single surface concentrates either the catalog or the audience, so the audience effect is sub-additive. **Overall digital presence score: 3.1/5** — strong content + commerce + owned hub; weak owned-audience capture.

## Publisher dispersion (the defining issue)

Cooper's corpus is split across at least six channels: **William Carey Publishing** (*Ephesiology*), **Wipf & Stock** (2026 *Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement* + companion), **Sacred Roots / Samuel Morris** (*First Christian Voices*), **Sacred Tribes Press** (*Contemporary Druidry*), and the self-managed **"Defense of the Faith from Ephesiology"** and **"Social Justice from Ephesiology"** imprints. There is no single discoverable surface that consolidates more than a fraction of the catalog. See [digital-presence/platforms.md](digital-presence/platforms.md) for the full table.

## What this means for onboarding

The owned hub, LMS, podcast, and degree ladder already exist — onboarding **consolidates and captures**, it does not build from zero. The bottleneck is audience capture (email + a personal creator identity + a single canonical corpus hub), not content creation.
