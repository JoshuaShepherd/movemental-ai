### Site structure

`ephesiology.com` is a genuine **content + commerce hub**, not a link-in-bio page. It surfaces:

- The **TELOS.** identity and three pillars (plant churches, care for the marginalized, defend the faith) — [ephesiology.com/welcome-to-telos](https://ephesiology.com/welcome-to-telos/)
- A dedicated **book page** for *Ephesiology* with endorsements — [ephesiology.com/ephesiology-the-book](https://ephesiology.com/ephesiology-the-book/)
- A **Library** cataloging the full ~17-title bibliography — [ephesiology.com/library-2](https://ephesiology.com/library-2/)
- The **podcast**, organized into thematic series ("Christ: The Foundation of Mission and Church," "Reimagining our Ecclesiology," "Faith and Politics")
- **Seminary@YourChurch / Master Classes** entry points into the LMS
- Newsletter signup + donation/partnership calls

**Assessment**: a real home with real content — the opposite of the link-in-bio problem. Its weakness is that it is one of *several* surfaces (LMS subdomain, Patheos column, Academia, retail pages) rather than a single consolidating front door, and its owned-audience capture (newsletter, social) is thin relative to the content depth.

## Domain Authority Summary

- **Owned domains**: `ephesiology.com` (content + TELOS. brand) and `masterclasses.ephesiology.com` (LMS + Kairos commerce) — both active, both substantive. This is a genuine two-domain owned estate, not a placeholder.
- **Institutional backing**: East-West Ministries International, Kairos University, Mission India Theological Seminary, and William Carey Publishing all provide strong contextual SEO and credibility.
- **Published works**: deep — ~17+ titles across 6+ imprints (a citation/SEO asset, but fragmented across many publisher domains).
- **Core SEO weaknesses**: (1) the misspelled Patheos slug `ephesilogy`; (2) no verified Google Scholar/ORCID to index the journal record; (3) the body of work is scattered across many self-imprints and brand pages rather than consolidated.
- **Core SEO opportunity**: `ephesiology.com` is already the natural consolidator. The move is to pull the Patheos column, the journal record, the interviews, and the full bibliography into one authoritative on-domain hub, and to build the owned-audience layer (newsletter, social) up to match the content depth — the opposite of the "build a hub" problem, this is a "concentrate what already exists" problem.
