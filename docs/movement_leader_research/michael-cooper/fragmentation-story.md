## The Story

Michael T. Cooper has spent **more than thirty years in missions** and built, almost single-handedly, a named field of study. He holds a **PhD in Intercultural Studies** from Trinity Evangelical Divinity School, planted churches for roughly **ten years in post-communist Romania**, directed the **School of Biblical and Religious Studies at Trinity International University** for about a decade, and has lectured at the London School of Economics, the University of Bordeaux, Loyola, and Baylor. In 2020 his book *Ephesiology: A Study of the Ephesian Movement* (William Carey Publishing) won the **Catalyst Services Reader's Choice Award** for the best book in missiology and gave the discipline a new word. He has since authored, co-authored, or edited **roughly seventeen titles**, hosts a **227-episode weekly podcast**, runs a cohort-based seminary program (**Ephesiology Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch**) in partnership with the accredited **Kairos University**, directs the **MA in Missiology of Movements** at Mission India Theological Seminary, and serves as **missiologist-in-residence at East West Ministries**, an agency working some fifty countries.

That is, by any measure, a *prolific* body of work. And that is exactly what makes Cooper's fragmentation different from the typical movement leader's. The usual story is a practitioner with deep influence and almost no published record. Cooper's is the inverse: **an enormous, coherent output that is scattered across so many imprints, platforms, and brand identities that almost none of its accumulated weight lands in any one place a stranger can find — and almost none of it converts into an audience he owns.**

Consider the asymmetry. Seventeen-plus books — but they are spread across at least six publishing channels: William Carey Publishing (*Ephesiology*), Wipf & Stock (the 2026 *Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement*), Sacred Roots / Samuel Morris (*First Christian Voices*), Sacred Tribes Press (*Contemporary Druidry*), and two self-managed imprints ("Defense of the Faith from Ephesiology" and "Social Justice from Ephesiology") under which much of the recent catalogue sits. Twenty-three works are listed on his Goodreads author page — carrying, in total, about **forty ratings**. A 227-episode podcast with a 4.8-star rating — from **twelve** raters. Brand social accounts (@ephesiology on X, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube) whose follower counts, where visible at all, sit in the low **hundreds**. A genuinely significant peer review in *Missiology*, the flagship journal of the Evangelical Missiological Society — and no unified place that gathers it alongside the book it reviews. The man has built a category of one ("Ephesiology"), and the category's own evidence is dispersed to the point of near-invisibility.

In short: Michael Cooper has produced one of the most complete corpora of any movement leader in this research set — a flagship framework, seventeen books, a weekly podcast, a seminary, journal articles, two hosted blogs, a recurring scholarly symposium — and **the discoverability and owned-audience value of that corpus is a fraction of what its volume should command.** The asymmetry is not between embodied influence and digital expression (as with most leaders); it is between *output* and *consolidation*.

## Relational Fragmentation

Cooper's **institutional reach is large and his owned relational layer is thin**. His audiences are gathered by institutions, not by him:

- The **East West Ministries** donor and missionary base (an agency with ~$33M annual revenue working ~50 countries) is a vast potential audience — owned by East West.
- The **Ephesiology Master Classes / Kairos** enrollees are on Kairos and TELOS. infrastructure.
- The **Mission India** MA students belong to that seminary.
- The **227-episode podcast** audience lives on Apple and Spotify feeds.
- The peer reviewers, conference audiences (LSE, Bordeaux, Loyola, Baylor), and the **Evangelical Missiological Society** / **Missio Nexus** networks know his work — but none of that recognition flows into a list Cooper can email.

Even his most valuable credential — being positioned by Alan Hirsch, Marvin Newell, and James Stamoolis as a contemporary heir to **Roland Allen** — is an endorsement-jacket fact, not an owned-audience asset.
