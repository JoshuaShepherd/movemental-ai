## In brief

Cooper's gap is the **inverse** of the typical pre-platform movement leader (and the precise inverse of Lucas Pulley, who had book-length thinking and no books). Cooper is **extraordinarily prolific** — 17+ books across 6+ imprints, 227 podcast episodes, his own accredited LMS, and seminary degree partnerships — yet sits on a **paper-thin owned-audience layer** and **severe informational dispersion**. The problem is **dispersion, not absence**; the problem is **not production**.

**Major strengths (LOW gap)**: books, the coined "Ephesiology" framework, the 227-episode podcast, the LMS / degree ladder, conference/academic speaking, endorsements received (13 for *Ephesiology*, incl. Hirsch).

**Major gaps (HIGH)**:
- **Owned content hub** — output dispersed across 6+ imprints + ephesiology.com; no single canonical, indexed, SEO-rich surface for all 17 titles.
- **Newsletter / owned list** — signup form only; no Substack; no cadence or list of scale.
- **Owned social audience** — ~120–175 brand-account followers; appears inactive.
- **Reader footprint** — ~40 Goodreads ratings for an award-winning bestseller.
- **Discoverability / SEO** — no Wikipedia article; no verified Google Scholar profile; misspelled Patheos slug.

**Medium gaps**: imprint consolidation; audiobook / alt formats (no audiobook of *Ephesiology*); broader press coverage.

**Informational fragmentation** is the defining gap: a 17-title corpus split across William Carey, Wipf & Stock, Sacred Roots, Sacred Tribes Press, and self/Ephesiology imprints, with no single discoverable surface. **Relational fragmentation** is the inverse pattern: enormous institutional trust without owned access — East-West's ~$33M base, Kairos, and Mission India route audiences through institutions Cooper does not own, and the 227-episode podcast converts to almost no owned list.

**Why this gap is an opportunity, not a risk**: (1) the content asset base is exceptional and finished — surface, don't commission; (2) the owned-layer gap is the easiest gap to close — aggregation and discoverability are exactly what Movemental is; (3) the network compounds (Hirsch, East-West, EMS / Missio Nexus, Motus Dei, GACX, possible Tampa Underground via Couchenour).

**Risks**: do not compete with his LMS; do not strip the scholarly register; respect East-West / Kairos / Mission India relationships; coordination cost is real; do not publish unverified entities.
