**Status**: Complete (for discoverable coverage)
**Last updated**: 2026-05-29
**Skill**: `/author-media-mentions`

---

Cooper's press footprint is **movement- and guild-internal**: missions-agency outlets, a theological seminary's own news blog, a lay apologetics site, and a hosted Patheos column. There is no surfaced mainstream-press (secular or general-Christian) coverage. This is consistent with a specialist missiologist whose audience is reached through the missions/missiology ecosystem rather than trade media.

### Notes on the strongest items

- **Missio Nexus interview (Jun 2020)** is the highest-credibility press item — Missio Nexus is the largest North American evangelical missions association, and its staff missiologist Marvin J. Newell also endorsed *Ephesiology*. This is the closest thing to a "trade" placement in Cooper's file.
- **The Laymen's Lounge (Nov 2021)** is the richest biographical interview surfaced — it is the primary public source for the "30+ years in missions / ~10 years church planting in Romania" narrative and the Catalyst award.
- **Kairos Partnership Spotlight (Jun 2022)** is an institutional feature tied to the Seminary@YourChurch / Master Classes degree partnership; it doubles as marketing for the LMS.
- **The Patheos column** is hosted (not owned) and carries a misspelled slug, which is itself an SEO/discoverability liability — see [digital-presence/platforms.md](../digital-presence/platforms.md).

## Media coverage gaps

Cooper has **not** (to the audit's knowledge) been covered in:

- *Christianity Today* / *CT Pastors*
- *Outreach Magazine*
- *The Gospel Coalition*
- *Religion News Service* or any mainstream secular press
- *Themelios* / EMQ / GCRJ (no review surfaced — see [media/reviews.md](reviews.md))

The absence of general-Christian and mainstream coverage is unsurprising for a specialist missiologist, but it is striking next to the output: a 17-book corpus, a 227-episode podcast, an accredited degree partnership, and an award have produced **no trade-press feature**. The coverage that exists is entirely inside the missions guild and his own institutional orbit.

## Assessment

- **Coverage is guild-internal and self-adjacent.** Missio Nexus and The Laymen's Lounge are the only two arms-length outlets; Kairos and Patheos are institutional/owned-adjacent.
- **Two-tier pattern.** A credible missions-association tier (Missio Nexus) sitting atop a thin, scattered guest-and-column tier — with nothing bridging into trade or mainstream press.
- **The Vaughn ("Jackson Wu") relationship is the most generative press asset** because it is sustained engagement, not a one-off mention, and it produced Cooper's most-quoted lines.

## Recommended press / media strategy (for Movemental onboarding)

1. **Pitch a *Christianity Today* or *Outreach* feature** around the contrarian, citable thesis ("Nowhere in the Bible is the church commanded to plant churches") tied to the 2026 archaeology volume *Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement* — a fresh, visual, news-pegged hook.
2. **Leverage the Hirsch endorsement** into a co-interview (Hirsch + Cooper) on movement ecclesiology for a missional-orbit outlet; converts a static blurb into live coverage.
3. **Fix the Patheos slug.** The misspelled `/blogs/ephesilogy/` is leaking discoverability; either correct it or 301-redirect and consolidate the column under the `ephesiology.com` brand.
4. **Repackage the Missio Nexus and Laymen's Lounge interviews** as canonical, on-site assets at `ephesiology.com` so the strongest existing coverage stops living only on third-party domains.
