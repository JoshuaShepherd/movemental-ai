Unlike practitioners with no published surface, Cooper has a substantial, multi-decade bibliography (~17+ titles) anchored by the 2020 flagship *Ephesiology: A Study of the Ephesian Movement* (William Carey Publishing). That book has accumulated a real, if modest, review and award footprint inside the missiology guild. This file catalogs every discoverable review.

## Assessment

- **The reception is real but guild-internal and low-volume.** One credentialed positive journal review (Priebe / *Missiology*), one unconfirmed journal review (Keene / *Global Missiology*), two substantive blog engagements (Farah, Vaughn), a single mixed Goodreads text review, and one award. That is a credible scholarly footprint — and a thin popular one.
- **The award and the Priebe review are the load-bearing assets.** Both should anchor any future jacket copy or Movemental hub.
- **The gaps are at the popular tier, not the academic tier.** No *Themelios* / EMQ / GCRJ review surfaced, and Amazon engagement is invisible. For a book that won a reader-survey award, the absence of a visible Amazon review wall is itself a dispersion symptom — the audience exists but is not concentrated on any one retail or review surface.
- **Recommended:** consolidate Priebe's pull-quote, the Catalyst award, the Hirsch endorsement, and Farah's "invaluable resource" line into a single review/praise band, and run a live re-check of *Themelios*/EMQ/GCRJ and Amazon before treating those as confirmed-absent.
