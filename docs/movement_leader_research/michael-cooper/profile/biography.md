## Narrative Biography

Michael T. Cooper is an American evangelical missiologist and missiological theologian best known for coining and building out **"Ephesiology"** — the study of the first-century church-planting movement centered in Ephesus — as a framework for understanding and catalyzing contemporary movements. He is a scholar *and* a field practitioner: more than thirty years in missions, roughly ten of them as a pioneer church planter in Romania after the fall of communism, alongside an academic résumé that runs from a PhD in Intercultural Studies at Trinity Evangelical Divinity School to guest lectures at the London School of Economics, the University of Bordeaux, Loyola University, and Baylor University.

His formation is unusually interdisciplinary. He holds a **BED in Environmental Design from Texas A&M** — an architecture-and-built-environment degree, not theology — followed by an **MA in Missions from Columbia International University** and the **TEDS doctorate** (focus on religious movements, minor in early Christian history). His early scholarship was ethnographic and comparative-religion in character: field research on contemporary Druidry, published as a 2008 *Missiology* article ("What I Learned about Christianity from the Druids") and a 2010 book (*Contemporary Druidry*), with further work in *Nova Religio* and *Pomegranate*. Only later did his work pivot decisively toward New Testament movement studies and, most recently, the archaeology and material culture of Asia Minor.

The institutional middle of his career sat at **Trinity International University**, where for about a decade (c. 2008–2018) he directed the School of Biblical and Religious Studies and taught undergraduate and graduate students. He also served as president/CEO of an international NGO and spent several years in international development. But the second half of his public life has been organized around a brand and an argument. In 2019 he launched **The Ephesiology Podcast** (now 227 episodes, co-hosted with Andrew Johnson); in 2020 William Carey Publishing released **_Ephesiology: A Study of the Ephesian Movement_**, which won that year's Catalyst Services Reader's Choice Award for best book in Missiology and became a William Carey bestseller. Around the book he built a small ecosystem: the **TELOS.** ministry (three pillars — plant churches, care for the marginalized, defend the faith), the **Ephesiology Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch** cohort program (in partnership with Kairos University), and a directed **MA in Missiology of Movements** at Mission India Theological Seminary.

His current institutional footprint centers on three roles held simultaneously: **missiologist-in-residence at East-West Ministries International**, **affiliate professor at Kairos University**, and **program director of the Mission India MA**. He is prolific — ~17 catalogued books (23 distinct works on Goodreads) across 2010–2026, solo and co-authored, with a 2026 archaeology volume (*Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement*, Wipf & Stock) and its visual companion still in the pipeline.

His personal biography, by contrast, is almost entirely **undocumented** — no public birth date, birthplace, or family information. He presents himself vocationally, not personally, and this profile honors that rather than inventing detail.

## Origin & Formation

Two pivots define Cooper's formation, and both are worth naming because they make his eventual framework legible.

The first is the **Environmental Design → missions** arc. Beginning in an architecture/built-environment discipline at Texas A&M and ending as a PhD missiologist is not a normal path. The design-trained eye does not disappear; it resurfaces decades later in his turn toward *material culture* — reading the Ephesian movement through inscriptions, architecture, and the photographic and cartographic record (his 2026 Wipf & Stock volume and its companion *Photographing the Movement of God*). Where many missiologists argue from text alone, Cooper increasingly argues from the built and excavated world.

The second is the **Druidry-research-to-Ephesian-movement** pivot. His doctoral and early-career identity was that of a *new-religious-movements scholar* — an ethnographer of contemporary Druidry, publishing in the comparative-religion and missiology journals. That methodological training (how does a movement start, what do its adherents believe, who leads it, how does it sustain or fracture) is precisely the lens he later turns on the *first-century* Ephesian movement. Ephesiology is, in a real sense, the NRM researcher applying his own analytic questions to the New Testament. The continuity is more striking than the change.

Underneath both is the **ten-year Romania church-planting decade** after the fall of communism. This is the field credibility that anchors the academic voice. Cooper does not write as a theorist who never planted; he writes as a pioneer planter who later went and got the PhD to explain what he had lived. That ordering — practice first, theory second — shapes the entire corpus.

## Career & Ministry Arc

The arc runs: (1) **field practitioner** — pioneer church planting in post-1989 Romania (~10 years) plus international development and an NGO presidency; (2) **academic** — the TEDS doctorate and roughly a decade directing the School of Biblical and Religious Studies at Trinity International University (c. 2008–2018), with early scholarship on Druidry and new religious movements; (3) **framework-builder** — the 2019 launch of The Ephesiology Podcast and the 2020 publication of *Ephesiology*, which crystallized his thesis and won a Reader's Choice Award; (4) **ecosystem-builder** — TELOS., the Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch program, and the Mission India MA, all organized around disseminating and operationalizing the Ephesiology framework; and (5) **material-culture deepening** — the 2026 Asia Minor archaeology volume, which grounds the movement claims in the excavated record.

The through-line: every stage feeds the next. The field decade supplies credibility; the NRM scholarship supplies method; the framework supplies a brand; the ecosystem supplies distribution; the archaeology supplies evidence. He is a rare figure who has closed the loop from practice to theory to institution to proof.

## Current Focus

- **Ephesiology as a teachable program**, not just a book — the Master Classes / Seminary@YourChurch cohorts (four certificate tracks laddering into five Kairos degrees) and the Mission India MA in Missiology of Movements.
- **Missiologist-in-residence at East-West Ministries International**, embedding the framework inside a large least-reached-focused church-planting agency.
- **The 227-episode Ephesiology Podcast** (co-hosted with Andrew Johnson) as the weekly dissemination engine.
- **The 2026 archaeology turn** — *Gods, Emperors, Philosophers, and a New Movement* and *Photographing the Movement of God* — moving the argument from text to material culture.
- A continuing **disciple-making-over-church-planting** corrective within the CPM/missional conversation (*Make Disciples*, 2023; the Patheos/Jackson Wu piece).
