## Calling Statement

### Synthesized Calling

Michael T. Cooper is called to **establish a missiological theology of movement** — to read the Bible as a sustained account of God's movement to the nations, to treat the first-century Ephesian church-planting movement as a discrete and normative object of study, and to recenter the contemporary movements conversation on disciple-multiplication and a "missiologically theocentric passion to declare the glory of God to the nations." His vocation is not to mobilize a method but to *ground* one: missiology first in theology, never in technique. He coins categories (Ephesiology, missiological theology, Christoform leadership), inverts received assumptions (church planting is a result, not the mandate), and equips the next generation of movement leaders through an accredited educational pipeline rooted in the New Testament text, the Apostolic Fathers, and increasingly the archaeology of Asia Minor. He is a scholar-practitioner — a pioneer church planter who went and got the PhD to explain what he had lived — who closes the loop from practice to theory to institution to material-culture proof.

### Core Calling Elements

**1. Missiological Theologian** — mission as the center of theology, not a subset; the Bible read as "a missiological theology."

**2. Category-Coiner / Definer** — establishes disciplines ("Ephesiology") and fences them; gives the field new, ownable language.

**3. Constructive Corrector** — inverts the church-planting-as-mandate assumption to recenter on disciple-making; corrects to recenter, not to demolish.

**4. Scholar-Practitioner Equipper** — builds an accredited certificate-to-degree pipeline (Master Classes → Kairos degrees; Mission India MA) that institutionalizes the framework.

**5. Patristic & Material-Culture Retriever** — recovers Christoform leadership from the Apostolic Fathers and grounds movement claims in the excavated record of Asia Minor.

## mDNA Alignment Profile

### Element 1: Jesus is Lord (Organizing Center)

**Alignment Strength**: High

**Evidence:** The theocentric center is explicit and load-bearing — mission is the "missiologically theocentric passion to declare the glory of God to the nations," and "disciples are not made unless they identify with the life of Christ." Christ-formation is the substance of discipleship, not activity.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong: every framework is tethered to a doxological frame; the closing move is consistently theocentric.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: more explicit practical content on how Lordship reshapes leadership culture day to day.

---

### Element 2: Disciple Making (Strategic Nerve)

**Alignment Strength**: Very High (the center of gravity)

**Evidence:** Disciple-making *is* his signature thesis. "Making disciples is the mandate while church planting is a result." *Make Disciples* (2023) maps the spiritual journey of the Christ-follower; "disciples multiply while volunteers keep busy" is the corpus's purest aphorism. Multiplication, not addition or church-count, is the measure.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Very Strong: a dedicated book, the most-quoted essay, and the through-line of the podcast.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: digital-context disciple-making; converting the framework into accessible formation pathways.

---

### Element 3: Missional-Incarnational Impulse (Living Among)

**Alignment Strength**: High

**Evidence:** Strong contextualization is a core theme — Paul and John "connected Jesus to the cultural narrative of Ephesus." His 30+ years across five continents and a decade in Romania embody incarnational presence; his contextualization/syncretism boundary work (EMS 13) polices faithful cultural connection. The connecting tie to the Hirsch/Frost missional-incarnational stream runs through the 2026 Frost collaboration (role UNCONFIRMED).

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong: contextualization is explicit and theologically guarded.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: translate the incarnational impulse for the Global South (oral/translated formats — none catalogued yet).

---

### Element 4: Apostolic Environment (APEST)

**Alignment Strength**: Medium-High

**Evidence:** Cooper does not use the APEST taxonomy directly (that is Hirsch's), but he occupies the *apostolic* function natively: the Ephesian movement is read through apostolic mission (Paul, John), and Christoform leadership is recovered from the *disciples of the apostles* (1 Clement, Ignatius, Polycarp). *Lead Like Paul* (the Five C's) is explicitly Pauline-apostolic leadership. The substance of an apostolic environment is present; the named five-fold vocabulary is not.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong on apostolic/pioneering and the recovery of early-church leadership.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: an explicit bridge between Christoform leadership and the APEST language of the broader ecosystem (a natural Hirsch × Cooper surface).

---

### Element 5: Organic Systems (Simple, Reproducible)

**Alignment Strength**: High

**Evidence:** "Healthy DNA" is precisely an organic-systems concept — replicable, transferable spiritual code that sustains (or, when degraded, fractures) movements. His fifth analytical question — *why did some defect into false teaching?* — is a systems-health diagnostic. Multiplication-over-addition is organic-systems logic.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong: DNA, multiplication, and the defection-diagnostic are systems-native.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: more explicit content on designing reproducible systems in contemporary contexts.

---

### Element 6: Liminality-Communitas (Growth Through Shared Hardship)

**Alignment Strength**: Medium-High (more developed than the typical leader)

**Evidence:** Unusually for this corpus, liminality-communitas is *substantively present* in the early-church/persecution material — *A Faithful Witness* (third-century church under persecution), *Crises and the Church* (how the church responds to crisis), and the Apostolic-Fathers retrieval all foreground formation under pressure. His own decade of pioneer church planting in post-communist Romania is lived liminality.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Stronger than median: persecution, crisis, and resilience are recurring themes.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: name the communitas dynamic explicitly and connect the patristic/persecution material to contemporary movement formation.

### Alignment Gaps
1. **Owned-audience layer** — the calling reaches large *institutional* audiences (East-West ~$33M base, Kairos, Mission India) but converts almost none to an owned relationship. The 227-episode podcast draws only 12 ratings. This is the central gap.
2. **Discoverability** — no canonical hub for a 17-title corpus; no Wikipedia article; no verified Scholar profile; misspelled Patheos slug.
3. **Format reach** — no *Ephesiology* audiobook; no translated/oral modules for the Global South audience his contextualization theme already serves.
4. **APEST/communitas naming** — substantively present but not connected to the ecosystem's shared vocabulary.

## Digital Calling Opportunity Map

**The defining move is consolidation, not creation.** Movemental's job is *not* to generate content for Cooper — he is among the most prolific leaders in the research set — but to **convert lent institutional reach into an owned relationship layer** and to surface a dispersed corpus.

1. **Unified "Ephesiology" corpus hub** — the single discoverable home the 17-title corpus has never had; the six imprints become source links, the hub becomes the front door.
2. **A real newsletter** (the "Movemental Theology" newsletter) — periodic, scholarly-but-accessible, seeded from the podcast and corpus; replaces the bare signup form. Target 1,500 subscribers in 6 months, 4,000 by 12 months.
3. **Audio essays / narrated corpus** — there is no *Ephesiology* audiobook; narrated flagship chapters and top podcast segments fill the obvious format gap.
4. **Translated / oral-format modules** — for India, Nepal, and Romania funnels.
5. **Short-form Asia-Minor archaeology series** — the visual companion (116 photographs + 7 maps) and "Let the Stones Speak" material are unusually well-suited to short-form video.
6. **Hirsch × Cooper cross-surface** — a movement-theology conversation cross-promoting both Movemental profiles and bridging Christoform leadership to APEST.
