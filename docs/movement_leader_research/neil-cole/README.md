## Executive Summary

Neil Cole is one of the half-dozen North American voices who functionally invented the modern simple/organic/missional church-multiplication conversation. He left a Grace Brethren senior-pastorate in Alta Loma, California in 1999, walked into the smoking section of a Long Beach coffeehouse called The Coffee Tavern, and started Awakening Chapel — the prototype that would seed *Church Multiplication Associates (CMA)*. Over the next two decades CMA's own promotional copy claimed "thousands of churches in fifty states and more than fifty nations" with planting rates as high as nearly four new congregations per week in 2003. He wrote *Cultivating a Life for God* (1999) — the manual for his **Life Transformation Group (LTG)** discipleship unit — followed by *Organic Church* (Jossey-Bass, 2005), the Leadership Network book that put "organic church" into the missional vocabulary alongside Hirsch and Frost's *Shaping of Things to Come*. *Church 3.0* (Jossey-Bass, 2010), *Organic Leadership* (Baker, 2009), *Search & Rescue* (Baker, 2008), *Church Transfusion* with Phil Helfer (Jossey-Bass, 2012), *Journeys to Significance* (Jossey-Bass, 2011), and *Primal Fire* (Tyndale Momentum, 2014) followed in rapid succession. He was a co-founder of Hirsch's **100 Movements** training stack in 2015 — the design team that produced the "six movement competencies."

**Then, around 2012–2013, he stopped.** He has spoken publicly about a self-described **13-year season of God-imposed silence** in the U.S. — no public speaking, no blogging, no social media. The Cole-Slaw blog goes dormant in June 2017 (its 250-post archive frozen since); his Jossey-Bass / Baker / Tyndale book trajectory ends with *Primal Fire* (2014) and *One Thing* (Thomas Nelson, 2015); from 2018 onward his books appear under his own self-publishing imprint **Starling Initiatives** (*Rising Tides* 2018, *VIRAL* 2021, *Pray* 2019/2022, *Journeys: Discovering God's Paths of Spiritual Formation* 2024, the 2025 reissue of *Primal Fire*). Starling is a smaller, quieter operation — a Facebook group, monthly Zoom trainings, an audiobook catalog Cole now narrates himself. CMAResources.org has lapsed and the domain has been hijacked by a Sydney-lottery prediction site, which is the strongest single visible signal that the CMA era is over.

**Re-emergence (2024–2025) is recent and tentative.** Three guest appearances surface in this audit: *Shifting Culture* Ep. 178 (April 2024) on *Journeys*; the reChurch Podcast Ep. 13–14 (Dec 2024 / Jan 2025) on *Organic Church* and broken church-leadership hierarchies; and KC Underground's *Upside Down Kingdom* (Aug 2025) hosted by Brian Johnson and Cory at Rob Wegner's KCU. The KCU appearance is significant for Movemental: it places Cole back inside the Underground / Hirsch / Wegner / Ferguson constellation as a **trusted-voice elder**, not a re-launching publishing brand.

This is a leader at an unusual inflection point. He has the largest published organic-church corpus of any North American author still living and active; an entire generation of practitioners (Wegner, Sanders, Pulley, Halter, Ford, Frost, Vanderstelt, Rhodes, Mancini, the Dales) was shaped by *Organic Church* and LTGs; he carries seminary credentials and a doctorate; he has a clear personal narrative arc (success → silence → re-emergence); and his **digital surface is a fragmentation case study** — orphaned blog, lapsed CMA domain, scattered books across five publishers, a self-published imprint without a real website, dormant YouTube playlists, social handles whose activity status is opaque. **He is the canonical "trusted voice" Movemental's ecosystem layer exists to serve** (see [docs/build/strategy/movement-leaders-as-ecosystem-layer.md](../../build/strategy/movement-leaders-as-ecosystem-layer.md)) — not an audience to convert, but a peer whose body of thought needs a coherent home.

His network is dense and very valuable. **Confirmed mutual endorsement / peer relationship with Alan Hirsch** (he endorses *Forgotten Ways* updated edition; Hirsch endorses *Organic Church*). **Co-founder of 100 Movements** alongside Hirsch, Dave Rhodes, Will Mancini, and Jessie Cruickshank (2015). **Co-author with Phil Helfer** (*Church Transfusion*) and **with Robert (Bob) E. Logan** (*Beyond Church Planting*, 2005) — Logan is the named mentor in his Christianity Today response (2010). **Contributor alongside Hirsch, Frost, Ferguson, Halter, McClung, the Dales** in Felicity Dale's *Black Swan Effect* (2014). **Endorser of Brian Sanders' *Underground Church*** (Zondervan, 2019). **Wolfgang Simson, Steve Addison, Curtis Sergeant, Jim Montgomery's DAWN team (Michael Steele), Frank Viola, Felicity & Tony Dale, Reggie McNeal, Leonard Sweet, Ed Stetzer** are all in his orbit. Of the master `network/network-map.md` Cole currently has only **one mapped edge** (mutual endorsement with Hirsch); this dossier surfaces ~20 additional candidate edges.

## Top 5 Opportunities

1. **Give the corpus a coherent home.** Across 17+ books, Cole's body of work spans five publishers, two self-published imprints, a dormant blog, an effectively-defunct CMA Resources site, and a quiet Starling Initiatives platform with no real authority. There is no single URL where a stranger can land and meet his thinking. **A curated `neilcole.com` or Movemental-hosted Cole Hub** — books-by-era, the LTG framework, the Greenhouse training arc, the silence narrative, the re-emergence interviews — would consolidate one of the most-cited bodies of thought in the missional canon. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort.)

2. **Tell the silence story.** The "13 years of silence" narrative (referenced on *Shifting Culture* and KCU's *Upside Down Kingdom*) is one of the most compelling evangelical-author arcs of the 2010s and almost no one has heard it. It is the natural lead asset — a book chapter, a long-form essay, a 60-minute documentary interview — that re-introduces Cole to the post-2015 generation of practitioners (Wegner, Pulley, Brian Johnson, the KCU/Tampa Underground network) who built on top of his 2005–2014 work without ever working with him directly. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort.)

3. **Repackage LTG and Greenhouse for a 2026 cohort audience.** The Life Transformation Group is a lightweight discipleship technology (2–3 people, weekly, Scripture + confession + prayer) that has aged extraordinarily well; it is functionally identical to what Disciple Making Movements (DMM), Curtis Sergeant's "The Only One," and microchurch leaders are doing today, but pre-dates them by a decade. Same with Organic Church Planters' Greenhouse (Cole + Paul Kaak). Both deserve a modern public-facing cohort, not the legacy CMA Resources PDFs and DVDs. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM-HIGH effort.)

4. **Re-edge the master network graph.** Cole currently has 1 mapped edge (Hirsch). This dossier surfaces ~20 high-confidence candidates: 100 Movements co-founders (Hirsch, Rhodes, Mancini, Cruickshank), CMA leadership team (Helfer, Kaak, Waken, Robert Logan, Ross Rohde, Tom Johnston, Mike Perkinson), simple-church peers (Frank Viola, Felicity & Tony Dale, Wolfgang Simson, Floyd McClung), endorsement ties (Brian Sanders, Hirsch, Stetzer, Sweet, Reggie McNeal), DMM bridge (Steve Addison, Curtis Sergeant, Jim Montgomery's DAWN team via Michael Steele), and the contemporary Underground/KCU bridge (Wegner, Brian Johnson). (HIGH priority, LOW effort — see proposed edges in summary.)

5. **Position Cole as the "patriarch elder" trusted voice — not a refresh-the-platform play.** Cole's own theology of "obscurity" and his decade of imposed silence make a "build his audience back to 100K" framing dissonant. A Movemental fit treats him the way Pulley's Movemental fit explicitly treats Pulley: **as an apostolic peer in the scenius**, not a platform to scale. Onboard him as a panel voice, podcast guest, and standing column — not as a featured solo author with a book launch funnel. (MEDIUM priority, LOW effort.)

## Movemental Recommendation

**Fit Score**: 8.4 / 10
**Recommendation**: **Onboard — high-fit elder voice; treat as ecosystem-layer trusted voice, not as an implementation audience or a platform-build target.**

Three things converge:

1. **Doctrinal alignment.** Cole's "DNA" (Divine Truth / Nurturing Relationships / Apostolic Mission), his "if we cannot multiply disciples we will never multiply leaders, churches, or movements" sequencing, his explicit decentralized ecclesiology, his APEST treatment in *Primal Fire*, and his *Organic Leadership* anti-hierarchy posture sit directly on Movemental's missional-incarnational thesis. There is no theological work to do — he already wrote the books Movemental's audience needs.

2. **Network density.** He bridges five clusters: the Hirsch/100 Movements/MLC core (Cluster 1); the Forge/missional orbit (via mutual endorsements with Frost, Hirsch, Halter); the simple/house-church axis (Frank Viola, the Dales, Wolfgang Simson, House2House); the DMM/CPM stream (Steve Addison, Curtis Sergeant, the IMB lineage that adapted LTG into CHAT); and the contemporary Underground/microchurch generation (Wegner, Brian Johnson, Lucas Pulley via Sanders' endorsement chain). Few authors in the master TAM list bridge that many clusters.

3. **Fragmentation case study.** His public-facing surface is precisely the pattern Movemental's ecosystem layer is built to serve — a deep corpus with no coherent home, a re-emergence moment with no platform, and an explicit personal theology of "obscurity" that prevents him from solving this himself. (See `fragmentation-story.md`.)

**Strategic caveat.** Cole has spent 13 years deliberately stepping out of the platform-and-influence economy. Any onboarding has to be aware of that. Frame as *"we'll carry your work for the next generation"* rather than *"we'll grow your audience."* The "patriarch elder" framing — same lane as Hirsch, Sweet, Robert Logan — is correct here, not the "trending author" framing.
