- **Multiplication-first**: Movemental's whole orientation toward decentralized scale is what Cole has been writing about since 1999.
- **APEST / five-fold**: *Primal Fire* is fundamentally compatible with Movemental's Hirsch-anchored APEST treatment.
- **Trusted voice / ecosystem layer**: Movemental's canonical doctrine treats movement leaders as ecosystem peers, not as audience to convert. Cole is a **paradigm case** of an ecosystem peer who built movement infrastructure two decades before Movemental existed.
- **Decentralized leadership**: *Organic Leadership* (2009) and *Church Transfusion* (2012) are explicit anti-hierarchy texts.
- **Egalitarian-friendly**: *Black Swan Effect* contribution shows the public posture.

There is no doctrinal gap to bridge. There is no copy-rewriting work. There is no "we need to update his frameworks for 2026 audiences" — they already work in 2026.

## Why the Score Isn't 10 / 10

1. **Operational scale is small.** Cole is not Hirsch in terms of contemporary platform reach. Movemental positioning has to be honest about that.
2. **The silence-period theology limits some Movemental moves.** A "build the platform" framing is wrong for Cole. A "patriarch elder feature" framing is right.
3. **CMA's measured impact is hard to verify.** The "thousands of churches in 50+ nations" claim is the organization's self-report; independent verification is not available from public sources.
4. **The 9Marks Reformed critique is real.** Movemental copy that engages Cole has to be theologically aware of this critique to be credible across the broader evangelical audience.
5. **Age and trajectory.** Cole is 66 in 2026. The window for major new book authorship is finite. Movemental should plan around 5-year, not 25-year, horizons.

## What Movemental Should Avoid

- **Don't run a platform-build campaign.** It will land badly with Cole's audience and dissonantly with his theology.
- **Don't market him as an "emerging voice" or "next-generation leader."** He is 66 and has been doing this since 1999.
- **Don't dismiss the 9Marks critique.** Acknowledge it; Movemental copy is stronger when it engages the counter-position.
- **Don't lead with the "thousands of churches in 50 nations" PR framing.** The 2024–2025 voice has self-corrected away from this.
- **Don't ask him to retell the early Awakening Chapel story to start every podcast appearance.** He has already told it 200 times. The fresh asset is the silence story, not the founding story.
- **Don't expect a high-cadence content output.** A quarterly newsletter, a monthly podcast appearance, an annual book is a realistic operating cadence — and is appropriate.
