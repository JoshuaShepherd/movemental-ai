# Audience Analysis: Neil Cole

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-04-29
**Confidence**: HIGH on the structural composition of his audience; LOW on precise volumetrics (no email list, no central platform, no public analytics).

---

## Audience Segments

Cole's audience splits into roughly five overlapping segments. Each segment relates to him through a different surface, has different intensity of engagement, and would respond to different Movemental on-ramps.

### 1. The "Organic Church" missional generation (2005–2014 trade-book readers)

- **Profile**: Pastors, church planters, missional / emerging-church-era practitioners who bought *Organic Church*, *Church 3.0*, and *Primal Fire* during the trade-publishing run.
- **Approximate count**: 50,000–150,000 lifetime book purchasers across major titles (extrapolating from cumulative Goodreads ratings of 2,062 — typical Goodreads-to-purchase ratio is 1:50 to 1:100).
- **Engagement intensity**: Variable; many will have read 1–2 books and not engaged further. A core ~5,000–10,000 are repeat-buyers across multiple Cole titles.
- **Where they live online**: Goodreads, Amazon, Audible, library systems; some on legacy missional blogs and Christianity Today reader bases.
- **Movemental on-ramp**: A Cole archive / hub + curated newsletter; the silence-story essay as lead magnet.

### 2. The CMA / Greenhouse / LTG practitioners (1999–2018)

- **Profile**: Pastors, lay leaders, church planters, and missionaries who were trained at a Greenhouse cohort, learned the LTG framework, or used CMA Resources curriculum.
- **Approximate count**: Cole's promotional copy claims "thousands of churches in 50 states and 50+ nations." Practitioners trained: low thousands; lay-level LTG users: tens of thousands.
- **Engagement intensity**: Deep — these are people who have run his discipleship technology in their own ministries.
- **Where they live online**: Largely offline / inside their own churches; some on the @CMAResources Facebook page, the dormant Cole-Slaw blog, and the legacy CMA Resources contact lists.
- **Movemental on-ramp**: A modernized LTG kit; a Greenhouse cohort relaunch; a "where are they now" alumni network feature.

### 3. The DMM / CPM / IMB ecosystem (global, 2005–present)

- **Profile**: Disciple-Making Movement and Church-Planting Movement practitioners — IMB strategists, Saddleback PEACE Plan operators, missionaries in CPM-receptive contexts.
- **Approximate count**: Tens of thousands globally — much larger than the North American direct readership; significantly under-counted in any English-language analytics.
- **Engagement intensity**: Functionally permanent — Cole's LTG framework is operationalized as the IMB's CHAT and used in field training.
- **Where they live online**: Steve Addison's Movements Lab, Curtis Sergeant's Metacamp, Saddleback PEACE Plan training, IMB internal training portals, *Mission Frontiers* magazine.
- **Movemental on-ramp**: A Cole-anchored DMM/CPM/microchurch elder-voice video series; partnership with Steve Addison and Curtis Sergeant.

### 4. The simple / house / underground / microchurch generation (2015–present)

- **Profile**: Brian Sanders' Underground Network (Tampa); Rob Wegner's KC Underground; Frank Viola's house-church readers; Felicity & Tony Dale's House2House network; the broader contemporary microchurch movement (Lucas Pulley, Tomy Wilkerson, Stacy Gaskins, Joel Repic, Brian Johnson).
- **Approximate count**: Low thousands of practitioners; tens of thousands of adjacent listeners through the Underground / KCU / Tampa Underground podcast ecosystems.
- **Engagement intensity**: Cole is treated as the **patriarch elder** of this generation. KCU's 2025 *Upside Down Kingdom* episode is the clearest signal — they don't introduce Cole; they receive him.
- **Where they live online**: Tampa Underground, KC Underground, Underground Network sites; Microchurches Podcast; Exponential Microchurch Next; the wider 100Movements / Movement Leaders Collective orbit.
- **Movemental on-ramp**: A Cole + Wegner + Sanders elder-and-inheritors podcast series; a 100 Movements 10-year reunion.

### 5. The 100 Movements / Hirsch / MLC / 5Q ecosystem

- **Profile**: Alan Hirsch's audience (Forge / 100 Movements / MLC / 5Q / Future Travelers); Will Mancini's Auxano audience; Dave Rhodes' coaching-and-training audience; Jessie Cruickshank's neuroscience-of-leadership audience.
- **Approximate count**: 50,000–200,000 missional readers and trainees globally.
- **Engagement intensity**: Cole is treated as a peer of Hirsch (mutual endorsement, co-design of 100 Movements). His direct visibility inside this audience has thinned during the silence period but the structural relationship remains intact.
- **Movemental on-ramp**: A Cole + Hirsch dual-author panel; positioning Cole alongside Hirsch as paired patriarch voices of the missional generation.

### 6. The Charis Fellowship (Grace Brethren) home tribe

- **Profile**: Cole's denominational home; Grace Theological Seminary alumni; Grace Brethren / Charis Fellowship pastors.
- **Approximate count**: Small (the denomination is small) but high-fidelity.
- **Engagement intensity**: GraceConnect has tracked Cole's career milestones with consistent attention since the early 2000s.
- **Movemental on-ramp**: A Charis Fellowship × Movemental partnership feature; recovery of the early-career biographical narrative.

### 7. The DTS / Kairos Pacific / academic-evangelical audience

- **Profile**: Seminary students, faculty, and graduates engaging Cole's work in coursework or lectureships.
- **Approximate count**: Small but institutionally placed.
- **Movemental on-ramp**: A Cole faculty profile; a Movemental Fellowship at Kairos Pacific or a DTS-style three-session lectureship.

## Demographic Inference

Without access to first-party analytics, demographic inference is necessarily speculative. Working assumptions based on the segment composition above:

- **Age**: Cole's primary audience has aged with him. The 2005–2014 trade-book readers are now 50–75; the 2015+ microchurch generation is 30–55. There is a clear "missing middle" generation (2016–2022) that has neither been recruited nor retained.
- **Geography**: Heavily North American (US Southern California / Sun Belt / Bible Belt) for the trade-book audience; significantly more globally distributed for the DMM/CPM/IMB segment.
- **Gender**: Likely majority male given the typical evangelical pastor / church-planter demographic, but Cole's *Black Swan Effect* contribution and his egalitarian-friendly posture have probably skewed his audience more egalitarian than the median evangelical-pastor catalog.
- **Theological**: Broadly conservative-evangelical; Pentecostal-charismatic friendly (per Charisma Magazine reach); Reformed-skeptical (9Marks pushback); ecumenical-leaning on practical ecclesiology.

## Volumetric Ceiling

A reasonable estimate for the total addressable Cole audience (people who would recognize his name and have engaged with his work or framework directly or indirectly):

- **Strong-engagement core**: 5,000–15,000 (CMA / Greenhouse / LTG practitioners + repeat-buyer core)
- **Medium-engagement**: 50,000–200,000 (one-time book buyers, podcast listeners, conference attenders, downstream framework adopters via DMM / Underground / KCU / 100M)
- **Weak-engagement / aware-of-name**: 500,000+ (anyone who has read about organic church in the missional canon since 2005)

If Movemental successfully consolidated Cole's surface, **a directly subscribed audience of 5,000–25,000 within 12 months is a defensible target.** That is small relative to a contemporary platform-author benchmark; it is large relative to what Cole's current Starling Initiatives footprint shows; and it is precisely the right size for the "trusted-voice elder, episodic feature" frame that fits him.

## Audience Pain Points (from podcast and interview content)

Themes Cole's listeners and readers consistently surface:

1. **Disillusionment with attractional / megachurch / institutional models**.
2. **Hunger for reproducible, simple, low-overhead discipleship tools** (where LTG fits).
3. **Frustration with hierarchical church leadership** (where *Organic Leadership* and *Primal Fire* fit).
4. **Desire to understand how movements actually scale** without becoming dependent on celebrity pastors or programmed church-growth (where *Organic Church*, *Church Transfusion*, and 100 Movements competencies fit).
5. **Burnout** — a substantial proportion of Cole's contemporary listeners are post-burnout, post-disillusionment, post-deconstruction practitioners; the silence story is unusually well-suited to that audience.

## Implications for Movemental

1. Cole's audience is **structurally aligned with Movemental's canonical doctrine** — the "trusted-voice / ecosystem-layer" framing is correct here.
2. The strongest single audience-bridging move is **a Cole + Wegner + Sanders multi-voice elder-and-inheritors series**, which would unify segments 4, 5, and 6 above.
3. The **silence story** is the lead asset because it speaks directly to the burnout pain-point that runs across most audience segments.
4. **Do not over-target the strong-engagement core.** They are already reading him. Movemental's value-add is making him newly visible to the medium- and weak-engagement segments who built on top of his work without ever having a guided introduction to it.
