# Competitive Landscape: Neil Cole

**Status**: Complete
**Last updated**: 2026-04-29
**Confidence**: HIGH on the structural map; MEDIUM on contemporary positioning (some peers have shifted lanes since 2014–2018).

---

## What "Competitive" Means Here

Cole does not have direct commercial competitors in the way a SaaS company does. The "competitive landscape" question for a missional ecosystem-layer voice is:

- Who else is occupying adjacent lanes in the same audience's mind?
- Who else is doing similar work with similar frameworks?
- Where does Cole's distinctive contribution still hold its edge, and where has it been overtaken?

---

## Adjacent Lane 1 — Missional / mDNA / 5Q (Hirsch axis)

**Lane occupants**: Alan Hirsch, Michael Frost, Tim Catchim, Deb Hirsch, Rich Robinson, Lance Ford, Rob Wegner, JR Woodward, Brad Brisco, Hugh Halter, Cam Roxburgh, Rowland Smith.

**Cole's position**: Peer of Hirsch; co-design relationship via 100 Movements. Distinct from this lane in that Cole is **less academic / system-theoretical** and **more practitioner / agricultural-metaphorical**.

**Where Cole holds his edge**:
- The LTG framework (operational; reproducible; pre-dates *Forgotten Ways*).
- The lifeguard-and-coffeehouse origin story (specific; geographic; visceral).
- The 13-year silence narrative (no other figure in this lane has it).

**Where the lane has overtaken him**:
- Social-media reach (Hirsch / Frost / Halter all have larger live platforms).
- Conference cadence (Forge / Saturate / Sentralized / Verge are running events at higher cadence).
- Contemporary trade-house book output (Hirsch published *Reframation* in 2019; Cole's last trade book is 2015 *One Thing*).

**Strategic implication for Movemental**: Treat Cole as **patriarch elder peer** of Hirsch, not as a downstream voice. The 100 Movements co-design is the strongest single piece of evidence for that framing.

## Adjacent Lane 2 — Simple / House / Underground / Microchurch

**Lane occupants**: Frank Viola, Felicity Dale, Tony Dale, Brian Sanders, Lucas Pulley, Joel Repic, Tomy Wilkerson, Stacy Gaskins, Rob Wegner, Brian Johnson, Larry Kreider.

**Cole's position**: Foundational predecessor; one of the most-cited voices when this lane describes its own lineage. Mutual contributor with the Dales and Sanders. The 2025 KCU *Upside Down Kingdom* episode formalizes Cole's elder relationship to the contemporary microchurch generation (Wegner, Pulley, Sanders' lineage).

**Where Cole holds his edge**:
- He is older and more credentialed than most lane occupants.
- His LTG framework is more reproducible than the Dales' "house church" or Sanders' "microchurch" frame.
- His writing depth (17 books) exceeds anyone else in the lane.
- His silence-and-obscurity theology speaks directly to lane-wide post-burnout themes.

**Where the lane has overtaken him**:
- Operational scale: Sanders' Underground Network has a more visible city-level ecosystem than CMA does as of 2026.
- Contemporary platform: Wegner's KCU and Sanders' Tampa Underground have more active podcasts, training programs, and publishing footprints than Starling Initiatives.
- Generational currency: the active practitioners under 45 in this lane look to Wegner / Sanders / Pulley first; Cole arrives as elder reference second.

**Strategic implication for Movemental**: The Cole + Wegner + Sanders elder-and-inheritors triad is the **single highest-leverage Movemental positioning move** for this lane.

## Adjacent Lane 3 — Disciple-Making / CPM / DMM

**Lane occupants**: Steve Addison, Curtis Sergeant, David Watson, Paul Watson, Jerry Trousdale, Stan Parks, Bobby Harrington, Bill Hull, Greg Ogden, Robert Coleman.

**Cole's position**: Foundational practitioner; bridge from organic-church to DMM/CPM via the LTG → CHAT adaptation at IMB. Cited across Addison's books and Sergeant's *The Only One*.

**Where Cole holds his edge**:
- LTG is operationalized in IMB's CHAT — the structural debt is undeniable.
- *Cultivating a Life for God* (1999) pre-dates most DMM literature.
- *Search & Rescue* and *Ordinary Hero* are accessible disciple-making books that bridge organic-church and DMM languages.

**Where the lane has overtaken him**:
- DMM literature has matured into a more rigorous CPM/DMM-specific vocabulary (Watson brothers' *Contagious Disciple Making*; Trousdale's *Miraculous Movements*).
- Field-data reporting from DMM contexts (especially Africa, India, China) is much more developed than Cole's CMA data.
- Discipleship.org (Bobby Harrington) has built a more contemporary disciple-making conference platform.

**Strategic implication for Movemental**: A Cole + Sergeant + Addison panel would be the structural equivalent of the 100 Movements design-team reunion for the DMM lane. Cole's role is the **organic-church bridge into DMM** — which is precisely what makes him valuable to a DMM-audience that has often described disciple-making in non-ecclesial terms.

## Adjacent Lane 4 — Apostolic / 5-Fold / APEST

**Lane occupants**: Alan Hirsch (*5Q*, *Permanent Revolution*), Tim Catchim, Jessie Cruickshank, JR Woodward, Mike Breen (3dM, *Building a Discipling Culture*), Wolfgang Simson.

**Cole's position**: *Primal Fire* (2014) is his APEST book; it is structurally compatible with Hirsch's *5Q* and frequently treated as paired reading.

**Where Cole holds his edge**:
- The five-author co-thinker construct (*Primal Fire*) is unique in the lane.
- Cole's apostolic-chair voice is irenic and pastoral rather than polemical.
- *Primal Fire* has the highest Goodreads rating (4.21) of any of his books.

**Where the lane has overtaken him**:
- Hirsch's *5Q* has built a robust assessment infrastructure (the 5Q assessment); Cole has no equivalent diagnostic instrument.
- Mike Breen's 3dM has built a more developed "Learning Communities" infrastructure around discipleship-and-apostolic culture.

**Strategic implication for Movemental**: Pair Cole's *Primal Fire* with Hirsch's *5Q* for any Movemental APEST programming — they are genuinely complementary rather than redundant.

## Adjacent Lane 5 — Spiritual Formation / Interior Life

**Lane occupants**: Henri Nouwen (deceased), Eugene Peterson (deceased), Ruth Haley Barton, Pete Scazzero, John Mark Comer, Tyler Staton, Mindy Caliguire, James Bryan Smith.

**Cole's position**: Adjacent via the **post-silence Starling Initiatives books** (*Pray*, *Journeys*, *VIRAL*). His theology of obscurity sits within this stream's vocabulary even though his vocational identity is church-multiplication, not formation.

**Where Cole holds his edge**:
- The 13-year silence is a singular embodied story.
- Combining a multiplication-coach's identity with a contemplative formation voice is rare.

**Where the lane has overtaken him**:
- Comer / Scazzero / Barton have far larger contemporary platforms in the formation lane.
- Cole's formation books (*Pray*, *Journeys*) have minimal trade-house distribution; they are Starling self-published and have low Goodreads counts.

**Strategic implication for Movemental**: Position Cole's formation work as the **practitioner-elder voice** within the formation conversation, not as a competitor to Comer / Scazzero / Barton. The bridge is: *"a man who built a multiplication movement and then was told to be silent for 13 years has something to say about formation that the lifelong formation teachers do not."*

## Adjacent Lane 6 — Critical / Reformed Ecclesiology

**Lane occupants**: Mark Dever, Jonathan Leeman, 9Marks, Kevin DeYoung, Tim Challies.

**Cole's position**: Direct critical target, especially from 9Marks. Three published critical reviews (*Organic Church*, *Church 3.0*, *Church Transfusion*).

**Where Cole holds his edge**:
- Cole's framework is structurally simpler and more reproducible than Reformed ecclesiology's polity-rich tradition.
- The 25-year track record is harder to dismiss than Reformed critics' thinner church-planting evidence.

**Where this lane sits in opposition**:
- Reformed ecclesiology continues to view Cole's framework as theologically thin.
- The 9Marks critique is not a competitor in audience terms but a counter-positioning in theological terms.

**Strategic implication for Movemental**: Acknowledge the 9Marks critique candidly in any Cole content. Movemental copy that pretends the critique does not exist will read as superficial; copy that engages it makes Cole look stronger.

## Key Takeaway

Cole does not compete with these voices. He is **upstream** of most of them:

- *Organic Church* (2005) pre-dates *Forgotten Ways* (2006).
- LTG (1999) pre-dates IMB CHAT.
- Greenhouse pre-dates Exponential Microchurch Next.
- *Primal Fire* (2014) sits alongside *5Q* (2017) as paired APEST reading.
- The 100 Movements design team (2015) makes him a co-architect, not a downstream consumer.

Movemental's positioning should treat Cole accordingly — as one of the founding voices of the contemporary missional / multiplication / apostolic conversation, in the same generational tier as Hirsch and Frost, distinct from but compatible with the disciple-making and microchurch streams.
