### Layer 3 — The Mature Voice (post-silence, 2018–present)

The Starling Initiatives publication series narrows the focus from movement-building to interior-listening:

- *Rising Tides* (2018) — navigating exponential change.
- *Pray* (2019/2022) — connecting with God in all of life.
- *VIRAL* (2021) — hearing God's voice in pandemic disruption.
- *Journeys* (2024) — discovering God's paths of spiritual formation; lessons from 13 years of silence.
- *Primal Fire* (2025 reissue) — bringing the APEST book back under his own imprint.

This third layer is **distinct in tone** from the first two and reflects a man who has become something other than the rapid-multiplication CMA executive director of 2008. The contemporary audience that picks up Cole today is most likely to enter through this layer first, and would be poorly served by a "buy *Organic Church* first" recommendation strategy.

## Themes That Have Aged Well (worth surfacing for 2026 audiences)

1. **The "lower the bar of how church is done, raise the bar of discipleship"** framing. This sentence is 16 years old and still cuts.
2. **The Life Transformation Group**. Two or three people, weekly, Scripture + accountability + prayer for non-Christians. It works as well in 2026 as it did in 1999, and is structurally identical to what contemporary microchurch and DMM practitioners are recommending.
3. **The DNA framework**. Lightweight; memorable; reproducible.
4. **Theology of obscurity**. The single most-resonant theme for the post-burnout / post-deconstruction generation.
5. **The KCU + Cole bridge**. The 2025 *Upside Down Kingdom* episode is doing real generational repair-work and Cole's contribution to it is fresh.

## Themes That Have Aged Less Well (handle with care)

1. **CMA's "thousands of churches in 50+ nations"** statistical framing reads as 2008-era megachurch-style PR-by-numbers; Cole's 2024–2025 voice has self-corrected away from this; Movemental copy should as well.
2. **The "Constantine was the fall, organic is the recovery"** historical sweep. The 9Marks critique on this point is fair.
3. **Some of the *Church 3.0* polemic against the institutional church**. The 2024–2025 voice is gentler and more inviting; lifting the older polemic into contemporary copy would mis-fit the subject.
4. **The 2010 organic-vs-attractional dichotomy**. *Church Transfusion* (2012) already softened this; Cole now consistently positions as integrative rather than oppositional.

## Implications for Movemental

1. **The right entry book for new readers in 2026 is *Primal Fire* or *Organic Leadership*, not *Organic Church*.** *Organic Church* is the historically important book; *Primal Fire* and *Organic Leadership* are the books that age best.
2. **The right secondary entry is *Journeys* (2024)** because it integrates the silence narrative with the Pauline-leadership-development frame.
3. **The most reproducible single asset is the LTG card.** Modernized, mobile-friendly, cross-platform, with a 5-minute video walkthrough by Cole — it is plug-and-play discipleship technology and would be a near-universal recruiting asset.
4. **The strongest editorial framing is "patriarch elder voice" not "next-generation thought leader."** Cole's contemporary voice is wisdom-toned, not platform-toned.
