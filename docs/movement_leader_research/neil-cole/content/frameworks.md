# Frameworks: Neil Cole

The signature frameworks, operating principles, and conceptual models that recur across Neil Cole's corpus. Each is grounded in his published work; see `profile/theology.md` and `content/books.md` for sources.

### Life Transformation Group (LTG)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 1999 (*Cultivating a Life for God: Multiplying Disciples Through Life Transformation Groups*, ChurchSmart) |
| Components | Two or three people, same gender; weekly rhythm of Scripture reading, mutual confession of sin, and prayer for the lost |
| Function | The smallest reproducible discipleship cell — the irreducible unit that already contains the whole DNA of the church |
| Reception | Adapted by the International Mission Board into a tool called CHAT |
| Source | [Cultivating a Life for God](https://starlinginitiatives.com/about-us/) |

The Life Transformation Group is Cole's foundational discipleship technology: a two-or-three-person, same-gender weekly unit of Scripture, confession, and prayer that makes disciple-making simple enough that anyone can reproduce it.

### DNA — Divine Truth, Nurturing Relationships, Apostolic Mission

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2005 (*Organic Church: Growing Faith Where Life Happens*, Jossey-Bass) |
| Components | **D**ivine Truth (faith) · **N**urturing Relationships (love) · **A**postolic Mission (hope) |
| Rule | All three must be present in every cell of the body; they should not be supplanted, supplemented, or separated |
| Parallel | Structurally parallel to Alan Hirsch's mDNA in *The Forgotten Ways* (mutually acknowledged) |
| Source | [Organic Church](https://www.frankviola.org/2010/09/07/missional-organic-church-an-interview-with-neil-cole-frank-viola/) |

DNA is Cole's three-strand definition of a healthy church — Divine Truth, Nurturing Relationships, and Apostolic Mission — which he insists must all be present in even the smallest unit of the body.

### Organic Church

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2005 (*Organic Church*, Jossey-Bass / Leadership Network Series), foreword by Leonard Sweet |
| Definition | "Church is not an event, a place or an organization; it is a family on mission together" |
| Posture | Life-shaped rather than factory-shaped; grown from a seed rather than assembled from a kit |
| Multiplication chain | "If we cannot multiply disciples, we will never multiply leaders… leaders… churches… we will never see a movement of God" |
| Source | [Organic Church](https://www.frankviola.org/2010/09/07/missional-organic-church-an-interview-with-neil-cole-frank-viola/) |

Organic Church reframes the church as a living, reproducing family on mission rather than an institution, growing decentralized faith where everyday life happens.

### Church 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2010 (*Church 3.0: Upgrades for the Future of the Church*, Jossey-Bass), foreword by Francis Chan |
| Components | 1.0 = pre-Constantinian organic church · 2.0 = post-Constantinian institutional church · 3.0 = contemporary recovery of organic patterns |
| Design rule | "Lowered the bar of how church is done so that anyone can do it, and raised the bar of what it means to be a disciple so that not just anyone will do it" |
| Source | [Church 3.0](https://starlinginitiatives.com/about-us/) |

Church 3.0 frames church history as a versioning sequence and casts the present moment as an upgrade back toward the reproducible, organic patterns of the earliest church.

### Organic Church Planters' Greenhouse

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | ~1999–2000 (developed at Church Multiplication Associates with Paul Kaak) |
| Components | Story 1 = "Foundations for Organic Church Planting" · Story 2 = field application |
| Function | The training environment that incubates and multiplies organic church planters |
| Source | [Starling Initiatives](https://starlinginitiatives.com/about-us/) |

The Greenhouse is the two-story training cycle Cole built with Paul Kaak to form and reproduce organic church planters at scale.

### Five Gifts of Jesus (APEST)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Introduced | 2014 (*Primal Fire: Reigniting the Church with the Five Gifts of Jesus*, Tyndale Momentum) |
| Components | Apostle (Neil Cole) · Prophet (Dezi Baker) · Evangelist (Ed Waken) · Shepherd (Phil Helfer) · Teacher (Paul Kaak) |
| Distinctive | Treated as gifts of Jesus (Eph 4:11), not gifts of the Spirit; decentralizing — gifts, not titles |
| Parallel | Compatible with Alan Hirsch's APEST treatment (*The Permanent Revolution*, *5Q*) |
| Source | [Primal Fire](https://kairos.aeu.edu/team/neil-cole) |

Primal Fire reads Ephesians 4's fivefold ministry as five gifts of Jesus distributed across the body — modeled by Cole and four co-thinkers, each carrying one gift — rather than concentrated in a senior pastor.

### Theology of Obscurity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Articulated | 2024–2025 (*Journeys*, 2024; interviews on *Shifting Culture* Ep. 178 and KC Underground's *Upside Down Kingdom*) |
| Components | A thirteen-year God-imposed season with no public U.S. speaking, blogging, or social media |
| Core claim | Intimacy with Christ produces fruit that platform-building cannot; obscurity is a positive spiritual category |
| Signature line | "I never felt like Jesus left me. Everyone else did — but He never did" |
| Source | [Upside Down Kingdom, KCU](https://www.kcunderground.com/podcast/upsidedown-kingdom-neil-cole) |

The theology of obscurity is Cole's late-career conviction, lived across thirteen years of chosen silence, that hidden faithfulness to Jesus bears fruit that visibility and platform never could.
