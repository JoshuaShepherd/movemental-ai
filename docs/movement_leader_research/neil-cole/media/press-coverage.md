## Major Features

### Charisma Magazine

**Feature article on Neil Cole and Awakening Chapel / CMA** — date approximately mid-2000s.

- Source confirmation: [GraceConnect / Charis Fellowship — "Grace Brethren Pastor Neil Cole Featured in Charisma Magazine"](https://graceconnect.us/grace-brethren-pastor-neil-cole-featured-in-charisma-magazine/) (the GraceConnect post links to and summarizes the Charisma feature).
- Significance: This is the source for the widely-circulated "smoking section of The Coffee Tavern" Awakening Chapel founding story. The Charisma feature is the primary press source that put Cole's organic-church work in front of the broader Pentecostal / charismatic reading audience.
- **Exact issue date and article title**: UNKNOWN — needs research (the GraceConnect post archives the reference but the original Charisma article URL was not surfaced in this audit).

### Leadership Network

**Feature on Neil Cole** — referenced via [GraceConnect — "Neil Cole Featured by Leadership Network"](https://graceconnect.us/neil-cole-featured-by-leadership-network/).

- Significance: Leadership Network is the parachurch body that owned the Jossey-Bass Leadership Network Series (publisher of *Organic Church*, *Church 3.0*, *Journeys to Significance*, *Church Transfusion*); a Leadership Network feature is roughly equivalent to a publisher-driven cover story.
- **Exact date and article URL**: UNKNOWN — needs research.

### Christianity Today

**Mark Galli — "Long Live Organic Church!"** (SoulWork column, January 2010), followed by **Neil Cole — "Long Live the Organic Church: A Response"** (CT, January 12, 2010).

- URL: [christianitytoday.com/ct/2010/januaryweb-only/12-21.0.html](https://www.christianitytoday.com/ct/2010/januaryweb-only/12-21.0.html)
- Significance: One of the most-cited public exchanges about the organic-church movement; Cole's response is one of the few Cole authored pieces that lives on a Tier-1 evangelical news outlet's website and is therefore one of the few stable archived articles in his footprint.

### Outreach Magazine

**"The Church Needs Skunk Works"** — featured Cole's perspective.

- URL: [outreachmagazine.com/features/5003-the-church-needs-skunk-works.html](https://outreachmagazine.com/features/5003-the-church-needs-skunk-works.html)
- Topic: small experimental units of innovation inside larger institutions ("skunk works"); Cole's organic-church framework applied to conventional churches.

## Pattern of Coverage

Press coverage parallels the books — 2005–2014 has the heaviest coverage; 2015–2022 is sparse; the 2022 DTS Lectureship and the 2024–2025 podcast cluster are the re-emergence. **No Tier-1 secular press coverage** (NYT / WaPo / *The Atlantic* / Religion News Service profile) of Cole has been surfaced — his coverage is concentrated in evangelical-press circuits (Charisma, Leadership Network, Christianity Today, Outreach Magazine, Faith Radio, DTS Voice, GraceConnect).
