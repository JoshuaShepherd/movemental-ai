## A Coffee Shop, a Lifeguard, an Artist, and a Pastor Who Walked Away

Neil Cole was born and raised in Los Angeles in 1960. By his own telling, he came to faith in Jesus while studying at California State University, Long Beach, where he completed a BA in Fine Art. Before ministry — and the mark this would leave on his later work is not incidental — he was an ocean lifeguard for Los Angeles County beaches. The lifeguard frame becomes the controlling metaphor of *Search & Rescue: Becoming a Disciple Who Makes a Difference* (Baker Books, 2008): the church is not a chapel for the saved, it is a search-and-rescue operation for the drowning.

After CSULB he went east to Indiana for an M.Div. in New Testament at Grace Theological Seminary in Winona Lake, the educational center of the Fellowship of Grace Brethren Churches (now "Charis Fellowship"). He returned to Southern California and pastored a Grace Brethren congregation in Alta Loma, California, through the 1990s. By outward measure he was on the standard evangelical pastorate track.

Then in **1999** he walked away. Cole left the Alta Loma pastorate and started a different kind of church in **the smoking section of a Long Beach coffeehouse called The Coffee Tavern**. He named it **Awakening Chapel**. From that single Long Beach community he and a small group of friends developed two operational technologies that would define his career: a 2-or-3-person same-gender accountability/Scripture/prayer unit he called the **Life Transformation Group (LTG)**; and a training cycle he and Paul Kaak called the **Organic Church Planters' Greenhouse** (Story 1 and Story 2). The 1999 manual on the LTG, *Cultivating a Life for God: Multiplying Disciples Through Life Transformation Groups* (ChurchSmart Resources), was his first book. He simultaneously founded **Church Multiplication Associates (CMA)**, the umbrella that turned Awakening Chapel from a single church into a planting network. By 2003, CMA was claiming roughly four new church starts every week.

## The 2005–2014 Run: Becoming One of the Faces of the Missional Conversation

In a remarkable nine-year window between **2005 and 2014**, Neil Cole became one of the half-dozen most-cited North American voices in the missional / simple-church / organic-church conversation. The trade-house run is striking:

- **2005** — *Beyond Church Planting* with Bob Logan (ChurchSmart). His first co-author was Robert E. Logan, executive director of CoachNet, Fuller Seminary teaching faculty, and a leading evangelical church-planting voice; Cole later names Logan as a mentor in his 2010 *Christianity Today* response piece.
- **2005** — *Organic Church: Growing Faith Where Life Happens*, with a foreword by Leonard Sweet, in the Jossey-Bass / Leadership Network Series. This is the book that put the term "organic church" into the missional vocabulary alongside Hirsch and Frost's *The Shaping of Things to Come*. Alan Hirsch's endorsement on the back cover calls Cole "one of the foremost thinkers and practitioners [of] organic forms of church."
- **2008** — *Search & Rescue* (Baker Books).
- **2009** — *Organic Leadership* (Baker Books / Shapevine series, with Reggie McNeal contributing) — his book on leading without hierarchy.
- **2010** — *Church 3.0: Upgrades for the Future of the Church* (Jossey-Bass), foreword by Francis Chan. The "next-step" book to *Organic Church*.
- **2010** — Public conversation with Frank Viola (joint Keith Giles interview, *Beyond Evangelical*). Cole and Viola overlapped audiences but disagreed on multiplication speed (Cole = rapid; Viola = mature-disciples-first).
- **2010** — Mark Galli's *Christianity Today* "Long Live the Organic Church" piece prompts Cole's published response on `christianitytoday.com` (Jan 12, 2010).
- **2011** — *Journeys to Significance* (Jossey-Bass) and *Ordinary Hero* (Baker).
- **2012** — *Church Transfusion* with Phil Helfer (Jossey-Bass) — how to transition a conventional church toward organic patterns.
- **2014** — *The Black Swan Effect* contributor alongside Hirsch, Frost, Ferguson, McClung, the Dales, and others (House2House, ed. Felicity Dale).
- **2014** — *Primal Fire: Reigniting the Church with the Five Gifts of Jesus* (Tyndale Momentum). His APEST book — written alongside Dezi Baker (prophetic), Ed Waken (evangelistic), Phil Helfer (shepherding), and Paul Kaak (teaching), with Cole carrying the apostolic chair.

Across this same window he was **on the Verge / Exponential / Saturate / 100Movements / DTS / Mosaix-adjacent conference circuit constantly**. Verge Network's speaker page lists him as a presenter ("What Is a Missional Community?" 2010; Exponential 2011 "On the Verge"; Verge teaching segments through 2013). Steve Addison's books cite him as a CPM/DMM peer; Curtis Sergeant credits him as a foundational voice for the IMB's church-planting strategy (the IMB adapted LTG into a tool called **CHAT**).

## Co-founding 100 Movements (2015)

In **November 2015**, Will Mancini publicly announced the launch of **100 Movements**, a new non-profit consultative training organization built around six "movement competencies." The named design team was: **Alan Hirsch, Neil Cole, Dave Rhodes, Will Mancini, and Jessie Cruickshank** (Harvard-trained learning expert). Mancini's blog post explicitly calls Cole "perhaps [having] led a multiplication movement more than any single practitioner in the United States." This is the first explicit, formal, public collaboration between Hirsch and Cole — a fact that the master `network/network-map.md` currently undercounts (it carries only the mutual-endorsement edge).

This is also, almost exactly, the moment the public Cole begins to thin out.

## The Silence (~2012–~2024)

In multiple 2024–2025 interviews Cole has described **a 13-year season in which God told him to stop speaking publicly in the U.S., refrain from social media, and cease blogging.** The quote KC Underground's *Upside Down Kingdom* surfaces from him: *"I never felt like Jesus left me. Everyone else did — but He never did."* His other framing, on *Shifting Culture*, sums up the period this way: *"His whole work can be summed up in this: listen to Jesus and do what He says."*

The visible evidence:

- The **Cole-Slaw blog** — active 2007 through 2017, with a posting rhythm that thins after 2013, a final cluster of posts in March–April 2016, one post on June 7, 2017, and silence after.
- **Big-house trade publishing stops at *One Thing*** (Thomas Nelson, Nov 2015). Tyndale, Jossey-Bass, Baker, and Thomas Nelson do not publish another Cole book between 2015 and 2026.
- **CMA Resources lapses.** The cmaresources.org domain at some point was lost or expired and is now serving a Sydney-lottery prediction page; the CMAResources Twitter and Facebook still nominally exist but are not actively maintained.
- The conference appearance cadence is much harder to verify in this window. He resurfaces in **November 2022** at DTS for the Missions and Evangelism Lectureship (three chapel sessions), but otherwise the catalogued public-speaking record between roughly 2016 and 2023 is thin.

What he did publish in that window came out under his own self-publishing imprint, **Starling Initiatives Publication Series**, on Amazon: *Rising Tides* (2018), *Pray* (2019/2022), *VIRAL* (2021), then *Journeys* (2024) and a *Primal Fire* reissue (2025). These books carry the same intellectual program as the earlier work but have not had the trade-house reach. He narrates the audiobooks himself.

## Re-emergence (2024–2025)

By **April 2024** he returns to the guest-podcast circuit. *Shifting Culture* Ep. 178 is the public marker — Cole talking about *Journeys* and the season of silence. Then **December 2024 / January 2025**: two episodes on the *reChurch Podcast* (hosted by Justin and Brooke Knoop), titled "The 100K Ministry Mistake That Opened My Eyes Forever" (Ep. 13) and "The REAL Reason Church Leadership is Broken" (Ep. 14). In **August 2025** he sits down with Brian Johnson and Cory at **KC Underground's *Upside Down Kingdom* podcast** — a significant placement, because KC Underground is Rob Wegner's organization, which means the founder of CMA is being received as an elder voice by the next generation of microchurch operators (Wegner, Pulley, Sanders' lineage).

His current institutional anchors as of 2026:

- **Starling Initiatives** — global collective, monthly Zoom trainings, private Facebook community, online courses ("coming soon"), audiobook publication. Headquarters Long Beach.
- **Kairos Pacific University** — Professor of Church Development.
- **100 Movements** — co-founder relationship; current operational involvement UNKNOWN.

Goodreads still ranks him at **2,062 ratings, 3.99 average across 60 distinct works**, with *Primal Fire* as his highest-rated title (4.21).

## What This Biography Is Not

It is not a biography of how a movement scaled. The CMA "thousands of churches in 50 states and 50+ nations" claim is the organization's self-report. **Independent verification is not possible from public sources.** The Internal-Mission-Board adaptation of LTG (CHAT) is the strongest external confirmation; Greenhouse cohorts at Frisco TX 2011, Virginia Mennonite Conference 2012, and others are the strongest documented training-event traces; and the citation density across other authors' books (Hirsch, Stetzer, Sweet, Sanders, Wegner, Ford, Halter, Sergeant, Addison) is the strongest reception evidence.

It is also not the biography of a celebrity-pastor brand. Cole has actively rejected that posture, both in his content (*Organic Leadership* is structurally an anti-hierarchy book; *Primal Fire* decentralizes leadership across five gifts) and in his behavior (the silence period is, in effect, a 13-year argument against the influence economy). The Movemental fit treats him accordingly — see [analysis/movemental-fit.md](../analysis/movemental-fit.md).
