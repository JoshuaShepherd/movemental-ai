## Network Insights

1. **Alan Hirsch is the keystone.** Removing him would fragment the network into 4-5 disconnected clusters. This validates Movemental's strategy of building around his established scenius.

2. **Forge is the organizational backbone.** More leaders connect through Forge than any other single organization. Forge America (Rowland Smith), Forge Canada (Cam Roxburgh), and Forge KC (Brad Brisco) are the main branches.

3. **The Underground Network is a self-contained sub-network.** Wegner, Pulley, Gaskins, Wilkerson form a tight cluster that connects to the main network primarily through Wegner's relationship with Hirsch and Ferguson.

   **Update 2026-04-29**: The Wegner ego network is **substantially denser than the original 2026-04-13 mapping showed**. Wegner is an 8-book author (not 1-book), with KCU co-founder Brian Johnson, Exponential CEO Todd Wilson, and Brian Phipps as additional weight-5 ties via co-authorship. Combined with the Future Travelers and Starfish Initiatives co-founder edges with Hirsch and the merger of NewThing into Exponential, **Wegner is now plausibly the most strategically positioned non-Hirsch bridge in the network**. He sits inside both the academic-theological Hirsch orbit (via *Starfish*, Future Travelers, Starfish Initiatives) AND the operational-multiplication merged Exponential entity (via NewThing GLT, Microchurch NEXT, Made for More with Todd Wilson). For Movemental strategy purposes, Wegner now functions as a near-peer-to-Hirsch keystone for the multiplication side of the field.

4. **JR Woodward's Missio Alliance bridge is strategically valuable.** It connects the Hirsch orbit to a broader progressive-missional circle (Fitch, Roxburgh, Morse) that otherwise has limited connections.

5. **Research gaps are significant.** Of 146 scored leaders, only 42 have research folders and only 3 (Hirsch, Brisco, Woodward) have rich network data. Expanding network research to the top 20 (score 85+) would dramatically improve the map.

6. **International connections are underrepresented.** Mark Sayers (Australia), Steve Addison (global), and Evan Mawarire (Zimbabwe) are top-scored but have minimal mapped connections. This is a research priority.

7. **The formation/soul-care cluster is disconnected.** Mindy Caliguire, Pete Scazzero, Ruth Haley Barton (all scored 85+) have no mapped connections to the movement cluster despite likely conference overlap. Research needed.
