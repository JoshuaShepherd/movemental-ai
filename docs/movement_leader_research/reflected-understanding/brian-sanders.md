# Brian Sanders: Reflected Understanding

A concise reflection spoken back so the language could be endorsed — familiar, but with edges that deepen. (Leader-safe: audience-sizing and commerce analysis are kept in staff notes, not here.)

---

## Calling

You're called to **make the church smaller so it can move again**. Not to plant another program-driven congregation, and not to add a campus, but to recover the simplest unit of the church and put it back into the hands of ordinary disciples. Your conviction, lived out for more than two decades in the poorest neighborhood of your own city, is that the microchurch — "the coalescing of three intentional practices: worship, community, and mission" — is *not just the smallest expression of the church, it is the most common.* That inversion is the whole of it. Where much of the Western church measures by the weekly gathering, you've spent your life insisting the church is empowered people on mission, and that the best way to lead them is to serve their God-given callings rather than recruit them to your own vision. You're called to the poor, the lost, and biblical justice — and to a structure "streamlined for mission" that releases people toward all three. The language you already use — *a smaller way*, *the ecclesial minimum*, *every Christian in every context*, *structured and streamlined for mission* — is the right language. It names a church that multiplies by sending, not by gathering, and it stays tethered to a real, sustained experiment rather than a theory.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

**Where it lives.** Your work lives in **books** — *Underground Church* (Zondervan, 2018, with Alan Hirsch's foreword), *Microchurches: A Smaller Way* (Underground Media, 2019), *The 6 Seasons of Calling* (Moody, 2022), and the earlier *Life In Intentional Christian Community*, *Different* (with Mike Patz), and *Life After Church* (IVP, 2007) — across four different imprints. It lives in the **Underground Network** itself: 100+ microchurches in Tampa and fifteen-to-thirty-plus sister movements in cities and countries around the world. It lives in **teaching and training** — the MicroChurch Conference, the Microchurch Network Intensive, the Immersive Visits — and in **media**: the Microchurches podcast on Apple and Spotify, guest appearances on shows like *The Roys Report*, the Exponential *Networked Church* material, and writing scattered across Medium and Underground Network resource pages.

**Credibility.** It's high. The Underground is a leading reference model for the microchurch movement in the West, endorsed and introduced by voices like Hirsch, Neil Cole, and Michael Frost; your books carry real readership; and the model has been copied into sister movements far beyond Tampa. The issue isn't authority — it's **visibility, connection, and reuse**.

**How it lives online now.** As **silos**. *Underground Church* sits on Zondervan and Amazon under the Exponential Series; *Microchurches* sits on the self-published Underground Media imprint; *The 6 Seasons of Calling* sits on Moody with its own narrator; the earlier titles sit on Amazon and IVP. The podcast lives on Apple and Spotify; the *Roys Report* appearance lives on a host's feed; the conference and intensive run through Underground Network's surfaces. There's no single place where a reader can walk from the story in *Underground Church* to the method in *Microchurches* to the cohort that trains it. So the work doesn't **move** in the sense of circulating, compounding, and connecting — it stays where it was first put.

**The NOTs the platform addresses directly.** Your content is largely **not** gathered into one place a reader can reach; **not** structured and repurposed so the Network Intensive becomes a public pathway and the conference sessions outlive each cycle; **not** interconnected so the books, the podcast, the trainings, and the sister movements visibly reference each other; **not** optimized so a disillusioned believer searching for "microchurch," "house church," or "church on mission" lands on your material first; and **not** owned and unified under a single surface you control. There's also a deeper layer most at risk of going unwritten — the judgment from twenty years of leading an intentional community: which microchurches to plant and which to let close, how to fund mission without becoming a program, how a network serves the small without smothering it. That embodied knowledge has always lived in the room and on the phone, long before any fraction of it reached the page.

## Constraints

Movement leaders living out mission-first ecclesiology work under **time** and **money** constraints that are emblematic, not personal failure. Your formation has always happened in mission before it happened in print — in the neighborhood, in the founding cohort, in the coaching of the next microchurch leader. The hours to catalogue an article archive, to turn the Network Intensive into a public sequence, to surface the founding story as long-form writing, or to build a unified digital home are scarce precisely because the work is in the room with practitioners and the poor. The desire to reach every Christian in every context is there; the time to translate everything you've already built into a findable, connected form typically is not. Those constraints shape every decision about what gets recorded, repurposed, or written down. A platform that reduces the lift — that helps inventory, structure, connect, and amplify what you've already authored, at your pace, with authorship staying yours — addresses those constraints without asking you to become a different kind of leader than the practitioner you've always been.
