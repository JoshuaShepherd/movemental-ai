# Dave Ferguson: Reflected Understanding

## Calling

You're called to **make heroes, not be one**. For more than three decades — from a high school cafeteria in Naperville in 1989 to a network that reached across dozens of nations — you've held one conviction with remarkable consistency: the mission of Jesus is a multiplication mission, and the church advances not by growing larger but by reproducing disciples, leaders, groups, churches, and networks at every level. Your own language carries this. "My fruit grows on other people's trees." "I'm the platform; I'm not the show." "As a leader, you reproduce who you are." That last line is doing real theological work — it makes a leader's inner health a kingdom issue, not a private one, which is exactly why *Multiplier* turns the multiplication question inward before it turns it outward.

Your calling is to invert the hero-pastor model that the American church quietly worships. Where that culture prizes platform, you prize permission giving; where it counts attendance and budget, you count disciples made and leaders released. The ICNU conversation — "I see in you" — is the small, repeatable act at the center of it: you name what God has already placed in someone before they can see it themselves, then you walk them through apprenticeship until they can do the same for someone else. You learned this from your own mentors and from watching your father plant a church, and you've made it teachable. None of this is theory for you. The 2025 succession at Community Christian — Ted Coniaris moving through an eighteen-month apprenticeship into the lead role while you stepped back to Co-Founding Pastor — was you living the five steps you wrote down. "Kingdom over Castle," the principle behind folding NewThing into Exponential the same year, is the same calling at organizational scale: structures should be subordinate to mission, even when the structure is yours.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

**Where it lives.** Your content lives in **books** (nine-plus trade titles across Zondervan, WaterBrook, and Salem, from *The Big Idea* in 2007 to *Multiplier* in 2026, plus a shelf of Exponential resources and free eBooks), in **three organizations you built** (Community Christian Church, the NewThing network, and Exponential — now unified), in **conference platforms** (Exponential's national and regional events, Exponential International), in **per-book sites** (heromakerbook.org, bless-book.org, multiplierbook.com), and across **personal channels** (daveferguson.org and an active blog, a Substack, plus Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, X, and LinkedIn). You also teach at Wheaton's graduate school and write for outlets like Christianity Today's pastors platform and the Global Leadership Network.

**Credibility.** It's high. You're a benchmark practitioner voice in church multiplication: a church recognized repeatedly among the most influential in America, a planting network that reached dozens of countries, the world's largest church-planting conference, frameworks that pastors actually use — BLESS, Hero Maker, the five-level typology — and a string of well-reviewed books. The issue was never authority. It's **visibility, connection, and reuse**.

**How it lives online now.** As **silos**. Hero Maker lives on its own site; BLESS on another; Multiplier on a third. Your sermons and Exponential talks live where they were delivered. The blog, the Substack, the social channels, and the institutional sites each hold a slice, but there is no single place where "everything Dave has taught about multiplication, hero making, and neighboring" is structured, linked, and easy to find. So the content doesn't **move** the way your frameworks do — it stays where it was first put.

**The NOTs this platform addresses directly.** Your content is largely **not** repurposed across forms — sermons and conference talks rarely become findable articles, and book themes rarely become courses or guided journeys; **not** interconnected, so Hero Maker, BLESS, the apprenticeship model, and the five-level typology don't visibly reference and build on each other; **not** connected to an AI that reflects your voice and your corpus so leaders can ask it questions in your language; **not** optimized for discoverability, so people searching for "how to multiply leaders," "church multiplication," "hero maker," or "how to share faith with neighbors" don't reliably land on your material first; and **not** owned and unified under one surface you control, separate from the three book sites and the institutional domains. The platform is built to speak to each of these — structure and repurpose, interconnection, a voice-faithful AI, discoverability, and a single owned home — so your content can move the way your movements do.

## Constraints

Movement leaders carry **time** and **money** constraints that are emblematic, not personal failure. You are leading a unified Exponential, teaching, coaching, still on the Community Christian team, writing, and speaking — and the merger year added a layer of integration work on top of all of it. Time for content strategy — for turning a talk into an article, a book chapter into a course, a framework into a guided journey — is scarce precisely because the work is in the room with planters, apprentices, and networks. You've spent a career insisting that your fruit grows on other people's trees; the same instinct that makes you a hero maker makes it hard to stop and tend your own published corpus. The desire to reach everyone God has given you is plainly there; the hours to structure, translate, and amplify everything you've already said, in every format, typically are not. Those constraints shape every content decision — what gets recorded, what gets written down, what becomes a course. A platform that reduces the lift — that helps structure, repurpose, connect, and amplify what you already do — meets those constraints without asking you to become a different kind of leader.
