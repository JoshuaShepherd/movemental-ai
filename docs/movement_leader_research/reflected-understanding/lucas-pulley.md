# Lucas Pulley: Reflected Understanding

## Calling

You're called to **activate the priesthood of all believers in the mission of God** — and to do it through the smallest durable form of the church, not the largest impressive one. You don't plant services; you cultivate the conditions in which ordinary people are equipped, empowered, and sent. Your conviction is that the church is not a building, a service, or a program but **an extended spiritual family committed to the leadership of Jesus and pursuing some piece of God's mission**. That family — worship, community, mission — is the *ecclesial minimum*, the irreducible unit. Everything beyond it is scaffolding that has to justify itself.

The language you already use is the right language: *the ecclesial minimum*; *priesthood of all believers, activated*; *starfish, not spider*; *covocational by default*; *your conversion is your commission*. It's the vocabulary of someone who has decided, on theological grounds, that decentralization is not a tactic but a more faithful reading of how Jesus's movement actually moves. You've paid for that conviction in the most literal way — pastoring a neighborhood house church inside Tampa's subsidized housing projects, beginning at the margins because that's where you believe the movement starts, not as sentiment but as strategy. Your Movements Director seat on the Underground Network Executive Team is the translocal expression of the same calling: serving Tampa's hundred-plus microchurches and dozens of sister networks worldwide, so that what you've learned planting and directing for fifteen years can be handed to the next planter sooner than the old pipelines would allow. You're not building a brand around yourself; you're trying to make it socially normal to "look dumb together" for the sake of the mission — to lower the threshold so more people cross it.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

**Where it lives.** Your body of practice is real and densely operational — and it's scattered across other people's surfaces. Your writing lives on **Medium** (essays like *10 Thoughts After 10 Years of Planting & Directing Microchurch Networks*, *Missional Culture & The Granny Shot*, *5 Ways "Calling" Is Being Hijacked*, *Small and Significant Microchurches in a Celebrity Age*) and on **Missio Alliance** (*The Parable of the White Pastor*, 2019). Your most carefully codified teaching artifact — the *Underground as Creedal Community* session — sits inside the enrollment-gated *Underground Essentials* course on Teachable. Your strongest spoken work lives on the **guest podcast circuit**: *Theology in the Raw* with Preston Sprinkle, *Shifting Culture* with Joshua Johnson, *The Church Digital* with Jeff Reed, *For Everyday Leaders*, *Exponential NEXT* with Rob Wegner, the Hubology series on *The Starfish and the Church* with Wegner and Lance Ford, *KC Underground*, and Koinesúnē. You're a recurring voice on Tampa Underground's in-house *Microchurches Podcast*. And you teach — at the annual Microchurch Conference, in the Calling Lab intensive, and in the coaching conversations you've had a thousand times.

**Credibility.** It's high — and it's practitioner credibility, which is exactly the kind that's hardest to fake. Fifteen years of planting and directing; a Master of Global Leadership in Missions and Missiology from Fuller; a coherent, articulated body of frameworks (the ecclesial minimum, mission-specific vs. distributive microchurches, the four spaces of belonging, the servant-equipper / Hubology model); and the trust of top-tier hosts who keep inviting you back. The issue isn't authority. It's **visibility, ownership, and reuse**.

**How it lives online now.** As **silos you don't own**. Every host who featured you lent you their audience and gave you no way to keep it. The essays sit under @lucas_pulley on Medium; the creedal-community teaching sits behind a Teachable paywall with no public preview; the podcast appearances live on eight different feeds. Right now lucaspulley.com functions as a link-in-bio page — there's no hosted show under your name, the newsletter is a form with no archive, and there's no single place where a stranger can move from any one piece of your work to every other. So it doesn't **move** in the sense of circulating and compounding. It stays where it was first put.

**The NOTs Movemental addresses directly.** Your content is largely **not** aggregated into one searchable home under your name; **not** interconnected, so a reader who finds *The Parable of the White Pastor* can follow the chain to *10 Thoughts* to the *Shifting Culture* conversation to the Microchurch Conference session that started it; **not** repurposed across forms (the gated *Underground Essentials* session into a public cohort sequence, the Hubology arc into an owned audio archive, the conference faculty block into a public syllabus); **not** connected to an AI that can surface the embodied, never-written layer at your pace; and **not** optimized so a planter in another city — who has never heard of Tampa Underground — lands on your teaching first. Movemental is built to speak to each of these: aggregate, interconnect, repurpose, amplify — so the body of practice you've already built can reach the practitioners it was written for.

## Constraints

Movement leaders with mission-first priorities live under **time** and **money** constraints that are emblematic, not personal failure. Your work is in the room — pastoring in the projects with Jami and your three kids, serving a hundred-plus Tampa microchurches and dozens of sister networks, faculty-ing the conference, running the Calling Lab. The hours to repurpose a talk into an article, to stand up a real newsletter, or to build a unified digital home are scarce precisely because the embodied work is where your calling lives. The covocational reality you teach as the network's natural financial architecture is your own reality too. Those constraints shape every content decision: what gets recorded, what gets written down, what becomes a course. A platform that reduces the lift — that does the inventorying and the connective tissue while leaving every draft yours to edit, refuse, or rewrite — addresses those constraints without asking you to become a different kind of leader, or to stop being the one in the room.
