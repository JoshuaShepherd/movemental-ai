# Neil Cole: Reflected Understanding

A concise reflection spoken back so the language could be endorsed — familiar, but with edges that deepen. (Leader-safe: audience-sizing and commerce analysis are kept in staff notes, not here.)

---

## Calling

You're called to **make disciples who make disciples** — and to keep the unit small enough that they actually can. Not to build an institution, not to scale a brand, but to recover the church's smallest reproducible cell and release it to multiply. That instinct goes all the way back to the smoking section of The Coffee Tavern: a different kind of church, started low and built to reproduce. The Life Transformation Group is the clearest expression of it — two or three people, Scripture, confession, prayer for the lost — because the smallest viable cell already has to carry the whole DNA: Divine Truth, Nurturing Relationships, Apostolic Mission. Your conviction that "the highest potential of the church is in its smallest unit" is not a slogan; it's the load-bearing claim of everything from *Cultivating a Life for God* to *Church 3.0*. You lowered the bar of how church is done so anyone can do it, and raised the bar of what it means to be a disciple so that not just anyone will.

And then there's the second calling, which you didn't choose and which is harder to name: the long obscurity. Thirteen years told to stop speaking, stop blogging, stop posting. What you've said about that season — *"I never felt like Jesus left me. Everyone else did — but He never did"* — is itself a teaching. It means the platform was never the point; intimacy with Christ that bears fruit the platform can't is the point. The language you already use — *organic*, *DNA*, *family on mission*, *listen to Jesus and do what He says* — is the right language. It stays agricultural rather than industrial, decentralized rather than hierarchical, and tethered to the actual cost of leading from the edge rather than the center.

## Existing Content (before the platform we're building)

**Where it lives.** Your content lives across a long trade-publishing run — *Organic Church*, *Church 3.0*, *Search & Rescue*, *Organic Leadership*, *Church Transfusion*, *Primal Fire*, and more, with Jossey-Bass, Baker, Tyndale, Thomas Nelson, and ChurchSmart — alongside the **Greenhouse** training materials and the LTG cards that traveled far beyond anything you published. It also lives in **Starling Initiatives** (your current collective, imprint, courses, and trainings), in the **Cole-Slaw blog** archive, in self-published audiobooks you narrate yourself, and in your teaching at **Kairos Pacific University**. The 100 Movements work you co-founded with Alan Hirsch, Dave Rhodes, Will Mancini, and Jessie Cruickshank lives on its own org surface.

**Credibility.** It's high, and it's durable. You're one of the most-cited North American voices in the organic-church and church-multiplication conversation; your LTG was adapted by the International Mission Board, and practitioners like Curtis Sergeant and Steve Addison treat your work as foundational. The next generation of microchurch leaders now receives you as an elder voice. The issue was never authority — it's **visibility, connection, and reuse** after a long silence.

**How it lives online now.** As **scattered fragments**, and some of it has decayed. The CMA Resources domain lapsed; the blog has been dormant since 2017; the trade books sit on retailer and publisher pages; the Starling material is newer and not yet deeply interconnected; the talks and trainings live in many places at once. There is no single surface where "everything Neil has said and written about organic church, LTGs, DNA, and multiplication" is structured, linked, and easy to find — so the work doesn't **move** in the sense of circulating and compounding.

**The NOTs the platform addresses directly.** Your content is largely **not** structured and repurposed to live as findable digital content (talks and trainings → articles, book themes → courses); **not** reconnected after the lapse so the LTG, Greenhouse, DNA, and organic-church material visibly reference each other; **not** connected to a personalized assistant that reflects your voice and corpus; **not** optimized so searchers for "organic church," "Life Transformation Group," "disciple-making movement," or "house church" land on your material first; and **not** unified under one surface you own. The platform is built to speak to each of these — structure and repurpose, reconnection, an assistant that serves your voice, discoverability, and ownership — so the work can move again without asking you to rebuild a platform you deliberately set down.

## Constraints

The constraints here are partly the ordinary ones — **time** is scarce because the work is in the room, with planters and disciples and students, not in content strategy — and partly distinctive to you. You spent thirteen years arguing, by your silence, that platform-building is not the point. So any work on visibility has to honor that conviction rather than contradict it: this is not "build your platform back." It's making the substantial body of work you've already given legible, connected, and findable for the people God is still sending to it, while you stay free to keep doing the quieter, more interior work — *Pray*, *VIRAL*, *Journeys* — that the late season has produced. Your decentralizing instinct is also a constraint in the best sense: anything that re-centers everything on a personality would cut against the grain of the LTG, the DNA, and the five gifts. A platform that reduces the lift — that structures, reconnects, and amplifies what you already do, without re-centering it on you — addresses those constraints without asking you to become a different kind of leader than you've spent a lifetime refusing to be.
