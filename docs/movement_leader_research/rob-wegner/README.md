## Executive Summary

Rob Wegner is the **founding director of Kansas City Underground (KCU)**, a decentralized microchurch mission agency he and his wife Michelle started in 2019 with about 80 disciples (now ~95 microchurches across 7 Collectives, 110+ activated missionaries, 5 Hubs operational/in-development). He is also **Microchurch NEXT Co-Director at Exponential** (alongside Brian Johnson), **on the Global Leadership Team / formerly North American Director of NewThing** (NewThing fully integrated into Exponential April 1, 2025), and **co-host of *The Starfish and the Church* podcast** (43 episodes, with Lance Ford). His career arc is unusual: **21 years as Pastor of Life Mission / Teaching Pastor at Granger Community Church (Indiana)** under Mark Beeson, then **4 years at Westside Family Church (Kansas City)**, then KCU. The pivot point was a transformative experience with grassroots church planters in **India** that reshaped his ecclesial assumptions — the experience that produced the photo-essay book *Share the Well* (with Michelle).

What the existing flat-file research missed: **Wegner is a multi-book author, not a one-book author.** This audit confirmed **8 books** (one in the 2008–2010 range, one 2012, three in 2018–2019, one 2021, two in the Exponential digital catalog through 2025). His co-author roster reads as a tour of the church-multiplication establishment: **Alan Hirsch, Lance Ford, Todd Wilson, Brian Phipps, Jack Magruder, Brian Johnson, Michelle Wegner**. *The Starfish and the Spirit* (Zondervan, March 30, 2021, 312 pp., ISBN 9780310098378, foreword by Ori Brafman) is his most prominent work — Goodreads avg 4.25 / 202 ratings; Audible avg 4.7 / 49 ratings. His earlier *Missional Moves* (Zondervan, 2012, with Magruder, foreword by Hirsch) is the seed of his current frame: "It's not that the church has a mission. It's that a mission has the church."

His digital surface, in contrast to that body of work, is **modest and structurally fragmented.** His personal site `robwegner.org` is a Tumblr-powered archive last meaningfully refreshed around 2021, with stale "Director / NewThing" and "Director of Microchurch Next / Leadership Network" titles. His former blog `entermission.typepad.com` is dormant. His written voice today lives across **at least seven owned-and-rented surfaces**: KC Underground (org), Exponential (rented platform via Microchurch NEXT), Outreach Magazine, Saturate, Verge Network, the joint *Starfish yoU* Substack (with Ford), and the Starfish Initiatives consulting site — none of which fully reflects his solo body of thought. His Instagram is the most active personal surface (~1,867 followers, 761 posts).

Within the network, Wegner is one of the **clearest bridge nodes between Cluster 1 (Hirsch / Forge / 100M) and Cluster 5 (Exponential / NewThing / Underground)**. The bridge edge is *The Starfish and the Spirit* itself — Hirsch and Ford on one side; Exponential, NewThing, and the Underground city movements on the other. Because of his Hubology podcast collaboration with **Lance Ford**, **Lucas Pulley**, and **Brian Johnson**, he is also the natural connector to Tampa Underground and Carbondale Underground.

For an 8-book author and three-organization principal, the personal hub is a year-zero rebuild candidate. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort.)

2. **Consolidate the multi-co-author bibliography.** Wegner's eight books span four publishers (Zondervan, Exponential, self-published) and seven different co-author groupings. There is no single page anywhere that lists them all in chronological order with descriptions. A canonical bibliography hub — with through-line themes (Granger India era → Westside mobilization era → KCU microchurch era → Starfish leadership era) — surfaces the coherent intellectual arc that's invisible in the retailer-page-by-retailer-page state today. (HIGH priority, LOW effort.)

3. **Index the *Starfish and the Church* corpus.** 43 episodes co-hosted with Lance Ford, with a parallel Substack (`starfishinitiatives.substack.com`) and a consulting brand (`starfishinitiatives.com`). Currently the three surfaces don't mutually link into a single learning path; the Substack is dormant on the web side; the consulting site has no episode index; the podcast has no transcript archive. A unified "Starfish ecosystem hub" with topical taxonomy and transcripts is one of the highest-leverage moves. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort.)

4. **Launch a real personal newsletter.** No personal newsletter under `robwegner.org` was found; the only newsletter Wegner is on is the joint Substack with Ford. For a co-author of *The Starfish and the Spirit*, this is dramatically under-leveraged. (HIGH priority, LOW effort.)

5. **Capture the 21-year Granger archive (if it survives).** Two decades of teaching at Granger Community Church under Mark Beeson — likely thousands of hours of sermons, training decks, and small-group materials — lives (if at all) on `granger.cc`'s legacy archive, indexed under Granger's brand rather than Wegner's. Negotiating right-of-use or simply linking what survives back into a Wegner timeline would resurface a quarter-century of trapped value. (MEDIUM priority, MEDIUM effort, requires Granger cooperation.)
