## Primary audience

**Practitioners building or leading microchurch / multiplication networks in North America.**

Specifically:

- **Microchurch planters and network leaders** — KCU's own ~95 microchurches, Tampa Underground, Carbondale, plus the dozens of smaller microchurch networks Wegner and Brian Johnson interview on Microchurch NEXT.
- **Multiplication-oriented church planters** — particularly those connected to Exponential, NewThing (now folded into Exponential), Forge, V3, and Movement Leaders Collective.
- **Teaching pastors at large attractional churches** considering missional pivot — Wegner's specific personal-arc credibility (megachurch insider → microchurch founder) makes this a natural audience.
- **NewThing-network catalytic leaders** — globally distributed across 1,600+ churches in 177 networks (per `robwegner.org/about/`).
- **Future Travelers cohort participants** — megachurch senior leaders coached by Hirsch + Wegner.
- **Disciples Made program users** — KC-based disciple-making practitioners working with Brian Phipps's framework.

## Secondary audience

- **Megachurch leaders curious about decentralization but not committed to it.** Wegner's *Starfish and the Spirit* speaks to this audience explicitly (the book's hybrid "starder / spiderfish" framing is aimed at leaders who can't or won't go all-starfish but want some of the benefits).
- **Discipleship-focused pastors** in evangelical and non-denominational settings — Wegner's *Five Provocative Questions* and *Five Ingredients* essays are aimed here.
- **Evangelical seminary students** considering alternatives to traditional pastoral roles — Wegner's collaborative-byline pattern and ordinary-people-lead theology resonate.
- **Marketplace Christians (covocational practitioners)** — implicit audience of the ordinary-people-as-missionaries framing; Wegner doesn't use "covocational" as a primary keyword (Brad Brisco does that work) but the audience overlap is real.
- **Movement leaders globally** — through NewThing's pre-merger international network and through KCU's documented partnerships with movements outside North America (per the 2026 Tampa Microchurch Conference attendance from South America, Europe, Australia).

## Tertiary / latent audience

- **Secular organizational-design practitioners** interested in Brafman's starfish framework. The Doug Kirkpatrick pickup is a small but real signal.
- **Christian academics** in ecclesiology, missiology, and leadership studies. The Andrews University review is a single data point but suggests the door is open.
- **Theologically-curious general Christian readers** picking up *Starfish and the Spirit* as a leadership book without a deep practitioner background.

## Demographic skew (estimated)

- **Age**: Median ~35–55. Skews older for the megachurch-pivot audience, younger for the microchurch-planter audience.
- **Gender**: Likely 60–70% male, consistent with North American evangelical leadership demographics. Wegner's collaborative-byline pattern (with Michelle, with non-male co-authors per Disciples Made / Future Travelers cohorts) is friendlier to female practitioners than the genre default, but the surface audience still skews male.
- **Race / ethnicity**: Predominantly white North American evangelical; with growing Asian (via India / DMM connection) and Latino (KCU's Kansas City context includes substantial Latino microchurches per Discovery Bible Study work) representation. KCU explicitly has multilingual / multi-ethnic microchurch presence; the broader audience is whiter.
- **Education**: Mixed — ranges from seminary-educated pastors to lay practitioners with no formal theological education. KCU's "ordinary people lead" theology actively courts the latter.
- **Geography**: Strongest in North America, particularly the Midwest (Kansas City, Indiana, Chicago) and the Southeast (Tampa, Atlanta). Growing internationally via NewThing inheritance.

## What this audience cares about (explicit and implicit)

### Explicit (named in Wegner's content)

1. **How do I start a microchurch?** — entry-level operational question. The KCU Missionary Pathway and *Find Your Place* answer this.
2. **How do I lead a microchurch network?** — operational complexity question. *Starfish and the Spirit*, the Hubology series, and *Five Provocative Questions* answer this.
3. **How do I disciple someone?** — *Five Ingredients*, *Extraordinary Prayer*, KC Underground Podcast.
4. **How do I plant the gospel in a way that reproduces?** — "Reproducible Gospel Language" KCU Podcast; *Deeply Rooted*.
5. **How do I find my own calling / mission?** — *Find Your Place*, *Made for More*.
6. **How do I lead an organization that doesn't drift toward control?** — *Navigating Change* (the KCU family-of-families restructure piece) speaks directly to this.
7. **What's a healthy relationship between megachurch and microchurch?** — Wegner's personal arc + *Starfish and the Spirit*.

### Implicit (audience care that Wegner addresses without making it the headline)

1. **Burnout / unsustainability of solo-heroic leadership** — every book and podcast Wegner does undercuts this implicitly.
2. **Anxiety about cultural Christianity's decline** — Wegner's coronavirus-microchurch piece and his "we are mostly invulnerable" framing speaks to this.
3. **Theological credibility for non-traditional church forms** — Wegner provides the Hirsch-adjacent academic credentialing for microchurch practice.
4. **Permission to leave a megachurch role** — the Roys Report quote ("we have better oversight in our decentralized network") is permission-giving for pastors mid-transition.
5. **Practical tools that are reproducible by ordinary people** — every framework is built to be remembered, not memorized.

## Audience size (estimates)

- **KCU audience**: ~95 microchurches × ~10–25 people each = ~1,000–2,500 KC adherents; plus 110+ activated missionaries + 300+ in training
- **Microchurch Conference reach**: ~550+ attendees (2026 Tampa) drawn from 200+ networks → ~5,000–10,000 first-degree practitioners globally
- **Exponential conference reach** (where Wegner speaks regularly): ~5,000–10,000 attendees per global conference
- **Microchurch NEXT podcast + KCU Podcast + Starfish and the Church** total cumulative audience: UNKNOWN but estimated in the **5–25K listener range** based on cohort comparables
- **Book reach**: *Starfish and the Spirit* with 202 Goodreads ratings + 49 Audible ratings is a midsize trade-Christian leadership book; reasonable estimate of total readers ~5–15K
- **Total addressable practitioner audience (rough)**: ~15–40K people who have meaningfully engaged with Wegner's content via at least one surface

These are estimates; specific numbers UNKNOWN.

## Strategic implication

Wegner's audience is **practitioner-dense, discoverability-light**. The audience exists; Wegner has access to it through Exponential, KCU, NewThing-inheritance, the Microchurch Conference, and three podcast venues. What's missing is the **gateway** — there is no single discoverable hub that converts a curious reader from Goodreads / Audible / a conference handout into a sustained Wegner audience member. Movemental's value proposition would be exactly this: capture the existing practitioner traffic and convert it into an owned, durable, cross-content audience.
