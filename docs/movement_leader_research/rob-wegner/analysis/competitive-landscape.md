## Wegner's specific differentiators

What does Wegner uniquely carry that the adjacent peers don't?

1. **The personal arc (megachurch insider → microchurch founder).** Brian Sanders was always Underground; Lance Ford was always pastoral-consulting; Hirsch was always missional-academic. Wegner spent 21 years at a flagship megachurch (Granger) and then 4 years at another large megachurch (Westside) before founding KCU. **He is one of very few people in this ecosystem who can credibly say "I was in your seat for two decades."**
2. **The bridge between Cluster 1 (Hirsch / Forge / 100M) and Cluster 5 (Exponential / NewThing / Underground).** Most peers sit on one side or the other. Wegner's *Starfish and the Spirit* co-authorship with Hirsch + Ford and his concurrent Exponential / NewThing principal roles make him the most explicit bridge node.
3. **The KCU operational track record.** 7 years of running a two-entity microchurch network in a major North American city, with ~95 microchurches, 110+ activated missionaries, 5 Hubs, 120+ DBS launched. This is more operational depth than most peers carry.
4. **The India formation story.** *Share the Well* and the recurring "India trip changed everything" testimony tie Wegner's North American work to indigenous global-South practice. Pulley does not have an analogous story; Sanders' story is Tampa/Ireland; Hirsch's is Australia.
5. **The 21,000-microchurch / 42,000-missionary horizon.** Most peers don't articulate a specific multiplication horizon. The named numbers are themselves a content asset.
6. **The "family of families" public restructure.** *Navigating Change* (October 2024) is unusually transparent self-correction. Most peers do not publish their own organizational drift in real time.
7. **The collaborative byline pattern.** 8 books, 7 different co-author groupings — more co-author breadth than most peers (most peers cluster around 1–3 recurring co-authors).

## Where Wegner is comparatively weaker

- **Less than Hirsch on theological / academic credibility.** Wegner is a practitioner; Hirsch is the theorist.
- **Less than Ford on consulting brand maturity.** Sentralized + Ford's authored books carry more independent author-platform weight than Wegner's solo footprint.
- **Less than Sanders on theological-architectural depth.** *Underground Church* is a more rigorous theological argument than *Starfish and the Spirit* on its own terms.
- **Less than Brisco on the bivocational / covocational lane.** Brisco owns that vocabulary in a way Wegner does not.
- **Less than Pulley on multicultural / power-redistribution voice.** Pulley's *Parable of the White Pastor* covers ground Wegner has not published on directly.
- **Less than Ferguson on multiplication-network institutional weight.** Ferguson runs Exponential (and now post-merger NewThing); Wegner is a principal but not the CEO.
- **Less than Halter on neighborhood / parish register.** Halter's incarnational vocabulary is sharper.

## How Movemental positions Wegner relative to adjacent voices

The strategic positioning that makes sense:

- **Within the master Movemental network**: Wegner is the **bridge node** — the leader who carries weight in both Cluster 1 (Hirsch / Forge / 100M) and Cluster 5 (Exponential / NewThing / Underground). Lean into the bridge framing.
- **Versus Sanders**: complementary, not competitive. Sanders is the theological-architectural voice; Wegner is the operational practitioner with a megachurch-conversion arc.
- **Versus Hirsch**: Wegner is the practitioner translation of Hirsch. They co-author together; the relationship is amplification, not competition.
- **Versus Ford**: co-host and co-author — same package. Movemental should onboard Wegner and Ford as a pair where possible.
- **Versus Brisco** (KC geographic overlap): adjacent lanes. Brisco owns covocational; Wegner owns microchurch operations. A coordinated KC content cluster (Brisco + Wegner) would be a unique Movemental asset.
- **Versus Pulley**: complementary, not competitive. Pulley adds the multicultural-microchurch-Tampa voice; Wegner adds the megachurch-conversion-KC voice. Hubology series with Ford is the natural collaboration thread.

The **biggest competitive risk** for Wegner is being lumped into "yet another microchurch leadership book author" rather than positioned distinctively. The personal arc (megachurch insider → India → microchurch founder), the operational scale of KCU, and the bridge position between two author clusters are the three things to lead with.
