## Themes (high-frequency)

The following themes appear across at least three Wegner surfaces (book, article, podcast, course):

### Tier 1 — defining themes (across books, podcasts, articles, KCU operations)

- **Microchurch as ecclesiology** — "an extended spiritual family led by ordinary people, not paid professionals." Defining frame across *Starfish and the Spirit*, *Extraordinary Prayer*, *Deeply Rooted*, KCU Podcast, Microchurch NEXT Podcast, every external interview.
- **Decentralized / distributed leadership (starfish)** — *The Starfish and the Spirit* (book + podcast + Substack), Hubology series, *Five Provocative Questions*. Ori Brafman is the secular intellectual scaffolding.
- **Disciple-making as the unit of work** — *Five Ingredients*, *Five Provocative Questions*, *Deeply Rooted*, KCU Missionary Pathway, BLESS Rhythms.
- **Ordinary people empowered as missionaries** — KCU founding mission ("a missionary on every street"); *Find Your Place*; *Extraordinary Prayer*; the recurring anti-celebrity-pastor frame.
- **Reproducibility / multiplication math** — 21,000 microchurches from 42,000 missionaries; 700 churches planted in 4 years (Westside era); 120,000 disciples in 950+ churches (India). The numbers are the size of the bet.

### Tier 2 — recurring themes

- **Missionary identity / formation** — *Extraordinary Prayer*, KCU Missionary Pathway, BLESS Rhythms, Verge Network "How to Train Leaders" article (2011). Wegner wants every disciple to think of themselves as a missionary in their own affinity network.
- **Reproducible gospel language** — KC Underground Podcast "Plant the Gospel: Reproducible Gospel Language" episode; the conviction that gospel articulation is itself a multiplication input.
- **Calling / personal mission discovery** — *Find Your Place* (book + 4-week Huddle + self-paced webinar + downloadable guide). Gifts × Passions × Story triangulated by the Spirit.
- **Habits / rule-of-life formation** — *Five Ingredients*; "Practice makes permanent" line.
- **Five social spaces of discipleship** — Public, Social, Personal, Transparent, Divine. Borrowed from Joseph Myers / Joe Saxton; surfaced in *Five Provocative Questions: Part Two*.
- **Megachurch-to-microchurch conversion** — Wegner's own personal arc. *Roys Report*, *Stetzer Leadership Podcast*, *Going From Megachurch to Microchurch*, *Starfish and the Spirit*.
- **Pandemic / crisis adaptability** — March 2020 Coronavirus article; the "mostly invulnerable" framing.
- **Family-of-families organizational health** — *Navigating Change* (October 2024); the public KCU restructuring narrative.

### Tier 3 — themes present but secondary

- **Indian disciple-making movement (DMM/DBS)** as a normative model — *Share the Well*; Discovery Bible Study practice in KCU.
- **The Five Hubs framework** — KCU operational architecture; appears in KCU staff page descriptions but not the headline of any book.
- **Servant-equipper / Hubology leadership** — joint with Lance Ford, Brian Johnson, Lucas Pulley; the *Starfish and the Church* Hubology series.
- **APEST and apostolic genius** — present via Hirsch co-authorship; not a Wegner-headline frame.
- **Spirit-empowered movement (pneumatology)** — the title difference in *Starfish and the Spirit*; named as the variable that distinguishes Christian from secular starfishes.
- **Coaching as a multiplication catalyst** — *Coaching is a Multiplication Catalyst* (Wendy McWherter, July 2024) is in the same Microchurch NEXT category Wegner edits; coaching is part of his framework but rarely the headline.
- **Ekklesia / linguistic clarity** — Josh Benadum article in same category; Wegner's relevant framing is the "extended spiritual family" definition.
- **Imitation / mimetic theory** — JR Woodward Microchurch NEXT article; not a Wegner-headline frame but adjacent.

## Themes underdeveloped or absent

- **Race / multicultural church power dynamics** — present in KCU's operational practice (multilingual microchurches; Movement Leaders Collective + Arrabon consulting on power redistribution per *Navigating Change*) but not a Wegner-headline theme. Compare to Lucas Pulley's *Parable of the White Pastor* — Wegner does not have an analogous published artifact.
- **Theological argument with academic register** — Wegner does not publish in seminary journals; the Andrews University review is *of* his work, not *by* him.
- **Specific geopolitical / cultural commentary** — Wegner stays close to ecclesial / leadership themes; he does not weigh in on broader cultural Christianity controversies in a sustained way.
- **Mental health / leadership burnout** — implicit but not explicit theme; Lance Ford addresses solo-heroic leadership critique more directly in his own work.
- **Women in leadership** — Michelle Wegner is an active KCU co-leader and *Share the Well* co-author; the broader theme is not a Wegner-headline frame. Worth flagging given Movemental's ecosystem-doctrine commitment to multi-voice inclusion.
- **Climate / creation care** — absent.
- **Christian nationalism / political Christianity** — absent.

## Strategic content positioning

Wegner's content is positioned to occupy **a specific lane**: practitioner-leadership, decentralized-microchurch, evangelical, multiplication-network, framework-driven, collaborative-byline. He is not competing with:

- Hirsch (more theological / academic; APEST + mDNA frames)
- Ford (more pastoral / leadership-burnout; UnLeader / With Me frames)
- Brian Sanders (more theological-architectural; Underground manifesto)
- Pulley (more ecclesial-minimum / multicultural; Parable of the White Pastor)
- Brisco (more covocational / NAMB-network; Forge KC)

He is **complementary to all of them**, which is why the network bridges work.
