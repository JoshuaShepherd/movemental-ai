## Framework

For each content domain, the gap analysis names three states:

- **Embodied** — what Wegner *knows* and practices (in his head, in conversation, in coaching, in operations)
- **Externalized** — what has been published / recorded / made retrievable on a public surface
- **Interconnected** — whether the externalized artifacts cross-link into a coherent corpus

The gap is the **delta between Embodied and Externalized**, multiplied by the **interconnection deficit** between externalized artifacts.

## Aggregate state

If we estimate the **totality of what Wegner knows** at 100 units:

- **Externalized** (publicly retrievable): ~20–25 units (8 books, ~20 articles, 3 podcasts hosted, 8+ guest appearances, ~10+ video appearances, ~8 programs)
- **Interconnected** (cross-linkable in a coherent corpus): ~5–8 units

This means **roughly 75–80% of what Wegner knows is not externalized** in any retrievable form, and **roughly 90–95% of what *is* externalized is not interconnected.**

For comparison, this is similar to but somewhat better than Lucas Pulley's profile (where ~5–10% is externalized and ~1–2% interconnected, per his fragmentation story). Wegner has **more public artifacts** than Pulley but **comparable interconnection deficit**.

## The biggest unrealized opportunities

In rough priority order (HIGH leverage, LOW friction first):

1. **Title coherence across all surfaces.** Replace "North American Director / NewThing" with current titles everywhere. Cheapest, highest-visibility hygiene move.
2. **Canonical bibliography page** with all 8 books in chronological order and themed by career era. Single page, single afternoon of work. Would meaningfully reset how Wegner is perceived (8-book author, not 1-book author).
3. **Personal newsletter** — seeded from Goodreads followers, Instagram, Substack co-subscribers, KCU partners, conference attendees. Biweekly or monthly, 500–900 words, with one curated link.
4. **Podcast appearances index** — a single page listing all guest appearances with dates, hosts, titles, links.
5. **Curated YouTube playlist** drawn from the 6+ host channels — with permission, surfaced as a "Rob Wegner Teaching Playlist" on the personal site.
6. **Resurrect or rebuild `robwegner.org`** with current titles, current bibliography, podcast catalog, and newsletter signup.
7. **Audio essays / narrated articles** of the top 5 essays (Five Ingredients, Five Provocative Questions parts 1+2, Coronavirus piece, Navigating Change) as a "Rob Wegner Essays" supplementary podcast feed.
8. **Negotiate access to Granger sermon archive** if it survives — potentially the single largest content unlock.
9. **Negotiate access to Westside teaching archive** similarly.
10. **First solo-authored book** — distilled from *Five Provocative Questions* + *Five Ingredients*, with KCU + India case studies. The first time Wegner's single voice is on a cover.

## Gaps that are not actually gaps

- **Co-author pattern**: This is theology, not a deficit. Don't try to "fix" it.
- **Anti-celebrity-pastor framing**: This is theology, not a deficit. The platform must hold it.
- **Practitioner not academic register**: This is the audience profile, not a gap.
- **No solo podcast**: This is theology again. Don't push for "The Rob Wegner Show."

The Movemental implication is that the work to be done is **architecture and discoverability**, not voice or output. Wegner has produced enough content for a coherent corpus; what's missing is the coherent corpus itself.
