## The Story

Rob Wegner spent nearly 25 years at Granger Community Church in Indiana before
moving to the Kansas City suburbs around 2015 knowing no one. From a starter
group of about 80 disciples in 2019, he founded Kansas City Underground (KCU),
a mission agency and decentralized microchurch network whose stated horizon is
**21,000 microchurches from 42,000 missionaries over 40 years**. While building
that, he picked up two more concurrent national-scale roles — Director of
Microchurch Next for **Exponential** and a seat on the Global Leadership Team
of **NewThing** — and co-authored **one** book, *The Starfish and the Spirit:
Unleashing the Leadership Potential of Churches and Organizations*, with Lance
Ford and Alan Hirsch. That is the part of his story that is searchable.

The rest is not. There is no `robwegner.com`. There is no Substack, no
personal newsletter, no Amazon author page that a reader can land on first.
His **one confirmed long-form article** — *The Rise of the Coronavirus and the
Microchurch* — sits on `exponential.org` with no return path to the book that
extends it, no return path to the KC Underground network it describes, no
return path to the podcast that has been working the same theme. His training
program, the **"Find Your Place" 4-session journey**, lives on
`kcunderground.org` as an organizational asset, not under a teacher byline.
The book lives on retailer sites; the podcast lives on the org site; the
articles live on the conference's site; the global leadership work lives on
NewThing's site (URL unverified); the speaking lives in Exponential conference
halls. Five sources, five ownership stacks, **zero** that route a stranger
through Rob Wegner's actual body of thought.

The deeper fragmentation is the part the research cannot see. Twenty-five
years at Granger Community Church is, in any reasonable estimate, **thousands
of hours of teaching, leading, framing, and decision-making** — and not one
sermon, talk, or memo from that era surfaces in this dossier. The KC
Underground Podcast has a known episode list of two
("Planting the Gospel — Reproducible Gospel Language" and "Upside Down
Kingdom: Choosing Obscurity Over Fame"); the actual count, transcript
archive, and topic taxonomy are unverified. Conference talks at Exponential
each year — almost certainly recorded — have not been catalogued here. His
reading, his coaching of microchurch planters, his Tuesday-night calls with
Michelle, the way he frames "missionary on every street" in a coffee
conversation: that is where the bulk of his thinking still lives, and it has
never been written down by him.

Rob Wegner's fragmentation is not the fragmentation of a 30-book author whose
work is scattered across 7 publishers. It is the fragmentation of a
**movement practitioner whose body of thought is mostly still inside other
people's containers** — Exponential's, NewThing's, KCU's, Granger's archive
(if it survives), and his own head. The book is the tip; the iceberg is not
yet public.
