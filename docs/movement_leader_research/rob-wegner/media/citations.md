## Citations within Wegner's own corpus

Wegner's *Starfish and the Spirit* (2021) explicitly draws on:

- **Ori Brafman & Rod Beckstrom**, *The Starfish and the Spider* (Penguin 2006) — foreword by Brafman in *Starfish and the Spirit*; the book's title and architecture are derivative
- **Alan Hirsch's APEST and mDNA** — co-author Hirsch contributes the missional-DNA frame
- **Joseph Myers / Joe Saxton's "five social spaces"** — used directly in *Five Provocative Questions: Part Two* (Exponential, December 2024)
- **Indian DMM / DBS practice** — *Share the Well* (with Michelle) is the specific case study

His Exponential bylines repeatedly reference *The Starfish and the Spirit* chapters 10–14 (the "five ingredients" content), suggesting he sees the book as the canonical source for his framework.

## Strategic implication

Wegner's work is **picked up unevenly**:

- **Deep within the church-multiplication ecosystem** (Exponential, Leadership Network, NewThing, Forge, Movement Leaders Collective) — heavy use, often unattributed
- **In the practitioner blog space** — moderate (a handful of named book reviewers)
- **In academic theology** — minimal (one Journal of Applied Christian Leadership review)
- **In secular organizational-theory** — surprisingly: at least one practitioner-consultant (Doug Kirkpatrick) has cited the *Starfish and the Church* podcast in a self-management context
- **In broader Christian press** (CT, RNS) — minimal (no surfaced citations)

A Movemental opportunity: **systematically tag the Wegner corpus** so that downstream uses (in Movemental's own knowledge graph and in third-party citations) trace back cleanly. The "It's not that the church has a mission..." line is one of the most-quoted-without-attribution lines in modern North American missional Christianity; making the source easy to cite is itself a service to the field.
