## Core thesis

**The church becomes itself when ordinary people carry the gospel into their actual networks of relationships — and that mission, not the institution, is the church's organizing principle.** A "missionary on every street and a microchurch in every network of relationships" is the operational shorthand. The starfish (decentralized, regenerative, distributed) is the organizational embodiment. The Spirit is the difference-maker that distinguishes Christian movements from the secular starfishes Brafman documented.

His shortest summary, lifted from *Missional Moves* (Zondervan, 2012):

> "It's not that the church has a mission. It's that a mission has the church."

## Theological tradition

**Primary**: **Missional-incarnational ecclesiology** in the Hirsch–Frost–Forge stream, applied to a North American multiplication context. Wegner came up inside non-denominational evangelical megachurch culture (Granger Community Church, ~21 years; Westside Family Church, ~4 years) but his ecclesial conviction now sits firmly outside attractional megachurch logic. He is not denominationally bound; he keynoted the **United Brethren in Christ National Conference 2025** ([ub.org/events/nc25](https://ub.org/events/nc25/)) but is not himself UB. The KCU formation, Microchurch NEXT branding, and *Starfish and the Spirit* book all read as **post-megachurch missional evangelicalism** — non-Reformed, non-mainline, charismatic-friendly but not Pentecostal-distinctive.

**Secondary influences**:

- **Indian disciple-making movement (DMM/DBS)** — Discovery Bible Study, oral-tradition disciple-making, household-of-peace evangelism. *Share the Well* (with Michelle Wegner) documents the Granger-India movement; the Indian fingerprint shows up in KCU's 120+ DBS launches and the "missionary pathway" framing.
- **Ori Brafman's starfish/spider organizational theory** — explicit foreword on *Starfish and the Spirit*. Brafman is Wegner's primary non-Christian organizational thinker.
- **Alan Hirsch's apostolic-genius / mDNA / APEST framework** — Hirsch wrote the foreword to *Missional Moves* (2012), co-authored *Starfish and the Spirit* (2021), co-founded Future Travelers with Wegner. The longest-running theological influence in Wegner's corpus.
- **Ralph Moore / the Hope Chapel multiplication tradition** — Wegner has co-presented with Moore in the YouTube panel "The Rise of the Microchurch" (with Daniel Yang).
- **Dave Ferguson's BLESS rhythms** — Wegner endorsed *BLESS* and embedded BLESS rhythms inside the KCU Missionary Pathway and Exponential's BLESS Rhythms video series.
- **Ed Stetzer / Outreach / multiplication-research evangelicalism** — Wegner was a guest on the *Stetzer Leadership Podcast* with Ford, and bylines in Outreach Magazine multiple times.
- **Brian Sanders' Underground Network paradigm** — KCU explicitly takes the "Underground" naming convention; Sanders has appeared on *Starfish and the Church*; the city-movement family lineage is Sanders → Tampa Underground → KCU.

## Distinctive positions

- **The church is not an event attended; it is a missionary network embodied.** This is the through-line from *Missional Moves* (2012) to *Deeply Rooted* (2025). The church is what happens when ordinary people carry the gospel into the relational webs they already have.
- **Ordinary people, not paid professionals, lead the church.** The microchurch definition is structurally anti-clergy: "extended spiritual family led by ordinary people, not paid professionals" (Wegner, 2019, *The Church Digital Podcast* #033).
- **Decentralization is not a tactic; it is closer to the New Testament grain.** Wegner argues the early Christian movement looked like a starfish (multiple centers, distributed authority, regenerative when attacked) and that twenty centuries of centralization has misshapen the church. The Spirit empowers the starfish; that is the title of his book.
- **Megachurch leadership and microchurch leadership are different vocations.** Wegner's Roys Report quote — "we have better oversight in our decentralized network than in most mega churches, and I can speak from experience" — is the most direct statement of his post-megachurch conviction.
- **Definitions matter ecclesially.** The five-provocative-questions framework starts with vision: "What is your definition of a disciple?" His critique of the American church is that it cannot multiply because it cannot define what it is multiplying.
- **Reproducible gospel language is a multiplication input.** Whether the gospel can be retold by an ordinary person within their own affinity network determines whether it travels at all. This is borrowed from Indian DMM practice but framed for the American suburb.
- **Practice over information.** "Practice makes permanent. Habit formation and rule-of-life is key in transformative disciplemaking environments" (*Five Ingredients*, May 2024). Wegner is suspicious of teaching-only models.
- **Pandemic and economic disruption favor microchurches.** The March 2020 "Rise of the Coronavirus and the Microchurch" article positioned KCU as "mostly invulnerable" to disruption — one of the more forceful early-pandemic microchurch claims in print.
- **Family-of-families over founder-of-organization.** The 2024 KCU restructuring is theologically charged — Wegner publicly named that the founder team had drifted into telling rather than inviting and engaged Movement Leaders Collective + Arrabon for three-year consulting on power redistribution. This is unusually transparent for a North American church-network founder.
- **India is normative, not exotic.** *Share the Well* and his repeated reference to the Indian movement positions DMM/DBS as a real model for North America, not as a far-away exception. Wegner consistently de-exoticizes the global South.
- **The Spirit, not the structure, is the multiplier.** His pushback on Brafman/Brafman-style organizational theory is that the spider/starfish framing is incomplete without pneumatology — hence the title's emphasis on *the Spirit*.

## Theological evolution

Wegner's voice has a clear arc from **inside the megachurch pushing missional** (Granger / *Missional Moves* / Future Travelers / India trips, ~2008–2014) → **transitional teaching pastor** at Westside Family Church (~2015–2019) → **founder of a decentralized microchurch network** (KCU, 2019 →) → **co-author of the keynote starfish-leadership book** (*Starfish and the Spirit*, 2021) → **public framer of the disciple-making ecosystem** (*Five Provocative Questions* + *Five Ingredients*, 2024) → **family-of-families restructure-and-confess publisher** (*Navigating Change*, October 2024).

The theology underneath is remarkably consistent — mission has the church; ordinary people lead; the Spirit empowers a distributed organism — but his vocabulary has thickened over time. The early *Missional Moves* book was tectonic-shift / paradigm-shift language. The middle *Made for More* / *Find Your Place* era is calling-and-mobilization language. The current era is starfish / hubology / disciple-making-ecosystem language. The same convictions; richer scaffolding.

## Where his voice is distinctive within that ecosystem

- **Megachurch-to-microchurch personal arc** — many missional voices have always been outsiders to attractional megachurch; Wegner spent 25 years inside it. That gives him credibility with both audiences.
- **Multi-co-author publishing pattern** — eight books, seven co-author groupings. He almost never publishes alone. Theologically this is consistent with his anti-celebrity-pastor frame.
- **India-shaped multiplication math** — his comfort with 21,000-microchurch / 42,000-missionary horizons is not common in North American evangelical leadership. The numbers come from Indian and African DMM practice.
- **Hubology / servant-equipper vocabulary** — Wegner is one of three or four people creating this specific language, alongside Ford, Brian Johnson, and Pulley. It is a distinctively decentralized-leadership vocabulary that the broader Exponential / NewThing world does not yet share.
- **Family-of-families self-correction** — publicly engaging Movement Leaders Collective and Arrabon to address founder-team drift toward control is unusually transparent for a North American church-network principal.
- **Operates inside both Cluster 1 and Cluster 5** — his bridge position between the Hirsch / Forge / 100M wing and the Exponential / NewThing / Underground wing is his single most strategically valuable theological feature.
