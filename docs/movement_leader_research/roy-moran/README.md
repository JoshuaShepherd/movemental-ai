## Executive Summary

Roy Moran is a **rare practitioner-bridge** in the North American missional landscape — a classically trained, Dallas Theological Seminary-educated pastor who planted a 1,000-person attractional church in 1992 and then walked his own congregation through a multi-year, public conversion to Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) practice. His distinctive contribution is the **Hybrid Church** framework: an integrated "come and go" strategy that holds attractional Sunday gatherings *and* multiplicative DMM practice (Discovery Bible Study, obedience-based discipleship, four-generation reproduction) in the same local-church body, rather than treating them as rival paradigms.

His credibility is built on **embodied case study** rather than theory. Shoal Creek Community Church (Kansas City, MO) — co-founded with his wife Candy and four others — is the longest-running, best-documented hybrid experiment in the U.S. *Spent Matches* (Thomas Nelson, 2015) tells that story; *Hybrid Church* (DMM Press, 2017) systematizes it for other senior leaders. Both books occupy a niche almost no one else occupies: DMM written *from inside* the Western attractional church, by someone who has actually pastored both modes in the same building.

His current footprint is global, not just local. As **Global Team Leader, Platform** at New Generations — the org founded by Jerry Trousdale that has documented millions of disciples and hundreds of thousands of churches emerging in the Global South — and as Executive Director of Mercy Alliance International, Moran sits at the convergence of (a) Western pastoral credibility, (b) Global South movement data, and (c) the David Watson / CityTeam / Beyond DMM lineage that mentored him. His weekly *Movement Conversations* podcast interviews global DMM practitioners and is one of only a handful of sustained DMM media properties in the English-speaking world.

The **strategic gap**: despite ~13 years of digital output (roymoran.com, podcast, conference talks, two books), Moran has no newsletter, no flagship course, no owned community, no aggregated video library, and no audiobook of *Spent Matches*. His expertise is distributed across at least 8 platforms he does not own. He is precisely the kind of practitioner Movemental's infrastructure is designed to consolidate: a credentialed Western pastor with a globally-validated framework, a clear convictional center, and a digital footprint that under-represents his actual influence.

## Top 5 Opportunities

1. **Produce a human-narrated audiobook of *Spent Matches*.** The 2024 *Hybrid Church* audiobook is AI-narrated (Audible "Virtual Voice"). *Spent Matches* — the trade-published, higher-equity title — has no audiobook at all. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort)

2. **Launch a flagship cohort/course: "Hybrid Church Lab."** Mercy Alliance already runs informal training. A structured 8-week cohort productizes 25 years of pastoral experience and the global DMM data flowing through New Generations — a niche no one else owns. (HIGH priority, MEDIUM effort)

3. **Capture an owned audience.** No newsletter, no email list. The *Movement Conversations* podcast, roymoran.com blog, and conference talks all funnel into platforms he doesn't control. Lead magnet: "Read *Spent Matches* in 20 Minutes" (a page already exists on his site as a giveaway). (HIGH priority, LOW effort)

4. **Consolidate the *Movement Conversations* archive.** Episodes are distributed across Apple/Spotify/Audible/Stitcher/iHeart/Deezer with no canonical web index. Single searchable hub by topic (indigenous incarnation, person of peace, supernatural manifestations, obedience-based discipleship). (MEDIUM priority, LOW effort)

5. **Document the Shoal Creek case study formally.** The 25-year hybrid experiment is referenced everywhere but never written up as a longitudinal case study with metrics (baptisms, DBS groups, multiplication depth, attendance vs. dispersion). This is the single most valuable asset Moran has. (HIGH priority, HIGH effort)

## Movemental Recommendation

**Fit Score**: 8.6/10
**Recommendation**: **Onboard — high-priority candidate**

Roy Moran scores higher than most candidates because he holds three properties simultaneously that are otherwise rare: (1) **Western institutional credibility** (DTS, 25-year senior pastor, megachurch-adjacent), (2) **Global South movement integration** (New Generations leadership, mentored by David Watson, weekly access to global DMM data), and (3) **a published framework** (Hybrid Church) that resolves the false choice between attractional and missional in a way North American pastors can actually act on.

The Movemental thesis — *fragmentation is solved by infrastructure, not new content* — describes Moran's situation precisely. He already has the content, the network, and the conviction. What he lacks is the owned platform, the course architecture, and the longitudinal data capture. Those are exactly the gaps Movemental fills.

The "trusted voices" ecosystem layer compounds quickly through him.
