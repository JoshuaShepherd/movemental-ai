## 💡 Strategic Insight: A Two-Continent Audience

**Position**: Roy Moran's audience is unusual because it sits across a normally-uncrossable line — U.S. seminary-trained pastors *and* global obedience-based DMM practitioners — held together by his unique credibility in both worlds.

**Why this matters**: Most missional voices reach pastors *or* practitioners but not both. Moran can preach at iDisciple to attractional megachurch leaders on Tuesday and interview a Nepali DMM catalyst on Wednesday and both audiences perceive him as one of their own.

**Implication for content strategy**: Movemental should not collapse these audiences into one funnel. They want different language, different formats, different proof points. But Moran is the rare leader through whom either segment can be invited into the other's frame.

## Primary Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Convicted Senior Pastors of Established Churches (45%)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Convicted Senior Pastors                                    │
│ % of Total: 45% | Engagement: ⭐⭐⭐ HIGH                     │
│                                                             │
│ 🎯 Core Need: An on-ramp from attractional to multiplicative│
│    without abandoning the existing congregation              │
│ 💰 Willingness to Pay: $200–$1,500 for cohort/course        │
│ 📱 Preferred Channel: Books → podcast → cohort              │
│ 📚 Content Preference: Story-driven, first-person, with     │
│    practical "how do I do this Monday morning" specificity  │
│                                                             │
│ ⚠️ Gap: Need a structured 8–12 week cohort with peer        │
│    pastor community — currently no flagship exists          │
│ 💡 Opportunity: Hybrid Church Lab cohort + peer mastermind  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Characteristics**:
- 40–65 years old, predominantly male, seminary-trained
- Pastoring 250–2,500 attendees, often founder or 10+ year tenured
- Theologically evangelical; not anti-institutional but increasingly aware the attractional model is fragile
- Have read Frost/Hirsch, maybe Stetzer, but find purely missional theology too theoretical
- The "spent matches" feeling — they've burned their best ideas and the numbers still don't translate to disciples

**How Roy Serves This Segment**: He is the older, slightly-ahead colleague who walked the same path inside the same kind of church and has a published map. *Spent Matches* is the entry door; the Hybrid Church framework is the operating manual.

**Content Priorities**:
1. First-person pastoral narrative (the "existential moment")
2. Sequenced multi-year transition pattern (year 1: language; year 2: structures; year 3: metrics)
3. Peer testimony from other pastors who made the shift
