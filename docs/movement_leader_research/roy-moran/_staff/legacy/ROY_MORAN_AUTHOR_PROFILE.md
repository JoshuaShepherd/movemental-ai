## Core Frameworks & Concepts

### 1. The Hybrid Church Framework

**Definition**: An integrated local-church strategy that holds two modes simultaneously — an *attractional* Sunday gathering *and* a *multiplicative* DMM track — in the same body, rather than treating them as rival paradigms or sequential phases.

**Metaphor**: The hybrid car — two engines, one vehicle, switching modes by terrain.

**Two expressions**:
1. **Gathering (Attractional)**: Sunday services built around creative storytelling and cultural touchpoints; designed for the ~30% of Americans open to attending a church.
2. **Network (Multiplicative)**: Reproducible disciple-making practices deployed through neighborhoods, workplaces, schools, and prisons; designed for the ~70% who will never come on a Sunday.

**Key principles** (drawn from *Spent Matches* and *Hybrid Church*):
- **Clarity matters** — language consistency prevents vision leakage
- **Obedience over information** — "I will" statements and accountability create transformation
- **Progress visibility** — regular reporting builds momentum and integrity
- **Community precedes conversion** — relationships first, gospel planting after
- **Sunday as launchpad, not destination** — "what you win people *with* is what you win them *to*"

**Key works**: *Spent Matches* (2015), *Hybrid Church* (2017), Trout Lake Hybrid Church Consultation (ongoing training event)

---

### 2. Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) Practice — Western Adaptation

**Definition**: Obedience-based, reproducible discipleship aimed at four generations of multiplication, applied in the North American context rather than only in the unreached-people-group contexts where DMM originated.

**Core practices Moran uses**:
- **Discovery Bible Study (DBS)** — a simple, reproducible inductive group method (read, retell, observe, obey, share)
- **Obedience-based metrics** — measure "I will" statements followed by next-meeting accountability, not attendance
- **"Person of peace"** discernment — Luke 10 framing for whom to start with
- **Four-generation reproduction** as the test of whether real multiplication is occurring

**Key works**: weekly *Movement Conversations* episodes (indigenous incarnation, supernatural manifestations, family-unit targeting, person of peace, unconventional leadership)

---

### 3. Spiritual Literacy

**Definition**: Moran's term for the formational outcome the church is supposed to produce — disciples who can read, interpret, and obey Scripture, then reproduce that capacity in others without professional clergy mediation.

**Why this language**: It reframes the win-condition. The Sunday gathering is no longer measured by attendance; it is measured by whether attendees become *literate disciples* who can lead others.

**Strategic role**: This is his current title at Shoal Creek — "directional leader and spiritual literacy advocate" — a deliberate post-pastoral framing.
