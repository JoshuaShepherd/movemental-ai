## Calling Statement (Synthesized)

> Roy Moran is called to **catalyze Disciple-Making Movements inside Western, place-based, attractional churches** — translating the obedience-based, reproducible practice that has produced movements across Africa, Asia, and Latin America into the language, structures, and pastoral leadership of the North American senior pastor. His calling is a *bridge calling*: not to plant the next great Sunday gathering and not to abandon the local church for pure movement work, but to hold the two together until the wider Western church can do the same.

**Core calling elements**:
- **Identity**: Practitioner-pastor (not academic, not full-time missionary)
- **Aim**: Spiritual literacy that reproduces without professional clergy mediation
- **Theater of operation**: The established U.S. congregation
- **Animating conviction**: *The disciples we are making are not making disciples* — and that is fixable.

## 💡 Strategic Insight: The Practitioner-Bridge mDNA Profile

Moran's mDNA profile is **shaped like a practitioner**, not like a theorist. Disciple-Making, Organic Systems, and Missional-Incarnational all score in the very-high band because his ministry has been operationally organized around them for over a decade. The lower (still strong) bands on Apostolic Environment and Liminality reflect that he leads from inside an established institution, not as a pioneer of new orgs — though New Generations and Mercy Alliance now extend that apostolic footprint.

**Strategic implication**: When Movemental positions Roy, the dominant frame should be **Disciple-Making + Organic Systems** in *Western* contexts. That is his uncontested niche.
