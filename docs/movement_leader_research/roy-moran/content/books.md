# Books

### Spent Matches: Igniting the Signal Fire for the Spiritually Dissatisfied

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Published | 2015 |
| Publisher | Thomas Nelson (Refraction) |
| Format | Paperback, ebook |
| Status | Active, in-print |

**Description**: Moran's flagship, narrative-driven account of the journey from an attractional Willow Creek–style congregation to a hybrid church that holds attractional gathering and multiplicative Disciple-Making Movement practice together. Written for the spiritually dissatisfied — both the disengaged seeker and the senior pastor who senses the attendance metric is hollow — it tells the story of the mid-2000s crisis when Moran admitted the disciples his church made were not making disciples. It introduces the "signal fire" and "spent matches" imagery and lays out his core convictions: obedience over information, community before conversion, and Sunday as launchpad rather than destination.

### Hybrid Church: How the local church can engage disciple-making movements strategy

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Published | 2017 |
| Publisher | DMM Press |
| Format | Kindle; 2024 AI-narrated audiobook (Audible Virtual Voice) |
| Status | Active |

**Description**: The systematic how-to companion to *Spent Matches*, aimed at senior leaders ready to act. It develops the Hybrid Church framework — the hybrid-car model of running an attractional Sunday gathering and a reproducible DMM network inside one congregation, switching modes by terrain. It walks through the practices a Western pastor needs to begin: Discovery Bible Study, obedience-based metrics, "person of peace" discernment, and four-generation reproduction as the test of genuine multiplication. A 2024 AI-narrated audiobook edition was released via Audible Virtual Voice.
