# Organizations

Roy Moran's organizational life spans a local congregation, a movement-training hub he founded, and a global Disciple-Making Movement network — the three together hold his attractional, training, and movement-data work.

## Shoal Creek Community Church

Moran co-founded Shoal Creek Community Church in the Kansas City metropolitan area in 1992 with his wife Candy and four others, planting it on the Willow Creek attractional template. It grew to more than 1,000 attendees and became the longest-running U.S. case study of a hybrid attractional-plus-multiplicative congregation. He served as senior pastor for roughly twenty-five years and now holds the role of directional leader and "spiritual literacy advocate."

## Mercy Alliance International

Moran is the founder and Executive Director of Mercy Alliance International, a Kansas City–based hub for Disciple-Making Movement training, hybrid coaching, and regional coordination. Its tagline is "Catalyzing Disciple-Making Movements," and it emphasizes the hybrid "come and go" transition for established Western churches. It is where his informal training cohorts and digital resources currently live.

## New Generations

Moran serves as Global Team Leader, Platform at New Generations, the network led by Jerry Trousdale that documents disciples and churches across Africa, Asia, and Latin America. He joined its leadership around 2017. The role gives him weekly access to global DMM data and connects him to the broader Beyond / New Generations / 24:14 Coalition constellation.

## Board memberships and lineage

Moran has served on the boards of Beyond (formerly CityTeam International), the David Watson organization from which his DMM practice descends; New Generations International; the Institute of International Medicine (INMED); and ReEngage Staffing Services. His DMM lineage runs through David Watson (mentor) and connects him to peers and co-laborers including Jerry Trousdale, Curtis Sergeant, Doug Cobb, Stan Parks, and Mike Moran.
