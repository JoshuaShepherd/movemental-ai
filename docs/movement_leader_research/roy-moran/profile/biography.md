# Roy Moran

Roy Moran is the rare Western voice trusted simultaneously by classically trained senior pastors and by global Disciple-Making Movement practitioners, because he is both. He co-founded Shoal Creek Community Church in the Kansas City metropolitan area in 1992 with his wife Candy and a handful of others, building it on the Willow Creek attractional template, and served as its senior pastor for roughly twenty-five years. Trained in Greek studies at Baylor University and credentialed with a Th.M. from Dallas Theological Seminary, he came to a decisive crisis in the mid-2000s when he was finally willing to admit out loud that the disciples his church was making were not making disciples. That recognition pulled him into a long mentorship under David Watson, the Disciple-Making Movement pioneer behind CityTeam International (now Beyond), and into the global DMM lineage. Rather than abandon his congregation, Moran ran an obedience-based DMM track inside the same body that held his attractional Sunday gathering, then wrote the playbook for doing it. His two books, *Spent Matches* (Thomas Nelson, 2015) and *Hybrid Church* (DMM Press, 2017), articulate that integrated "come and go" model. Today he serves as Global Team Leader, Platform at New Generations, the Jerry Trousdale–led network documenting disciples and churches across Africa, Asia, and Latin America; as founder and Executive Director of Mercy Alliance International, a Kansas City hub for DMM training; and as host of the weekly *Movement Conversations* podcast. He remains Shoal Creek's directional leader and "spiritual literacy advocate."

## Early life and formation

Moran graduated from Radford High School in 1972 and went on to study Greek at Baylor University, an evangelical-Baptist formation that grounded him in the biblical languages. He completed a Th.M. at Dallas Theological Seminary around 1980, giving him the classical Western seminary credentialing that later made his turn toward DMM credible to an audience of seminary-trained pastors.

## Shoal Creek Community Church

In 1992, Moran co-founded Shoal Creek Community Church in Kansas City, Missouri, with his wife Candy and four others, planting it on the Willow Creek seeker-sensitive model. The church grew to more than 1,000 attendees. Over twenty-five years it became the longest-running U.S. case study of a hybrid attractional-plus-multiplicative congregation. Moran has since transitioned from senior pastor into a "directional leader and spiritual literacy advocate" role, a deliberately post-pastoral framing of his ongoing relationship to the body he founded.

## Disciple-Making Movements

Moran's mid-2000s realization that his disciples were not reproducing drew him into Disciple-Making Movement practice under David Watson's mentorship around 2008–2014. He adapted DMM — obedience-based, reproducible discipleship aimed at four generations of multiplication — for the North American context rather than treating it as foreign-mission theory. Core practices he teaches include Discovery Bible Study (read, retell, observe, obey, share), obedience-based metrics built on "I will" statements and accountability, and "person of peace" discernment drawn from Luke 10.

## The Hybrid Church framework

His signature contribution is the Hybrid Church framework: an integrated local-church strategy that holds an attractional Sunday gathering and a multiplicative DMM track inside the same body, switching modes by terrain like a hybrid car. The gathering is built for the minority of Americans open to attending a church; the multiplicative network is built for the majority who will never come on a Sunday. He summarizes its load-bearing convictions as clarity of language, obedience over information, visible progress, community before conversion, and Sunday as launchpad rather than destination.

## New Generations and Mercy Alliance

Around 2017 Moran joined the leadership of New Generations, where he now serves as Global Team Leader, Platform — connecting him weekly to global DMM data through colleagues including Jerry Trousdale. He also founded and directs Mercy Alliance International, a Kansas City–based hub for DMM training, hybrid coaching, and regional coordination. He has served on the boards of Beyond (formerly CityTeam International), New Generations International, the Institute of International Medicine (INMED), and ReEngage Staffing Services.

## Media and ongoing work

Since 2020, Moran has hosted the weekly *Movement Conversations — Powered by New Generations* podcast, one of only a handful of sustained DMM media properties in the English-speaking world, featuring interviews with global movement catalysts. He continues to write at roymoran.com, where his archive spans Disciple-Making Movements, Hybrid Church, DBS practice, and lessons from twenty-five years at Shoal Creek.
