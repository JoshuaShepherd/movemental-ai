# Voice Analysis

Roy Moran writes and speaks as a practitioner-pastor, not an academic. His prose is first-person, candid, and story-led, built around vivid metaphor and a refusal of triumphalism. He admits what did not work, frames DMM as adaptive rather than magic, and speaks more from his own congregation than from aggregate statistics.

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tone | warm, prophetic, pastoral |
| Register | accessible practitioner / pastoral |
| Posture | reforming, not abandoning; convicted, not anti-institutional |
| Theological frame | evangelical, Great Commission–centric, Christology-grounded |
| Stance toward metrics | modest and embodied; "I will" obedience over attendance |

**Recurring phrases**:

- "disciple-making movements"
- "person of peace"
- "obedience-based discipleship"
- "spiritual literacy"
- "obedience over information"
- "four generations"
- "the disciples we are making are not making disciples"
- "come and go"
- "reproducible" and "multiplicative"

**Favorite metaphors**:

- **signal fire**
- **spent matches**
- **the hybrid car** (two engines, one vehicle, switching modes by terrain)
- **launchpad** (Sunday as launchpad, not destination)
- **beachhead**

**Failure-mode guard**: Moran consistently avoids triumphalism and "burn it down" rhetoric. He critiques the attractional model while staying inside it, acknowledges what didn't work, and resists framing DMM as a step-by-step program — "I'm not selling a step-by-step program but a fresh application."
