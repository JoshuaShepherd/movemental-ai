## 💡 Strategic Insight: The Hybrid Bridge

**Position**: Roy Moran uniquely bridges the classical Western pastoral establishment (Dallas Theological Seminary, 25-year senior pastor of a 1,000+ attendee attractional church) with the global Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) world (mentored by David Watson; current Global Team Leader at New Generations).

**Why this matters**: Almost every other DMM voice in North America comes from one of two camps — Global South missions practitioners (whose credibility doesn't transfer to suburban U.S. pastors) or post-evangelical missional theorists (whose practice doesn't transfer to existing congregations). Moran is the rare third option: a senior pastor who walked his own established church through the conversion and lived to write the field manual.

**Strategic implication**: He is uniquely credible to two audiences who normally don't share leaders — the seminary-trained suburban pastor *and* the obedience-based DMM practitioner. His Hybrid Church framework is the only widely-published model that gives a Western senior leader an actual on-ramp into DMM without requiring them to abandon their existing congregation.

## Three-Sentence Bio (for citations and intros)

> Roy Moran is the co-founder and directional leader of Shoal Creek Community Church in Kansas City, where he served as senior pastor for 25 years before transitioning to Global Team Leader, Platform at New Generations. Trained at Baylor and Dallas Theological Seminary and originally shaped by the Willow Creek attractional model, Moran's decisive turn came when he realized his disciples weren't making disciples — a recognition that drew him into Disciple-Making Movement (DMM) practice under the mentorship of David Watson. He is the author of *Spent Matches* (Thomas Nelson, 2015) and *Hybrid Church* (DMM Press, 2017), hosts the weekly *Movement Conversations* podcast, and serves as Executive Director of Mercy Alliance International.
