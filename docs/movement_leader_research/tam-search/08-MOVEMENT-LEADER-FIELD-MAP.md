## 3. CLUSTER MAP

The field organizes into 7 natural clusters based on connection density, shared organizations, and thematic overlap. Most leaders belong to one primary cluster but bridge to others.

### Cluster 1: THE HIRSCH CORE (mDNA / APEST / Movement Theology)
**Hub**: Alan Hirsch
**Binding orgs**: Forge, 5Q Collective, MLC, 100Movements, Future Travelers
**Members**: Hirsch, Debra Hirsch, Rich Robinson, Tim Catchim, Jessie Cruickshank, Mark Nelson, Rob Kelly, Brandon Kelly, Mark Cotterill, Kim Hammond, Darren Cronshaw, Darryn Altclass, Emanuel Prinz
**Character**: The theoretical engine. Produces frameworks (mDNA, APEST/5Q, communitas, liminality). Defines vocabulary. Trains trainers.

**Hub**: Dave Ferguson
**Binding orgs**: Exponential, NewThing (now merged), Send Network/NAMB

## 5. FIT RUBRIC: MOVEMENT LEADER CANDIDACY

A sharpened evaluative rubric. Not a replacement for the full 100-point scoring rubric in `03-RUBRIC.md` — this is a faster triage tool for initial assessment.

### The 5 Markers (each scored 0-3)

**M1: Body of Work** (0-3)
- 0: No published works or durable frameworks
- 1: Active content (blog, podcast, articles) but no books/courses
- 2: 1-2 published books OR significant training curriculum
- 3: Multiple books, named frameworks, or a movement's foundational texts

**M2: Movemental Orientation** (0-3)
- 0: Attractional/institutional/consumer logic
- 1: Uses some movement vocabulary but institutional in practice
- 2: Clear missional/multiplication/formation orientation in content and practice
- 3: Has catalyzed actual multiplication (people who make people who make people)

**M3: Distributed Influence** (0-3)
- 0: Influence limited to one local context
- 1: Regional recognition or single-network influence
- 2: Multi-network recognition, invited speaker, cited by peers
- 3: International influence, widely endorsed, shapes the conversation

**M4: Conceptual Contribution** (0-3)
- 0: Applies others' frameworks without original contribution
- 1: Distinctive voice/perspective within existing frameworks
- 2: Has named or developed frameworks used by others
- 3: Has introduced vocabulary, models, or practices that define a stream (e.g., mDNA, BELLS, LifeShapes)

**M5: Peer Adjacency** (0-3)
- 0: No overlap with seed network
- 1: Shares a publisher or has appeared at one shared conference
- 2: Co-speaker, mutual endorser, or connected through 2+ shared orgs
- 3: Co-author, co-founder, or foreword relationship with a Core member

### Scoring

| Score | Verdict | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 13-15 | **Strong fit** | Full profile + scoring + reflected understanding |
| 10-12 | **Probable fit** | Profile + scoring |
| 7-9 | **Worth investigating** | Quick profile, check for hidden strength |
| 4-6 | **Weak fit** | Monitor only |
| 0-3 | **Does not fit** | Remove |

## 7. PATTERNS AND STRATEGIC OBSERVATIONS

### 1. The ecosystem has a clear center-periphery structure
Alan Hirsch is the gravitational center. Every cluster connects back to him through at most 2 hops (usually 1). This is both a strength (coherent scenius) and a risk (single point of dependency). The Movemental platform needs to make the network legible as a NETWORK, not as "Alan Hirsch and friends."
