## Executive Summary

Tania Harris's audience is a **charismatic-core, evangelical-curious, formation-hungry** set — believers who want to hear God for themselves, plus the church leaders who want to build communities where everyone can. The defining audience facts are two: first, her reach is **modest and niche but unusually well-monetized** — she has a genuine free→email→course→church-licence funnel that converts attention into owned relationships and revenue, which most leaders in the research set lack. Second, her audience skews **AU/UK/NZ** (Australian base, strong UK/European endorser and broadcast footprint, NZ pricing presence) and **female-leaning** (single-woman church planter, egalitarian exemplar, Woman Alive / CBE / Junia Project surfaces). The owned-audience layer is small (IG ~2.8K, ministry FB ~3.9K, LinkedIn 2K+, podcast 106 eps at 5.0/29 ratings, Goodreads <100 ratings total) and partly invisible (godconversations.com returns HTTP 403 to bots; YouTube/X/newsletter metrics UNKNOWN). This profile maps the segments, content consumption, unmet needs, and distribution strategy to *grow* a modest but deeply engaged audience without cannibalizing the existing paid funnel.

## Market Framing

### Market framing

Harris's addressable market is the **global charismatic/Pentecostal-plus-curious-evangelical formation world** — narrower than a mass-trade-devotional market, but deeper and more loyal, and crucially with a **working monetization path** already in place.

- **Believers hungry to hear God**: a very large latent market (hearing-God / discernment is a perennial best-selling devotional category — Willard, Shirer, Virkler), but Harris's *engaged* subset is niche.
- **Charismatic/Pentecostal core + broader evangelical curious**: her doctrine of "phenomenological equivalency" and Christocentric testing makes her unusually portable beyond the Pentecostal base — she is read by cautious evangelicals who want continuationism with guardrails.
- **Church leaders building hearing communities**: the cohort/licence buyers (smaller, higher-value).
- **Women in ministry**: a distinct, loyal, values-aligned tail (egalitarian exemplar).

## Primary Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Believers hungry to hear God (~35% — core audience)

**Who**: everyday Christians — charismatic/Pentecostal core, plus curious evangelicals — who want to hear God for themselves and aren't sure how, or worry they're "not the type."
**Where they find her**: *God Conversations* (book); the podcast; the free 7-day devotional; Hope 103.2 "Everyday God Conversations"; Premier Christianity.
**Why they engage**: she insists hearing God is "a normal part of every Christian's life," not for "the wise and the weird" — democratizing, warm, story-first, and testable.
**What they want more of**: practical "how do I know it's God?" guidance, real God-stories ("show, don't tell"), and reassurance via the Three Tests.

### Segment 2: Charismatic/Pentecostal practitioners seeking rigour (~25% — depth audience)

**Who**: ACC/AOG and broader charismatic believers and emerging leaders who love the experience but want it grounded — and cautious continuationists who need guardrails.
**Where they find her**: the books (esp. the Brill monograph for the serious end); the podcast (Cartledge, Yong guests); ACC/Hillsong orbit; Regents TC (UK).
**Why they engage**: "the confidence to know we can hear from God + the humility to know we can get it wrong" — rigour and experience held together, Scripture as the test, never bypassed.
**What they want more of**: theological grounding made accessible; the phenomenological-equivalency case; engagement with the cessationist objection (cf. the Premier Unbelievable? debate).

### Segment 3: Church leaders building hearing communities (~20% — pipeline/revenue audience)

**Who**: pastors and ministry leaders who want their *whole congregation* to discern the Spirit together, across traditions.
**Where they find her**: *The Church Who Hears God's Voice*; the "50 Days" church licence; the Pastors & Leaders 7-week Zoom cohort; PWYC leadership coaching; the Leaders' Resources hub.
**Why they engage**: she offers a transferable, packaged ecclesial model (videos + study guide + leadership training + launch event) with communal accountability built in ("God speaks twice").
**What they want more of**: turnkey, deployable curriculum; cohort support; cross-tradition adaptability.

### Segment 4: Women in ministry (~12% — values-aligned audience)

**Who**: women in or aspiring to ministry leadership, especially in egalitarian-affirming charismatic/evangelical contexts.
**Where they find her**: CBE *Mutuality*; The Junia Project; Woman Alive (UK); Hope 103.2 singles content; her own example as a single-woman church planter and former Academic Dean.
**Why they engage**: she is a credible egalitarian exemplar — ordained, PhD'd, planted a church alone out of a context "where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag."
**What they want more of**: egalitarian exegesis, single-in-ministry encouragement, and leadership modelling.

### Segment 5: The Pentecostal academy (~8% — peer/validation audience)

**Who**: practical theologians, Pentecostal-studies scholars, and theological-college students/faculty.
**Where they find her**: the Brill monograph (GPCS vol. 47); *JPT* 30(2) 2021; *JEPTA* 41(2) 2021; Academia.edu; conference papers; Regents TC.
**Why they engage**: a serious empirical practical-theology account of revelatory experience (Cartledge/Astley methods), endorsed by Vondey, Grey, Macchia, Archer, Newton.
**What they want more of**: peer-reviewed grounding; the empirical field-research base; ongoing scholarly engagement.

## Distribution Channel Strategy

### Primary (high priority)
1. **Unified, bot-readable "God Conversations" hub** — the canonical front door for all segments; resolves the 403 liability; surfaces books, podcast, frameworks, and devotional. Teachable/Shop become linked endpoints, never replaced.
2. **Grow the owned email list** — amplify the existing free-devotional capture (build on a strength, not invent one). The list is the asset; the courses are the destination.
3. **The God Conversations podcast** — already her strongest owned channel; index by theme/guest, drive ratings, cut clips into discovery and email funnels.
4. **The course + church-licence ladder (Teachable)** — the revenue spine for leaders; **complement and feed it, never cannibalize it.**

### Secondary
5. **Broadcast / guest circuit** — Hope 103.2, TBN UK, Premier, and the apologetics/debate lane (Premier Unbelievable?) — outsized reach for AU/UK; natural booking hook into the hub.
6. **Egalitarian / women-in-ministry channels** — CBE, The Junia Project, Woman Alive — for the values-aligned segment.
7. **Academic channels** — Brill / JPT / JEPTA / Academia / Regents TC — for the peer/validation segment.

### Emerging
8. **Short-form discernment & dreams video** — "show, don't tell" stories and dream-interpretation clips for YouTube/IG/TikTok (no TikTok presence yet — an open lane).
9. **Theme-indexed podcast clips** — repurpose 106 episodes into discoverable, list-feeding snippets.

## Content Strategy Recommendations

- **Lead with consolidation and discoverability, then growth.** Movemental's job is to fix the 403, surface a dispersed-but-excellent corpus, and grow a modest owned audience — *not* to build a funnel (she already has one) or generate net-new product.
- **Grow the list she already captures** — amplify the devotional/email mechanism; segment by interest (hungry believer / church leader / academy).
- **Surface the frameworks as evergreen assets** — the Three Tests and Three Questions become linkable, GEO-friendly reference pages.
- **Lean into the AU/UK/NZ and female-leaning skew** — these are her real strengths, not gaps to apologize for; broadcast and egalitarian channels are high-leverage.
- **Protect the funnel and the register** — aggregate-and-link Teachable / the "50 Days" licence, never re-sell or undercut them; keep her dialectical voice (confidence *and* humility); never render her as a hype-prophetic performer or as bypassing Scripture/the local church.
- **Frame as a trusted voice** — per Movemental doctrine, present her as a Spirit-led-discipleship trusted voice (definition + practitioner fit), never as a parallel audience funnel or a recruiting roster. Flag UNKNOWN metrics rather than estimating them as fact.
