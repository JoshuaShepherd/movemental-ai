## Executive Summary

Rev. Dr. Tania Harris's calling is that of a **practical theologian who democratizes hearing God** — a Pentecostal pastor-scholar called to make discerning the Spirit's voice "a normal part of every Christian's life" rather than the property of "the wise and the weird." Where most charismatic teaching on hearing God works in either the experiential-hype register or the cautious-cessationist register, Harris holds both poles together: she pairs first-hand charismatic experience with PhD-grade theological rigour (a Brill monograph, peer-reviewed articles) and insists revelation be tested by Scripture and confirmed in the local church. Her vocation is to equip every believer to "recognise and respond" to the Spirit, and to build **"the church who hears God's voice"** — an ecclesial container where discernment is communal and accountable, not isolated and ungoverned. ## Calling Statement

### Synthesized Calling

Tania Harris is called to **democratize hearing God** — to take the experience of discerning the Spirit's voice out of the hands of a specialist elite ("the wise and the weird," the lone prophet, the platform charismatic) and return it to every believer as their ordinary inheritance, anchored in Acts 2:17 and 39. Her vocation runs along two rails that she refuses to let separate: *charismatic experience* and *theological rigour*. She is a Pentecostal who got the PhD to give her movement a credible account of what it has always practiced — building a theology in which contemporary revelatory experience is **phenomenologically equivalent** to the biblical experience without being canon-forming. And she is a scholar who never leaves the local church behind: her constructive aim is "the church who hears God's voice," a community where revelation is **tested by Scripture** ("Would Jesus say this?"), **confirmed by others** ("God speaks twice"), and **discerned together** rather than claimed alone. She teaches by story — "I wanted to write a book that will *show* you" — modelling discernment through real God-encounters rather than abstract rules. She is a practitioner-scholar: a single woman who planted and pastored a church, learned to hear God "through tears, questions and plenty of arguments," and then theologized the path so others could walk it with both *confidence* and *humility*.

### Core Calling Elements

**1. Democratizer of the Prophetic** — hearing God is "a normal part of every Christian's life," not the property of special leaders or strange mystics; "we can all hear him for ourselves."

**2. Experience-and-Rigour Bridge-Builder** — pairs first-hand charismatic experience with PhD-grade practical theology; "biblical depth + practical wisdom" in warm pastoral prose.

**3. Discernment Architect** — gives the field testable, ownable tools: the Three Tests (*Would Jesus say this? Is someone else saying this? Are spiritual signs following?*) and the Three Questions framework.

**4. Story-First Equipper** — teaches discernment through biblical and personal narrative case studies ("show, don't tell"), not rule-lists.

**5. Ecclesial Anchorer** — corrects ungoverned individualism by locating discernment inside "the church who hears God's voice"; the local church is the God-ordained container.

## mDNA Alignment Profile

### Element 1: Jesus is Lord (Organizing Center)

**Alignment Strength**: High

**Evidence:** Christ is the explicit benchmark of all discernment — the first and most important test is "*Would Jesus say this?*" (alignment with Christ's character and teaching in Scripture). The aim of hearing God is not novelty or power but to be "led in the way of Jesus" — "most God conversations are about everyday matters of the heart that lead us in the way of Jesus." Lordship is the governing filter, not a tagline.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong: every discernment framework is tethered to Christ as the test; the Christocentric filter is load-bearing, not decorative.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: more explicit content on how Christ's Lordship reshapes the *content* (not just the testing) of what believers hear.

---

### Element 2: Disciple Making (Strategic Nerve)

**Alignment Strength**: Very High (the center of gravity)

**Evidence:** Harris's entire project is a form of **Spirit-led discipleship** — the conviction that ordinary formation happens as believers learn to recognise and respond to the Spirit in "everyday matters of the heart." This is her strongest mDNA suit. *God Conversations* is part-memoir, part-teaching on hearing the Spirit as accessible to every follower of Jesus; the courses, devotional, and church campaign all operationalize a reproducible discipleship practice (discernment) for the whole body.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Very Strong: a dedicated discipleship method (hearing-and-responding), books, courses, and a 7-day devotional that operationalize it.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: frame the discernment practice explicitly as a *disciple-multiplication* engine (hearers who form hearers), not only individual formation.

---

### Element 3: Missional-Incarnational Impulse (Living Among)

**Alignment Strength**: Medium

**Evidence:** The incarnational impulse is present but indirect. Her democratizing instinct — revelation belongs to "every believer," not the platform elite — is an incarnational levelling of access. Her broadcast/media presence (Hope 103.2, TBN UK, Premier) and cross-tradition reach (endorsers across UK/Europe/NZ) embody dispersed, everyday presence. But "sending into culture" is not a primary register of her work; her gravity is inward formation and discernment more than incarnational mission to the unreached.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Present: the "every believer / everyday" framing is incarnational in spirit; broad media footprint.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: connect hearing God to missional sending — how a discerning church is led *outward*, not only formed inward. Largely uncatalogued.

---

### Element 4: Apostolic Environment (APEST)

**Alignment Strength**: Medium-High

**Evidence:** Harris does not use the APEST taxonomy (that is Hirsch's vocabulary; absent from her corpus). But she occupies the **prophetic/discernment** function natively — her whole field is teaching the body to hear the Spirit — while carefully distinguishing her democratized "revelatory experience" from the specialist *gift of prophecy*. As an ordained female pastor, church planter, and Academic Dean, she models **egalitarian fivefold-style leadership** (egalitarian exegesis for The Junia Project; ACC's affirming position), expanding who may exercise these functions. The substance of an apostolic/prophetic environment is present; the named five-fold vocabulary is not.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong on the prophetic/discernment function and on egalitarian access to leadership ministry.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: an explicit bridge between her discernment ecclesiology and the APEST language of the broader ecosystem (a natural Harris × Hirsch surface), without importing vocabulary she doesn't use.

---

### Element 5: Organic Systems (Simple, Reproducible)

**Alignment Strength**: High

**Evidence:** Harris's frameworks are built to *reproduce*. The Three Tests are a portable, testable discernment grid any believer or church can run; "the church who hears God's voice" is an ecclesial *system* (Spirit at Work → Theology → Strategies for building it) designed to be transferable across "any church tradition." The "50 Days" church licence — 7 videos + study guide + leadership training + launch event — is literally a reproducible system dropped into a local congregation. Discernment as a simple, repeatable communal practice is organic-systems logic.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong: testable grids, a transferable ecclesial model, and a packaged church campaign are systems-native.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: more explicit content on how the hearing-church practice *self-replicates* across congregations.

---

### Element 6: Liminality-Communitas (Growth Through Shared Hardship)

**Alignment Strength**: Medium-High

**Evidence:** Liminality is autobiographically present and load-bearing. Harris planted and pastored a church **as a single woman** out of a "conservative background where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag" — a genuinely liminal vocation. She is candid that learning to hear God came "through tears, questions and plenty of arguments… the process doesn't change, but I have." Her singleness, her boundary-crossing into the academy, and her dialectical posture (confidence *and* humility, holding two poles in tension) are all liminal. The *communitas* side appears in her ecclesial insistence that discernment is forged together ("God speaks twice"), though it is named less explicitly than the personal liminality.

**Content Alignment:**
- ✅ Strong personal-liminality testimony; communal-discernment ("God speaks twice") supplies the communitas thread.
- ⚠️ Opportunity: name the communitas dynamic explicitly — the *shared* struggle of a community learning to hear and be wrong together.

## Digital Calling Opportunity Map

**The defining move is consolidation and growth, not creation.** Harris is unusually well-productized for the research set — she already has a real free→email→course→church-licence funnel that most leaders lack. Movemental's job is to **consolidate her dispersed surfaces into one discoverable owned home and grow a modest audience** — while *complementing*, never cannibalizing, her Teachable courses and church licence (her revenue engines).

1. **Unified "God Conversations" trusted-voice hub** — a discoverable, bot-readable home that fixes the 403 liability and surfaces books, podcast, frameworks, and devotional in one place; her Teachable/Shop become linked source endpoints, not competitors.
2. **Grow the owned email list** — she already has the free 7-day devotional + fortnightly email as a capture mechanism; amplify it. The list is the asset; the courses are the destination.
3. **Surface the frameworks as evergreen reference** — the Three Tests and Three Questions are ownable, linkable, GEO-friendly assets that currently live buried behind a blocked site.
4. **Index the 106-episode podcast** — by theme and guest (Cartledge, Yong, Boyd, Burke), with clips — to feed discovery and the email funnel.
5. **Short-form discernment/dreams video** — "show, don't tell" stories and dream-interpretation are unusually suited to short-form; she has the YouTube channel but small/undisclosed reach to grow.
6. **Harris × Hirsch / ecosystem cross-surface** — a Spirit-led-discipleship conversation bridging her discernment ecclesiology to the wider movemental stream and her egalitarian leadership to APEST language.

## Strategic Recommendations

**Priority 1: Consolidate and surface.** Build the canonical, bot-readable God Conversations hub; resolve the 403 discoverability liability; index the 106 episodes; present the books and frameworks as the front door to a corpus that is currently hard to find.

**Priority 2: Grow the owned audience.** Amplify the existing devotional/email capture (its presence is a strength to build on, not invent); pursue a Wikipedia article; surface the academic record (Brill, JPT, JEPTA) as credibility proof.

**Priority 3: Frame as a trusted voice, not a parallel funnel.** Per Movemental doctrine, Harris's ecosystem surface is a **trusted-voice / Spirit-led-discipleship** layer — not a fourth audience sibling beside churches/nonprofits/institutions. Present her as definition-plus-practitioner-fit, never as a recruiting roster.

**Priority 4: Complement, never cannibalize.** Movemental hosts the public-facing hub, frameworks, and audience-capture layer; **Teachable keeps the paid courses and the "50 Days" church licence keeps the cohorts and revenue.** Aggregate-and-link to Teachable/Shop, never re-sell or undercut them. Preserve her dialectical register (confidence *and* humility), her democratizing voice, and both antithesis guardrails: do **not** render her as a hype-prophetic performer, and do **not** render her as bypassing Scripture or the local church. Flag UNKNOWN entities (newsletter size, YouTube/X metrics, birth data) rather than fabricating.
