## Executive Summary

This audit inventories Rev. Dr Tania Harris's content across all formats and channels. The headline finding is a near-mirror of the usual movement-leader pattern: Harris is **productization-rich but reach-light**. She has built one of the most mature owned funnels in the trusted-voice set — a real free-devotional → fortnightly-email → paid-course → church-licence ladder, anchored by a published trade-and-academic corpus (4 books, including a Brill monograph and a PhD behind it), a 106-episode solo podcast, a Teachable LMS with a church-licensing B2B product and a German-language edition, an own blog, peer-reviewed articles, and a steady broadcast/interview circuit. Yet the audience layer underneath is **modest and niche**: ~122 total Goodreads ratings across all titles, ~2,800 Instagram followers, an apparently small YouTube channel, no Wikipedia article, and no Google Scholar / ORCID profile indexing the academic record.

The editorial issue is therefore **discoverability and amplification, not production, funnel design, or voice quality.** Harris has already solved the hard part most leaders never reach — she has a working, paid, multi-currency funnel and a church-licensing institutional wedge. What is missing is **owned-audience scale, surfaced discoverability, and format reach** (short-form video, podcast indexing, translations, additional audiobooks). The overall digital presence assessment is **strong commerce + funnel + owned hub; weak reach + discoverability**.

**Key Outcomes:**
- Complete content inventory by format and topic
- Quality / coverage assessment per format
- Format coverage matrix
- Content gap identification (reach-and-discoverability-centric)
- Repurposing roadmap and SEO/GEO recommendations (own the "hearing God's voice / God conversations" category)

## Quality & Coverage Assessment

**High performers:** the trade trilogy (*God Conversations* → *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* → *God Dreams*) as a coherent, progressively-deepening reader ladder; the Brill monograph as the credentialing spine; the 106-episode, 5.0★ podcast; and — most distinctively — the **Teachable funnel with a working free→paid→church-licence ladder and a German edition.** The funnel maturity is genuinely ahead of most leaders in the trusted-voice set.

**Medium / under-surfaced:** the own blog and the peer-reviewed article record (both real, both poorly indexed); the cohort and coaching offers (present but low-visibility); the companion radio strand.

**Under-captured / under-leveraged:**
- **Modest reach despite strong productization** — the headline tension. The funnel is built; the audience funneling *into* it is small (~122 Goodreads ratings, ~2,800 IG followers, ~3.9K ministry-page followers, small YT).
- **Book reviews are low-volume** — a bestseller-quality trade trilogy drawing well under 100 unique-title ratings each is a clear reader-footprint under-capture.
- **YouTube under-leveraged** — a small channel sitting on top of 106 podcast episodes and the most visual book in the corpus (*God Dreams*).
- **No Wikipedia / no Google Scholar / no ORCID** — neither the popular entity nor the academic record is machine-discoverable.
- **Single-topic concentration** — nearly everything orbits one theme: hearing God's voice / God conversations. This is a strength for category ownership but a risk for reach ceiling and discoverability breadth.
- **Only one audiobook** — three of four books have none, in a genre where audio is a primary devotional-consumption format.
- **No book translations** — the German *course* edition proves cross-language demand the books have never captured.

## Content Gap Analysis

The gaps are about **reach, discoverability, and format breadth** — not funnel design (already strong) or content volume (already deep on its one topic).

### High-Priority Gaps
1. **Modest owned-audience scale.** The funnel exists; the list, social, and reader footprints feeding it are niche. Growing owned audience is the single highest-leverage fix.
2. **Podcast under-indexed.** 106 episodes with no canonical topic/series index — the largest latent, already-produced content asset, currently hard to navigate or surface.
3. **Discoverability / SEO/GEO.** No Wikipedia article; no Google Scholar / ORCID; the blog and academic record under-indexed; site 403s bots.
4. **Short-form video gap.** YouTube small; no TikTok; the highly visual *God Dreams* material un-repurposed into Reels/Shorts.

### Medium-Priority Gaps
5. **Audiobook gap.** Only *God Conversations* is narrated; *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* and *God Dreams* have none.
6. **No book translations.** The German course proves demand; no translated books exist.
7. **Single-topic concentration risk.** Reach ceiling tied to one theme; the egalitarian / women-in-ministry and dreams sub-lanes are under-developed as distinct surfaces.
8. **B2B church-licence under-surfaced.** "50 Days" is a strong institutional wedge but its value-prop and pricing are gated and under-marketed.

## Repurposing Roadmap

### High-Value
1. **Podcast → indexed archive + clips.** Build a canonical 106-episode index by topic (hearing God / discernment tests / dreams / church-as-container / guest theology), then cut short-form audio/video clips from the back catalogue.
2. **Books → short-form video.** Repurpose *God Dreams* (the most visual book) and the three discernment tests into Reels/Shorts where YouTube/short-form is thin.
3. **Books → narrated audio.** Fill the audiobook gap for *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* and *God Dreams* (self-narrated, matching the *God Conversations* model).
4. **Funnel → surfaced, amplified.** Make the already-built free-devotional → fortnightly-email → course → church-licence ladder visible and discoverable on an owned hub (it currently sits behind a bot-blocked site).
5. **"50 Days" church licence → institutional B2B campaign.** Surface the value-prop, case studies, and a clear leader on-ramp for the church-licensing product.

### Medium-Value
6. **Blog + guest articles → canonical essay index.** Aggregate the own blog, Premier, CBE, Junia, and Pneuma Review pieces into one chronological masthead.
7. **Course → additional translations.** Extend the German-edition model to further languages where demand is shown.
8. **Academic record → re-surfaced + indexed list.** Recover the *JPT* / *JEPTA* / conference titles with DOIs; stand up Google Scholar + ORCID.
9. **Framework explainers → assessments.** A "Is this God speaking?" discernment diagnostic built on the Three Tests.

## Content Architecture Recommendations

### Topic Clusters (pillar strategy — organized around her named frameworks)

1. **Hearing God's voice / God conversations** — Pillar: *God Conversations* (2017). Supporting: the podcast, the devotional, the "How does God speak?" framing.
2. **Discerning God's voice (the Three Tests)** — Pillar: the "Would Jesus say this? / Is someone else saying this? / Are signs following?" grid. Supporting: courses, podcast segments, a diagnostic.
3. **The church who hears God's voice (ecclesial model)** — Pillar: *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* (2022). Supporting: the "50 Days" church licence, leaders' resources. **The B2B / institutional lane.**
4. **God dreams & visions** — Pillar: *God Dreams* (2024) + *God Dreams: the Course*. Supporting: the MP3 magnet, the most-visual short-form content.
5. **The theology of revelatory experience** — Pillar: *Hearing God's Voice* (Brill). Supporting: phenomenological equivalency, the academic credentialing surface.
6. **Women in ministry / egalitarian discipleship** — secondary lane: CBE, Junia Project, her own ordained-female-planter story.

### SEO / GEO Optimization — Own the "Hearing God's Voice" Category

Her single-topic concentration, usually a risk, is also her SEO/GEO opportunity: she is a credible, PhD-backed, productized authority on **hearing God's voice / God conversations** — a high-intent devotional search category she can plausibly own.

- **Establish the canonical entity** — one authoritative, bot-accessible hub (the current site 403s crawlers — a direct discoverability tax); pursue a **Wikipedia article** and a verified **Google Scholar + ORCID** so generative-answer engines resolve "hearing God's voice," "how do you know it's God," and "phenomenological equivalency" to Harris.
- **Framework pages** — canonical, structured pages for the **Three Tests**, the **Three Questions**, **phenomenological equivalency**, and the **revelatory-experience** category (each cross-linking the pillar book + course + podcast series), so the framework set is machine-extractable.
- **Index the 106-episode podcast** — a navigable, topic-tagged archive turns the deepest asset into a discoverable, searchable surface.
- **Repurpose to short-form video** — where YouTube is thin and *God Dreams* is highly visual.
- **Capture the audience** — the funnel is built; the missing layer is *scale*. Surface the free devotional and fortnightly email far more aggressively as the owned-relationship entry point.
