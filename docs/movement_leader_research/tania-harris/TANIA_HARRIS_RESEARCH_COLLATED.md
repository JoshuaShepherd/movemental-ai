## Editorial bio

Tania Harris is an Australian Pentecostal pastor, practical theologian, author, and broadcaster, ordained with the Australian Christian Churches and based in Sydney. Her vocation grew out of a personal turning point: as a young woman (around age 21) she prayed what she calls a "radical prayer" — "Lord, speak to me. And if you make it really clear, I'll do whatever you say" — and subsequently experienced what she describes as God beginning to speak to her. At 26 she sensed a call to full-time ministry, an unexpected direction given a conservative church background "where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag, much less be pastors." She retrained with a Masters in Ministry, and in her early 30s planted and led a church in a beachside suburb of Melbourne as a single woman.[^hope][^regents]

From the pastorate she moved into theological education — lecturing at Harvest Bible College in Melbourne (where she pioneered online training and was named Alumna of the Year, 2013), then relocating to Sydney as Academic Dean of Hillsong College. Her own experiences of hearing God became the seed of a wider ministry: she founded **God Conversations** (publicly active from c.2013–2014) to address three questions — *How does God speak? How do you know it's God? And what difference does it make to the church?* The ministry now spans a monthly podcast, four books, online courses, a church-licensing campaign resource, media appearances across Australia, New Zealand and the UK, and consulting / training for denominations and ministers in "Spirit-led discipleship."[^bio][^gc]

Harris is unusual in pairing a popular-ministry footprint with serious academic work. She earned a **PhD from Alphacrucis College in 2020** for empirical practical-theology research on Pentecostal revelatory experience across three Australian churches, published as the scholarly monograph *Hearing God's Voice* (Brill, *Global Pentecostal and Charismatic Studies* vol. 47, 2023). Her trade books *God Conversations* (Authentic Media, 2017), *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* (Paternoster, 2022), and *God Dreams* (Authentic Media, 2024) translate that research for a general Christian readership. Her current footprint includes leading God Conversations, a visiting lectureship in Pentecostal Spirituality at Regents Theological College (UK), and ongoing speaking, broadcasting and writing — all oriented around equipping ordinary believers to discern the Spirit within the accountability of the local church.[^brill][^regents]

### Antithesis prohibition (non-negotiable)

Do not frame hearing God as **rare, elite, or "weird"** — the property of special leaders, prophets, or strange mystics. Harris's most-corrected reader misreading is that hearing God is for the spiritually advanced; her consistent correction is that it is ordinary, accessible to every believer, and reliably testable. Equally, do not write her at *either* extreme she dismantles: not the cessationist "isn't Scripture enough?" dismissal, and not the ungoverned, individualistic charismatic claim that bypasses Scripture and community. In her voice, revelation is always held within Scripture (the test) and the local church (the container); experience is paired with discernment, confidence with humility. Signature contrast: hearing God is not for "the wise and the weird." Derived from the Eden interview, the Pneuma Review interview, and the "How do you know it's God's voice?" guest blog. ([eden.co.uk](https://www.eden.co.uk/blog/tania-harris-talks-god-conversations-and-cake-p129058), [pneumareview.com](https://pneumareview.com/ready-to-be-the-voice-of-god-with-tania-harris/))

## Theological positioning

| Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Pentecostal / charismatic (classical); ordained Australian Christian Churches (AOG lineage); bridges charismatic practice with academic practical theology |
| Ecclesiology | The local church is the God-ordained context for recognising and responding to the Spirit — "the church who hears God's voice"; discernment is corporate, not individualistic |
| Missiology | Spirit-led discipleship: hearing God as the engine of formation and mission; consults with denominations/movements on cultures that facilitate hearing-God experiences |
| Leadership | Egalitarian; ordained female pastor and church planter; equips leaders to build hearing communities; trains pastors and denominations |
| Christology | Christocentric discernment — Jesus is the benchmark for all revelation ("Would Jesus say this?"); "the more you know Jesus, the easier it is to recognise the voice of the Spirit" |
| Anthropology / formation | Every believer has direct access to God's voice (Acts 2:17,39); most God conversations are "everyday matters of the heart that lead us in the way of Jesus" |
| Culture | Continuationist engagement with media and broadcast; bridges the academy and the popular church across AU/NZ/UK |

### Distinctive claims

- Hearing God is **not for spiritual elites** — "we can all hear him for ourselves"; it is "a normal part of every Christian's life," not for "the wise and the weird."
- Contemporary revelatory experience has **phenomenological equivalency** with the biblical experience while remaining **distinct from canon** (Unmediated, Extra-Biblical, High-Level, Universally Accessible).
- Revelation must be **tested, not bypassed** — Scripture (and Jesus' character) is the anchor; "God speaks twice" (communal confirmation) guards against the lone prophet.
- Discernment requires **confidence and humility together** — "the confidence to know we can hear from God as well as the humility to know we can get it wrong."
