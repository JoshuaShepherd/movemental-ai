## Named Frameworks

1. **The Three Tests for Discerning God's Voice** — *Would Jesus say this?* (Christ-alignment, first/most important) → *Is someone else saying this?* ("God speaks twice"; communal confirmation) → *Are spiritual signs following this?* (supernatural confirmation). Modeled on Peter's Joppa vision (Acts 10). Her signature discernment grid.
2. **The Three Questions** — *How does God speak? How do you know it's God? What difference does it make to the church?* The organising trio behind both trade books.
3. **Phenomenological Equivalency** (PhD central claim) — contemporary hearing-God experience is the *same kind* of experience as the biblical one, while remaining distinct from canon.
4. **The Revelatory Experience (four characteristics)** — Unmediated · Extra-Biblical · "High-Level" · Universally Accessible — distinct from the specialist *gift of prophecy*.
5. **"Show, Don't Tell" stories method** — teach discernment through biblical and personal narrative case studies, not abstract rules.
6. **The Church Who Hears God's Voice (ecclesial model)** — the local church as the God-ordained container for discernment and Spirit-led discipleship.
