### Canonical antithesis prohibition
She repeatedly corrects the misconception that hearing God is **rare, elite, or "weird"** — the property of special leaders/prophets or strange mystics. She insists it is **ordinary, accessible to every believer, and reliably testable**. Secondarily she corrects *both* extremes: the **cessationist / "isn't Scripture enough?"** dismissal AND the **ungoverned, individualistic charismatic** claim that bypasses Scripture and community. Signature contrast phrase: not for "the wise and the weird." Do NOT write her as a hype-prophetic "words-of-knowledge" performer, and do NOT write her as bypassing Scripture/the local church — both are anti-patterns she actively dismantles. (Derived from Eden interview, Pneuma Review, Evangelism in Australia guest blog.)

### Canonical named frameworks
1. **The Three Tests for Discerning God's Voice** — (1) *Would Jesus say this?* (alignment with Christ's character/teaching in Scripture — first & most important); (2) *Is someone else saying this?* ("God speaks twice" — communal confirmation, not the lone isolated prophet); (3) *Are spiritual signs following this?* (supernatural confirmation; God's word carries "creative power"). Modeled on the early church / Peter's Joppa vision (Acts 10). Her signature discernment grid.
2. **The Three Questions** — *How does God speak? How do you know it's God? What difference does it make to the church?* The organizing trio behind both trade books.
3. **Phenomenological Equivalency** (PhD central claim) — contemporary hearing-God experience is phenomenologically *equivalent* to the biblical experience (not canon-forming, but the same *kind* of experience); Pentecostals err by fitting their experience into a "phenomenologically inferior framework."
4. **The Revelatory Experience — four characteristics** — Unmediated · Extra-Biblical · "High-Level" · Universally Accessible. Her technical category, distinguished from the *gift of prophecy*.
5. **"Show, Don't Tell" stories method** — teach discernment through biblical and personal narrative case studies (how God spoke, how they knew, how they acted) rather than abstract rules.
6. **The Church Who Hears God's Voice (ecclesial model)** — the local church as the God-ordained container for discernment; book structured as Spirit at Work → A Theology for Hearing God's Voice → Strategies for Building the Church Who Hears God's Voice.

## 8. THEOLOGY (positioning)

| Domain | Position |
|--------|----------|
| Tradition | Pentecostal/charismatic (classical); ordained Australian Christian Churches (AOG); bridges charismatic practice with academic practical theology; roots in a "conservative background where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag" |
| Pneumatology | Centre of gravity: the Holy Spirit actively speaks to every believer today; Pentecost democratised access (Acts 2:17,39) |
| Doctrine of revelation | Continuationist; God "still speaks via his Holy Spirit just as he did in Bible times"; contemporary experience has "phenomenological equivalency" with the biblical experience but is distinct from canon — "Unmediated, Extra-Biblical, High-Level" and universally accessible (vs the specialist gift of prophecy) |
| Revelation ↔ Scripture | Scripture is the test/anchor, never bypassed; personal revelation is "Extra-Biblical" but must align with Jesus as revealed in Scripture; test by 1 John 4:1 |
| Ecclesiology/accountability | The local church is the God-ordained context for discerning revelation ("the church who hears God's voice") |
| Women in ministry | Egalitarian/affirming; ordained female pastor + church planter; egalitarian exegesis (1 Tim 2:11) for The Junia Project; notes ACC's egalitarian position |
| Spiritual formation | "Spirit-led discipleship"; most God conversations are "everyday matters of the heart that lead us in the way of Jesus" |
| Christology | Christocentric discernment: Jesus is the benchmark — "Would Jesus say this?" |
| Culture | Engaged; media/broadcast; bridges academy and popular church |

**Distinctive claims:** democratising the prophetic (not for the "wise and the weird"; "we can all hear him for ourselves"); rigour + experience together; "confidence to know we can hear from God + humility to know we can get it wrong"; revelatory experience ≠ Scripture but is real and testable; the church as the safe container (ecclesial, not individualistic).
