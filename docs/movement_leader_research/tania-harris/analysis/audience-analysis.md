## Framing

Tania Harris ([godconversations.com](https://www.godconversations.com/tania-harris/)) does not address the broad evangelical reading public the way a trade-devotional author does. She serves a **single, well-defined need** — "how does God speak, how do you know it's God, and what difference does it make to the church?" — and the audience that gathers around that need is coherent, engaged, and global, but **niche in size**. Her reach is built less on a mass social following (which is modest) and more on a **productized funnel** (free devotional → email list → self-paced course → church licence) and on **academic + broadcast credibility** that lets her cross from the charismatic world into the wider evangelical and academic conversation.

---

## Primary audience segments

### 1. Christians who want to hear God's voice for themselves (core audience)

**Who**: ordinary believers — not clergy, not academics — who want practical, trustworthy help discerning the Spirit in everyday life. Her signature register is that hearing God is "a normal part of every Christian's life," not the property of "the wise and the weird" ([Eden interview](https://www.eden.co.uk/blog/tania-harris-talks-god-conversations-and-cake-p129058)).
**Size estimate**: UNKNOWN as a discrete number; reached through her podcast (106 episodes), the free 7-day devotional ([/devotional](https://www.godconversations.com/devotional/)), the fortnightly email, and the *God Conversations* trade book (Authentic Media, 2017).
**Where they find her**: the *God Conversations with Tania Harris* podcast ([Apple](https://podcasts.apple.com/au/podcast/god-conversations-with-tania-harris/id763485403), 5.0/29; [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/show/0XH6n2KMlGlvApnRZFrQ2Y), 4.9); Hope 103.2 radio segment "Everyday God Conversations" ([Hope 103.2](https://hope1032.com.au/everyday-god-conversations/)); the books at Koorong / Amazon.
**Why they engage**: she pairs PhD-grade rigour with warm pastoral prose and teaches through **stories** ("show, don't tell") rather than abstract rules — confidence *and* humility held together.
**What they want more of**: practical, testable discernment they can apply this week (the Three Tests — "Would Jesus say this? Is someone else saying this? Are spiritual signs following?").

### 2. Pentecostal / charismatic believers and leaders (home audience)

**Who**: classical Pentecostal and charismatic Christians for whom "hearing God's voice" is a live, normal expectation. Harris is an ordained **Australian Christian Churches** (ACC / Australian AOG) minister who worships at Hillsong ([Regents](https://www.regents-tc.ac.uk/blog/team-member/revd-tania-harris/); [CBE](https://www.cbeinternational.org/person/tania-harris/)).
**Size estimate**: UNKNOWN; reached through ACC/Pentecostal church networks, her former roles (Academic Dean, Hillsong College; faculty, Harvest Bible College), and church speaking across AU/NZ/UK/Europe.
**Where they find her**: ACC and Pentecostal church platforms; the church-licence campaign "50 Days of God Conversations" ([/50-days-licence](https://www.godconversations.com/product/50-days-licence/)); leaders' resources ([/leadersresources](https://www.godconversations.com/leadersresources/)).
**Why they engage**: she gives their lived charismatic experience a **theologically responsible, Scripture-anchored** account that resists both cessationist dismissal and ungoverned, individualistic "words-of-knowledge" excess.
**What they want more of**: ecclesial discernment frameworks — the local church as the safe container ("the church who hears God's voice"), not the lone prophet.

### 3. Church leaders and pastors (institutional / licence audience)

**Who**: senior pastors and ministry leaders who want to build congregations where everyone — not just the leadership — discerns the Spirit.
**Size estimate**: UNKNOWN; this is the audience the church licence, the Pastors & Leaders group training ([/grouptraining](https://www.godconversations.com/50days/grouptraining/)), and the leadership coaching ([/leadershipcoaching](https://www.godconversations.com/leadershipcoaching/)) are built to serve.
**Where they find her**: the God Conversations church-campaign products; endorsements from senior pastors across AU/NZ/UK/Europe (Hillsong pastoral care, Elim UK, New Life Churches NZ, ICF Switzerland, City Church Aarhus, Christus Centrum Tostedt — see [Koorong](https://koorong.com/product/god-conversations-stories-of-how-god-speaks-and_9781780781884)).
**Why they engage**: *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* (Paternoster, 2022) gives them a theology **plus a deployable strategy** ("equipping everyone to recognise and respond to the Spirit") that works across traditions.
**What they want more of**: turnkey, church-deployable campaign material — exactly what the 50-Days licence provides (7×~20-min videos + study guide + leadership training + launch event).

### 4. Women in ministry / egalitarian evangelicals (values-aligned audience)

**Who**: women in ministry and egalitarian-leaning evangelicals who look for credible female theological voices.
**Size estimate**: UNKNOWN; reached through CBE International and The Junia Project audiences.
**Where they find her**: CBE *Mutuality* ("A Vision of Equal Wheels," [2016](https://www.cbeinternational.org/person/tania-harris/)); The Junia Project egalitarian exegesis incl. a 1 Tim 2:11 piece ([author page](https://juniaproject.com/author/tania-harris/)); Woman Alive (UK).
**Why they engage**: she is an **ordained female pastor and former church planter** who planted "as a single woman" out of "a conservative background where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag" — a lived egalitarian witness, not just a position.
**What they want more of**: credible, ordained female leadership modeling in the Pentecostal/charismatic world, where it remains comparatively scarce.

### 5. Academic Pentecostal / practical-theology readers (peer / validation audience)

**Who**: scholars in Pentecostal studies and practical theology.
**Size estimate**: small, high-signal; the *Journal of Pentecostal Theology* / *JEPTA* / Brill Global Pentecostal & Charismatic Studies readership.
**Where they find her**: the Brill monograph *Hearing God's Voice* (GPCS vol. 47, [2023](https://brill.com/display/title/68992)); peer-reviewed articles (*JPT* 30(2) 2021; *JEPTA* 41(2) 2021); conference papers ([Academia.edu](https://independent.academia.edu/TaniaHarris)); visiting lectureship at Regents Theological College (UK).
**Why they engage**: her "phenomenological equivalency" thesis and empirical field study (Cartledge's "Testimony in the Spirit" + Astley's "ordinary theology") are a serious contribution; endorsed by Wolfgang Vondey, Frank Macchia, Kenneth Archer, Jacqueline Grey ([Brill](https://brill.com/display/title/68992)).
**What they want more of**: continued peer-reviewed grounding bridging charismatic practice and the academy.

## Geographic skew: AU / UK / NZ first, US second

Harris's footprint skews to the **Commonwealth charismatic world**, then the US:

- **Australia** (home): Sydney-based; ACC ordination; Hillsong; Hope 103.2 national radio; Eternity News profile ([link](https://www.eternitynews.com.au/people/tania-harris/)).
- **UK**: visiting lecturer at Regents TC; Premier Christianity author page + Premier Unbelievable? debate (2023); Woman Alive; TBN UK broadcaster profile ([tbn.uk](https://tbn.uk/people/dr-tania-harris/)); UK publishers (Authentic / Paternoster).
- **NZ + Europe**: endorsers and speaking ties in New Zealand, Denmark, Germany, Switzerland (inferred from endorsers' churches).
- **US**: present but lighter — Amazon US listings, a German course edition, but no evidence of a large US platform.

This is the inverse of a US-centric evangelical author: her trust is densest in the AU/UK/NZ charismatic ecosystem and the international Pentecostal academy.

## Audience quality signal: the people who platform her

Who chooses to platform Harris is diagnostic of a **respectable, cross-bridging** audience — charismatic-practical, academic-Pentecostal, and egalitarian-evangelical at once:

- **Academic Pentecostalism** — Wolfgang Vondey (U Birmingham), Frank Macchia (Vanguard), Kenneth Archer (Southeastern), Jacqueline Grey and Jon Newton (Alphacrucis) endorsed the Brill monograph; she has hosted Mark Cartledge (ep. 034) and Amos Yong (ep. 041) on the podcast.
- **Charismatic-practitioner world** — senior-pastor endorsers across Hillsong, Elim UK, New Life Churches NZ, ICF Switzerland, and German/Danish churches.
- **Egalitarian evangelicalism** — CBE International and The Junia Project.
- **Broadcast** — Hope 103.2 (AU), Premier (UK), TBN UK.

This is a **credible, cross-tradition** set rather than a mass-media one. It overlaps Movemental's trusted-voice ecosystem on the Spirit-led-discipleship axis, though it is **adjacent** to (not centered on) movement ecclesiology.

## Implication for Movemental onboarding

- Movemental's job is **not to build her a funnel** — she already has one of the more complete productized funnels in this research set (free devotional → email → Teachable course → church licence → cohort). It is to **feed it**: widen the top of the funnel by surfacing her corpus and frameworks to a broader audience and converting more discovery into her existing email list.
- Movemental should **grow the modest reach** — the owned social audience and reader footprint are small relative to the quality of the work and the maturity of the funnel. Discoverability (no Wikipedia, under-leveraged YouTube, low book-review volume) is the lever (see [gap-analysis.md](gap-analysis.md)).
- Movemental must **complement, not cannibalize**, her Teachable courses and church-licence revenue — host public-facing teaching and corpus, while the paid course and licence cohorts remain her native commerce surfaces (see [movemental-fit.md](movemental-fit.md)).
- Her ecosystem layer is framed, per canonical doctrine, as a **trusted voice** on Spirit-led discipleship — not as a fourth audience funnel.
