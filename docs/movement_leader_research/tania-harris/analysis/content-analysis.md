## Thematic map of the corpus

Harris's published writing, teaching, podcasting, and courses are **unusually concentrated**: nearly everything orbits one question — *how does God speak, how do you know it's God, and what difference does it make to the church?* ([Eden](https://www.eden.co.uk/blog/tania-harris-talks-god-conversations-and-cake-p129058)). This is the inverse of a sprawling, many-themed corpus: it is a **single deep well**, theologized academically and delivered pastorally. The themes below are ordered by centrality (1–10), mirroring the substrate's content map but in fuller prose.

### Primary themes

#### 1. Hearing God's voice / the "God conversation" (centrality 10 — the center of gravity)
The whole corpus is built here. Her coined brand framing — "God Conversations" — recasts hearing God as a **relational, two-way conversation**, not a one-off mystical event.
- *God Conversations: Stories of How God Speaks and What Happens When We Listen* (Authentic Media, [2017](https://www.amazon.com/God-Conversations-Stories-Speaks-Happens/dp/1780781881)) — part memoir, part teaching; the flagship trade entry.
- The "How To Hear God's Voice" / 50 Days course ([Teachable](https://godconversations.teachable.com/)).
- The 106-episode podcast ([Apple](https://podcasts.apple.com/au/podcast/god-conversations-with-tania-harris/id763485403)) and the Hope 103.2 "Everyday God Conversations" radio strand.
- Premier Christianity articles; the free 7-day devotional ([/devotional](https://www.godconversations.com/devotional/)).

#### 2. Democratising the prophetic — "a normal part of every Christian's life" (centrality 9)
Her most-repeated corrective: hearing God is **ordinary, accessible to every believer, and testable** — not for "the wise and the weird," not the property of elite prophets or strange mystics. Pentecost democratised access (Acts 2:17, 39). This theme is the polemical engine beneath the practical teaching.

#### 3. Discernment — testing what you hear (centrality 9)
The practical payload: **how do you know it's God?** Anchored in her signature grid (the Three Tests) and in Scripture as the non-negotiable anchor (test by 1 John 4:1; "Would Jesus say this?"). She holds **confidence and humility together** — "the confidence to know we can hear from God *and* the humility to know we can get it wrong" ([Pneuma Review](https://pneumareview.com/ready-to-be-the-voice-of-god-with-tania-harris/)).

### Secondary themes

#### 4. The church who hears God's voice — ecclesial discernment (centrality 8)
Discernment is **communal, not individualistic**. The local church is the God-ordained container for testing revelation ("God speaks twice" — communal confirmation, not the lone prophet).
- *The Church Who Hears God's Voice: Equipping Everyone to Recognise and Respond to the Spirit* (Paternoster, [2022](https://www.amazon.com/Church-Who-Hears-Gods-Voice/dp/1788932463)) — theology → strategy for any church tradition.
- The "50 Days of God Conversations" church-licence campaign ([/50-days-licence](https://www.godconversations.com/product/50-days-licence/)) operationalizes this for congregations.

#### 5. Pentecostal practical theology of revelatory experience (centrality 8 — the academic spine)
Her scholarly contribution: contemporary hearing-God experience is **phenomenologically equivalent** to the biblical experience (the same *kind* of experience, though not canon-forming), and Pentecostals err by fitting their experience into a "phenomenologically inferior framework."
- *Hearing God's Voice: Towards a Theology of Contemporary Pentecostal Revelatory Experience* (Brill, Global Pentecostal & Charismatic Studies vol. 47, [2023](https://brill.com/display/title/68992)) — the commercial form of her 2020 Alphacrucis PhD.
- Peer-reviewed articles (*JPT* 30(2) 2021; *JEPTA* 41(2) 2021); conference papers on "Where Pentecostalism and Evangelicalism Part Ways" and "The Impact of Revelatory Experiences on the Theology of Scripture" ([Academia](https://www.academia.edu/44556023/)).

#### 6. Revelation ↔ Scripture (the boundary) (centrality 7)
She holds a careful dialectic: personal revelation is "Extra-Biblical" but **never bypasses Scripture** — it must align with Jesus as revealed in Scripture. She corrects *both* the cessationist "isn't Scripture enough?" dismissal and the ungoverned charismatic claim that bypasses Scripture and community ("The Theological Problem of Spirit versus Scripture," [Pneuma Review 2022](https://pneumareview.com/)).

### Emerging themes

#### 7. Dreams and visions (centrality 6 — the newest extension)
Her latest expansion of the core topic into a specific revelatory mode.
- *God Dreams: How to Hear God's Voice in Dreams and Visions* (Authentic Media, [2024](https://us.amazon.com/God-Dreams-Hear-Voice-Visions/dp/1788933958)) + *God Dreams: the Course* ([/gdcourse](https://www.godconversations.com/gdcourse/)).
- Teaching: "Nearly all dream-visions are symbolic… A picture truly can tell a thousand words" ([Pneuma Review interview](https://pneumareview.com/ready-to-be-the-voice-of-god-with-tania-harris/)); Premier Christianity "God still speaks through dreams" ([2025](https://www.premierchristianity.com/tania-harris/2902.bio)).

#### 8. Women in ministry / egalitarian leadership (centrality 5 — adjacent witness)
Not the topic of any book, but a consistent thread in her articles and her lived story — an ordained female pastor who planted "as a single woman" from "a conservative background where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag."
- CBE *Mutuality* "A Vision of Equal Wheels" ([2016](https://www.cbeinternational.org/person/tania-harris/)); The Junia Project egalitarian exegesis (1 Tim 2:11, [author page](https://juniaproject.com/author/tania-harris/)).

## Signature intellectual move — rigour delivered as story

Harris's defining move is **"show, don't tell."** Where most "hearing God" books offer rules, keys, or steps, she teaches through **narrative case studies** — biblical and personal — of how God spoke, how the hearer knew, and how they acted. Her own framing: "There are a lot of good books that will tell you how to hear God's voice. I wanted to write a book that will *show* you" ([Eden](https://www.eden.co.uk/blog/tania-harris-talks-god-conversations-and-cake-p129058)).

Three things make this distinctive in the "hearing God" field:

- **She brings empirical practical theology to a devotional topic.** Her PhD used Cartledge's "Testimony in the Spirit" and Astley's "ordinary theology" to study hearing-God experience across three Australian Pentecostal churches — field research where the genre usually offers testimony or method. The result is a category most popular authors lack: the **revelatory experience** as a defined object (Unmediated · Extra-Biblical · "High-Level" · Universally Accessible), distinguished from the gift of prophecy.
- **She governs experience with Scripture and community.** Her teaching is built to resist both poles — neither cessationist dismissal nor ungoverned individualism. The local church is the container; Scripture is the test; Jesus is the benchmark.
- **She democratises without trivialising.** "We can all hear him for ourselves" sits next to a rigorous, testable discernment grid. The accessibility is doctrinally disciplined, not loose.

This is the clearest differentiator of her voice relative to adjacent authors: she is the **scholar-practitioner who theologizes "hearing God"** while keeping it ordinary and pastoral — refusing both the detachment of pure academic pneumatology and the formulaic register of charismatic how-to.

## Argument architecture — her typical talk / chapter shape

1. **Story first** — a concrete God-encounter (hers or a biblical figure's): "show, don't tell."
2. **The democratising claim** — this is normal; it's "for every believer," not "the wise and the weird."
3. **The discernment question** — "but how do you *know* it's God?" → the Three Tests.
4. **The dialectical balance** — confidence *and* humility; experience *and* Scripture; power *and* problems.
5. **The ecclesial turn** — bring it back to the church as the safe container; "God speaks twice."

Visible across *God Conversations*, *The Church Who Hears God's Voice*, the podcast, and the guest-blog corpus.

## What the corpus notably does *not* do

- Does **not** treat hearing God as **rare, elite, or "weird"** — the property of special prophets or strange mystics. Her core corrective is the opposite.
- Does **not** bypass Scripture or the local church — both the cessationist dismissal and the ungoverned individualistic-charismatic claim are anti-patterns she actively dismantles.
- Does **not** offer formulaic "keys" or method-first steps in the Virkler register; she resists the formulaic in favour of story + testable discernment.
- Does **not** range broadly across topics — it is a **single-topic corpus** (hearing God / Spirit-led discipleship). No movement-ecclesiology, leadership-systems, or church-planting theory.
- Does **not** include co-authored books or translations beyond a German course edition (see [gap-analysis.md](gap-analysis.md)).

## Strategic implication

Harris's content is **Movemental-ready in substance**: a coherent voice, a coined brand, three trade books plus a Brill monograph, a 106-episode podcast, and a complete productized course/licence funnel — all anchored in one well-defined topic. The editorial issue is not voice quality (high and coherent) and not production (mature). It is **breadth, discoverability, and reach**: a single-topic corpus with a modest owned audience, low book-review volume, no Wikipedia, and an under-leveraged YouTube. Her rigour-plus-pastoral register must be **respected, not flattened** into pure devotion; the work is **surfacing, indexing, and audience growth**, not rewriting (see [gap-analysis.md](gap-analysis.md) and [movemental-fit.md](movemental-fit.md)).
