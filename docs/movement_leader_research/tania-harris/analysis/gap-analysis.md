## Summary gap statement

Harris's gap profile is **lopsided in an unusual direction**: her **productization is mature and her academic standing is genuine**, but her **owned audience and discoverability are modest**. She has built one of the more complete commerce funnels in this research set — free 7-day devotional → fortnightly email → self-paced Teachable courses → church licence → leader cohorts — and she backs it with a PhD and a Brill monograph. Yet the **volume of attention flowing into that machinery is small**: low-to-mid-thousands of social followers, fewer than 100 distinct Goodreads ratings across all titles, no Wikipedia article, no verified Google Scholar profile, and an under-leveraged YouTube channel.

The problem is **not the funnel and not the credibility**. The problem is **reach and discoverability** — a well-built, well-credentialed, single-topic body of work that too few people can find, and a single topic that limits how wide the audience can grow.

## Owned-reach gap (the core constraint)

Harris's defining gap is the **inverse of her productization**. The funnel is built to convert and monetize attention — but the attention itself is thin. Aggregate owned social reach sits in the **low-to-mid thousands** ([IG ~2,794](https://www.instagram.com/taniamharris/); [ministry FB ~3.9K](https://www.facebook.com/godconversationstaniaharris/); [personal FB ~7.7K](https://www.facebook.com/tania.harris.71/); [LinkedIn 2K+](https://au.linkedin.com/in/tania-harris-586b958)), with meaningful overlap and no single channel of real scale. The podcast is highly loyal (Apple 5.0/29; Spotify 4.9) but small. The email list size is **undisclosed** and, given the surrounding numbers, is unlikely to be large. For an author with a finished funnel and genuine academic standing, the missing layer is **volume at the top of the funnel** — discovery, not machinery.

## Discoverability gap

Three concrete, fixable deficits suppress discovery:

- **No Wikipedia article** — verified absent. A published academic and three-book author with international reach has no canonical encyclopedic entry, which weakens search authority and bio propagation.
- **No verified Google Scholar profile** — her peer-reviewed work (*JPT* 30(2) 2021, *JEPTA* 41(2) 2021, the Brill monograph, conference papers) is not consolidated under a claimed Scholar profile, scattering her citation footprint.
- **Site is bot-hostile** — godconversations.com returns HTTP 403 to automated fetches, which both this research pass had to work around and which can hamper indexing and AI-surface discovery.

## YouTube under-leverage

The @godconversations channel exists with teaching videos ("The God who Still Speaks," "How to Hear God's Voice," "Amazing God Conversations of the Bible") and an "Everyday God Conversations" playlist, but it **appears small and under-leveraged** relative to her teaching output. Subscriber/view counts are login-gated (UNKNOWN), but the channel is not functioning as a discovery engine the way her body of recorded teaching could support.

## Single-topic concentration

Everything orbits one question — *how does God speak, how do you know it's God, what difference does it make to the church?* This is a **strength for clarity and brand coherence** ("God Conversations" is instantly legible) but a **ceiling on audience breadth**: there is no adjacent lane (leadership, formation, ecclesiology-at-large) drawing in readers who aren't already interested in hearing God specifically. The newest book (*God Dreams*) extends *within* the topic rather than beyond it.

## Format / translation gaps

- **No book translations** found, despite endorsers and speaking ties in Germany, Denmark, Switzerland, and the wider UK/Europe/NZ charismatic world — only a single **German course** edition exists ([Teachable](https://godconversations.teachable.com/p/wie-man-gottes-stimme-horen-kann)).
- The 2017 flagship has a self-narrated Audible edition, but the later books' audio/format coverage is unclear.

## Canonical-doctrine framing

Per [docs/build/strategy/movement-leaders-as-ecosystem-layer.md](../../../build/strategy/movement-leaders-as-ecosystem-layer.md): movement leaders are **trusted voices and an ecosystem layer**, not implementation audiences or a parallel funnel segment. Harris is a trusted voice on **Spirit-led discipleship** — adjacent to, not centered on, movement ecclesiology. Movemental does not "customize for Harris" and does not absorb her commerce. It provides the **owned discovery / corpus / relational layer** that widens the top of her existing funnel — while her Teachable courses, church licence, and cohorts remain her native commerce surfaces.

## Why this gap is an opportunity, not a risk

1. **The funnel is already built — Movemental just has to feed it.** Most leaders in this set need a funnel; Harris needs **traffic into one that already works**. Surfacing her corpus and frameworks to a wider audience and converting discovery into her existing email list is exactly Movemental's job — lower-risk than building commerce from scratch.

2. **The discoverability gaps are the easiest to close.** A Wikipedia article, a claimed Scholar profile, an indexed corpus hub, and a populated video presence are aggregation-and-discovery work — precisely what the platform is — not editorial overhaul. Her voice is coherent and platform-ready (see [content-analysis.md](content-analysis.md)).

3. **She diversifies the roster.** A credible, academically grounded **egalitarian female Pentecostal voice** broadens a trusted-voices set that skews male and Western, and brings the Spirit-led-discipleship axis the set is otherwise light on.

## Risks to note

- **Do not cannibalize her courses.** The Teachable courses and the 50-Days church licence are her own revenue engine. Movemental hosts public-facing corpus and audience growth; the paid courses and licence cohorts remain hers. Complement, never compete (see [movemental-fit.md](movemental-fit.md)).
- **Do not flatten her register.** Her differentiation is **rigour delivered as pastoral story**; reducing her to either pure devotion or dry academia destroys it.
- **Do not over-claim reach.** Many of her metrics are login-gated/undisclosed; her owned audience is genuinely modest and must be framed as a **growth target**, not an existing asset.
- **Doctrine is adjacent, not core.** She is a Spirit-led-discipleship / hearing-God specialist, not a movement-ecclesiology theorist; framing must respect that adjacency (it is the primary reason she sits below the anchor set — see [movemental-fit.md](movemental-fit.md)).
- **Respect the anti-patterns she dismantles.** Never present her as a hype-prophetic performer or as a Scripture-bypassing individualist; both are positions she actively corrects.
