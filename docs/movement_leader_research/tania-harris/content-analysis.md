## In brief

Harris's published writing, teaching, and podcasting cluster around **one center of gravity — hearing God's voice — worked out across several recurring themes**:

1. **How God speaks today** (center of gravity) — *God Conversations* (2017); the podcast (106 eps); the flagship course.
2. **How you know it's God — discernment** — the **Three Tests** ("Would Jesus say this? Is someone else saying this? Are spiritual signs following?"); *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* (2022).
3. **Democratising the prophetic** — hearing God is "a normal part of every Christian's life," not for "the wise and the weird."
4. **The church as the discernment container** — communal confirmation ("God speaks twice"), not the lone prophet; the ecclesial model of *The Church Who Hears God's Voice*.
5. **Dreams & visions** — symbolic, picture-language revelation — *God Dreams* (2024) + the course.
6. **Spirit-led discipleship** — most God conversations are "everyday matters of the heart that lead us in the way of Jesus."
7. **Accessible scholarship + women in ministry** — the Brill monograph / PhD (rigour) and the egalitarian thread (CBE, The Junia Project).

**Signature intellectual move**: holding poles together — **confidence *and* humility** ("we can hear from God *and* we can get it wrong"), **experience *and* Scripture** (Scripture is the test, never bypassed), **rigour *and* warmth** (PhD-grade theology in pastoral prose). Her method is **"show, don't tell"** — teaching discernment through biblical and personal narrative case studies rather than abstract rules. Her central scholarly claim is **phenomenological equivalency**: contemporary hearing-God experience is the same *kind* as the biblical experience, though not canon-forming.

**Frameworks**: six named, anchored in an ownable brand category — the **Three Tests for Discerning God's Voice**, the **Three Questions** (how does God speak / how do you know it's God / what difference to the church), **phenomenological equivalency**, the **Revelatory Experience** (four characteristics), the **"show, don't tell" stories method**, and the **church who hears God's voice** ecclesial model.

**Antithesis she dismantles**: that hearing God is rare, elite, or "weird" (the property of special prophets or strange mystics). She insists it is ordinary, accessible to every believer, and testable — while *also* correcting the cessationist "isn't Scripture enough?" dismissal and the ungoverned, individualistic charismatic claim that bypasses Scripture and community.

**Strategic implication**: the content is Movemental-ready in substance, coherent in theme, and uniquely backed by a **complete productized funnel** (free → email → course → church licence → cohort → coaching) and **credible academic standing** (Brill monograph + PhD). The editorial issue is **reach and integration**, not voice quality or coherence — amplify a modest niche audience and wire the content surfaces into the existing funnel. Her dual register (academic rigour + warm pastoral prose) must be respected, never flattened into hype-prophetic performance.
