## Strategic frame

Harris is the **already-productized** onboarding case. Unlike the dispersed-corpus leader (scattered books, no funnel) or the thin-surface practitioner (no corpus to surface), Harris arrives with the hard part already solved: a **working, paid, multi-currency funnel** — free 7-day devotional → fortnightly email → Teachable courses → "50 Days" church licence — sitting on top of a coherent 4-book corpus, a 106-episode podcast, and a PhD-backed academic spine. What she lacks is **reach**: ~122 Goodreads ratings across all titles, ~2,800 Instagram followers, a small YouTube channel, no Wikipedia, no Google Scholar. The playbook's job is therefore **surface and amplify the existing funnel, and grow the audience flowing into it** — not build a funnel from scratch and not flatten her register.

Four guiding principles:

1. **Surface and amplify the funnel that already exists.** Her free→email→course→church-licence ladder is more mature than most of the trusted-voice set. The win is making it *discoverable and amplified* (the site currently 403s crawlers), not redesigning it.
2. **Grow owned audience into the funnel.** The funnel converts; what is thin is the top — list size, social reach, reader footprint, video. Audience growth is the single highest-leverage lever.
3. **Respect the register.** Her differentiation is *accessible scholarship* — PhD-grade rigour in warm pastoral, story-driven prose, organised around questions ("How does God speak? How do you know it's God?"). No hype-prophetic flattening, and no bypassing of Scripture or the local church — both are anti-patterns she actively dismantles.
4. **Complement, never cannibalize her Teachable business.** Her courses, church licence, cohorts, and coaching are her revenue engine. Movemental hosts the public-facing trusted-voice surface and the owned-audience growth layer; Teachable keeps the paid courses, the church licences, and the cohorts. Movemental drives traffic *into* her funnel, never around it.

## Positioning

**The accessible-scholarship voice for hearing God's voice.** Harris is the rare figure who can hold *both* poles the category usually splits between: the academic (Brill monograph, PhD, "phenomenological equivalency") and the everyday ("a normal part of every Christian's life," not for "the wise and the weird"). Movemental's positioning move is to make her profile the **canonical, discoverable front door to "hearing God's voice / God conversations"** — the place a stranger who searches "how do you know it's God" lands, finds the Three Tests, the pillar book, the 106-episode podcast, the free devotional, and the path into her courses and church licence.

Sub-positioning to carry across surfaces:
- **"We can all hear him for ourselves."** The democratising thesis — hearing God is ordinary, accessible to every believer, and reliably testable — is her clearest, most shareable line and should anchor the lead magnet and framework pages.
- **"Confidence *and* humility."** The dialectical balance ("the confidence to know we can hear from God + the humility to know we can get it wrong") differentiates her from both cessationist dismissal and ungoverned charismatic claim.
- **Spirit-led discipleship within the local church.** Revelation is always held inside Scripture (the test) and the church (the container) — the ecclesial frame that makes her a *trusted voice* in the Movemental sense, not a lone-prophet performer.

## Content pillars

Condensed from her named frameworks into four publishing pillars:

1. **Hearing God's voice / God conversations** — the center of gravity. *God Conversations* (2017), the podcast, the free devotional. The flagship pillar page and lead magnet.
2. **Discerning God's voice — the Three Tests** — "Would Jesus say this? / Is someone else saying this? / Are spiritual signs following?" The most-shareable, most teachable framework; anchors the diagnostic.
3. **The church who hears God's voice** — *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* (2022) + the "50 Days" church licence. **The institutional B2B lane.**
4. **God dreams & visions** — *God Dreams* (2024) + its course. The most visual pillar — the short-form-video frontier.

(*Hearing God's Voice* / Brill is the academic credentialing spine beneath all four; women-in-ministry / egalitarian discipleship — CBE, Junia — remains a secondary surface.)

## Owned-audience growth — the core lever

The funnel is built; the audience flowing into it is thin. 4 books, 106 episodes, and a paid LMS sit on ~122 Goodreads ratings, ~2,800 IG followers, and a fortnightly list of undisclosed (likely modest) size. The work is **growing the top of an already-converting funnel** — not building a new one. The plan:

1. **Surface the free devotional everywhere.** The free 7-day "God Conversations" devotional is her best on-ramp; it should be the primary CTA on the Movemental profile, every podcast episode page, every book entry, and every framework page.
2. **Convert the 106-episode podcast into list growth.** Every episode page carries the devotional + fortnightly-email CTA; the "Everyday God Conversations" short segments become the recurring on-ramp.
3. **Convert the corpus.** Each book's hub entry offers a chapter excerpt or the Three-Tests explainer in exchange for email; *God Conversations* (the flagship) is the primary magnet.
4. **Lead magnet**: a designed **"How Do You Know It's God? — the Three Tests in 15 Minutes"** PDF explainer — *Would Jesus say this? / Is someone else saying this? / Are signs following?* — with a path into the podcast, the free devotional, and the courses.
5. **Welcome sequence (5 emails)**: *(1)* Welcome + the Three-Tests PDF; *(2)* "Hearing God is not for the wise and the weird" (the democratising thesis); *(3)* Confidence *and* humility — how to discern without certainty-or-paralysis; *(4)* When God speaks in dreams (the *God Dreams* lane); *(5)* The church who hears God's voice + the courses/church-licence path.
6. **Seed list**: fortnightly-email subscribers, devotional signups, podcast listeners, and — with permission — Hope 103.2 / Regents / God Conversations community contacts.

**Targets**: 1,000 net-new subscribers in 6 months; 2,500 by 12 months. *(Set lower than a high-reach leader's — Harris is niche-by-design; quality and B2B church conversion matter more than raw count.)*

## What NOT to do

1. **Do not compete with or duplicate her Teachable business.** Her courses, "50 Days" church licence, cohorts, and coaching are her revenue engine. Movemental hosts the public trusted-voice surface and grows the owned audience; Teachable keeps the paid funnel. **Complement, never cannibalize** — drive traffic *into* her funnel, never around it.
2. **Do not flatten the register.** Her value is accessible scholarship — rigour in warm, story-driven, question-framed pastoral prose. No hype-prophetic flattening.
3. **Do not write her as a lone-prophet "words-of-knowledge" performer**, and do not write her as bypassing Scripture or the local church. Revelation is always held inside Scripture (the test) and the church (the container) — both extremes are anti-patterns she actively dismantles. Carry "confidence *and* humility."
4. **Do not frame hearing God as rare, elite, or "weird."** Her signature correction is that it is *ordinary, accessible to every believer, and reliably testable* — not for "the wise and the weird."
5. **Do not place her as a parallel-audience funnel.** Per Movemental doctrine she is a **trusted voice / ecosystem layer**, not a fourth audience card. The "50 Days" church licence is an *organization-facing* surface that serves Movemental's primary org audience — frame it that way, not as a roster funnel.
6. **Do not chase vanity social growth.** Her reach is niche-by-design; the wins are email capture, podcast indexing, B2B church conversion, and discoverability — not follower counts.
7. **Do not publish unverified entities.** No invented metrics; honor every UNKNOWN (YouTube counts, list size, "The Easy Way to Hear God's Voice," Hillsong Colour platform slot) per the substrate.
