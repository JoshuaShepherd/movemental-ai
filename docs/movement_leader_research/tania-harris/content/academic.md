## Assessment

Harris is a **bona fide scholar-practitioner** — a PhD awarded 2020, a Brill monograph in a recognised series, two peer-reviewed 2021 journal articles, conference papers, an Academic Deanship (Hillsong College), and a current UK visiting lectureship (Regents). Her framing should lean **into** the academic credentials, which are genuine and senior-endorsed.

The academic record's profile is **focused and coherent — the opposite of dispersion**:

- **A single, integrated scholarly core.** The monograph, the *JPT* and *JEPTA* articles, and the conference papers all derive from one PhD on Pentecostal revelatory experience — mutually reinforcing, not scattered.
- **A recognised-publisher monograph.** The Brill GPCS volume, endorsed by Vondey, Grey, Macchia, and Archer, gives the popular books a credibility floor most hearing-God authors lack.
- **The under-indexing is the gap, not the output.** No Google Scholar page, no ORCID, and the specific titles of the two 2021 journal articles were not captured — so the peer-reviewed record cannot yet be cited cleanly.
- **The practitioner foundation under the academic record** (church planter, Academic Dean) is the credibility bridge between the academy and the popular church audience.

The fragmentation story for the academic layer is **strong, coherent scholarly capital that is under-indexed and under-claimed** — recover the article metadata and stand up a unified scholar profile, and the academic credential becomes a fully citable asset.
