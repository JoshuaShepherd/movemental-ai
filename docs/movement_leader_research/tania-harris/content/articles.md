## Assessment

Harris is **content-coherent in short form and distributed across reputable platforms** — a healthier structural picture than a multi-brand dispersion problem.

- **Single owned brand, single masthead.** All her own short-form lives under God Conversations; there is no naming drift across surfaces.
- **Strong borrowed-audience footprint.** Premier Christianity, CBE, The Junia Project, and The Pneuma Review each carry her writing to established readerships.
- **The discoverability friction is the 403 wall**, not fragmentation: her own blog cannot be indexed by bots, so the depth/cadence of her best owned writing is externally invisible.
- **Two consistent threads** (hearing God; egalitarian ministry) plus a peer-reviewed layer give the corpus range without losing focus.

The fragmentation story for articles is **mild**: the writing is coherent and well-placed; the gaps are (a) an un-crawlable owned archive and (b) no single URL that aggregates the third-party pieces back to her brand.
