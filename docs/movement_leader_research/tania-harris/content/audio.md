## Recurring thematic spine across her audio

Patterns that show up across the podcast and the Everyday strand:

1. **How God speaks** — the modes and means of contemporary revelation.
2. **How you know it's God** — the Three Tests ("Would Jesus say this? Is someone else saying this? Are spiritual signs following?").
3. **"A normal part of every Christian's life"** — democratising hearing God; not for "the wise and the weird."
4. **Confidence + humility held together** — "we can hear from God *and* we can get it wrong."
5. **The church as the discernment container** — communal confirmation ("God speaks twice"), not the lone prophet.
6. **Dreams & visions** — symbolic, picture-language revelation (tied to *God Dreams*).
7. **Spirit-led discipleship** — most God conversations are "everyday matters of the heart that lead us in the way of Jesus."

## Assessment

Harris's audio profile is a **mature, owned, decade-spanning asset with exceptional satisfaction but modest scale** — and it is the opposite of a guest-only profile.

- **She owns a 106-episode, ~13-year, monthly hosted show** with near-perfect ratings (Apple 5.0/29, Spotify 4.9). That is a sustained, durable audio asset most movement leaders never build.
- **Satisfaction is exceptional; scale is niche.** The 5.0/29 and 4.9 scores signal a small but devoted, high-conversion audience — the inverse of a large-but-lukewarm following.
- **A second strand extends reach** — "Everyday God Conversations" on Hope 103.2 puts the brand on mainstream Christian radio, a borrowed-audience amplifier.
- **The audiobook gap is partly closed** — *God Conversations* is self-narrated.
- **The episode index is under-surfaced.** 106 episodes live on the platform feeds; the full title/guest catalogue was not crawlable and there is no clean searchable index on her own (403-walled) domain.

The fragmentation story for audio is **scale and discovery, not production or quality**: the content is deep and well-loved; the audience-growth and back-catalogue-surfacing layers are the opportunity.
