## Assessment

Harris is a **focused, single-subject author with both popular and academic register coverage** — a fundamentally different profile from a disperse, multi-imprint catalogue. The strength is **coherence and laddering**:

- **Four titles, one subject, two registers.** The popular line (*God Conversations* → *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* → *God Dreams*) and the academic line (the Brill monograph) reinforce a single thesis: hearing God is ordinary, accessible to every believer, and testable.
- **The academic anchor is real.** A Brill monograph in a recognised series, endorsed by senior Pentecostal scholars, gives the trade books a credibility floor most hearing-God authors lack.
- **The audiobook gap is already partly closed** — *God Conversations* is self-narrated, an asset Cooper's flagship lacks.
- **Reach is modest and niche.** ~122 Goodreads ratings is healthy per-title but small in absolute terms; the body of work is under-discovered rather than under-built.

**Onboarding implication**: do not treat Harris as needing more books or consolidation across imprints. Treat her as needing **reach amplification on a coherent, well-built catalogue** — a reading-order on-ramp (*God Conversations* → *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* → *God Dreams*, with the Brill monograph as the scholarly deep-end), audiobook completion for the remaining titles, and discovery work to grow the modest readership.
