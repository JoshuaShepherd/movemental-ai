## Headline finding

**Her estate is concentrated and productized; the gap is reach, not infrastructure.** Harris owns a coherent hub (`godconversations.com`), a real Teachable LMS, a monthly podcast, four books (incl. a Brill academic monograph), and a credentialed PhD — all wired into a genuine **free → email → paid course → church-licence funnel**. That funnel maturity is the standout. What she lacks is *scale*: a modest, niche audience (low-thousands social), under ~130 total Goodreads ratings, gated/unknown YouTube and newsletter figures, and **no Wikipedia article** (verified absent). **Overall digital presence score: 3.4/5** — strong content + commerce + owned hub + funnel; modest reach.

## The defining contrast (vs. a dispersion-pattern leader)

Where a dispersion-pattern leader scatters a large catalog across many imprints and brand domains on top of a thin audience, **Harris is the opposite shape**: a *small, concentrated, well-productized* estate — one hub, one LMS, one podcast feed, four books, one academic profile — with a real free-to-paid-to-licence funnel already built. Her constraint is **audience scale**, not consolidation. Onboarding **grows reach and instruments measurement**; it does not have to build the funnel or the hub from zero. See [digital-presence/platforms.md](digital-presence/platforms.md) for the full table and score.

---

## What this means for onboarding

The hub, LMS, podcast, funnel, and credentialed corpus already exist — onboarding **drives traffic, grows the modest audience, and instruments the gaps** (confirm the ESP, publish a newsletter archive, fix the 403/crawlability, stand up a verified scholar profile, surface the media on-domain). The bottleneck is reach + measurement, not content or infrastructure.
