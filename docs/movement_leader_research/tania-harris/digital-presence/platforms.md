## The platform inventory

Crucially, her profile is the **inverse of a dispersion problem** — the catalog and the funnel are *concentrated* and *productized* (free → email → course → church licence). What she lacks is **scale**: a modest, niche social following, sub-100 total Goodreads ratings, login-gated/unknown YouTube and newsletter figures, and **no Wikipedia article**. The bottleneck is reach, not infrastructure.

## What this inventory means

Three things.

1. **Her estate is concentrated and productized, not dispersed.** Where a dispersion-pattern leader scatters output across many imprints and brand domains, Harris runs **one hub, one LMS, one podcast feed, four books across three publishers, one academic profile**. The funnel is designed: free 7-day devotional + MP3 lead magnet → email list → paid self-paced "50 Days" → **church-licence campaign** + pastors' cohort. That free-to-paid-to-licence ladder is more mature than most leaders in the comparison set possess, and it is the single strongest asset to build on.

2. **The gap is reach, not infrastructure.** Four books + 106 episodes + a Brill monograph have produced a **modest, niche audience**: IG ~2.8K, FB page ~3.9K, LinkedIn 2K+, fewer than 100 total Goodreads ratings, 29 Apple podcast ratings, and **no Wikipedia article** (verified absent). YouTube subscriber, X follower, and newsletter list figures are login-gated/undisclosed → UNKNOWN. The funnel is ready; it needs traffic and audience growth, not rebuilding.

3. **The framework is ownable and credentialed.** "God Conversations" is a distinctive, ownable brand around a clear thesis — hearing God as an ordinary, testable, two-way conversation accessible to *every* believer ("not for the wise and the weird") — backed by a PhD and a Brill monograph. That academic standing plus the productized funnel is a rare combination; the unrealized leverage is converting a tidy, credible, conversion-shaped estate into one with real scale.
