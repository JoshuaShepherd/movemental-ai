---

Harris's web presence is the **opposite of a thin-content practitioner's, and unusually well-productized**. She owns a single, well-organized branded hub (`godconversations.com`), runs a real Teachable learning-management storefront, maintains an academic profile, and is carried across publisher, podcast, and media catalogs. Crucially, the owned hub is wired as a **funnel** — free devotional / lead-magnet → email list → paid self-paced course → church-licence campaign — which most leaders in the comparison set lack. The weakness is **reach, not infrastructure**: a tidy, conversion-shaped estate sitting on a modest, niche audience (see [digital-presence/platforms.md](platforms.md)).

> **Sourcing caveat:** `godconversations.com` is a WordPress site that returns **HTTP 403 to automated fetches**. Its page structure below was reconstructed from search snippets plus corroborating third-party pages (Teachable, Regents TC, CBE, Brill, Koorong, Hope 103.2, Pneuma Review, Academia.edu). Treat exact on-page copy as indicative, not verbatim.

## Domain Authority Summary

- **Owned estate**: `godconversations.com` (content + commerce + funnel hub) and the `godconversations.teachable.com` LMS storefront — both active, both substantive. A single, coherent owned front door rather than a scattered one.
- **Funnel maturity (the standout)**: free 7-day devotional + MP3 lead magnet → email list → paid self-paced course → **church-licence campaign** + pastors' cohort. This productized free-to-paid path is more developed than most leaders in the comparison set.
- **Institutional backing**: Australian Christian Churches (ordaining body), Hillsong College, Regents TC (UK), Alphacrucis College, Brill (academic publisher), CBE/The Junia Project — strong contextual SEO and credibility.
- **Core SEO weaknesses**: (1) the **403-to-bots** WordPress config suppresses crawlability/discoverability; (2) no verified Google Scholar/ORCID to index the journal record; (3) the academic and media proof live off-domain rather than consolidated on the hub; (4) **no Wikipedia article** (verified absent) to anchor entity-level discoverability.
- **Core SEO opportunity**: `godconversations.com` is already the natural consolidator and the funnel already exists. The moves are to **fix the 403/crawlability** so the hub can be indexed, pull the academic record and media onto the domain, and **grow the modest audience** that the well-built funnel is ready to capture.
