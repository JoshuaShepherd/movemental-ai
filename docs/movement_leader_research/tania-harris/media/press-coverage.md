### Notes on the strongest items

- **Premier Unbelievable? debate (2023)** is the highest-profile press item: Premier's flagship UK debate show, in head-to-head format against a named cessationist (Jim Osman) on exactly her thesis — God still speaking through dreams, visions, and prophecy. It is the closest thing to a "marquee" placement in her file and converts her academic position into live, watchable content.
- **Premier Christianity column (2017, 2025)** gives her a recurring UK byline — an owned-adjacent platform rather than a one-off mention.
- **Hope 103.2** is her **standing AU broadcast home**: the "Everyday God Conversations" segment is ongoing, making it the most sustained media relationship in the file.
- **The Pneuma Review interview + article** is the most credentialed Pentecostal-studies placement, pairing a popular-level *God Dreams* interview with a scholarly "Spirit versus Scripture" article.

## Media coverage gaps

Harris has **not** (to the audit's knowledge) been covered in:

- *Christianity Today* / *CT Pastors* (US trade)
- *Outreach Magazine* / *The Gospel Coalition*
- *Religion News Service* or any mainstream secular press
- General AU secular press (no surfaced features)

The absence of mainstream and US-trade coverage is unsurprising for a Pentecostal practical theologian whose circuit is UK + AU Christian media. It is worth noting that the coverage that *does* exist skews **UK-weighted** (Premier ×2, Eden, TBN UK, Regents) with a strong AU broadcast anchor (Hope 103.2), and an academic-Pentecostal tier (Pneuma Review) — a more cross-national footprint than the comparison set typically shows, even if all of it is Christian-internal.

## Assessment

- **Coverage is Christian-media-internal but cross-national (UK + AU).** Premier (UK), Hope 103.2 (AU), Pneuma Review (Pentecostal studies), TBN UK (broadcast), and institutional profiles (Regents, CBE) — a real, multi-outlet circuit rather than a single placement.
- **Two-tier pattern.** A flagship debate/broadcast tier (Premier Unbelievable?, TBN UK, Hope 103.2) sitting atop a steady interview-and-column tier (Pneuma Review, Equipping the Church, Eden, Eternity) — with nothing bridging into trade or mainstream press.
- **The Premier Unbelievable? debate is the most generative press asset** because it is dynamic, watchable, and pegged to a live theological controversy — repurposable as clips, and a natural hook for further coverage.

## Recommended press / media strategy (for Movemental onboarding)

1. **Pitch a US-trade feature** (*Christianity Today* / *Outreach*) around the contrarian-yet-pastoral thesis — hearing God as an *ordinary, testable* practice for *every* believer ("not for the wise and the weird") — pegged to *God Dreams* (2024) and the discernment grid ("Would Jesus say this?").
2. **Repackage the Premier Unbelievable? debate** into short clips and an on-domain landing page so the strongest existing coverage stops living only on a third-party site.
3. **Leverage the cross-national circuit** — convert the UK (Premier, TBN, Regents) and AU (Hope 103.2, Eternity) relationships into a coordinated launch channel for the next book/course rather than ad-hoc one-offs.
4. **Consolidate media on-domain** — pull the interviews, the debate, and the column onto `godconversations.com/media` (and fix the 403-to-bots crawl issue) so the press footprint is discoverable and indexable.
