## Structural observations

1. **Two rings, two audiences.** *God Conversations* (2017) drew a pastoral/practitioner ring weighted toward international church networks (Hillsong, Alpha, Elim, ICF, NZ/Danish/German churches). *Hearing God's Voice* (2023) drew an academic Pentecostal ring (Vondey, Macchia, Archer, Grey). The shift mirrors her own academy↔popular-church bridge.

2. **The supervisor anchors both.** Jon Newton — her PhD primary supervisor — endorses *both* the popular debut and the Brill monograph, the only person to span both rings. He is the connective tissue of her credentialing trail.

3. **Real international spread, modest weight.** The 2017 ring genuinely spans five-plus countries, confirming early international church reach. But the heaviest individual names sit in the academic ring (Vondey, Macchia), signaling that her strongest credibility is scholarly, not celebrity-platform.

4. **No Movemental-world endorser.** Unlike leaders whose endorsement rings include Alan Hirsch or the missional-ecclesiology set, none of Harris's confirmed endorsers sit inside the core Movemental target world. The speculative bridge names (Sayers, Caine, Hirsch, Greig, Riches) were checked and are absent. Her credentialing is Pentecostal-academic and international-church, not movemental.

5. **The trail flows inward, not outward.** Harris is well-endorsed but has no surfaced record of endorsing others — consistent with her solo, sole-authored working posture (see collaborators.md).
