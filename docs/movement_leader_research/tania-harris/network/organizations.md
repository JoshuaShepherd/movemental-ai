## Org-level observations

1. **One owned ministry, productized end-to-end.** God Conversations is the structural heart of her network: a single donor-funded ministry that runs a genuine free→email→course→church-licence funnel (devotional, fortnightly email, Teachable courses, "50 Days" church campaign licence, leader cohort training, coaching). Most leaders in the comparison set lack a funnel this complete — this is her strongest structural asset, and the reason her fit score (7.4) clears doctrine-adjacency concerns.

2. **Classical-Pentecostal / ACC center of gravity.** Her institutional orbit is Australian classical Pentecostalism: ordained ACC, PhD from Alphacrucis (ACC-aligned), Academic Dean at Hillsong College, faculty at Harvest Bible College, home church Hillsong. The overseas extension (Regents TC) is UK Elim Pentecostal. This is a coherent, single-tradition spine.

3. **Academy ↔ popular church bridge.** She holds credible academic standing (Brill monograph, PhD, Regents lectureship, peer-reviewed journal articles) while running a popular-level teaching ministry. The same institutions recur as endorsers and supervisors (Alphacrucis → Grey/Newton; Harvest → Newton; Regents → Read), so her academic affiliations and her credentialing trail are tightly interlocking rather than scattered.

4. **Egalitarian affiliations are deliberate, not decorative.** CBE International and The Junia Project place her publicly inside the evangelical/Pentecostal egalitarian conversation — consistent with her own story (female pastor and church planter from "a conservative background where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag").

5. **Reach is modest and niche.** The org footprint is real but small-scale: a niche teaching ministry plus visiting/contributor roles, not a large network or denomination she leads. No formal board seats surfaced (UNKNOWN).
