## Narrative Biography

Tania Harris is an Australian Pentecostal pastor, practical theologian, author, and broadcaster best known for founding **God Conversations** — a Sydney-based ministry built around a single, deceptively simple thesis: that **hearing God's voice is a normal part of every Christian's life**, not the rare property of "the wise and the weird." She is a scholar *and* a practitioner: an ordained minister with the Australian Christian Churches who planted and pastored a church as a single woman in her early thirties, and who later returned to the academy to complete a PhD and publish a peer-reviewed monograph with Brill on the very experience she had spent two decades teaching.

Her formation runs in an unusual order — **teacher, then pastor, then scholar**. She began as a sports teacher (B.Ed, Deakin University; Dux of Teaching, 1991) before a formative answered prayer at age 21 set her on a path toward ministry. At 26 she sensed a call to full-time ministry and returned to study for a Master of Ministry; in her early thirties she planted an ACC church in a beachside Melbourne suburb. Alongside the pastorate she joined the faculty of Harvest Bible College, lectured in ministry, pioneered online learning, and in 2013 was named the college's Alumna of the Year. It was out of this teaching-and-pastoring life that **God Conversations** — first a ministry and podcast, around 2013–2014 — was born.

The second half of her public life has been organized around building out that ministry into a full teaching enterprise and grounding it in serious scholarship. She relocated to Sydney and served as **Academic Dean of Hillsong College**. Between roughly 2015 and 2020 she undertook doctoral research at **Alphacrucis College**, producing an empirical study of how ordinary Pentecostals hear God's voice. The trade book *God Conversations* (Authentic Media) appeared in 2017; the **PhD was awarded in 2020**; the ecclesial sequel *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* (Paternoster) followed in 2022; the scholarly monograph *Hearing God's Voice* (Brill) in 2023; and *God Dreams* (Authentic Media) in 2024. Around the books she built a productized funnel — a free 7-day devotional, a fortnightly email, a flagship self-paced course ("50 Days"), a church-licence campaign, group training, and leadership coaching — that distributes the same teaching at multiple price points and into local churches.

Her current footprint centers on three things held together: **founding director of God Conversations** in Sydney, **visiting lecturer in Pentecostal Spirituality at Regents Theological College (UK)**, and an active author/speaker/broadcaster/podcaster ministry with an international reach (Australia, New Zealand, the UK, continental Europe, and the US). She remains a single-theme author by design — every book, course, and podcast episode orbits the same question of how God speaks and how to recognise and respond.

Her personal biography is **partly documented and partly UNKNOWN**: her marital status (single, never married, no children) is verified and self-disclosed, and is part of her public testimony; her birth date, birthplace, and heritage are simply not published. This profile honors that distinction rather than inventing detail.

## Origin & Calling Story

The hinge of Harris's whole ministry is a single prayer prayed at age 21:

> "Lord, speak to me. And if you make it really clear, I'll do whatever you say."

By her own account she expected nothing dramatic — and then God began to speak. That experience of two-way conversation became the seed of everything that followed: the ministry name, the books, the courses, the doctoral thesis. The origin story matters because it sets her register. She does not present hearing God as an elite gift she possesses and others lack; she presents it as something that began for her as a *terrified, ordinary* request and grew through years of trial and error. As she puts it of the early years: "In the beginning I struggled with the process a lot. There were tears, questions and plenty of arguments… the process doesn't change, but I have." ([eden.co.uk](https://www.eden.co.uk/blog/tania-harris-talks-god-conversations-and-cake-p129058), [hope1032.com.au — singles](https://hope1032.com.au/relationships/stop-waiting-singles-advice-tania-harris/))

A second, formative thread is **singleness**. Harris expected to marry — "I expected to get married… And then it didn't happen" — and instead planted and led a church as a single woman. She has chosen to teach openly out of that experience rather than hide it, and it shapes her pastoral credibility on hearing God in disappointment and unmet expectation: she is not a teacher whose own life went to plan. ([hope1032.com.au — singles](https://hope1032.com.au/relationships/stop-waiting-singles-advice-tania-harris/))

## Career & Ministry Arc

The arc runs in five stages, each feeding the next:

1. **Teacher** — trained and worked as a sports teacher (B.Ed, Deakin, Dux of Teaching 1991). The pedagogical instinct — teach a skill, build a curriculum, show rather than tell — never leaves her.
2. **Pastor / church planter** — answered call at 26, MA in Ministry, then planted and led an ACC church in a beachside Melbourne suburb in her early 30s, as a single woman. This is the field credibility under the later teaching.
3. **Bible-college faculty** — Harvest Bible College, Melbourne: lectured in ministry, pioneered online learning, Alumna of the Year 2013. The classroom and the pastorate run in parallel here.
4. **Ministry founder & broadcaster** — God Conversations launches as a ministry and podcast (~2013–2014); first Hope 103.2 feature, "Conversations with God," 24 Sep 2014. She relocates to Sydney and becomes Academic Dean of Hillsong College.
5. **Scholar & productized teacher** — doctoral research at Alphacrucis (c.2015–2020); the trade book (2017), PhD (2020), ecclesial sequel (2022), Brill monograph (2023), and *God Dreams* (2024); the full course funnel and the Regents TC visiting-lecturer role.

The through-line: **practice first, then theory, then a teachable system.** Harris did not theorise hearing God and then try it; she lived it as a young believer, taught it as a pastor and lecturer, and only later went and got the doctorate to explain — empirically and theologically — what she and her congregations had experienced. That ordering (lived → taught → researched → productized) is the signature of the whole corpus.

## Current Focus

- **God Conversations as a teaching enterprise**, not just a podcast — the free devotional and email feeding the "50 Days" flagship course, the "God Dreams" course, a German-language edition, and a **church-licence campaign** ("50 Days of God Conversations") that lets whole congregations run the teaching as a season.
- **Equipping pastors and leaders** — group training (a 7-week Zoom cohort hosted by Tania), pay-what-you-can leadership coaching, and a leaders' resources hub — building toward "the church who hears God's voice."
- **The 2024 *God Dreams* turn** — extending the hearing-God framework specifically into dreams and visions, with an accompanying course and a wave of interviews (Pneuma Review, Equipping the Church, Premier Christianity).
- **Academic standing** — the Brill monograph plus peer-reviewed articles keep one foot in the Pentecostal-studies academy; visiting lecturing at Regents TC (UK) extends that into formation.
- **Broadcast** — the monthly *God Conversations* podcast and the "Everyday God Conversations" strand on Hope 103.2, plus appearances on Premier Unbelievable?, TBN UK, and Eternity News.
