**PhD detail:** Alphacrucis College (Australia), awarded **2020**. Thesis: *"Towards a Theology of (Contemporary) Pentecostal Revelatory Experience"* — an empirical practical-theology study of "hearing God's voice" across three Australian Pentecostal churches, drawing on Mark Cartledge's "Testimony in the Spirit" method and Jeff Astley's "ordinary theology." Supervisors: **Jon Newton** (primary), **Angelo Cettolin**, and **Darren Cronshaw** (associate). The thesis was published commercially as the Brill monograph *Hearing God's Voice* (2023). ([brill.com/display/title/68992](https://brill.com/display/title/68992); [thesis PDF](https://d3nr8uzk0yq0qe.cloudfront.net/media/documents/Harris_Tania_-_Hearing_Gods_Voice.pdf))

**Note on the teacher → pastor → scholar arc:** Harris's first profession was education — she trained as a sports teacher, was named Dux of Teaching at Deakin in 1991, and began her career in the classroom. That pedagogical formation is worth flagging: her later ministry is built almost entirely around *teaching people to hear God* (courses, guidebooks, a "show, don't tell" method), and her doctoral work is itself a study of how ordinary believers learn to discern. The teacher never disappears; the whole God Conversations enterprise is, in effect, a curriculum.

## Ordination & Denomination

Harris is an **ordained minister with the Australian Christian Churches (ACC)** — the Australian Assemblies of God, the country's largest classical-Pentecostal national movement. She worships at Hillsong (her home church) and previously served as **Academic Dean of Hillsong College** in Sydney. The ACC's egalitarian position on women in ministry is part of why she can be both an ordained female pastor and a church planter; she has written that she came out of "a conservative background where women weren't allowed to carry the offering bag." ([regents-tc.ac.uk](https://www.regents-tc.ac.uk/blog/team-member/revd-tania-harris/), [cbeinternational.org](https://www.cbeinternational.org/person/tania-harris/))

## Published Works (overview)

Harris is a **focused, single-theme author**: every book orbits the same subject — how God speaks to ordinary believers and how to discern it. She has **four catalogued titles** (2017–2024), one of which is a peer-reviewed academic monograph:

- *God Conversations* (Authentic Media, 2017) — the breakout trade book; part memoir, part teaching.
- *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* (Paternoster, 2022) — the ecclesial sequel; discernment in community.
- *Hearing God's Voice* (Brill, *Global Pentecostal & Charismatic Studies* vol. 47, 2023) — her scholarly monograph, the published PhD.
- *God Dreams* (Authentic Media, 2024) — hearing God through dreams and visions.
