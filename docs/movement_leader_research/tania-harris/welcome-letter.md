Tania

It started with a prayer most of us would be too careful to pray. "Lord, speak to me. And if you make it really clear, I'll do whatever you say." You were around twenty-one. And then — as you tell it — he did. That single conversation has become the work of a lifetime: a podcast now past a hundred episodes, four books, a doctorate, courses in two languages, a church-wide campaign that congregations can run together, and a ministry whose whole premise is that what happened to you at twenty-one is not unusual at all. Hearing God, you keep insisting, is "a normal part of every Christian's life" — not for the wise and the weird, but for every believer. That conviction is rarer than it sounds, and it is exactly why we wanted to write to you.

What struck us as we sat with your work is how much you've already built, and how well. Most of the leaders we research have deep influence and almost no infrastructure. You're the other kind. You took your own God conversations and turned them into something a stranger can actually walk into: a free devotional and a fortnightly email at the front door, *God Conversations* and *The Church Who Hears God's Voice* and *God Dreams* across the middle, the "How To Hear God's Voice" course and "50 Days" for whole congregations at the deep end — and underneath all of it the Brill monograph and the PhD, doing the quiet structural work of making sure the popular teaching can bear weight. You give people the confidence to know they can hear from God *and* the humility to know they can get it wrong. You hand them the Three Tests — *Would Jesus say this? Is someone else saying this? Are spiritual signs following?* — so the gift is never ungoverned, never the lone prophet, always held inside the church. That balance is the whole thing. It's what makes you a voice worth trusting.

Here is the one thing the research kept surfacing, gently. The work is excellent and the work is scattered. Three publishers for four books. The courses on one subdomain, the podcast on three platforms, the writing split across your own blog and Premier Christianity and CBE and The Junia Project and Pneuma Review, the academic work in Brill and the journals and a thesis PDF on a cloud server. There's no Wikipedia page and no Scholar profile, and — this one matters — your own site quietly turns away the automated readers that increasingly decide what people find at all. The result is that someone searching "how do I know it's God's voice?" may never arrive at the person who has thought about that question more carefully than almost anyone writing today. The pieces are all there. They just don't yet live behind one findable front door.

We want to be precise about what Movemental is for, because it is *not* a replacement for anything you've made. Your courses are yours. The 50 Days licence is yours. The voice — story-first, warm, accessible, rigorous underneath — is unmistakably yours, and we'd protect it, not flatten it. What Movemental does is the consolidation and discoverability layer: gathering the scattered pieces into one searchable, machine-legible home so the books, the 106 episodes, the articles, and the courses finally point to each other; surfacing your back-catalogue as a findable teaching archive; building the SEO and GEO around the question you already own — hearing God's voice — so that when a believer or a pastor goes looking, the question and the woman who answers it arrive together. The funnel you built keeps doing its work. We just make the front of it findable, and we send the traffic *toward* your courses, never around them.

A year from now, the picture is simple. There's one front door. A reader who has never heard of God Conversations searches a question about hearing God, finds your corpus gathered in one place, follows a thread from a podcast episode into a chapter into the "How To Hear God's Voice" course, and joins a list that is yours. The niche you've quietly owned for a decade becomes discoverable, and the modest, faithful reach grows — not by changing the message, but by letting it be found.

We'd be honoured to have you in the ecosystem as a trusted voice on Spirit-led discipleship and hearing God — not as a recruit, not as a roster line, but as exactly what you already are: a credible, careful guide to one of the most important and most misunderstood questions in the church. You did the hard, slow work of building it well. We'd love to help the world find it.

— Movemental
