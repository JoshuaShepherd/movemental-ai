# Biography

Tiffany Smith — who also writes and brands her work as "Missional Mayhem" and is occasionally bylined TD Smith — is a Southern Baptist missiologist, missional strategist, leadership developer, and writer who has spent more than two decades mobilizing leaders at local and global levels. A Texas native with what she calls "a deep love for living the sent life," her vocation began overseas: roughly two years as an International Mission Board (IMB) missionary in Japan ignited a lifelong commitment to multiplying disciples and engaging the nations, a commitment that has shaped every role since.

She is academically trained for the work she does. She holds a Doctorate in Missional Leadership from New Orleans Baptist Theological Seminary (NOBTS), a Master of Divinity in Theology from Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary (SWBTS), and a Bachelor of Science in Psychology from Texas A&M University.

Her institutional path runs through the heart of Southern Baptist mission infrastructure. She served as a Missions Mobilization Associate with the Southern Baptists of Texas Convention, as a National Mobilizer with the Send Institute, and as a NAMB Pipeline Consultant building online training for churches developing a leadership pipeline. Since August 2021 she has served as Send Network Strategies Coordinator at the North American Mission Board (NAMB), coordinating church-planting strategy across North America. She has also been described as a NAMB Regional Equipper. Now based in the Atlanta metro, she carries the institutional fluency of someone who has worked inside denominational systems while increasingly putting her own voice into public through her personal platform, Missional Mayhem — a blog ("Perspectives on a Missional Life") and a podcast of the same name launched in 2025.
