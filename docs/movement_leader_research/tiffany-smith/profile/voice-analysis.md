# Voice Analysis

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tone | Warm, pastoral, candid, and quietly bold — encouraging without being saccharine; willing to name disappointment, weariness, and the cost of leading |
| Register | Practitioner-strategist crossed with devotional writer — equally at home in mobilization/pipeline language and in prayer, abiding, and Scripture |
| Posture | Catalytic and sending — addresses leaders and planters as people being developed and sent, not as an audience to entertain |
| Cadence | Reflective and rhythm-aware; titles and openings often pose a tension ("When Church Community Disappoints," "Do Not Grow Weary," "Chasing after glory…") before reframing it through Jesus |

**Recurring phrases**: "living the sent life", "multiplying disciples", "movement-focused work", "Movement Catalyst", "Perspectives on a Missional Life", "intentional, limited, and scalable relationships", "the ministry of presence", "from disruption to missional opportunity", "do not grow weary"

**Favorite metaphors**: **Jesus' relational circles — the 3, the 12, and the 72** (concentric rings of intimacy and sending); **the pipeline** (leaders developed and sequenced toward sending); **the table** (gathering, women's voices, hospitality); **abiding / anchoring rhythms** (the vine, roots, staying connected in chaos); **mayhem / chaos** (the disruptive, opportunity-laden cultural moment her brand name names); **the sojourner / coddiwomple** (purposeful travel toward an as-yet-unknown destination)
