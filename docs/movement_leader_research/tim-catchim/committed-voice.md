## Editorial bio

Tim Catchim writes, coaches, and pastors from Nashville, Tennessee, where the same theological surface — the fivefold ministry of Ephesians 4 — runs through every part of his work. With Alan Hirsch he co-authored The Permanent Revolution: Apostolic Imagination and Practice for the 21st Century Church (InterVarsity Press), followed by The Permanent Revolution Playbook: APEST for the People of God in 2014, a six-week format that put APEST into the hands of working teams.

In 2021 he founded Trimtab, a coaching and consulting agency built around APEST application, team dynamics, and organizational design, and now anchored by the Co-Lab community of practitioners. He simultaneously serves as Team Leader of OneLife, a movemental church plant in Nashville in its foundation-laying phase — the embodied lab where the theory, the playbook, and the coaching get tested in actual congregational life.

Catchim is multi-vocational by conviction, not circumstance. The same disposition that started a curbside recycling service and the Go Green Initiative now integrates atonement theology, MBTI, Enneagram, and APEST into the way he reads leaders and designs teams. A graduate of Faulkner University in Biblical Studies, he brings a builder's pragmatism to the apostolic vocabulary he helped popularize — translating frameworks into rhythms a Nashville church plant, a coaching cohort, and a writing partnership with Hirsch can each carry.
