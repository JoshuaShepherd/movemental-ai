# Movemental-Specific Analysis: Tim Catchim

**Research Date**: January 4, 2026

## Platform Gaps Assessment

### Current Platform Limitations

**Trimtab Website (trimtab.guide)**:
- **Ownership**: Owned platform (good)
- **Functionality**: Presents coaching/consulting services
- **Limitations**: May not fully support content publishing, community engagement, or content ecosystem building
- **Content Forms**: Primarily service descriptions, may not support articles, videos, courses

**OneLife Website (onelifenashville.wixsite.com)**:
- **Ownership**: Wix platform (rented)
- **Functionality**: Basic church website
- **Limitations**: Wix subdomain may impact SEO and discoverability
- **Content Forms**: Basic information, may not support rich content ecosystem

**Publisher Platforms**:
- **Ownership**: Rented platforms (Amazon, Apple Books, IVP)
- **Functionality**: Book distribution
- **Limitations**: No content ecosystem, no community engagement, limited discoverability beyond books

**Profile Platforms**:
- **Ownership**: Rented platforms (clay.earth, Hermeneutics Stack Exchange)
- **Functionality**: Professional profiles, discussion
- **Limitations**: No content ownership, limited content ecosystem

### Platform Fragmentation

**Current Fragmentation**:
- Content distributed across multiple platforms:
  - Trimtab (coaching/consulting)
  - OneLife (church)
  - Publisher platforms (books)
  - Profile platforms (professional profiles)
  - Discussion platforms (Hermeneutics Stack Exchange)

**Impact**:
- Content not unified in single ecosystem
- Discoverability challenges across platforms
- Limited cross-platform content interconnection
- Community engagement fragmented

### Platform Ownership

**Owned Platforms**:
- trimtab.guide (owned domain)

**Rented Platforms**:
- onelifenashville.wixsite.com (Wix subdomain)
- Publisher platforms (Amazon, Apple Books, IVP)
- Profile platforms (clay.earth)
- Discussion platforms (Hermeneutics Stack Exchange)
