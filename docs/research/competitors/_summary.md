## Three structural advantages Movemental holds

1. **Theological specificity grounded in named missiology (Hirsch's *The Forgotten Ways*, APEST, dual intelligences).** No competitor — including Gloo — has authored content depth at this level. "Values-aligned" is broad; missiological framing is specific. This is the single most defensible asset.

2. **Single-vendor delivery on all four stages (Safety, Sandbox, Skills, Solutions).** No competitor delivers all four in-house. Salesforce relies on partner ecosystem for Solutions. Blackbaud splits products and Pro Services. Deloitte's Dot Good and Accenture's nonprofit practices are services-only with no productized stages. Gloo's Solutions analog is delivered through Faith Assistant (a partner). Movemental's $50K Path Bundle is end-to-end with single-vendor accountability.

3. **Warm-network distribution through Hirsch + Brisco (NAMB Send Network).** Brisco's NAMB position and Hirsch's global movement-leader network are distribution channels no competitor can replicate. Pushpay sells via inside sales. Subsplash sells via inside sales. Virtuous sells via inside sales + partner channel. Big-4 sells via field sales to large foundations. None of them reach missional churches through trust networks the way Movemental can.

## Three structural risks

1. **Capital asymmetry.** Gloo (public, ~$873M valuation), Virtuous ($141M raised, $100M SGE round 2024), Subsplash (Roper-owned, public parent), and Salesforce (public, multi-hundred-billion market cap) have orders-of-magnitude more capital than Movemental. Capital does not guarantee winning — but in a category that's still being defined, capital lets competitors absorb experimentation costs Movemental cannot.

2. **Bundling pressure inside ChMS / CRM incumbents.** The pattern of 2025 has been incumbents shipping productized AI features inside existing platforms: Pushpay's three AI launches (People Search, Studio AI, Genny), Subsplash's Pulpit AI + Roper acquisition, Tithe.ly's TithelyAI, Bloomerang's AI Content Assistant + Journey Automation, Virtuous's Insights + Momentum AI, Salesforce's Fundraising Gift Proposals + Program Summaries, Blackbaud's Intelligence for Good. Every Movemental prospect already has at least one vendor telling them "we have AI." The risk is not that competitors out-innovate Movemental — it's that they out-bundle Movemental at the budget conversation.

3. **Big-4 consultancies entering the category.** Deloitte launched **Dot Good** in early 2026 — a dedicated nonprofit AI services suite at "discounted from Big-4" rates. Accenture has not yet matched, but is likely to follow within 18 months. Big-4 consultancies do not compete for individual churches, but at the institutional tier (large faith-aligned NGOs, denominational service arms, faith-based universities), they will.

## Recommended landscape positioning language

### For investor conversations

> "Our category has one venture-backed direct competitor — Gloo — which IPO'd in November 2025 at roughly $873M. They are a platform-and-marketplace play. Movemental is a partner-and-services play, with single-vendor delivery on all four stages of an AI adoption path: governance documentation, sandbox exploration, staff skills development, and bespoke solutions deployment. The closest *functional* analog in the larger market is Virtuous on the nonprofit-CRM side — they raised $100M in 2024 and bought three companies in 2025, but their economics are productized SaaS, not bespoke missionally-framed services. We don't compete with the ChMS incumbents on platform; we work above them. We don't compete with Big-4 on enterprise scale; we work below them. The structural moat is theological depth grounded in Alan Hirsch's missiology and warm-network distribution through Brad Brisco's NAMB position — neither of which is buildable with capital."

### For customer conversations

> "Most AI-for-church and AI-for-nonprofit options today are one of three things: a feature inside a software platform you already pay for, a free training program from a consulting firm whose actual product is something else, or a Big-4 engagement starting at six figures. Movemental is a fourth thing: a four-stage path that starts at $2,500 for a board-ready AI policy and tops out at $50,000 for the full Path Bundle, delivered by a team grounded in missional theology. We don't replace your ChMS, your CRM, or your giving platform. We do the formation work and the bespoke agent work that none of them are structured to deliver."
