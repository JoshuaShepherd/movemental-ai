## Conclusion

**The venture-backed AI-for-nonprofits / AI-for-churches startup category has exactly one company at meaningful scale as of May 2026: Gloo.**

This is itself useful intelligence. The category is structurally **uncrowded** — most "AI for the church" tooling is either:
- A feature inside an established ChMS / giving platform (Subsplash, Pushpay, Tithe.ly, Bloomerang, Virtuous)
- A small founder-led project without VC backing (Pastors.AI, Pewbeam, etc.)
- A consumer app outside the B2B competitive frame (Bible Chat)

The one venture-backed pure-play, Gloo, has now IPO'd — meaning the next entrant has to compete with a public-company benchmark. That raises the bar for new venture-backed entrants and gives Movemental room to differentiate on **theological specificity, single-vendor four-stage delivery, and movement-leader distribution** rather than competing for capital with Gloo's market cap.

## Recommended monitoring cadence

- **Quarterly**: Re-run search queries 1–4 above to catch new entrants
- **Trigger-based**: Watch for Y Combinator, Greylock, Andreessen Horowitz, or Sequoia investments tagged as faith-tech or nonprofit-AI
- **Event-based**: Monitor the Gloo AI & the Church Hackathon annual results — winners often surface as next-cycle startups
- **People-based**: Track Pat Gelsinger's investment activity; Joe Suh's next moves at Pastors.AI; any Gloo-Faith Assistant deeper integration that could spawn a spinout
