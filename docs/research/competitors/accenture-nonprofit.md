# Accenture Nonprofit Practice

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Dublin, Ireland (legal HQ); operational HQ distributed globally
- **Founded**: 1989 (as Andersen Consulting; rebranded Accenture 2001); **Accenture Development Partnerships** founded as Accenture's social-impact arm in 2003
- **Leadership**: Public partnership; Public Service practice and Accenture Development Partnerships are two relevant pillars
- **Employees**: ~775,000 globally; nonprofit-focused practitioners are a small sub-segment
- **Funding history**: Public on NYSE since 2001 (ACN)
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Public; ~$200B+ market cap
- **Public/private status**: Public (NYSE: ACN)

Accenture's nonprofit-relevant surfaces are (1) the **Public Service practice's nonprofit consulting offering**, which includes AI/data-driven decision making, and (2) **Accenture Development Partnerships** (ADP), the firm's social-impact arm that delivers below-cost services to NGOs. In 2025 Accenture announced an AWS partnership to "accelerate AI-powered reinvention and cloud modernization for public service organizations" and published their **Technology Vision 2025** centered on AI agent autonomy. Unlike Deloitte's Dot Good, Accenture has no recently-launched, dedicated *nonprofit-AI* brand as of the sources reviewed. [^1][^2][^3]

## 2. Category

**big-4** — Accenture is a global consulting firm. **Competing only at the institutional tier** for faith-aligned NGOs and large denominational service arms. Threat profile is below Deloitte's because Accenture has not (as of mid-2026) launched a dedicated nonprofit-AI brand analogous to Dot Good.

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- Public-company scale (~$70B annual revenue, ~$200B market cap)
- AWS partnership for AI + cloud modernization
- ADP brand and volunteer practitioner pool for development-sector NGOs
- Foundation network and federal grantee relationships

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- Theological depth and missiological framing — Accenture is generic enterprise consulting
- Practical price point for churches and small institutions
- Single-firm end-to-end delivery
- Movement-leader distribution
- Named refusals
- Speed and same-quarter delivery

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Accenture is a global consulting firm that does AI work for the largest faith-aligned NGOs at 7-figure engagement scale; Movemental is a faith-aligned, missionally-framed, four-stage path that Accenture's enterprise economics structurally cannot deliver.* Concede that Accenture is the right call for the largest international faith-based NGOs (the World Visions, Compassions, Habitat globals) that need a Big-4 brand and 7-figure engagement capacity.

In a sales conversation: do not pitch against Accenture at the $1M+ engagement level — those deals are not Movemental's ICP. For prospects below that threshold, **price, speed, and missiological grounding** are the differentiators. Movemental's $50K Path Bundle delivers more than an Accenture discovery phase costs.
