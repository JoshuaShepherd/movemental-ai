# Bloomerang

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Indianapolis, Indiana, USA [^1]
- **Founded**: 2012 by Jay Love [^1][^2]
- **Leadership**: Jay Love (Founder); Tammy Hammond (Chief Product Officer); Ann Fellman (CMO) [^1][^3]
- **Employees**: Estimated 300–500 (basis: ZoomInfo / LeadIQ ranges, not officially published) [^4]
- **Funding history**: PE-backed via JMI Equity and Warburg Pincus; $60M syndicated debt financing led by CIBC Innovation Financial Services in June 2023 [^2]
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Not publicly disclosed — *Estimated $400M–$800M based on Warburg Pincus / JMI ownership and 2023 debt round; not publicly confirmed*
- **Public/private status**: Private; PE-owned (JMI Equity + Warburg Pincus)

Bloomerang is a mid-market nonprofit CRM with explicit AI ambition. The 2024 launch of AI Content Assistant (described as the only giving platform with generative-AI writing inside the email builder at the time) and the July 2025 release of Journey Automation (multi-channel donor engagement) signal a sustained AI investment cycle. Public AI posture explicitly states no training on customer data and third-party penetration testing. [^3][^5]

## 2. Category

**nonprofit-crm** — Bloomerang is a mid-market nonprofit CRM, **partially in Movemental's category today** for the nonprofit segment via productized AI features, though without the Solutions-tier services motion or theological depth.

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- Mid-market nonprofit installed base (estimated tens of thousands of customers)
- PE-backed runway (JMI + Warburg Pincus)
- Productized AI shipping in market since 2024
- Strong brand among small-to-mid nonprofits

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- Theological depth and missiological framing
- Solutions tier — bespoke agent build with theological tuning
- Skills tier — paid cohort + LMS (not free training content)
- Church + faith-aligned-institution focus
- Movement-leader distribution
- Named refusals beyond a privacy policy

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Bloomerang is a productized nonprofit CRM with AI features for content and workflow; Movemental delivers a full four-stage path with theological grounding and a Solutions tier that productized CRM economics cannot replicate.* Concede that Bloomerang is a strong mid-market CRM and that AI Content Assistant solves a real writing-time problem for development teams. Don't compete with that.

In a sales conversation: do not contest Bloomerang's CRM or content-AI; concede them. Position Movemental as **the four-stage formation work that operates above Bloomerang's CRM** — Safety Documentation gives a credentialed AI policy artifact (Bloomerang has a privacy statement, not a board-ready policy), Skills Development is a paid cohort (Bloomerang offers free training content), and Solutions Deployment is bespoke agent work tuned to the organization's mission.
