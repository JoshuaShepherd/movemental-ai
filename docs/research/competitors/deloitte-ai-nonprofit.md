# Deloitte AI Nonprofit Practice (Dot Good + Monitor Institute)

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Deloitte global parent — London, UK; Deloitte US — Hermitage, NY operations + offices nationwide
- **Founded**: 1845 (Deloitte parent); Monitor Institute joined Deloitte 2013; **Dot Good launched early 2026** [^1][^2]
- **Leadership**: Deloitte US partnership; Monitor Institute by Deloitte and Dot Good are practice-area brands within the Strategy & Analytics service line
- **Employees**: Deloitte global ~460,000+; nonprofit-focused practice estimated low hundreds of dedicated practitioners (Dot Good is new and small as of 2026)
- **Funding history**: Private partnership; not applicable
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Not applicable (private partnership); Deloitte global revenue $67.2B+ FY2024
- **Public/private status**: Private partnership

Deloitte's relevant surface for Movemental is the recently-launched **Dot Good** initiative — a nonprofit AI services suite combining Deloitte's social-impact knowledge (via Monitor Institute by Deloitte) with AI/strategy capabilities, offered at discounted rates to address nonprofit affordability. Dot Good launched in early 2026 after Deloitte interviewed 50 nonprofit leaders to scope demand. It includes a pro bono AI learning series designed for nonprofit professionals at any level of AI maturity. [^1][^2][^3][^4]

## 2. Category

**big-4** — Deloitte is a Big-4 / global consulting firm, **competing only at the institutional tier** (large faith-aligned NGOs, denominational service arms, faith-based universities). Not a competitor for individual churches or small nonprofits.

GTM motion: **Field sales + executive relationships at large faith-aligned NGOs + foundation networks**. Deloitte's positioning to nonprofits is "discounted from our standard rates," which still places engagement size at 6+ figures minimum. [^2][^4]

Deloitte's Dot Good is **the closest analog in shape to all four Movemental stages**. The differences are scale (Deloitte engagements are 6-7 figures; Movemental's are 4-5 figures), theological framing (Deloitte is generic-mission), and ICP (Deloitte targets institutions; Movemental serves churches through institutions). ## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- $67B+ revenue parent and the Big-4 brand premium
- Existing relationships with foundations, large NGOs, and federal grantees
- Access to Deloitte's full AI/data engineering capability
- Pro bono AI Learning Series creating top-of-funnel awareness

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- Theological depth and missiological framing — Dot Good is generic-nonprofit, not faith-aligned
- Practical price point for churches and small institutions — Deloitte's "discounted" rates start at 6 figures
- Single-firm delivery on all four stages without partner handoffs
- Movement-leader distribution (Hirsch + Brisco)
- Named refusals — Deloitte's AI ethics work is broad-corporate, not movement-specific
- Speed — Dot Good engagements run on Deloitte timelines (months to start, multi-quarter delivery)

| Timeframe | Level | Reasoning |

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Deloitte's Dot Good is a Big-4 nonprofit AI consulting offering at 6-figure-plus engagement sizes, generic-mission and slow; Movemental is a faith-aligned, missionally-framed, four-stage path priced from $2,500 with same-month start and theological depth Dot Good's structural model cannot replicate.* Concede that Deloitte is the right call for the largest faith-aligned NGOs that need a Big-4 brand on the engagement.

In a sales conversation: do not pitch against Deloitte at the Fortune-500-foundation level — those deals are not Movemental's ICP. For mid-tier institutions where the prospect is comparing Dot Good and Movemental, the right move is **price + speed + theological framing**: Movemental delivers Stages 1–2 in weeks (not quarters), at a fraction of Deloitte's price, with named missiological grounding Deloitte's brand cannot match. The Path Bundle ($50K total) versus a single Deloitte phase is a defensible price comparison.
