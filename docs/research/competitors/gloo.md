# Gloo

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Boulder, Colorado, USA [^1][^2]
- **Founded**: 2013 [^1]
- **Leadership**: Pat Gelsinger (Executive Chairman & Head of Technology, appointed March 2025; former CEO of Intel) [^2][^3]
- **Employees**: Estimated 100–250 (basis: pre-IPO disclosures inferred; not officially published in sources reviewed)
- **Funding history**: ~$110M growth round in 2024 (pre-IPO); **IPO October–November 2025 raising $72.8M at ~$873M valuation** (filed for up to $109M, 9.1M shares at $10–$12); Pat Gelsinger backed Gloo personally for years [^2][^3][^4]
- **Current valuation / market cap**: ~$873M target IPO valuation (October 2025 prospectus); public stock price drives current market cap [^2]
- **Public/private status**: **Public** (IPO'd Nov 2025) — Gloo Holdings

Gloo is the most significant venture-backed AI-for-faith competitor in market. It IPO'd in November 2025 raising $72.8M, having previously raised $110M in growth funding. The company hired former Intel CEO Pat Gelsinger as Executive Chairman and Head of Technology in March 2025 — a major credibility signal. Gloo positions itself as the **technology platform serving the faith ecosystem** and has launched the **Flourishing AI Christian (FAI-C) Benchmark**, a public evaluation framework that explicitly grades how well frontier LLMs reflect Christian worldview and values. Gloo also operates the **AI & the Church Hackathon** (which Pastors.AI won in 2024). [^1][^4][^5][^6]

## 2. Category

**ai-startup** — Gloo is **already in Movemental's category**, on the missionally-framed AI side. The differences are scope (Gloo positions as ecosystem platform; Movemental as four-stage path with Solutions tier), structure (Gloo is now public; Movemental is privately held), and theological content (Gloo's stance is "values-aligned Christian worldview"; Movemental's is missiologically grounded in Hirsch's frame). This is the single closest direct competitor in the entire competitive landscape.

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- Public-company capital base (~$873M IPO valuation; ~$182M cumulative funding)
- Pat Gelsinger as Executive Chair — Big Tech industry credibility
- 12+ years of operating history and existing church customer base ("thousands")
- AI & the Church Hackathon as ecosystem-building muscle
- FAI-C Benchmark as a public artifact that frames the category
- Marketplace model that creates network effects

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- **Theological depth** — Gloo's "values-aligned" framing is broad-Christian; Movemental's missiological grounding (Hirsch's *Forgotten Ways*, APEST, etc.) is specific and authored
- **Bespoke Solutions tier** — Gloo's Solutions analog is delivered through partner Faith Assistant; Movemental delivers Solutions in-house with single-vendor accountability
- **Movement-leader distribution** — Brisco's NAMB Send Network position and Hirsch's global movement-leader network are distribution channels Gloo does not have
- **Named refusals** — Gloo's stance is values-alignment as a *positive* claim; Movemental can articulate what it explicitly will not do
- **Path Bundle pricing** — productized $50K end-to-end engagement vs. Gloo's platform-and-API metering economics
- **Formation-first orientation** — Movemental's stages are formation work; Gloo's are technology platform work

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Gloo is the public, Pat-Gelsinger-led, platform-and-marketplace play for values-aligned AI in the faith ecosystem; Movemental is the missionally-framed, formation-first, four-stage path with bespoke Solutions and warm-network distribution Gloo's platform economics structurally cannot deliver.* Concede that Gloo is the most credible AI-for-faith *platform* — the FAI-C Benchmark, the hackathon, and Gelsinger's involvement give them a category-defining position. Don't pretend otherwise.

In a sales conversation: name Gloo explicitly. The honest framing: Gloo is platform infrastructure, Movemental is formation partnership. They are not mutually exclusive. A church or institution can run on Gloo's platform and engage Movemental's four-stage path — in fact, Solutions Deployment may *integrate with* Gloo APIs where useful. The differentiation has three pillars:
1. **Theological specificity** — Hirsch's missiology vs. broad "Christian worldview"
2. **Single-vendor delivery on all four stages** — Movemental does Safety, Sandbox, Skills, and Solutions in-house; Gloo delivers Solutions through partners
3. **Movement-leader distribution** — Brisco's NAMB position and Hirsch's network mean Movemental reaches missional churches through trusted relationships, not platform marketing

**Strategic question for the team**: should Movemental pursue a partnership with Gloo (Movemental as a marketplace participant or aligned-services partner) or maintain parallel positioning? The answer probably depends on what Gloo's marketplace economics look like post-IPO. This is the most important strategic question in the competitive landscape.
