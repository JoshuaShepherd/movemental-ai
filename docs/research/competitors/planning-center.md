# Planning Center

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Carlsbad, California, USA [^1]
- **Founded**: 1996 (some sources cite 2006 for the modern Planning Center product; founder Jeff Berg) [^1][^2]
- **Leadership**: Jeff Berg (Founder & CEO); Jeff Spillman (long-tenure leadership) [^1]
- **Employees**: Estimated 100–200 (not officially published) [^3]
- **Funding history**: **Founder-owned and bootstrapped — never raised outside funding, debt-free**; in 2024 formed the Ministry Centered Foundation and declared the company "officially not for sale" [^1]
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Not applicable — private, bootstrapped, explicitly not-for-sale
- **Public/private status**: Private; founder-owned; structurally insulated from M&A via the Ministry Centered Foundation

Planning Center is the bootstrapped, transparent-pricing, no-sales-team alternative to the PE-and-VC-backed players in the ChMS category. They publish prices, sell via the product, and operate small (estimated low-100s headcount). The 2024 Ministry Centered Foundation move makes them structurally non-acquirable, which matters for competitive forecasting — they will not be rolled into a Roper or a Subsplash. Their AI posture is deliberately cautious: a 2025 list-building AI feature was launched inside "PicoLabs" (their tech sandbox) before being moved to the main product, with explicit no-training-on-customer-data contractual language. [^4][^5]

## 2. Category

**chms** — Planning Center is a ChMS / services-platform incumbent. **Not in Movemental's category today** and unlikely to enter aggressively given their bootstrapped, cautious posture. The threat profile is meaningfully different from Pushpay or Subsplash because their growth thesis is restraint, not acquisition.

## 3. Product offerings

| Offering | Description | Price | Customer segment | GTM motion |
|----------|-------------|-------|------------------|------------|
| Services | Service planning, scheduling, song/team management | Tiered $14–$199/mo (transparent) [^6] | Worship-led churches | Self-serve only |
| People (CRM) | Member database, groups, check-in | Tiered self-serve [^6] | Churches all sizes | Self-serve |
| Giving | Online + recurring giving | Bundled or standalone | Churches | Self-serve |
| Calendar / Registrations / Check-Ins / Groups / Publishing | Modular product suite | Each priced separately | Churches | Self-serve |
| AI list-building (beta in PicoLabs, 2025) | Natural-language search to find people groups | Beta — pricing TBD [^4] | Existing customers | Feature add |

GTM motion: **Pure product-led growth — no sales team, transparent pricing, no contracts**. Modular pricing means churches pay per product family. Posture is explicitly "we will not provide an AI tool we don't trust." [^1][^4]

Planning Center's *posture* is the closest competitive surface to Movemental's Safety Documentation framing — they publicly model restraint and named refusals around AI. But it is content/posture, not a paid governance product. [^4]

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- 20+ year incumbent product line and deep installed base in worship-team workflows
- Trusted-brand status among small-to-mid worship-led churches
- Bootstrapped operating discipline (long-term cost advantage)
- Public AI posture that resonates with theologically careful customers

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- Sales motion and Solutions tier — Planning Center has no high-touch enterprise practice
- Bespoke agent build — Planning Center's product-led-growth thesis structurally precludes services
- Skills/cohort programs — no formation track of any kind
- Path bundle pricing reaches institutions; Planning Center's modular self-serve does not

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Planning Center is a careful self-serve product company with a worship-team center of gravity; Movemental is a high-touch formation and Solutions partner with theological depth and a bespoke agent build path that PCO's product-led-growth thesis structurally precludes.* Concede that PCO has the most coherent and trustworthy AI restraint statement in the category. That is real, and it should not be contested.

In a sales conversation: do not contest PCO's stack — most prospects who run PCO are running it for service planning and worship-team scheduling, which Movemental does not replace. Position Movemental as the **layer of formation work that lives above PCO's operational tools** — the AI policy and stance work, the staff-formation cohort, and the bespoke agent build that PCO will not ship by design. Use PCO's restraint posture as a positive — the church already takes AI seriously, and Movemental is the partner that builds on that posture.
