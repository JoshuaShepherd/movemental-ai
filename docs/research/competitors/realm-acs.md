# Realm by ACS Technologies

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Florence, South Carolina, USA (additional offices in Greenville, SC and Phoenix, AZ) [^1][^2]
- **Founded**: 1978 (ACS Technologies, the parent company) [^1]
- **Leadership**: Privately held; specific 2025 leadership not publicly listed in primary sources reviewed
- **Employees**: Estimated 200–500 across product lines (basis: LinkedIn / Tracxn ranges, not officially published) [^3]
- **Funding history**: Privately held. No reported outside funding rounds.
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Not publicly disclosed
- **Public/private status**: Private; long-tenured family/founder ownership pattern

ACS Technologies is the longest-running ChMS incumbent in the US market — 47+ years in business serving ~50,000 churches, schools, and faith-based organizations. Realm is the modern web-based ChMS in the ACS portfolio, alongside legacy products ACS, PDS, and HeadMaster. The company's posture is institutional and stable rather than aggressive — they are not a 2025 AI-headlines company in the way Pushpay, Subsplash, or Virtuous are. [^1][^4][^5]

## 2. Category

**chms** — Realm is a legacy ChMS incumbent. **Not in Movemental's category today** and structurally unlikely to enter. The threat profile is below the other ChMS players because there is no public AI roadmap or M&A momentum in the sources reviewed.

## 3. Product offerings

| Offering | Description | Price | Customer segment | GTM motion |
|----------|-------------|-------|------------------|------------|
| Realm | Modern web-based ChMS (people, accounting, community, giving, events) | Custom — contact sales [^4] | Mid-large churches, schools | Inside sales |
| ACS | Legacy desktop/server ChMS | Maintenance pricing | Long-tenure customers | Migration motion |
| PDS | Catholic-specific ministry software | Custom | Catholic dioceses + parishes | Diocesan-level deals |
| HeadMaster | School/academy administration | Custom | Faith-based schools | Inside sales |

GTM motion: **Inside sales + denominational and institutional relationships built over decades**. Realm does not publish prices and pricing is contact-sales only. [^4]

## 4. Overlap with Movemental's four stages

| Movemental stage | Competitor offering | Overlap level |
|------------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Safety Documentation ($2,500) | None published | none |
| Sandbox Discovery ($15,000) | None | none |

Realm overlaps with Movemental at **zero stages today**. Their threat to Movemental is purely competitive-mindshare (a church running Realm has a vendor relationship that creates noise in a Movemental sales motion), not product overlap. [^1][^4]

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- 47-year operating history and deep relationships with denominations and large legacy churches
- ~50,000 customer footprint across churches, schools, and faith-based organizations
- Catholic-specific product (PDS) and faith-based-school product (HeadMaster) reaching adjacent segments

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- AI-native architecture — Realm is a legacy modernization on top of older code
- Public AI roadmap and named stance — Realm has neither in the 2025 sources reviewed
- Theological depth and named missiological frame
- Bespoke agent build (Solutions stage) — Realm sells productized software only
- Movement-leader distribution

## 6. Threat assessment

| Timeframe | Level | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Today (2026) | low | No public AI roadmap; vendor relationship creates noise but does not directly compete with Movemental. |
| Year 2 (2027) | low | Slow-moving incumbent; without an M&A event or capital infusion, unlikely to enter Movemental's category. |
| Year 5 (2030) | medium | Long-tenure customer base is a defensible asset that a future buyer (PE or strategic) could re-platform onto AI-native infrastructure; this is the only credible path to elevated threat. |

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *ACS / Realm is the legacy administrative system of record for many large traditional churches and faith-based schools; Movemental is the AI-native formation and Solutions layer that does not replace Realm but does work the church or denomination has not had a partner for.* Concede that Realm's strength is institutional continuity and that switching ChMS systems is high-friction; Movemental's offer should not require that switch.

In a sales conversation where the prospect runs Realm: route directly to Safety Documentation and Skills Development as the entry points — Movemental's work fits *above* Realm's system of record. The Solutions stage can integrate with Realm via API where needed without displacing it. Use Realm's stability as a positive in the pitch — the church already has its admin spine, and Movemental is the formation layer that spine never had.
