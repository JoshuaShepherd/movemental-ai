# Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud (Agentforce Nonprofit)

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: San Francisco, California, USA (Salesforce parent)
- **Founded**: 1999 (Salesforce); Salesforce.org (the nonprofit arm) was a separate entity merged into Salesforce in 2019
- **Leadership**: Marc Benioff (Salesforce Chair & CEO); Salesforce.org has its own leadership pillar inside the parent company
- **Employees**: ~76,000 globally (parent company); the nonprofit cloud team is a sub-segment, not separately broken out
- **Funding history**: Public company since 2004 (NYSE: CRM)
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Public; market cap fluctuates around $250B–$300B (parent)
- **Public/private status**: Public (NYSE: CRM)

Salesforce's nonprofit offering has gone through a major repositioning in 2024–2025. The legacy Nonprofit Success Pack (NPSP) is being de-emphasized in favor of Nonprofit Cloud — recently rebranded **Agentforce Nonprofit** to align with Salesforce's enterprise-wide AI agent push. Pricing: 10 free licenses via the Power of Us program, then $60/user/mo Enterprise or $100/user/mo Unlimited. The 2024 Salesforce nonprofit AI announcement introduced **Fundraising Gift Proposals** (generative AI personalized proposals) and **Program Summaries**. [^1][^2][^3]

## 2. Category

**nonprofit-crm** — Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud is the enterprise-tier nonprofit CRM. **Already in Movemental's category for the institutional segment** through productized AI agents (Agentforce Nonprofit), though without faith-aligned theological framing.

## 3. Product offerings

| Offering | Description | Price | Customer segment | GTM motion |
|----------|-------------|-------|------------------|------------|
| Nonprofit Cloud / Agentforce Nonprofit | Enterprise nonprofit CRM with AI agents | $60/user/mo Ent; $100/user/mo Unlimited; 10 licenses free via Power of Us [^1] | Mid-large + institutional nonprofits | Field sales + partner ecosystem |
| Fundraising Gift Proposals | Generative AI personalized donor proposals | Bundled in upper tiers [^4] | Mid-large nonprofits with MGOs | Feature add |
| Program Summaries | Generative AI program performance summaries | Bundled [^4] | Program-heavy nonprofits | Feature add |
| Einstein for Nonprofits | Underlying AI platform | Bundled / metered | All nonprofit customers | Platform layer |
| Data Cloud | Customer data integration layer | Bundled in Einstein 1 editions | Enterprise tier | Bundled |
| NPSP (legacy) | Free Salesforce package on Sales Cloud | Free + Sales Cloud licenses | Long-tenure customers | Migration motion to NPC |

GTM motion: **Field sales + Power of Us nonprofit discount program + massive partner / consultancy ecosystem (RSM, Fionta, Plative, Belmar, Twistellar, etc.)**. The partner ecosystem is the most distinctive structural feature — Salesforce sells the platform; partners sell services. [^5][^6]

## 4. Overlap with Movemental's four stages

| Movemental stage | Competitor offering | Overlap level |
|------------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Safety Documentation ($2,500) | Salesforce trust framework + Einstein Trust Layer; not nonprofit-specific or theologically grounded | adjacent |
| Sandbox Discovery ($15,000) | Trailhead + sandbox orgs are exploration environments for builders, not formation cohorts | adjacent |
| Skills Development ($15k cohort + $5k/yr LMS) | Trailhead is a free LMS; Salesforce certifications are paid; partner-led admin training is widespread | adjacent |
| Solutions Deployment (from $30,000) | Massive partner ecosystem delivers bespoke implementations every day | analogous |

This is one of two profiles where overlap reaches **analogous** on Solutions — Salesforce's partner-delivered implementations are functionally a Solutions Deployment for any large nonprofit. The differences are theological framing and missiological grounding, not the existence of a services motion. [^5][^6]

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- Public-company scale, capital, and brand
- Largest nonprofit CRM partner ecosystem in the world
- Power of Us discount creating natural lead-flow
- Trailhead — best-in-class free training infrastructure for the nonprofit sector
- Einstein/Agentforce platform investments measured in billions

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- Theological depth and missiological framing — Salesforce nonprofit work is generic-mission
- Single-vendor delivery — Movemental delivers all four stages directly; with Salesforce, the prospect must hire a separate partner for implementation
- Faith-aligned ICP focus — Salesforce serves all nonprofits; Movemental's focus narrows the relevance of the work
- Movement-leader distribution

## 6. Threat assessment

| Timeframe | Level | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Today (2026) | medium | Salesforce wins the institutional-tier RFP by default in many faith-aligned NGOs; Movemental loses the platform conversation but can still win the formation work alongside it. |
| Year 2 (2027) | high | Agentforce Nonprofit will mature; expect more out-of-the-box AI agents that displace use-cases Movemental might otherwise win. |
| Year 5 (2030) | high | Salesforce becomes the default platform for any institutional faith-aligned nonprofit, and the partner ecosystem will increasingly include faith-aligned implementation firms. |

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Salesforce / Agentforce Nonprofit is the platform of record for institutional nonprofits with a partner ecosystem that delivers implementations; Movemental is not a Salesforce partner and not trying to be — Movemental is the missionally-formed AI partner that operates above any platform, including Salesforce.* Concede that Salesforce is the right platform call for many large faith-aligned NGOs and that Movemental should not try to displace it.

In a sales conversation: never compete on the platform layer. If the prospect is on Salesforce or moving to Agentforce Nonprofit, **route the work to Solutions Deployment** as agents that integrate with Salesforce via API. Position Movemental as the partner that does *what Salesforce partners do not* — theological tuning, missiological framing, named refusals, formation-first staff capability building. The win is co-existence with Salesforce, not displacement. Movemental should explore becoming a Salesforce ecosystem partner if the math works.
