# Subsplash

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Seattle, Washington, USA [^1]
- **Founded**: 2005 (originally a design and consulting studio; pivoted to church technology with the launch of the first iPhone church apps 2009–2015) [^1]
- **Leadership**: Tim Turner (Co-founder & CEO); Kristy Turner (Co-founder) [^1][^2]
- **Employees**: ~293–311 (293 reported with 2024 revenue; ~311 as of February 2026) [^3]
- **Funding history**: K1 Investment Management was the prior controlling investor; July 21, 2025, K1 sold Subsplash to Roper Technologies (NYSE: ROP) for $800M [^4][^5]
- **Current valuation / market cap**: $800M acquisition price (July 2025); now a Roper Technologies portfolio company [^4][^6]
- **Public/private status**: Subsidiary of Roper Technologies (public parent, NYSE: ROP)

Subsplash is the second-largest US church-tech platform by customer count. It serves more than 20,000 faith-based organizations and reported ~$61.3M revenue in 2024. Its July 2025 sale to Roper Technologies — a public industrial-software conglomerate known for buy-and-hold serial acquisition — is the most consequential 2025 event in the ChMS category and changes the competitive picture: Subsplash now has Roper's balance sheet and acquisition machinery behind it. [^4][^5][^7]

## 2. Category

**chms** — Subsplash positions itself as an "AI-powered engagement platform" rather than a pure ChMS. The bundle includes mobile apps, websites, livestream, giving, ChMS, group messaging, and Pulpit AI sermon repurposing. Like Pushpay, it is **not in Movemental's category today** but is the most likely incumbent to converge on AI-services territory inside its installed base.

## 3. Product offerings

| Offering | Description | Price | Customer segment | GTM motion |
|----------|-------------|-------|------------------|------------|
| Subsplash app + website | Custom mobile app + church website | Estimated ~$149/mo basic [^8] | Churches all sizes | Inside sales |
| Subsplash Giving | Online + recurring + text-to-give | $0/mo + 2.3–2.99% card / 1% ACH | Bundled or standalone | Inside sales |
| Subsplash Tap (2025) | NFC tap-to-give and tap-to-engage | Bundled | Existing customers | Feature add |
| Pulpit AI | Sermon → 20+ content pieces (clips, devotionals, small-group guides, transcripts) in pastor's voice | Bundled in upper tiers; standalone available [^9] | Preaching teams | Feature add |
| ChMS / media / livestream / messaging | Full engagement bundle | Estimated $300–600/mo mid-tier; $700–1,200+/mo enterprise [^8] | Mid-large churches | Bundle / contract |

GTM motion: **Inside sales + bundled multi-year contracts (12–24 months with early termination penalties)**. Branded as the "Ultimate Engagement Platform." Pricing structure is opaque and contact-sales-only on the website. [^8][^10]

## 4. Overlap with Movemental's four stages

| Movemental stage | Competitor offering | Overlap level |
|------------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Safety Documentation ($2,500) | No policy/governance product; AI is sold as a feature [^9] | none |
| Sandbox Discovery ($15,000) | No analog | none |
| Skills Development ($15k cohort + $5k/yr LMS) | No cohort or formation offering | none |
| Solutions Deployment (from $30,000) | Pulpit AI is the closest analog — productized AI for sermon repurposing inside the platform; not bespoke build | adjacent |

Pulpit AI is meaningful as a product because it shows Subsplash is willing to ship a vertical AI feature with a clear ministry frame (sermon-to-discipleship-content). It is **not a Solutions Deployment** in Movemental's sense — it is a self-serve productized feature, not a custom agent build with theological tuning. The risk is that under Roper, Subsplash could acquire or spin up the consulting wrap around Pulpit AI to compete for Solutions budget. [^4][^9][^11]

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- 20,000+ existing church relationships [^7]
- Roper Technologies parentage — public-company balance sheet, M&A capability, multi-decade hold horizon [^4][^5]
- A shipping AI-for-pastors feature (Pulpit AI) with name recognition
- Integrated giving + app + website + ChMS bundle that creates switching cost

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- AI-native architecture vs. Subsplash's bundle-of-apps with AI grafted on top
- Theological depth and missiological framing (Hirsch corpus) — Pulpit AI is content generation, not formation
- Warm-network distribution (Hirsch + Brisco/NAMB) — Subsplash sells via inside sales
- Named refusals and stance — Subsplash markets AI as ubiquitous productivity, not bounded craft
- Bespoke agent build (Solutions Deployment) — Subsplash has no consulting motion

## 6. Threat assessment

| Timeframe | Level | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Today (2026) | medium | Pulpit AI is in-market and demos well to pastors; Subsplash sales reps will frame AI as solved within their stack and slow Movemental deals. |
| Year 2 (2027) | high | Roper's playbook is layered acquisitions inside a vertical; Subsplash could buy a faith-tech AI consultancy or formation-cohort vendor and bolt it on. |
| Year 5 (2030) | high | If Roper invests in vertical AI services on top of Subsplash, the bundled offering could occupy three of Movemental's four stages (Sandbox, Skills, Solutions) inside the 20K-church base — though theological depth remains unmatched. |

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Subsplash sells engagement infrastructure with a content-AI feature on top; Movemental builds the missional-formation layer with theological depth, named refusals, and a bespoke agent build path that Pulpit AI is structurally incapable of replicating.* The honest concession is that Pulpit AI is a real, useful product for pastors who want sermon clips and discussion guides — and that's a different problem than Movemental solves.

In a sales conversation where the prospect already runs Subsplash: do not contest Pulpit AI or the engagement bundle. Concede those. Position Movemental as **what happens after the church has decided AI is part of ministry life and now needs a stance and a build path** — Safety Documentation gives the policy Pulpit AI's deployment requires, Skills Development builds staff capability across departments (not just the preaching team), and Solutions Deployment delivers agents tuned to the church's missiology. Frame Subsplash as a complementary surface that benefits from Movemental's formation work, not a replacement target.
