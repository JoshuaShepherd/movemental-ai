# Tithe.ly

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Nashville, Tennessee, USA (operations across 3 continents — US, Australia, UK) [^1][^2]
- **Founded**: 2013 (CEO Dean Sweetman started building the product in 2012) [^3]
- **Leadership**: Dean Sweetman (Co-founder & CEO) — pastor and church-planter background (planted 50+ churches before founding Tithe.ly); Frank Barry (Co-founder) [^3]
- **Employees**: ~136–139 (136 reported with 2024 revenue; ~139 as of February 2026) [^4][^2]
- **Funding history**: Reported relationship with Ministry Brands portfolio (date and structure unclear from public sources; Tithe.ly continues to operate under its own brand and CEO) [^5][^1]
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Not publicly disclosed. Revenue figures vary by source: $18.7M (Nov 2024 GetLatka) vs. $50–100M estimate (LeadIQ); use the GetLatka figure as the more rigorously sourced number [^4]
- **Public/private status**: Private (relationship with Ministry Brands portfolio reported but not confirmed via primary source)

Tithe.ly is the price-disruptor in the church-tech category. Its $119/month All-Access bundle and free giving tier (2.9% + $0.30 per card) sit deliberately below Pushpay and Subsplash. The customer base is reported between 37,000–50,000 churches across sources — large by count, smaller per-church than Pushpay. Tithe.ly NEXT 2025 (the company's product event) introduced Tithely 3.0, Tithely Tap (NFC giving), QuickGive, and a beta of TithelyAI. [^6][^7][^8]

## 2. Category

**chms** — Tithe.ly is a ChMS / giving incumbent at the SMB-church end of the market. It does not currently target the institution tier. **Not in Movemental's category today**, but their AI roadmap (TithelyAI) and Sweetman's pastor-credibility position them to compete on Movemental's edges within the SMB-church segment.

Sweetman's personal brand and explicit "AI for the Modern Church Leader" content marketing position the company as friendly to small-church pastors. [^7][^10]

Tithe.ly's "AI for the Modern Church Leader" content is the closest soft-overlap with Safety Documentation — they're publicly framing themselves as the friendly AI guide for pastors. It's content marketing, not a paid governance product, but it occupies adjacent mind-space. TithelyAI in beta is too early to assess as a Solutions analog. [^7][^10]

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- 37,000–50,000 reported church relationships, weighted toward SMB churches
- Self-serve product-led growth motion (lowest friction in category)
- Sweetman's personal credibility as a pastor-turned-CEO (~30 years ministry, 50+ church plants)
- Free giving tier creates a permanent foothold in churches that may upgrade later

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- Theological depth via Hirsch corpus — Sweetman has church-plant credibility but Movemental has missiological grounding
- Multi-tenant agent architecture — Tithe.ly is a SaaS product stack, not an agent platform
- Solutions-tier bespoke build — Tithe.ly only sells productized SaaS
- Institution-tier capacity — Tithe.ly is built for SMB churches; Movemental's $30K+ Solutions stage and Path Bundle reach institutions
- Named refusals — Tithe.ly markets AI permissively as "easy-to-use AI tools to save time"

## 6. Threat assessment

| Timeframe | Level | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Today (2026) | medium | Direct overlap is low (different category) but Tithe.ly's "AI for Church Leaders" content sits in front of pastors who would otherwise be looking at Movemental's Safety Documentation. |
| Year 2 (2027) | medium | TithelyAI GA in 2026 will give them a productized AI feature, but their team size (~140 people) and price-point bias keep them out of the Solutions tier. |
| Year 5 (2030) | medium | Without a major capital event or repositioning, Tithe.ly remains a ChMS/giving incumbent for SMB churches, not a Movemental competitor. The threat ceiling is competing for Safety Documentation budget at the small-church end. |

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Tithe.ly sells SaaS at a self-serve price point with a friendly pastor-CEO front; Movemental delivers theological depth, a four-stage formation path, and bespoke agent build that no $119/month bundle can include.* The honest concession is that Tithe.ly's pricing makes it the right call for a 200-attendance church that wants giving + a basic app. Movemental should not pretend to compete in that segment.

In a sales conversation: do not contest Tithe.ly's product or price — the prospects who land on Movemental's surface have already self-selected as larger or more theologically intentional than Tithe.ly's core base. Position Movemental as **the next layer up after Tithe.ly has solved giving and basic ChMS** — Safety Documentation is the AI policy Tithe.ly's free content marketing surfaces but does not deliver as a credentialed artifact; Skills Development is the staff-formation cohort Tithe.ly's self-serve model cannot run; Solutions Deployment is the bespoke agent work Tithe.ly's SaaS catalog has no slot for.
