# Virtuous

## 1. Current state

- **Headquarters**: Phoenix, Arizona, USA [^1]
- **Founded**: 2014 [^1]
- **Leadership**: Gabe Cooper (Co-founder & CEO); Nathan Chappell (Chief AI Officer, appointed 2025) [^1][^2]
- **Employees**: Estimated 200–400 (basis: PitchBook/LeadIQ ranges; not officially published) [^3]
- **Funding history**: $141M total raised across 8 rounds; $100M growth round from Susquehanna Growth Equity in September 2024 [^1][^4]
- **Current valuation / market cap**: Not publicly disclosed; the 2024 SGE round implies a valuation in the high-9-figure range — *Estimated $700M–$1.2B based on $100M growth round at typical SGE multiples; not publicly confirmed*
- **Public/private status**: Private; venture / growth-equity-backed (Susquehanna Growth Equity)

Virtuous is the most aggressive AI-for-nonprofits player in Movemental's adjacent space. The 2024 $100M raise from Susquehanna Growth Equity funded a 2025 acquisition spree (Momentum AI for major-gift agents, VOMO for volunteer management, RaiseDonors for digital fundraising) and the appointment of Nathan Chappell — a recognized voice in fundraising AI — as Chief AI Officer. Customer count is 10,000+ nonprofits including Ronald McDonald House, Habitat for Humanity, and Arkansas Children's Hospital. Virtuous is the single Tier 1 competitor that is *already in Movemental's category* on the nonprofit side. [^1][^4][^5]

## 2. Category

**nonprofit-crm** — Virtuous sells "Responsive Fundraising" CRM with AI as a first-class layer. Unlike the ChMS players, Virtuous is **already a direct competitor for the nonprofit segment of Movemental's market today**. The Momentum AI acquisition adds an explicit "intelligent agent" for major gift officers — analogous in shape (though different in theological grounding) to a Movemental-built agent.

## 4. Overlap with Movemental's four stages

| Movemental stage | Competitor offering | Overlap level |
|------------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Safety Documentation ($2,500) | No formal AI policy / governance product; Chappell's role implies policy-shaping content but not a paid artifact | adjacent |
| Sandbox Discovery ($15,000) | Virtuous Insights + Momentum AI provide a productized AI surface inside the CRM — closest competitor to a Sandbox feel, though not an exploration-and-curation environment | adjacent |
| Skills Development ($15k cohort + $5k/yr LMS) | Virtuous User Summit + community + content; no paid cohort or formal LMS | adjacent |
| Solutions Deployment (from $30,000) | Momentum AI is a productized "intelligent agent for MGOs" — closest analog in market to a Movemental agent build, but productized self-serve, not bespoke | analogous |

This is the only Tier 1 competitor where the overlap level reaches **analogous** in the Solutions stage. Virtuous + Momentum AI is the closest thing to "an AI agent built specifically for fundraising work in a nonprofit." It is productized, not bespoke; it is not theologically grounded; but it is in market and selling. Movemental should treat Virtuous as the benchmark to beat for nonprofit prospects — not the ChMS players. [^5][^7]

## 5. Structural advantages and disadvantages versus Movemental

**They have that Movemental does not:**
- $141M of cumulative capital and a 2024 $100M growth round — orders of magnitude more than a bootstrapped or seed-stage Movemental [^1][^4]
- 10,000+ nonprofit customer relationships including major brands (Ronald McDonald House, Habitat) [^1]
- Three 2025 acquisitions (Momentum, VOMO, RaiseDonors) consolidating the nonprofit-AI stack
- Chief AI Officer (Nathan Chappell) with public profile and conference circuit presence
- Annual user summit creating dedicated mindshare

**Movemental has that they do not:**
- Theological depth and missiological framing — Virtuous is generic-nonprofit, with no opinionated formation lens
- Bespoke Solutions tier — Virtuous sells productized AI, not custom agent builds with theological tuning
- Multi-tenant agent architecture explicitly sized for movement-leader plus organizational platforms
- Warm-network distribution through Hirsch + Brisco — Virtuous distributes through standard nonprofit-software channels
- Named refusals — Virtuous markets AI as scale-and-personalization, with no public boundary statements
- Church segment focus — Virtuous serves nonprofits broadly; Movemental's church orientation is differentiated

## 7. Recommended Movemental positioning response

**One-sentence differentiator**: *Virtuous sells productized fundraising AI at scale to any nonprofit; Movemental delivers theologically-grounded, missionally-formed agent work for churches and faith-aligned institutions — Virtuous's product can never be that, and their growth-equity thesis structurally biases against becoming it.* The honest concession is that Virtuous is the most credible AI-for-nonprofits company in market today and that Momentum AI's MGO agent is a real productivity tool. Concede that Virtuous is the right call for a generic-mission nonprofit that needs faster donor outreach. Don't pretend otherwise.

In a sales conversation: do not contest Virtuous's CRM + Momentum AI for major-gift productivity; concede that. Position Movemental as **the formation layer above the CRM** — Safety Documentation is the AI policy Virtuous's customers are starting to ask for and the platform doesn't deliver as a credentialed artifact; Skills Development is the staff-formation cohort Virtuous's User Summit *gestures at* but does not run as a paid program; Solutions Deployment is the bespoke agent build with theological tuning that Virtuous's productized-software economics cannot replicate. Where overlap is real (nonprofit fundraising), explicitly invite the comparison: name Virtuous, name what they do well, name what they structurally cannot do, and route the prospect to Stages 1 and 4 first.
