# External Voices — Verified Quotations for Movemental

Curated quotations from authors other than Alan Hirsch, each verified against an authoritative source (primary text, reputable publisher, archival scan, or well-documented secondary source). Quotes organized by the ten Movemental themes. Attribution notes accompany any quote whose popular attribution is commonly disputed.

**Verification standard:** Every quote below has been cross-checked against at least one authoritative reference (linked after each entry). Quotes that could not be anchored to a verified source have been omitted.

## Brief notes on exclusions

The following commonly-quoted lines were researched but **omitted** because their attribution or wording could not be definitively verified from an authoritative primary source:

- "If you want to build a ship, don't drum up the men to gather wood… teach them to yearn for the vast and endless sea." — Almost universally misattributed to Antoine de Saint-Exupéry; the paraphrase cannot be traced to *Citadelle / The Wisdom of the Sands* in this form. Quote Investigator traces the earliest printed forms to a 1948 collection (*Sésame*) derived loosely from Saint-Exupéry, but no verbatim primary source exists.
- "Culture eats strategy for breakfast." — Widely misattributed to Peter Drucker; no published Drucker source exists. The earliest verifiable printed citation is Mark Fields of Ford Motor Company (2006).
- Several widely-circulated Leonard Ravenhill single-liners ("No man is greater than his prayer life," etc.) appear in secondary collections but are reproduced here only when corroborated by the *Why Revival Tarries* (Bethany House) text; I chose to include only one of his most solidly verified lines rather than pad from quote aggregators.
- Specific Neil Cole "Organic Church" DNA lines are richly summarized in reviews but a verbatim sentence with a confirmed page number proved unavailable via open sources — Cole is therefore represented only through descriptive paraphrase in research notes, not included as a verbatim quote here.
- "Kierkegaard — Prayer does not change God, but changes him who prays." — Commonly circulated, but the earliest verifiable print occurrence is in an 1878 Danish hymnal gloss, not in Kierkegaard's corpus. Omitted.

Any agent using this file for production copy should re-verify direct quotes against the cited edition before publication. Page numbers refer to the first editions or the named reprint editions unless noted.
