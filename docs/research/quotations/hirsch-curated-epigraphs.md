# Hirsch-Curated Epigraphs

**Verification:** These quotes were extracted verbatim from the chapter opening blocks in Hirsch's published books (corpus at `/Users/joshuashepherd/Desktop/dev/repos/alan-books/corpus/alan_hirsch/`). Each appears as an epigraph above a specific chapter. Where Hirsch's citation differs from scholarly consensus, a note appears inline.

**Why this file:** Hirsch chooses his epigraphs like a DJ picks opening tracks. These are movement-shaping, paradigm-shifting lines he's vetted over decades of teaching and writing. For Movemental, they are the single most reliable "Hirsch-adjacent smart-voice" quote pool.

## 1. Movement, Paradigm & Change

> "A great deal more failure is the result of an excess of caution than of bold experimentation. The future is not entered by men and women of caution."
> — **Anonymous / proverbial**, as epigraph to *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 1 "A View from the Edge"
> *Themes: movement, boldness, risk*

> "In a time of drastic change it is the learners who inherit the future. The learned usually find themselves equipped to live in a world that no longer exists."
> — **Eric Hoffer**, epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 2 "Preface / Introduction"
> (From Hoffer's essays; reflective of his broader writing on change and adaptation.)
> *Themes: movement, learning, adaptation*

> "The great Christian revolutions came not by the discovery of something that was not there before, but by the realization of something that was always there."
> — **Hans Urs von Balthasar**, epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 2 "Preface / Introduction"
> *Themes: movement, recovery, latent*

> "No great improvements in the lot of mankind are possible, until a great change takes place in the fundamental constitution of their modes of thought."
> — **John Stuart Mill**, epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 2 "A View from Above"
> *Themes: movement, paradigm, thought*

> "There is nothing more difficult to carry out, nor more doubtful of success, nor more dangerous to handle, than to initiate a new order of things."
> — **Niccolò Machiavelli**, *The Prince* (1532) — cited in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 13 "Appendix 1"
> *Themes: movement, risk, innovation*

> "The composition of this book has been for the author a long struggle of escape … a struggle of escape from habitual modes of thought and expression. The ideas which are here expressed so laboriously are extremely simple and should be obvious. The difficulty lies, not in the new ideas, but in escaping from the old ones, which ramify, for those brought up as most of us have been, into every corner of our minds."
> — **John Maynard Keynes**, preface to *The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money* (1936), cited repeatedly as epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways*
> *Themes: movement, paradigm, unlearning*

> "After a time of decay comes the turning point. The powerful light that has been banished returns. There is movement, but it is not brought about by force. The movement is natural, arising spontaneously. The old is discarded and the new is welcome. Before the change, conditions are without form and void. Afterward, there is perfect clarity."
> — **Ancient Chinese saying** (from the *I Ching*, Hexagram 24, "Fu / Return"), epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 2
> *Themes: movement, cycles, emergence*

> "We are the people of the parenthesis — at the end of one era but not quite at the beginning of the next one. Maps no longer fit the new territories. In order to make sense of it all, we must cultivate a vision."
> — **Jean Houston**, epigraph in *The Shaping of Things to Come* — ch. 11 "Imagination and the Leadership Task"
> *Themes: movement, liminal, vision*

> "The empires of the future will be the empires of the imagination."
> — **Winston Churchill**, speech at Harvard University, September 6, 1943 — cited in *The Shaping of Things to Come* — ch. 11
> *Themes: movement, imagination, future*

> "Everything is fine, but the ship is still heading in the wrong direction."
> — **Edward de Bono**, epigraph in *The Shaping of Things to Come* — ch. 9 "The Medium Really Is the Message"
> *Themes: movement, direction, systems*

## 2. Platform, Systems & Design

> "If you have attempted change, improvement, advancement, and growth, but have not changed the underlying structures that cause the current tendencies for behavior, you are unlikely to succeed."
> — **Robert Fritz**, epigraph in *Metanoia* — ch. 8 "Platformed"
> *Themes: platform, structure, change*

> "Every system is perfectly designed to get the result it gets."
> — **W. Edwards Deming**, epigraph in *Metanoia* — ch. 8 "Platformed"
> *Themes: platform, systems, design*

> "The platform revolution is here, and the world it is ushering in is here to stay."
> — **Sangeet Paul Choudary**, epigraph in *Metanoia* — ch. 8 "Platformed"
> (Author of *Platform Revolution* and *Platform Scale*.)
> *Themes: platform, revolution, technology*

> "Since everything, then, is cause and effect, dependent and supporting, mediate and immediate, and all is held together by a natural though imperceptible chain, I hold it impossible to know the parts without knowing the whole and to know the whole without knowing the parts in detail."
> — **Blaise Pascal**, *Pensées* — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 9 "Organic Systems"
> *Themes: platform, interconnected, whole*

> "We are leaving the age of organized organizations and moving into an era where the ability to understand, facilitate, and encourage processes of self-organization will be crucial."
> — **Gareth Morgan**, *Imaginization* (1997) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 9 "Organic Systems"
> *Themes: platform, self-organization, networks*

> "The most probable assumption is that no currently working business theory will still be valid ten years hence. And yet, surprisingly few executives accept the reality that turning a business around requires fundamental changes in the assumptions on which the business is run."
> — **Peter Drucker**, "A Turnaround Primer" — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 9 "Organic Systems"
> *Themes: platform, assumptions, turnaround*

> "The first responsibility of a leader is to define reality."
> — **Max De Pree**, *Leadership Is an Art* (1987) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 8 "APEST Culture"
> *Themes: platform, leadership, reality*

## 3. Discipleship, Formation & Practice

> "We can only live changes: we cannot think our way to humanity. Every one of us, every group, must become the model of that which we desire to create."
> — **Ivan Illich**, epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 5 "Disciple Making"
> *Themes: discipleship, practice, living*

> "The greatest proof of Christianity for others is not how far a man can logically analyze his reasons for believing, but how far in practice he will stake his life on his beliefs."
> — **T.S. Eliot**, "Christianity and Culture" (1939) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 5 "Disciple Making"
> *Themes: discipleship, staking-life, witness*

> "You never know how much you really believe anything until its truth or falsehood becomes a matter of life or death to you."
> — **C.S. Lewis**, *A Grief Observed* (1961) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 7 "Liminality and Communitas"
> *Themes: discipleship, belief, test*

> "The clergy-laity dichotomy … is one of the principal obstacles to the church. The idea that only 'holy men,' namely, ordained ministers, are really qualified and called to ministry has done great damage to the church's mission."
> — **Howard Snyder**, *The Community of the King* — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 8 "APEST Culture"
> *Themes: discipleship, priesthood, dichotomy*

> "The spontaneous expansion of the Church reduced to its elements is a very simple thing. It asks for no elaborate organization, no large finances, no great numbers of paid officials. It has nothing to do with these things and would be hindered by them. It depends on nothing but the Spirit of God working through men and women who are indwelt by that Spirit, and who are willing to die to themselves that Christ may live in them."
> — **Roland Allen**, *The Spontaneous Expansion of the Church* (1927) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 4
> *Themes: discipleship, spontaneous, simplicity*

> "The greatest proof of Christianity for others is … how far in practice he will stake his life on his beliefs."
> — **T.S. Eliot**, same as above — featured here for cross-theme reference
> *Themes: discipleship, practice, risk*

> "It is not enough to say one's prayers in private, maintain high personal morality, and attend church regularly. All the different aspects of our world need to be interrogated in the light of the unique achievement of Jesus."
> — **Jim Wallis**, epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 4
> *Themes: discipleship, whole-life, public*

## 4. Technology, Media & Culture

> "The medium really is the message."
> — **Marshall McLuhan** (echoed in chapter title) — chapter theme of *The Shaping of Things to Come* — ch. 9
> (Hirsch structures an entire chapter around this claim.)
> *Themes: technology, medium, message*

> "If everyone lives roughly the same lies about the same things, then there is no one to call them lies; they jointly establish their own sanity and call themselves normal."
> — **Ernest Becker**, *The Denial of Death* (1973) — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 4 "Kultcha-Schmultcha"
> *Themes: technology, culture, sanity*

> "Human existence cannot derive its ultimate meaning from society because society itself is in need of meaning."
> — **Abraham Heschel**, *Who Is Man?* (1965) — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 4 "Kultcha-Schmultcha"
> *Themes: technology, culture, meaning*

> "Because the Light is radically different from the Darkness, the manifestation of the Light always opposes the Darkness."
> — **Gene Davenport**, epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 4 "Kultcha-Schmultcha"
> *Themes: technology, contrast, witness*

## 5. Imagination, Seeing & Paradigm

> "If the doors of perception were cleansed every thing would appear to man as it is, infinite. For man has closed himself up, till he sees all things through narrow chinks of his cavern."
> — **William Blake**, *The Marriage of Heaven and Hell* (1790–93) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 2; also quoted in *Reframation*
> *Themes: imagination, perception, infinite*

> "To my mind, there must be at the bottom of it all, not an utterly simple equation, but something so compelling, and so inevitable, so beautiful, we will all say to each other, 'Oh, how could we have been so stupid for so long?'"
> — **John Wheeler**, former chair of the Department of Physics, Princeton — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 3 "Preparing for the Journey"
> *Themes: imagination, beauty, inevitability*

> "We do not see things as they are, but as we are."
> — **Jewish proverb** (also attributed to Anaïs Nin in *Seduction of the Minotaur*, 1961) — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 7 "The Lying Mirror"
> *Themes: imagination, perception, self*

> "From the lying mirror to the movement of stars, everybody's looking for who they are."
> — **Bruce Cockburn**, "The Gift" — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 7
> *Themes: imagination, identity, search*

> "There are two ways to live your life. One is as though nothing is a miracle. The other is as though everything is a miracle."
> — **Albert Einstein**, widely attributed (earliest documented in an Einstein letter, 1946) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 13
> *Themes: imagination, wonder, miracle*

> "The right to search for the truth implies also a duty; one must not conceal any part of what one has recognized to be true."
> — **Albert Einstein**, in a speech at the Nobel Anniversary dinner (1954) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 2 "A View from Above"
> *Themes: imagination, truth, duty*

## 6. Risk, Adventure & Liminality

> "If you want to build a ship, don't summon people to buy wood, prepare tools, assign tasks, and organize the work. Rather, teach them to long for the endless immensity of the sea."
> — **Often attributed to Antoine de Saint-Exupéry** — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 1 "A View from the Edge"
> (Attribution note: Quote Investigator traces the paraphrase to a 1948 secondary collection; no verbatim source in Saint-Exupéry's *Citadelle / The Wisdom of the Sands*. Hirsch cites it as Saint-Exupéry; use with the attribution note if publishing in a primary-source-strict venue.)
> *Themes: risk, vision, yearning*

> "I should have been a true and passionate Christian. The Adventurer. But now I live in 1924, and the Christian venture is done. The adventure is gone out of Christianity. We must start on a new venture towards God."
> — **D.H. Lawrence**, *Last Poems* / letters (1924) — epigraph in *The Faith of Leap* — ch. 1 "The Spirit's Edge"
> *Themes: risk, adventure, venture*

> "When we get out of the glass bottles of our ego, and when we escape like squirrels turning in the cages of our personality and get into the forests again, we shall shiver with cold and fright but things will happen to us so that we don't know ourselves. Cool, unlying life will rush in, and passion will make our bodies taut with power, we shall stamp our feet with new power and old things will fall down, we shall laugh, and institutions will curl up like burnt paper."
> — **D.H. Lawrence**, quoted extensively in *The Faith of Leap* — ch. 1
> *Themes: risk, wildness, institutions*

> "It is the unknown that defines our existence. We are constantly seeking, not just for answers to our questions, but for new questions. We are explorers."
> — **Benjamin Sisko** (character), *Star Trek: Deep Space Nine* — epigraph in *The Faith of Leap* — ch. 1
> (Sisko's voice-over in the episode "Explorers." Hirsch cites as "Ben Cisco.")
> *Themes: risk, explorers, questions*

> "Some things benefit from shocks; they thrive and grow when exposed to volatility, randomness, disorder, and stressors and love adventure, risk, and uncertainty. … The antifragile gets better."
> — **Nassim Nicholas Taleb**, *Antifragile* (Random House, 2012) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 7 "Liminality and Communitas"
> *Themes: risk, antifragility, volatility*

> "Spontaneity, total commitment, and primal responses that arise from the depths — these are the raw materials out of which a missionary church is made."
> — **John V. Taylor**, *The Go-Between God* (SCM, 1972) — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 3 "The Spirit's Edge"
> *Themes: risk, spontaneity, missionary*

## 7. Church, Community & Witness

> "Strictly speaking one ought to say that the Church is always in a state of crisis and that its greatest shortcoming is that it is only occasionally aware of it. … To encounter crisis is to encounter the possibility of truly being the Church."
> — **David Bosch**, *Transforming Mission* (Orbis, 1991) — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 7
> *Themes: church, crisis, authenticity*

> "We argue that the political task of Christians is to be the Church rather than to transform the world. … The Church doesn't have a social strategy; the Church is a social strategy."
> — **Stanley Hauerwas**, *The Peaceable Kingdom* / developed across his corpus — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 5 "The Church That Jesus Built"
> *Themes: church, strategy, being*

> "It is easy to think that we love an abstract, spiritualized, de-historicized Church just as it is easy to love abstract, spiritualized, de-historicized people. In truth to love abstractions is not to love at all; it is but a sentimental attachment to our own whimsies."
> — **Richard John Neuhaus**, *Freedom for Ministry* — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 5
> *Themes: church, abstraction, love*

> "The Church is nothing but a section of humanity in which Christ has truly taken form."
> — **Dietrich Bonhoeffer**, *Ethics* — epigraph in *Untamed* — ch. 5
> *Themes: church, humanity, incarnation*

> "The gospel must be proclaimed afresh in new ways to each generation, since every generation has its own unique questions."
> — **Often cited in missiological literature**; appears in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 6
> *Themes: church, generation, gospel*

> "What we have is … a pagan society whose public life is ruled by beliefs which are the direct result of the rejection of Christianity — it is far tougher and more resistant to the gospel than any of the pagan societies with which the church has been in contact during the past two hundred years. Here, without possibility of doubt, we are at the end of a chapter of missionary history."
> — **Lesslie Newbigin**, *Foolishness to the Greeks* / *The Gospel in a Pluralist Society* — epigraph in *The Forgotten Ways* — ch. 1
> *Themes: church, post-christendom, missionary*
