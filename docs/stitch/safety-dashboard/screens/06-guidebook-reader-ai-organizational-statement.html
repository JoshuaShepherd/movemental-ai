AI Organizational Statement

Establishing the foundational posture for our institution's engagement with generative systems.

Section 01 of 05 | Estimated reading time: 6 mins

The advent of generative artificial intelligence represents a profound shift in the technological landscape, one that demands a thoughtful, deliberate, and deeply grounded response. As an institution dedicated to the preservation and interpretation of human knowledge, we must approach these new tools not with uncritical enthusiasm, but with a rigorous commitment to our core values of stewardship, authenticity, and human agency.

Our engagement with these systems must be guided by a clear understanding of their capabilities and, more importantly, their limitations. AI models, despite their impressive output, lack the essential human capacities for genuine understanding, empathy, and moral reasoning. They are sophisticated pattern-matching engines, not conscious entities. Therefore, they must remain tools in the service of human endeavor, never substitutes for human judgment.

In our curation and presentation of knowledge, we assert the primacy of the human author. The stewardship of the digital image and text requires a commitment to provenance and transparency. When generative systems are employed in our workflows—whether for summarization, translation, or data analysis—their use must be clearly disclosed, and their outputs rigorously verified by subject matter experts.

Furthermore, we must be vigilant against the inherent biases embedded within these models, which reflect the inequalities and prejudices of the data upon which they are trained. Our institutional mandate requires us to actively interrogate these biases and to ensure that our use of AI does not perpetuate or amplify marginalization. We must cultivate a technological posture that is interrogative, ethical, and unwaveringly committed to the truth.
