THE GUIDEBOOK · LAYER 01

AI Organizational Statement.

What we believe about AI in relation to our mission.

In review Last revised 2 days ago | Open comments (3)

I. Foundational Posture

Our engagement with artificial intelligence is neither entirely defensive nor reflexively enthusiastic. It is governed by our core mission to foster human flourishing. In this context, we recognize the profound capability of generative tools while remaining deeply committed to the stewardship of the digital image. We do not view these technologies as replacements for human insight, but as instruments that require rigorous contextual grounding.

"Technology must remain subordinate to the curatorial intent of the institution. We do not archive the machine's output; we archive the human's selective direction of it."

II. Constraints & Boundaries

All generative models deployed within our ecosystem must adhere to a strict 'Category-of-Care' protocol. This ensures that algorithmic summarization or generation is distinctly marked and never presented as authoritative primary source material.

Furthermore, the implementation of these tools must undergo quarterly review by the ethics council, ensuring alignment with our theological grounding and preserving the tactile, editorial quality of our digital environment. The 'Field Guide' aesthetic demands human precision, and AI is permitted only where it aids that precision without dilution.
