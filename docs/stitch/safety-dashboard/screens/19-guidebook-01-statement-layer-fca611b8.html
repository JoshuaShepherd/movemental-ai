THE GUIDEBOOK · LAYER 01

AI Organizational Statement.

What we believe about AI in relation to our mission.

In review | Last revised 2 days ago | chat_bubble Open comments (3)

1. Introduction & Posture

As an organization dedicated to human flourishing, we recognize that artificial intelligence represents a profound shift in technological capability. We approach this shift not with fear, but with careful stewardship, ensuring that these tools serve our core mission rather than redefining it.

This statement outlines our foundational beliefs regarding the integration of AI systems into our operations, pastoral care, and communication strategies. It serves as the bedrock for all subsequent policies and procedural guidelines.

"Technology must always remain a tool in the hands of the faithful, never a replacement for genuine human connection and pastoral discernment."

2. Theological Grounding

Our understanding of technology is rooted in the belief that human beings possess unique dignity and moral agency. While AI can process information at unprecedented scales, it cannot possess wisdom, empathy, or spiritual discernment.

Therefore, we mandate that AI systems are utilized strictly for auxiliary, administrative, and analytical tasks, strictly forbidding their use in contexts requiring deep empathetic engagement or definitive moral judgments.

3. Scope of Application

This statement applies to all staff, volunteers, and third-party vendors operating on behalf of the organization. It governs the procurement, deployment, and daily use of any software system categorized as 'artificial intelligence' or 'machine learning'.
