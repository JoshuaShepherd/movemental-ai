## Joshua Shepherd

**Role:** Founder & Lead Engineer

**One-line (card):** Full-stack architect with deep experience in platform infrastructure, AI systems, and movement-leader technology. Builds the system layer that holds everything together.

**Bio:**

Joshua Shepherd is a writer, formation guide, and the creator of Trail Guide — a hybrid digital and relational ecosystem designed to equip spiritual leaders and imaginative practitioners for life in a rapidly evolving machine age. His work bridges disciplines and dissolves silos: theology and technology, trust and experimentation, wisdom and tools.

Joshua is not a futurist by training, but a shepherd by instinct. Over the past decade, he has led spiritual formation initiatives, co-developed ethical frameworks with nonprofit and faith-based leaders, and served as a behind-the-scenes architect of dialogue between communities often kept apart by pace or power. His recent work centers on the question: *How do we remain human in the age of machines — and how do we do it together?*

He is the founder and host of several small-scale experimental cohorts that bring together movement leaders, spiritual innovators, and those wrestling vocationally with emerging technologies. Those cohorts reflect a commitment to relationship-first design, a deep theology of incarnation, and a belief that faithful imagination is a communal act.

At Movemental, Joshua leads product and engineering — translating that same posture into infrastructure for thought leaders and formation-minded organizations.

## Alan Hirsch

**Role:** Missional Strategist

**One-line (card):** Movement thinker, author, and founding influence behind Movemental's formation philosophy. Decades of work in missional theology, church planting, and leadership development.

**Bio:**

Alan Hirsch is known for helping the church recover its apostolic nature — and rediscover movement as its native expression. His work centers on a Christological conviction: the risen Christ already coded into his church everything it needs for movement. Our task is recovery, not invention. From leading a missional community in Melbourne's inner city to shaping networks across six continents, Alan's journey reflects the theology he teaches: lived practice inseparable from deep reflection. His current work focuses on translating four decades of frameworks, writing, and teaching into formational resources that equip leaders wherever they are.

Alan has written widely on apostolic movement, discipleship, and the renewal of the church. His books — including *The Forgotten Ways*, *5Q*, *Reframation*, and *The Permanent Revolution* — have shaped the conversation around missional ecclesiology across six continents. He co-founded the 5Q Collective and Movement Leaders Collective, and partners with 100 Movements and Forge to train and equip leaders for missional engagement.

**Credentials (optional footer):** Author of *The Forgotten Ways*, *5Q*, *Reframation*, *The Permanent Revolution*, *Untamed*, and more. Co-founder of 5Q Collective and Movement Leaders Collective. Partner with 100 Movements and Forge. Speaker and movement catalyst working across six continents.

## Brad Brisco

**Role:** Strategic Partner

**One-line (card):** Missional church strategist, author, and multiplication leader — helping churches and planters bridge institutional structures and grassroots mission.

**Bio:**

Brad Brisco is a missional church strategist, author, and multiplication leader who has shaped how North American churches understand missional transition, covocational church planting, and neighborhood-based mission. Through published books, curriculum resources, organizational leadership with NAMB's Send Network, and extensive training roles (including Forge America and Forge Kansas City), he has established himself as a bridge between institutional church structures and grassroots missional movements.

Raised in Wichita, Kansas, Brad spent more than a decade in the restaurant business before entering full-time ministry. He has taught at the college level for many years (including courses in Christian history, Paul, discipleship, theology of place, and apostolic movements) and writes and teaches on missional ecclesiology, place, hospitality, and sustainable models of leadership — including the integration of marketplace vocation with planting and sending.

Representative books include *The Missional Quest* and *Next Door as It Is in Heaven* (with Lance Ford), *Covocational Church Planting*, and contributions to the broader missional conversation through resources such as *Missional Essentials* and *ReThink*. He co-founded the Sentralized conference and maintains an active voice through the Missional Church Network and other channels.
