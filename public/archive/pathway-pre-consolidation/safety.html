The architecture of Safety

Safety produces a five-layer Guidebook. Each layer does a different kind of work. Skip any of them and the others cannot hold.

AI is being used inside your organization right now — on personal accounts, in the margins of real work, with no shared frame for what's allowed. Safety brings that use into the open by naming five layers your senior leadership owns together.

  1. 01

    Statement

    The AI Use Charter — a brief, public-facing declaration of what AI is and is not for in your organization. It is the document a donor, board member, or reporter reads to know where you stand.

    + AI Use Charter

  2. 02

    Policy

    The operational policies your staff actually use. They translate the Charter into specific guidance for the people who make AI decisions at 9pm on a Tuesday.

    + Acceptable Use Policy

    + Communication to the Constituent Base

  3. 03

    Context

    The boundaries of human presence your mission cannot abdicate. Pastoral, programmatic, educational, clinical — named specifically, not as categories.

    + Pastoral, Programmatic & Educational Care Boundaries

  4. 04

    Rules

    The rules your organization sets about data and disclosure: what may be shared with AI, by whom, under what review; and when AI involvement must be named to the people you serve.

    + Data Handling Standards

    + Disclosure & Attribution Standard

  5. 05

    Response Plans

    The plans your organization has in place for when AI is used against you or produces harmful output under your name. Pre-arranged protocols, not improvised reactions.

    + Voice Cloning & Impersonation Response Plan

The seven artifacts inside the five layers are what your board reviews and ratifies. The layers are the memory of why each artifact exists.

SafeStart — the facilitated version of Safety. $1,000.

Two weeks of facilitated work.

Two weeks. Four phases. Eight hours of synchronous work plus drafting between sessions. We facilitate with your leadership team — we do not draft policy alone and hand it over.

  1. Week 1 — Phase 01

    Day-one alignment

    A 90-minute facilitated session with your senior leadership. We surface what you already know, what is contested, and what nobody has decided yet. Sets the boundaries for the next two weeks.

  2. Week 1 — Phase 02

    Drafting

    We author the five layers against your specific organizational context. You review iteratively, asynchronously, with one named owner per layer on your side.

  3. Week 2 — Phase 03

    Synchronous review

    A two-hour session in week two. Leadership reads the drafts together, surfaces remaining disagreement, and decides what to keep, what to revise, and what to refuse.

  4. Week 2 — Phase 04

    Ratification handoff

    Final versions land inside your private organizational dashboard, where your leadership team reviews, comments, revises, and ratifies each artifact. You walk into your next leadership meeting with the answer approved, or with the dashboard open in front of the room.

Your senior team owns this work from day one. We draft against your specific organizational context; you review, revise, and ratify. We do not delegate Safety to a side project, and we do not let you delegate it to your most technical staff member.
