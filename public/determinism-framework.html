Where AI Fits

Determinism, discernment, and what stays human.

Green light

Never only human

Write · Translate · Plan · Test · Code

Deterministic. Same inputs → same kind of output. Skills needed. Using AI here is always only okay.

Why green? These tasks have clear criteria and repeatable outcomes. What’s needed are skills—knowing how to prompt, review, and correct. AI can do the heavy lifting; the human brings judgment about the goal and the bar. No moral or relational singularity at stake.

Yellow light

Sometimes only human

Communicate · Coach · Counsel · Collaborate · Create

Non-deterministic. Same inputs can yield different outcomes. Discernment and character are needed. Sometimes only human.

Why yellow? These involve other people, context, and meaning. Outcomes depend on relationship, timing, and who’s in the room. Discernment and character are required: when to use AI as a draft or aid, and when the presence and voice of the human are irreplaceable. Not never AI; not always AI. Sometimes only human.

Red light

Always only human

Pray · Preach · Proclaim · Prophesy

Human will and restraint. These require the person and, where faith is at stake, the divine. Always only human.

Why red? These are acts of person and vocation, not just content production. They require human will and restraint—who stands before God and before the community, who speaks in first person, who bears the weight of the word. The agency and the accountability are always human (and, in faith, divine). AI does not pray, preach, proclaim, or prophesy.
