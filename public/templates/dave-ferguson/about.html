About

Dave Ferguson

Co-founding pastor, movement builder, and multiplication strategist — helping churches multiply churches and leaders multiply leaders.

01

A life built around multiplication.

Dave Ferguson is the co-founding pastor of Community Christian Church in Naperville, Illinois — recognized as one of America's most influential churches. What began as a church plant has grown into a multi-site missional community with a singular focus: reproduction over addition.

He is the visionary leader of NewThing Network, an international church-planting movement that equips and connects church planters around the world. In 2025, NewThing joined forces with Exponential Conference, where Dave serves as President and CEO — leading the world's largest church planting conference.

Dave's work isn't about building a personal brand. It's about creating systems that multiply — churches that plant churches, leaders who develop leaders, and frameworks that reproduce across contexts.

02

Background & education

Dave holds a Bachelor of Arts in Bible and Communications from Lincoln Christian University (1986) and a Master of Arts in Educational Ministries, specializing in Church Planting, from Wheaton Graduate School (1991).

He lives in the Chicago area with his wife, Sue. They have three children: Amy, Joshua, and Caleb.

His writing — including Hero Maker, Exponential, B.L.E.S.S., Finding Your Way Back to God (co-authored with his brother Jon Ferguson), and others — has been published by Zondervan, Multnomah, and WaterBrook Multnomah.

Why one platform?

Dave's credibility in the room — Community Christian Church, NewThing, Exponential, the conference stage, church planting networks — is clear. Online, it's partial: his name and books show up; his talks and movement resources add to it. But the full picture isn't assembled in one place that multiplies.

Movemental is built so that voice stays central — same mission, same message — with content that's findable, connected, and able to reproduce through a unified ecosystem.
