The cost of being the hero

In the previous lesson we explored why leaders naturally default to the hero role. It feels good. People celebrate you. The organization seems to depend on you. But there is a significant cost to carrying the hero identity, and in this lesson we will confront it honestly.

The personal cost

When you are the hero, everything runs through you. Every decision, every initiative, every crisis lands on your desk. At first this feels empowering, but over time it becomes exhausting.

The organizational cost

Hero-driven organizations hit a ceiling. They can only grow as large as the hero's capacity. When the hero is absent, things stall.

The church was never meant to be an institution that dispenses religious goods and services. It was meant to be a movement.

Reflection questions

  1. Where in your leadership are you carrying the hero burden?
  2. What warning signs do you recognize in your organization?
  3. Who are the people around you whose potential is being limited by your hero role?
