Introduction

About four years prior to releasing the first edition I attended a seminar on missional church where in making a point the speaker asked a question: "How many Christians do you think there were in the year AD 100?" He then asked: "And how many do you think there were when Constantine came on the scene, say, AD 310?"2 Here are the somewhat surprising answers:

  • AD 100: as few as 25,000 Christians
  • AD 310: up to 20,000,000 Christians

He then asked the question that has haunted me to this day: "How do you account for the fact that a tiny, persecuted Jewish sect became the most significant religious force in the Roman Empire in two centuries?"

It was as if God was calling me to dedicate myself to finding the answer. It was a quest to understanding what it is that could produce such amazing growth and impact. I wanted to apply my findings for the church in any other time and place, including of course the twenty-first century. I totally dedicated myself to the task. This quest led me to discover a generative system that undergirds all genuine, highly transformative expressions up, which I call movement DNA, or mDNA for short. And herein, I believe,
