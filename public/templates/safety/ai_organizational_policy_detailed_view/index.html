LAYER 02

AI Organizational Policy

What we will do, and what we refuse to do, operationally

Ratified May 21, 2026 Last revised May 18, 2026 Annual review due May 2027

This policy establishes the boundaries of artificial intelligence deployment within the Northridge Community Center ecosystem. It is designed to preserve human agency, dignity, and direct relational care while leveraging computational systems for structural and administrative support. The integration of AI is not viewed as an inevitable progression to be accepted passively, but as a specific capability to be governed tightly.

We operate from the theological and ethical premise that computational systems lack moral agency, spiritual discernment, and the capacity for true empathy. Therefore, any task requiring pastoral care, final judgment on human standing, or the representation of a human's sincere voice must remain exclusively within the human domain. AI serves as a tool for amplification of human intent, not a substitute for human presence.

The following directives are binding upon all staff, volunteers, and contracted partners operating within our technical infrastructure. Violations of these operational boundaries are considered breaches of community trust and will be addressed through the standard governance accountability processes outlined in Layer 01 of the Guidebook.

CORE BOUNDARIES

Named Refusals

Specific applications of AI technology that are explicitly prohibited within our operational context.

01

Eligibility determinations

AI systems shall not be used to autonomously determine an individual's eligibility for community resources, pastoral care, financial assistance, or leadership roles. Algorithmic sorting or scoring of community members based on engagement metrics or demographic data to restrict access is strictly forbidden.

02

Behavioral surveillance

We refuse the deployment of AI-driven facial recognition, sentiment analysis, or predictive behavioral modeling on our campuses or within our digital platforms. The gathering of community members must be characterized by trust and freedom, not automated scrutiny or the creation of behavioral dossiers.

03

Personal voice

AI shall not be used to generate personal communications—including pastoral emails, counseling responses, or direct relational messaging—under the guise of a specific human author. All AI-assisted communications must be clearly demarcated as institutional or administrative, never masquerading as personal human interaction.

04

AI substitution in care

The deployment of "AI chaplains," automated counseling chatbots, or synthetic companions for congregant care is prohibited. Spiritual guidance, crisis intervention, and emotional support remain the sacred obligation of human caregivers within the community.

05

Deepfakes

The creation, distribution, or utilization of synthetic media designed to mimic the exact likeness or voice of a real individual (deepfakes) is banned across all community communications, educational materials, and creative expressions, regardless of the intended context or consent.
