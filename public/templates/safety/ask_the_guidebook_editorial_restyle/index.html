You asked: "Can I use ChatGPT to summarize prospect research notes for tomorrow's meeting?"

VERDICT

Yes, with constraints.

You may use ChatGPT to summarize research notes, provided the notes contain only publicly available information (Tier 3 data). You must not input any non-public personal details, financial estimates derived internally, or confidential donor history (Tier 2 or Tier 1 data) into any public AI tool, including standard ChatGPT.

GUIDEBOOK CLAUSES REFERENCED
LAYER 02 · POLICY · CONSTRAINED USE

"Employees may utilize public generative AI tools for administrative efficiency, provided no confidential, proprietary, or Tier 1/Tier 2 personal data is entered into the prompt or context window."

Read full clause arrow_forward
LAYER 03 · GUIDELINE · DATA CLASSIFICATION

"Prospect wealth estimates generated internally are considered Tier 2 (Confidential) data. Public biographical information sourced from news articles is considered Tier 3 (Public) data."

Read full clause arrow_forward
LAYER 01 · PRINCIPLE · TRANSPARENCY

"We maintain an obligation to protect stakeholder privacy above operational expediency. If the classification of data is uncertain, it must be treated as Confidential until verified."

Read full clause arrow_forward
WHAT TO DO NEXT
  1. Review your notes: Manually strip out any internal wealth estimates, donor history, or private contact information before pasting into ChatGPT.
  2. Consider Enterprise tools: If your department has access to the approved internal AI assistant (Governance AI Enterprise), use that instead, as it is approved for Tier 2 data.
  3. When in doubt, flag it: If you are unsure if a piece of information is Tier 2 or Tier 3, do not use the public AI tool. Contact the data governance lead.
