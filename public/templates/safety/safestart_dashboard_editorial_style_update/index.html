LAYER 01 · STATEMENT · DRAFT

AI Organizational Statement

A proposed framework for integrating artificial intelligence into our core operational methodology without compromising human-centric values.

edit_calendar Drafted May 13, 2026 by Joshua Shepherd | Awaiting your review | chat_bubble 1 comment from your team

The advent of generative artificial intelligence presents a profound inflection point for modern institutions. It is not merely a tool for efficiency, but a structural shift in how knowledge is synthesized, decisions are formulated, and value is generated. As an organization committed to long-term impact, we must approach this transition with both rigorous optimism and deliberate caution.

Our primary mandate is not technological adoption for its own sake, but the prudent application of technology to amplify our core mission.

add_comment + Comment
Northridge believes that the tools of our age — including AI — must always serve the irreducibly human work of building relationships with the people we are called to serve. We reject the premise that automation equates to abdication of responsibility. Instead, we view AI as a capability multiplier that frees our team to focus on higher-order strategic thinking and deep empathy.

To ensure this alignment, we propose a three-tiered framework for AI integration across our operational layers. First, foundational augmentation: deploying secure, private models to handle routine data processing and initial synthesis. Second, strategic partnership: utilizing advanced reasoning models to challenge assumptions and model complex scenarios during the planning phase. Third, and most critically, human-in-the-loop oversight: mandating that all outputs involving sensitive stakeholder communication or core strategic shifts are reviewed, refined, and ultimately owned by a senior leader.

Implementation Timelines

  • Q3 2026
    Initial rollout of secure internal LLM sandbox for the research and analytics team. Mandatory training on prompt engineering and data privacy protocols.
  • Q4 2026
    Pilot program integrating AI-assisted drafting in external communications, limited to non-critical updates. Establishment of the AI Ethics Oversight Committee.
