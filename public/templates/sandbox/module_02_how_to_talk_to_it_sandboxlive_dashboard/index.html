How to talk to it.

The five core AI interaction skills.

Note

The concept of 'prompt engineering' is rapidly evolving. Focus on clear articulation over syntax tricks.

READING — 17 MINUTES

Five things matter.

Most of what gets called 'prompt engineering' is overcomplicated. The industry has spawned a cottage industry of complex templates, nested brackets, and pseudo-code meant to constrain the model. While technical precision has its place in automated pipelines, for the daily operational user—the strategist, the director, the analyst—it is an unnecessary barrier.

When you talk to it, you are not writing code; you are providing instructions to an entity that understands nuance, context, and intent. The critical failure point is rarely the syntax of the prompt; it is the clarity of the thinker providing it.

We have identified five core skills that separate effective AI utilization from mere curiosity. These are not technical tricks, but communication fundamentals adapted for a non-human counterpart. They require you to be explicit where you are usually implicit, and structured where you are usually casual.

EXERCISE — RECOMMENDED, NOT REQUIRED

Try a request with and without context.

Take a real task from your work this week. Draft a simple, one-sentence request for it. Run it. Then, rewrite the request adding specific contextual framing (who you are, who the audience is, what the stakes are). Compare the tonal difference.
