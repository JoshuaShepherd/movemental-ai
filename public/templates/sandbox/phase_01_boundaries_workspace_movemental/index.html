Boundaries

What this Sandbox protects

Drafted by the cohort with Joshua Shepherd · 3 of 7 cohort members signed · Sponsor countersign pending

The Boundaries agreement establishes the safe operating environment for the Northridge Community Center Sandbox. It defines three critical categories: Publication Boundaries govern what information may leave this space; Privacy Boundaries dictate what data may enter it; and Personal Boundaries establish the safeguards necessary to protect the cognitive and emotional well-being of the cohort members undertaking this deeply transformative work.

Publication Boundaries

What may leave the Sandbox

All outputs, transcripts, synthetic data, and strategic mappings generated within this Sandbox are strictly internal to the cohort. They may not be published, shared, or referenced outside of defined review cycles.

Exceptions: Finalized summary reports presented to the Sponsor (Linda Ozawa) and methodological frameworks retained by the facilitator (Joshua Shepherd) for continuous improvement, provided all identifying organizational data is abstracted.

Personal Boundaries

Safeguards for the people doing the work

Identified Patterns

  • ■ Cognitive fatigue from sustained abstraction.
  • ■ Parasocial attachment to synthetic entities.
  • ■ Reality recalibration during transition periods.

Agreed Safeguards

  • ■ Strict 90-minute time-boxed immersion sessions.
  • ■ Mandatory 15-minute in-person debriefs post-session.
  • ■ Unconditional permission to pause or stop a session.
