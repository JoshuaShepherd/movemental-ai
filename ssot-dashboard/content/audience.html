Audience Definition

Who we serve. All content assumes this “who” and language.

Primary Definition

Movemental serves thought leaders who catalyze movements—individuals who: (1) think systematically—frameworks, principles, reproducible approaches; (2) build networks—collaboration over solo effort; (3) multiply impact—systems that outlast them; (4) prioritize authenticity—voice, theological integrity, mission over algorithm optimization; (5) seek sustainability—economic models that enable full-time movement work.

Naming

We call them: “Movement leaders” (primary, clearest) or “movemental leaders” (secondary, emphasizes systematic/infrastructure focus).

They call themselves: Role-based—pastors, church leaders, professors, church planters, practitioners, missionaries, emerging leaders. They rarely say “movemental leader” until we introduce it; then they recognize themselves (“I do catalyze movements, I do think in terms of multiplication”).

Demographic Breakdown

40% Church leaders & pastors (seminary-trained, graduate degrees; North America / Australia-NZ / UK-Europe). 25% Academic (seminary professors, researchers, missiology/ecclesiology). 20% Movement practitioners (church planters, missional community leaders, organizational catalysts). 15% International (Global South, cross-cultural). Emerging across all: Millennials/Gen Z, next-gen leaders.

Psychographic

Characteristics: Movemental thinking, network orientation, theological depth, cultural engagement, multiplication focus. Pains: Digital sharecropping (70–90% to platforms), $50K–$150K barrier, fragmented presence (5–8 platforms), limited amplification, algorithm dependency. Aspirations: Own platform and audience, sustainable revenue, network amplification, preserve voice, lasting digital legacy.

Six Archetypes

Core movement leaders (Tier 1); emerging movement voices (Tier 2); academic movement scholars (Tier 3); practitioner movement leaders (Tier 4); justice & reconciliation leaders (Tier 5); global movement connectors (Tier 6). Assessment: Movement alignment & theological depth, audience size & engagement, content creation & platform readiness, revenue potential, network effects, strategic fit.
